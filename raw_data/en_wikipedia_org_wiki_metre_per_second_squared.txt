From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

SI derived unit of acceleration

| Metre per second squared |
| --- |
| [Unit system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System_of_units_of_measurement "System of units of measurement") | [SI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_System_of_Units "International System of Units") |
| Unit of | [acceleration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acceleration "Acceleration") |
| Symbol | ⁠m/s 2⁠ |

The **metre per second squared** or **metre per square second** is the [unit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Units_of_measurement "Units of measurement") of [acceleration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acceleration "Acceleration") in the [International System of Units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_System_of_Units "International System of Units") (SI). As a [derived unit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SI_derived_unit "SI derived unit"), it is composed from the [SI base units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SI_base_unit "SI base unit") of length, the [metre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metre "Metre"), and of time, the [second](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second "Second"). Its symbol is written in several forms as **m/s 2**, **m·s−2** or **ms−2**, m s 2{\displaystyle {\tfrac {\operatorname {m} }{\operatorname {s} ^{2}}}}![Image 6: {\displaystyle {\tfrac {\operatorname {m} }{\operatorname {s} ^{2}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b4980c39292a56fcf74979519de573484cbb088e), or less commonly, as **(m/s)/s**.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metre_per_second_squared#cite_note-1)

As acceleration, the unit is interpreted physically as change in velocity or speed per time interval, i.e. [metre per second](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metre_per_second "Metre per second") per [second](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second "Second") and is treated as a vector quantity.

Example
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Metre_per_second_squared&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Example")]

When an object experiences a constant acceleration of one metre per second squared (1 m/s 2) from a state of rest, it achieves the speed of 5 m/s after 5 seconds and 10 m/s after 10 seconds. The average acceleration _a_ can be calculated by dividing the speed _v_ (m/s) by the time _t_ (s), so the average acceleration in the first example would be calculated:

a=Δ v Δ t=5 m/s 5 s=1(m/s)/s=1 m/s 2{\displaystyle a={\frac {\Delta v}{\Delta t}}={\frac {5{\text{ m/s}}}{5{\text{ s}}}}=1{\text{ (m/s)/s}}=1{\text{ m/s}}^{2}}![Image 7: {\displaystyle a={\frac {\Delta v}{\Delta t}}={\frac {5{\text{ m/s}}}{5{\text{ s}}}}=1{\text{ (m/s)/s}}=1{\text{ m/s}}^{2}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b9b5dc0cf47d48f6b0e483c63649905b4981f68f).

Related units
-------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Metre_per_second_squared&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Related units")]

[Newton's second law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%27s_second_law "Newton's second law") states that force equals mass multiplied by acceleration. The unit of force is the [newton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton_(unit) "Newton (unit)") (N), and mass has the SI unit [kilogram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kilogram "Kilogram") (kg). One newton equals one [kilogram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kilogram "Kilogram") metre per second squared. Therefore, the unit metre per second squared is equivalent to newton per kilogram, N·kg−1, or N/kg.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metre_per_second_squared#cite_note-2)

Thus, the Earth's gravitational field (near ground level) can be quoted as 9.8 metres per second squared, or the equivalent 9.8 N/kg.

Acceleration can be measured in ratios to gravity, such as [g-force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G-force "G-force"), and [peak ground acceleration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peak_ground_acceleration "Peak ground acceleration") in earthquakes.

Unicode character
-----------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Metre_per_second_squared&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Unicode character")]

The "metre per second squared" symbol is encoded by [Unicode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode "Unicode") at code point U+33A8㎨SQUARE M OVER S SQUARED. This is for compatibility with East Asian encodings and not intended to be used in new documents.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metre_per_second_squared#cite_note-Unicode-U3300-3)
