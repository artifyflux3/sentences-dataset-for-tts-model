The term lacunosus is from Latin , meaning "full of gaps". This type of cloud appears as holes present in an altocumulus cloud cover resembling a honeycomb. It is considered to be rare, since it is typically short-lived.

[ 1 ] Lacunosus cloud types (including this one) forms when a layer of cold air and a layer of warmer air come in contact with each other. This then causes the cold air to sink in the form of localized downdrafts . These downdrafts then strike through the cloud and create the holes.
