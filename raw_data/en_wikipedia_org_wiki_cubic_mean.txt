Definition [ edit ] For n {\displaystyle n} real numbers x i ∈ ∈ R {\displaystyle x_{i}\in \mathbb {R} } the cubic mean is defined as: x ¯ ¯ c u b i c = 1 n ∑ ∑ i = 1 n x i 3 3 = x 1 3 + x 2 3 + ⋯ ⋯ + x n 3 n 3 .

{\displaystyle {\bar {x}}_{\mathrm {cubic} }={\sqrt[{3}]{{\frac {1}{n}}\sum _{i=1}^{n}{x_{i}^{3}}}}={\sqrt[{3}]{{x_{1}^{3}+x_{2}^{3}+\cdots +x_{n}^{3}} \over n}}.} [ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ] For example, the cubic mean of two numbers is: x 1 3 + x 2 3 2 3 {\displaystyle {\sqrt[{3}]{\frac {x_{1}^{3}+x_{2}^{3}}{2}}}} .

Applications [ edit ] The cubic mean is used to predict the life expectancy of machine parts .

[ 3 ] [ 4 ] [ 5 ] [ 6 ] The cubic mean wind speed has been used a measure of local potential for wind energy .

[ 7 ] The cubic mean is also used in biology to measure the mean dimensions of spherical bacteria ( cocci ) [ 8 ] and of larger animals that are approximately spheroidal in shape.

[ 9 ] In this case using the conventional arithmetic mean will not give an accurate result because the size of a spherical bacterium increases as the cube of the radius.
