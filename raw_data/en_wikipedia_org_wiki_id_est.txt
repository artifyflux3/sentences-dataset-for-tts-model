Latin Translation Notes
_I, Vitelli, dei Romani sono belli_ Go, O Vitellius, at the war sound of the Roman god Perfectly correct Latin sentence usually reported as funny by modern Italians because the same exact words, in Italian, mean "Romans' calves are beautiful", which has a ridiculously different meaning.
_[ibidem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibid. "Ibid.")_ (_ibid._)in the same place Usually used in [bibliographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bibliography "Bibliography") citations to refer to the last source previously referenced.
_id est_ (_i.e._)that is (literally "it is")"That is (to say)" in the sense of "that means" and "which means", or "in other words", "namely", or sometimes "in this case", depending on the context.
_id quod plerumque accidit_ that which generally happens Phrase used in legal language to indicate the most probable outcome from an act, fact, event or cause
_[idem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idem "Idem")_ (_id._)the same Used to refer to something that has already been cited; ditto. See also _ibidem_.
_idem quod_ (_i.q._)the same as Not to be confused with an [intelligence quotient](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intelligence_quotient "Intelligence quotient").
_Idus Martiae_ the [Ides of March](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ides_of_March "Ides of March")In the [Roman calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_calendar "Roman calendar"), the Ides of March refers to the 15th day of March. In modern times, the term is best known as the date on which [Julius Caesar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julius_Caesar "Julius Caesar") was assassinated in 44 BC; the term has come to be used as a metaphor for impending doom.
_Jesu juva_ (_J.J._)Jesus, help!Used by [Johann Sebastian Bach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach "Johann Sebastian Bach") at the beginning of his compositions, which he ended with "S.D.G." ([Soli Deo gloria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soli_Deo_gloria "Soli Deo gloria")). Compare [Besiyata Dishmaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Besiyata_Dishmaya "Besiyata Dishmaya").
_Iesus Nazarenus Rex Iudaeorum_ (_[INRI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/INRI "INRI")_)[Jesus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesus "Jesus") the [Nazarene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nazarene_(title) "Nazarene (title)"), [King of the Jews](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesus,_King_of_the_Jews "Jesus, King of the Jews")From [Vulgate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulgate "Vulgate"); [John](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gospel_of_John "Gospel of John")[19:19](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=John+19:19&version=vulgate). [John 19:20](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Bible_(American_Standard)/John#19:20 "wikisource:Bible (American Standard)/John") states that this inscription was written in three languages—Aramaic, Latin and Greek—at the top of the [cross](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/True_Cross "True Cross") during the [crucifixion of Jesus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crucifixion_of_Jesus "Crucifixion of Jesus").
[_igitur qui desiderat pacem, praeparet bellum_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Si_vis_pacem,_para_bellum "Si vis pacem, para bellum")Therefore whoever desires peace, let him prepare for war[Publius Flavius Vegetius Renatus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Publius_Flavius_Vegetius_Renatus "Publius Flavius Vegetius Renatus"), _[De re militari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De\_re\_militari "De re militari")_; similar to _[si vis pacem, para bellum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Si\_vis\_pacem,\_para\_bellum "Si vis pacem, para bellum")_ and [_in pace ut sapiens aptarit idonea bello_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Id_est#in_pace_ut_sapiens_aptarit_idonea_bello).
_igne natura renovatur integra_ through fire, nature is reborn whole An [alchemical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alchemy "Alchemy") aphorism invented as an alternate meaning for the acronym [INRI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/INRI "INRI").
_igni ferroque_ with fire and iron Phrase describing [scorched earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scorched_earth "Scorched earth") tactics. Also rendered as _igne atque ferro_, _ferro ignique_, and other variations.
_ignis aurum probat_ fire tests gold Phrase referring to the refining of character through difficult circumstances
_ignis fatuus_ foolish fire[Will-o'-the-wisp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will-o%27-the-wisp "Will-o'-the-wisp").
_[ignorantia juris non excusat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ignorantia\_juris\_non\_excusat "Ignorantia juris non excusat")_ (or _ignorantia legis non excusat_ or _ignorantia legis neminem excusat_)ignorance of the law is no excuse Legal principle whereby ignorance of a law does not allow one to escape liability
_[ignoratio elenchi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ignoratio\_elenchi "Ignoratio elenchi")_ ignorance of the issue The [logical fallacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formal_fallacy "Formal fallacy") of irrelevant conclusion: making an argument that, while possibly valid, doesn't prove or support the proposition it claims to. An _ignoratio elenchi_ that is an intentional attempt to mislead or confuse the opposing party is known as a _[red herring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ignoratio\_elenchi "Ignoratio elenchi")_. _Elenchi_ is from the Greek _[elenchos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elenchos "Elenchos")_.
_[ignotum per ignotius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ignotum\_per\_ignotius "Ignotum per ignotius")_ unknown by means of the more unknown An explanation that is less clear than the thing to be explained. Synonymous with _obscurum per obscurius_.
_ignotus_ (_ign._)unknown
_illum oportet crescere me autem minui_ He must become greater; I must become less In the [Gospel of John](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gospel_of_John "Gospel of John")[3:30](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Bible_(King_James)/John#3:30 "s:Bible (King James)/John"), a phrase said by [John the Baptist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_the_Baptist "John the Baptist") after baptizing [Jesus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesus "Jesus"). Motto of [Saint John the Baptist Catholic School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint_John_the_Baptist_Catholic_School "Saint John the Baptist Catholic School"), [San Juan, Metro Manila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Juan,_Metro_Manila "San Juan, Metro Manila").
_[imago Dei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imago\_Dei "Imago Dei")_ image of God From the religious concept that man was created in "God's image".
_[imitatio dei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imitatio\_dei "Imitatio dei")_ imitation of a god A principle, held by several religions, that believers should strive to resemble their god(s).
_imperium in imperio_ an order within an order 1.   Group of people who owe utmost fealty to their leader(s), subordinating the interests of the larger group to the authority of the internal group's leader(s).
2.   A "[fifth column](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fifth_column "Fifth column")" organization operating against the organization within which they seemingly reside.
3.   "[State within a state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_within_a_state "State within a state")"
_imperium sine fine_ an empire without an end In [Virgil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgil "Virgil")'s _[Aeneid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aeneid "Aeneid")_, [Jupiter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jupiter_(mythology) "Jupiter (mythology)") ordered [Aeneas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aeneas "Aeneas") to found a city ([Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rome "Rome")) from which would come an everlasting, never-ending empire, the endless (_sine fine_) empire.
_impossibilium nulla obligatio est_ there is no obligation to do the impossible[Publius Juventius Celsus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Publius_Juventius_Celsus "Publius Juventius Celsus"), _Digesta_ L 17, 185.
_[imprimatur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imprimatur "Imprimatur")_ let it be printed An authorization to publish, granted by some censoring authority (originally a [Catholic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church "Roman Catholic Church") bishop).
_[in absentia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_absentia\_(disambiguation) "In absentia (disambiguation)")_ in the absence Used in a number of situations, such as in a trial carried out in the absence of the accused.
_in absentia lucis, tenebrae vincunt_ in the absence of light, darkness prevails
_in actu_ in act In the very act; in reality.
[_Dominica_] _in albis_ [_depositis_][Sunday in Setting Aside the] White Garments Latin name of the [Octave of Easter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octave_of_Easter#Western_churches "Octave of Easter").
_in articulo mortis_ at the point of death
_in bono veritas_ truth is in the good
_[in camera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_camera "In camera")_ in the chamber In secret. See also _camera obscura_.
_in casu_ (_i.c._)in the event In this case.
_in cauda venenum_ the poison is in the tail Using the [metaphor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metaphor "Metaphor") of a [scorpion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scorpion "Scorpion"), this can be said of an account that proceeds gently, but turns vicious towards the end—or more generally waits till the end to reveal an intention or statement that is undesirable in the listener's ears.
_in com. Ebor._ In the county of Yorkshire Abbreviation of _in comitatu Eboraci_. Eboracum was the Roman name for York and this phrase is used in some Georgian and Victorian books on the genealogy of prominent Yorkshire families.
_in Christi lumine pro mundi vita_ in the light of Christ for the life on the world Motto of the [Pontifical Catholic University of Chile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Chile "Pontifical Catholic University of Chile").
_[incurvatus in se](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incurvatus\_in\_se "Incurvatus in se")_ turned/curved inward on oneself
_in defensum castitatis_ in defence of chastity Used in reference to the deaths of some [Christian martyrs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_martyrs "Christian martyrs") in Catholicism
_in Deo speramus_ in God we hope Motto of [Brown University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brown_University "Brown University").
_[in dubio pro reo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_dubio\_pro\_reo "In dubio pro reo")_ in doubt, on behalf of the [alleged] culprit Expresses the judicial principle that in case of doubt the decision must be in favor of the accused (in that anyone is innocent until there is proof to the contrary).
_in duplo_ in double In duplicate
_in effigie_ in the likeness In (the form of) an image; in effigy (as opposed to "in the flesh" or "in person").
_in esse_ in existence In actual existence; as opposed to _in posse_.
_in extenso_ in the extended In full; at full length; complete or unabridged
_in extremis_ in the furthest reaches At the very end. In extremity; in dire straits; also "at the point of death" (cf. _in articulo mortis_).
_in facie_ in the face Refers to [contempt of court](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contempt_of_court "Contempt of court") committed in open court in front of the judge; contrast _[ex facie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ex\_facie "Ex facie")_.
_in fide scientiam_ To our faith add knowledge Motto of [Newington College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newington_College "Newington College").
_in fidem_ into faith To the verification of faith.
_in fieri_ in becoming In progress; pending.
_in fine_ (_i.f._)in the end At the end. Used in footnotes, for example, "p. 157 _in fine_": "the end of page 157".
_[in flagrante delicto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_flagrante\_delicto "In flagrante delicto")_ in a blazing wrong, while the crime is blazing Caught in the act (esp. a crime or in a "compromising position"); equivalent to "caught red-handed" in English idiom.
_in flore_ in blossom Blooming.
_in foro_ in forum In court ([legal term](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_legal_Latin_terms "List of legal Latin terms")).
_[in forma pauperis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_forma\_pauperis "In forma pauperis")_ in the character or manner of a pauper
_in girum imus nocte et consumimur igni_ We enter the circle at night and are consumed by fire A [palindrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palindrome "Palindrome") said to describe the behavior of moths. Also the title of a film by [Guy Debord](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guy_Debord "Guy Debord").
_in harmonia progressio_ progress in harmony Motto of [Bandung Institute of Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bandung_Institute_of_Technology "Bandung Institute of Technology"), Indonesia.
_in hoc sensu_, or _in sensu hoc_ (_s.h._)in this sense Recent academic abbreviation for "in this sense".
_[in hoc signo vinces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_hoc\_signo\_vinces "In hoc signo vinces")_ by this sign you will conquer Words which [Constantine the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constantine_the_Great "Constantine the Great") claimed to have seen in a vision before the [Battle of the Milvian Bridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Milvian_Bridge "Battle of the Milvian Bridge").
_in hunc effectum_ for this purpose Describes a meeting called for a particular stated purpose only.
_[in ictu oculi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_ictu\_oculi "In ictu oculi")_ in the blink of an eye
_in illo ordine_ (_i.o._)in that order Recent academic substitution for the spacious and inconvenient "...,respectively".
_in illo tempore_ in that time At that time, or in those days, often used at the start of a liturgical scripture reading to mark an undetermined time in the past or to replace contextual material which is not being read.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Id_est#cite_note-2)
_in inceptum finis est_ lit.: in the beginning is the end or: the beginning foreshadows the end
_in limine_ at the outset/threshold Preliminary, in law, a [motion in limine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motion_in_limine "Motion in limine") is a [motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motion_(legal) "Motion (legal)") that is made to the judge before or during trial, often about the admissibility of evidence believed prejudicial.
_in loco_ in the place, on the spot That is, 'on site', e.g. "The nearby labs were closed for the weekend, so the water samples were analyzed _in loco_."
_[in loco parentis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_loco\_parentis "In loco parentis")_ in the place of a parent Assuming parental or custodial responsibility and authority (e.g., [schoolteachers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schoolteacher "Schoolteacher") over students); a legal term.
_in luce Tua videmus lucem_ in Thy light we see light Motto of [Valparaiso University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valparaiso_University "Valparaiso University"). The phrase comes from [Psalm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_of_Psalms "Book of Psalms") 36:9: "For with you is the fountain of life; in your light we see light."
_in lumine tuo videbimus lumen_ in your light we will see the light Motto of [Columbia University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columbia_University "Columbia University") New York City, [Presbyterian Boys' Senior High School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presbyterian_Boys%27_Senior_High_School "Presbyterian Boys' Senior High School") Ghana, [Ohio Wesleyan University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ohio_Wesleyan_University "Ohio Wesleyan University"), [University of Fort Hare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Fort_Hare "University of Fort Hare") South Africa
_in manus tuas commendo spiritum meum_ into your hands I entrust my spirit According to [Luke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gospel_of_Luke "Gospel of Luke") 23:46, the last words of [Jesus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesus "Jesus") on the cross
_[in medias res](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_medias\_res "In medias res")_ into the middle of things From [Horace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horace "Horace"). Refers to the literary technique of beginning a narrative in the middle of, or at a late point in, the story, after much action has already taken place. Examples include the _[Iliad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iliad "Iliad")_, the _[Odyssey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odyssey "Odyssey")_, _[Os Lusíadas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Os\_Lus%C3%ADadas "Os Lusíadas")_, _[Othello](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Othello "Othello")_, and _[Paradise Lost](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paradise\_Lost "Paradise Lost")_. Compare _ab initio_.
_in memoriam_ into the memory Equivalent to "in the memory of". Refers to remembering or honoring a deceased person.
_[in natura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_natura "In natura")_ in nature
_[in necessariis unitas, in dubiis libertas, in omnibus caritas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_necessariis\_unitas,\_in\_dubiis\_libertas,\_in\_omnibus\_caritas "In necessariis unitas, in dubiis libertas, in omnibus caritas")_ in necessary things unity, in doubtful things liberty, in all things charity"[Charity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charity_(virtue) "Charity (virtue)")" (_caritas_) is being used in the classical sense of "compassion" (cf. _[agape](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agape "Agape")_). Motto of the [Cartellverband der katholischen deutschen Studentenverbindungen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartellverband_der_katholischen_deutschen_Studentenverbindungen "Cartellverband der katholischen deutschen Studentenverbindungen"). Often misattributed to [Augustine of Hippo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augustine_of_Hippo "Augustine of Hippo").[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]
_in nocte consilium_ advice comes in the night; "sleep on it"Motto of [Birkbeck College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birkbeck,_University_of_London "Birkbeck, University of London"), University of London, an evening higher-education institution[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Id_est#cite_note-3)
_in nomine diaboli_ in the name of the devil
_[in nomine Domini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_nomine\_Domini "In nomine Domini")_ in the name of the Lord Motto of [Trinity College, Perth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trinity_College,_Perth "Trinity College, Perth"), Australia; the name of a 1050 [papal bull](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papal_bull "Papal bull")
_in nomine patris, et filii, et spiritus sancti_ in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit Invocation of the [Holy Trinity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Trinity "Holy Trinity"); part of the Latin Mass
_in nuce_ in a nut in a nutshell; briefly stated; potential; in the embryonic phase
_[in odium fidei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_odium\_fidei "In odium fidei")_ in hatred of the faith Used in reference to the deaths of some [Christian martyrs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_martyrs "Christian martyrs") in Catholicism
_in omnia paratus_ ready for anything Motto of the [United States Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Army "United States Army")'s [18th Infantry Regiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/18th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States) "18th Infantry Regiment (United States)")
_in omnibus amare et servire Domino_ in everything, love and serve the Lord The motto of [Ateneo de Iloilo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ateneo_de_Iloilo "Ateneo de Iloilo"), a school in the Philippines
_in omnibus requiem quaesivi, et nusquam inveni nisi in angulo cum libro_ Everywhere I have searched for peace and nowhere found it, except in a corner with a book Quote by [Thomas à Kempis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_%C3%A0_Kempis "Thomas à Kempis")
_in ovo_ in the egg An experiment or process performed in an egg or embryo (e.g. in ovo electroporation of chicken embryo).
_in pace ut sapiens aptarit idonea bello_ in peace, like the wise man, make preparations for war[Horace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horace "Horace"), [_Satires_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satires_(Horace) "Satires (Horace)") 2/2:111; similar to _[si vis pacem, para bellum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Si\_vis\_pacem,\_para\_bellum "Si vis pacem, para bellum")_ and [_igitur qui desiderat pacem, praeparet bellum_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Id_est#igitur_qui_desiderat_pacem,_praeparet_bellum).
[_in pace requiescat_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requiescat_in_pace "Requiescat in pace")in peace may he rest Alternate form of _requiescat in pace_ ("let him rest in peace"). Found in this form at the end of [The Cask of Amontillado](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Cask_of_Amontillado "The Cask of Amontillado") by [Edgar Allan Poe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edgar_Allan_Poe "Edgar Allan Poe").
_in pari materia_ upon the same matter or subject In [statutory interpretation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statutory_interpretation#Canons "Statutory interpretation"), when a statute is ambiguous, its meaning may be determined in light of other statutes on the same subject matter.
_[in pari delicto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_pari\_delicto "In pari delicto")_ in equal fault
_[in partibus infidelium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_partibus\_infidelium "In partibus infidelium")_ in the parts of the infidels"In the land of the [infidels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infidel "Infidel")"; used to refer to bishoprics that remain as [titular sees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titular_see "Titular see") even after the corresponding territory was conquered, usually by Muslim rulers.
_[in pectore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_pectore "In pectore")_ in the heart A [cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism) "Cardinal (Catholicism)") named in secret by the [pope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope "Pope"). See also _ab imo pectore_.
_[in personam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_personam "In personam")_ against a person Directed towards a particular person
_in posse_ in potential In the state of being possible; as opposed to _in esse_.
_in propria persona_ in one's own person For one's self, for the sake of one's [personhood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personhood "Personhood"); acting on one's own behalf, especially a person representing themselves in a legal proceeding; abbreviated _pro per_. See also _pro se_: [litigant in person](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Litigant_in_person "Litigant in person"), [_pro se_ legal representation in the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pro_se_legal_representation_in_the_United_States "Pro se legal representation in the United States").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Id_est#cite_note-4)
_in principio erat Verbum_ in the beginning was the Word ([Logos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logos_(Christianity) "Logos (Christianity)"))[Beginning of the Gospel of John](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_1:1 "John 1:1")
_[in re](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_re "In re")_ in the matter [of]Legal term used to indicate that a judicial proceeding may not have formally designated adverse parties or is otherwise uncontested. The term is commonly used in case citations of [probate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probate "Probate") proceedings, for example, _In re Smith's Estate_; it is also used in [juvenile courts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juvenile_court "Juvenile court"), as, for instance, _[In re Gault](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_re\_Gault "In re Gault")_.
_in rebus_ in the thing [itself]Primarily of philosophical use to discuss properties and property exemplification. In [philosophy of mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophy_of_mathematics "Philosophy of mathematics"), it is typically contrasted with "ante rem" and, more recently, "post res" [structuralism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Structuralism_(philosophy_of_mathematics) "Structuralism (philosophy of mathematics)"). Sometimes _in re_ is used in place of _in rebus_.
_in regione caecorum rex est luscus_ In the land of the blind, the one-eyed man is king.Quote of [Desiderius Erasmus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desiderius_Erasmus "Desiderius Erasmus") from _[Adagia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adagia "Adagia")_ (first published 1500, with numerous expanded editions through 1536), III, IV, 96.
_[in rem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_rem "In rem")_ against the thing Legal term indicating a court's jurisdiction over a piece of [property](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Property "Property") rather than a [legal person](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_personality "Legal personality"); contrast with [personal (_ad personam_) jurisdiction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_jurisdiction "Personal jurisdiction"). See [In rem jurisdiction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In_rem_jurisdiction "In rem jurisdiction"); [Quasi in rem jurisdiction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quasi_in_rem_jurisdiction "Quasi in rem jurisdiction")
_in rerum natura_ in the nature of things See also [Lucretius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucretius "Lucretius")' _[De rerum natura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De\_rerum\_natura "De rerum natura")_ (_On the Nature of Things_).
_in retentis_ among things held back Used to describe documents kept separately from the regular records of a court for special reasons.
_in saecula_ (_saeculorum_), _in saeculum saeculi_ _roughly_: down to the times of the times forever (and ever); liturgical
_in saeculo_ in the times In the [secular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secularism "Secularism") world, esp. outside a [monastery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monastery "Monastery"), or before death.
_in salvo_ in safety

_in se magna ruunt_ great things collapse of their own weight[Lucan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucan "Lucan"), _[Pharsalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharsalia "Pharsalia")_ 1:81
_in silvam non ligna feras_ Do not carry wood to the forest[Horace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horace "Horace"), _[Satires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satires\_(Horace) "Satires (Horace)")_ 1:10
