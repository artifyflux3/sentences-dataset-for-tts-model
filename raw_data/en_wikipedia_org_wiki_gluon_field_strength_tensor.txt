In [theoretical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theoretical_physics "Theoretical physics")[particle physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particle_physics "Particle physics"), the **gluon field strength tensor** is a second-order [tensor field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_field "Tensor field") characterizing the [gluon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gluon "Gluon") interaction between [quarks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quark "Quark").

The [strong interaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strong_interaction "Strong interaction") is one of the [fundamental interactions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fundamental_interaction "Fundamental interaction") of nature, and the [quantum field theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_field_theory "Quantum field theory") (QFT) to describe it is called _[quantum chromodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum\_chromodynamics "Quantum chromodynamics")_ (QCD). [Quarks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quark "Quark") interact with each other by the strong force due to their [color charge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Color_charge "Color charge"), mediated by gluons. Gluons themselves possess color charge and can mutually interact.

The gluon field strength tensor is a [rank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_rank "Tensor rank")-2 tensor field on the [spacetime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacetime "Spacetime") with values in the [adjoint bundle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adjoint_bundle "Adjoint bundle") of the chromodynamical SU(3) [gauge group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauge_group "Gauge group") (see [vector bundle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_bundle "Vector bundle") for necessary definitions).

Throughout this article, Latin indices (typically _a_, _b_, _c_, _n_) take values 1, 2, ..., 8 for the eight gluon [color charges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Color_charge "Color charge"), while Greek indices (typically _α_, _β_, _μ_, _ν_) take values 0 for timelike components and 1, 2, 3 for spacelike components of [four-vectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Four-vector "Four-vector") and four-dimensional spacetime tensors. In all equations, the [summation convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_notation "Einstein notation") is used on all color and tensor indices, unless the text explicitly states that there is no sum to be taken (e.g. “no sum”).

As with the gluon field, in a specific coordinate system and fixed gauge _G αβ_ are 3×3 traceless Hermitian matrix-valued functions, while _G a αβ_ are real-valued functions, the components of eight four-dimensional second-order tensor fields.

The gluon color field can be described using the language of [differential forms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_form "Differential form"), specifically as an adjoint bundle-valued [curvature 2-form](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curvature_form "Curvature form") (note that fibers of the adjoint bundle are the **su**(3) [Lie algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lie_algebra "Lie algebra")); ![Image 11: {\displaystyle \mathbf {G} =\mathrm {d} {\boldsymbol {\mathcal {A}}}\mp g_{\text{s}}\,{\boldsymbol {\mathcal {A}}}\wedge {\boldsymbol {\mathcal {A}}},}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/56c210424a3be3b5f8cd9535d34671d15f313484) where ![Image 12: {\displaystyle {\boldsymbol {\mathcal {A}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/d98ee6d0424e28cc96f6b0274400b4b6db014829) is the gluon field, a [vector potential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_potential "Vector potential") 1-form corresponding to **G**, and ∧ is the (antisymmetric) [wedge product](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exterior_algebra "Exterior algebra") of this algebra, producing the structure constants _f abc_. The [Cartan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89lie_Cartan "Élie Cartan")-derivative of the field form (i.e. essentially the divergence of the field) would be zero in the absence of the "gluon terms", i.e. those ![Image 13: {\displaystyle {\boldsymbol {\mathcal {A}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/d98ee6d0424e28cc96f6b0274400b4b6db014829) which represent the non-abelian character of the SU(3).

A more mathematically formal derivation of these same ideas (but a slightly altered setting) can be found in the article on [metric connections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metric_connection "Metric connection").

### Comparison with the electromagnetic tensor

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gluon_field_strength_tensor&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Comparison with the electromagnetic tensor")]

This almost parallels the [electromagnetic field tensor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetic_field_tensor "Electromagnetic field tensor") (also denoted _F_) in [quantum electrodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_electrodynamics "Quantum electrodynamics"), given by the [electromagnetic four-potential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetic_four-potential "Electromagnetic four-potential")_A_ describing a spin-1 [photon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photon "Photon"): ![Image 14: {\displaystyle F_{\alpha \beta }=\partial _{\alpha }A_{\beta }-\partial _{\beta }A_{\alpha },}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/548dafed7fd2b5215c86cfa8e43b9818327246ca) or in the language of differential forms, ![Image 15: {\displaystyle \mathbf {F} =\mathrm {d} \mathbf {A} .}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8a139e71fd887181846735a22a5ba51c136f40c4)

The key difference between quantum electrodynamics and quantum chromodynamics is that the gluon field strength has extra terms which lead to [self-interactions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-interaction "Self-interaction") between the gluons and [asymptotic freedom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asymptotic_freedom "Asymptotic freedom"). This is a complication of the strong force making it inherently [non-linear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonlinear_system "Nonlinear system"), contrary to the linear theory of the electromagnetic force. QCD is a [non-abelian gauge theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-abelian_gauge_theory "Non-abelian gauge theory"). The word _non-abelian_ in [group-theoretical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_theory "Group theory") language means that the group operation is not [commutative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commutative_property "Commutative property"), making the corresponding Lie algebra non-trivial.

QCD Lagrangian density
----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gluon_field_strength_tensor&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: QCD Lagrangian density")]

Characteristic of field theories, the dynamics of the field strength are summarized by a suitable [Lagrangian density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrangian_density "Lagrangian density") and substitution into the [Euler–Lagrange equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euler%E2%80%93Lagrange_equation "Euler–Lagrange equation") (for fields) obtains the [equation of motion for the field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equation_of_motion#Analogues_for_waves_and_fields "Equation of motion"). The Lagrangian density for massless quarks, bound by gluons, is[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gluon_field_strength_tensor#cite_note-Greiner,_Sch%C3%A4fer-2)![Image 16: {\displaystyle {\mathcal {L}}=-{\frac {1}{2}}\operatorname {tr} (G_{\alpha \beta }G^{\alpha \beta })+{\bar {\psi }}(iD_{\mu })\gamma ^{\mu }\psi ,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/34a3f5fba17d19189354ca8a9888cca5f4dd1f0c) where "tr" denotes [trace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trace_(linear_algebra) "Trace (linear algebra)") of the 3×3 matrix _G αβ G αβ_, and _γ μ_ are the 4×4[gamma matrices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamma_matrices "Gamma matrices"). In the fermionic term ![Image 17: {\displaystyle i{\bar {\psi }}(iD_{\mu })\gamma ^{\mu }\psi }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/faed0b5284e4c4d6f3975b7bf39fdec49f1506cc), both color and spinor indices are suppressed. With indices explicit, ![Image 18: {\displaystyle \psi _{i,\alpha }}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0846cb02a6cb13b29255d6d776a396dfe9628d36), where ![Image 19: {\displaystyle i=1,\ldots ,3}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ac75093d7cba6aca2456567ad2f3a366e3b589fe) are color indices, and ![Image 20: {\displaystyle \alpha =1,\ldots ,4}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/cf448eadc2501851f5acca14e54679e727679a98) are Dirac spinor indices.

Gauge transformations
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gluon_field_strength_tensor&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Gauge transformations")]

In contrast to QED, the gluon field strength tensor is not gauge invariant by itself. Only the product of two contracted over all indices is gauge invariant.

Equations of motion
-------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gluon_field_strength_tensor&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Equations of motion")]

Treated as a classical field theory, the equations of motion for the quark fields are[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gluon_field_strength_tensor#cite_note-Yagi,_Hatsuda,_Miake-1)![Image 21: {\displaystyle (i\hbar \gamma ^{\mu }D_{\mu }-mc)\psi =0,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1d5560070e7e5655318af170728f3f1218f3187d) which is like the [Dirac equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dirac_equation "Dirac equation"), and the equations of motion for the gluon (gauge) fields are ![Image 22: {\displaystyle [D_{\mu },G^{\mu \nu }]=g_{\text{s}}j^{\nu },}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/e04d6bf547aa3e079555b2443c350fd256191814) which are similar to the [Maxwell equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maxwell_equations "Maxwell equations") (when written in tensor notation). More specifically, these are the [Yang–Mills equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yang%E2%80%93Mills_theory "Yang–Mills theory") for quark and gluon fields. The [color charge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Color_charge "Color charge")[four-current](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Four-current "Four-current") is the source of the gluon field strength tensor, analogous to the electromagnetic four-current as the source of the electromagnetic tensor. It is given by ![Image 23: {\displaystyle j^{\nu }=t^{b}j_{b}^{\nu },\quad j_{b}^{\nu }={\bar {\psi }}\gamma ^{\nu }t^{b}\psi ,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7a72ce8c42482af82f42fabf7e12eefa201ed3ee) which is a conserved current, since color charge is conserved. In other words, the color four-current must satisfy the [continuity equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continuity_equation "Continuity equation")![Image 24: {\displaystyle D_{\nu }j^{\nu }=0.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a4550f37d29926e45780838eea4a715a2ad8d555)
