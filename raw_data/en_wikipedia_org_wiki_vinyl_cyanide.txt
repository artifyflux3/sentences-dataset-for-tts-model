**Acrylonitrile** is an [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the [formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula")CH 2 CHCN and the [structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_chemistry "Organic chemistry")H 2 C=CH−C≡N. It is a colorless, [volatile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volatile_organic_compound "Volatile organic compound") liquid. It has a pungent odor of garlic or onions.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-atsdr-4) Its [molecular structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_structure "Molecular structure") consists of a [vinyl group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_group "Vinyl group") (−CH=CH 2) linked to a [nitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrile "Nitrile") (−C≡N). It is an important [monomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monomer "Monomer") for the manufacture of useful [plastics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plastic "Plastic") such as [polyacrylonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyacrylonitrile "Polyacrylonitrile"). It is [reactive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reactivity_(chemistry) "Reactivity (chemistry)") and [toxic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toxic "Toxic") at low doses.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-ullmann-5)

Acrylonitrile is one of the components of ABS plastic ([acrylonitrile butadiene styrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrylonitrile_butadiene_styrene "Acrylonitrile butadiene styrene")).[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-6)

Structure and basic properties
------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Acrylonitrile&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Structure and basic properties")]

Acrylonitrile is an [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the [formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula")CH 2 CHCN and the [structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_chemistry "Organic chemistry")H 2 C=CH−C≡N. It is a colorless, [volatile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volatile_organic_compound "Volatile organic compound") liquid although commercial samples can be yellow due to [impurities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Impurities "Impurities"). It has a pungent odor of garlic or onions.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-atsdr-4) Its [molecular structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_structure "Molecular structure") consists of a [vinyl group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_group "Vinyl group") (−CH=CH 2) linked to a [nitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrile "Nitrile") (−C≡N). It is an important [monomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monomer "Monomer") for the manufacture of useful [plastics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plastic "Plastic") such as [polyacrylonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyacrylonitrile "Polyacrylonitrile"). It is [reactive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reactivity_(chemistry) "Reactivity (chemistry)") and [toxic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toxic "Toxic") at low doses.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-ullmann-5)

Acrylonitrile was first synthesized by the French chemist [Charles Moureu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Moureu "Charles Moureu") in 1893.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-7) Acrylonitrile is produced by [catalytic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalyst "Catalyst")[ammoxidation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammoxidation "Ammoxidation") of [propylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propylene "Propylene"), also known as the [SOHIO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_Oil_of_Ohio "Standard Oil of Ohio") process. In 2002, world production capacity was estimated at 5 million tonnes per year,[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-ullmann-5)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-8) rising to about 6 million tonnes by 2017.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-sweet-9)[Acetonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetonitrile "Acetonitrile") and [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide") are significant byproducts that are recovered for sale.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-ullmann-5) In fact, the [2008–2009 acetonitrile shortage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetonitrile#Acetonitrile_shortage_in_2008.E2.80.932009 "Acetonitrile") was caused by a decrease in demand for acrylonitrile.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-10)

2 CH 3−CH=CH 2 + 2 NH 3 + 3 O 2 → 2 CH 2=CH−C≡N + 6 H 2 O
In the SOHIO process, [propylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propylene "Propylene"), [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia"), and air (oxidizer) are passed through a [fluidized bed reactor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluidized_bed_reactor "Fluidized bed reactor") containing the catalyst at 400–510°C and 50–200 kPa g. The reactants pass through the reactor only once, before being quenched in aqueous sulfuric acid. Excess propylene, carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, and dinitrogen that do not dissolve are vented directly to the atmosphere, or are incinerated. The aqueous solution consists of acrylonitrile, acetonitrile, [hydrocyanic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrocyanic_acid "Hydrocyanic acid"), and [ammonium sulfate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonium_sulfate "Ammonium sulfate") (from excess ammonia). A recovery column removes bulk water, and acrylonitrile and acetonitrile are separated by distillation. One of the first useful catalysts was [bismuth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bismuth "Bismuth")[phosphomolybdate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phosphomolybdic_acid "Phosphomolybdic acid") (Bi 9 PMo 12 O 52) [supported](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalyst_support "Catalyst support") on silica.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-11) Further improvements have since been made.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-ullmann-5)

Various [green chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_chemistry "Green chemistry") routes to acrylonitrile are being explored from renewable feedstocks, such as [lignocellulosic biomass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lignocellulosic_biomass "Lignocellulosic biomass"), [glycerol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycerol "Glycerol") (from [biodiesel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiesel "Biodiesel") production), or [glutamic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glutamic_acid "Glutamic acid") (which can itself be produced from renewable feedstocks). The lignocellulosic route involves fermentation of the biomass to [propionic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionic_acid "Propionic acid") and [3-hydroxypropionic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3-hydroxypropionic_acid "3-hydroxypropionic acid"), which are then converted to acrylonitrile by dehydration and [ammoxidation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammoxidation "Ammoxidation").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-GrasselliTrifir%C3%B22016-12)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-sweet-9) The glycerol route begins with its [dehydration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dehydration "Dehydration") to [acrolein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrolein "Acrolein"), which undergoes ammoxidation to give acrylonitrile.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-Guerrero-P%C3%A9rezBa%C3%B1ares2015-13) The glutamic acid route employs [oxidative decarboxylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxidative_decarboxylation "Oxidative decarboxylation") to 3-cyanopropanoic acid, followed by a decarbonylation-elimination to acrylonitrile.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-Le_N%C3%B4treScott2011-14) Of these, the glycerol route is broadly considered to be the most viable, although none of these green methods are commercially competitive.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-GrasselliTrifir%C3%B22016-12)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-Guerrero-P%C3%A9rezBa%C3%B1ares2015-13)

Acrylonitrile is used principally as a [monomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monomer "Monomer") to prepare [polyacrylonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyacrylonitrile "Polyacrylonitrile"), a [homopolymer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homopolymer "Homopolymer"), or several important [copolymers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copolymer "Copolymer"), such as [styrene-acrylonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Styrene-acrylonitrile "Styrene-acrylonitrile") (SAN), [acrylonitrile butadiene styrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrylonitrile_butadiene_styrene "Acrylonitrile butadiene styrene") (ABS), [acrylonitrile styrene acrylate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrylonitrile_styrene_acrylate "Acrylonitrile styrene acrylate") (ASA), and other [synthetic rubbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synthetic_rubber "Synthetic rubber") such as [acrylonitrile butadiene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrile_rubber "Nitrile rubber") (NBR). [Hydrodimerization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrodimerization "Hydrodimerization") of acrylonitrile[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-15)[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-16) affords [adiponitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adiponitrile "Adiponitrile"), used in the synthesis of certain [nylons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nylon "Nylon"):

2 CH 2=CHCN + 2 e− + 2 H+ → NCCH 2−CH 2−CH 2−CH 2 CN
Acrylonitrile is also a precursor in the manufacture of [acrylamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrylamide "Acrylamide") and [acrylic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrylic_acid "Acrylic acid").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-ullmann-5)

### Synthesis of chemicals

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Acrylonitrile&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Synthesis of chemicals")]

Hydrogenation of acrylonitrile is one route to propionitrile. Hydrolysis with sulfuric acid gives acrylamide sulfate, CH=CHC(O)NH 2·H 2 SO 4. This salt can be converted to [acrylamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrylamide "Acrylamide") with treatment with base or to [methyl acrylate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_acrylate "Methyl acrylate") by treatment with [methanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanol "Methanol").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-ullmann-5)

The reaction of acrylonitrile with protic nucleophiles is a common route to a variety of specialty chemicals. The process is called [cyanoethylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanoethylation "Cyanoethylation"):

YH + H 2 C=CHCN → Y−CH 2−CH 2 CN
Typical protic nucleophiles are [alcohols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcohol_(chemistry) "Alcohol (chemistry)"), [thiols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiol "Thiol"), and especially [amines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amine "Amine").[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-UllmannAmine-17) When [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide") is used the product is [succinonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Succinonitrile "Succinonitrile").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-18)

Acrylonitrile and derivatives, such as 2-chloroacrylonitrile, are [dienophiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dienophile "Dienophile") in [Diels–Alder reactions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diels%E2%80%93Alder_reaction "Diels–Alder reaction").

Acrylonitrile is [toxic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toxic "Toxic") with [LD50](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LD50 "LD50") = 81 mg/kg (rats). It undergoes [explosive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Explosive "Explosive")[polymerization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymerization "Polymerization"). The burning material releases fumes of [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide") and [oxides of nitrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen_dioxide "Nitrogen dioxide"). It is classified as a [Class 1 carcinogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_IARC_Group_1_carcinogens "List of IARC Group 1 carcinogens") (carcinogenic) by the [International Agency for Research on Cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Agency_for_Research_on_Cancer "International Agency for Research on Cancer") (IARC),[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-:1-19) and workers exposed to high levels of airborne acrylonitrile are diagnosed more frequently with [lung cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lung_cancer "Lung cancer") than the rest of the population.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-20) Acrylonitrile is one of seven [toxicants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toxicant "Toxicant") in [cigarette](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cigarette "Cigarette") smoke that are most associated with [respiratory tract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Respiratory_tract "Respiratory tract")[carcinogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carcinogenesis "Carcinogenesis").[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-21) The mechanism of action of acrylonitrile appears to involve oxidative stress and oxidative DNA damage.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-22) Acrylonitrile increases cancer in high dose tests in male and female rats and mice[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-23) and induces [apoptosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apoptosis "Apoptosis") in human umbilical cord [mesenchymal stem cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesenchymal_stem_cells "Mesenchymal stem cells").[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-pmid_=_24248151-24)

It evaporates quickly at room temperature (20°C) to reach dangerous concentrations; [skin irritation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skin_irritation "Skin irritation"), respiratory irritation, and eye irritation are the immediate effects of this exposure.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-:0-25) Pathways of exposure for humans include [emissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exhaust_gas "Exhaust gas"), [auto exhaust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auto_exhaust "Auto exhaust"), and [cigarette smoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cigarette_smoke "Cigarette smoke") that can expose the human subject directly if they inhale or smoke. Routes of exposure include inhalation, oral, and to a certain extent dermal uptake (tested with volunteer humans and in rat studies).[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-EPAfacts-26) Repeated exposure causes skin sensitization and may cause central nervous system and [liver damage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liver_damage "Liver damage").[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-:0-25)

There are two main excretion processes of acrylonitrile. The primary method is excretion in urine when acrylonitrile is metabolized by being directly conjugated to [glutathione](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glutathione "Glutathione"). The other method is when acrylonitrile is enzymatically converted into 2-cyanoethylene oxide which will produce [cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanide "Cyanide") end products that ultimately form [thiocyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiocyanate "Thiocyanate"), which is excreted via urine.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-EPAfacts-26) Exposure can thus be detected via blood draws and urine sampling.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-:1-19)

In July 2024, the [International Agency for Research on Cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Agency_for_Research_on_Cancer "International Agency for Research on Cancer") upgraded acrylonitrile's classification from 'possibly carcinogenic' to [carcinogenic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carcinogen "Carcinogen") for humans. The Agency found [sufficient evidence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_evidence "Scientific evidence") linking it to [lung cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lung_cancer "Lung cancer").[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-27)[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-28)

### Use in plastic bottles

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Acrylonitrile&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Use in plastic bottles")]

In June 1974 [Coca-Cola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coca-Cola "Coca-Cola") introduced the acrylonitrile/styrene 32oz Easy‐Goer plastic bottle, offering energy savings during manufacture, increased durability, and weight savings over glass.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-29) In March 1977 after a suit filed by the [Natural Resources Defense Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_Resources_Defense_Council "Natural Resources Defense Council") the FDA rescinded approval of acrylonitrile bottles citing adverse effects on test animals.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-30)[Monsanto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsanto "Monsanto"), Coca-Cola's bottle manufacturer refuted the decision, stating "repeated tests have demonstrated that there is no detectable migration of acrylonitrile into the bottle's content." After several appeals in court by September 1977 the FDA finalized their ban.[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-31)[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-32)

A large amount of acrylonitrile (approximately 6500 tons) leaked from an industrial polymer plant owned by [Aksa Akrilik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aksa_(company) "Aksa (company)") after the violent [17th August 1999 earthquake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1999_%C4%B0zmit_earthquake "1999 İzmit earthquake") in Turkey. Over 5000 people were affected and the exposed animals had died.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-ttb-33) The leak was only noticed by the company 8 hours after the incident. Healthcare workers did not know about the health effects of acrylonitrile and tried to treat the victims with painkillers and IV fluids.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-tabella-34) One lawyer, Ayşe Akdemir, sued the company with 44 families as the plaintiffs.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-tabella-34) Aksa Akrilik was sued by 200 residents who were affected by acrylonitrile.[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-bigpara-35) An increase in cancer cases in the area was confirmed by the [Turkish Medical Association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_Medical_Association "Turkish Medical Association"),[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-bigpara-35) as the cancer rate in the affected area has increased by 80%, from 1999 to April 2002.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-tabella-34) In 2003, the owner of Aksa Akrilik died from lung cancer related to acrylonitrile exposure.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-tabella-34) As of 2001, this is the largest known acrylonitrile leak.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-ttb-33)

Acrylonitrile is not naturally formed on Earth. It has been detected at the sub-ppm level at industrial sites. It persists in the air for up to a week. It decomposes by reacting with oxygen and hydroxyl radical to form [formyl cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formyl_cyanide "Formyl cyanide") and [formaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formaldehyde "Formaldehyde").[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-36) Acrylonitrile is [harmful to aquatic life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquatic_toxicology "Aquatic toxicology").[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-:0-25) Acrylonitrile has been detected in the atmosphere of [Titan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titan_(moon) "Titan (moon)"), a moon of [Saturn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturn "Saturn").[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-SP-20170728-37)[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-SA-20170728-38)[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-WP-20170808-39) Computer simulations suggest that on Titan conditions exist such that the compound could form structures similar to [cell membranes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_membrane "Cell membrane") and [vesicles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vesicle_(biology_and_chemistry) "Vesicle (biology and chemistry)") on Earth, called [azotosomes.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypothetical_types_of_biochemistry#Azotosome "Hypothetical types of biochemistry")[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-SP-20170728-37)[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_cyanide#cite_note-SA-20170728-38)
