_**MathWorld**_ is an online [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics") reference work, created and largely written by [Eric W. Weisstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_W._Weisstein "Eric W. Weisstein").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MathWorld#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MathWorld#cite_note-:0-3) It is sponsored by and licensed to [Wolfram Research, Inc.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram_Research,_Inc. "Wolfram Research, Inc.") and was partially funded by the [National Science Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Science_Foundation "National Science Foundation")'s [National Science Digital Library](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Science_Digital_Library "National Science Digital Library") grant to the [University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Illinois_at_Urbana%E2%80%93Champaign "University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MathWorld#cite_note-:0-3)

History
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MathWorld&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

[Eric W. Weisstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_W._Weisstein "Eric W. Weisstein"), the creator of the site, was a [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics") and [astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy "Astronomy") student who got into the habit of writing notes on his mathematical readings. In 1995 he put his notes online and called it "Eric's Treasure Trove of Mathematics." It contained hundreds of pages/articles, covering a wide range of mathematical topics. The site became popular as an extensive single resource on mathematics on the web. In 1998, he made a contract with [CRC Press](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CRC_Press "CRC Press") and the contents of the site were published in print and [CD-ROM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CD-ROM "CD-ROM") form, titled _CRC Concise Encyclopedia of Mathematics_. The free online version became only partially accessible to the public. In 1999 Weisstein went to work for Wolfram Research, Inc. (WRI), and WRI renamed the Math Treasure Trove to _MathWorld_ and hosted it on the company's website[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_] without access restrictions.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

CRC lawsuit
-----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MathWorld&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: CRC lawsuit")]

In 2000, CRC Press sued [Wolfram Research Inc. (WRI)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram_Research "Wolfram Research"), WRI president [Stephen Wolfram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_Wolfram "Stephen Wolfram"), and author [Eric W. Weisstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_W._Weisstein "Eric W. Weisstein"), due to what they considered a breach of contract: that the _MathWorld_ content was to remain in print only. The site was taken down by a court [injunction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Injunction "Injunction").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MathWorld#cite_note-4)

The case was settled out of court in late 2001, with WRI paying an unspecified amount and complying with other [stipulations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stipulation "Stipulation"). Among these stipulations is the inclusion of a copyright notice at the bottom of the website and broad rights for the CRC Press to produce _MathWorld_ in printed book form. In November 2001 site then became once again available free to the public.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MathWorld#cite_note-History-5) In 2008, the litigants reached an agreement to allow new edition of MathWorld to be printed.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MathWorld#cite_note-History-5)

This case made a wave of headlines in online publishing circles. The _[PlanetMath](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlanetMath "PlanetMath")_ project was a result of MathWorld's being unavailable.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MathWorld#cite_note-6)
