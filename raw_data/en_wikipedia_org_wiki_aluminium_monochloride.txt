Chemical compound

**Aluminium monochloride**, or **chloridoaluminium** is the [metal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal "Metal")[halide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halide "Halide") with the formula AlCl. Aluminium monochloride as a molecule is thermodynamically stable at high temperature and low pressure only.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_monochloride#cite_note-2) This compound is produced as a step in the [Alcan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcan "Alcan") process to smelt [aluminium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium "Aluminium") from an aluminium-rich [alloy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alloy "Alloy"). When the alloy is placed in a reactor that is heated to 1,300°C and mixed with [aluminium trichloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_chloride "Aluminium chloride"), a gas of aluminium monochloride is produced.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_monochloride#cite_note-3)

2 Al(alloy) + AlCl 3(gas) → 3 AlCl(gas)
It then [disproportionates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disproportionation "Disproportionation") into aluminium melt and aluminium trichloride upon cooling to 900°C.

This molecule has been detected in the [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium"), where molecules are so dilute that intermolecular collisions are unimportant.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_monochloride#cite_note-4)
