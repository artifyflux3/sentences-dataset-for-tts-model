Legal status of persons Birthright Birthplace Aboard aircraft and ships Jus sanguinis Jus soli Birth tourism Nationality Citizenship missing multiple transnational Naturalization Ius Doni Oath Test Law Lost citizenship denaturalized renounced Immigration Alien Enemy Criminalization Diplomatic protection Expatriate Foreign worker Illegal Law Integration Permanent residency Refugee Right to homeland Voluntary return Identity cleansing Right of return v t e The right to homeland is according to some legal scholars a universal human right , which is derived from the Universal Declaration of Human Rights , including its Article 9.

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] The concept evolved in German jurisprudence and is recognized in German constitutional law to a certain degree. Notable proponents of the concept include legal scholars Kurl Rabl , Rudolf Laun , Otto Kimminich , Dieter Blumenwitz , Felix Ermacora and Alfred-Maurice de Zayas . The concept is relevant to debates concerning ethnic cleansing in Europe after World War II (notably of Germans and Hungarians), ethnic cleansing in Palestine , Cyprus and other areas.
