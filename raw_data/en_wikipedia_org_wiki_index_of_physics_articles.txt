[Physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics") (Greek: [physis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physis "Physis")–φύσις meaning "nature") is the [natural science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_science "Natural science") which examines basic concepts such as [mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass "Mass"), [charge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charge_(physics) "Charge (physics)"), [matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matter "Matter")[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_of_physics_articles#cite_note-feynman-1) and its [motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motion_(physics) "Motion (physics)") and all that derives from these, such as [energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy "Energy"), [force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Force "Force") and [spacetime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacetime "Spacetime").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_of_physics_articles#cite_note-2) More broadly, it is the general analysis of [nature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nature "Nature"), conducted in order to understand how the [world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World "World") and [universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universe "Universe") behave.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_of_physics_articles#cite_note-3)
