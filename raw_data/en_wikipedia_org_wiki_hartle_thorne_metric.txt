Taub Newman Yau Thorne others Physics portal Category v t e The Hartle–Thorne metric is an approximate solution of the vacuum Einstein field equations of general relativity [ 1 ] that describes the exterior of a slowly and rigidly rotating, stationary and axially symmetric body.

[ 2 ] The metric was found by James Hartle and Kip Thorne in the 1960s to study the spacetime outside neutron stars , white dwarfs and supermassive stars . It can be shown that it is an approximation to the Kerr metric (which describes a rotating black hole) when the quadrupole moment is set as q = − − a 2 a M 3 {\displaystyle q=-a^{2}aM^{3}} , which is the correct value for a black hole but not, in general, for other astrophysical objects.
