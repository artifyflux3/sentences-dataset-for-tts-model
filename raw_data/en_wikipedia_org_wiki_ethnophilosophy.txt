The implicit concept is that a specific culture can have a philosophy that is not applicable and accessible to all peoples and cultures in the world; however, this concept is disputed by traditional philosophers.

[ 1 ] It has been criticised as an aspect of ethnology , rather than philosophy which is undertaken by the individual.
