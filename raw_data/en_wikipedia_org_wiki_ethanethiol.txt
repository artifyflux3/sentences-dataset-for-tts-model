**Ethanethiol**, commonly known as **ethyl mercaptan**, is an [organosulfur compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organosulfur_compound "Organosulfur compound") with the formula CH 3 CH 2 SH.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-eros-5) It is a colorless liquid with a distinct odor. Abbreviated EtSH, it consists of an ethyl group (Et), CH 3 CH 2, attached to a [thiol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiol "Thiol") group, SH. Its structure parallels that of [ethanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanol "Ethanol"), but with [sulfur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfur "Sulfur") in place of [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen"). The odor of EtSH is infamous. Ethanethiol is more [volatile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volatility_(chemistry) "Volatility (chemistry)") than ethanol due to a diminished ability to engage in [hydrogen bonding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_bonding "Hydrogen bonding"). Ethanethiol is toxic in high concentrations. It occurs naturally as a minor component of [petroleum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petroleum "Petroleum"), and may be added to otherwise odorless gaseous products such as [liquefied petroleum gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquefied_petroleum_gas "Liquefied petroleum gas") (LPG) to help warn of gas leaks. At these concentrations, ethanethiol is not harmful.

Ethanethiol is prepared by the reaction of [ethylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethylene "Ethylene") with [hydrogen sulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_sulfide "Hydrogen sulfide") in the presence of various [catalysts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalysis "Catalysis"). It is also prepared commercially by the reaction of [ethanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanol "Ethanol") with [hydrogen sulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_sulfide "Hydrogen sulfide") gas over an acidic solid catalyst, such as [alumina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alumina "Alumina").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-John_Wiley_&_Sons,_Inc-6)

Ethanethiol was originally reported by [Zeise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Christopher_Zeise "William Christopher Zeise") in 1834.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-7) Zeise treated calcium ethyl sulfate with a suspension of [barium sulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barium_sulfide "Barium sulfide") saturated with hydrogen sulfide. He is credited with naming the C 2 H 5 S- group as mercaptum.

Ethanethiol can also be prepared by a [halide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halide "Halide") displacement reaction, where ethyl halide is treated with aqueous [sodium bisulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_bisulfide "Sodium bisulfide"). This conversion was demonstrated as early as 1840 by [Henri Victor Regnault](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henri_Victor_Regnault "Henri Victor Regnault").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-8)

Ethanethiol has a strongly disagreeable [odor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odor "Odor") that [humans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humans "Humans") can detect in minute concentrations. The threshold for human detection is as low as one part in 2.8 billion parts of air (0.36 parts per billion). Its odor resembles that of [leeks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leek "Leek"), [onions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Onion "Onion"), [durian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Durian "Durian") or cooked [cabbage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabbage "Cabbage").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-9)

Employees of the [Union Oil Company of California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unocal_Corporation "Unocal Corporation") reported first in 1938 that [turkey vultures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey_vultures "Turkey vultures") would gather at the site of any gas leak. After finding that this was caused by traces of ethanethiol in the gas it was decided to boost the amount of ethanethiol in the gas, to make detection of leaks easier.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-10)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-11)

Ethanethiol is intentionally added to [butane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Butane "Butane") and [propane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propane "Propane") (see: [LPG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquefied_petroleum_gas "Liquefied petroleum gas")) to impart an easily noticed smell to these normally odorless fuels that pose the threat of [fire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fire "Fire"), [explosion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Explosion "Explosion"), and [asphyxiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asphyxiation "Asphyxiation").

In the underground mining industry, ethanethiol or ethyl mercaptan is referred to as "stench gas".[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-12) The gas is released into mine ventilation systems during an emergency to alert mine workers. In [Ontario](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ontario "Ontario"), mining legislation dictates that "The alarm system in an underground mine shall, consist of the introduction into all workplaces of sufficient quantities of ethyl mercaptan gas or similar gas to be readily detectable by all workers".[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-13)

Ethanethiol is a [reagent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reagent "Reagent") in [organic synthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_synthesis "Organic synthesis").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-eros-5) In the presence of [sodium hydroxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_hydroxide "Sodium hydroxide"), it gives the powerful [nucleophile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleophile "Nucleophile") EtS−. The salt [sodium ethanethiolate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_ethanethiolate "Sodium ethanethiolate") can be generated quantitatively by reaction of ethanethiol with a suspension of [sodium hydride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_hydride "Sodium hydride") in [dimethylformamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethylformamide "Dimethylformamide").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanethiol#cite_note-14)

Ethanethiol can be oxidized to ethyl [sulfonic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfonic_acid "Sulfonic acid"), using strong [oxidizing agents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxidizing_agents "Oxidizing agents"). Weaker oxidants, such as [ferric oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferric_oxide "Ferric oxide") or [hydrogen peroxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_peroxide "Hydrogen peroxide") give the [disulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disulfide "Disulfide"), diethyl disulfide:

2 EtSH + H 2 O 2 → EtS-SEt + 2 H 2 O
Like other thiols, it behaves comparably to hydrogen sulfide. For example, it binds, concomitant with deprotonation to "[soft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HSAB_theory "HSAB theory")" [transition metal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transition_metal "Transition metal")[cations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cation "Cation"), such as Hg 2+, Cu+, and Ni 2+ to give polymeric thiolato complexes, Hg(SEt)2, CuSEt, and Ni(SEt)2, respectively.
