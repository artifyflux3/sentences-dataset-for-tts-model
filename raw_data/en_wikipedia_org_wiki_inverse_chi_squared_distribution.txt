}{2}}\!\left({\sqrt {-2it}}\right)} In probability and statistics, the inverse-chi-squared distribution (or inverted-chi-square distribution [ 1 ] )  is a continuous probability distribution of a positive-valued random variable. It is closely related to the chi-squared distribution .  It is used in Bayesian inference as conjugate prior for the variance of the normal distribution .

[ 2 ] Definition [ edit ] The inverse chi-squared distribution (or inverted-chi-square distribution [ 1 ] )  is the probability distribution of a random variable whose multiplicative inverse (reciprocal) has a chi-squared distribution .

If X {\displaystyle X} follows a chi-squared distribution with ν ν {\displaystyle \nu } degrees of freedom then 1 / X {\displaystyle 1/X} follows the inverse chi-squared distribution with ν ν {\displaystyle \nu } degrees of freedom.
