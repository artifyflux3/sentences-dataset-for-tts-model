Early life [ edit ] He was born in Carnoustie , Scotland .

[ 1 ] Esson attended St John's College , Oxford .

Career [ edit ] He then became a Fellow of Merton College .

[ 2 ] In 1892, he became the Savilian Professor of Geometry at the University of Oxford , based at New College . He worked on problems in chemistry with Augustus George Vernon Harcourt .

In 1869 he was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society and in 1895 delivered, jointly with Harcourt, their Bakerian Lecture on the Laws of Connexion between the Conditions of a Chemical Change and its Amount. III. Further Researches on the Reaction of Hydrogen Dioxide and Hydrogen Iodide .

[ 3 ] He was on the governing body of Abingdon School until 1900.

[ 4 ] Personal life [ edit ] In 1874, Esson leased 13 Bradmore Road in North Oxford .

[ 5 ] He died in Abingdon , England .
