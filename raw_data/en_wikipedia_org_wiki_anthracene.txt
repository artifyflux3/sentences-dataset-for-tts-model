**Anthracene** is a solid [polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon") (PAH) of formula C 14 H 10, consisting of three fused [benzene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzene "Benzene") rings. It is a component of [coal tar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coal_tar "Coal tar"). Anthracene is used in the [production](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_production "Economic production") of the red [dye](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dye "Dye")[alizarin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alizarin "Alizarin") and other dyes, as a [scintillator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scintillator "Scintillator") to detect high energy particles, as production of pharmaceutical drugs. Anthracene is colorless but exhibits a blue (400–500 nm peak) [fluorescence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluorescence "Fluorescence") under [ultraviolet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet "Ultraviolet") radiation.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-13)

History and etymology
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Anthracene&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History and etymology")]

Crude anthracene (with a melting point of only 180°) was discovered in 1832 by [Jean-Baptiste Dumas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Dumas "Jean-Baptiste Dumas") and [Auguste Laurent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auguste_Laurent "Auguste Laurent")[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-:0-14) who crystalized it from a fraction of coal tar later known as "anthracene oil". Since their (inaccurate) measurements showed the proportions of carbon and hydrogen of it to be the same as in [naphthalene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naphthalene "Naphthalene"), Laurent called it _paranaphtaline_ in his 1835 publication of the discovery,[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-15) which is translated to English as **paranaphthalene**.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-:0-14) Two years later, however, he decided to rename the compound to its modern name derived from [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language "Ancient Greek language"): ἄνθραξ, [romanized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization_of_Ancient_Greek "Romanization of Ancient Greek"):_anthrax_, [lit.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Literal_translation "Literal translation")'coal' because after discovering other polyaromatic hydrocarbons he decided it was only one of isomers of naphthalene.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-16) This notion was disproved in 1850s and 1860s.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-17)[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-18)

Occurrence and production
-------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Anthracene&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Occurrence and production")]

Anthracene, as many other [polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbons "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons"), is generated during combustion processes. Most human exposure is through [tobacco smoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tobacco_smoke "Tobacco smoke") or ingestion of [charred](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charring "Charring") food.

The mineral form of anthracene is called freitalite and is related to a coal deposit.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-19) Coal tar, which contains around 1.5% anthracene, remains a major industrial source of this material. Common impurities are [phenanthrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenanthrene "Phenanthrene") and [carbazole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbazole "Carbazole").

A classic laboratory method for the preparation of anthracene is by cyclodehydration of o-methyl- or o-methylene-substituted diarylketones in the so-called [Elbs reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elbs_reaction "Elbs reaction"), for example from _o_-tolyl phenyl ketone.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-20)

Reduction of anthracene with alkali metals yields the deeply colored radical anion salts M+[anthracene]− (M = Li, Na, K). Hydrogenation gives 9,10-[dihydroanthracene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dihydroanthracene "Dihydroanthracene"), preserving the aromaticity of the two flanking rings.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-21)

In any solvent except water,[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-22) anthracene [photodimerizes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photochemistry "Photochemistry") by the action of [UV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet "Ultraviolet") light:

[![Image 19: Anthracene dimer](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Anthracene_Photodimerisation.svg/360px-Anthracene_Photodimerisation.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Anthracene_Photodimerisation.svg "Anthracene dimer")
The [dimer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimer_(chemistry) "Dimer (chemistry)"), called dianthracene (or sometimes paranthracene), is connected by a pair of new carbon-carbon bonds, the result of the [4+4] [cycloaddition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cycloaddition "Cycloaddition"). It reverts to anthracene thermally or with [UV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet "Ultraviolet") irradiation below 300 nm. Substituted anthracene derivatives behave similarly. The reaction is affected by the presence of [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen").[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-23)[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-24)

Anthracene also reacts with dienophile [singlet oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singlet_oxygen "Singlet oxygen") in a [4+2]-cycloaddition ([Diels–Alder reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diels%E2%80%93Alder_reaction "Diels–Alder reaction")):

[![Image 20: Diels alder reaction of anthracene with singlet oxygen](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Anthracene_singlet_oxygen_Diels-Alder_reaction.svg/500px-Anthracene_singlet_oxygen_Diels-Alder_reaction.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Anthracene_singlet_oxygen_Diels-Alder_reaction.svg "Diels alder reaction of anthracene with singlet oxygen")

Diels alder reaction of anthracene with singlet oxygen

Chemical [oxidation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxidation "Oxidation") occurs readily, giving [anthraquinone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthraquinone "Anthraquinone"), C 14 H 8 O 2 (below), for example using [hydrogen peroxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_peroxide "Hydrogen peroxide") and [vanadyl acetylacetonate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanadyl_acetylacetonate "Vanadyl acetylacetonate").[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-25)

[![Image 21: Anthraquione](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Anthraquinone_acsv.svg/120px-Anthraquinone_acsv.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Anthraquinone_acsv.svg "Anthraquione")

Anthraquione

Electrophilic substitution of anthracene occurs at the 9 position. For example, formylation affords [9-anthracenecarboxaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/9-Anthracenecarboxaldehyde "9-Anthracenecarboxaldehyde"). Substitution at other positions is effected indirectly, for example starting with anthroquinone.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-26) Bromination of anthracene gives 9,10-dibromoanthracene.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-27)

Anthracene proper has application as an [organic semiconductor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_semiconductor "Organic semiconductor") and [chemical feedstock](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Chemical_feedstock&action=edit&redlink=1 "Chemical feedstock (page does not exist)") for various preservatives and dyes.

[![Image 22](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Fluorescence_of_Anthracene_under_UV_light.jpg/250px-Fluorescence_of_Anthracene_under_UV_light.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Fluorescence_of_Anthracene_under_UV_light.jpg)

Fluorescence of anthracene under UV light

Anthracene is a wide band-gap [organic semiconductor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_semiconductor "Organic semiconductor"), with an [emission spectrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emission_spectrum "Emission spectrum") peaking between [400 nm and 440 nm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dark_violet "Dark violet"). Organic [field-effect transistors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Field-effect_transistor "Field-effect transistor") have been constructed from it. In [particle physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particle_physics "Particle physics"), it is used as a [scintillator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scintillator "Scintillator") to detect high-energy [photons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photon "Photon"), [electrons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron "Electron"), or [alpha particles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpha_particle "Alpha particle").[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-28) Plastics, such as [polyvinyltoluene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyvinyltoluene "Polyvinyltoluene"), can be doped with anthracene to produce an approximately water-equivalent scintillator in [radiation therapy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiation_therapy "Radiation therapy")[dosimetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dosimetry "Dosimetry").

Anthracene is commonly used as a UV tracer in conformal coatings applied to printed wiring boards. The anthracene tracer allows the conformal coating to be inspected under UV light.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-29)

It is also used in [wood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wood "Wood")[preservatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Preservative "Preservative"), [insecticides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide"), and coating [materials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commodity "Commodity").[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

[![Image 23](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Antracene_diradical_AFM2.png/250px-Antracene_diradical_AFM2.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Antracene_diradical_AFM2.png)

False-color [AFM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic_force_microscopy "Atomic force microscopy") image of anthracene diradical, where hydrogen atoms are removed at carbons 9 and 10

A variety of anthracene derivatives find specialized uses. Industrially, anthracene is converted mainly to [anthraquinone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthraquinone "Anthraquinone"), a precursor to dyes.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-Ullmann-30) Derivatives having a [hydroxyl group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroxide "Hydroxide") are 1-hydroxyanthracene and 2-hydroxyanthracene, homologous to [phenol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenol "Phenol") and [naphthols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naphthol_(disambiguation) "Naphthol (disambiguation)"), and hydroxyanthracene (also called anthrol, and anthracenol)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-31)[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-32) are [pharmacologically](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharmacology "Pharmacology") active. Anthracene may also be found with multiple hydroxyl groups, as in [9,10-dihydroxyanthracene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/9,10-dihydroxyanthracene "9,10-dihydroxyanthracene").

Some anthracene derivatives are used as pharmaceutical drugs, including [bisantrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bisantrene "Bisantrene"), [trazitiline](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Trazitiline&action=edit&redlink=1 "Trazitiline (page does not exist)"), and [benzoctamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzoctamine "Benzoctamine").

Many investigations indicate that anthracene is noncarcinogenic: "consistently negative findings in numerous in vitro and in vivo genotoxicity tests". Early experiments suggested otherwise because crude samples were contaminated with other polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons. Furthermore, it is readily biodegraded in soil. It is especially susceptible to degradation in the presence of light.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-Ullmann-30) The [International Agency for Research on Cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Agency_for_Research_on_Cancer "International Agency for Research on Cancer") (IARC) classifies anthracene as [IARC group 2B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IARC_group_2B "IARC group 2B"), possibly carcinogenic to humans.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene#cite_note-33)
