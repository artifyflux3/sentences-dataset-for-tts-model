A **polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon** (**PAH**) is any member of a class of [organic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") that is composed of multiple fused [aromatic rings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aromatic_ring "Aromatic ring"). Most are produced by the incomplete combustion of [organic matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_matter "Organic matter")— by engine exhaust fumes, tobacco, incinerators, in roasted meats and cereals,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-1) or when biomass burns at lower temperatures as in [forest fires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest_fires "Forest fires").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-3) The simplest representative is [naphthalene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naphthalene "Naphthalene"), having two aromatic rings, and the three-ring compounds [anthracene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene "Anthracene") and [phenanthrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenanthrene "Phenanthrene"). PAHs are uncharged, non-polar and planar. Many are colorless. Many of them are also found in fossil fuel deposits such as [coal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coal "Coal") and in [petroleum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petroleum "Petroleum"). Exposure to PAHs can lead to different types of [cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cancer "Cancer"), to [fetal development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fetal_development "Fetal development") complications, and to [cardiovascular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardiovascular "Cardiovascular") issues.[_[medical citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying\_reliable\_sources\_(medicine) "Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources (medicine)")_]

Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons are discussed as possible [starting materials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis "PAH world hypothesis") for [abiotic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abiotic "Abiotic") syntheses of [materials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Material "Material") required by the [earliest forms of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abiogenesis#PAH_world_hypothesis "Abiogenesis").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-NASA-20140221-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-NASA-20110413-5)

Nomenclature and structure
--------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Nomenclature and structure")]

The terms **polyaromatic hydrocarbon**,[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-6) or **polynuclear aromatic hydrocarbon**[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-7) (abbreviated as PNA) are also used for this concept.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ATSDR2011a-8)

By definition, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons have multiple aromatic rings, precluding [benzene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzene "Benzene") from being considered a PAH. Sources such as the [US EPA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency "United States Environmental Protection Agency") and [CDC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centers_for_Disease_Control_and_Prevention "Centers for Disease Control and Prevention") consider [naphthalene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naphthalene "Naphthalene") to be the simplest PAH.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-9) Most authors exclude compounds that include [heteroatoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heteroatom "Heteroatom") in the rings, or carry [substituents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Substituent "Substituent").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-10)

A polyaromatic hydrocarbon may have rings of various sizes, including some that are not aromatic. Those that have only six-membered rings are said to be **alternant**.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-11)

Most PAHs, like naphthalene, anthracene, and coronene, are planar. This geometry is a consequence of the fact that the [σ-bonds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%CE%A3-bond "Σ-bond") that result from the merger of sp 2[hybrid orbitals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbital_hybridisation "Orbital hybridisation") of adjacent carbons lie on the same plane as the carbon atom. Those compounds are [achiral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chirality "Chirality"), since the plane of the molecule is a symmetry plane.

In rare cases, PAHs are not planar. In some cases, the non-planarity may be forced by the [topology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topology "Topology") of the molecule and the stiffness (in length and angle) of the carbon-carbon bonds. For example, unlike [coronene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coronene "Coronene"), [corannulene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corannulene "Corannulene") adopts a bowl shape in order to reduce the bond stress. The two possible configurations, concave and convex, are separated by a relatively low energy barrier (about 11 [kcal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kilocalorie "Kilocalorie")/[mol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mole_(chemistry) "Mole (chemistry)")).[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-12)

In theory, there are 51 structural isomers of coronene that have six fused benzene rings in a cyclic sequence, with two edge carbons shared between successive rings. All of them must be non-planar and have considerable higher bonding energy (computed to be at least 130 kcal/mol) than coronene; as of 2002, none of them had been synthesized.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-13)

Other PAHs that might seem to be planar, considering only the carbon skeleton, may be distorted by repulsion or steric hindrance between the [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen") atoms in their periphery. Benzo[c]phenanthrene, with four rings fused in a "C" shape, has a slight helical distortion due to repulsion between the closest pair of hydrogen atoms in the two extremal rings.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-14) This effect also causes distortion of picene.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-echi2007-15)

Adding another benzene ring to form dibenzo[c,g]phenanthrene creates [steric hindrance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steric_hindrance "Steric hindrance") between the two extreme hydrogen atoms.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-16) Adding two more rings on the same sense yields [heptahelicene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helicene "Helicene") in which the two extreme rings overlap.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-17) These non-planar forms are chiral, and their [enantiomers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enantiomer "Enantiomer") can be isolated.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-gutm2012-18)

### Benzenoid hydrocarbons

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Benzenoid hydrocarbons")]

The **benzenoid hydrocarbons** have been defined as condensed polycyclic unsaturated fully-conjugated hydrocarbons whose molecules are essentially planar with all rings six-membered. Full conjugation means that all carbon atoms and carbon-carbon bonds must have the sp 2 structure of benzene. This class is largely a subset of the alternant PAHs, but is considered to include unstable or hypothetical compounds like [triangulene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triangulene "Triangulene") or [heptacene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heptacene "Heptacene").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-gutm2012-18)

As of 2012, over 300 benzenoid hydrocarbons had been isolated and characterized.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-gutm2012-18)

Bonding and aromaticity
-----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Bonding and aromaticity")]

The [aromaticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aromaticity "Aromaticity") varies for PAHs. According to [Clar's rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clar%27s_rule "Clar's rule"),[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-19) the [resonance structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resonance_structure "Resonance structure") of a PAH that has the largest number of disjoint aromatic [pi sextets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pi_bond "Pi bond")—i.e. [benzene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzene "Benzene")-like moieties—is the most important for the characterization of the properties of that PAH.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-20)

Chrysene

For example, [phenanthrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenanthrene "Phenanthrene") has two Clar structures: one with just one aromatic sextet (the middle ring), and the other with two (the first and third rings). The latter case is therefore the more characteristic electronic nature of the two. Therefore, in this molecule the outer rings have greater aromatic character whereas the central ring is less aromatic and therefore more reactive.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-21) In contrast, in [anthracene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene "Anthracene") the resonance structures have one sextet each, which can be at any of the three rings, and the aromaticity spreads out more evenly across the whole molecule.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-22) This difference in number of sextets is reflected in the differing [ultraviolet–visible spectra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet%E2%80%93visible_spectroscopy "Ultraviolet–visible spectroscopy") of these two isomers, as higher Clar pi-sextets are associated with larger [HOMO-LUMO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HOMO-LUMO "HOMO-LUMO") gaps;[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-23) the highest-wavelength absorbance of phenanthrene is at 293 nm, while anthracene is at 374 nm.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-24) Three Clar structures with two sextets each are present in the four-ring [chrysene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chrysene "Chrysene") structure: one having sextets in the first and third rings, one in the second and fourth rings, and one in the first and fourth rings.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-25) Superposition of these structures reveals that the aromaticity in the outer rings is greater (each has a sextet in two of the three Clar structures) compared to the inner rings (each has a sextet in only one of the three).

PAHs are [nonpolar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_polarity#Nonpolar_molecules "Chemical polarity") and [lipophilic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipophilicity "Lipophilicity"). Larger PAHs are generally [insoluble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solubility "Solubility") in water, although some smaller PAHs are soluble.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-26)[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-27) The larger members are also poorly soluble in [organic solvents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_solvent "Organic solvent") and in [lipids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipid "Lipid"). The larger members, e.g. perylene, are strongly colored.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-gutm2012-18)

Polycyclic aromatic compounds characteristically yield [radicals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_radical "Free radical") and [anions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anion "Anion") upon treatment with alkali metals. The large PAH form dianions as well.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-28) The [redox potential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redox_potential "Redox potential") correlates with the size of the PAH.

The dominant sources of PAHs in the environment are from human activity: wood-burning and combustion of other [biofuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biofuels "Biofuels") such as dung or crop residues contribute more than half of annual global PAH emissions, particularly due to biofuel use in India and China.[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramesh2011-31)[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-abdel2016-32) As of 2004, industrial processes and the extraction and use of [fossil fuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fossil_fuels "Fossil fuels") made up slightly more than one quarter of global PAH emissions, dominating outputs in industrial countries such as the United States.[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramesh2011-31)

A year-long sampling campaign in Athens, Greece found a third (31%) of PAH urban [air pollution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_pollution "Air pollution") to be caused by wood-burning, like diesel and oil (33%) and gasoline (29%). It also found that wood-burning is responsible for nearly half (43%) of annual PAH cancer-risk ([carcinogenic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carcinogen "Carcinogen") potential) compared to the other sources and that wintertime PAH levels were 7 times higher than in other seasons, especially if atmospheric dispersion is low.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-33)[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-34)

Lower-temperature combustion, such as [tobacco smoking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tobacco_smoking "Tobacco smoking") or [wood-burning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wood-burning "Wood-burning"), tends to generate low molecular weight PAHs, whereas high-temperature industrial processes typically generate PAHs with higher molecular weights.[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Tobis-35) Incense is also a source.[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-36)

PAHs are typically found as complex mixtures.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37)[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Tobis-35)

PAHs may result from the incomplete [combustion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combustion "Combustion") of [organic matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_matter "Organic matter") in natural [wildfires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildfire "Wildfire").[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-abdel2016-32)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramesh2011-31) Substantially higher outdoor air, soil, and water concentrations of PAHs have been measured in Asia, Africa, and Latin America than in Europe, Australia, the U.S., and Canada.[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramesh2011-31)[_[relevant?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Writing\_better\_articles#Stay\_on\_topic "Wikipedia:Writing better articles")_]

Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons are primarily found in natural sources such as [bitumen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bitumen "Bitumen").[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-38)[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-39)

PAHs can also be produced geologically when organic sediments are chemically transformed into [fossil fuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fossil_fuel "Fossil fuel") such as oil and [coal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coal "Coal").[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37) The rare minerals [idrialite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idrialite "Idrialite"), [curtisite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curtisite "Curtisite"), and [carpathite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carpathite "Carpathite") consist almost entirely of PAHs that originated from such sediments, that were extracted, processed, separated, and deposited by very hot fluids.[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-40)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-echi2007-15)[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-41) High levels of such PAHs have been detected in the [Cretaceous-Tertiary (K-T) boundary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cretaceous-Tertiary_boundary "Cretaceous-Tertiary boundary"), more than 100 times the level in adjacent layers. The spike was attributed to massive fires that consumed about 20% of the terrestrial above-ground biomass in a very short time.[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-42)

PAHs are prevalent in the [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") (ISM) of galaxies in both the nearby and distant Universe and make up a dominant emission mechanism in the mid-infrared wavelength range, containing as much as 10% of the total integrated infrared luminosity of galaxies.[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-youtube.com-43) PAHs generally trace regions of cold molecular gas, which are optimum environments for the formation of stars.[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-youtube.com-43)

NASA's [Spitzer Space Telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spitzer_Space_Telescope "Spitzer Space Telescope") and [James Webb Space Telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Webb_Space_Telescope "James Webb Space Telescope") include instruments for obtaining both images and spectra of light emitted by PAHs associated with [star formation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_formation "Star formation"). These images can trace the surface of star-forming [clouds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud") in our own galaxy or identify star forming galaxies in the distant universe.[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-44) In June 2013, PAHs were detected in the [upper atmosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_atmosphere "Upper atmosphere") of [Titan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titan_(moon) "Titan (moon)"), the largest [moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon "Moon") of the [planet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planet "Planet")[Saturn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturn "Saturn").[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-45)

[Volcanic eruptions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcanic_eruption "Volcanic eruption") may emit PAHs.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37)

Certain PAHs such as [perylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perylene "Perylene") can also be generated in [anaerobic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypoxia_(environmental) "Hypoxia (environmental)") sediments from existing organic material, although it remains undetermined whether abiotic or microbial processes drive their production.[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-46)[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-47)[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-48)

Distribution in the environment
-------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=15 "Edit section: Distribution in the environment")]

### Aquatic environments

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=16 "Edit section: Aquatic environments")]

Most PAHs are insoluble in water, which limits their mobility in the environment, although PAHs [sorb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sorption "Sorption") to fine-grained organic-rich [sediments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sediment "Sediment").[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-49)[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-50)[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-51)[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Choi-2007-52) Aqueous solubility of PAHs decreases approximately [logarithmically](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logarithmic_growth "Logarithmic growth") as [molecular mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_mass "Molecular mass") increases.[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Johnsen-2005-53)

Two-ringed PAHs, and to a lesser extent three-ringed PAHs, dissolve in water, making them more available for biological uptake and [degradation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodegradation "Biodegradation").[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Choi-2007-52)[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Johnsen-2005-53)[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-54) Further, two- to four-ringed PAHs [volatilize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volatility_(chemistry) "Volatility (chemistry)") sufficiently to appear in the atmosphere predominantly in gaseous form, although the physical state of four-ring PAHs can depend on temperature.[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-55)[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-56) In contrast, compounds with five or more rings have low solubility in water and low volatility; they are therefore predominantly in solid [state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_matter "State of matter"), bound to [particulate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particulates "Particulates")[air pollution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_pollution "Air pollution"), [soils](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soils "Soils"), or [sediments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sediment "Sediment").[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Choi-2007-52) In solid state, these compounds are less accessible for biological uptake or degradation, increasing their persistence in the environment.[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Johnsen-2005-53)[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Haritash-2009-57)

Human exposure varies across the globe and depends on factors such as smoking rates, fuel types in cooking, and pollution controls on power plants, industrial processes, and vehicles.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramesh2011-31)[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Choi2010-58) Developed countries with stricter air and water pollution controls, cleaner sources of cooking (i.e., gas and electricity vs. coal or biofuels), and prohibitions of public smoking tend to have lower levels of PAH exposure, while developing and undeveloped countries tend to have higher levels.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramesh2011-31)[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Choi2010-58) Surgical smoke plumes have been proven to contain PAHs in several independent research studies.[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-59)

[![Image 31](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Kochen_%C3%BCber_offenem_Feuer.JPG/250px-Kochen_%C3%BCber_offenem_Feuer.JPG)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Kochen_%C3%BCber_offenem_Feuer.JPG)

A wood-burning open-air cooking [stove](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stove "Stove"). [Smoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smoke "Smoke") from solid fuels like [wood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wood "Wood") is a large source of PAHs globally.

Burning solid fuels such as [coal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coal "Coal") and [biofuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biofuel "Biofuel") in the home for cooking and heating is a dominant global source of PAH emissions that in developing countries leads to high levels of exposure to [indoor particulate air pollution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indoor_air_pollution_in_developing_nations "Indoor air pollution in developing nations") containing PAHs, particularly for women and children who spend more time in the home or cooking.[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramesh2011-31)[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-60)

[![Image 32](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Cairo_in_smog.jpg/250px-Cairo_in_smog.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Cairo_in_smog.jpg)

[Smog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smog "Smog") in [Cairo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cairo "Cairo"). Particulate air pollution, including smog, is a substantial cause of human exposure to PAHs.

Emissions from vehicles such as cars and trucks can be a substantial outdoor source of PAHs in particulate air pollution.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramesh2011-31) Geographically, major roadways are thus sources of PAHs, which may distribute in the atmosphere or deposit nearby.[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Srogi2007-61)[Catalytic converters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalytic_converter "Catalytic converter") are estimated to reduce PAH emissions from gasoline-fired vehicles by 25-fold.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37)

People can also be occupationally exposed during work that involves fossil fuels or their derivatives, wood-burning, [carbon electrodes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_electrode "Carbon electrode"), or exposure to [diesel exhaust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diesel_exhaust "Diesel exhaust").[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Boffetta1997-62)[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Wagner2015-63) Industrial activity that can produce and distribute PAHs includes [aluminum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminum "Aluminum"), [iron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron "Iron"), and [steel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steel "Steel") manufacturing; [coal gasification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coal_gasification "Coal gasification"), [tar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tar "Tar") distillation, [shale oil extraction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shale_oil_extraction "Shale oil extraction"); production of [coke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coke_(fuel) "Coke (fuel)"), [creosote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creosote "Creosote"), [carbon black](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_black "Carbon black"), and [calcium carbide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calcium_carbide "Calcium carbide"); road paving and [asphalt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asphalt_concrete "Asphalt concrete") manufacturing; [rubber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rubber "Rubber")[tire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tire "Tire") production; manufacturing or use of [metal working](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metalworking "Metalworking") fluids; and activity of coal or [natural gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_gas "Natural gas")[power stations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_station "Power station").[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37)[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Boffetta1997-62)[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Wagner2015-63)

In industrial countries, people who smoke tobacco products, or who are exposed to [second-hand smoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Passive_smoking "Passive smoking"), are among the most highly exposed groups; [tobacco smoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tobacco_smoke "Tobacco smoke") contributes to 90% of indoor PAH levels in the homes of smokers.[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Choi2010-58) For the general population in developed countries, the diet is otherwise the dominant source of PAH exposure, particularly from smoking or grilling meat or consuming PAHs deposited on plant foods, especially broad-leafed vegetables, during growth.[[64]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-64) Exposure also occurs through drinking alcohol aged in charred barrels, flavored with peat smoke, or made with roasted grains.[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-65) PAHs are typically at low concentrations in drinking water.[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Choi2010-58)

### Environmental pollution and degradation

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=18 "Edit section: Environmental pollution and degradation")]

[![Image 33: A worker's glove touches a dense patch of black oil on a sandy beach.](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Manripo071210_3.jpg/250px-Manripo071210_3.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Manripo071210_3.jpg)

[Crude oil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crude_oil "Crude oil") on a beach after a 2007 [oil spill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oil_spill "Oil spill") in Korea.

PAHs typically disperse from [urban](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urban_area "Urban area") and [suburban](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suburban "Suburban")[non-point sources](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonpoint_source_pollution "Nonpoint source pollution") through road [runoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surface_runoff "Surface runoff"), [sewage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sewage "Sewage"), and [atmospheric circulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation "Atmospheric circulation") and subsequent deposition of particulate air pollution.[[66]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Davis-66)[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Hylland2006-67)[Soil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil "Soil") and river [sediment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sediment "Sediment") near industrial sites such as creosote manufacturing facilities can be highly contaminated with PAHs.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37)[Oil spills](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oil_spill "Oil spill"), creosote, [coal mining](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coal_mining "Coal mining") dust, and other fossil fuel sources can also distribute PAHs in the environment.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37)[[68]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-68)

Two- and three-ringed PAHs can disperse widely while dissolved in water or as gases in the atmosphere, while PAHs with higher molecular weights can disperse locally or regionally adhered to particulate matter that is suspended in air or water until the particles land or settle out of the [water column](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_column "Water column").[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ravi2008-37) PAHs have a strong affinity for [organic carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_carbon "Organic carbon"), and thus highly organic sediments in [rivers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/River "River"), [lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake "Lake"), and the [ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean "Ocean") can be a substantial sink for PAHs.[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Srogi2007-61)

[Algae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algae "Algae") and some [invertebrates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invertebrate "Invertebrate") such as [protozoans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protozoans "Protozoans"), [mollusks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mollusks "Mollusks"), and many [polychaetes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polychaete "Polychaete") have limited ability to [metabolize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metabolism "Metabolism") PAHs and [bioaccumulate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioaccumulate "Bioaccumulate") disproportionate concentrations of PAHs in their tissues; however, PAH metabolism can vary substantially across invertebrate species.[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Hylland2006-67)[[69]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-69) Most [vertebrates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertebrate "Vertebrate") metabolize and excrete PAHs relatively rapidly.[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Hylland2006-67) Tissue concentrations of PAHs do not increase ([biomagnify](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomagnify "Biomagnify")) from the lowest to highest levels of food chains.[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Hylland2006-67)

PAHs transform slowly to a wide range of degradation products. Biological degradation by [microbes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microorganism "Microorganism") is a dominant form of PAH transformation in the environment.[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Haritash-2009-57)[[70]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Johnsen2005-70)[Soil-consuming invertebrates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Detritivore "Detritivore") such as [earthworms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earthworm "Earthworm") speed PAH degradation, either through direct metabolism or by improving the conditions for microbial transformations.[[70]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Johnsen2005-70) Abiotic degradation in the atmosphere and the top layers of surface waters can produce nitrogenated, halogenated, hydroxylated, and oxygenated PAHs; some of these compounds can be more toxic, water-soluble, and mobile than their parent PAHs.[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Hylland2006-67)[[71]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-71)[[72]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-72)

The [British Geological Survey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Geological_Survey "British Geological Survey") reported the amount and distribution of PAH compounds including parent and alkylated forms in urban soils at 76 locations in [Greater London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greater_London "Greater London").[[73]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneKim2014-73) The study showed that parent (16 PAH) content ranged from 4 to 67 mg/kg (dry soil weight) and an average PAH concentration of 18 mg/kg (dry soil weight) whereas the total PAH content (33 PAH) ranged from 6 to 88 mg/kg and [fluoranthene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluoranthene "Fluoranthene") and [pyrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrene "Pyrene") were generally the most abundant PAHs.[[73]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneKim2014-73)[Benzo[_a_]pyrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzo(a)pyrene "Benzo(a)pyrene") (B _a_ P), the most toxic of the parent PAHs, is widely considered a key marker PAH for environmental assessments;[[74]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-74) the normal background concentration of B _a_ P in the London urban sites was 6.9 mg/kg (dry soil weight).[[73]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneKim2014-73)[London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London "London") soils contained more stable four- to six-ringed PAHs which were indicative of combustion and pyrolytic sources, such as coal and oil burning and traffic-sourced particulates. However, the overall distribution also suggested that the PAHs in London soils had undergone weathering and been modified by a variety of pre-and post-depositional processes such as volatilization and microbial [biodegradation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodegradation "Biodegradation").

Managed [burning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burning "Burning") of [moorland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moorland "Moorland") vegetation in the UK has been shown to generate PAHs which become incorporated into the [peat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peat "Peat") surface.[[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneRawlins2013-75) Burning of moorland vegetation such as [heather](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calluna "Calluna") initially generates high amounts of two- and three-ringed PAHs relative to four- to six-ringed PAHs in surface sediments, however, this pattern is reversed as the lower [molecular weight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_weight "Molecular weight") PAHs are attenuated by biotic decay and [photodegradation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodegradation "Photodegradation").[[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneRawlins2013-75) Evaluation of the PAH distributions using statistical methods such as principal component analyses (PCA) enabled the study to link the source (burnt moorland) to pathway (suspended stream sediment) to the depositional sink (reservoir bed).[[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneRawlins2013-75)

### Rivers, estuarine and coastal sediments

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=21 "Edit section: Rivers, estuarine and coastal sediments")]

Concentrations of PAHs in river and estuarine [sediments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sediment "Sediment") vary according to a variety of factors including proximity to municipal and industrial discharge points, wind direction and distance from major urban roadways, as well as tidal regime which controls the diluting effect of generally cleaner marine sediments relative to freshwater discharge.[[66]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Davis-66)[[76]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneHarrison2008-76)[[77]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneChenery2011-77) Consequently, the concentrations of [pollutants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pollutants "Pollutants") in estuaries tends to decrease at the river mouth.[[78]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-78) Understanding of sediment hosted PAHs in estuaries is important for the protection of commercial [fisheries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fisheries "Fisheries") (such as [mussels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mussel "Mussel")) and general environmental habitat conservation because PAHs can impact the health of suspension and sediment feeding organism.[[79]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-79) River-estuary surface sediments in the UK tend to have a lower PAH content than sediments buried 10–60 cm from the surface reflecting lower present day industrial activity combined with improvement in environmental legislation of PAH.[[77]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneChenery2011-77) Typical PAH concentrations in UK estuaries range from about 19 to 16,163 µg/kg (dry sediment weight) in the [River Clyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/River_Clyde "River Clyde") and 626 to 3,766 µg/kg in the [River Mersey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/River_Mersey "River Mersey").[[77]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneChenery2011-77)[[80]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneHarrison2007-80) In general estuarine sediments with a higher natural [total organic carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Total_organic_carbon "Total organic carbon") content (TOC) tend to accumulate PAHs due to high [sorption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sorption "Sorption") capacity of organic matter.[[80]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-VaneHarrison2007-80) A similar correspondence between PAHs and TOC has also been observed in the sediments of tropical [mangroves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mangrove "Mangrove") located on the coast of southern China.[[81]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-81)

[Cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cancer "Cancer") is a primary human health risk of exposure to PAHs.[[82]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Bostrom2002-82) Exposure to PAHs has also been linked with cardiovascular disease and poor fetal development.

PAHs have been linked to [skin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skin_cancer "Skin cancer"), [lung](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lung_cancer "Lung cancer"), [bladder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bladder_cancer "Bladder cancer"), [liver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liver_cancer "Liver cancer"), and [stomach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stomach_cancer "Stomach cancer") cancers in well-established animal model studies.[[82]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Bostrom2002-82) Specific compounds classified by various agencies as possible or probable human carcinogens are identified in the section "[Regulation and Oversight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#Regulation_and_oversight)" below.

[![Image 34: A line drawing of an 18th-century man and boy, the man carrying long tools such as a broom](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Chimney_sweeps.jpg/250px-Chimney_sweeps.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Chimney_sweeps.jpg)

An 18th-century drawing of [chimney sweeps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chimney_sweep "Chimney sweep").

Historically, PAHs contributed substantially to our understanding of adverse health effects from exposures to [environmental contaminants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pollution "Pollution"), including chemical [carcinogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carcinogenesis "Carcinogenesis").[[83]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-83) In 1775, [Percivall Pott](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percivall_Pott "Percivall Pott"), a surgeon at [St. Bartholomew's Hospital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_Bartholomew%27s_Hospital "St Bartholomew's Hospital") in London, observed that [scrotal cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chimney_sweeps%27_carcinoma "Chimney sweeps' carcinoma") was unusually common in chimney sweepers and proposed the cause as occupational exposure to [soot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soot "Soot").[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Dipple1985-84) A century later, [Richard von Volkmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_von_Volkmann "Richard von Volkmann") reported increased skin cancers in workers of the [coal tar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coal_tar "Coal tar") industry of Germany, and by the early 1900s increased rates of cancer from exposure to soot and coal tar was widely accepted. In 1915, [Yamigawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yamagiwa_Katsusabur%C5%8D "Yamagiwa Katsusaburō") and [Ichicawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=K%C5%8Dichi_Ichikawa&action=edit&redlink=1 "Kōichi Ichikawa (page does not exist)") were the first to experimentally produce cancers, specifically of the skin, by topically applying coal tar to rabbit ears.[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Dipple1985-84)

In 1922, [Ernest Kennaway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernest_Kennaway "Ernest Kennaway") determined that the carcinogenic component of coal tar mixtures was an organic compound consisting of only carbon and hydrogen. This component was later linked to a characteristic [fluorescent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluorescence "Fluorescence") pattern that was similar but not identical to [benz[_a_]anthracene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benz(a)anthracene "Benz(a)anthracene"), a PAH that was subsequently demonstrated to cause [tumors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tumor "Tumor").[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Dipple1985-84) Cook, Hewett and [Hieger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Izrael_Hieger "Izrael Hieger") then linked the specific spectroscopic fluorescent profile of [benzo[_a_]pyrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzo(a)pyrene "Benzo(a)pyrene") to that of the carcinogenic component of coal tar,[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Dipple1985-84) the first time that a specific compound from an environmental mixture (coal tar) was demonstrated to be carcinogenic.

In the 1930s and later, epidemiologists from Japan, the UK, and the US, including [Richard Doll](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Doll "Richard Doll") and various others, reported greater rates of death from [lung cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lung_cancer "Lung cancer") following occupational exposure to PAH-rich environments among workers in [coke ovens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coke_oven "Coke oven") and [coal carbonization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coal_carbonization "Coal carbonization") and [gasification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gasification "Gasification") processes.[[85]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-85)

#### Mechanisms of carcinogenesis

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=25 "Edit section: Mechanisms of carcinogenesis")]

[![Image 35](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Benzopyrene_DNA_adduct_1JDG.png/250px-Benzopyrene_DNA_adduct_1JDG.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Benzopyrene_DNA_adduct_1JDG.png)

An [adduct](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adduct "Adduct") formed between a [DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA "DNA") strand and an [epoxide derived from a benzo[_a_]pyrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/(%2B)-Benzo(a)pyrene-7,8-dihydrodiol-9,10-epoxide "(+)-Benzo(a)pyrene-7,8-dihydrodiol-9,10-epoxide") molecule (center); such adducts may interfere with normal DNA replication.

The structure of a PAH influences whether and how the individual compound is carcinogenic.[[82]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Bostrom2002-82)[[86]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Baird2005-86) Some carcinogenic PAHs are [genotoxic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genotoxicity "Genotoxicity") and induce [mutations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mutation "Mutation") that initiate cancer; others are not genotoxic and instead affect cancer promotion or progression.[[86]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Baird2005-86)[[87]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-87)

PAHs that affect [cancer initiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cancer#Pathophysiology "Cancer") are typically first chemically modified by [enzymes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enzyme "Enzyme") into metabolites that react with DNA, leading to mutations. When the DNA sequence is altered in genes that regulate [cell replication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitosis "Mitosis"), cancer can result. Mutagenic PAHs, such as benzo[_a_]pyrene, usually have four or more aromatic rings as well as a "bay region", a structural pocket that increases reactivity of the molecule to the metabolizing enzymes.[[88]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Xue2005-88) Mutagenic metabolites of PAHs include [diol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diol "Diol") epoxides, [quinones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quinones "Quinones"), and [radical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_(chemistry) "Radical (chemistry)") PAH [cations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cation "Cation").[[88]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Xue2005-88)[[89]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-89)[[90]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-90) These metabolites can bind to DNA at specific sites, forming bulky complexes called [DNA adducts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA_adduct "DNA adduct") that can be stable or unstable.[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Dipple1985-84)[[91]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Henkler2012-91) Stable adducts may lead to [DNA replication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA_replication "DNA replication") errors, while unstable adducts react with the DNA strand, removing a [purine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purine "Purine") base (either [adenine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adenine "Adenine") or [guanine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guanine "Guanine")).[[91]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Henkler2012-91) Such mutations, if they are not repaired, can transform genes encoding for normal [cell signaling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_signaling "Cell signaling") proteins into cancer-causing [oncogenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oncogene "Oncogene").[[86]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Baird2005-86) Quinones can also repeatedly generate [reactive oxygen species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reactive_oxygen_species "Reactive oxygen species") that may independently damage DNA.[[88]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Xue2005-88)

Enzymes in the [cytochrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytochrome "Cytochrome") family ([CYP1A1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CYP1A1 "CYP1A1"), [CYP1A2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CYP1A2 "CYP1A2"), [CYP1B1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CYP1B1 "CYP1B1")) metabolize PAHs to diol epoxides.[[92]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Nebert2004-92) PAH exposure can increase production of the cytochrome enzymes, allowing the enzymes to convert PAHs into mutagenic diol epoxides at greater rates.[[92]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Nebert2004-92) In this pathway, PAH molecules bind to the [aryl hydrocarbon receptor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aryl_hydrocarbon_receptor "Aryl hydrocarbon receptor") (AhR) and activate it as a [transcription factor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transcription_factor "Transcription factor") that increases production of the cytochrome enzymes. The activity of these enzymes may at times conversely protect against PAH toxicity, which is not yet well understood.[[92]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Nebert2004-92)

Low molecular weight PAHs, with two to four aromatic hydrocarbon rings, are more potent as [co-carcinogens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Co-carcinogen "Co-carcinogen") during the promotional stage of cancer. In this stage, an initiated cell (a cell that has retained a carcinogenic mutation in a key gene related to cell replication) is removed from growth-suppressing signals from its neighboring cells and begins to clonally replicate.[[93]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramesh2004-93) Low-molecular-weight PAHs that have bay or bay-like regions can dysregulate [gap junction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gap_junction "Gap junction") channels, interfering with intercellular communication, and also affect [mitogen-activated protein kinases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitogen-activated_protein_kinase "Mitogen-activated protein kinase") that activate transcription factors involved in cell proliferation.[[93]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramesh2004-93) Closure of gap junction protein channels is a normal precursor to cell division. Excessive closure of these channels after exposure to PAHs results in removing a cell from the normal growth-regulating signals imposed by its local community of cells, thus allowing initiated cancerous cells to replicate. These PAHs do not need to be enzymatically metabolized first. Low molecular weight PAHs are prevalent in the environment, thus posing a significant risk to human health at the promotional phases of cancer.

### Cardiovascular disease

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=26 "Edit section: Cardiovascular disease")]

Adult exposure to PAHs has been linked to [cardiovascular disease](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardiovascular_disease "Cardiovascular disease").[[94]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-94) PAHs are among the complex suite of contaminants in [tobacco smoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tobacco_smoke "Tobacco smoke") and [particulate air pollution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particulates "Particulates") and may contribute to cardiovascular disease resulting from such exposures.[[95]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Lewtas2007-95)

In laboratory experiments, animals exposed to certain PAHs have shown increased development of plaques ([atherogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atherogenesis "Atherogenesis")) within arteries.[[96]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramos2005-96) Potential mechanisms for the [pathogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pathogenesis "Pathogenesis") and development of atherosclerotic plaques may be similar to the mechanisms involved in the carcinogenic and mutagenic properties of PAHs.[[96]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramos2005-96) A leading hypothesis is that PAHs may activate the cytochrome enzyme [CYP1B1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CYP1B1 "CYP1B1") in [vascular smooth muscle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vascular_smooth_muscle "Vascular smooth muscle") cells. This enzyme then metabolically processes the PAHs to quinone metabolites that bind to DNA in reactive adducts that remove purine bases. The resulting mutations may contribute to unregulated growth of vascular smooth muscle cells or to their migration to the inside of the artery, which are steps in [plaque](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atheroma "Atheroma") formation.[[95]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Lewtas2007-95)[[96]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramos2005-96) These quinone metabolites also generate [reactive oxygen species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reactive_oxygen_species "Reactive oxygen species") that may alter the activity of genes that affect plaque formation.[[96]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Ramos2005-96)

[Oxidative stress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxidative_stress "Oxidative stress") following PAH exposure could also result in cardiovascular disease by causing [inflammation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inflammation "Inflammation"), which has been recognized as an important factor in the development of atherosclerosis and cardiovascular disease.[[97]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-97)[[98]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-98)[Biomarkers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomarker "Biomarker") of exposure to PAHs in humans have been associated with inflammatory biomarkers that are recognized as important predictors of cardiovascular disease, suggesting that oxidative stress resulting from exposure to PAHs may be a mechanism of cardiovascular disease in humans.[[99]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-99)

### Fetal development impacts

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=27 "Edit section: Fetal development impacts")]

Multiple [epidemiological](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epidemiology "Epidemiology") studies of people living in Europe, the United States, and China have linked [in utero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uterus "Uterus") exposure to PAHs, through air pollution or parental occupational exposure, with poor fetal growth, reduced immune function, and poorer [neurological](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neurological "Neurological") development, including lower [IQ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intelligence_quotient "Intelligence quotient").[[100]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-100)[[101]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-101)[[102]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-102)[[103]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-103)

Detection and optical properties
--------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon&action=edit&section=29 "Edit section: Detection and optical properties")]

A spectral database exists[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-NASA-20140221-4) for tracking polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) in the [universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universe "Universe").[[109]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-109) Detection of PAHs in materials is often done using [gas chromatography-mass spectrometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas_chromatography-mass_spectrometry "Gas chromatography-mass spectrometry") or [liquid chromatography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_chromatography "Liquid chromatography") with [ultraviolet-visible](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet-visible_spectroscopy "Ultraviolet-visible spectroscopy") or [fluorescence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluorescence_spectroscopy "Fluorescence spectroscopy") spectroscopic methods or by using rapid test PAH indicator strips.[[110]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-110) Structures of PAHs have been analyzed using infrared spectroscopy.[[111]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-111)

PAHs possess very characteristic [UV absorbance spectra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet-visible_spectroscopy "Ultraviolet-visible spectroscopy"). These often possess many absorbance bands and are unique for each ring structure. Thus, for a set of [isomers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomer "Isomer"), each isomer has a different UV absorbance spectrum than the others. This is particularly useful in the identification of PAHs. Most PAHs are also [fluorescent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluorescent "Fluorescent"), emitting characteristic wavelengths of light when they are excited (when the molecules absorb light). The extended pi-electron electronic structures of PAHs lead to these spectra, as well as to certain large PAHs also exhibiting [semi-conducting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semiconductor "Semiconductor") and other behaviors.

[![Image 36](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/PIA22568-CatsPawNebula-Spitzer-20181023.jpg/330px-PIA22568-CatsPawNebula-Spitzer-20181023.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:PIA22568-CatsPawNebula-Spitzer-20181023.jpg)

PAHs may be abundant in the universe.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-NASA-20110413-5)[[112]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-112)[[113]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-113)[[114]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-114) They seem to have been formed as early as a couple of billion years after the [Big Bang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Bang "Big Bang"), and are associated with [new stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_formation "Star formation") and [exoplanets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exoplanet "Exoplanet").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-NASA-20140221-4) More than 20% of the [carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon") in the universe may be associated with PAHs.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-NASA-20140221-4) PAHs are considered possible [starting material](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis "PAH world hypothesis") for the [earliest forms of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abiogenesis#PAH_world_hypothesis "Abiogenesis").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-NASA-20140221-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-NASA-20110413-5) Light emitted by the [Red Rectangle Nebula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Rectangle_Nebula "Red Rectangle Nebula") possesses spectral signatures that suggest the presence of [anthracene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthracene "Anthracene") and [pyrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrene "Pyrene").[[115]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Battersby-115)[[116]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ReferenceA-116) This report was considered a controversial hypothesis that as nebulae of the same type as the Red Rectangle approach the ends of their lives, convection currents cause carbon and hydrogen in the nebulae's cores to get caught in stellar winds, and radiate outward. As they cool, the atoms supposedly bond to each other in various ways and eventually form particles of a million or more atoms. Adolf Witt and his team inferred[[115]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Battersby-115) that PAHs—which may have been vital in the formation of [early life on Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earliest_known_life_forms "Earliest known life forms")—can only originate in nebulae.[[116]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-ReferenceA-116)

[![Image 37](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Polycyclic_Aromatic_Hydrocarbons_In_Space.jpg/250px-Polycyclic_Aromatic_Hydrocarbons_In_Space.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Polycyclic_Aromatic_Hydrocarbons_In_Space.jpg)

Two extremely bright stars illuminate a mist of PAHs in this [Spitzer Space Telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spitzer_Space_Telescope "Spitzer Space Telescope") image.[[117]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-117)

PAHs, subjected to [interstellar medium (ISM)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") conditions, are transformed, through [hydrogenation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogenation "Hydrogenation"), [oxygenation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygenate "Oxygenate"), and [hydroxylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroxylation "Hydroxylation"), to more complex [organic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound")—"a step along the path toward [amino acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acid "Amino acid") and [nucleotides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleotide "Nucleotide"), the raw materials of [proteins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein "Protein") and [DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA "DNA"), respectively".[[118]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Space-20120920-118)[[119]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-AJL-20120901-119) Further, as a result of these transformations, the PAHs lose their [spectroscopic signature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectroscopy "Spectroscopy") which could be one of the reasons "for the lack of PAH detection in [interstellar ice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_ice "Interstellar ice")[grains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_dust#Dust_grain_formation "Cosmic dust"), particularly the outer regions of cold, dense clouds or the upper molecular layers of [protoplanetary disks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protoplanetary_disk "Protoplanetary disk")."[[118]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-Space-20120920-118)[[119]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-AJL-20120901-119)

Low-temperature chemical pathways from simple [organic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") to complex PAHs are of interest. Such chemical pathways may help explain the presence of PAHs in the low-temperature atmosphere of [Saturn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturn "Saturn")'s moon [Titan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titan_(moon) "Titan (moon)"), and may be significant pathways, in terms of the [PAH world hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis "PAH world hypothesis"), in producing precursors to biochemicals related to life as we know it.[[120]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-120)[[121]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon#cite_note-121)
