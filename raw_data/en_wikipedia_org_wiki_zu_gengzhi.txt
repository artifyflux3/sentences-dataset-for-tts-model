Zu Geng or Zu Gengzhi ( Chinese : 祖暅之 ; Wade–Giles : Tsu Keng-chih ; ca. 480 – ca. 525) was a Chinese mathematician, politician, and writer. His courtesy name was Jingshuo ( 景爍 ). He was the son of the famous mathematician Zu Chongzhi .

[ 1 ] He is known principally for deriving and proving the formula for the volume of a sphere. He additionally measured the angular distance between Polaris and the celestial north pole , which was greater 1600 years ago than it is today.
