From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Constant derivative of the metric tensor

In [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics"), the **nonmetricity tensor** in [differential geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_geometry "Differential geometry") is the [covariant derivative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covariant_derivative "Covariant derivative") of the [metric tensor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metric_tensor "Metric tensor").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonmetricity_tensor#cite_note-Hehl1995-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonmetricity_tensor#cite_note-KopeikinEK2011-2) It is therefore a [tensor field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_field "Tensor field") of [order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_order "Tensor order") three. It vanishes for the case of [Riemannian geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riemannian_geometry "Riemannian geometry") and can be used to study non-Riemannian spacetimes.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonmetricity_tensor#cite_note-Puntigam1997-3)

Definition
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nonmetricity_tensor&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Definition")]

By components, it is defined as follows.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonmetricity_tensor#cite_note-Hehl1995-1)

Q μ α β=∇μ g α β{\displaystyle Q_{\mu \alpha \beta }=\nabla _{\mu }g_{\alpha \beta }}![Image 12: {\displaystyle Q_{\mu \alpha \beta }=\nabla _{\mu }g_{\alpha \beta }}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/42abf40601db843e577166f52f38d24ccc2e12a5)
It measures the rate of change of the components of the metric tensor along the flow of a given vector field, since

∇μ≡∇∂μ{\displaystyle \nabla _{\mu }\equiv \nabla _{\partial _{\mu }}}![Image 13: {\displaystyle \nabla _{\mu }\equiv \nabla _{\partial _{\mu }}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7f3de49d1431d9bf620d78555a3112b9a1666d8e)
where {∂μ}μ=0,1,2,3{\displaystyle \{\partial _{\mu }\}_{\mu =0,1,2,3}}![Image 14: {\displaystyle \{\partial _{\mu }\}_{\mu =0,1,2,3}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8104ff1a5ccbc3896a9559ddd486650342b66d5c) is the coordinate basis of vector fields of the tangent bundle, in the case of having a 4-dimensional [manifold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifold "Manifold").

Relation to connection
----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nonmetricity_tensor&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Relation to connection")]

We say that a [connection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Connection_(mathematics) "Connection (mathematics)")Γ{\displaystyle \Gamma }![Image 15: {\displaystyle \Gamma }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4cfde86a3f7ec967af9955d0988592f0693d2b19) is compatible with the metric when its associated covariant derivative of the [metric tensor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metric_tensor "Metric tensor") (call it ∇Γ{\displaystyle \nabla ^{\Gamma }}![Image 16: {\displaystyle \nabla ^{\Gamma }}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/71e07ac82b89b8ea550d9c022fb0e5704bd9d067), for example) is zero, i.e.

∇μ Γ g α β=0.{\displaystyle \nabla _{\mu }^{\Gamma }g_{\alpha \beta }=0.}![Image 17: {\displaystyle \nabla _{\mu }^{\Gamma }g_{\alpha \beta }=0.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/3531084172d76b63e9129aa81ee9082bf2f99b1e)
If the connection is also torsion-free (i.e. totally symmetric) then it is known as the [Levi-Civita connection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levi-Civita_connection "Levi-Civita connection"), which is the only one without [torsion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torsion_tensor "Torsion tensor") and compatible with the metric tensor. If we see it from a geometrical point of view, a non-vanishing nonmetricity tensor for a metric tensor g{\displaystyle g}![Image 18: {\displaystyle g}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/d3556280e66fe2c0d0140df20935a6f057381d77) implies that the modulus of a vector defined on the [tangent bundle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tangent_bundle "Tangent bundle") to a certain point p{\displaystyle p}![Image 19: {\displaystyle p}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/81eac1e205430d1f40810df36a0edffdc367af36) of the manifold, _changes_ when it is evaluated along the direction (flow) of another arbitrary vector.
