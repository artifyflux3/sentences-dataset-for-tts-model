The **steradian** (symbol: **sr**) or **square radian**[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steradian#cite_note-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steradian#cite_note-2) is the unit of [solid angle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solid_angle "Solid angle") in the [International System of Units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_System_of_Units "International System of Units") (SI). It is used in [three-dimensional geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-dimensional_geometry "Three-dimensional geometry"), and is analogous to the [radian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radian "Radian"), which quantifies [planar angles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planar_angle "Planar angle"). A solid angle in the form of a [circular cone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circular_cone "Circular cone") can be projected onto a sphere from its centre, delineating a [spherical cap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spherical_cap "Spherical cap") where the cone intersects the sphere. The magnitude of the solid angle expressed in steradians is defined as the quotient of the surface area of the spherical cap and the square of the sphere's radius. This is analogous to the way a plane angle projected onto a circle delineates a [circular arc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circular_arc "Circular arc") on the circumference, whose length is proportional to the angle. Steradians can be used to measure a solid angle of any projected shape. The solid angle subtended is the same as that of a cone with the same projected area. A solid angle of one steradian subtends a [cone aperture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cone_aperture "Cone aperture") of approximately 1.144 radians or 65.54 degrees.

In the SI, solid angle is considered to be a [dimensionless](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimensionless_quantity "Dimensionless quantity") quantity, the ratio of the area projected onto a surrounding sphere and the square of the sphere's radius. This is the number of square radians in the solid angle. This means that the SI steradian is the number of square radians in a solid angle equal to one square radian, which of course is the number one. It is useful to distinguish between dimensionless quantities of a different [kind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kind_of_quantity "Kind of quantity"), such as the radian (in the SI, a ratio of quantities of dimension length), so the symbol sr is used. For example, [radiant intensity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiant_intensity "Radiant intensity") can be measured in watts per steradian (W‚ãÖsr‚àí1). The steradian was formerly an [SI supplementary unit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SI_supplementary_unit "SI supplementary unit"), but this category was abolished in 1995 and the steradian is now considered an [SI derived unit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SI_derived_unit "SI derived unit").

The name _steradian_ is derived from the [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_language "Greek language")œÉœÑŒµœÅŒµœåœÇ _stereos_ 'solid' + radian.

Definition
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Steradian&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Definition")]

A steradian can be defined as the solid angle [subtended](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subtended_angle "Subtended angle") at the centre of a [unit sphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unit_sphere "Unit sphere") by a unit [area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Area "Area") (of any shape) on its surface. For a general sphere of [radius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radius "Radius")_r_, any portion of its surface with area _A_ = _r_ 2 subtends one steradian at its centre.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steradian#cite_note-3)

A solid angle in the form of a circular cone is related to the area it cuts out of a sphere:
