From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Branch of mathematics

**Multilinear algebra** is the study of [functions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Function_(mathematics) "Function (mathematics)") with multiple [vector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_space "Vector space")-valued [arguments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argument_of_a_function "Argument of a function"), with the functions being [linear maps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_map "Linear map") with respect to each argument. It involves concepts such as [matrices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matrix_(mathematics) "Matrix (mathematics)"), [tensors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor "Tensor"), [multivectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multivector "Multivector"), [systems of linear equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System_of_linear_equations "System of linear equations"), [higher-dimensional spaces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Higher-dimensional_space "Higher-dimensional space"), [determinants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Determinant "Determinant"), [inner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inner_product "Inner product") and [outer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_product "Outer product") products, and [dual spaces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_space "Dual space"). It is a mathematical tool used in [engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Engineering "Engineering"), [machine learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_learning "Machine learning"), [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics"), and [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multilinear_algebra#cite_note-1)

Origin
------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Multilinear_algebra&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Origin")]

While many theoretical concepts and applications involve [single vectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_space "Vector space"), mathematicians such as [Hermann Grassmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermann_Grassmann "Hermann Grassmann") considered structures involving pairs, triplets, and [multivectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multivector "Multivector") that generalize [vectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_(mathematics_and_physics) "Vector (mathematics and physics)"). With multiple combinational possibilities, the space of [multivectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multivector "Multivector") expands to 2 _n_ dimensions, where _n_ is the dimension of the relevant vector space.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multilinear_algebra#cite_note-2) The [determinant can be formulated abstractly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Determinant#Abstract_formulation "Determinant") using the structures of multilinear algebra.

Multilinear algebra appears in the study of the mechanical response of materials to stress and strain, involving various moduli of [elasticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elasticity_(physics) "Elasticity (physics)"). The term "[tensor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor "Tensor")" describes elements within the multilinear space due to its added structure. Despite Grassmann's early work in 1844 with his _[Ausdehnungslehre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ausdehnungslehre "Ausdehnungslehre")_, which was also republished in 1862, the subject was initially not widely understood, as even ordinary linear algebra posed many challenges at the time.

The concepts of multilinear algebra find applications in certain studies of [multivariate calculus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multivariate_calculus "Multivariate calculus") and [manifolds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifold "Manifold"), particularly concerning the [Jacobian matrix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacobian_matrix "Jacobian matrix"). [Infinitesimal differentials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_(infinitesimal) "Differential (infinitesimal)") encountered in single-variable calculus are transformed into [differential forms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_forms "Differential forms") in [multivariate calculus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multivariable_calculus "Multivariable calculus"), and their manipulation is carried out using [exterior algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exterior_algebra "Exterior algebra").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multilinear_algebra#cite_note-3)

Following Grassmann, developments in multilinear algebra were made by [Victor Schlegel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victor_Schlegel "Victor Schlegel") in 1872 with the publication of the first part of his _System der Raumlehre_[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multilinear_algebra#cite_note-4) and by [Elwin Bruno Christoffel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elwin_Bruno_Christoffel "Elwin Bruno Christoffel"). Notably, significant advancements came through the work of [Gregorio Ricci-Curbastro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gregorio_Ricci-Curbastro "Gregorio Ricci-Curbastro") and [Tullio Levi-Civita](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tullio_Levi-Civita "Tullio Levi-Civita"),[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multilinear_algebra#cite_note-5) particularly in the form of _absolute differential calculus_ within multilinear algebra. [Marcel Grossmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcel_Grossmann "Marcel Grossmann") and [Michele Besso](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michele_Besso "Michele Besso") introduced this form to [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein"), and in 1915, Einstein's publication on [general relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity "General relativity"), explaining the [precession of Mercury's perihelion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precession_of_the_perihelion_of_Mercury "Precession of the perihelion of Mercury"), established multilinear algebra and tensors as important mathematical tools in physics.

In 1958, [Nicolas Bourbaki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolas_Bourbaki "Nicolas Bourbaki") included a chapter on multilinear algebra titled "_Algèbre Multilinéaire_" in their series [Éléments de mathématique](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89l%C3%A9ments_de_math%C3%A9matique "Éléments de mathématique"), specifically within the algebra book. The chapter covers topics such as bilinear functions, the [tensor product](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_product "Tensor product") of two [modules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Module_(ring_theory) "Module (ring theory)"), and the properties of tensor products.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multilinear_algebra#cite_note-6)
