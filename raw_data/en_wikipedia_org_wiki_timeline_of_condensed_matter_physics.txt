From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article lists the main historical events in the history of [condensed matter physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Condensed_matter_physics "Condensed matter physics"). This branch of [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics") focuses on understanding and studying the physical properties and transitions between [phases of matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phases_of_matter "Phases of matter"). Condensed matter refers to materials where particles ([atoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atom "Atom"), [molecules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecule "Molecule"), or [ions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ion "Ion")) are closely packed together or under interaction, such as [solids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solid "Solid") and [liquids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid "Liquid"). This field explores a wide range of phenomena, including the [electronic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronics "Electronics"), [magnetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetism "Magnetism"), [thermal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_physics "Thermal physics"), and mechanical properties of [matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matter "Matter").

This timeline includes developments in subfields of condensed matter physics such as theoretical [crystallography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystallography "Crystallography"), [solid-state physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solid-state_physics "Solid-state physics"), [soft matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soft_matter "Soft matter") physics, [mesoscopic physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesoscopic_physics "Mesoscopic physics"), [material physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Material_physics "Material physics"), [low-temperature physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryogenics "Cryogenics"), microscopic theories of [magnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetism "Magnetism") in matter and [optical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optics "Optics") properties of matter and [metamaterials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metamaterial "Metamaterial").

Even if material properties were modeled before 1900, condensed matter topics were considered as part of physics since the development of [quantum mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_mechanics "Quantum mechanics") and microscopic theories of matter. According to [Philip W. Anderson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_W._Anderson "Philip W. Anderson"), the term "condensed matter" appeared about 1965.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-pwa-princeton-1)

For [history of fluid mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_fluid_mechanics "History of fluid mechanics"), see [timeline of fluid and continuum mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics "Timeline of fluid and continuum mechanics").

Before quantum mechanics
------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Before quantum mechanics")]

*   28,000–12,000 [BP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Before_Present "Before Present") – [Upper Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_Paleolithic "Upper Paleolithic"): earliest evidence of [ceramic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceramic "Ceramic") objects made for ritual purposes.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-2)
*   10,000–3300 BC – [Neolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic "Neolithic"): development of [pottery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pottery "Pottery"), as well as early evidence of [glass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glass "Glass") production and [metalworking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metalworking "Metalworking").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-3)
*   3300–1200 BC – [Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age "Bronze Age"): development of [metallurgy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metallurgy "Metallurgy"), with [copper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copper "Copper") and [tin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tin "Tin") being combined to create [bronze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze "Bronze").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-4)
*   1200–300 BC – [Iron Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron_Age "Iron Age"): development of [ferrous metallurgy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferrous_metallurgy "Ferrous metallurgy"), allowing [iron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron "Iron") and [steel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steel "Steel") to largely replace bronze.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-5)

[![Image 1](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Magnetite-118736.jpg/250px-Magnetite-118736.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Magnetite-118736.jpg)

A piece of [magnetite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetite "Magnetite") with permanent magnetic properties were noticed already in [Ancient Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greece "Ancient Greece")

*   8th century BC: first writings on the magnetic properties of [lodestone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lodestone "Lodestone") in [Ancient Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greece "Ancient Greece").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:2-6)
*   6th century BC – [Thales of Miletus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thales_of_Miletus "Thales of Miletus") observes that rubbing fur on various substances, such as [amber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amber "Amber"), would cause an attraction between the two, which is now known to be caused by [static electricity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Static_electricity "Static electricity").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Baigrie-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-stewart-8)
*   5th century BC – [Leucippus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leucippus "Leucippus") and [Democritus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democritus "Democritus") postulate the philosophy of [atomism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomism "Atomism").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-9)
*   4th century BC – [Aristotle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aristotle "Aristotle") describes the composition of matter in terms of the four [classical elements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_element "Classical element"), founding [Aristotelian physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aristotelian_physics "Aristotelian physics").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-10)
*   1st century AD – [Pliny the Elder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder "Pliny the Elder") in his [_Natural History_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_History_(Pliny) "Natural History (Pliny)") records the story of [Magnes the shepherd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnes_the_shepherd "Magnes the shepherd") who discovered the magnetic properties of some iron stones _._[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:2-6)
*   160 AD – [Claudius Ptolemy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemy "Ptolemy") writes his book [_Optics_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optics_(Ptolemy) "Optics (Ptolemy)") on [reflection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflection_(physics) "Reflection (physics)") and [refraction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Refraction "Refraction") of light, and tabulated angles of refraction for several media. He found a refraction law valid for small angles.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-11)

### Classical theories before the 19th century

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Classical theories before the 19th century")]

*   1611 – [Johannes Kepler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannes_Kepler "Johannes Kepler") first states the [Kepler conjecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kepler_conjecture "Kepler conjecture") about [sphere packing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sphere_packing "Sphere packing") in three-dimensional [Euclidean space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_space "Euclidean space"). It states that no arrangement of equally sized [spheres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sphere "Sphere") filling space has a greater [average density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Packing_density "Packing density") than that of the cubic close packing ([face-centered cubic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Face-centered_cubic "Face-centered cubic")) and [hexagonal close packing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexagonal_close_packing "Hexagonal close packing") arrangements.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-12)
*   1621 – [Willebrord Snellius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Willebrord_Snellius "Willebrord Snellius") reformulates the laws of refraction and reflection of light into [Snell's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snell%27s_law "Snell's law").[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-13)
*   1660 – [Robert Hooke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Hooke "Robert Hooke") postulates the simplest equation of linear elasticity known as [Hooke's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hooke%27s_law "Hooke's law").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-14)
*   1687 – [Isaac Newton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Newton "Isaac Newton") postulates the [Newton's laws of motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%27s_laws_of_motion "Newton's laws of motion").[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-15)
*   1701 – Newton studies heat, leading to [Newton's law of cooling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%27s_law_of_cooling "Newton's law of cooling").[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-16)
*   1729 – Scientist [Stephen Gray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_Gray_(scientist) "Stephen Gray (scientist)") discovers the electrical conduction of metals.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-17)
*   1778 – [Diamagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamagnetism "Diamagnetism") was first discovered when [Anton Brugmans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anton_Brugmans "Anton Brugmans") observed in 1778 that [bismuth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bismuth "Bismuth") was repelled by magnetic fields.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-18)
*   1781 – [René Just Haüy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Just_Ha%C3%BCy "René Just Haüy") (often termed the "Father of Modern Crystallography"[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-19)) discovers that crystals always cleave along crystallographic planes. Based on this observation, and the fact that the inter-facial angles in each crystal species always have the same value, Haüy concluded that crystals must be periodic and composed of regularly arranged rows of tiny polyhedra (_molécules intégrantes_). This theory explained why all crystal planes are related by small rational numbers (the law of rational indices).[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-20)[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-21)

[![Image 2](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Hall_effect_A.png/250px-Hall_effect_A.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Hall_effect_A.png)

Schema of the classical [Hall effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hall_effect "Hall effect") discovered in 1879, where a voltage is created perpendicular to the current in a circuit due to the influence of a magnetic field.

*   1800 – The [Voltaic pile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voltaic_pile "Voltaic pile"), the first [electric battery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_battery "Electric battery") is developed by [Alessandro Volta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alessandro_Volta "Alessandro Volta").[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-22)
*   1803–1808 – [John Dalton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Dalton "John Dalton") reconsiders the [atomic theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic_theory "Atomic theory") of matter in order to understand chemistry.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-23)
*   1816 – [David Brewster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Brewster "David Brewster") discovers stress [birefringence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birefringence "Birefringence") in diamond.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-24)
*   1819 – Experimentally [Pierre Louis Dulong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Louis_Dulong "Pierre Louis Dulong") and [Alexis Thérèse Petit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexis_Th%C3%A9r%C3%A8se_Petit "Alexis Thérèse Petit") find that the [specific heat capacity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Specific_heat_capacity "Specific heat capacity") of solids was close to a constant value given by [Dulong–Petit law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dulong%E2%80%93Petit_law "Dulong–Petit law").[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-25)
*   1821 – [Thomas Johann Seebeck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Johann_Seebeck "Thomas Johann Seebeck") discovers the [thermoelectric effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermoelectric_effect "Thermoelectric effect"), related by the [Seebeck coefficient](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seebeck_coefficient "Seebeck coefficient").[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-26)
*   1822 – [Joseph Fourier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Fourier "Joseph Fourier") proposes Fourier's law of [thermal conduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_conduction "Thermal conduction") and the [heat equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_equation "Heat equation").[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-27)
*   1826 – [Moritz Ludwig Frankenheim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moritz_Ludwig_Frankenheim "Moritz Ludwig Frankenheim") derives the 32 crystal classes by using the [crystallographic restriction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystallographic_restriction_theorem "Crystallographic restriction theorem"), consistent with Haüy's laws, that only 2, 3, 4 and 6-fold rotational axes are permitted.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-28)
*   1827 – [Georg Ohm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georg_Ohm "Georg Ohm"), publishes the proportional relation between electric current and voltage in metals, known as [Ohm's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ohm%27s_law "Ohm's law").[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-29)
*   1834 – [Jean-Charles Peltier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Charles_Peltier "Jean-Charles Peltier") discovers the [Peltier effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peltier_effect "Peltier effect"): heating by an electric current at the junction of two different metals.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-30)
*   1839 – [William Hallowes Miller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Hallowes_Miller "William Hallowes Miller") invents zonal relations by projecting the faces of a crystal upon the surface of a circumscribed sphere. [Miller indices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miller_index "Miller index") are defined which form a notation system in crystallography for planes in [crystal (Bravais) lattices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bravais_lattice "Bravais lattice").[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-31)
*   1840 – [James Prescott Joule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Prescott_Joule "James Prescott Joule") formulates the equation for [Joule heating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joule_heating "Joule heating") quantifying the amount of heat produced in a circuit as proportional to the product of the time duration, the resistance, and the square of the current passing through it.[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-32)
*   1845 – [Michael Faraday](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Faraday "Michael Faraday") studies the interaction of light and magnetic fields with matter ([Faraday rotation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faraday_rotation "Faraday rotation")).[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-33)
*   1848 – [Louis Pasteur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Pasteur "Louis Pasteur") discovers that [sodium ammonium tartrate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tartaric_acid "Tartaric acid") can crystallize in left- and right-handed forms and showed that the two forms can rotate [polarized light](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polarization_(waves) "Polarization (waves)") in opposite directions. This was the first demonstration of [molecular chirality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chirality_(chemistry) "Chirality (chemistry)"), and also the first explanation of [isomerism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomer "Isomer").[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-34)
*   1850 – [Auguste Bravais](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auguste_Bravais "Auguste Bravais") develops the concept of [Bravais lattices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bravais_lattice "Bravais lattice") to describe periodicity in crystals. He derives the 14 space lattices.[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-35)
*   1853 – Discovery of [Wiedemann–Franz law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wiedemann%E2%80%93Franz_law "Wiedemann–Franz law") relating thermal and electrical conductivities, by [Gustav Wiedemann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gustav_Heinrich_Wiedemann "Gustav Heinrich Wiedemann") and [Rudolph Franz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudolph_Franz "Rudolph Franz").[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-36)
*   1854 – [Lord Kelvin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lord_Kelvin "Lord Kelvin") discovers the thermoelectric [Thomson effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermoelectric_effect "Thermoelectric effect").[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-37)
*   1859 – [Gustav Kirchhoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gustav_Kirchhoff "Gustav Kirchhoff") introduces the concept of a [blackbody](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackbody "Blackbody") and proves that its emission spectrum depends only on its temperature.[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Peacock7-38)
*   1861–1865 – [James Clerk Maxwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Clerk_Maxwell "James Clerk Maxwell") summarizes the fundamental equations of electromagnetism into an early version of [Maxwell's equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maxwell%27s_equations "Maxwell's equations") and relates electromagnetism to light in his publications _[On Physical Lines of Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/On\_Physical\_Lines\_of\_Force "On Physical Lines of Force")_ and _[A Dynamical Theory of the Electromagnetic Field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A\_Dynamical\_Theory\_of\_the\_Electromagnetic\_Field "A Dynamical Theory of the Electromagnetic Field")_.[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-39)
*   1867 – [Dmitry Chernov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dmitry_Chernov "Dmitry Chernov") establishes the critical temperatures of [steel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steel "Steel").[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:4-40)
*   1872 – The [Boltzmann transport equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boltzmann_equation "Boltzmann equation"), describing the statistical behaviour of a [thermodynamic system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermodynamic_system "Thermodynamic system") not in a state of [equilibrium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermodynamic_equilibrium "Thermodynamic equilibrium"), is devised by [Ludwig Boltzmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludwig_Boltzmann "Ludwig Boltzmann").[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Encyclopaediaof-41)
*   1872 – [Ludvig Lorenz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludvig_Lorenz "Ludvig Lorenz") finds the Lorenz number, the constant of the Wiedemann–Franz law.[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-42)
*   1874 – [Karl Ferdinand Braun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_Ferdinand_Braun "Karl Ferdinand Braun") discovered current [rectification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rectification_(electricity) "Rectification (electricity)") using a point-contact [metal–semiconductor junction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal%E2%80%93semiconductor_junction "Metal–semiconductor junction").[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-43)
*   1875 – [John Kerr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Kerr_(physicist) "John Kerr (physicist)") discovers the double refraction of solid and liquids, now known as the [Kerr effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerr_effect "Kerr effect").[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-44)
*   1876 – [Josiah Willard Gibbs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josiah_Willard_Gibbs "Josiah Willard Gibbs") introduces the concept of [phase transition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phase_transition "Phase transition").[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:4-40)
*   1879 – [Edwin Hall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edwin_Hall "Edwin Hall") discovers the [Hall effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hall_effect "Hall effect").[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-45)
*   1879 – [Leonhard Sohncke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonhard_Sohncke "Leonhard Sohncke") lists the 65 crystallographic point systems using [rotations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotation_(mathematics) "Rotation (mathematics)") and [reflections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflection_(mathematics) "Reflection (mathematics)") in addition to [translations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Translation_(geometry) "Translation (geometry)").[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-46)
*   1880 – The first demonstration of the direct [piezoelectric effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piezoelectricity "Piezoelectricity") by the brothers [Pierre Curie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Curie "Pierre Curie") and [Jacques Curie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Curie "Jacques Curie").[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-47)
*   1883 – [Thomas Edison](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Edison "Thomas Edison") discovers [thermionic emission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermionic_emission "Thermionic emission") or the Edison effect.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-48)
*   1887 – [Floris Osmond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Floris_Osmond "Floris Osmond") names the phases of steel.[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:4-40)
*   1887 – [Heinrich Hertz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinrich_Hertz "Heinrich Hertz") discovers the [photoelectric effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photoelectric_effect "Photoelectric effect").[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-49)
*   1888–1889 – Crystalline optical properties of [liquid crystals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_crystal "Liquid crystal") and their ability to flow are first described by [Friedrich Reinitzer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedrich_Reinitzer "Friedrich Reinitzer") and confirmed by [Otto Lehmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otto_Lehmann_(physicist) "Otto Lehmann (physicist)").[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-50)
*   1891 – Derivation of the 230 [space groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_group "Space group") (by adding [mirror-image symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mirror_image "Mirror image") to Sohncke's work) by a collaborative effort of [Evgraf Fedorov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evgraf_Fedorov "Evgraf Fedorov") and [Arthur Schoenflies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Moritz_Schoenflies "Arthur Moritz Schoenflies").[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-51)[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-52)
*   1895 – [Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilhelm_Conrad_R%C3%B6ntgen "Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen") discovers [X-rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-ray "X-ray") in experiments with electron beams in plasma.[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Peacock7-38)
*   1895 – [Hendrik Lorentz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hendrik_Lorentz "Hendrik Lorentz") derives the [Lorentz force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lorentz_force "Lorentz force") for charged particles in electric and magnetic fields.[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Dahl-53)
*   1895 – [Pierre Curie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Curie "Pierre Curie") discovers empirically that the [magnetic susceptibility](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetic_susceptibility "Magnetic susceptibility") of many materials is inversely proportional to temperature according to [Curie's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curie%27s_law "Curie's law"). He also found that permanent magnetism was lost after a certain [Curie temperature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curie_temperature "Curie temperature").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:2-6)
*   1896–1897 – [Pieter Zeeman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pieter_Zeeman "Pieter Zeeman") first observes the [Zeeman splitting effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zeeman_effect "Zeeman effect") by applying a magnetic field to light sources.[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Zeeman-54)
*   1897 – [J. J. Thomson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._J._Thomson "J. J. Thomson")'s experimentation with [cathode rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cathode_rays "Cathode rays") led him to suggest a fundamental unit more than a 1000 times smaller than an atom, based on the high [charge-to-mass ratio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charge-to-mass_ratio "Charge-to-mass ratio"). He called the particle a "corpuscle", but later scientists preferred the term [electron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron "Electron").[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-55)

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Paul_Drude.jpg/250px-Paul_Drude.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Paul_Drude.jpg)

Paul Drude, author of the [Drude model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drude_model "Drude model") in 1900. He understood that thermal properties of metals could be understood as a gas of [free electrons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_electron_model "Free electron model").

*   1900 – [Max Planck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Planck "Max Planck") uses for the first time [quantum theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_mechanics "Quantum mechanics") to explain [black-body radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black-body_radiation "Black-body radiation").
*   1900 – [Paul Drude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Drude "Paul Drude") proposes the [Drude model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drude_model "Drude model") to explain thermal and electric properties of metals.
*   1901 – [Thermionic emission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermionic_emission "Thermionic emission") is first theoretically modeled by [Owen Willans Richardson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Owen_Willans_Richardson "Owen Willans Richardson")
*   1905 – [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein")'s [_Annus mirabilis_ papers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annus_mirabilis_papers "Annus mirabilis papers") postulating [special relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_relativity "Special relativity"), the theory for [Brownian motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brownian_motion "Brownian motion") and explaining the photoelectric effect using quantum mechanics.
*   1905 – [Paul Langevin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Langevin "Paul Langevin") derives the classical theory for [diamagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamagnetism "Diamagnetism").
*   1907: 
    *   [Einstein solid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_solid "Einstein solid") model predicts the deviations for the [specific heat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_capacity "Heat capacity") of solids from Dulong–Petit law.
    *   The first theory describing [crystallographic defects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystallographic_defect "Crystallographic defect") is developed by [Vito Volterra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vito_Volterra "Vito Volterra").
    *   [Pierre Weiss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Weiss "Pierre Weiss") introduces the [magnetic domain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetic_domain "Magnetic domain") theory of [ferromagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferromagnetism "Ferromagnetism").

*   1909 – Lorentz develops the classical [Lorentz oscillator model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lorentz_oscillator_model "Lorentz oscillator model") to describe the optical response of materials.[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-56)
*   1911 – [Heike Kamerlingh Onnes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heike_Kamerlingh_Onnes "Heike Kamerlingh Onnes") and [Gilles Holst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gilles_Holst "Gilles Holst") discover [superconductivity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superconductivity "Superconductivity") in mercury.
*   1912: 
    *   [Max von Laue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_von_Laue "Max von Laue") discovers [diffraction of X-rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-ray_crystallography "X-ray crystallography") by crystals.
    *   [Peter Debye](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Debye "Peter Debye") develops a model for the specific heat of solids in terms of [phonons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phonon "Phonon"), known as [Debye model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debye_model "Debye model").
    *   [Geertruida Lorentz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geertruida_de_Haas-Lorentz "Geertruida de Haas-Lorentz"), applies Einstein's Brownian motion equations to [noise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noise_(electronics) "Noise (electronics)") in electrical circuits.

*   1913 – [William Henry Bragg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Henry_Bragg "William Henry Bragg") and [Lawrence Bragg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lawrence_Bragg "Lawrence Bragg") use X-rays to analyze crystals.
*   1917 – Weiss and [Auguste Piccard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auguste_Piccard "Auguste Piccard") first observe the [magnetocaloric effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetic_refrigeration "Magnetic refrigeration").
*   1919 – [Walter H. Schottky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_H._Schottky "Walter H. Schottky") introduces the concept of [shot noise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shot_noise "Shot noise") while studying vacuum tubes.
*   1919 – [Hendrika Johanna van Leeuwen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hendrika_Johanna_van_Leeuwen "Hendrika Johanna van Leeuwen") rediscovers the [Bohr–Van Leeuwen theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bohr%E2%80%93Van_Leeuwen_theorem "Bohr–Van Leeuwen theorem"), showing that magnetic properties of matter are due to quantum mechanics.
*   1920: 
    *   [Ferroelectricity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferroelectricity "Ferroelectricity") gets discovered in [Rochelle salt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rochelle_salt "Rochelle salt") by [Joseph Valasek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Valasek "Joseph Valasek").[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Valasek-57)
    *   [Hermann Staudinger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermann_Staudinger "Hermann Staudinger"), suggest that small molecules can be link together through [covalent bonds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covalent_bond "Covalent bond") to form [polymer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymer "Polymer").[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-58)
    *   [Wilhelm Lenz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilhelm_Lenz "Wilhelm Lenz") describes for the first time the [Ising model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ising_model "Ising model") as a model for magnetism in matter.

*   1923 – [Pierre Auger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Victor_Auger "Pierre Victor Auger") discovers the [Auger effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auger_effect "Auger effect"), where filling the inner-shell vacancy of an atom is accompanied by the emission of an electron from the same atom.
*   1923 – [Louis de Broglie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis,_7th_duc_de_Broglie "Louis, 7th duc de Broglie") extends [wave–particle duality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wave%E2%80%93particle_duality "Wave–particle duality") to particles, postulating that electrons in motion are associated with waves. He predicts that the wavelengths are given by the [Planck constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_constant "Planck constant")_h_ divided by the [momentum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Momentum "Momentum") of the _mv_ = _p_ of the [electron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron "Electron"): _λ_ = _h_ / _mv_ = _h_ / _p_.[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Peacock7-38)
*   1923–1927 [Electron wave diffraction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron_diffraction "Electron diffraction") is demonstrated experimentally independently by [Davisson–Germer experiments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Davisson%E2%80%93Germer_experiment "Davisson–Germer experiment") and the experiments by [George Paget Thomson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Paget_Thomson "George Paget Thomson") and Alexander Reid.
*   1924 – [Satyendra Nath Bose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satyendra_Nath_Bose "Satyendra Nath Bose") explains Planck's law using a new statistical law that governs [bosons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boson "Boson"), and Einstein generalizes it to predict [Bose–Einstein condensate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bose%E2%80%93Einstein_condensate "Bose–Einstein condensate"). The theory becomes known as [Bose–Einstein statistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bose%E2%80%93Einstein_statistics "Bose–Einstein statistics").[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Peacock7-38)
*   1924 – [Wolfgang Pauli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfgang_Pauli "Wolfgang Pauli") outlines the [Pauli exclusion principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pauli_exclusion_principle "Pauli exclusion principle") which states that no two identical [fermions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermion "Fermion") may occupy the same quantum state simultaneously, a fact that explains many features of the [periodic table](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Periodic_table "Periodic table").[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Peacock7-38)
*   1925 – [Werner Heisenberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Werner_Heisenberg "Werner Heisenberg"), [Max Born](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Born "Max Born"), and [Pascual Jordan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pascual_Jordan "Pascual Jordan") develop the [matrix mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matrix_mechanics "Matrix mechanics") formulation of quantum mechanics.[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Peacock7-38)
*   1925 – [Ernst Ising](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernst_Ising "Ernst Ising") finds the analytical solution to the 1D Ising model.
*   1926: 
    *   [Enrico Fermi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enrico_Fermi "Enrico Fermi") discovers the [spin-statistics theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin-statistics_theorem "Spin-statistics theorem") connection.
    *   [Paul Dirac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Dirac "Paul Dirac") introduces [Fermi–Dirac statistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermi%E2%80%93Dirac_statistics "Fermi–Dirac statistics").
    *   [Erwin Schrödinger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Schr%C3%B6dinger "Erwin Schrödinger") uses de Broglie's electron wave postulate (1924) to develop a [Schrödinger equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schr%C3%B6dinger_equation "Schrödinger equation"); also introduces the [Hamiltonian operator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamiltonian_operator "Hamiltonian operator") in quantum mechanics.
    *   [Johnson–Nyquist noise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johnson%E2%80%93Nyquist_noise "Johnson–Nyquist noise") is first measured by [John B. Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Bertrand_Johnson "John Bertrand Johnson") at [Bell Labs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bell_Labs "Bell Labs"). He described his findings to [Harry Nyquist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Nyquist "Harry Nyquist"), also at Bell Labs, who was able to explain the results.

*   1927: 
    *   [Max Born](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Born "Max Born") and [J. Robert Oppenheimer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._Robert_Oppenheimer "J. Robert Oppenheimer") introduce the [Born–Oppenheimer approximation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Born%E2%80%93Oppenheimer_approximation "Born–Oppenheimer approximation"), which allows the quick approximation of the energy and wavefunctions of smaller molecules.
    *   Pauli models the paramagnetic contribution of itinerant electrons due to spins ([Pauli paramagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pauli_paramagnetism "Pauli paramagnetism")).
    *   [Walter Heitler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Heitler "Walter Heitler") and [Fritz London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fritz_London "Fritz London") introduce the concepts of [valence bond theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valence_bond_theory "Valence bond theory") and apply it to the [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen") molecule.
    *   [Llewellyn Thomas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Llewellyn_Thomas "Llewellyn Thomas") and Fermi develop the [Thomas–Fermi model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas%E2%80%93Fermi_model "Thomas–Fermi model") for a [gas in a box](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas_in_a_box "Gas in a box").
    *   [Chandrasekhara Venkata Raman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C._V._Raman "C. V. Raman") studies optical photon scattering by electrons, now known as [Raman spectroscopy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raman_spectroscopy "Raman spectroscopy").
    *   Walter Heitler uses Schrödinger's wave equation to show how two hydrogen atom [wavefunctions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wavefunction "Wavefunction") join, with plus, minus, and exchange terms, to form a [covalent bond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covalent_bond "Covalent bond").
    *   [Robert S. Mulliken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_S._Mulliken "Robert S. Mulliken") works, in coordination with Hund, to develop a molecular orbital theory where electrons are assigned to states that extend over an entire molecule and, in 1932, introduces many new molecular orbital terminologies, such as [σ bond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%CE%A3_bond "Σ bond"), [π bond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%CE%A0_bond "Π bond"), and [δ bond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%CE%94_bond "Δ bond").
    *   [Eugene Wigner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_Wigner "Eugene Wigner") relates [degeneracies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Degenerate_energy_levels "Degenerate energy levels") of quantum states to [irreducible representations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irreducible_representation "Irreducible representation") of symmetry groups.
    *   [Arnold Sommerfeld](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arnold_Sommerfeld "Arnold Sommerfeld"), extends Drude's model using [Fermi–Dirac statistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermi%E2%80%93Dirac_statistics "Fermi–Dirac statistics") leading to the [free electron model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_electron_model "Free electron model").
    *   [Douglas Hartree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douglas_Hartree "Douglas Hartree") introduced the [Hartree equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hartree_equation "Hartree equation") for atoms.[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-59)

*   1928–1930 – [John Hasbrouck Van Vleck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Hasbrouck_Van_Vleck "John Hasbrouck Van Vleck") formalizes the quantum theory of magnetism and formulates [Van Vleck paramagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_Vleck_paramagnetism "Van Vleck paramagnetism").
*   1928 – [Linus Pauling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linus_Pauling "Linus Pauling") outlines the quantum nature of the [chemical bonds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_bond "Chemical bond").
*   1928 – [Friedrich Hund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedrich_Hund "Friedrich Hund") and Robert S. Mulliken introduce the concept of [molecular orbitals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_orbital "Molecular orbital").
*   1929: 
    *   [Felix Bloch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Felix_Bloch "Felix Bloch") demonstrates [Bloch's theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloch%27s_theorem "Bloch's theorem").
    *   [John Lennard-Jones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Lennard-Jones "John Lennard-Jones") introduces the [linear combination of atomic orbitals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_combination_of_atomic_orbitals "Linear combination of atomic orbitals") (LCAO) approximation for the calculation of [molecular orbitals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_orbital "Molecular orbital").
    *   The [electron hole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron_hole "Electron hole") concept is pioneered by [Rudolf Peierls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudolf_Peierls "Rudolf Peierls") to understand [semiconductors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semiconductor "Semiconductor").
    *   Peierls coins the term [Umklapp scattering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umklapp_scattering "Umklapp scattering").[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-60)
    *   First observation of [plasma oscillations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasma_oscillation "Plasma oscillation") by [Irving Langmuir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irving_Langmuir "Irving Langmuir") and [Lewi Tonks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lewi_Tonks "Lewi Tonks").[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-61)
    *   [Egil Hylleraas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egil_Hylleraas "Egil Hylleraas") finds an approximate solution to the [helium atom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helium_atom "Helium atom").[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-62)

*   1930: 
    *   [Léon Brillouin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Brillouin "Léon Brillouin") develops the concept of [Brillouin zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brillouin_zone "Brillouin zone").
    *   Bloch introduces the theory of [spin waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin_wave "Spin wave") and [magnons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnon "Magnon"),
    *   [Erich Hückel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erich_H%C3%BCckel "Erich Hückel") introduces the [Hückel molecular orbital method](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%C3%BCckel_molecular_orbital_method "Hückel molecular orbital method"), which expands on orbital theory to determine the energies of orbitals of [pi electrons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pi_electrons "Pi electrons") in conjugated hydrocarbon systems.
    *   [Fritz London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fritz_London "Fritz London") explains [van der Waals forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_der_Waals_force "Van der Waals force") as due to the interacting fluctuating [dipole moments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bond_dipole_moment "Bond dipole moment") between molecules.
    *   Landau formulates the concept of [Landau quantization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landau_quantization "Landau quantization"), explaining the diamagnetic contribution of a free electron gas (Landau diamagnetism) and predicting the [De Haas–Van Alphen effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De_Haas%E2%80%93Van_Alphen_effect "De Haas–Van Alphen effect"). This effect was measured a few months after by [Wander Johannes de Haas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wander_Johannes_de_Haas "Wander Johannes de Haas") and his student Pieter M. van Alphen.

*   1931: 
    *   [Onsager reciprocal relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Onsager_reciprocal_relations "Onsager reciprocal relations") are first proposed by [Lars Onsager](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lars_Onsager "Lars Onsager")
    *   [Ralph Kronig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ralph_Kronig "Ralph Kronig") and [William Penney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Penney,_Baron_Penney "William Penney, Baron Penney") solve the infinite periodic array of rectangular potential barriers ([Kronig–Penney model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kronig%E2%80%93Penney_model "Kronig–Penney model")).
    *   [Alan Herries Wilson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Herries_Wilson "Alan Herries Wilson") develops the theory of [electronic band structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_band_structure "Electronic band structure") to describe the conduction properties of solids. He also distinguished between [intrinsic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intrinsic_semiconductor "Intrinsic semiconductor") and [extrinsic semiconductors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extrinsic_semiconductor "Extrinsic semiconductor").
    *   The concept of [excitons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exciton "Exciton") is proposed by [Yakov Frenkel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yakov_Frenkel "Yakov Frenkel").
    *   [John Lennard-Jones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Lennard-Jones "John Lennard-Jones") proposes the [Lennard-Jones interatomic potential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lennard-Jones_potential "Lennard-Jones potential").
    *   [Ernst Ruska](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernst_Ruska "Ernst Ruska") creates the first [electron microscope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron_microscope "Electron microscope").[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Peacock7-38)

*   1932 – [Werner Heisenberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Werner_Heisenberg "Werner Heisenberg") applies [perturbation theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perturbation_theory "Perturbation theory") to the two-electron problem to show how [resonance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resonance_(chemistry) "Resonance (chemistry)") arising from electron exchange can explain [exchange forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exchange_interaction "Exchange interaction").
*   1933: 
    *   [Walther Meissner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walther_Meissner "Walther Meissner") and [Robert Ochsenfeld](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Ochsenfeld "Robert Ochsenfeld") discover the [Meissner effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meissner_effect "Meissner effect") by measuring the magnetic field distribution outside superconducting tin and lead samples.
    *   Landau models [antiferromagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antiferromagnetism "Antiferromagnetism") for the first time.
    *   Landau introduces the concept of electron-phonon quasiparticle, termed [polaron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polaron "Polaron").
    *   [Erich Mollwo](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Erich_Mollwo&action=edit&redlink=1 "Erich Mollwo (page does not exist)")[[de](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erich_Mollwo "de:Erich Mollwo")], working with [Robert Wichard Pohl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Wichard_Pohl "Robert Wichard Pohl") concludes that color in [alkali metal halides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkali_metal_halide "Alkali metal halide") is due to the existence of [F-centers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F-center "F-center") (color centers).[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-63)

*   1935: 
    *   J.N. Rjabinin and [Lev Shubnikov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lev_Shubnikov "Lev Shubnikov") experimentally discover [type-II superconductivity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Type-II_superconductor "Type-II superconductor").[[64]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-64)[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-65)
    *   The [London equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London_equations "London equations") get developed by brothers [Fritz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fritz_London "Fritz London") and [Heinz London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinz_London "Heinz London").
    *   Hartree introduces [Hartree–Fock method](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hartree%E2%80%93Fock_method "Hartree–Fock method").[[66]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-66)

*   1937: 
    *   Landau introduces [Landau theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landau_theory "Landau theory") of [phase transitions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phase_transition "Phase transition").
    *   [Jan Hendrik de Boer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jan_Hendrik_de_Boer "Jan Hendrik de Boer") and [Evert Verwey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evert_Verwey "Evert Verwey"), and independently Peierls, and [Nevill Francis Mott](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nevill_Francis_Mott "Nevill Francis Mott") introduce the problem of the [metal–insulator transition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal%E2%80%93insulator_transition "Metal–insulator transition").[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:5-67)[[68]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-68) Other developments include the discovery of the [Verwey transition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verwey_transition "Verwey transition") and the theory of the [Mott insulator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mott_insulator "Mott insulator").[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:5-67)
    *   [Wannier functions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wannier_function "Wannier function") are introduced by [Gregory Wannier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gregory_Wannier "Gregory Wannier").
    *   [Conyers Herring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conyers_Herring "Conyers Herring") theorizes the possibility of [Weyl semimetals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weyl_semimetal "Weyl semimetal").[[69]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-69)

*   1938 – [Superfluidity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superfluid "Superfluid") is discovered by the team of [Pyotr Kapitsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyotr_Kapitsa "Pyotr Kapitsa").
*   1941 – Landau introduces the concept of [second sound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_sound "Second sound").[[70]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-70)
*   1944 – [Lars Onsager](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lars_Onsager "Lars Onsager") find an analytical solution for the 2D Ising model.
*   1947 – The first [transistor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transistor "Transistor") is developed by [William Shockley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shockley "William Shockley"), [John Bardeen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Bardeen "John Bardeen") and [Walter Houser Brattain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Houser_Brattain "Walter Houser Brattain").
*   1947 – The theory of single layer graphite ([graphene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphene "Graphene")) is first published by [P. R. Wallace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/P._R._Wallace "P. R. Wallace").
*   1948 – [Louis Néel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_N%C3%A9el "Louis Néel") discovers [ferrimagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferrimagnetism "Ferrimagnetism")
*   1945–1946 – First [neutron diffraction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neutron_diffraction "Neutron diffraction") experiments are carried out by [Ernest O. Wollan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernest_O._Wollan "Ernest O. Wollan") and independently by [Clifford Shull](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clifford_Glenwood_Shull "Clifford Glenwood Shull").
*   1947–1948 – [Hendrik Casimir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hendrik_Casimir "Hendrik Casimir") and [Dirk Polder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dirk_Polder "Dirk Polder") at [Philips Research Labs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philips_Natuurkundig_Laboratorium "Philips Natuurkundig Laboratorium") propose the existence of [Casimir–Polder effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casimir_effect "Casimir effect") between two polarizable atoms and between such an atom and a conducting plate.[[71]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:3-71)[[72]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:1-72) After a conversation with [Niels Bohr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niels_Bohr "Niels Bohr"), who suggested it had something to do with [zero-point energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-point_energy "Zero-point energy").
*   1947–1948 – The formal development of [quantum field theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_field_theory "Quantum field theory") by [Richard Feynman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Feynman "Richard Feynman"), [Julian Schwinger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_Schwinger "Julian Schwinger"), [Shin'ichirō Tomonaga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shin%27ichir%C5%8D_Tomonaga "Shin'ichirō Tomonaga") and [Freeman Dyson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freeman_Dyson "Freeman Dyson").
*   1949 – Werner Ehrenberg and [Raymond E. Siday](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R._E._Siday "R. E. Siday") first predict [Aharonov–Bohm effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aharonov%E2%80%93Bohm_effect "Aharonov–Bohm effect").[[73]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-73)

### Second half of the 20th century

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Second half of the 20th century")]

[![Image 4](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Liquid_helium_Rollin_film.jpg/250px-Liquid_helium_Rollin_film.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Liquid_helium_Rollin_film.jpg)

The [liquid helium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_helium "Liquid helium") is in the [superfluid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superfluidity "Superfluidity") phase. Discovered by [Pyotr Kapitsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyotr_Kapitsa "Pyotr Kapitsa") in 1938. First theoretically model with [Ginzburg–Landau theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ginzburg%E2%80%93Landau_theory "Ginzburg–Landau theory") in 1950.

*   1950 – The [Ginzburg–Landau theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ginzburg%E2%80%93Landau_theory "Ginzburg–Landau theory") phenomenological theory of superconductors is formulated by [Vitaly Ginzburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitaly_Ginzburg "Vitaly Ginzburg") and Landau.
*   1950 – Tomonaga introduces the [Luttinger liquid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luttinger_liquid "Luttinger liquid") model for electrons in 1D.
*   1952 – The [plasmon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasmon "Plasmon") (quantum of [plasma oscillation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasma_oscillation "Plasma oscillation") in metals) is proposed by [David Pines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Pines "David Pines") and [David Bohm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Bohm "David Bohm").
*   1952 – [Friedel oscillations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedel_oscillations "Friedel oscillations") are first described by [Jacques Friedel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Friedel "Jacques Friedel").
*   1953 – The occurrence of [Van Hove singularities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_Hove_singularity "Van Hove singularity") is first analyzed by [Léon Van Hove](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Van_Hove "Léon Van Hove") for the case of [phonon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phonon "Phonon") densities of states.
*   1953 – [Charles H. Townes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_H._Townes "Charles H. Townes"), [James P. Gordon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_P._Gordon "James P. Gordon"), and [Herbert Zeiger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbert_Zeiger "Herbert Zeiger") demonstrate the first [maser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maser "Maser").[[74]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:0-74)
*   1954: 
    *   [Lindhard theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lindhard_theory "Lindhard theory") for [electric-field screening](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric-field_screening "Electric-field screening") is published by [Jens Lindhard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jens_Lindhard "Jens Lindhard").
    *   The [tight-binding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tight_binding "Tight binding") method is conceived by [John Clarke Slater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_C._Slater "John C. Slater") and [George Fred Koster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Fred_Koster "George Fred Koster").[[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-SlaterKoster-75)
    *   [Bernd T. Matthias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernd_T._Matthias "Bernd T. Matthias") comes up with the empirical [Matthias rules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matthias_rules "Matthias rules") for finding superconductors.[[76]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-76)
    *   [Herbert Fröhlich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbert_Fr%C3%B6hlich "Herbert Fröhlich") introduces the [Frölich Hamiltonian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polaron "Polaron") for polarons.[[77]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-77)
    *   Publication of [_Dynamical Theory of Crystal Lattices_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamical_Theory_of_Crystal_Lattices "Dynamical Theory of Crystal Lattices") by [Max Born](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Born "Max Born") and [Huang Kun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huang_Kun "Huang Kun"), introducing [Born–Huang approximation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Born%E2%80%93Huang_approximation "Born–Huang approximation") and [Cauchy–Born rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cauchy%E2%80%93Born_rule "Cauchy–Born rule").

*   1954–1957 – [Malvin Ruderman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malvin_Ruderman "Malvin Ruderman") and [Charles Kittel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Kittel "Charles Kittel") develop the theory of indirect [exchange interaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exchange_interaction "Exchange interaction"), later expanded by Tadao Kasuya and Kei Yosida into the [RKKY theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RKKY_interaction "RKKY interaction").
*   1955 – [Dresselhaus spin–orbit coupling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dresselhaus_effect "Dresselhaus effect") is discovered by [Gene Dresselhaus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_Dresselhaus "Gene Dresselhaus").[[78]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-78)
*   1955 – [Takeo Matsubara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Takeo_Matsubara "Takeo Matsubara") introduces his [many-body Green's function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green%27s_function_(many-body_theory) "Green's function (many-body theory)") based on [Matsubara frequency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matsubara_frequency "Matsubara frequency") formalism.
*   1956 – Theory of interacting electrons in solids, [Fermi liquid theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermi_liquid_theory "Fermi liquid theory") is developed by Landau
*   1957: 
    *   [BCS theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BCS_theory "BCS theory") by Bardeen, [Leon Cooper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leon_Cooper "Leon Cooper") and [John Robert Schrieffer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Robert_Schrieffer "John Robert Schrieffer").
    *   [Rolf Landauer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolf_Landauer "Rolf Landauer"), who first suggested a version the [Landauer formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landauer_formula "Landauer formula").
    *   [Ryogo Kubo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryogo_Kubo "Ryogo Kubo") who first presents the [Kubo formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kubo_formula "Kubo formula"),[[79]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Kubo_I-79)[[80]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Kubo_II-80) to express the [linear response](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_response_function "Linear response function") of an observable quantity due to a time-dependent perturbation using quantum mechanics.
    *   [Jack Kilby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Kilby "Jack Kilby") proposes the first [integrated circuit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit "Integrated circuit").[[81]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-micromodules-81)

*   1957–1959 – Kubo, [Paul C. Martin](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Paul_C._Martin&action=edit&redlink=1 "Paul C. Martin (page does not exist)")[[de](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_C._Martin_(Physiker) "de:Paul C. Martin (Physiker)")] and Schwinger introduced the [KMS condition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KMS_state "KMS state") used it in 1959 to define thermodynamic Green's functions.
*   1958 – [Philip W. Anderson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_W._Anderson "Philip W. Anderson") starts developing the theory of metal-insulator transitions and [Anderson localization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anderson_localization "Anderson localization").
*   1958 – [John Hopfield](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Hopfield "John Hopfield") coins the [polariton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polariton "Polariton") in theory of [Hopfield dielectric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hopfield_dielectric "Hopfield dielectric").[[82]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-82)
*   1958–1960 – The first [laser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laser "Laser") is built by [Theodore Maiman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theodore_Maiman "Theodore Maiman") at [Hughes Aircraft Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hughes_Aircraft_Company "Hughes Aircraft Company"), based on a patent from Townes and [Arthur Leonard Schawlow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Leonard_Schawlow "Arthur Leonard Schawlow").[[74]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-:0-74)
*   1959 – [Rashba spin-orbit coupling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashba_effect "Rashba effect") is discovered by [Emmanuel Rashba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmanuel_Rashba "Emmanuel Rashba") and Valentin I. Sheka.[[83]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-83)
*   1961–1964 – Schwinger, O. V. Konstantinov and Vladimir I. Perel, [Leo Kadanoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_Kadanoff "Leo Kadanoff") and [Gordon Baym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gordon_Baym "Gordon Baym"), and [Leonid Keldysh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonid_Keldysh "Leonid Keldysh") independently develop [Keldysh formalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keldysh_formalism "Keldysh formalism").[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-84)
*   1962: 
    *   [Jeffrey Goldstone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeffrey_Goldstone "Jeffrey Goldstone"), [Yoichiro Nambu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoichiro_Nambu "Yoichiro Nambu"), [Abdus Salam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdus_Salam "Abdus Salam"), and [Steven Weinberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steven_Weinberg "Steven Weinberg") develop what is now known as [Goldstone's Theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goldstone_boson "Goldstone boson"): if there is a continuous symmetry transformation under which the Lagrangian is invariant, then either the vacuum state is also invariant under the transformation, or there must be spinless particles of zero mass, thereafter called [Nambu-Goldstone bosons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goldstone_boson "Goldstone boson").
    *   [Philip W. Anderson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_W._Anderson "Philip W. Anderson") proposes a [spontaneous symmetry breaking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spontaneous_symmetry_breaking "Spontaneous symmetry breaking") mechanism (later called [Higgs mechanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Higgs_mechanism "Higgs mechanism")) for superconductors.
    *   [Josephson effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josephson_effect "Josephson effect") of electron tunneling in superconductors is predicted by [Brian Josephson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Josephson "Brian Josephson").
    *   The [Little–Parks effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Little%E2%80%93Parks_effect "Little–Parks effect") is discovered by William A. Little and Ronald D. Parks.[[85]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Little-85)

*   1963 – [John Hubbard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Hubbard_(physicist) "John Hubbard (physicist)"), [Martin Gutzwiller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Gutzwiller "Martin Gutzwiller") and Junjiro Kanamori each independently propose the [Hubbard model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hubbard_model "Hubbard model").
*   1964 – [Jun Kondō](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jun_Kond%C5%8D "Jun Kondō") models the resistance minimum in metals leading to the [Kondo model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kondo_model "Kondo model") and the prediction of the [Kondo effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kondo_effect "Kondo effect"). The development of the [density functional theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Density_functional_theory "Density functional theory") starts with the theorems of [Walter Kohn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Kohn "Walter Kohn") and [Pierre Hohenberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Hohenberg "Pierre Hohenberg").
*   1966–1967: [Mermin–Wagner theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mermin%E2%80%93Wagner_theorem "Mermin–Wagner theorem") is proved by [N. David Mermin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N._David_Mermin "N. David Mermin"), [Herbert Wagner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbert_Wagner_(physicist) "Herbert Wagner (physicist)") and independently by Pierre Hohenberg.[[86]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-86)
*   1966–1968 – [Zhores Alferov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhores_Alferov "Zhores Alferov") and independently [Herbert Kroemer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbert_Kroemer "Herbert Kroemer") created the first lasers based on [heterostructures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heterojunction "Heterojunction").[[87]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Josephson2010-87)
*   1967 – [Volker Heine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volker_Heine "Volker Heine") coins the term ''condensed matter''.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-pwa-princeton-1)
*   1967 – [Negative-index materials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Negative-index_metamaterial "Negative-index metamaterial") are first described theoretically by [Victor Veselago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victor_Veselago "Victor Veselago").[[88]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-slyusarmeta-88)
*   1970 – French scientist [Madeleine Veyssié](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Madeleine_Veyssi%C3%A9&action=edit&redlink=1 "Madeleine Veyssié (page does not exist)")[[fr](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madeleine_Veyssi%C3%A9 "fr:Madeleine Veyssié")], coins the term soft matter ([French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language "French language"): _matière molle_).[[89]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-89)
*   1971: 
    *   The [spin Hall effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin_Hall_effect "Spin Hall effect") is predicted by [Mikhail I. Dyakonov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mikhail_Dyakonov "Mikhail Dyakonov") and Vladimir I. Perel.
    *   [Pierre-Gilles de Gennes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre-Gilles_de_Gennes "Pierre-Gilles de Gennes") introduces the [reptation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reptation "Reptation") model for [polymer physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymer_physics "Polymer physics").
    *   Polder and Michael Van Hove derive the theory for [near-field radiative heat transfer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Near-field_radiative_heat_transfer "Near-field radiative heat transfer") between arbitrary non-magnetic media.

*   1971–75 – [Michael Fisher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Fisher "Michael Fisher"), [Kenneth G. Wilson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenneth_G._Wilson "Kenneth G. Wilson"), and [Leo Kadanoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_Kadanoff "Leo Kadanoff") come up with the [renormalization group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renormalization_group "Renormalization group").
*   1972 – [David Lee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Lee_(physicist) "David Lee (physicist)"), [Douglas Osheroff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douglas_Osheroff "Douglas Osheroff") and [Robert Coleman Richardson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Coleman_Richardson "Robert Coleman Richardson") discovered two phase transitions of [helium-3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helium-3 "Helium-3") along the melting curve, which were soon realized to be the two superfluid phases.
*   1972 – The concept of [Berezinskii–Kosterlitz–Thouless phase transition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berezinskii%E2%80%93Kosterlitz%E2%80%93Thouless_transition "Berezinskii–Kosterlitz–Thouless transition") in the [XY model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_XY_model "Classical XY model") is developed by [Vadim Berezinskii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vadim_Berezinskii "Vadim Berezinskii"), [J. Michael Kosterlitz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._Michael_Kosterlitz "J. Michael Kosterlitz") and [David J. Thouless](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_J._Thouless "David J. Thouless").
*   1973 – [Peter Mansfield](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Mansfield "Peter Mansfield") formulates the physical theory of [nuclear magnetic resonance imaging](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_magnetic_resonance_imaging "Nuclear magnetic resonance imaging") (NMRI)[[90]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-90)[[91]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-91)[[92]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-92)[[93]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-93)
*   1979: 
    *   [Giorgio Parisi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giorgio_Parisi "Giorgio Parisi") finds a solution to Sherrington–Kirkpatrick model for [spin glasses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin_glass "Spin glass").
    *   [Su–Schrieffer–Heeger model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Su%E2%80%93Schrieffer%E2%80%93Heeger_model "Su–Schrieffer–Heeger model") is devised by Wu-Pei Su, [John Robert Schrieffer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Robert_Schrieffer "John Robert Schrieffer"), and [Alan J. Heeger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_J._Heeger "Alan J. Heeger") to describe the increase of electrical conductivity of [polyacetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyacetylene "Polyacetylene") polymer chain when doped.[[94]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-94)[[95]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-95)
    *   [Alexey Ekimov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexey_Ekimov "Alexey Ekimov") creates the first [quantum dots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_dot "Quantum dot") and their quantum size effects.[[96]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-96)

*   1980 – The integer [quantum Hall effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_Hall_effect "Quantum Hall effect") is discovered by [Klaus von Klitzing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Klaus_von_Klitzing "Klaus von Klitzing")
*   1980 – [Richard Feynman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Feynman "Richard Feynman") proposes [quantum computing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_computing "Quantum computing").
*   1981 – The [scanning tunneling microscope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scanning_tunneling_microscope "Scanning tunneling microscope") (STM), an instrument for imaging surfaces at the atomic level, was developed by [Gerd Binnig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gerd_Binnig "Gerd Binnig") and [Heinrich Rohrer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinrich_Rohrer "Heinrich Rohrer").
*   1982 – The [fractional quantum Hall effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fractional_quantum_Hall_effect "Fractional quantum Hall effect") is discovered by [Robert Laughlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Laughlin "Robert Laughlin"), [Horst Störmer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horst_St%C3%B6rmer "Horst Störmer"), and [Daniel Tsui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Tsui "Daniel Tsui").
*   1982 – First observation of a [quasicrystal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quasicrystal "Quasicrystal") by [Dan Shechtman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dan_Shechtman "Dan Shechtman").[[97]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-97)
*   1982 – [Frank Wilczek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Wilczek "Frank Wilczek") explores the fractional statistics of quasiparticles in two dimensions and coins the term "[anyon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anyon "Anyon")".
*   1985 – [Fullerene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullerene "Fullerene")[C 60](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buckminsterfullerene "Buckminsterfullerene") discovered by [Richard Smalley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Smalley "Richard Smalley"), [Robert Curl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Curl "Robert Curl"), and [Harry Kroto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Kroto "Harry Kroto").
*   1985 – [Patrick A. Lee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_A._Lee "Patrick A. Lee") and [A. Douglas Stone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A._Douglas_Stone "A. Douglas Stone") coin the term [universal conductance fluctuations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_conductance_fluctuations "Universal conductance fluctuations").[[98]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-98)
*   1986 – Binnig, [Calvin Quate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calvin_Quate "Calvin Quate") and [Christoph Gerber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christoph_Gerber "Christoph Gerber") invent the first [atomic force microscope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic_force_microscope "Atomic force microscope") (AFM).
*   1986 – Discovery of [high-temperature superconductivity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High-temperature_superconductivity "High-temperature superconductivity") by [K. Alex Müller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K._Alex_M%C3%BCller "K. Alex Müller") and [Georg Bednorz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georg_Bednorz "Georg Bednorz").
*   1987 – [Karl Alexander Müller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_Alexander_M%C3%BCller "Karl Alexander Müller") and [Georg Bednorz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georg_Bednorz "Georg Bednorz") discover [high-temperature superconductivity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High-temperature_superconductivity "High-temperature superconductivity") in ceramics.
*   1988 – [Giant magnetoresistance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giant_magnetoresistance "Giant magnetoresistance") is discovered by [Albert Fert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Fert "Albert Fert") and [Peter Grünberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Gr%C3%BCnberg "Peter Grünberg").
*   1988 – The [conductance quantum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conductance_quantum "Conductance quantum") are first demonstrated in [quantum point contacts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_point_contact "Quantum point contact").[[99]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-99)
*   1989 – [Jainendra K. Jain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jainendra_K._Jain "Jainendra K. Jain") proposes the concept of [composite fermions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Composite_fermion "Composite fermion").[[100]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-100)
*   1991 – [Carbon nanotubes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_nanotube "Carbon nanotube") are discovered by [Sumio Iijima](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumio_Iijima "Sumio Iijima")
*   1995 – Experimental [Bose–Einstein condensate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bose%E2%80%93Einstein_condensate "Bose–Einstein condensate") is first demonstrated by [Eric Cornell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_Allin_Cornell "Eric Allin Cornell"), [Carl Wieman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Wieman "Carl Wieman") and [Wolfgang Ketterle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfgang_Ketterle "Wolfgang Ketterle").
*   1997 – Experiment discovery of rare-earth oxides that behave as [spin ice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin_ice "Spin ice") by [Steven T. Bramwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steven_T._Bramwell "Steven T. Bramwell"), Mark Harris and collaborators, who also coined the term.[[101]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-101)
*   1998 – [Thomas Callister Hales](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Callister_Hales "Thomas Callister Hales") proves Kepler's conjecture.
*   1998 –[Lene Hau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lene_Hau "Lene Hau") produces [slow light](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slow_light "Slow light") with a speed of 17 m/s.[[102]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-102)

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Graphen.jpg/250px-Graphen.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Graphen.jpg)

[Graphene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphene "Graphene"): a single atomic layer of [graphite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphite "Graphite") first produced in 2004.

*   2000 – The [thermal conductance quantum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_conductance_quantum "Thermal conductance quantum") is first measured.[[103]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-Schwab-103)
*   2000 [Alexei Kitaev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexei_Kitaev "Alexei Kitaev") introduces the theory of the [Kitaev chain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kitaev_chain "Kitaev chain").[[104]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-104)
*   2001 – Attosecond pulsed sources are developed independently by [Pierre Agostini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Agostini "Pierre Agostini") and [Ferenc Krausz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferenc_Krausz "Ferenc Krausz"), leading to the development of [attosecond physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attosecond_physics "Attosecond physics").[[105]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-105)
*   2003 – [Deborah S. Jin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deborah_S._Jin "Deborah S. Jin") and her collaboration produce the first [fermionic condensate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermionic_condensate "Fermionic condensate").[[106]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-NASA-106)
*   2004 – Single-layer graphene was first unambiguously produced and identified by the group of [Andre Geim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andre_Geim "Andre Geim") and [Konstantin Novoselov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Konstantin_Novoselov "Konstantin Novoselov").[[107]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-107)
*   2005 – [Charles Kane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_L._Kane "Charles L. Kane") and [Gene Mele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_Mele "Eugene Mele") propose the [quantum spin Hall effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_spin_Hall_effect "Quantum spin Hall effect").[[108]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-108)
*   2008-2010 – Andreas P. Schnyder, [Shinsei Ryu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shinsei_Ryu "Shinsei Ryu"), Akira Furusaki and Andreas W. W. Ludwig;[[109]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-109)[[110]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-110) and well as [Alexei Kitaev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexei_Kitaev "Alexei Kitaev"),[[111]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-111) develop the [periodic table of topological matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Periodic_table_of_topological_invariants "Periodic table of topological invariants").
*   2013 – The [quantum anomalous Hall effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_anomalous_Hall_effect "Quantum anomalous Hall effect") is first observed by the team of [Xue Qikun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xue_Qikun "Xue Qikun").[[112]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-112)
*   2012 – Wilczek proposes the idea of [time crystals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_crystal "Time crystal").[[113]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-113)
*   2015 – [M. Zahid Hasan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M._Zahid_Hasan "M. Zahid Hasan")'s team demonstrates the existence of [Weyl semimetals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weyl_semimetal "Weyl semimetal").[[114]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-114)
*   2018 – Twisted graphene superconductivity is demonstrated by [Pablo Jarillo-Herrero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pablo_Jarillo-Herrero "Pablo Jarillo-Herrero").[[115]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-115)
*   2024 – [Altermagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Altermagnetism "Altermagnetism") is discovered in experiment.[[116]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_condensed_matter_physics#cite_note-116)
