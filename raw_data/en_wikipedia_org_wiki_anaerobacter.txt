They are anaerobic chemotrophs and are unusual spore-formers as they produce more than one spore per bacterial cell (up to five).

[ 2 ] They fix nitrogen . Their G+C content is 29%.

[ 3 ] Only one species of this genus ( Anaerobacter polyendosporus ) has been described.
