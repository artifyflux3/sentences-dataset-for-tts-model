Tedersoo et al. (2018) [ 1 ] Classes Neozygitomycetes Entomophthoromycetes Entomophthoromycota is a division of kingdom fungi . In 2007, it was placed at the taxonomic rank of subphylum in the most recent revision of the entire fungus kingdom .

[ 2 ] In 2012, it was raised to the rank of phylum as "Entomophthoromycota" in a scientific paper by Richard A. Humber 2012.

[ 3 ] Divided into three classes and six families ( Ancylistaceae , Basidiobolaceae , Completoriaceae , Entomophthoraceae , Meristacraceae , and Neozygitaceae ), it contains over 250 species that are mostly arthropod pathogens or soil- and litter -borne saprobes .

[ 4 ] Taxonomy [ edit ] Circumscribed by mycologist Richard Humber in 2012, it contains species formerly classified in the division Zygomycota . Basidiobolomycetes was transferred to Basidiobolomycota .

[ 1 ] Description [ edit ] Species in the Entomophthoromycota generally share several characteristics. Their vegetative cells are coenocytic ; sporulation occurs by production of forcibly discharged dispersive or infective conidia ; and their zygospores (which also function as resting spores ) are homothallic .
