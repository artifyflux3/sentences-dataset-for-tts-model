Naming [ edit ] BD+20 594b indicates that the planet circles a star found in the Bonner Durchmusterung catalogue, BD +20¬∞ 594 , the 594th entry in the +20-degree zone (declinations from +19 to +20 degrees), [ 2 ] and that it is the first planet discovered orbiting that star.

K2-56b indicates that the planet circles a star catalogued in the Kepler 2 mission catalogue (part of the extended K2 Kepler mission ), the 56th one in the catalogue, [ 3 ] and that it is the first planet discovered orbiting that star.

Planet [ edit ] With a radius of 2.2 R üú® and a mass of 16.31 M üú® , BD+20594b is substantially smaller than Neptune .

[ 4 ] Taking the estimates of its radius and mass at face value, the composition of the planet would be rocky, hence making it classified as a mega-Earth . BD+20594b's exact composition is still unknown.

The planet was discovered on January 28, 2016 by astrophysicist N√©stor Espinoza and his team from the Catholic University of Chile , using data from the two-wheeled Kepler mission (K2). It orbits a K-type star 496.08 light years away in the constellation Taurus .

[ 5 ] It is believed that planets with a radius greater than 1.6 times the Earth's are not usually rocky, [ 6 ] making BD+20594b an exception to this rule.
