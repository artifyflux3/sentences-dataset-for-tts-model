Egyptian scribe

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Rhind_Mathematical_Papyrus.jpg/250px-Rhind_Mathematical_Papyrus.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Rhind_Mathematical_Papyrus.jpg)

A portion of the [Rhind Mathematical Papyrus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhind_Mathematical_Papyrus "Rhind Mathematical Papyrus")

**Ahmes** ([Ancient Egyptian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian_language "Ancient Egyptian language"): _[jꜥḥ-ms](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/j%EA%9C%A5%E1%B8%A5-ms "wikt:jꜥḥ-ms")_ “, a common Egyptian name also transliterated **[Ahmose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmose_(disambiguation) "Ahmose (disambiguation)")**) was an [ancient Egyptian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egypt "Ancient Egypt")[scribe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scribe "Scribe") who lived towards the end of the [Fifteenth Dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/15th_Dynasty "15th Dynasty") (and of the [Second Intermediate Period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Intermediate_Period "Second Intermediate Period")) and the beginning of the [Eighteenth Dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eighteenth_dynasty_of_Egypt "Eighteenth dynasty of Egypt") (and of the [New Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Kingdom_of_Egypt "New Kingdom of Egypt")). He transcribed the [Rhind Mathematical Papyrus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhind_Mathematical_Papyrus "Rhind Mathematical Papyrus"), a work of [ancient Egyptian mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian_mathematics "Ancient Egyptian mathematics") that dates to approximately 1550 BC;[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmes#cite_note-1) he is the earliest contributor to [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics") whose name is known.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmes#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmes#cite_note-3) Ahmes claimed not to be the writer of the work but rather just the scribe. He claimed the material came from an even older document from around 2000 B.C.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmes#cite_note-4)
