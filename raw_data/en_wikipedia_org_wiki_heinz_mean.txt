For different values of x , this Heinz mean interpolates between the arithmetic ( x = 0) and geometric ( x = 1/2) means such that for 0 < x < ⁠ 1 / 2 ⁠ : A B = H 1 2 ⁡ ⁡ ( A , B ) < H x ⁡ ⁡ ( A , B ) < H 0 ⁡ ⁡ ( A , B ) = A + B 2 .

{\displaystyle {\sqrt {AB}}=\operatorname {H} _{\frac {1}{2}}(A,B)<\operatorname {H} _{x}(A,B)<\operatorname {H} _{0}(A,B)={\frac {A+B}{2}}.} The Heinz means appear naturally when symmetrizing α α {\textstyle \alpha } -divergences.

[ 3 ] It may also be defined in the same way for positive semidefinite matrices , and satisfies a similar interpolation formula.
