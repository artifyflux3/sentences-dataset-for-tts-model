From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

[![Image 1](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Diacetylene.svg/250px-Diacetylene.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Diacetylene.svg)

[Diacetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diacetylene "Diacetylene"), the simplest polyyne

A **polyyne**[[a]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-2) is any [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with alternating [single](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Single_bond "Single bond") and [triple bonds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triple_bond "Triple bond"); that is, a series of consecutive [alkynes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkyne "Alkyne"), (−C≡C−)_n_ with _n_ greater than 1. These compounds are also called [polyacetylenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyacetylene "Polyacetylene"), especially in the natural products and chemical ecology literature,[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-Blacklock2008-3) even though this nomenclature more properly refers to [acetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetylene "Acetylene") polymers composed of alternating single and double bonds (which are [polyenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyene "Polyene")) (−CR=CR′−)_n_ with _n_ greater than 1. They are also sometimes referred to as **oligoynes**,[[b]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-4)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-gibt-5) or **carbinoids** after "[carbyne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_acetylenic_carbon "Linear acetylenic carbon")" (−C≡C−)∞, the hypothetical allotrope of carbon that would be the ultimate member of the series.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-Heimann-6)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-chalif1-7) The synthesis of this substance has been claimed several times since the 1960s, but those reports have been disputed.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-kroto-8) Indeed, the substances identified as short chains of "carbyne" in many early organic synthesis attempts[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-akagi-9) would be called polyynes today.

The simplest polyyne is [diacetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diacetylene "Diacetylene") or butadiyne, H−C≡C−C≡C−H. Along with [cumulenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cumulene "Cumulene"), polyynes are distinguished from other organic chains by their rigidity and high conductivity,[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-Bryce_2021-10) both of which make them promising as wires in [molecular nanotechnology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_nanotechnology "Molecular nanotechnology"). Polyynes have been detected in interstellar [molecular clouds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud") where [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen") is scarce.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-interstellar-11)

The first reported synthesis of a polyyne was performed in 1869 by [Carl Andreas Glaser](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Carl_Andreas_Glaser&action=edit&redlink=1 "Carl Andreas Glaser (page does not exist)")[[de](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Glaser "de:Carl Glaser")], who observed that copper phenylacetylide (CuC≡C−C 6 H 5) undergoes [oxidative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxidation "Oxidation")[dimerization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimer_(chemistry) "Dimer (chemistry)") in the presence of air to produce diphenylbutadiyne (C 6 H 5−C≡C−C≡C−C 6 H 5).[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-chalif1-7)

Interest in these compounds has stimulated research into their preparation by [organic synthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_synthesis "Organic synthesis") by several general routes. As a main synthetic tool usually acetylene homocoupling reactions like the [Glaser coupling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glaser_coupling "Glaser coupling") or its associated Elinton and Hay protocols are used.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-12)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-chalif1-7) Moreover, many of such procedures involve a [Cadiot–Chodkiewicz coupling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cadiot%E2%80%93Chodkiewicz_coupling "Cadiot–Chodkiewicz coupling") or similar reactions to unite two separate alkyne building-blocks or by alkylation of a pre-formed polyyne unit.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-photopolymerizable-13) In addition to that, [Fritsch–Buttenberg–Wiechell rearrangement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fritsch%E2%80%93Buttenberg%E2%80%93Wiechell_rearrangement "Fritsch–Buttenberg–Wiechell rearrangement") was used as crucial step during the synthesis of the longest known polyyne (C 44).[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-chalif2-14) An elimination of chlorovinylsilanes was used as a final step in the synthesis of the longest known phenyl end-capped polyynes.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-%CE%B2-Chlorovinylsilanes_as_masked_alky-15)

### Organic and organosilicon polyynes

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Polyyne&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Organic and organosilicon polyynes")]

Using various techniques, polyynes H(−C≡C−)_n_ H with _n_ up to 4 or 5 were synthesized during the 1950s.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-walt1-16) Around 1971, [T. R. Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T._R._Johnson "T. R. Johnson") and [D. R. M. Walton](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=D._R._M._Walton&action=edit&redlink=1 "D. R. M. Walton (page does not exist)") developed the use of end-caps of the form –SiR 3, where R was usually an [ethyl group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethyl_group "Ethyl group"), to protect the polyyne chain during the chain-doubling reaction using [Hay's catalyst](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hay%27s_catalyst&action=edit&redlink=1 "Hay's catalyst (page does not exist)") (a [copper(I)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copper "Copper")–[TMEDA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetramethylethylenediamine "Tetramethylethylenediamine")[complex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coordination_compound "Coordination compound")).[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-walt1-16)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-walt2-17) With that technique they were able to obtain polyynes like (CH 3 CH 2)3 Si(−C≡C−)_n_ Si(CH 2 CH 3)3 with _n_ up to 8 in pure state, and with _n_ up to 16 in solution. Later Tykwinski and co-workers were able to obtain ((CH 3)2 CH)3 Si(−C≡C−)_n_ Si(CH(CH 3)2)3 polyynes with chain length up to C 20.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-eisler-18)

A polyyne compound with 10 acetylenic units (20 atoms), with the ends capped by Fréchet-type [aromatic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aromatic "Aromatic")[polyether](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyether "Polyether")[dendrimers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dendrimer "Dendrimer"), was isolated and characterized in 2002.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-gibt-5) Moreover, the synthesis of dicyanopolyynes with up to 8 acetylenic units was reported.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-19) The longest [phenyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenyl "Phenyl") end-capped polyynes were reported by Cox and co-workers in 2007.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-%CE%B2-Chlorovinylsilanes_as_masked_alky-15) As of 2010, the polyyne with the longest chain yet isolated had 22 acetylenic units (44 carbon atoms), end-capped with [tris(3,5-di-t-butylphenyl)methyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tris(3,5-di-t-butylphenyl)methyl&action=edit&redlink=1 "Tris(3,5-di-t-butylphenyl)methyl (page does not exist)") groups.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-chalif2-14)

[Alkynes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkyne "Alkyne") with the formula H(−C≡C−)_n_ H and _n_ from 2 to 6 can be detected in the decomposition products of partially oxidized [copper(I) acetylide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copper(I)_acetylide "Copper(I) acetylide") ((Cu+)2(−C≡C−) (an acetylene derivative known since 1856 or earlier) by [hydrochloric acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrochloric_acid "Hydrochloric acid"). A "carbonaceous" residue left by the decomposition also has the spectral signature of (−C≡C−)_n_ chains.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-cataldo-20)

[![Image 2](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Organic_polyynes.jpg/250px-Organic_polyynes.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Organic_polyynes.jpg)

Organic and [organosilicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organosilicon "Organosilicon") polyynes

[Organometallic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organometallic_chemistry "Organometallic chemistry") polyynes capped with metal complexes are well characterized. As of the mid-2010s, the most intense research has concerned [rhenium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhenium "Rhenium") (Re(−C≡C−)_n_ Re, _n_ = 3–10),[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-21)[ruthenium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruthenium "Ruthenium") (RuRu(−C≡C−)_n_ RuRu, _n_ = 4–10),[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-22)[iron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron "Iron") (Fe(−C≡C−)6 Fe),[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-23)[platinum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Platinum "Platinum") (Pt(−C≡C−)_n_ Pt, _n_ = 8–14),[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-24)[palladium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palladium "Palladium") (Ar(−C≡C−)_n_ Pd, _n_ = 3–5, Ar = [aryl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aryl "Aryl")),[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-25) and [cobalt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cobalt "Cobalt") (Co 3 C(−C≡C−)_n_ CCo 3, _n_ = 7–13)[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-26) complexes.

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Organometallic_polyynes.tif/lossy-page1-841px-Organometallic_polyynes.tif.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Organometallic_polyynes.tif)

Examples of known [organometallic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organometallic_chemistry "Organometallic chemistry") polyynes. 

Long polyyne chains are said to be inherently unstable in bulk because they can cross-link with each other exothermically.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-kroto-8) Explosions are a real hazard in this area of research.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-baughseek-27) They can be fairly stable, even against moisture and [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen"), if the end hydrogen atoms are replaced with a suitably inert [end-group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/End-group "End-group"), such as [_tert_-butyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Butyl_group "Butyl group") or [trifluoromethyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trifluoromethyl "Trifluoromethyl").[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-lagow-28) Bulky end-groups, that can keep the chains apart, work especially well at stabilizing polyynes.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-gibt-5) In 1995 the preparation of carbyne chains with over 300 carbon atoms was reported using this technique.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-lagow-28) However the report has been contested by a claim that the detected molecules were [fullerene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullerene "Fullerene")-like structures rather than long polyynes.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-kroto-8)

Polyyne chains have also been stabilised to heating by co-deposition with [silver nanoparticles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver_nanoparticle "Silver nanoparticle"),[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-29) and by complexation with a [mercury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercury_(element) "Mercury (element)")-containing [tridentate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tridentate_ligand "Tridentate ligand")[Lewis acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lewis_acid "Lewis acid") to form layered [adducts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adducts "Adducts").[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-30) Long polyyne chains encapsulated in double-walled [carbon nanotubes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_nanotubes "Carbon nanotubes") or in the form of [rotaxanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotaxanes "Rotaxanes")[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-31) have also been shown to be stable.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-32) Despite rather low stability of longer polyynes there are some examples of their use as synthetic precursors in organic and organometallic synthesis.[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-33)

Synthetic polyynes of the form R(−C≡C−)_n_ R, with _n_ about 8 or more, often have a smoothly curved or helical backbone in the crystalline solid state, presumably due to crystal packing effects.[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-34) For example, when the cap R is [triisopropylsilyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triisopropylsilyl "Triisopropylsilyl") and _n_ is 8, [X-ray crystallography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-ray_crystallography "X-ray crystallography") of the substance (a crystalline orange/yellow solid) shows the backbone bent by about 25–30 degrees in a broad arch, so that each C−C≡C angle deviates by 3.1 degrees from a straight line. This geometry affords a denser packing, with the bulky cap of an adjacent molecule nested into the concave side of the backbone. As a result, the distance between backbones of neighboring molecules is reduced to about 0.35 to 0.5 nm, near the range at which one expects spontaneous cross-linking. The compound is stable indefinitely at low temperature, but decomposes before melting. In contrast, the homologous molecules with _n_ = 4 or _n_ = 5 have nearly straight backbones that stay at least 0.5 to 0.7 nm apart, and melt without decomposing.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-eisler-18)

A wide range of organisms synthesize polyynes.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-Blacklock2008-3)[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-Shun-35) These chemicals have various biological activities, including as flavorings and pigments, chemical repellents and toxins, and potential application to biomedical research and pharmaceuticals. In plants, polyynes are found mainly in [Asterids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asterids "Asterids") clade, especially in the [sunflower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunflower "Sunflower"), [carrot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carrot "Carrot"), [ginseng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ginseng "Ginseng") and [bellflower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Campanula "Campanula") families. However, they can also be found in some members of the [tomato](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tomato "Tomato"), [olax](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olax "Olax"), and [sandalwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandalwood "Sandalwood") families.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-36) The earliest polyyne to be isolated was dehydromatricaria ester (DME) in 1826; however, it was not fully characterized until later.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-Blacklock2008-3)[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-37)

8,10-Octadecadiynoic acid

The simple fatty acid 8,10-octadecadiynoic acid is isolated from the root bark of the legume _[Paramacrolobium coeruleum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paramacrolobium\_coeruleum "Paramacrolobium coeruleum")_ of the family [Caesalpiniaceae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caesalpiniaceae "Caesalpiniaceae") and has been investigated as a [photopolymerizable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photopolymerisation "Photopolymerisation") unit in synthetic [phospholipids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phospholipid "Phospholipid").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-photopolymerizable-13)

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Thiarubrine_B.svg/250px-Thiarubrine_B.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Thiarubrine_B.svg)

[Thiarubrine B](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Thiarubrine_B&action=edit&redlink=1 "Thiarubrine B (page does not exist)")

[Thiarubrine B](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Thiarubrine_B&action=edit&redlink=1 "Thiarubrine B (page does not exist)") is the most prevalent among several related light-sensitive [pigments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pigment "Pigment") that have been isolated from the [giant ragweed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giant_ragweed "Giant ragweed") (_Ambrosia trifida_), a plant used in herbal medicine. The thiarubrines have antibiotic, antiviral, and [nematocidal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nematocidal "Nematocidal") activity, and activity against HIV-1 that is mediated by exposure to light.[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-38)

[Cicutoxin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicutoxin "Cicutoxin")

Polyynes such as [falcarindiol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falcarindiol "Falcarindiol") can be found in [Apiaceae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apiaceae "Apiaceae") vegetables like [carrot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carrot "Carrot"), [celery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celery "Celery"), [fennel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fennel "Fennel"), [parsley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parsley "Parsley") and [parsnip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parsnip "Parsnip") where they show cytotoxic activities.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-39) Aliphatic C 17-polyynes of the falcarinol type were described to act as metabolic modulators[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-40)[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-41) and are studied as potential health-promoting [nutraceuticals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nutraceutical "Nutraceutical").[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-42) Falcarindiol is the main compound responsible for bitterness in [carrots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carrots "Carrots"), and is the most active among several polyynes with potential anticancer activity found in [Devil's club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Devil%27s_club "Devil's club") (_Oplopanax horridus_). Other polyynes from [plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plant "Plant") include [oenanthotoxin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oenanthotoxin "Oenanthotoxin") and [cicutoxin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicutoxin "Cicutoxin"), which are poisons found in [water dropwort](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oenanthe_(plant) "Oenanthe (plant)") (_Oenanthe spp._) and [water hemlock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cicuta "Cicuta") (_Cicuta spp._).

[Ichthyothereol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ichthyothereol "Ichthyothereol")

_[Ichthyothere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ichthyothere "Ichthyothere")_ is a genus of plants whose active constituent is a polyyne called [ichthyothereol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ichthyothereol "Ichthyothereol"). This compound is highly [toxic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toxic "Toxic") to [fish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fish "Fish") and [mammals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mammals "Mammals").[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-CasconMors1965-43)_Ichthyothere terminalis_[leaves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leaf "Leaf") have traditionally been used to make poisoned bait by indigenous peoples of the lower [Amazon basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_basin "Amazon basin").[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-CasconMors1965-43)

[![Image 10](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Z-dihydromatricaria_acid.png/250px-Z-dihydromatricaria_acid.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Z-dihydromatricaria_acid.png)

_Z_-Dihydromatricaria acid

Dihydromatricaria acid is a polyyne produced and secreted by [soldier beetles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soldier_beetle "Soldier beetle") as a chemical defense.[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-44)

The [octatetraynyl radicals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octatetraynyl_radical "Octatetraynyl radical") and [hexatriynyl radicals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexatriynyl_radical "Hexatriynyl radical") together with their ions are detected in space where hydrogen is rare.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-interstellar-11) Moreover, there have been claims[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-45) that polyynes have been found in astronomical impact sites on Earth as part of the mineral [chaoite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chaoite "Chaoite"), but this interpretation has been contested.[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne#cite_note-46) See [Astrochemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrochemistry "Astrochemistry").
