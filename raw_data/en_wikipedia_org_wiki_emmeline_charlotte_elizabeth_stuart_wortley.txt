**Lady Emmeline Charlotte Elizabeth Stuart-Wortley** (née **Manners**; 1806 – 20 October 1855) was an English poet and writer, best known for her _Travels in the United States, etc. During 1849 and 1850_. She was editor of _[The Keepsake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Keepsake "The Keepsake")_ volumes for 1837 and 1840.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmeline_Charlotte_Elizabeth_Stuart-Wortley#cite_note-1)

She was a daughter of [John Manners, 5th Duke of Rutland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Manners,_5th_Duke_of_Rutland "John Manners, 5th Duke of Rutland"), and his wife, the former [Lady Elizabeth Howard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elizabeth_Manners,_Duchess_of_Rutland "Elizabeth Manners, Duchess of Rutland"). On 17 February 1831, married Hon. [Charles Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie "Charles Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie"), a son of [James Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie, 1st Baron Wharncliffe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Stuart-Wortley,_1st_Baron_Wharncliffe "James Stuart-Wortley, 1st Baron Wharncliffe"). They had a daughter, [Victoria, Lady Welby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victoria,_Lady_Welby "Victoria, Lady Welby").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmeline_Charlotte_Elizabeth_Stuart-Wortley#cite_note-schmitz1985-2)

After the death of her husband in 1844, she began to travel as a wealthy Victorian widow, along with her daughter, Victoria. In October 1855, she died of [dysentery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dysentery "Dysentery") in the area between Antioch and Beirut, while travelling through Ottoman Empire regions following the history of the early Christians.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmeline_Charlotte_Elizabeth_Stuart-Wortley#cite_note-significs-3)
