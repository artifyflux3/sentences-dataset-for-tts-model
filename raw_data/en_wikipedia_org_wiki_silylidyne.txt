**Silylidyne** is a chemical substance occurring as a molecule found in stars and probably existing in interstellar space, or as a [monolayer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monolayer "Monolayer") on the surface of solid [silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon "Silicon"). The **SiH** molecule is a [radical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_(chemistry) "Radical (chemistry)"), and can be made experimentally by striking an [electric arc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_arc "Electric arc") to silicon on a low pressure [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen") gas.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silylidyne#cite_note-cos-1)

As a surface layer, silicon hydrides form when the silicon is cleaned with hydrofluoric acid. These hydrides decompose to SiH when heated to 750 K.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silylidyne#cite_note-2) Other ways to coat a silicon surface in hydrogen is via reaction with [atomic hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic_hydrogen "Atomic hydrogen"), or hot [silane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silane "Silane").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silylidyne#cite_note-bent-3) A (111) crystal face will become covered in the pure monohydride, but other faces on a silicon crystal will also have the [dihydride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silylidene "Silylidene") and [trihydride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silyl "Silyl") groups.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silylidyne#cite_note-bent-3)

The silylidyne group, not to be confused with silylidyne, is a ≡SiH group that is bonded with a triple bond. The hydrogen can be substituted by other groups to make a more generic family of silylidynes. It is known as a ligand on molybdenum.

Silylidyne in space was first noticed in [sun spots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_spot "Sun spot") in 1933. Later on it was found on the solar disk, photosphere, and in cool stars. [Mira variable stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mira_variable_star "Mira variable star") of type [M](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M-type_star "M-type star") or S may have emission lines of SiH. In even cooler [brown dwarfs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brown_dwarf "Brown dwarf") and planets, SiH is not found, but instead silicon takes form as [silicon monoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon_monoxide "Silicon monoxide") at low pressures, and SiH 4 at high pressures. Minute amounts of SiH as a transient species may be found in these bodies as SiO and SiH 4 react with water.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silylidyne#cite_note-yur-4)

The Si-H bond strength is 80 kcal/mol.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silylidyne#cite_note-bent-3)

The most prominent spectral band is due to A 2 Δ → X 2 Π transitions. Higher level excited states are the B 2 Σ− and C 2 Σ+, D 2 Δ, E 2 Σ+.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silylidyne#cite_note-yur-4)

The life time of the molecules in the A state is 530 nanoseconds. They decay to the X ground state.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silylidyne#cite_note-yur-4)
