Chemical compound

**Aluminium monofluoride**, also known as **fluoridoaluminium**, is the [chemical compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_compound "Chemical compound") with the [formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula") AlF. This elusive species is formed by the reaction between [aluminium trifluoride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_fluoride "Aluminium fluoride") and metallic [aluminium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium "Aluminium") at elevated temperatures but quickly reverts to the reactants when cooled.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_monofluoride#cite_note-1)[Clusters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cluster_chemistry "Cluster chemistry") derived from related aluminium(I) [halides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halides "Halides") can be stabilized using specialized [ligands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ligand "Ligand").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_monofluoride#cite_note-2)

This molecule has been detected in the [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium"), where molecules are so dilute that intermolecular [collisions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collision_theory "Collision theory") are unimportant.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_monofluoride#cite_note-3)
