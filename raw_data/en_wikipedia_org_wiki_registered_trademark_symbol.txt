From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Typographical symbol (®)

For other uses, see [Trademark (disambiguation)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark_(disambiguation) "Trademark (disambiguation)").

Not to be confused with the character Ⓡ (see [Enclosed Alphanumerics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclosed_Alphanumerics "Enclosed Alphanumerics")) or ™ (the [trademark symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark_symbol "Trademark symbol")) or © (the [copyright symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyright_symbol "Copyright symbol")) or ℠ (the [service mark symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service_mark_symbol "Service mark symbol")).

The **registered trademark symbol**, **®**, is a [typographic symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typographic_symbol "Typographic symbol") that provides notice that the preceding word or symbol is a [trademark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark "Trademark") or [service mark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service_mark "Service mark") that has been registered with a national trademark office. A trademark is a symbol, word, or words legally registered or established by use as representing a company, product or service.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol#cite_note-ukipo-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol#cite_note-Sec_29-2)

Unregistered trademarks can instead be marked with the [trademark symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark_symbol "Trademark symbol"), ™, while unregistered service marks are marked with the [service mark symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service_mark_symbol "Service mark symbol"), ℠. The proper manner to display these symbols is immediately following the mark; the symbol is commonly in [superscript](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superscript "Superscript") style, but that is not legally required. In many jurisdictions, only registered trademarks confer easily defended legal rights.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol#cite_note-3)

In the US, the registered trademark symbol was originally introduced in the [Trademark Act of 1946](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanham_Act "Lanham Act").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol#cite_note-4)

Because the symbol is not commonly available on [typewriters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typewriter "Typewriter") (or [ASCII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASCII "ASCII")), it was common to approximate it with "(r)"[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol#cite_note-5) or "(R)"[[a]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol#cite_note-6) It is also legal in the US to use the text "Registered, U.S. Patent and Trademark Office" or "Reg U.S. Pat & TM Off."[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol#cite_note-Sec_29-2)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol#cite_note-7)

Computer usage
--------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Registered_trademark_symbol&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Computer usage")]

See also: [Unicode input](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode_input "Unicode input")

The registered trademark character was added to several [extended ASCII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extended_ASCII "Extended ASCII") character sets, including [ISO-8859-1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO-8859-1 "ISO-8859-1") from which it was inherited by [Unicode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode "Unicode") as U+00AE®REGISTERED SIGN.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol#cite_note-8)

*   _Warenzeichen_ grapheme, U+1F12E🄮CIRCLED WZ, used in some German publications, especially dictionaries, as informative and independent of the actual protection status of the name.
