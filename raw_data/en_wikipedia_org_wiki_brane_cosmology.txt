**Brane cosmology** refers to several theories in [particle physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particle_physics "Particle physics") and [cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_cosmology "Physical cosmology") related to [string theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/String_theory "String theory"), [superstring theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superstring_theory "Superstring theory") and [M-theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M-theory "M-theory").

Brane and bulk
--------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Brane_cosmology&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Brane and bulk")]

Main article: [Brane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane "Brane")

Animation showing multiple brane universes in the bulk

The central idea is that the visible, four-dimensional [spacetime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacetime "Spacetime") is restricted to a [brane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane "Brane") inside a [higher-dimensional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaluza%E2%80%93Klein "Kaluza–Klein") space, called the "bulk" (also known as "hyperspace"). If the additional [dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimension "Dimension") are [compact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compact_dimension "Compact dimension"), then the observed universe contains the extra dimension, and then no reference to the bulk is appropriate. In the bulk model, at least some of the extra dimensions are extensive (possibly infinite), and other branes may be moving through this bulk. Interactions with the bulk, and possibly with other branes, can influence our brane and thus introduce effects not seen in more standard cosmological models.

Why gravity is weak and the cosmological constant is small
----------------------------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Brane_cosmology&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Why gravity is weak and the cosmological constant is small")]

Some versions of brane cosmology, based on the [large extra dimension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_extra_dimension "Large extra dimension") idea, can explain the weakness of [gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravity "Gravity") relative to the other [fundamental forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fundamental_forces "Fundamental forces") of nature, thus solving the [hierarchy problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hierarchy_problem "Hierarchy problem"). In the brane picture, the [electromagnetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetism "Electromagnetism"), [weak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weak_interaction "Weak interaction") and [strong nuclear force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strong_interaction "Strong interaction") are localized on the brane, but gravity has no such constraint and propagates on the full spacetime, called the bulk. Much of the gravitational attractive power "leaks" into the bulk. As a consequence, the force of gravity should appear significantly stronger on small (subatomic or at least sub-millimetre) scales, where less gravitational force has "leaked". In [2004](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2004 "2004"), various experiments were under way to test this.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-1) Extensions of the large extra dimension idea with [supersymmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supersymmetry "Supersymmetry") in the bulk appear to be promising in addressing the so-called [cosmological constant problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmological_constant_problem "Cosmological constant problem").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-4)

Models of brane cosmology
-------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Brane_cosmology&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Models of brane cosmology")]

One of the earliest documented attempts to apply brane cosmology as part of a conceptual theory is dated to 1983.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-5)

The authors discussed the possibility that the Universe has (3+N)+1{\displaystyle (3+N)+1}![Image 10: {\displaystyle (3+N)+1}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f1e8e394e1686b7a53bfe06c51d15d3a71f68c88) dimensions, but ordinary particles are confined in a potential well which is narrow along N{\displaystyle N}![Image 11: {\displaystyle N}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f5e3890c981ae85503089652feb48b191b57aae3) spatial directions and flat along three others, and proposed a particular five-dimensional model.

In 1998/99, [Merab Gogberashvili](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Merab_Gogberashvili&action=edit&redlink=1 "Merab Gogberashvili (page does not exist)") published a number of articles on [arXiv](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ArXiv "ArXiv") where he showed that if we consider the universe as a thin shell (mathematical [synonym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synonym "Synonym") for "brane") expanding in 5-dimensional space then there is a possibility of obtaining a single scale for particle theory corresponding to the 5-dimensional [cosmological constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmological_constant "Cosmological constant") and the thickness of the universe, thus solving the [hierarchy problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hierarchy_problem "Hierarchy problem").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-7) Gogberashvili also showed that the four-dimensionality of the universe is the result of the [stability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stability_theory "Stability theory") requirement found in mathematics since the extra component of the [Einstein field equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_field_equations "Einstein field equations") giving the confined solution for [matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matter "Matter") fields coincides with one of the conditions of stability.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-8)

In 1999, there were proposed the closely related [Randall–Sundrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randall%E2%80%93Sundrum_model "Randall–Sundrum model") scenarios, RS1 and RS2. (See _[Randall–Sundrum model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randall%E2%80%93Sundrum\_model "Randall–Sundrum model")_ for a nontechnical explanation of RS1). These particular models of brane cosmology have attracted a considerable amount of attention. For instance, the related Chung-Freese model, which has applications for [spacetime metric engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Warp-field_experiments&action=edit&redlink=1 "Warp-field experiments (page does not exist)"), followed in 2000.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-9)

Later, the [ekpyrotic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ekpyrotic "Ekpyrotic") and [cyclic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclic_Model "Cyclic Model") proposals appeared. The ekpyrotic theory hypothesizes that the origin of the [observable universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Observable_universe "Observable universe") occurred when two parallel branes collided.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-10)

Empirical tests
---------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Brane_cosmology&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Empirical tests")]

See also: [Large extra dimension §Empirical tests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_extra_dimension#Empirical_tests "Large extra dimension")

As of now, no experimental or observational evidence of [large extra dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_extra_dimension "Large extra dimension"), as required by the Randall–Sundrum models, has been reported. An analysis of results from the [Large Hadron Collider](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_Hadron_Collider "Large Hadron Collider") in December 2010 severely constrains the black holes produced in theories with large extra dimensions.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-arxiv.org-11) The [recent multi-messenger gravitational wave event GW170817](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GW170817 "GW170817") has also been used to put weak limits on large extra dimensions.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-12)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brane_cosmology#cite_note-13)
