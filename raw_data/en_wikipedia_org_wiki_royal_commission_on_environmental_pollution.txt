( October 2011 ) The Royal Commission on Environmental Pollution in the United Kingdom was created under Royal Warrant in 1970 to advise the monarch , Government , Parliament and the public on environmental issues. It was closed on 1 April 2011, as part of the Coalition Government's spending cuts.

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] Overview [ edit ] The Commission's reports covered both the natural environment and the built environment , including topics such as nuclear power , energy use and climate change , the environmental impact of housing , and the use of pesticides and chemicals .

The Commission was a non-departmental public body sponsored by the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs , to whom it reported.
