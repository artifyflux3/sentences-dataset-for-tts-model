Coordinate system

Not to be confused with [Circle quadrant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circle_quadrant "Circle quadrant").

[![Image 7](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Cartesian_coordinates_2D.svg/250px-Cartesian_coordinates_2D.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Cartesian_coordinates_2D.svg)

The four quadrants of a Cartesian coordinate system

The axes of a two-dimensional [Cartesian system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartesian_coordinate_system "Cartesian coordinate system") divide the [plane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plane_(geometry) "Plane (geometry)") into four infinite [regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Region_(mathematics) "Region (mathematics)"), called **quadrants**, each bounded by two [half-axes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Half-axis "Half-axis"). The axes themselves are, in general, not part of the respective quadrants.

These are often numbered from 1st to 4th and denoted by [Roman numerals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_numerals "Roman numerals"): I (where the signs of the (_x_;_y_) coordinates are I (+;+), II (−;+), III (−;−), and IV (+;−). When the axes are drawn according to the mathematical custom, the numbering goes [counter-clockwise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clockwise "Clockwise") starting from the upper right ("northeast") quadrant.

Mnemonic
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Quadrant_(plane_geometry)&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Mnemonic")]

In the above graphic, the words in quotation marks are a [mnemonic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mnemonic "Mnemonic") for remembering which three trigonometric functions (sine, cosine, tangent and their reciprocals) are positive in each quadrant. The expression reads "All Science Teachers Crazy" and proceeding counterclockwise from the upper right quadrant, we see that "All" functions are positive in quadrant I, "Science" (for sine) is positive in quadrant II, "Teachers" (for tangent) is positive in quadrant III, and "Crazy" (for cosine) is positive in quadrant IV. There are several variants of [this mnemonic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mnemonics_in_trigonometry "Mnemonics in trigonometry").
