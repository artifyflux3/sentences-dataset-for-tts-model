From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This is a **timeline of subatomic particle discoveries**, including all particles thus far discovered which appear to be [elementary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elementary_particle "Elementary particle") (that is, indivisible) given the best available evidence. It also includes the discovery of [composite particles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Composite_particle "Composite particle") and [antiparticles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antiparticle "Antiparticle") that were of particular historical importance.

More specifically, the inclusion criteria are:

*   Elementary particles from the [Standard Model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_Model "Standard Model") of [particle physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particle_physics "Particle physics") that have so far been observed. The Standard Model is the most comprehensive existing model of particle behavior. All Standard Model particles including the [Higgs boson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Higgs_boson "Higgs boson") have been verified, and all other observed particles are combinations of two or more Standard Model particles.
*   Antiparticles which were historically important to the development of particle physics, specifically the [positron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Positron "Positron") and [antiproton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antiproton "Antiproton"). The discovery of these particles required very different experimental methods from that of their ordinary matter counterparts, and provided evidence that _all_ particles had antiparticles—an idea that is fundamental to [quantum field theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_field_theory "Quantum field theory"), the modern mathematical framework for particle physics. In the case of most subsequent particle discoveries, the particle and its anti-particle were discovered essentially simultaneously.
*   Composite particles which were the first particle discovered containing a particular elementary constituent, or whose discovery was critical to the understanding of particle physics.

| Time | Event |
| --- | --- |
| 1800 | [William Herschel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Herschel "William Herschel") discovers "heat rays" (now known as [infrared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrared "Infrared")) |
| 1801 | [Johann Wilhelm Ritter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Wilhelm_Ritter "Johann Wilhelm Ritter") made the hallmark observation that invisible rays just beyond the violet end of the visible spectrum were especially effective at lightening [silver chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver_chloride "Silver chloride")-soaked paper. He called them "[de-oxidizing rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De-oxidizing_rays "De-oxidizing rays")" to emphasize [chemical reactivity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reactivity_(chemistry) "Reactivity (chemistry)") and to distinguish them from "heat rays" at the other end of the invisible spectrum (both of which were later determined to be [photons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photons "Photons")). The more general term "chemical rays" was adopted shortly thereafter to describe the oxidizing rays, and it remained popular throughout the 19th century. The terms chemical and heat rays were eventually dropped in favor of **[ultraviolet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet "Ultraviolet")** and **[infrared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrared "Infrared")**[radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiation "Radiation"), respectively.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-hockberger-1) |
| 1895 | Discovery of the ultraviolet radiation below 200 nm, named **[vacuum ultraviolet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vacuum_ultraviolet "Vacuum ultraviolet")** (later identified as [photons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photons "Photons")) because it is strongly absorbed by air, by the German physicist [Victor Schumann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victor_Schumann "Victor Schumann")[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-Lyman-2) |
| 1895 | **[X-ray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-ray "X-ray")** produced by [Wilhelm Röntgen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilhelm_R%C3%B6ntgen "Wilhelm Röntgen") (later identified as [photons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photon "Photon"))[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-3) |
| 1897 | **[Electron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron "Electron")** discovered by [J. J. Thomson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._J._Thomson "J. J. Thomson")[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-4) |
| 1899 | **[Alpha particle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpha_particle "Alpha particle")** discovered by [Ernest Rutherford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernest_Rutherford "Ernest Rutherford") in [uranium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uranium "Uranium")[radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiation "Radiation")[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-5) |
| 1900 | **[Gamma ray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamma_ray "Gamma ray")** (a high-energy [photon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photon "Photon")) discovered by [Paul Villard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Ulrich_Villard "Paul Ulrich Villard") in uranium decay[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-6) |
| 1911 | **[Atomic nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic_nucleus "Atomic nucleus")** identified by [Ernest Rutherford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernest_Rutherford "Ernest Rutherford"), based on [scattering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scattering "Scattering") observed by [Hans Geiger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hans_Geiger "Hans Geiger") and [Ernest Marsden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernest_Marsden "Ernest Marsden")[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-7) |
| 1919 | **[Proton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proton "Proton")** discovered by [Ernest Rutherford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernest_Rutherford "Ernest Rutherford")[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-8) |
| 1931 | **[Deuteron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deuterium "Deuterium")** discovered by [Harold Urey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harold_Urey "Harold Urey")[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-10) (predicted by Rutherford in 1920[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-Rutherford-11)) |
| 1932 | **[Neutron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neutron "Neutron")** discovered by [James Chadwick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Chadwick "James Chadwick")[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-12) (predicted by Rutherford in 1920[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-Rutherford-11)) Main article: [Discovery of the neutron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_the_neutron "Discovery of the neutron") |
| 1932 | **[Antielectron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antielectron "Antielectron")** (or **positron**), the first antiparticle, discovered by [Carl D. Anderson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_David_Anderson "Carl David Anderson")[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-13) (proposed by [Paul Dirac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Dirac "Paul Dirac") in 1927 and by [Ettore Majorana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ettore_Majorana "Ettore Majorana") in 1928) |
| 1937 | **[Muon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muon "Muon")** (or **mu lepton**) discovered by [Seth Neddermeyer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seth_Neddermeyer "Seth Neddermeyer"), Carl D. Anderson, J.C. Street, and E.C. Stevenson, using [cloud chamber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloud_chamber "Cloud chamber") measurements of [cosmic rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_ray "Cosmic ray")[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-14) (it was mistaken for the [pion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pion "Pion") until 1947[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-15)) |
| 1947 | **[Pion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pion "Pion")** (or **pi meson**) discovered by [C. F. Powell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C._F._Powell "C. F. Powell")'s group, including [César Lattes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Lattes "César Lattes") (first author) and [Giuseppe Occhialini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giuseppe_Occhialini "Giuseppe Occhialini") (predicted by [Hideki Yukawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hideki_Yukawa "Hideki Yukawa") in 1935[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-16)) |
| 1947 | **[Kaon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaon "Kaon")** (or **K meson**), the first [strange particle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strange_particle "Strange particle"), discovered by [George Dixon Rochester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Dixon_Rochester "George Dixon Rochester") and [Clifford Charles Butler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clifford_Charles_Butler "Clifford Charles Butler")[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-17) |
| 1950 | **[Λ 0](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lambda_baryon "Lambda baryon")** (or **lambda baryon**) discovered during a study of cosmic-ray interactions[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-18) |
| 1955 | **[Antiproton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antiproton "Antiproton")** discovered by [Owen Chamberlain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Owen_Chamberlain "Owen Chamberlain"), [Emilio Segrè](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emilio_Segr%C3%A8 "Emilio Segrè"), [Clyde Wiegand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clyde_Wiegand "Clyde Wiegand"), and [Thomas Ypsilantis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Ypsilantis "Thomas Ypsilantis")[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-19) |
| 1956 | **[Electron neutrino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron_neutrino "Electron neutrino")** detected by [Frederick Reines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_Reines "Frederick Reines") and [Clyde Cowan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clyde_Cowan "Clyde Cowan") (proposed by [Wolfgang Pauli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfgang_Pauli "Wolfgang Pauli") in 1930 to explain the apparent violation of [conservation of energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_of_energy "Conservation of energy") in beta decay)[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-20) At the time it was simply referred to as _neutrino_ since there was only one known neutrino. Main article: [Cowan–Reines neutrino experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cowan%E2%80%93Reines_neutrino_experiment "Cowan–Reines neutrino experiment") |
| 1962 | **[Muon neutrino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muon_neutrino "Muon neutrino")** (or **mu neutrino**) shown to be distinct from the electron neutrino by a group headed by [Leon Lederman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leon_Lederman "Leon Lederman")[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-21) |
| 1964 | **[Omega baryon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omega_baryon "Omega baryon")**[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-22) and **[Xi baryon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_baryon "Xi baryon")** discovery at [Brookhaven National Laboratory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brookhaven_National_Laboratory "Brookhaven National Laboratory")[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-23) |
| 1969 | **[Partons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parton_(particle_physics) "Parton (particle physics)")** (internal constituents of [hadrons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadron "Hadron")) observed in [deep inelastic scattering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_inelastic_scattering "Deep inelastic scattering") experiments between [protons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proton "Proton") and [electrons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron "Electron") at [SLAC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SLAC "SLAC");[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-24)[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-25) this was eventually associated with the [quark model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quark_model "Quark model") (predicted by [Murray Gell-Mann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murray_Gell-Mann "Murray Gell-Mann") and [George Zweig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Zweig "George Zweig") in 1964) and thus constitutes the discovery of the **[up quark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Up_quark "Up quark")**, **[down quark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Down_quark "Down quark")**, and **[strange quark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strange_quark "Strange quark")**. |
| 1974 | **[J/ψ meson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J/psi_meson "J/psi meson")** discovered by groups headed by [Burton Richter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burton_Richter "Burton Richter") and [Samuel Ting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samuel_Ting "Samuel Ting"), demonstrating the existence of the **[charm quark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charm_quark "Charm quark")**[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-26)[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-27) (proposed by [James Bjorken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Bjorken "James Bjorken") and [Sheldon Glashow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheldon_Glashow "Sheldon Glashow") in 1964[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-28)) |
| 1975 | **[Tau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tau_(particle) "Tau (particle)")** discovered by a group headed by [Martin Perl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Perl "Martin Perl")[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-29) |
| 1977 | **[Upsilon meson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upsilon_meson "Upsilon meson")** discovered at [Fermilab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermilab "Fermilab"), demonstrating the existence of the **[bottom quark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bottom_quark "Bottom quark")**[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-30) (proposed by [Kobayashi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makoto_Kobayashi_(physicist) "Makoto Kobayashi (physicist)") and [Maskawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toshihide_Maskawa "Toshihide Maskawa") in 1973) |
| 1979 | **[Gluon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gluon "Gluon")** observed indirectly in [three-jet events](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-jet_event "Three-jet event") at [DESY](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DESY "DESY")[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-31) |
| 1983 | **[W and Z bosons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W_and_Z_bosons "W and Z bosons")** discovered by [Carlo Rubbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlo_Rubbia "Carlo Rubbia"), [Simon van der Meer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simon_van_der_Meer "Simon van der Meer"), and the CERN [UA1 collaboration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UA1_experiment "UA1 experiment")[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-32)[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-33) (predicted in detail by [Sheldon Glashow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheldon_Glashow "Sheldon Glashow"), [Mohammad Abdus Salam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdus_Salam "Abdus Salam"), and [Steven Weinberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steven_Weinberg "Steven Weinberg")) |
| 1995 | **[Top quark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Top_quark "Top quark")** discovered at [Fermilab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermilab "Fermilab")[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-34)[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-35) |
| 1995 | **[Antihydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antihydrogen "Antihydrogen")** produced and measured by the [LEAR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LEAR "LEAR") experiment at [CERN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CERN "CERN")[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-36) |
| 2000 | **[Quark-gluon fireball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quark%E2%80%93gluon_plasma "Quark–gluon plasma")** discovered at [CERN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CERN "CERN")[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-37) |
| 2000 | **[Tau neutrino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tau_neutrino "Tau neutrino")** first observed directly at [Fermilab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermilab "Fermilab")[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-38) |
| 2011 | **[Antihelium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antihelium "Antihelium")-4** produced and measured by the [STAR detector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/STAR_detector "STAR detector"); the first particle to be discovered by the experiment |
| 2012 | A particle exhibiting most of the predicted characteristics of the **[Higgs boson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Higgs_boson "Higgs boson")** discovered by researchers conducting the [Compact Muon Solenoid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compact_Muon_Solenoid "Compact Muon Solenoid") and [ATLAS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ATLAS_experiment "ATLAS experiment") experiments at CERN's [Large Hadron Collider](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_Hadron_Collider "Large Hadron Collider")[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_particle_discoveries#cite_note-39) |
