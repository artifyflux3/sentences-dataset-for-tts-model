13 May] 1866) was a Russian mathematician of Jewish-Austrian origin.

[ 1 ] He was a student of Joseph Johann Littrow , and the advisor of Pafnuty Chebyshev and August Davidov .

[ 2 ] He was born in Neu-Raußnitz (today Rousínov in Czech Republic , then in Austrian Empire ) and studied at the University of Vienna and Vienna Polytechnic Institute . In 1824 he moved to Saint Petersburg and then accepted a position at the Kazan University . In 1834 he became a professor of applied mathematics at the Moscow University . There he is best remembered as a founder of the Moscow Mathematical Society and its journal Matematicheskii Sbornik .

[ 3 ] For his mechanics textbook, in 1836 Brashman was awarded the Demidov Prize by the Russian Academy of Sciences . The academy elected him a corresponding member in 1855. He died in Moscow in 1866.
