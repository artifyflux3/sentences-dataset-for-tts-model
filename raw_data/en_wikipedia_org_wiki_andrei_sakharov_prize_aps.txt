The Andrei Sakharov Prize is a prize that is to be awarded every two years by the American Physical Society since 2006. The recipients are chosen for " outstanding leadership and/or achievements of scientists in upholding human rights .

" It is named after Andrei Sakharov (1921-1989), a Soviet nuclear physicist , dissident, and human rights activist. Since 2007, it has been valued at $10,000.

[ 1 ] The first Sakharov Prize was awarded to physicist and former Soviet gulag prisoner Yuri Orlov.
