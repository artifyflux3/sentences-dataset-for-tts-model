Mice that flew to the Moon on Apollo 17

[![Image 10](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Apollo_17_CM_Houston.jpg/250px-Apollo_17_CM_Houston.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Apollo_17_CM_Houston.jpg)

Five mice and three astronauts traveled to the Moon and returned to Earth in [Apollo 17](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_17 "Apollo 17")'s [Command Module _America_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_command_module "Apollo command module"), now on display at [Space Center Houston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_Center_Houston "Space Center Houston").

[![Image 11](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Ronald_Evans.jpg/250px-Ronald_Evans.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Ronald_Evans.jpg)

NASA astronaut [Ronald Evans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ronald_Evans_(astronaut) "Ronald Evans (astronaut)") and the mice orbited the Moon together for over six days in 1972.

**Fe**, **Fi**, **Fo**, **Fum**, and **Phooey** were five [mice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mouse "Mouse") who traveled to the [Moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon "Moon") and circled it 75 times on the 1972 [Apollo 17](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_17 "Apollo 17") mission. NASA gave them identification numbers **A3305**, **A3326**, **A3352**, **A3356**, and **A3400**, and their nicknames were given by the Apollo 17 crew ([Eugene Cernan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_Cernan "Eugene Cernan"), [Harrison Schmitt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harrison_Schmitt "Harrison Schmitt"), and [Ronald Evans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ronald_Evans_(astronaut) "Ronald Evans (astronaut)")). The four male mice, one female mouse, and Evans orbited the Moon for a record-setting six days and four hours in the [Apollo command module](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_command_module "Apollo command module")_America_ as Cernan and Schmitt performed the [Apollo program](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_program "Apollo program")'s last lunar excursions.

The mice travelled in individual compartments of tubes inside an aluminium container with "a sufficient food supply, temperature control, and a reserve of [potassium superoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potassium_superoxide "Potassium superoxide") that absorbed the CO 2 from their respiration and provided them with fresh oxygen."[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey#cite_note-1) One of the male mice died (A-3352[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey#cite_note-bioCoreSP368-2)) during the trip, and the four survivors were euthanized and dissected for their expected biological information upon their return from the Moon.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey#cite_note-3)

The three [astronauts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronauts "Astronauts") and the five mice were the last Earthlings to travel to and orbit the Moon.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey#cite_note-4) Evans and the five mice share two living-being [spaceflight records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_spaceflight_records "List of spaceflight records"), the longest amount of time spent in lunar orbit (147 hours 43 minutes), and the most lunar orbits completed (75).[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey#cite_note-5)

Mission
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Mission")]

Apollo 17 launched December 7, 1972, and returned to Earth on December 19. A biological cosmic ray experiment (BIOCORE) carried the five [pocket mice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Little_pocket_mouse "Little pocket mouse") (_Perognathus longimembris_), a species chosen for the experiment because they had well documented biological responses. Some advantages of the species included their small size, ease of maintenance in an isolated state (requiring no drinking water for the expected duration of the mission and producing highly concentrated waste), and their proven capability of withstanding environmental stress.

Fe, Fi, Fo, Fum, and Phooey had been implanted with radiation monitors under their scalps to see whether they would suffer damage from cosmic rays.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey#cite_note-biocoreSPP330-6) Four of the five mice survived the flight; the cause of death of the fifth was not determined.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey#cite_note-biocoreSPP330-6)

After their return to Earth, the four remaining live mice were euthanized and dissected. Although [lesions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lesion "Lesion") in the scalp and liver were detected, they appeared to be unrelated to one another and were not thought to be the result of cosmic rays. No damage was found in the mice's [retinas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retinas "Retinas") or [viscera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viscera "Viscera").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey#cite_note-biocoreSPP330-6) At the time of the publication of the Apollo 17 Preliminary Science Report the mice's brains had not yet been examined,[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey#cite_note-biocoreSPP330-6) but subsequent studies showed no significant effect on their brains.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fe,_Fi,_Fo,_Fum,_and_Phooey#cite_note-bioCoreSP368-2)
