**Cyanamide** is an [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the [formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula")[C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon")[N](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen "Nitrogen")2[H](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen")2. This white solid is widely used in agriculture and the production of pharmaceuticals and other organic compounds. It is also used as an [alcohol-deterrent drug](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcohol-deterrent_drug "Alcohol-deterrent drug"). The molecule features a [nitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrile "Nitrile") group attached to an [amino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino "Amino") group. Derivatives of this compound are also referred to as cyanamides, the most common being [calcium cyanamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calcium_cyanamide "Calcium cyanamide") (CaCN 2).[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Ullmann-3)

Tautomers and self-condensations
--------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cyanamide&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Tautomers and self-condensations")]

Containing both a nucleophilic and electrophilic site within the same molecule, cyanamide undergoes various reactions with itself. Cyanamide exists as two [tautomers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tautomer "Tautomer"), one with the connectivity N≡C–NH 2 and the other with the formula HN=C=NH ("[carbodiimide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbodiimide "Carbodiimide")" tautomer). The N≡C–NH 2 form dominates, but in a few reactions (e.g. [silylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silylation "Silylation")) the diimide form appears to be important.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Ullmann-3)

Cyanamide dimerizes to give [2-cyanoguanidine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2-Cyanoguanidine "2-Cyanoguanidine") (dicyandiamide). This dimerization is hindered or reversed by acids and is inhibited by low temperatures. The cyclic [trimer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trimer_(chemistry) "Trimer (chemistry)") is called [melamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melamine "Melamine").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Ullmann-3)

Cyanamide is produced by hydrolysis of [calcium cyanamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calcium_cyanamide "Calcium cyanamide"), which in turn is prepared from [calcium carbide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calcium_carbide "Calcium carbide") via the [Frank–Caro process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank%E2%80%93Caro_process "Frank–Caro process").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-4)

CaCN 2 + H 2 O + CO 2 → CaCO 3 + H 2 NCN
The conversion is conducted on slurries.

Cyanamide can be regarded as a functional single carbon fragment which can react as an [electrophile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrophile "Electrophile") or [nucleophile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleophile "Nucleophile"). The main reaction exhibited by cyanamide involves additions of compounds containing an acidic proton. Water, hydrogen sulfide, and hydrogen selenide react with cyanamide to give [urea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urea "Urea"), [thiourea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiourea "Thiourea"), and [selenourea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selenourea "Selenourea"), respectively:

H 2 NCN + H 2 E → H 2 NC(E)NH 2 (E = O, S, Se)
In this way, cyanamide behaves as a dehydration agent and thus can induce condensation reactions. Alcohols, thiols, and amines react analogously to give alkylisoureas, isothioureas, and [guanidines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guanidine "Guanidine"). The anti-ulcer drug [cimetidine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cimetidine "Cimetidine") is generated using such reactivity. Related reactions exploit the bifunctionality of cyanamide to give [heterocycles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heterocycle "Heterocycle"), and this latter reactivity is the basis of several pharmaceutical syntheses such as the aminopyrimidine [imatinib](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imatinib "Imatinib"), and agrichemicals [Amitrol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amitrol "Amitrol") and [hexazinone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexazinone "Hexazinone"). The hair-loss treatment [minoxidil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minoxidil "Minoxidil") and the [anthelmintics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthelmintic "Anthelmintic")[albendazole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albendazole "Albendazole"), [flubendazole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flubendazole "Flubendazole"), and [mebendazole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mebendazole "Mebendazole") feature 2-aminoimidazole substructures derived from cyanamide.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Ullmann-3) Cyanamide is also used in the synthesis of other pharmaceutical drugs including [tirapazamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tirapazamine "Tirapazamine"), [etravirine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Etravirine "Etravirine"), [revaprazan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revaprazan "Revaprazan"), and dasantafil.

The cyanamide [anion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anion "Anion") has the character of a pseudo [chalcogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chalcogen "Chalcogen"), cyanamide can therefore be regarded as analogue to [water](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water "Water") or [hydrogen sulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_sulfide "Hydrogen sulfide").

A convenient method for the preparation of secondary amines which are not contaminated with primary or tertiary amines is the reaction of cyanamide with alkyl halides to _N,N_-dialkylcyanamides which can easily be hydrolyzed to dialkylamines and then decarboxylated.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-5) Cyanamide adds itself in the presence of N-bromosuccinimide to olefinic double bonds. The addition product is converted by bases to N-Cyanaziridine,[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-6) cyclized in the presence of acids to imidazolines, which can be further reacted to vicinal diamines by alkaline cleavage.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-7)

Cyanamide is also a versatile synthetic building block for [heterocycles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heterocyclic_compound "Heterocyclic compound"): it forms 2-aminobenzimidazole with [1,2-diaminobenzene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/O-Phenylenediamine "O-Phenylenediamine")[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-8) and it forms with the readily available cyclic [enamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enamine "Enamine") 4-(1-cyclohexenyl)morpholine[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-9) and with elemental [sulfur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfur "Sulfur") a 2-aminothiazole in good yields.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-10)

Sodium dicyanamide is available in good yield and high purity from cyanamid and [cyanogen chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanogen_chloride "Cyanogen chloride"),[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-allyl-11)[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-12) which is suitable as an [intermediate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reaction_intermediate#Chemical_Processing_Industry "Reaction intermediate") for the synthesis of active pharmaceutical ingredients.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-13) A guanidino group is introduced by reaction of cyanamide with [sarcosine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarcosine "Sarcosine") In the industrial synthesis of [creatine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creatine "Creatine"):.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-14)

This synthesis route mostly avoids problematic impurities like [chloroacetic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroacetic_acid "Chloroacetic acid"), [iminodiacetic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iminodiacetic_acid "Iminodiacetic acid"), or [dihydrotriazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Dihydrotriazine&action=edit&redlink=1 "Dihydrotriazine (page does not exist)") that occur in other routes. The physiological precursor [guanidinoacetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycocyamine "Glycocyamine") is obtained analogously by reacting cyanamide with [glycine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycine "Glycine").

Methods to [stabilize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stabilizer_(chemistry) "Stabilizer (chemistry)") cyanamide make it available on an industrial scale. Due to the strong affinity towards self-condensation in alkaline media (see above) solutions of cyanamide are stabilized by the addition of 0.5 wt% of [monosodium phosphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monosodium_phosphate "Monosodium phosphate") as buffer. Solid cyanamide is produced by careful evaporation of the solvent and subsequent addition of a hydrolysis-labile [ester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ester "Ester") of [formic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formic_acid "Formic acid"). The ester absorbs traces of moisture (suppression of urea formation), neutralizes alkalinity (ammonia) and continually releases small amounts of formic acid.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Wehrstedt-15)

Cyanamide, under the trade name Dormex, is a common agricultural [rest-breaking agent](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Rest-breaking_agent&action=edit&redlink=1 "Rest-breaking agent (page does not exist)") applied in spring to stimulate uniform opening of [buds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bud "Bud"), early foliation and bloom. Cyanamide can effectively compensate for the moderate lack of [chilling units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chilling_unit "Chilling unit") accumulated in the previous autumn and save the harvest that would otherwise be lost. It is particularly effective for woody plants such as blueberries, grapes, apples, peaches and kiwifruit. Most recently the product was approved for use on almonds and pistachios in the USA. Overdosage, high concentration and error in timing of application can damage the buds (especially of [peach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peach "Peach") trees).[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-PO-16) Growers may avoid damage by applying 30 days prior to bud break according to the label.

A 50% aqueous solution of cyanamide is also used as a [biocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biocide "Biocide") (disinfectant) particularly in [pig farming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pig_farming "Pig farming"), because it effectively kills salmonella and shigella and fights [flies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fly "Fly") in all stages of development.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-17)

Environmental aspects
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cyanamide&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Environmental aspects")]

Cyanamide degrades via hydrolysis to urea, an excellent fertilizer. [Fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungi "Fungi"), like _[Myrothecium verrucaria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myrothecium\_verrucaria "Myrothecium verrucaria")_, accelerate this process utilizing the enzyme [cyanamide hydratase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide_hydratase "Cyanamide hydratase").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-18)

Cyanamide functional group
--------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cyanamide&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Cyanamide functional group")]

Cyanamide is the name for a functional group with the formula R 1 R 2 N−C≡N where R 1 and R 2 can be a variety of groups. These compounds are called **cyanamides**. One example is naphthylcyanamide, C 10 H 7 N(CH 3)CN, which has been produced by the [von Braun reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Von_Braun_reaction "Von Braun reaction"),[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-19) a general method for the conversion of [tertiary amines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tertiary_amine "Tertiary amine") to cyanamides using [cyanogen bromide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanogen_bromide "Cyanogen bromide") as reagent.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-20) Alternatively, secondary amines can attack an aryl [cyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanate_ester "Cyanate ester") to give a carbamimidate; heating then eliminates the arenol to give a cyanamide. A similar reaction occurs with sulfonyl cyanides, but thiocyanates require a thiophilic metal to induce elimination.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Rev-21): 1389

Some cyanamides where R 1 and R 2 are identical alkyl groups are prepared directly by [alkylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkylation "Alkylation") of a [salt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salt_(chemistry) "Salt (chemistry)") of the parent cyanamide.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-allyl-11) Likewise, [acyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acyl "Acyl") cyanamides can be formed from an [acyl chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acyl_chloride "Acyl chloride") and cyanamide, often with a base.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Rev-21): 1388

Alternatively, dehydration of [ureas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ureas "Ureas") or dehydrosulfurization of thioureas can produce cyanamides, sometimes with rearrangement.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Rev-21): 1390–1392 [Isonitrile dichlorides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isonitrile_dichloride "Isonitrile dichloride") react with [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia") to give cyanamides.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Rev-21): 1392  As a stabler valence isomer of [carbodiimides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbodiimide "Carbodiimide"), cyanamides form when carbodiimides are heated or undergo electrophilic substitution.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Rev-21): 1393

Secondary cyanamides are stable, but primary cyanamides trimerize to the corresponding [1,3,5-triazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1,3,5-triazine "1,3,5-triazine").[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Rev-21): 1398

Cyanamides are more acidic and less basic than alkylamines, protonating at the terminal nitrogen. However, nickel(0) complexes are known in which nickel coordinates to both nitrogen atoms. When protonated, the central carbon is very electrophilic, and will add a variety of nucleophiles.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Rev-21): 1393–1395, 1399

Due to its high permanent dipole moment (i.e., 4.32 ± 0.08 D),[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-22) cyanamide was detected in [spectral emissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectral_emission "Spectral emission") coming from the [Sgr B2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sgr_B2 "Sgr B2")[molecular cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud") (T < 100 K) through its microwave transitions as the first known interstellar molecule containing the NCN frame.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-23)

It is used as an [alcohol-deterrent drug](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcohol-deterrent_drug "Alcohol-deterrent drug") in Canada, Europe, and Japan.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Ullmann-3)

Cyanamide has a modest toxicity in humans.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Schep-24) Workplace exposure to hydrogen cyanamide sprays or exposure in people living in the vicinity of spraying have been reported as causing [respiratory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Respiratory "Respiratory") irritation, [contact dermatitis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contact_dermatitis "Contact dermatitis"), [headache](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Headache "Headache"), and [gastrointestinal symptoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gastrointestinal_symptom "Gastrointestinal symptom") of nausea, [vomiting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vomiting "Vomiting"), or [diarrhea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diarrhea "Diarrhea").[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanamide#cite_note-Schep-24)
