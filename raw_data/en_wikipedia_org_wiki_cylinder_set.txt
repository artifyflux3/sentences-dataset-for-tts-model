Applications [ edit ] Cylinder sets are often used to define a topology on sets that are subsets of S Z {\displaystyle S^{\mathbb {Z} }} and occur frequently in the study of symbolic dynamics ; see, for example, subshift of finite type . Cylinder sets are often used to define a measure , using the Kolmogorov extension theorem ; for example, the measure of a cylinder set of length m might be given by 1/ m or by 1/2 m .

Cylinder sets may be used to define a metric on the space: for example, one says that two strings are Îµ-close if a fraction 1âˆ’Îµ of the letters in the strings match.

Since strings in S Z {\displaystyle S^{\mathbb {Z} }} can be considered to be p -adic numbers , some of the theory of p -adic numbers can be applied to cylinder sets, and in particular, the definition of p -adic measures and p -adic metrics apply to cylinder sets. These types of measure spaces appear in the theory of dynamical systems and are called nonsingular odometers . A generalization of these systems is the Markov odometer .

Cylinder sets over topological vector spaces are the core ingredient in the [ citation needed ] definition of abstract Wiener spaces , which provide the formal definition of the Feynman path integral or functional integral of quantum field theory , and the partition function of statistical mechanics .
