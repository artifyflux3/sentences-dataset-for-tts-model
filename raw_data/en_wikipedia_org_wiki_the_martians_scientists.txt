"**The Martians**" ([Hungarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_language "Hungarian language"): _"A marslakók"_) were a group of prominent [scientists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientist "Scientist") (mostly, but not exclusively, [physicists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physicist "Physicist") and [mathematicians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematician "Mathematician")) of [Hungarian Jewish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Hungary "History of the Jews in Hungary") descent who emigrated from Europe to the United States in the early half of the 20th century.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Martians_(scientists)#cite_note-whitman_2012-1)

[Leo Szilard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_Szilard "Leo Szilard") jokingly suggested that [Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungary "Hungary") was a front for aliens from [Mars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mars "Mars"). In an answer to the question of why there is no evidence of intelligent life beyond Earth (called the [Fermi paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermi_paradox "Fermi paradox")) despite its high probability of existence, Szilárd responded: "They are already here among us– they just call themselves Hungarians." This account is featured in [György Marx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gy%C3%B6rgy_Marx "György Marx")'s book _The Voice of the Martians._[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Martians_(scientists)#cite_note-Marx-2)

The original story from [György Marx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gy%C3%B6rgy_Marx "György Marx")'s book _The Voice of the Martians_:

> The universe is vast, containing myriads of stars... likely to have planets circling around them.... The [simplest living things](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abiogenesis "Abiogenesis") will multiply, evolve by natural selection and become more complicated till eventually active, thinking creatures will emerge.... Yearning for fresh worlds... they should spread out all over the Galaxy. These highly exceptional and talented people could hardly overlook such a beautiful place as our Earth.– "And so," [Fermi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermi "Fermi") came to his overwhelming question, "if all this has been happening, they should have arrived here by now, so where are they?"– It was [Leo Szilard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_Szilard "Leo Szilard"), a man with an impish sense of humor, who supplied the perfect reply to the [Fermi Paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermi_Paradox "Fermi Paradox"): "They are among us," he said, "but they call themselves Hungarians."[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Martians_(scientists)#cite_note-Marx-2)

When the question was put to [Edward Teller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Teller "Edward Teller")– who was particularly proud of his [monogram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monogram "Monogram"), _E.T._ (abbreviation of _extraterrestrial_)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Martians_(scientists)#cite_note-Marx-2)– he looked worried, and said: "Von Kármán must have been talking."[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Martians_(scientists)#cite_note-6)

According to [György Marx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gy%C3%B6rgy_Marx "György Marx"), the extraterrestrial origin of the Hungarian scientists is proved by the fact that the names of [Leó Szilárd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Le%C3%B3_Szil%C3%A1rd "Leó Szilárd"), [John von Neumann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_von_Neumann "John von Neumann"), and [Theodore von Kármán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theodore_von_K%C3%A1rm%C3%A1n "Theodore von Kármán") cannot be found on the map of [Budapest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budapest "Budapest"), but craters can be found on the [Moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon "Moon") bearing their names:[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Martians_(scientists)#cite_note-Marx-2)[Szilard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Szilard_(crater) "Szilard (crater)"), [Von Neumann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Von_Neumann_(crater) "Von Neumann (crater)"), [Von Kármán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Von_K%C3%A1rm%C3%A1n_(lunar_crater) "Von Kármán (lunar crater)"), and a crater on Mars, [Von Kármán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Von_K%C3%A1rm%C3%A1n_(Martian_crater) "Von Kármán (Martian crater)").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Martians_(scientists)#cite_note-7)
