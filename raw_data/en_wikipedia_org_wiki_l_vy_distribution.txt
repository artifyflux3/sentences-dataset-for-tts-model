In spectroscopy , this distribution, with frequency as the dependent variable, is known as a van der Waals profile .

[ note 1 ] It is a special case of the inverse-gamma distribution . It is a stable distribution .

Applications [ edit ] The frequency of geomagnetic reversals appears to follow a Lévy distribution The time of hitting a single point, at distance α α {\displaystyle \alpha } from the starting point, by the Brownian motion has the Lévy distribution with c = α α 2 {\displaystyle c=\alpha ^{2}} . (For a Brownian motion with drift, this time may follow an inverse Gaussian distribution , which has the Lévy distribution as a limit.) The length of the path followed by a photon in a turbid medium follows the Lévy distribution.

[ 2 ] A Cauchy process can be defined as a Brownian motion subordinated to a process associated with a Lévy distribution.
