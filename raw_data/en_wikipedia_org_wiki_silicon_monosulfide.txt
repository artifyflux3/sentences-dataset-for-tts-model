Chemical compound

**Silicon monosulfide** is a [chemical compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_compound "Chemical compound") of [silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon "Silicon") and [sulfur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfur "Sulfur"). The [chemical formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula") is SiS. Molecular SiS has been detected at high temperature in the gas phase.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon_monosulfide#cite_note-Greenwood-1) The gas phase molecule has an Si-S bondlength of 192.93 pm, this compares to the normal single bond length of 216 pm,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon_monosulfide#cite_note-Greenwood-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon_monosulfide#cite_note-2) and is shorter than the Si=S bond length of around 201 pm reported in an organosilanethione.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon_monosulfide#cite_note-Greenwood-1) Historically a pale yellow-red amorphous solid compound has been reported.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon_monosulfide#cite_note-Rochow-3) The behavior of silicon can be contrasted with germanium which forms a stable solid [monosulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germanium_monosulfide "Germanium monosulfide").
