J. Patterson. It was created after the discovery of a new species, Cafeteria roenbergensis , a tiny (5–10 μm) eukaryotic organism that is eaten by protozoa and small invertebrates . The name is meant to indicate the importance of the genus in the food web .

Several species are recognised: Cafeteria biegae [ 2 ] Cafeteria burkhardae [ 2 ] [ 3 ] Cafeteria chilensis [ 2 ] Cafeteria graefeae [ 2 ] Cafeteria loberiensis [ 2 ] Cafeteria maldiviensis [ 2 ] ?
