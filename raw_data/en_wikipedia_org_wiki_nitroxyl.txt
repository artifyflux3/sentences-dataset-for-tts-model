Chemical compound

**Nitroxyl** (common name) or **azanone** (IUPAC name)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-2) is the chemical compound HNO. It is well known in the gas phase.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-Wiberg&Holleman-4) Nitroxyl can be formed as a short-lived intermediate in solution. Its conjugate base, NO−, the nitroxide anion, is the [reduced form](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redox_reaction "Redox reaction") of [nitric oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitric_oxide "Nitric oxide") (NO) and is [isoelectronic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isoelectronic "Isoelectronic") with [dioxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dioxygen "Dioxygen"). The bond dissociation energy of H−NO is 49.5 kcal/mol (207 kJ/mol), which is unusually weak for a bond to the hydrogen atom.

Generation
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nitroxyl&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Generation")]

Nitroxyl is produced from the reagents [Angeli's salt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angeli%27s_salt "Angeli's salt") (Na 2 N 2 O 3) and [Piloty's acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piloty%27s_acid "Piloty's acid") (PhSO 2 NHOH).[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-5) Other notable studies on the production of HNO exploit cycloadducts of acyl nitroso species, which are known to decompose via hydrolysis to HNO and acyl acid. Upon [photolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation "Photodissociation") these compounds release the acyl nitroso species which then further decompose.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-6) HNO is generated via [organic oxidation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_oxidation "Organic oxidation") of [cyclohexanone oxime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxime "Oxime") with [lead tetraacetate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lead_tetraacetate "Lead tetraacetate") to form 1-nitrosocyclohexyl acetate:[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-7)

[![Image 21: Nitrosocyclohexyl acetate](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Nitrosocyclohexyl_acetate.png/500px-Nitrosocyclohexyl_acetate.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Nitrosocyclohexyl_acetate.png "Nitrosocyclohexyl acetate")

Nitrosocyclohexyl acetate

This compound can be [hydrolyzed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrolyzed "Hydrolyzed") under [basic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Base_(chemistry) "Base (chemistry)") conditions in a [phosphate buffer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buffer_solution "Buffer solution") to HNO, [acetic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetic_acid "Acetic acid"), and [cyclohexanone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclohexanone "Cyclohexanone").

[Dichloramine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dichloramine "Dichloramine") reacts with the [hydroxide ion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroxide_ion "Hydroxide ion"), which is always present in water, to yield nitroxyl and the [chloride ion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloride_ion "Chloride ion").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-White_Chlorine-8)

Alkali metals react with [nitric oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitric_oxide "Nitric oxide") to give salts of the form MNO (M= metal).[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-9) However, generation of the (unstable) free acid from these salts is not entirely straightforward (see below).

Reactions
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nitroxyl&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Reactions")]

Nitroxyl is a [weak acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weak_acid "Weak acid"), with [p _K_ a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acid_dissociation_constant "Acid dissociation constant") of about 11, the conjugate base being the triplet state of NO−, sometimes called **nitroxide**. Nitroxyl itself, however, is a singlet ground state. Thus, deprotonation of nitroxyl uniquely involves the forbidden spin crossing from the singlet state starting material to triplet state product:

_1_ HNO + B− → _3_ NO− + BH
Due to the [spin-forbidden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin-forbidden_reactions "Spin-forbidden reactions") nature of deprotonation, proton abstraction is many orders of magnitude slower (_k_= 4.9×10 4 M−1 s−1 for deprotonation by OH−) than what one would expect for a heteroatom proton-transfer process (processes that are so fast that they are sometimes [diffusion-controlled](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diffusion-controlled_reaction "Diffusion-controlled reaction")).

The _K_ a of starting from or ending with the electronic excited states has also been determined. When process of deprotonating singlet state HNO to obtain singlet state NO− has a p _K_ a is about 23. On the other hand, when deprotonating triplet HNO to obtain triplet NO−, the p _K_ a is about −1.8.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-PNAS-10)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-Arch-11)

Nitroxyl rapidly decomposes by a bimolecular pathway to [nitrous oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrous_oxide "Nitrous oxide") (_k_ at 298 K = 8×10 6 M s):[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-PNAS-10)

2 HNO → N 2 O + H 2 O
The reaction proceeds via dimerization to [hyponitrous acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyponitrous_acid "Hyponitrous acid"), H 2 N 2 O 2, which subsequently undergoes dehydration. Therefore, HNO is generally prepared _[in situ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In\_situ "In situ")_ as described above.

Nitroxyl is very reactive towards nucleophiles, including [thiols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiol "Thiol"). The initial adduct rearranges to a [sulfinamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfinamide "Sulfinamide"):[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-Arch-11)

HNO + RSH → RS(O)NH 2

Detection
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nitroxyl&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Detection")]

In biological samples, nitroxyl can be detected using [fluorescent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluorescent "Fluorescent") sensors, many of which are based on the reduction of copper(II) to copper(I) with concomitant increase in fluorescence.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroxyl#cite_note-12)

Medicinal chemistry
-------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nitroxyl&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Medicinal chemistry")]

Nitroxyl donors, known as [nitroso](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroso "Nitroso") compounds, show potential in the treatment of heart failure and ongoing research is focused on finding new molecules for this task.
