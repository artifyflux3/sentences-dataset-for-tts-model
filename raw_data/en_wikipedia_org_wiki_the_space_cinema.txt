**Vue International** ( [_vew_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key"), like "view"), is a multinational cinema holding company headquartered in [London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London "London"), England, and registered in [St Helier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_Helier "St Helier"), Jersey. It operates in the United Kingdom, Ireland and Denmark as Vue, with international operations in Germany (as **CinemaxX**); Italy (as **The Space Cinema**); Poland and Lithuania ([Multikino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multikino "Multikino")); Netherlands (**Vue Netherlands**).

The company was founded in 1999 as **Spean Bridge Cinemas** by Stewart Blair, a former executive of [United Artists Theatres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regal_Cinemas "Regal Cinemas") and Tim Richards, a former executive of [Warner Bros. International Theatres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warner_Village_Cinemas "Warner Village Cinemas"). It was named after a holiday to the Scottish Highland village of the [same name](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spean_Bridge "Spean Bridge") by Blair.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-1)

[![Image 4](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/Vue_Cinema_Wood_Green_London.jpg/250px-Vue_Cinema_Wood_Green_London.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Vue_Cinema_Wood_Green_London.jpg)

Vue Cinema, Wood Green, London

The first cinema to open was under The Circuit Cinema brand in [Livingston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Livingston,_West_Lothian "Livingston, West Lothian"), Scotland, on 5 October 2000.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-auto-2) The company was later renamed as **SBC International Cinemas** and opened cinemas in [Faro, Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faro,_Portugal "Faro, Portugal") (closed in 2014) and [Taipei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taipei "Taipei"), Taiwan (still operating as SBC).[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-3)

In May 2003, SBC bought **[Warner Village Cinemas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warner_Village_Cinemas "Warner Village Cinemas")** from its owners, [Village Roadshow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Village_Roadshow "Village Roadshow") and [Warner Bros.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warner_Bros. "Warner Bros."), for £250 million. At the time, SBC owned four cinemas, and Warner Village Cinemas owned 36.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-4) In April 2005, the chain acquired the [Ster Century](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ster_Century "Ster Century") chain from Aurora Entertainment; this included the highest grossing cinema in the United Kingdom or Ireland at [Liffey Valley Shopping Centre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liffey_Valley_Shopping_Centre "Liffey Valley Shopping Centre") in Dublin, Ireland.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-5)

On 20 June 2006, Vue's executive team completed a management buyout of the company with the backing of Bank of Scotland Corporate; the management team retaining a 51% stake. Also, as part of the buyout, Vue took full ownership of the four Village sites it had been operating under contract from Village Roadshow. The private equity firm [Doughty Hanson & Co](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doughty_Hanson_%26_Co "Doughty Hanson & Co") acquired Vue in November 2010.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-6)

Vue bought the company, Apollo, in May 2012, retaining 14 new sites across the United Kingdom, making it the third largest cinema company in the United Kingdom, behind [Odeon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odeon_Cinemas "Odeon Cinemas") and [Cineworld](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cineworld "Cineworld").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-7)

In May 2013, Vue Entertainment acquired [Multikino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multikino "Multikino"), the Polish cinema operator owning thirty cinemas with almost 250 screens in Poland and Baltic countries.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-8)

In June 2013, Doughty Hanson & Co announced it had sold Vue to the Canadian pension funds [Alberta Investment Management Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alberta_Investment_Management_Corporation "Alberta Investment Management Corporation") and [OMERS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OMERS "OMERS") for £935 million.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-9)

In November 2014, Vue International acquired The Space Cinema in Italy.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-10)

In March 2015, Vue announced it would build the United Kingdom's first [eSports](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ESports "ESports") arena in cooperation with Gfinity, costing £350,000.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-CityAM-11) The Fulham Broadway cinema was converted into a six hundred seat 'Gfinity Arena,' to host e gaming events [[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-Engadget-12)

In August 2015, Vue International acquired JT Bioscopen, the second-largest cinema chain in the Netherlands, bringing Vue's number of sites to over 200.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-Corporate_My_Vue-13)

In June 2018, Vue acquired the Irish operator Showtime Cinemas, adding a further two cinemas to their estate in the United Kingdom and Ireland, now totalling 89 cinemas.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-14)

In March 2020, Vue temporarily closed its cinemas due to the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic"), stating that it would "remain closed until further notice".[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-15) Though numerous lockdowns affected a brief reopening in mid-2020, eventually, all cinemas reopened in May 2021, with COVID restrictions in force until spring 2022.

On 18 May 2020, Vue announced that it would close its single Multikino site in [Riga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riga "Riga"), Latvia, after operating for almost 10 years.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-16)

In January 2023, Vue International Group completed a restructuring which resulted in a change in the consolidating entity from Vue International Bidco plc (a UK incorporated company) to Vue Entertainment International Limited (a Jersey incorporated company).[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-17)

In January 2024, the company concluded the sale of its operations in Taiwan, resulting in a reduction of 1 cinema site.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-18)

Vue International operates 225 sites and almost 2,000 screens across 8 countries.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Space_Cinema#cite_note-19)
