Chemical compound

**Methyl formate**, also called **methyl methanoate**, is the methyl [ester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ester "Ester") of [formic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formic_acid "Formic acid"). The simplest example of a carboxylate ester, it is a colourless liquid with an ethereal odour, high [vapor pressure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vapor_pressure "Vapor pressure"), and low [surface tension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surface_tension "Surface tension"). The gas form is odourless and toxic, and used to be used in fridges. It is a precursor to many other compounds of commercial interest.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_formate#cite_note-Ullmann_2009-4)

Production
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methyl_formate&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Production")]

In the laboratory, methyl formate can be produced by the [condensation reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Condensation_reaction "Condensation reaction") of [methanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanol "Methanol") and [formic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formic_acid "Formic acid"), as follows:

HCOOH + CH 3 OH → HCOOCH 3 + H 2 O
Industrial methyl formate, however, is usually produced by the combination of [methanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanol "Methanol") and [carbon monoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_monoxide "Carbon monoxide") ([carbonylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbonylation "Carbonylation")) in the presence of a strong base, such as [sodium methoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_methoxide "Sodium methoxide"):[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_formate#cite_note-Ullmann_2009-4)

[![Image 41: Base-catalyzed carbonylation of methanol with carbon monoxide to methyl formate](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Industrial_synthesis_of_methyl_formate.svg/580px-Industrial_synthesis_of_methyl_formate.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Industrial_synthesis_of_methyl_formate.svg "Base-catalyzed carbonylation of methanol with carbon monoxide to methyl formate")

Base-catalyzed carbonylation of methanol with carbon monoxide to methyl formate

This process, practiced commercially by BASF among other companies gives 96% selectivity toward methyl formate. The [catalyst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalyst "Catalyst") for this process is sensitive to water, which can be present in the [carbon monoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_monoxide "Carbon monoxide") feedstock, which is commonly derived from [synthesis gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synthesis_gas "Synthesis gas"). Very dry [carbon monoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_monoxide "Carbon monoxide") is, therefore, essential.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_formate#cite_note-5)

Uses
----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methyl_formate&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Uses")]

Methyl formate is used primarily to manufacture [formamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide "Formamide"), [dimethylformamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethylformamide "Dimethylformamide"), and [formic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formic_acid "Formic acid"). These compounds are precursors or building blocks for many useful derivatives.

Because of its high [vapor pressure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vapor_pressure "Vapor pressure"), it is used for quick-drying finishes and as a [blowing agent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blowing_agent "Blowing agent") for some [polyurethane foam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyurethane_foam "Polyurethane foam") applications (for example Ecomate**®** manufactured by Foam Supplies Inc.)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_formate#cite_note-6) and as a replacement for [CFCs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chlorofluorocarbon "Chlorofluorocarbon"), [HCFCs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HCFC "HCFC"), and [HFCs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organofluorine_chemistry#Hydrofluorocarbons "Organofluorine chemistry"). Methyl formate has near zero [ozone depletion potential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozone_depletion_potential "Ozone depletion potential") and zero [global warming potential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_warming_potential "Global warming potential") and a short atmospheric life of 3 days.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_formate#cite_note-FoamAndBlow_Excerpt-7)

It is also used as an [insecticide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide").[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

A historical use of methyl formate, which sometimes brings it attention, was in refrigeration. Before the introduction of less-toxic refrigerants, methyl formate was used as an alternative to [sulfur dioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfur_dioxide "Sulfur dioxide") in domestic refrigerators, such as some models of the famous GE Monitor Top.
