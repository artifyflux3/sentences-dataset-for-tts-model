Nymphæum or Nymphaion ( Ancient Greek : Νυμφαῖον, Νύμφαιον or Νυμφαίη ; Latin : Nymphaeum ) was an ancient harbour on the coast of Illyria , three miles to the north of Lissus .

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ] The site has been identified with the area of modern day Shëngjin , Albania .

[ 4 ] [ 5 ] Perhaps emerged since the 5th century BC, Nymphaeum would have been presumably one of the earliest Greek colonies on the Albanian coast.

[ 6 ] The harbor of Nymphaeum was mentioned by Pliny the Elder (23 CE – 79), Lucanus [ 7 ] (39 AD – 65 AD), Livy (59 BC – AD 17) and Julius Caesar [ 8 ] (100 BC - 44 BC). The harbour was used by Marcus Antonius and his fleet when they arrived in Illyricum during Caesar's Civil War .
