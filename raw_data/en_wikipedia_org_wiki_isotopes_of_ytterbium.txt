All of the remaining radioactive isotopes have half-lives that are less than 2 hours, with the majority of them being less than 20 minutes. This element also has 18 meta states , with the most stable being 169m Yb (half-life 46 seconds).

The known isotopes of ytterbium range from 149 Yb to 187 Yb. The primary decay mode before the most abundant stable isotope, 174 Yb is electron capture giving thulium isotopes; the primary mode after is beta emission givivg lutetium isotopes. Of interest [ why?

] to modern quantum optics , the different ytterbium isotopes follow either Bose–Einstein statistics or Fermi–Dirac statistics , leading to different behavior in optical lattices .
