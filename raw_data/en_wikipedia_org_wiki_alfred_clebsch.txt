He attended the University of Königsberg and was habilitated at Berlin . He subsequently taught in Berlin and Karlsruhe . His collaboration with Paul Gordan in Giessen led to the introduction of Clebsch–Gordan coefficients for spherical harmonics , which are now widely used in quantum mechanics .

Together with Carl Neumann at Göttingen , he founded the mathematical research journal Mathematische Annalen in 1868.

In 1883, Saint-Venant translated Clebsch's work on elasticity into French and published it as Théorie de l'élasticité des Corps Solides .
