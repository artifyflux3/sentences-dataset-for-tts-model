Chemical compound

**Tricarbon monosulfide** (C 3 S) or **tricarbon sulfur**[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-roe-1) is a reactive molecular substance that has been detected in [outer space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space "Outer space"). Tricarbon monosulfide is a [heterocumulene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heterocumulene "Heterocumulene") or thiocumulene, consisting of a straight chain of three [carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon") atoms and a terminal [sulfur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfur "Sulfur") atom.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-etim-2)

Properties
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tricarbon_monosulfide&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Properties")]

The [dipole moment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_dipole_moment "Electric dipole moment") of tricarbon monosulfide is 3.704 [debye](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debye "Debye"). The bond lengths are 1.275 Å, for terminal C=C bond, 1.292 Å for internal bond, and 1.535 Å for the C=S bond. The similar bond lengths between the carbon atoms indicate they each have a [double bond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Double_bond "Double bond") nature.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-etim-2) The rotational constants for 12 C 12 C 12 C 32 S are B 0 = 2890.38000 MHz and D 0 = 0.00022416.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-Yama-3)

There is a characteristic infrared absorption band at 2047.5 cm−1 due to stretching of a C=C bond.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-roe-1)

Formation
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tricarbon_monosulfide&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Formation")]

Along with the related [dicarbon monosulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dicarbon_monosulfide "Dicarbon monosulfide") (CCS), tricarbon monosulfide was made by a glow discharge though [carbon disulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_disulfide "Carbon disulfide") vapour in helium. Microwave emission lines from rotational transitions matched up with previously unknown molecular lines from the [Taurus molecular cloud 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TMC-1 "TMC-1").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-4)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-Yama-3) Maximal concentrations occurred with a carbon disulfide pressure of 0.02 [torr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torr "Torr").

In molecular clouds, the formation mechanism is speculated to be CCS + CH → CCCS + H.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-5)

On dust grains, in space the formation mechanism is theorised to be: [CCC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon "Tricarbon") + H 2 S → C 3•HSH → CCCS + H 2 when irradiated with visible or UV light. THis reaction has been reproduced in a solid argon matrix.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-roe-1)

Natural occurrence
------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tricarbon_monosulfide&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Natural occurrence")]

Tricarbon monosulfide has been detected in [molecular clouds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud") in space, such as the [Taurus molecular cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taurus_molecular_cloud "Taurus molecular cloud")[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-etim-2) and the [Kleinmann–Low Nebula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kleinmann%E2%80%93Low_Nebula "Kleinmann–Low Nebula").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-terc-6) The ratio of [tricarbon monoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monoxide "Tricarbon monoxide") to tricarbon monosulfide reflects the ratio of sulfur to oxygen in the cloud. The ratio of concentration of sulfur to oxygen analogues follows the same pattern.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-etim-2) The clouds can be cold and dark, or warm.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-terc-6) CCCS has also been found in the stellar envelope of carbon-rich [AGB stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asymptotic_giant_branch "Asymptotic giant branch"), including in [IRC+10216](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IRC%2B10216 "IRC+10216").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricarbon_monosulfide#cite_note-7)
