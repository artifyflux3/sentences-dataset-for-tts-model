[ 1 ] Career [ edit ] Algra was born on 5 April 1959 in Utrecht .

[ 1 ] He studied classical languages and literature at Utrecht University. In 1985 he became a university lecturer. Three years later he obtained his PhD with a dissertation on Concepts of space in classical and hellenistic greek philosophy .

[ 1 ] Between 2000 and 2003 he was Academy professor of the Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences (KNAW) at Utrecht University.

[ 2 ] In 2001 he started as professor of Ancient and Medieval philosophy at the same university.

[ 1 ] In 2003 he gave the Alfred Edward Taylor lecture at the University of Edinburgh .

[ 3 ] In 2004 Algra became member of the Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences.

[ 4 ] He headed a KNAW committee which looked into the state of trust in Dutch science after a case in which Diederik Stapel had fabricated data, a report called Vertrouwen in wetenschap was published in May 2013.
