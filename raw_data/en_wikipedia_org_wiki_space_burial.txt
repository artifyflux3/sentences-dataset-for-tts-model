Space burials launch cremated remains out of the atmosphere.

**Space burial** is the launching of human remains into space. Missions may go into orbit around the Earth or to extraterrestrial bodies such as the Moon, or farther into space.

Remains are sealed until the spacecraft burns up upon re-entry into the Earth's atmosphere or they reach their extraterrestrial destinations. Suborbital flights briefly transport them into space then return to Earth where they can be recovered. Small samples of remains are usually launched to minimize the cost of launching mass into space, thereby making such services more affordable.

History and typology
--------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Space_burial&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History and typology")]

The concept of launching human remains into space using conventional rockets was proposed by the science fiction author [Neil R. Jones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neil_R._Jones "Neil R. Jones") in the novella "The Jameson Satellite", which was published in the pulp magazine _[Amazing Stories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazing\_Stories "Amazing Stories")_ in 1931.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-1) It was later proposed as a commercial service in the 1965 movie, [_The Loved One_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Loved_One_(film) "The Loved One (film)"),[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-2) and by Richard DeGroot in a _[Seattle Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Seattle\_Times "The Seattle Times")_ newspaper article on April 3, 1977.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-3) Since 1997, the private company [Celestis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celestis "Celestis") has conducted numerous space burials flying as [secondary payloads](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secondary_payload "Secondary payload").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-CelestisFAQ-4)

[![Image 2](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Space_shuttle_enterprise_star_trek-cropcast.jpg/500px-Space_shuttle_enterprise_star_trek-cropcast.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Space_shuttle_enterprise_star_trek-cropcast.jpg)

Gene Roddenberry (third from the right) in 1976 with most of the cast of _Star Trek_ at the rollout of the Space Shuttle [_Enterprise_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Enterprise "Space Shuttle Enterprise") at the [Rockwell International](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockwell_International "Rockwell International") plant at [Palmdale, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palmdale,_California "Palmdale, California"), US

The [first space burial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_Roddenberry#Spaceflight "Gene Roddenberry") occurred in 1992 when the NASA Space Shuttle[_Columbia_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Columbia "Space Shuttle Columbia") (mission [STS-52](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/STS-52 "STS-52")) carried a sample of [Gene Roddenberry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_Roddenberry "Gene Roddenberry")'s cremated remains into space and returned them to Earth.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-Shuttle_bore_Roddenberry's_ashes-5)

The first private space burial, Celestis' _Earthview 01: The Founders Flight_, was launched on April 21, 1997. An aircraft departing from the [Canary Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canary_Islands "Canary Islands") carried a [Pegasus rocket](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pegasus_rocket "Pegasus rocket") containing samples of the remains of 24 people to an altitude of 11 km (6.8 mi) above the Atlantic Ocean. The rocket then carried the remains into an elliptical orbit with an [apogee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apogee "Apogee") of 578 km (359 mi) and a perigee of 551 km (342 mi), orbiting the Earth once every 96 minutes until re-entry on May 20, 2002, northeast of Australia. Famous people on this flight included Roddenberry and [Timothy Leary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timothy_Leary "Timothy Leary").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-CelestisFoundersFlight-6)

Short flights that cross the boundary of space without attempting to reach orbital velocity are a cost-effective method of space burial. The remains do not burn up and are either recovered or lost.

The first Moon burial was that of [Eugene Merle Shoemaker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_Merle_Shoemaker "Eugene Merle Shoemaker"), a portion of whose [cremated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cremated "Cremated") remains were flown to the Moon by NASA.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-ESAshes-7) Shoemaker's former colleague [Carolyn Porco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carolyn_Porco "Carolyn Porco"), a University of Arizona professor, proposed and produced the tribute of having Shoemaker's ashes launched aboard the NASA's _[Lunar Prospector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lunar\_Prospector "Lunar Prospector")_ spacecraft.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-DSPTribute-8) Ten days after Shoemaker's passing, Porco had the go-ahead from NASA administrators and delivered the ashes to the _Lunar Prospector_ Mission Director Scott Hubbard at the NASA [Ames Research Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ames_Research_Center "Ames Research Center").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-ESAshes-7)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-AstronomyMag-9) The ashes were accompanied by a piece of brass foil inscribed with an image of [Comet Hale–Bopp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comet_Hale%E2%80%93Bopp "Comet Hale–Bopp"), an image of a [Meteor Crater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meteor_Crater "Meteor Crater") in northern Arizona, and a passage from [William Shakespeare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shakespeare "William Shakespeare")'s _[Romeo and Juliet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romeo\_and\_Juliet "Romeo and Juliet")_.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-ESAshes-7) The _Lunar Prospector_ spacecraft was launched on January 6, 1998, and impacted the south polar region of the Moon on July 31, 1999.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-LunarProspector-10)

Missions to the Moon are proposed by both [Elysium Space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elysium_Space "Elysium Space")[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-11) and Celestis as part of a mission by [Astrobotic Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrobotic_Technology "Astrobotic Technology") of Pittsburgh. The [first mission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peregrine_Mission_One "Peregrine Mission One") in January 2024 failed to reach the Moon due to a failure of the spacecraft and instead reentered Earth's atmosphere shortly after.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-12)

In 2014, Celestis launched Celestis Pets, a pet memorial spaceflight service for animal cremated remains.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-13) Prior to then, Bismarck, a Monroe, Washington [police dog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Police_dog "Police dog") may have flown on a 2012 memorial spaceflight. When this news broke, Celestis' president said that if dog ashes were on the rocket, the person who supplied the cremated remains likely violated the contract they signed with Celestis.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-14)

### Dedicated spacecraft

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Space_burial&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Dedicated spacecraft")]

On May 17, 2017, Elysium Space announced the world's first memorial flight involving a dedicated spacecraft. The [CubeSat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CubeSat "CubeSat") was placed as a secondary payload on a [SpaceX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SpaceX "SpaceX")[Falcon 9](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falcon_9 "Falcon 9") rocket as part of a dedicated rideshare mission called SSO-A planned by [Spaceflight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spaceflight_Industries "Spaceflight Industries"). The launch took place from [Vandenberg Air Force Base](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vandenberg_Air_Force_Base "Vandenberg Air Force Base") in California on December 3, 2018.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-15) The launch was successful, however, industry sources have noted that the Elysium Star spacecraft remained attached to the deployer due to a failure to procure proper licensing.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-16)

*   [Leiji Matsumoto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leiji_Matsumoto "Leiji Matsumoto") (1938–2023), Japanese creator of numerous anime and manga series including _[Galaxy Express 999](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy\_Express\_999 "Galaxy Express 999")_, _[Space Battleship Yamato](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space\_Battleship\_Yamato "Space Battleship Yamato")_ and _[Space Pirate Captain Harlock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space\_Pirate\_Captain\_Harlock "Space Pirate Captain Harlock")_, announced his intention to have a symbolic portion of his cremated remains to be launched into space on a future Elysium Space mission.[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-47)

Leiji Matsumoto at a book signing event in 2014

*   [Majel Barrett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Majel_Barrett "Majel Barrett") (1932–2008), American actress who played [Christine Chapel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christine_Chapel "Christine Chapel") in the original _[Star Trek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star\_Trek:\_The\_Original\_Series "Star Trek: The Original Series")_ series; wife of [Gene Roddenberry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_Roddenberry "Gene Roddenberry"). A symbolic portion of both her cremated remains and Roddenberry's cremated remains will be launched into space on a future Celestis mission.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-FutureFlightParticipants-48)[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-CelestisRoddenberryFuture-49)
*   [William R. Pogue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_R._Pogue "William R. Pogue") (1930–2014), American astronaut.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-FutureFlightParticipants-48)[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-CelestisPogueFuture-50)
*   [Luise Clayborn Kaish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luise_Clayborn_Kaish "Luise Clayborn Kaish") (1925–2013), American sculptor and painter.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-FutureFlightParticipants-48)[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_burial#cite_note-CelestisKaishFuture-51)
