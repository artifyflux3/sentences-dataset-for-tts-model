Chemical compound

**Diacetylene** (also known as **butadiyne**) is the [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the formula [C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon")4[H](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen")2 or H−C≡C−C≡C−H. It is the simplest compound containing two [triple bonds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triple_bond "Triple bond"). It is first in the series of [polyynes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyyne "Polyyne"), which are of theoretical but not of practical interest.

Occurrence
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Diacetylene&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Occurrence")]

Diacetylene has been identified in the atmosphere of [Titan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titan_(moon) "Titan (moon)") and in the [protoplanetary nebula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protoplanetary_nebula "Protoplanetary nebula")[CRL 618](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CRL_618 "CRL 618") by its characteristic [vibrational spectrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vibrational_spectrum "Vibrational spectrum"). It is proposed to arise by a reaction between [acetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetylene "Acetylene") and the [ethynyl radical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethynyl_radical "Ethynyl radical") (C 2 H), which is produced when acetylene undergoes [photolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photolysis "Photolysis"). This radical can in turn attack the triple bond in acetylene and react efficiently even at low temperatures. Diacetylene has also been detected on the [Moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon "Moon").[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Preparation
-----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Diacetylene&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Preparation")]

This compound may be made by the [dehydrohalogenation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dehydrohalogenation "Dehydrohalogenation") of 1,4-dichloro-2-butyne by [potassium hydroxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potassium_hydroxide "Potassium hydroxide") (in [alcoholic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcohol_(chemistry) "Alcohol (chemistry)") medium) at ~70°C:[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diacetylene#cite_note-1)

Cl−CH 2−C≡C−CH 2−Cl + 2 KOH → H−C≡C−C≡C−H + 2 [KCl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KCl "KCl") + 2 H 2 O
The bis([trimethylsilyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trimethylsilyl "Trimethylsilyl"))-protected derivative may be prepared by the [Hay coupling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hay_coupling "Hay coupling") of [(trimethylsilyl)acetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trimethylsilylacetylene "Trimethylsilylacetylene"):[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diacetylene#cite_note-2)

2 (CH 3)3 Si−C≡C−H → (CH 3)3 Si−C≡C−C≡C−Si(CH 3) + [H 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen")
