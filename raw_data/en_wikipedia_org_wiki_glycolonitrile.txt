Chemical compound

**Glycolonitrile**, also called **hydroxyacetonitrile** or **formaldehyde cyanohydrin**, is the [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the formula HOCH 2 CN. It is the simplest [cyanohydrin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanohydrin "Cyanohydrin") and it is derived from [formaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formaldehyde "Formaldehyde").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycolonitrile#cite_note-3) It is a colourless liquid that dissolves in water and ether. Because glycolonitrile decomposes readily into formaldehyde and [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide"), it is listed as an [extremely hazardous substance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_extremely_hazardous_substances "List of extremely hazardous substances"). In January 2019, astronomers reported the detection of glycolonitrile, another possible [building block of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abiogenesis "Abiogenesis") among [other such molecules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_interstellar_and_circumstellar_molecules "List of interstellar and circumstellar molecules"), in [outer space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space "Outer space").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycolonitrile#cite_note-EA-20190123-4)

Synthesis and reactions
-----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Glycolonitrile&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Synthesis and reactions")]

Glycolonitrile is produced by reacting [formaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formaldehyde "Formaldehyde") with [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide") at near-neutral pH, but with small amounts of catalytic base.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycolonitrile#cite_note-Ullmann-5) Glycolonitrile polymerizes under alkaline conditions above [pH](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PH "PH") 7.0. As the product of polymerization is an amine with a basic character, the reaction is self-catalysed, gaining in speed with ongoing conversion.

Glycolonitrile can react with ammonia to give [aminoacetonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aminoacetonitrile "Aminoacetonitrile"), which can be hydrolysed to give [glycine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycine "Glycine"):

HOCH 2 CN + NH 3 → H 2 NCH 2 CN + H 2 O H 2 NCH 2 CN + 2 H 2 O → H 2 NCH 2 CO 2 H + NH 3
The industrially important [chelating agent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chelating_agent "Chelating agent")[EDTA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EDTA "EDTA") is prepared from glycolonitrile and [ethylenediamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethylenediamine "Ethylenediamine") followed by hydrolysis of the resulting tetranitrile. [Nitrilotriacetic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrilotriacetic_acid "Nitrilotriacetic acid") is prepared similarly.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycolonitrile#cite_note-Ullmann-5)
