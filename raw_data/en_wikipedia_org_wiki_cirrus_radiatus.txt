The name cirrus radiatus is derived from Latin , meaning "rayed, striped".

[ 1 ] This variety of cirrus clouds occurs in parallel bands that often cover the entire sky and appear to converge at a single point [ 2 ] or two opposite points on the horizon. Cirrus radiatus is often partly made up of cirrocumulus or cirrostratus .
