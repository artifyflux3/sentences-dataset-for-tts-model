The rhombus has a square as a special case, and is a special case of a [kite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kite_(geometry) "Kite (geometry)") and [parallelogram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallelogram "Parallelogram").

In [geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometry "Geometry"), a **rhombus** (pl.: **rhombi** or **rhombuses**) is an **equilateral quadrilateral**, a [quadrilateral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quadrilateral "Quadrilateral") whose four sides all have the same length. Other names for rhombus include _**diamond**_, _**lozenge**_, and _**calisson**_.

Every rhombus is [simple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simple_polygon "Simple polygon") (non-self-intersecting), and is a special case of a [parallelogram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallelogram "Parallelogram") and a [kite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kite_(geometry) "Kite (geometry)"). A rhombus with right angles is a [square](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square "Square").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-1)

Etymology
---------

The name _rhombus_ comes from [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "Ancient Greek")ῥόμβος _rhómbos_, meaning something that spins, such as a [bullroarer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bullroarer "Bullroarer") or an ancient precursor of the [button whirligig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Button_whirligig "Button whirligig").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-2) The word was used both by [Euclid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclid "Euclid") and [Archimedes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes "Archimedes"), who also used the term "solid rhombus" for a [bicone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bicone "Bicone"), two right circular [cones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cone "Cone") sharing a common base.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-3) A planar _rhombus_ is a [cross section](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cross_section_(geometry) "Cross section (geometry)") of a bicone.

The name _diamond_ comes from the shape of an [octahedral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regular_octahedron "Regular octahedron")[diamond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamond "Diamond") gemstone; the [diamonds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamonds_(suit) "Diamonds (suit)") suit in [playing cards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Playing_card "Playing card") is named after the shape – it was originally called _carreaux_ (lit. "squares") in French.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-4) In the context of [polyiamonds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyiamond "Polyiamond"), shapes like [polyominos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyomino "Polyomino") but constructed from equilateral triangles, a _diamond_ is a rhombus with a 60° angle.

The etymology of _[lozenge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lozenge\_(shape) "Lozenge (shape)")_ is uncertain. It might come from a shape of some [lauzinaj](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lauzinaj "Lauzinaj") almond pastries, or from the shape of tombstones. A lozenge is often specifically to a rhombus with a 45° angle.

A _[calisson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calisson "Calisson")_ is a type of rhombus-shaped French sweet.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-5)

A [simple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simple_polygon "Simple polygon") (non-[self-intersecting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_self-intersecting_polygons "List of self-intersecting polygons")) quadrilateral is a rhombus [if and only if](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/If_and_only_if "If and only if") it is any one of the following:[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-7)

*   a [parallelogram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallelogram "Parallelogram") in which a [diagonal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diagonal "Diagonal") bisects an [interior angle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internal_and_external_angle "Internal and external angle")
*   a parallelogram in which at least two consecutive sides are equal in length
*   a parallelogram in which the diagonals are perpendicular (an [orthodiagonal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthodiagonal "Orthodiagonal") parallelogram)
*   a quadrilateral with four sides of equal length (by definition)
*   a quadrilateral in which the diagonals are [perpendicular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perpendicular "Perpendicular") and [bisect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bisection "Bisection") each other
*   a quadrilateral in which each diagonal bisects two opposite interior angles
*   a quadrilateral _ABCD_ possessing a point _P_ in its plane such that the four triangles _ABP_, _BCP_, _CDP_, and _DAP_ are all [congruent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congruence_(geometry) "Congruence (geometry)")[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-8)
*   a quadrilateral _ABCD_ in which the [incircles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incircle_and_excircles_of_a_triangle "Incircle and excircles of a triangle") in triangles _ABC_, _BCD_, _CDA_ and _DAB_ have a common point[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-9)

Basic properties
----------------

Every rhombus has two [diagonals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diagonal "Diagonal") connecting pairs of opposite vertices, and two pairs of parallel sides. Using [congruent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congruence_(geometry) "Congruence (geometry)")[triangles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triangle "Triangle"), one can [prove](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_proof "Mathematical proof") that the rhombus is [symmetric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetry "Symmetry") across each of these diagonals. It follows that any rhombus has the following properties:

*   Opposite [angles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angle "Angle") of a rhombus have equal measure.
*   The two diagonals of a rhombus are [perpendicular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perpendicular "Perpendicular"); that is, a rhombus is an [orthodiagonal quadrilateral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthodiagonal_quadrilateral "Orthodiagonal quadrilateral").
*   Its diagonals bisect opposite angles.

The first property implies that every rhombus is a [parallelogram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallelogram "Parallelogram"). A rhombus therefore has all of the [properties of a parallelogram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallelogram#Properties "Parallelogram"): for example, opposite sides are parallel; adjacent angles are [supplementary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supplementary_angles "Supplementary angles"); the two diagonals [bisect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bisection "Bisection") one another; any line through the midpoint bisects the area; and the sum of the squares of the sides equals the sum of the squares of the diagonals (the [parallelogram law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallelogram_law "Parallelogram law")). Thus denoting the common side as _a_ and the diagonals as _p_ and _q_, in every rhombus

![Image 9: {\displaystyle \displaystyle 4a^{2}=p^{2}+q^{2}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1593d88a02f3a1f00c020e3bf23b484197031ed9)
Not every parallelogram is a rhombus, though any parallelogram with perpendicular diagonals (the second property) is a rhombus. In general, any quadrilateral with perpendicular diagonals, one of which is a line of symmetry, is a [kite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kite_(geometry) "Kite (geometry)"). Every rhombus is a kite, and any quadrilateral that is both a kite and parallelogram is a rhombus.

A rhombus is a [tangential quadrilateral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tangential_quadrilateral "Tangential quadrilateral").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-Mathworld-10) That is, it has an [inscribed circle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inscribed_figure "Inscribed figure") that is tangent to all four sides.

[![Image 10](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Rhombus1.svg/330px-Rhombus1.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Rhombus1.svg)

A rhombus. Each angle marked with a black dot is a right angle. The height _h_ is the perpendicular distance between any two non-adjacent sides, which equals the diameter of the circle inscribed. The diagonals of lengths _p_ and _q_ are the red dotted line segments.

Area
----

As for all [parallelograms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallelogram "Parallelogram"), the [area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Area "Area")_K_ of a rhombus is the product of its [base](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Base_(geometry) "Base (geometry)") and its [height](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Height "Height") (_h_). The base is simply any side length _a_:

![Image 15: {\displaystyle K=a\cdot h.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b33557d5f358091ebfd4da051ff2309184422464)
The area can also be expressed as the [base](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edge_(geometry) "Edge (geometry)")[squared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square_(algebra) "Square (algebra)") times the sine of any angle:

![Image 16: {\displaystyle K=a^{2}\cdot \sin \alpha =a^{2}\cdot \sin \beta ,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/bccdbeb10b8b7b76b9408ffa8353253e60d10aef)
or in terms of the height and a [vertex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertex_(geometry) "Vertex (geometry)")[angle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angle "Angle"):

![Image 17: {\displaystyle K={\frac {h^{2}}{\sin \alpha }},}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4cf74e116423e35f5e8183dd4fa3980e1a4410c6)
or as half the product of the [diagonals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diagonal "Diagonal")_p_, _q_:

![Image 18: {\displaystyle K={\frac {p\cdot q}{2}},}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/710eb72777bf88622b380160ed74291ee61ea95a)
or as the [semiperimeter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semiperimeter "Semiperimeter") times the [radius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radius "Radius") of the [circle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circle "Circle")[inscribed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inscribed_figure "Inscribed figure") in the rhombus (inradius):

![Image 19: {\displaystyle K=2a\cdot r.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/65269268c41f5a5aa0c220406f061cb45639b8b1)
Another way, in common with parallelograms, is to consider two adjacent sides as vectors, forming a [bivector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bivector "Bivector"), so the area is the magnitude of the bivector (the magnitude of the vector product of the two vectors), which is the [determinant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Determinant "Determinant") of the two vectors' Cartesian coordinates: _K_ = _x_ 1 _y_ 2 – _x_ 2 _y_ 1.

Dual properties
---------------

The [dual polygon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_polygon "Dual polygon") of a rhombus is a [rectangle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rectangle "Rectangle"):[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombus#cite_note-11)

*   A rhombus has all sides equal, while a rectangle has all angles equal.
*   A rhombus has opposite angles equal, while a rectangle has opposite sides equal.
*   A rhombus has an inscribed circle, while a rectangle has a [circumcircle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circumcircle "Circumcircle").
*   A rhombus has an axis of symmetry through each pair of opposite vertex angles, while a rectangle has an axis of symmetry through each pair of opposite sides.
*   The diagonals of a rhombus intersect at equal angles, while the diagonals of a rectangle are equal in length.
*   The figure formed by joining the midpoints of the sides of a rhombus is a [rectangle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rectangle "Rectangle"), and vice versa.

### As the faces of a polyhedron

Convex polyhedra with rhombi include the infinite set of rhombic [zonohedrons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zonohedron "Zonohedron"), which can be seen as projective envelopes of [hypercubes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypercube "Hypercube").

*   A [rhombohedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombohedron "Rhombohedron") (also called a rhombic hexahedron) is a three-dimensional figure like a [cuboid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuboid "Cuboid") (also called a rectangular parallelepiped), except that its 3 pairs of parallel faces are up to 3 types of rhombi instead of rectangles.
*   The [rhombic dodecahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombic_dodecahedron "Rhombic dodecahedron") is a [convex polyhedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convex_polyhedron "Convex polyhedron") with 12 [congruent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congruence_(geometry) "Congruence (geometry)") rhombi as its [faces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Face_(geometry) "Face (geometry)").
*   The [rhombic triacontahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombic_triacontahedron "Rhombic triacontahedron") is a [convex polyhedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convex_polyhedron "Convex polyhedron") with 30 [golden rhombi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_rhombus "Golden rhombus") (rhombi whose diagonals are in the [golden ratio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_ratio "Golden ratio")) as its faces.
*   The [great rhombic triacontahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_rhombic_triacontahedron "Great rhombic triacontahedron") is a nonconvex [isohedral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isohedral_figure "Isohedral figure"), [isotoxal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isotoxal "Isotoxal")[polyhedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyhedron "Polyhedron") with 30 intersecting rhombic faces.
*   The [rhombic hexecontahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombic_hexecontahedron "Rhombic hexecontahedron") is a [stellation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellation "Stellation") of the rhombic triacontahedron. It is nonconvex with 60 [golden rhombic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_rhombus "Golden rhombus") faces with [icosahedral symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Icosahedral_symmetry "Icosahedral symmetry").
*   The [rhombic enneacontahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombic_enneacontahedron "Rhombic enneacontahedron") is a polyhedron composed of 90 rhombic faces, with three, five, or six rhombi meeting at each vertex. It has 60 broad rhombi and 30 slim ones.
*   The [rhombic icosahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombic_icosahedron "Rhombic icosahedron") is a polyhedron composed of 20 rhombic faces, of which three, four, or five meet at each vertex. It has 10 faces on the polar axis with 10 faces following the equator.
