From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Photography and other imagery of planet [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth") from [outer space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space "Outer space")[[a]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_image_of_Earth#cite_note-1) started in the 1940s, first from [rockets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocket "Rocket") in [suborbital flight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suborbital_flight "Suborbital flight"), subsequently from [satellites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satellite "Satellite") around Earth, and then from [spacecraft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacecraft "Spacecraft") beyond Earth's orbit.

*   [List of notable images of Earth from space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_notable_images_of_Earth_from_space "List of notable images of Earth from space")
*   [Aerial photography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aerial_photography "Aerial photography")– Taking images of the ground from the air
*   [Earth observation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_observation "Earth observation")– Information about the Earth environment, remote or in situ
*   [Earth in culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_in_culture "Earth in culture")– Cultural perspectives about Earth
*   [Earth phase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_phase "Earth phase")– Phases of Earth as seen from the Moon
*   [Extraterrestrial sky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extraterrestrial_sky "Extraterrestrial sky")– Extraterrestrial view of outer space
*   [Overview effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overview_effect "Overview effect")– Cognitive shift after seeing Earth from space
*   [Satellite imagery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satellite_imagery "Satellite imagery")– Images taken from an artificial satellite
*   [Space selfie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_selfie "Space selfie")– Self-portrait photo taken in outer space
*   [View of Earth from Mars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/View_of_Earth_from_Mars "View of Earth from Mars")– Extraterrestrial view of outer space

1.   **[^](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_image_of_Earth#cite_ref-1 "Jump up")**Within the context of this timeline, outer space is considered as starting at the [Kármán line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%A1rm%C3%A1n_line "Kármán line"), 100 kilometres (62 miles) [above mean sea level](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level "Above mean sea level") (AMSL).
