**Propionaldehyde** or **propanal** is the [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the formula CH 3 CH 2 CHO. It is the 3-carbon [aldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aldehyde "Aldehyde"). It is a colourless, flammable liquid with a pungent and fruity odour. It is produced on a large scale industrially.

Propionaldehyde is mainly produced industrially by [hydroformylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroformylation "Hydroformylation") of [ethylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethylene "Ethylene"):

CO + H 2 + C 2 H 4 → CH 3 CH 2 CHO
In this way, several hundred thousand tons are produced annually.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-Ullmann-2)

### Laboratory preparation

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Propionaldehyde&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Laboratory preparation")]

Propionaldehyde may also be prepared by oxidizing [1-propanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1-propanol "1-propanol") with a mixture of [sulfuric acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfuric_acid "Sulfuric acid") and [potassium dichromate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potassium_dichromate "Potassium dichromate"). The reflux condenser contains water heated at 60°C, which condenses unreacted propanol, but allows propionaldehyde to pass. The propionaldehyde vapor is immediately condensed into a suitable receiver. In this arrangement, any propionaldehyde formed is immediately removed from the reactor, thus it does not get over-oxidized to [propionic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionic_acid "Propionic acid").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-3)

Propionaldehyde exhibits the reactions characteristic of alkyl [aldehydes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aldehyde "Aldehyde"), e.g. hydrogenation, [aldol condensations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aldol_condensation "Aldol condensation"), oxidations, etc. It is the simplest aldehyde with a prochiral methylene such that α-functionalized derivatives (CH 3 CH(X)CHO) are [chiral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiral "Chiral"). If water is available, propionaldehyde exists in equilibrium with **1,1-propanediol**, a [geminal diol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geminal_diol "Geminal diol").

[2-Methylpentanal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2-Methylpentanal "2-Methylpentanal") (CH 3 CH 2 CH 2 CH(CH 3)CHO) arises by [aldol condensation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aldol_condensation "Aldol condensation") of propionaldehyde followed by dehydration and hydrogenation.

Both industrially and in the laboratory, propionaldehyde has primary application as a chemical building block.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-Ullmann-2): 4 [[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-4)

It is predominantly used as a precursor to [trimethylolethane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trimethylolethane "Trimethylolethane") (CH 3 C(CH 2 OH)3) through a [condensation reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Condensation_reaction "Condensation reaction") with [formaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formaldehyde "Formaldehyde"). This triol is an important intermediate in the production of [alkyd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkyd "Alkyd")[resins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resin "Resin"). It is used in the synthesis of several common [aroma compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aroma_compound "Aroma compound") ([cyclamen aldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclamen_aldehyde "Cyclamen aldehyde"), [helional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helional "Helional"), [lilial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lilial "Lilial")).[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-Ullmann-2)

Reduction of propionaldehyde gives [_n_‑propanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-propanol "N-propanol"), and [reductive amination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reductive_amination "Reductive amination") gives [propanamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propanamine "Propanamine"). Rising demand for non-chlorocarbon solvents has caused some manufacturers to substitutively brominate _n_‑propanol to [propyl bromide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1-bromopropane "1-bromopropane"). However, the majority of applications use _n_‑propanol proper in [esters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ester "Ester") or [glycol ethers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycol_ether "Glycol ether"), or as a gentle [alkylant](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Alkylant&action=edit&redlink=1 "Alkylant (page does not exist)") for primary and secondary [amines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amine "Amine").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-Ullmann-2): 5

Oxidants instead give [propionic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionic_acid "Propionic acid") and propionates, typically used as [preservatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_preservative "Food preservative").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-Ullmann-2): 5

Many laboratory uses exploit its participation in condensation reactions.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-5)[_[better source needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS "Wikipedia:NOTRS")_] With [_tert_-butylamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tert-Butylamine "Tert-Butylamine") it gives CH 3 CH 2 CH=N-_t_-Bu, a three-carbon building block used in [organic synthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_synthesis "Organic synthesis").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-6)

Propionaldehyde along with [acrolein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrolein "Acrolein") has been detected in the [molecular cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud")[Sagittarius B2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagittarius_B2 "Sagittarius B2") near the center of the [Milky Way Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way_Galaxy "Milky Way Galaxy"), about 26,000 light years from Earth.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-9)

Measurements by the COSAC and Ptolemy instruments on [comet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comet "Comet")[67/P](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/67/P "67/P")'s surface, revealed sixteen [organic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound"), four of which were seen for the first time on a comet, including [acetamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide "Acetamide"), [acetone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetone "Acetone"), [methyl isocyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate "Methyl isocyanate") and propionaldehyde.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-wapo20150730-10)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-esa20150730-11)[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-SCI-20150731-12)

With an [LD50](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LD50 "LD50") of 1690 mg/kg (oral),[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde#cite_note-Ullmann-2) propionaldehyde exhibits low acute toxicity, but is a lung and eye irritant and is a combustible liquid.
