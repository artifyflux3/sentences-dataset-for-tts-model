In [particle physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particle_physics "Particle physics") and [physical cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_cosmology "Physical cosmology"), **Planck units** are a [system of units of measurement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System_of_units_of_measurement "System of units of measurement") defined exclusively in terms of four universal [physical constants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_constant "Physical constant"): _[c](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speed\_of\_light "Speed of light")_, _[G](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitational\_constant "Gravitational constant")_, _[ħ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reduced\_Planck\_constant "Reduced Planck constant")_, and [_k_ B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boltzmann_constant "Boltzmann constant") (described further below). Expressing one of these physical constants in terms of Planck units yields a numerical value of [1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1_(number) "1 (number)"). They are a system of [natural units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_units "Natural units"), defined using fundamental properties of [nature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nature#Matter_and_energy "Nature") (specifically, properties of [free space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vacuum "Vacuum")) rather than properties of a chosen [prototype object](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prototype_(metrology) "Prototype (metrology)"). Originally proposed in 1899 by German physicist [Max Planck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Planck "Max Planck"), they are relevant in research on unified theories such as [quantum gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_gravity "Quantum gravity").

The term **Planck scale** refers to quantities of space, time, energy and other units that are similar in magnitude to corresponding Planck units. This region may be characterized by particle [energies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy "Energy") of around 10 19 GeV or 10 9 J, [time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time "Time") intervals of around 5×10−44 s and [lengths](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Length "Length") of around 10−35 m (approximately the energy-equivalent of the Planck mass, the Planck time and the Planck length, respectively). At the Planck scale, the predictions of the [Standard Model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_Model "Standard Model"), [quantum field theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_field_theory "Quantum field theory") and [general relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity "General relativity") are not expected to apply, and [quantum effects of gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_Gravity "Quantum Gravity") are expected to dominate. One example is represented by the conditions in the [first 10−43 seconds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chronology_of_the_universe "Chronology of the universe") of our universe after the [Big Bang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Bang "Big Bang"), approximately 13.8 billion years ago.

The four [universal constants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_constant "Universal constant") that, by definition, have a numeric value 1 when expressed in these units are:

*   _c_, the [speed of light](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speed_of_light "Speed of light") in vacuum,
*   _G_, the [gravitational constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitational_constant "Gravitational constant"),
*   _ħ_, the [reduced Planck constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reduced_Planck_constant "Reduced Planck constant"), and
*   _k_ B, the [Boltzmann constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boltzmann_constant "Boltzmann constant").

Variants of the basic idea of Planck units exist, such as alternate choices of normalization that give other numeric values to one or more of the four constants above.

Introduction
------------

Any system of measurement may be assigned a mutually independent set of base quantities and associated [base units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Base_unit_(measurement) "Base unit (measurement)"), from which all other quantities and units may be derived. In the [International System of Units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_System_of_Units "International System of Units"), for example, the [SI base quantities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SI_base_quantity "SI base quantity") include length with the associated unit of the [metre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metre "Metre"). In the system of Planck units, a similar set of base quantities and associated units may be selected, in terms of which other quantities and coherent units may be expressed.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-:2-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-Gravitation-2): 1215  The Planck unit of length has become known as the Planck length, and the Planck unit of time is known as the Planck time, but this nomenclature has not been established as extending to all quantities.

All Planck units are derived from the dimensional universal physical constants that define the system, and in a convention in which these units are omitted (i.e. treated as having the dimensionless value 1), these constants are then eliminated from equations of physics in which they appear. For example, [Newton's law of universal gravitation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%27s_law_of_universal_gravitation "Newton's law of universal gravitation"), ![Image 2: {\displaystyle F=G{\frac {m_{1}m_{2}}{r^{2}}}=\left({\frac {F_{\text{P}}l_{\text{P}}^{2}}{m_{\text{P}}^{2}}}\right){\frac {m_{1}m_{2}}{r^{2}}},}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/07f2f9c2687d8c7a616c3409542a39cd6972e09d) can be expressed as: ![Image 3: {\displaystyle {\frac {F}{F_{\text{P}}}}={\frac {\left({\dfrac {m_{1}}{m_{\text{P}}}}\right)\left({\dfrac {m_{2}}{m_{\text{P}}}}\right)}{\left({\dfrac {r}{l_{\text{P}}}}\right)^{2}}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/de1f1c8f9bbaa01428d0ca4c14a43c7c4723f52f)Both equations are [dimensionally consistent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimensional_analysis "Dimensional analysis") and equally valid in _any_ system of quantities, but the second equation, with G absent, is relating only [dimensionless quantities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimensionless_quantities "Dimensionless quantities") since any ratio of two like-dimensioned quantities is a dimensionless quantity. If, by a shorthand convention, it is understood that each physical quantity is the corresponding ratio with a coherent Planck unit (or "expressed in Planck units"), the ratios above may be expressed simply with the symbols of physical quantity, without being scaled explicitly by their corresponding unit: ![Image 4: {\displaystyle F'={\frac {m_{1}'m_{2}'}{r'^{2}}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/28037b43b7cf26ce1612559c758a5c718e3a43a0)This last equation (without G) is valid with _F_′, _m_ 1′, _m_ 2′, and _r_′ being the dimensionless ratio quantities _corresponding to_ the standard quantities, written e.g. _F_′ ≘ _F_ or _F_′ = _F_/_F_ P, but not as a direct equality of quantities. This may seem to be "setting the constants c, G, etc., to 1" if the correspondence of the quantities is thought of as equality. For this reason, Planck or other natural units should be employed with care. Referring to "_G_ = _c_ = 1", [Paul S. Wesson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_S._Wesson "Paul S. Wesson") wrote that, "Mathematically it is an acceptable trick which saves labour. Physically it represents a loss of information and can lead to confusion."[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-3)

History and definition
----------------------

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Max_Planck_1933.jpg/250px-Max_Planck_1933.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Max_Planck_1933.jpg)

Max Planck in 1933

The concept of [natural units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_units "Natural units") was introduced in 1874, when [George Johnstone Stoney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Johnstone_Stoney "George Johnstone Stoney"), noting that electric charge is quantized, derived units of length, time, and mass, now named [Stoney units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stoney_units "Stoney units") in his honor. Stoney chose his units so that _G_, _c_, and the [electron charge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elementary_charge "Elementary charge")_e_ would be numerically equal to 1.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-4) In 1899, one year before the advent of quantum theory, [Max Planck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Planck "Max Planck") introduced what became later known as the Planck constant.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-planck-1899-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-TOM-6) At the end of the paper, he proposed the base units that were later named in his honor. The Planck units are based on the quantum of [action](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Action_(physics) "Action (physics)"), now usually known as the Planck constant, which appeared in the [Wien approximation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wien_approximation "Wien approximation") for [black-body radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black-body_radiation "Black-body radiation"). Planck underlined the universality of the new unit system, writing:[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-planck-1899-5)

> _... die Möglichkeit gegeben ist, Einheiten für Länge, Masse, Zeit und Temperatur aufzustellen, welche, unabhängig von speciellen Körpern oder Substanzen, ihre Bedeutung für alle Zeiten und für alle, auch ausserirdische und aussermenschliche Culturen nothwendig behalten und welche daher als »natürliche Maasseinheiten«bezeichnet werden können._

> ... it is possible to set up units for length, mass, time and temperature, which are independent of special bodies or substances, necessarily retaining their meaning for all times and for all civilizations, including extraterrestrial and non-human ones, which can be called "natural units of measure".

Planck considered only the units based on the universal constants ![Image 6: {\displaystyle G}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f5f3c8921a3b352de45446a6789b104458c9f90b), ![Image 7: {\displaystyle h}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b26be3e694314bc90c3215047e4a2010c6ee184a), ![Image 8: {\displaystyle c}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/86a67b81c2de995bd608d5b2df50cd8cd7d92455), and ![Image 9: {\displaystyle k_{\rm {B}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4bd537f082cb894d2f69b1cc050dd36f7d489a3d) to arrive at natural units for [length](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Length "Length"), [time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time "Time"), [mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass "Mass"), and [temperature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature "Temperature").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-TOM-6) His definitions differ from the modern ones by a factor of ![Image 10: {\displaystyle {\sqrt {2\pi }}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7a9b009153bbbb3273a7e7279cb6b084fd650a80), because the modern definitions use ![Image 11: {\displaystyle \hbar }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/de68de3a92517953436c93b5a76461d49160cc41) rather than ![Image 12: {\displaystyle h}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b26be3e694314bc90c3215047e4a2010c6ee184a).[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-planck-1899-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-TOM-6)

Unlike the case with the [International System of Units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_System_of_Units "International System of Units"), there is no official entity that establishes a definition of a Planck unit system. Thus, depending on the author's choice, this charge unit is given by ![Image 19: {\displaystyle q_{\text{P}}={\sqrt {4\pi \epsilon _{0}\hbar c}}\approx 1.875546\times 10^{-18}{\text{ C}}\approx 11.7\ e}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/15491489376695abf5ac895c159a990a773c56ed) for ![Image 20: {\displaystyle k_{\text{e}}=1}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/96a3353482af6ce383aae6366c5cc2833d83b373), or ![Image 21: {\displaystyle q_{\text{P}}={\sqrt {\epsilon _{0}\hbar c}}\approx 5.290818\times 10^{-19}{\text{ C}}\approx 3.3\ e}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8f003767a878c1b78f73068577774563b6858519) for ![Image 22: {\displaystyle \varepsilon _{0}=1}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1fed767ce6400c809b15275efc75fccb532dc14e). Compared to [Stoney units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stoney_units "Stoney units"), Planck base units are all larger by a factor ![Image 23: {\textstyle {\sqrt {{1}/{\alpha }}}\approx 11.7}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/dcb89302c71d0075995869fa2dd497587ae71ba3), where ![Image 24: {\displaystyle \alpha }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b79333175c8b3f0840bfb4ec41b8072c83ea88d3) is the [fine-structure constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fine-structure_constant "Fine-structure constant").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-19)

Some Planck units, such as of time and length, are many [orders of magnitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orders_of_magnitude "Orders of magnitude") too large or too small to be of practical use, so that Planck units as a system are typically only relevant to theoretical physics. In some cases, a Planck unit may suggest a limit to a range of a physical quantity where present-day theories of physics apply.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-:1-20) For example, our understanding of the [Big Bang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Bang "Big Bang") does not extend to the [Planck epoch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chronology_of_the_universe#Planck_epoch "Chronology of the universe"), i.e., when the universe was less than one Planck time old. Describing the universe during the Planck epoch requires a theory of [quantum gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_gravity "Quantum gravity") that would incorporate quantum effects into [general relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity "General relativity"). Such a theory does not yet exist.

Several quantities are not "extreme" in magnitude, such as the Planck mass, which is about 22 micrograms: very large in comparison with subatomic particles, and within the mass range of living organisms.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-Penrose2005-21): 872  Similarly, the related units of energy and of momentum are in the range of some everyday phenomena.

Significance
------------

Planck units have little [anthropocentric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropocentric "Anthropocentric") arbitrariness, but do still involve some arbitrary choices in terms of the defining constants. Unlike the [metre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metre "Metre") and [second](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second "Second"), which exist as [base units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SI_base_unit "SI base unit") in the [SI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SI "SI") system for historical reasons, the [Planck length](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_length "Planck length") and Planck time are conceptually linked at a fundamental physical level. Consequently, natural units help physicists to reframe questions. [Frank Wilczek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Wilczek "Frank Wilczek") puts it succinctly:

> We see that the question [posed] is not, "Why is gravity so feeble?" but rather, "Why is the proton's mass so small?" For in natural (Planck) units, the strength of gravity simply is what it is, a primary quantity, while the proton's mass is the tiny number 1/13 [quintillion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Names_of_large_numbers "Names of large numbers").[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-22)

While it is true that the electrostatic repulsive force between two protons (alone in free space) greatly exceeds the gravitational attractive force between the same two protons, this is not about the relative strengths of the two fundamental forces.

When Planck proposed his units, the goal was only that of establishing a universal ("natural") way of measuring objects, without giving any special meaning to quantities that measured one single unit. During the 1950s, multiple authors including [Lev Landau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lev_Landau "Lev Landau") and [Oskar Klein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oskar_Klein "Oskar Klein") argued that quantities on the order of the Planck scale indicated the limits of the validity of quantum field theory. [John Archibald Wheeler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Archibald_Wheeler "John Archibald Wheeler") proposed in 1955 that quantum fluctuations of spacetime become significant at the Planck scale, though at the time he was unaware of the Planck units.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-Gorelik-23)[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-Wheeler-24)

Planck scale
------------

In [particle physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particle_physics "Particle physics") and [physical cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_cosmology "Physical cosmology"), the Planck scale is an [energy scale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_scale "Energy scale") around 1.22×10 28[eV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronvolt "Electronvolt") (the Planck energy, corresponding to the [energy equivalent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass%E2%80%93energy_equivalence "Mass–energy equivalence") of the Planck mass, 2.176 45×10−8 kg) at which [quantum effects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_effects "Quantum effects") of [gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravity "Gravity") become significant. At this scale, present descriptions and theories of sub-atomic particle interactions in terms of [quantum field theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_field_theory "Quantum field theory") break down and become inadequate, due to the impact of the apparent [non-renormalizability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renormalization "Renormalization") of gravity within current theories.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-:1-20)

### Relationship to gravity

At the Planck length scale, the strength of gravity is expected to become comparable with the other forces, and it has been theorized that all the fundamental forces are unified at that scale, but the exact mechanism of this unification remains unknown.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-25) The Planck scale is therefore the point at which the effects of quantum gravity can no longer be ignored in other [fundamental interactions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fundamental_interactions "Fundamental interactions"), where current calculations and approaches begin to break down, and a means to take account of its impact is necessary.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-26) On these grounds, it has been speculated that it may be an [approximate lower limit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micro_black_hole#Minimum_mass_of_a_black_hole "Micro black hole") at which a black hole could be formed by collapse.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-particlecreate-27)

While physicists have a fairly good understanding of the other fundamental interactions of forces on the quantum level, [gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravity "Gravity") is problematic, and cannot be integrated with [quantum mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_mechanics "Quantum mechanics") at very high energies using the usual framework of quantum field theory. At lesser energy levels it is usually ignored, while for energies approaching or exceeding the Planck scale, a new theory of [quantum gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_gravity "Quantum gravity") is necessary. Approaches to this problem include [string theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/String_theory "String theory") and [M-theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M-theory "M-theory"), [loop quantum gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loop_quantum_gravity "Loop quantum gravity"), [noncommutative geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noncommutative_geometry "Noncommutative geometry"), and [causal set theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Causal_sets "Causal sets").[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-scholarpedia-28)

### In cosmology

In [Big Bang cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Bang_cosmology "Big Bang cosmology"), the **Planck epoch** or **Planck era** is the earliest stage of the [Big Bang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Bang "Big Bang"), before the [time passed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_time "Cosmic time") was equal to the Planck time, _t_ P, or approximately 10−43 seconds.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-Planck-UOregon-29) There is no currently available physical theory to describe such short times, and it is not clear in what sense the concept of [time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time "Time") is meaningful for values smaller than the Planck time. It is generally assumed that [quantum effects of gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_gravity "Quantum gravity") dominate physical interactions at this time scale. At this scale, the [unified force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_unification "Grand unification") of the [Standard Model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_Model "Standard Model") is assumed to be [unified with gravitation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theory_of_everything "Theory of everything"). Immeasurably hot and dense, the state of the Planck epoch was succeeded by the [grand unification epoch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_unification_epoch "Grand unification epoch"), where gravitation is separated from the unified force of the Standard Model, in turn followed by the [inflationary epoch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inflationary_epoch "Inflationary epoch"), which ended after about 10−32 seconds (or about 10 11 _t_ P).[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-KolbTurner1994-30)

### Analysis of the units

#### Planck length

The Planck length is about 10−20 times the diameter of a [proton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proton "Proton").[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-baez1999-34) It can be motivated in various ways, such as considering a particle whose [reduced Compton wavelength](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reduced_Compton_wavelength "Reduced Compton wavelength") is comparable to its [Schwarzschild radius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schwarzschild_radius "Schwarzschild radius"),[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-baez1999-34)[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-Adler2010-35)[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-36) though whether those concepts are in fact simultaneously applicable is open to debate.[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-37) (The same heuristic argument simultaneously motivates the Planck mass.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-Adler2010-35))

The Planck length is a distance scale of interest in speculations about quantum gravity. The [Bekenstein–Hawking entropy of a black hole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_hole_thermodynamics "Black hole thermodynamics") is one-fourth the area of its [event horizon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Event_horizon "Event horizon") in units of Planck length squared.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-:0-11): 370  Since the 1950s, it has been conjectured that quantum fluctuations of the spacetime metric might make the familiar notion of distance inapplicable below the Planck length.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-Wheeler-24)[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-38)[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-Gorelik-23) This is sometimes expressed by saying that "spacetime becomes a [foam at the Planck scale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_foam "Quantum foam")".[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-39) It is possible that the Planck length is the shortest physically measurable distance, since any attempt to investigate the possible existence of shorter distances, by performing higher-energy collisions, would result in black hole production. Higher-energy collisions, rather than splitting matter into finer pieces, would simply produce bigger black holes.[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-40)

The strings of [string theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/String_theory "String theory") are modeled to be on the order of the Planck length.[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-41)[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-42) In theories with [large extra dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_extra_dimension "Large extra dimension"), the Planck length calculated from the observed value of ![Image 32: {\displaystyle G}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f5f3c8921a3b352de45446a6789b104458c9f90b) can be smaller than the true, fundamental Planck length.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-:0-11): 61 [[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-43)

#### Planck time

No current physical theory adequately describes the earliest period of the Big Bang model on the order of the Planck time.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-Planck-UOregon-29)

#### Planck energy

The Planck energy _E_ P is approximately equal to the energy released in the combustion of the fuel in an automobile fuel tank (57.2 L at 34.2 MJ/L of chemical energy). The [ultra-high-energy cosmic ray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultra-high-energy_cosmic_ray "Ultra-high-energy cosmic ray")[observed in 1991](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oh-My-God_particle "Oh-My-God particle") had a measured energy of about 50 J, equivalent to about 2.5×10−8 _E_ P.[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-44)[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-45)

Proposals for theories of [doubly special relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doubly_special_relativity "Doubly special relativity") posit that, in addition to the speed of light, an energy scale is also invariant for all inertial observers. Typically, this energy scale is chosen to be the Planck energy.[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-46)[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-47)

#### Planck unit of force

The Planck unit of force is the gravitational attractive force of two bodies of 1 Planck mass each that are held 1 Planck length apart. One convention for the Planck charge is to choose it so that the electrostatic repulsion of two objects with Planck charge and mass that are held 1 Planck length apart balances the Newtonian attraction between them.[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-48)

Some authors have argued that the Planck force is on the order of the maximum force that can occur between two bodies.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-49)[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-50) However, the validity of these conjectures has been disputed.[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-51)[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-52)

#### Planck temperature

At the Planck temperature, the wavelength of light emitted by [thermal radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_radiation "Thermal radiation") reaches the Planck length. There are no known physical models able to describe temperatures greater than _T_ P; a [quantum theory of gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_theory_of_gravity "Quantum theory of gravity") would be required to model the extreme energies attained.[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-53) Hypothetically, a system in [thermal equilibrium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_equilibrium "Thermal equilibrium") at the Planck temperature might contain Planck-scale black holes, constantly being formed from thermal radiation and decaying via [Hawking evaporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawking_radiation "Hawking radiation"). Adding energy to such a system might _decrease_ its temperature by creating larger black holes, whose Hawking temperature is lower.[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-54)

Nondimensionalized equations
----------------------------

Physical quantities that have different dimensions (such as time and length) cannot be equated even if they are numerically equal (e.g., 1 second is not the same as 1 metre). In theoretical physics, however, this scruple may be set aside, by a process called [nondimensionalization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nondimensionalization "Nondimensionalization"). The effective result is that many fundamental equations of physics, which often include some of the constants used to define Planck units, become equations where these constants are replaced by a 1.

Examples include the [energy–momentum relation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy%E2%80%93momentum_relation "Energy–momentum relation")![Image 33: {\displaystyle E^{2}=(mc^{2})^{2}+(pc)^{2}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5a3bbbd27c9f1455a3d3ea919509609163767f76) (which becomes ![Image 34: {\displaystyle E^{2}=m^{2}+p^{2}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8f2ff27326061cb0966d0921362fbcc8450ccda0)) and the [Dirac equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dirac_equation "Dirac equation")![Image 35: {\displaystyle (i\hbar \gamma ^{\mu }\partial _{\mu }-mc)\psi =0}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/e4ac38859ceaaaaffca56a77087d640db903af72) (which becomes ![Image 36: {\displaystyle (i\gamma ^{\mu }\partial _{\mu }-m)\psi =0}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8c772bdcc5961dc3f405d5d9ed22cea568a8de01)).

Alternative choices of normalization
------------------------------------

As already stated above, Planck units are derived by "normalizing" the numerical values of certain fundamental constants to 1. These normalizations are neither the only ones possible nor necessarily the best. Moreover, the choice of what factors to normalize, among the factors appearing in the fundamental equations of physics, is not evident, and the values of the Planck units are sensitive to this choice.

The factor 4 π is ubiquitous in [theoretical physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theoretical_physics "Theoretical physics") because in three-dimensional space, the surface area of a [sphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sphere "Sphere") of radius _r_ is 4 π _r_ 2. This, along with the concept of [flux](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flux "Flux"), are the basis for the [inverse-square law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inverse-square_law "Inverse-square law"), [Gauss's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauss%27s_law "Gauss's law"), and the [divergence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divergence "Divergence") operator applied to [flux density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flux_density "Flux density"). For example, [gravitational](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitational_field "Gravitational field") and [electrostatic fields](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrostatic_field "Electrostatic field") produced by point objects have spherical symmetry, and so the electric flux through a sphere of radius _r_ around a point charge will be distributed uniformly over that sphere. From this, it follows that a factor of 4 π _r_ 2 will appear in the denominator of Coulomb's law in [rationalized form](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heaviside%E2%80%93Lorentz_units#Maxwell's_equations "Heaviside–Lorentz units").[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-John_D_2002-31): 214–15  (Both the numerical factor and the power of the dependence on _r_ would change if space were higher-dimensional; the correct expressions can be deduced from the geometry of [higher-dimensional spheres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-sphere "N-sphere").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-:0-11): 51 ) Likewise for Newton's law of universal gravitation: a factor of 4 π naturally appears in [Poisson's equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poisson%27s_equation "Poisson's equation") when relating the gravitational potential to the distribution of matter.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-:0-11): 56

Hence a substantial body of physical theory developed since Planck's 1899 paper suggests normalizing not _G_ but 4 π _G_ (or 8 π _G_) to 1. Doing so would introduce a factor of ⁠1/4 π⁠ (or ⁠1/8 π⁠) into the nondimensionalized form of the law of universal gravitation, consistent with the modern rationalized formulation of Coulomb's law in terms of the vacuum permittivity. In fact, alternative normalizations frequently preserve the factor of ⁠1/4 π⁠ in the nondimensionalized form of Coulomb's law as well, so that the nondimensionalized Maxwell's equations for electromagnetism and [gravitoelectromagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitoelectromagnetism "Gravitoelectromagnetism") both take the same form as those for electromagnetism in SI, which do not have any factors of 4 π. When this is applied to electromagnetic constants, _ε_ 0, this unit system is called "_rationalized_". When applied additionally to gravitation and Planck units, these are called **rationalized Planck units**[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-55) and are seen in high-energy physics.[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-56)

The rationalized Planck units are defined so that _c_ = 4 _πG_ = _ħ_ = _ε_ 0 = _k_ B = 1.

There are several possible alternative normalizations.

### Gravitational constant

In 1899, Newton's law of universal gravitation was still seen as exact, rather than as a convenient approximation holding for "small" velocities and masses (the approximate nature of Newton's law was shown following the development of [general relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity "General relativity") in 1915). Hence Planck normalized to 1 the [gravitational constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitational_constant "Gravitational constant")_G_ in Newton's law. In theories emerging after 1899, _G_ nearly always appears in formulae multiplied by 4 π or a small integer multiple thereof. Hence, a choice to be made when designing a system of natural units is which, if any, instances of 4 π appearing in the equations of physics are to be eliminated via the normalization.

*   Normalizing 4 π _G_ to 1: 
    *   [Gauss's law for gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauss%27s_law_for_gravity "Gauss's law for gravity") becomes _Φ_**g** = −_M_ (rather than _Φ_**g** = −4 π _M_ in Planck units).
    *   Eliminates 4 π _G_ from the [Poisson equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poisson_equation "Poisson equation").
    *   Eliminates 4 π _G_ in the [gravitoelectromagnetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitoelectromagnetism "Gravitoelectromagnetism") (GEM) equations, which hold in weak [gravitational fields](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitational_field "Gravitational field") or [locally flat spacetime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Locally_flat_spacetime "Locally flat spacetime"). These equations have the same form as Maxwell's equations (and the [Lorentz force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lorentz_force "Lorentz force") equation) of [electromagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetism "Electromagnetism"), with [mass density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_density "Mass density") replacing [charge density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charge_density "Charge density"), and with ⁠1/4 π _G_⁠ replacing _ε_ 0.
    *   Normalizes the [characteristic impedance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Characteristic_impedance "Characteristic impedance")_Z_**g** of [gravitational radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitational_radiation "Gravitational radiation") in free space to 1 (normally expressed as ⁠4 π _G_/_c_⁠).[[b]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_time#cite_note-59)
    *   Eliminates 4 π _G_ from the Bekenstein–Hawking formula (for the [entropy of a black hole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_hole_thermodynamics "Black hole thermodynamics") in terms of its mass _m_ BH and the area of its [event horizon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Event_horizon "Event horizon")_A_ BH) which is simplified to _S_ BH = π _A_ BH = (_m_ BH)2.

*   Setting 8 π _G_ = 1. This would eliminate 8 π _G_ from the [Einstein field equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_field_equations "Einstein field equations"), [Einstein–Hilbert action](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein%E2%80%93Hilbert_action "Einstein–Hilbert action"), and the [Friedmann equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedmann_equations "Friedmann equations"), for gravitation. Planck units modified so that 8 π _G_ = 1 are known as _reduced Planck units_, because the Planck mass is divided by ![Image 37: {\displaystyle {\sqrt {8\pi }}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7901b9eceb5e1d32986dce4674fec59091dd3a68). Also, the [Bekenstein–Hawking formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bekenstein%E2%80%93Hawking_formula "Bekenstein–Hawking formula") for the entropy of a black hole simplifies to _S_ BH = (_m_ BH)2/2 = 2 π _A_ BH.
