From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Region of Earth's atmosphere

**Upper atmosphere** is a collective term that refers to various layers of the [atmosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmosphere "Atmosphere") of the Earth above the [troposphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troposphere "Troposphere")[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_atmosphere#cite_note-Morton_2022-1) and corresponding regions of the atmospheres of other planets, and includes:

*   The [mesosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesosphere "Mesosphere"), which on Earth lies between the altitudes of about 50 and 80 kilometres (31 and 50 mi), sometimes considered part of the "middle atmosphere" rather than the upper atmosphere
*   The [thermosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermosphere "Thermosphere"), which on Earth lies between the altitudes of about 80 and 700 kilometres (50 and 435 mi)
*   The [exosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exosphere "Exosphere"), which on Earth lies between the altitudes of about 700 kilometres (435 mi) and 10,000 kilometres (6,200 mi)
*   The [ionosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionosphere "Ionosphere"), an ionized portion of the upper atmosphere which includes the upper mesosphere, thermosphere, and lower exosphere and on Earth lies between the altitudes of 50 and 1,000 kilometres (31 and 621 mi)
