In the 19th century, Charles Hermite found a proof that requires no prerequisite knowledge beyond basic calculus . Three simplifications of Hermite's proof are due to Mary Cartwright , Ivan Niven , and Nicolas Bourbaki . Another proof, which is a simplification of Lambert's proof, is due to Miklós Laczkovich . Hermite did not present his proof as an end in itself but as an afterthought within his search for a proof of the transcendence of π π .

{\displaystyle \pi .} He discussed the recurrence relations to motivate and to obtain a convenient integral representation.
