In [theoretical physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theoretical_physics "Theoretical physics"), **F-theory** is a branch of [string theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/String_theory "String theory") developed by Iranian-American physicist [Cumrun Vafa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cumrun_Vafa "Cumrun Vafa").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F-theory#cite_note-1) The new [vacua](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vacua "Vacua") described by F-theory were discovered by Vafa and allowed string theorists to construct new realistic vacua — in the form of F-theory compactified on elliptically fibered [Calabi–Yau four-folds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calabi%E2%80%93Yau_manifold "Calabi–Yau manifold"). The letter "F" supposedly stands for "Father" in relation to ["Mother"-theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M-theory "M-theory").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F-theory#cite_note-2)

Compactifications
-----------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=F-theory&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Compactifications")]

Main article: [Compactification (physics)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compactification_(physics) "Compactification (physics)")

F-theory is formally a 12-dimensional theory, but the only way to obtain an acceptable background is to [compactify](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compactification_(physics) "Compactification (physics)") this theory on a [two-torus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torus "Torus"). By doing so, one obtains [type IIB](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Type_IIB_string "Type IIB string")[superstring theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superstring_theory "Superstring theory") in 10 dimensions. The [SL(2,Z)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SL(2,Z) "SL(2,Z)")[S-duality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S-duality "S-duality") symmetry of the resulting type IIB string theory is manifest because it arises as the group of [large diffeomorphisms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_diffeomorphism "Large diffeomorphism") of the two-dimensional [torus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torus "Torus").

More generally, one can compactify F-theory on an elliptically fibered [manifold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifold "Manifold") ([elliptic fibration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptic_fibration "Elliptic fibration")), i.e. a [fiber bundle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiber_bundle "Fiber bundle") whose fiber is a two-dimensional torus (also called an [elliptic curve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptic_curve "Elliptic curve")). For example, a subclass of the [K3 manifolds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K3_manifold "K3 manifold") is elliptically fibered, and F-theory on a K3 manifold is dual to [heterotic string](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heterotic_string "Heterotic string") theory on a two-torus. Also, the moduli spaces of those theories should be isomorphic.

The large number of semirealistic solutions to string theory referred to as the [string theory landscape](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/String_theory_landscape "String theory landscape"), with 10 272,000{\displaystyle 10^{272,000}}![Image 8: {\displaystyle 10^{272,000}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c7ea2462969aa025b37c43ee26b6a3e026316b53) elements or so, is dominated by F-theory compactifications on [Calabi–Yau four-folds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calabi%E2%80%93Yau_four-fold "Calabi–Yau four-fold").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F-theory#cite_note-3) There are about 10 15{\displaystyle 10^{15}}![Image 9: {\displaystyle 10^{15}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f50f542aca44501ae585479ee4e36e60b11646f7) of those solutions consistent with the Standard Model of particle physics.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F-theory#cite_note-4)

Phenomenology
-------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=F-theory&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Phenomenology")]

New models of [Grand Unified Theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Unified_Theory "Grand Unified Theory") have recently been developed using F-theory.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F-theory#cite_note-5)

Extra time dimension
--------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=F-theory&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Extra time dimension")]

F-theory has the [metric signature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metric_signature "Metric signature") (10,2), which means that it includes a [second time dimension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_time_dimensions "Multiple time dimensions").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F-theory#cite_note-6)
