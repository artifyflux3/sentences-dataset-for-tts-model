Cybergeddon (from tech.

cyber- , lit. "computer"; Hebrew: Megiddo , extracted from Har Megiddo ("mountain of final battle")) refers to cataclysm resulting from a large-scale sabotage of all computerized networks, systems and activities. It combines cyberterrorism , cyberwarfare , cybercrime , and hacktivism into scenarios of wide-scale internet disruption or economic collapse .

[ 1 ] Economic or industrial infrastructure could be targeted, such as banks [ 2 ] or industrial control systems .

[ 3 ] Since 2012, the number of Internet-based attacks and their complexity has increased.

[ 4 ] The Defense Technical Information Center cited nuclear electromagnetic pulse attacks as a part of the military action that may bring about cybergeddon.
