[ 2 ] Its members are strictly anaerobic and thermophilic , growing at optimal temperatures between 55 °C and 70 °C.

[ 3 ] The name of this phylum is based on an early genus, dubbed " Coprothermobacter ", a term whose etymology derives from the Greek words " kopros ", meaning manure, and " thermos ", warm, referring to the fact that these bacteria are capable of living at relatively high temperatures, with a maximum growth temperature of 75 °C.

[ 4 ] Notes [ edit ] In October 2021, the name of this phylum has been accepted as validly published , according to the emendations of the rules of the International Code of Nomenclature of Prokaryotes proposed to include the rank of phylum.
