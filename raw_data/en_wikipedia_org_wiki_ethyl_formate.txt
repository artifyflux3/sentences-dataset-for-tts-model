Chemical compound

**Ethyl formate** is an [ester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ester "Ester") formed when [ethanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanol "Ethanol") (an [alcohol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcohol_(chemistry) "Alcohol (chemistry)")) reacts with [formic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formic_acid "Formic acid") (a [carboxylic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carboxylic_acid "Carboxylic acid")). Ethyl formate has the characteristic smell of [rum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rum "Rum") and is partially responsible for the flavor of [raspberries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry "Raspberry"),[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethyl_formate#cite_note-guardian-3) occurring naturally in some plant oils, fruits, and juices.

Exposure
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ethyl_formate&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Exposure")]

Ethyl formate is [generally recognized as safe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generally_recognized_as_safe "Generally recognized as safe") by the [U.S. Food and Drug Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration_(United_States) "Food and Drug Administration (United States)").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethyl_formate#cite_note-alt-4)

According to the [U.S Occupational Safety and Health Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupational_Safety_and_Health_Administration "Occupational Safety and Health Administration") (OSHA), ethyl formate can irritate eyes, skin, [mucous membranes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mucous_membrane "Mucous membrane"), and the [respiratory system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Respiratory_system "Respiratory system") of humans and other animals; it is also a [central nervous system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_nervous_system "Central nervous system")[depressant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Depressant "Depressant").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethyl_formate#cite_note-osha-5) In industry, it is used as a solvent for [cellulose nitrate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellulose_nitrate "Cellulose nitrate"), [cellulose acetate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellulose_acetate "Cellulose acetate"), oils, and greases. It can be used as a substitute for [acetone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetone "Acetone"); workers may also be exposed to it under the following circumstances:[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethyl_formate#cite_note-osha-5)

*   during spray, brush, or dip applications of [lacquers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lacquer "Lacquer")
*   during the manufacture of [safety glass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Safety_glass "Safety glass")
*   when [fumigating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fumigation "Fumigation") tobacco, cereals, and [dried fruits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dried_fruit "Dried fruit") (as an alternative to [methyl bromide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_bromide "Methyl bromide") under the [U.S. Department of Agriculture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Agriculture "United States Department of Agriculture") quarantine system[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethyl_formate#cite_note-alt-4))

OSHA considers a time-weighted average of 100 [parts per million](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parts_per_million "Parts per million") (300 milligrams per cubic meter) over an eight-hour period as the [permissible exposure limit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Permissible_exposure_limit "Permissible exposure limit"). The [U.S. National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Institute_for_Occupational_Safety_and_Health "National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health") (NIOSH) also considers a time-weighted average of 100 ppm over an eight-hour period as the [recommended exposure limit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recommended_exposure_limit "Recommended exposure limit").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethyl_formate#cite_note-6)

In space
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ethyl_formate&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: In space")]

Ethyl formate has been identified in dust clouds in an area of the Milky Way galaxy called [Sagittarius B2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagittarius_B2 "Sagittarius B2"). It is among 50 molecular species identified using the [30 metre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IRAM_30-m "IRAM 30-m")[IRAM radiotelescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institut_de_radioastronomie_millim%C3%A9trique "Institut de radioastronomie millimétrique").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethyl_formate#cite_note-guardian-3)
