He was recommended by Muhammad , and treated Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas and Abu Bakr . When the latter was dying, he designated his illness as poisoning.

Life and career [ edit ] Trained in Yemen , [ 1 ] he is reported to have written a book named Dialog in Medicine . He was also a student and teacher at the Academy of Gundishapur in Persia .

[ citation needed ] He was half brother of Abu Bakra al-Thaqafi (also known as Nufay ibn al-Harith).

[ 2 ] Some historians maintain that he received his medical education at the Jundishapur medical school of Persia where he learnt the teachings of Aristotle and Galen .
