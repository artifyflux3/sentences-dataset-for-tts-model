In the early years it was published by Williams and Norgate , 14 Henrietta Street, London, with the U.S. Agent being Sherman, French & Co,. 6 Beacon Street, Boston, Mass . The subscription c. 1911 was "Ten Shillings per annum, post free".

[ 1 ] It ceased publication in 1968.

[ 2 ] Key figures [ edit ] The Hibbert Journal was, from October 1902 to January 1948, edited by L. P. Jacks .

[ 3 ] [ 4 ] Philosopher Dawes Hicks assisted him as sub-editor from its beginnings until his death in 1941.
