**W. H. Freeman and Company** is an [imprint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imprint_(trade_name) "Imprint (trade name)") of Macmillan Higher Education, a division of [Macmillan Publishers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macmillan_Publishers "Macmillan Publishers"). Macmillan publishes [monographs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monograph "Monograph") and [textbooks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Textbook "Textbook") for the sciences under the imprint.

History
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=W._H._Freeman_and_Company&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

The company, W. H. Freeman and Company Publishing[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._H._Freeman#cite_note-1) was founded in 1946 by [William H. Freeman Jr.](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=William_H._Freeman_(publisher)&action=edit&redlink=1 "William H. Freeman (publisher) (page does not exist)"),[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._H._Freeman#cite_note-2) who had been a salesman and editor at [Macmillan Publishing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macmillan_Publishing "Macmillan Publishing").

Freeman later founded _Freeman, Cooper and Company_ in San Francisco.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._H._Freeman#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._H._Freeman#cite_note-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._H._Freeman#cite_note-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._H._Freeman#cite_note-6)

Works
-----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=W._H._Freeman_and_Company&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Works")]

Titles published by W. H. Freeman include [James Watson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Watson "James Watson")'s _Recombinant DNA_ (1983), William J. Kaufmann III's _The Universe_ (1985), Jon Rogawski's _Calculus_ (2007), and [Peter Atkins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Atkins "Peter Atkins")’ _Physical Chemistry_ (2014).[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._H._Freeman#cite_note-7)
