From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Geometric object moved along a path to generate a shape

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg/40px-Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg)

Look up _**[generatrix](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/generatrix "wiktionary:generatrix")**_ in Wiktionary, the free dictionary.

In [geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometry "Geometry"), a **generatrix** ([/dʒ ɛ n ə ˈ r eɪ t r ɪ k s/](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English")) or **describent** is a [point](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point_(geometry) "Point (geometry)"), [curve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curve "Curve") or [surface](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surface_(mathematics) "Surface (mathematics)") that, when moved along a given path, generates a new [shape](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shape "Shape").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generatrix#cite_note-1) The path directing the motion of the generatrix motion is called a **directrix** or **dirigent**.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generatrix#cite_note-2)

Examples
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Generatrix&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Examples")]

A [cone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cone "Cone") can be generated by moving a line (the generatrix) fixed at the future apex of the cone along a closed curve (the directrix); if that directrix is a circle perpendicular to the line connecting its center to the apex, the motion is [rotation around a fixed axis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotation_around_a_fixed_axis "Rotation around a fixed axis") and the resulting shape is a circular cone.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generatrix#cite_note-3)

The generatrix of a [cylinder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cylinder_(geometry) "Cylinder (geometry)"), a limiting case of a cone, is a line that is kept [parallel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallel_(geometry) "Parallel (geometry)") to some axis.
