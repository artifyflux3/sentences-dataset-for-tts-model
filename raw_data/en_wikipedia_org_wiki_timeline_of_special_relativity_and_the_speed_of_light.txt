From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

[![Image 1](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Einstein_en_Lorentz.jpg/250px-Einstein_en_Lorentz.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Einstein_en_Lorentz.jpg)

[Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein") and [Hendrik Lorentz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hendrik_Lorentz "Hendrik Lorentz") in 1921 in [Leiden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leiden "Leiden")

This timeline describes the major developments, both experimental and theoretical, of:

*   [Einstein’s](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein")[special theory of relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_relativity "Special relativity") (SR),
*   its predecessors like the theories of [luminiferous aether](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luminiferous_aether "Luminiferous aether"),
*   its early competitors, i.e.: 
    *   Ritz’s [ballistic theory of light](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emission_theory_(relativity) "Emission theory (relativity)"),
    *   the models of [electromagnetic mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetic_mass "Electromagnetic mass") created by [Abraham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Abraham "Max Abraham") (1902), [Lorentz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hendrik_Lorentz "Hendrik Lorentz") (1904), [Bucherer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_Bucherer "Alfred Bucherer") (1904) and [Langevin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Langevin "Paul Langevin") (1904).

This list also mentions the origins of standard notation (like _c_) and terminology (like _theory of relavity_).

Criteria for inclusion
----------------------

Task Force One, the world's first nuclear-powered task force. [_Enterprise_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Enterprise_(CVN-65) "USS Enterprise (CVN-65)"), [_Long Beach_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Long_Beach_(CGN-9) "USS Long Beach (CGN-9)") and [_Bainbridge_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Bainbridge_(CGN-25) "USS Bainbridge (CGN-25)") in formation in the Mediterranean, 18 June 1964. _Enterprise_ crew members are spelling out Einstein's mass–energy equivalence formula _E_ = _mc_ 2 on the flight deck.

Theories other than SR are not described here exhaustively, but only to the extent that is directly relevant to SR – i.e. at points when they:

*   anticipated some elements of SR, like Fresnel’s hypothesis of partial aether drag,
*   led to new experiments testing SR, like Stokes’s model of complete aether drag,
*   were disproved or questioned, e.g. by the experiments of [Oliver Lodge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oliver_Lodge "Oliver Lodge").

For a more detailed timeline of [aether theories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aether_theories "Aether theories") – e.g. their emergence with the [wave theory of light](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_optics "Physical optics") – see a [separate article](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_luminiferous_aether "Timeline of luminiferous aether"). Also, not all experiments are listed here – repetitions, even with much higher precision than the original, are mentioned only if they influence or challenge the opinions at their time. It was the case with:

*   Michelson and Morley (1886) repeating the experiment of Fizeau (1851), contradicting Michelson’s interpretation of his 1881 experiment;
*   Michelson–Morley (1887), more conclusive than the original experiment by Michelson (1881) and difficult to reconcile with their experiment of 1886, or other first-order measurements;
*   Kaufmann’s 1906 repetition of his 1902 experiment, because he claimed to contradict the model of Einstein and Lorentz, considered consistent with the data from 1902;
*   Miller (1933) or Marinov (1974), with results different than Michelson–Morley.

For lists of repetitions, see the articles of particular experiments. The measurements of speed of light are also mentioned only to the minimum extent, i.e. when they proved for the first time that _c_ is finite and invariant. Innovations like the use of [Foucault's rotating mirror](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foucault%27s_measurements_of_the_speed_of_light "Foucault's measurements of the speed of light") or the [Fizeau wheel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fizeau%27s_measurement_of_the_speed_of_light_in_air "Fizeau's measurement of the speed of light in air") are not listed here – see the article about [speed of light](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speed_of_light "Speed of light").

This timeline also ignores, for reasons of volume and clarity:

*   the long story of [spacetime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacetime "Spacetime") and the concept of time as the fourth dimension; e.g. the ideas of [Lagrange](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Louis_Lagrange "Joseph Louis Lagrange") and [Wells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H._G._Wells "H. G. Wells");
*   mathematical innovations that influenced the formalism of SR, e.g. the introduction of [fibre bundles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fibre_bundle "Fibre bundle");
*   indirect evidence for SR, through the evidence for relativistic theories like [general relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity "General relativity") or [relativistic quantum mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relativistic_quantum_mechanics "Relativistic quantum mechanics");
*   publication of countless textbooks and popular science books or articles, even very influential classics like _[Mr Tompkins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mr\_Tompkins "Mr Tompkins")_ by [George Gamow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Gamow "George Gamow");
*   the cultural impact of SR, e.g. publication of documentaries or commemorations of SR during the [World Year of Physics 2005](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Year_of_Physics_2005 "World Year of Physics 2005");
*   new, untested theories modifying SR like [Doubly special relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doubly_special_relativity "Doubly special relativity") or [Variable speed of light](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variable_speed_of_light "Variable speed of light").

Before the 19th century
-----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Before the 19th century")]

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Illustration_from_1676_article_on_Ole_R%C3%B8mer%27s_measurement_of_the_speed_of_light.jpg/250px-Illustration_from_1676_article_on_Ole_R%C3%B8mer%27s_measurement_of_the_speed_of_light.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Illustration_from_1676_article_on_Ole_R%C3%B8mer%27s_measurement_of_the_speed_of_light.jpg)

A redrawn version of the illustration from the 1676 news report. Rømer compared the apparent duration of Io's orbits as Earth moved towards Jupiter (F to G) and as Earth moved away from Jupiter (L to K).

*   1632 – [Galileo Galilei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galileo_Galilei "Galileo Galilei") writes about the relativity of motion and that some forms of motion are undetectable; this would be later called the [relativity principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relativity_principle "Relativity principle"), essential for special relativity as one of its postulates.
*   1674 – [Robert Hooke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Hooke "Robert Hooke") makes his observations of the [Gamma Draconis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamma_Draconis "Gamma Draconis") star, or γ Draconis for short. He proves a variation in its position on the sky, which would be later identified as [stellar aberration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aberration_(astronomy) "Aberration (astronomy)").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-1)
*   1676 – [Ole Rømer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ole_R%C3%B8mer "Ole Rømer") gives [the first piece of evidence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R%C3%B8mer%27s_determination_of_the_speed_of_light "Rømer's determination of the speed of light") that the [speed of light](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speed_of_light "Speed of light") is finite, through his observation of the [moons of Jupiter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moons_of_Jupiter "Moons of Jupiter");[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-2) the discovery divides scientists of his time.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-3)
*   1690 – [Christiaan Huygens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christiaan_Huygens "Christiaan Huygens") gives the first estimate of the speed of light in air or vacuum, based on Rømer’s work. The result is equivalent to about 2×10 8 m/s in modern units, correct only to the [order of magnitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_of_magnitude "Order of magnitude").
*   1727 – [James Bradley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Bradley "James Bradley") correctly identifies the peculiar behaviour of γ Draconis as stellar aberration. Bradley uses this fact to estimate the speed of light in air or vacuum, and his result is more [accurate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accuracy "Accuracy") than Huygens’s: about 3.0×10 8 m/s in modern units. For the first time, the measurement is correct to the first two [significant figures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Significant_figures "Significant figures").

*   1810 – [François Arago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Arago "François Arago") observes that the speed of light of stars – measured with stellar aberration – may be independent of the relative motion of stars and the Earth; or at least, no differences are observable with a naked eye.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-4)
*   1818 – [Augustin-Jean Fresnel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augustin-Jean_Fresnel "Augustin-Jean Fresnel") proposes his model of partial aether dragging to explain Arago’s finding.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-5)
*   1845 – [George Gabriel Stokes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Gabriel_Stokes "George Gabriel Stokes") creates his own model of complete aether dragging.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-stokes1845-6)
*   1851 – The [Fizeau experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fizeau_experiment "Fizeau experiment") with light in flowing water confirms Fresnel’s model.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-fiz1-7)
*   1861 – [James Clerk Maxwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Clerk_Maxwell "James Clerk Maxwell") publishes [his equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maxwell%27s_equations "Maxwell's equations") of the [electromagnetic field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetic_field "Electromagnetic field"), which had a great impact on the later works on aether and special relativity.
*   1868 – [Martinus Hoek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martinus_Hoek "Martinus Hoek") modifies the experiment of Fizeau, with the same conclusions.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-hoek-8)
*   1871 – [George Biddell Airy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Biddell_Airy "George Biddell Airy") observes the [stellar aberration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_aberration "Stellar aberration") in a telescope filled with water, confirming Fresnel’s model and contradicting Stokes’s.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-Airy-9)

[![Image 4](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Michelson_morley_experiment_1887.jpg/250px-Michelson_morley_experiment_1887.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Michelson_morley_experiment_1887.jpg)

Michelson and Morley's [interferometric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interferometer "Interferometer") setup, mounted on a stone slab that floats in an annular trough of [mercury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercury_(element) "Mercury (element)")

*   1881 – [Albert Michelson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Michelson "Albert Michelson") performs his original [interferometric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interferometry "Interferometry") experiment. It detects no aether wind, contradicting Fresnel’s model in favour of Stokes’s.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-michel1-10)
*   1885 – [Ludwig Lange](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludwig_Lange_(physicist) "Ludwig Lange (physicist)") introduces the idea of [inertial frame of reference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inertial_frame_of_reference "Inertial frame of reference").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-11)[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-12) It is essential to relativity as an element of the modern formulation of the relativity principle.
*   1886 – Albert Michelson and [Edward Morley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Morley "Edward Morley") repeat the Fizeau experiment with higher precision, confirming its result and contradicting the earlier conclusions of Michelson.
*   1887 – [Woldemar Voigt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woldemar_Voigt "Woldemar Voigt") publishes his coordinate transformations preserving the [wave equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wave_equation "Wave equation"). They are very similar – but not equivalent – to the later [Lorentz transformations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lorentz_transformations "Lorentz transformations").[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-doppler-13)
*   1887 – the [Michelson–Morley experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michelson%E2%80%93Morley_experiment "Michelson–Morley experiment") fails to detect aether wind, disproving some aether theories and leading to new ones.
*   1889 – [George FitzGerald](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Francis_FitzGerald "George Francis FitzGerald") conjectures the [length contraction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Length_contraction "Length contraction") to explain the Michelson–Morley experiment.

*   1892 – [Hendrik Lorentz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hendrik_Lorentz "Hendrik Lorentz") – independently of FitzGerald – proposes the same explanation, with a formula only approximating the special-relativistic length contraction to the first order.
*   1893 – [Oliver Lodge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oliver_Lodge "Oliver Lodge") makes an interferometric experiment questioning the [aether drag hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aether_drag_hypothesis "Aether drag hypothesis").
*   1894 – [Paul Drude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Drude "Paul Drude") introduces the symbol _c_ for speed of light in vacuum.
*   1895 – Hendrik Lorentz corrects his 1892 model, proposing a contraction by the [Lorentz factor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lorentz_factor "Lorentz factor") (γ).
*   1895 – [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein") probably makes his [thought experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thought_experiment "Thought experiment") about chasing a light beam, later relevant to his work on special relativity.
*   1897 – Oliver Lodge publishes another experimental result questioning aether drag.
*   1897 – [Joseph Larmor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Larmor "Joseph Larmor") publishes his coordinate transformations extending the length contraction formula. These transformations imply a form of [time dilation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_dilation "Time dilation") and were an approximation of the full Lorentz transformations.
*   1898 – [Henri Poincaré](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henri_Poincar%C3%A9 "Henri Poincaré") states that simultaneity is relative.
*   1899 – [Hendrik Antoon Lorentz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hendrik_Antoon_Lorentz "Hendrik Antoon Lorentz") publishes an early version of his coordinate transformations, including the _local time_.

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Hermann_Minkowski_Portrait.jpg/250px-Hermann_Minkowski_Portrait.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Hermann_Minkowski_Portrait.jpg)

Hermann Minkowski, who introduced the spacetime formalism to special relativity in 1908.

*   1902 – [Lord Rayleigh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lord_Rayleigh "Lord Rayleigh") writes that Lorentz’s hypothesis of length contraction predicts a form of [birefringence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birefringence "Birefringence") and [tries to observe it](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Experiments_of_Rayleigh_and_Brace "Experiments of Rayleigh and Brace").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-14) The null result questions Lorentz’s model, but it would be later explained by a combination of length contraction and time dilation.
*   1902 – [Max Abraham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Abraham "Max Abraham") develops his classical model of the electron. It anticipated some elements of special relativity like the non-linear dependence of momentum on velocity – or, in other, more debatable terms, the [relativistic mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relativistic_mass "Relativistic mass"). However, Abraham’s formula was different than in SR or in Lorentz’s theory.
*   1902 – [Walter Kaufmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Kaufmann_(physicist) "Walter Kaufmann (physicist)") publishes his measurements of how the electron’s momentum – or, using later terms, its [relativistic mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relativistic_mass "Relativistic mass") – depends on its speed. The results seem to confirm Abraham’s model.
*   1903 – [Olinto De Pretto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olinto_De_Pretto "Olinto De Pretto") presents his aether theory with some form of [mass–energy equivalence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass%E2%80%93energy_equivalence "Mass–energy equivalence").[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-15) It was described by a formula looking like Einstein’s _E_ = _mc_ 2, but with different meanings of the terms.
*   1903 – [Frederick Thomas Trouton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_Thomas_Trouton "Frederick Thomas Trouton") and [H.R. Noble](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=H.R._Noble&action=edit&redlink=1 "H.R. Noble (page does not exist)") publish the results of [their experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trouton%E2%80%93Noble_experiment "Trouton–Noble experiment") with capacitors, showing no aether drift.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-TroutonNoble1903-16)[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-17)
*   1904 – [DeWitt Bristol Brace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DeWitt_Bristol_Brace "DeWitt Bristol Brace") conducts an improved version of Rayleigh’s 1902 experiment, again with null result.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-18)
*   1904 – Hendrik Lorentz explains the experimental results of Rayleigh, Brace, Trouton and Noble, using his refined coordinate transformations; he also proves that Maxwell’s equations are invariant under them. Lorentz also presents his own classical model of the electron, including the length contraction absent in the work of Abraham – but consistent with Kaufmann’s data so far.
*   1904 – [Alfred Bucherer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_Bucherer "Alfred Bucherer") and [Paul Langevin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Langevin "Paul Langevin") independently publish a model of the electron and its mass increasing with speed, in a way different both from Abraham’s and Lorentz’s theories. This hypothesis was also consistent with Kaufmann’s results at that stage.
*   1904 – [Henri Poincaré](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henri_Poincar%C3%A9 "Henri Poincaré") presents the principle of relativity for [electromagnetism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetism "Electromagnetism").
*   1905 – Poincaré introduces the name _Lorentz transformations_ and is the first to present them in their full form that would be later present in Einstein’s special relativity proper. Also, Poincaré is the first to describe the relativistic [velocity-addition formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Velocity-addition_formula "Velocity-addition formula") – implicitly in his publication and explicitly in his letter to Lorentz.
*   **1905** – [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein") publishes his special theory of relativity, including the [mass–energy equivalence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass%E2%80%93energy_equivalence "Mass–energy equivalence") that would be later written as _E_ = _mc_ 2.
*   1906 – [Alfred Bucherer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_Bucherer "Alfred Bucherer") introduces the name _theory of relativity_, based on [Max Planck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Planck "Max Planck")’s term _relative theory_.
*   1906 – Walter Kaufmann publishes his new measurements of the mass–velocity dependence, and claims to disprove the formula of Lorentz and Einstein. At the same time, he accepts that both the old model of Abraham (1902) and the later model of Bucherer & Langevin (1904) are consistent with the data.
*   1907 – [Max Von Laue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Von_Laue "Max Von Laue") describes how the relativistic [velocity-addition formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Velocity-addition_formula "Velocity-addition formula") recreates the Fresnel drag coefficients.
*   1908 – [Hermann Minkowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermann_Minkowski "Hermann Minkowski") publishes his [spacetime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacetime "Spacetime") formalism of special relativity.
*   1908 – [Frederick Thomas Trouton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_Thomas_Trouton "Frederick Thomas Trouton") and [Alexander Rankine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Rankine "Alexander Rankine") conduct [an experiment with electric circuit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trouton%E2%80%93Rankine_experiment "Trouton–Rankine experiment"), proving that the length contraction is not the only relativistic effect and some form of time dilation is present – similarly to the previous experiments by Rayleigh (1902) and Brace (1904).[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-19)
*   1908 – [Walther Ritz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walther_Ritz "Walther Ritz") publishes [his ballistic theory of light](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ritz_ballistic_theory "Ritz ballistic theory") as an alternative to special relativity and Maxwell’s electrodynamics.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-20)
*   1909 – [Paul Ehrenfest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Ehrenfest "Paul Ehrenfest") publishes the [Ehrenfest paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ehrenfest_paradox "Ehrenfest paradox") about rigidity in special relativity.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-21)
*   1909 – [Gilbert N. Lewis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gilbert_N._Lewis "Gilbert N. Lewis") and [Richard Tolman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Tolman "Richard Tolman") coin the disputed term _relativistic mass_.

[![Image 6](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Sagnac_interferometer.svg/250px-Sagnac_interferometer.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Sagnac_interferometer.svg)

Schematic representation of a Sagnac interferometer.

*   1910 – [Vladimir Ignatowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Ignatowski "Vladimir Ignatowski") makes the first derivations of Lorentz transformations that rely mostly – and almost entirely – on the [relativity principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relativity_principle "Relativity principle"), without appealing to [Maxwell’s equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maxwell%E2%80%99s_equations "Maxwell’s equations");[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-22) such derivations are sometimes called _single-postulate_.
*   1910 – [Edmund Taylor Whittaker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edmund_Taylor_Whittaker "Edmund Taylor Whittaker")[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-23) and [Vladimir Varićak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Vari%C4%87ak "Vladimir Varićak")[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-24) introduce the idea of [rapidity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rapidity "Rapidity"), but without using this name.
*   1911 – [Alfred Robb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_Robb "Alfred Robb") coins the term _rapidity_.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-25)
*   1911 – [Paul Langevin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Langevin "Paul Langevin") presents the [twin paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twin_paradox "Twin paradox") implied by [time dilation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_dilation "Time dilation").[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-26)
*   1911 – [Max von Laue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_von_Laue "Max von Laue") writes that special relativity and Lorentz aether theory predict the [Sagnac effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagnac_effect "Sagnac effect"), absent in Ritz's ballistic theory or in Stokes's theory of aether drag.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-27)
*   1913 – [Georges Sagnac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Sagnac "Georges Sagnac") observes the effect named after him, disproving Ritz's ballistic theory or aether drag. However, he favours Lorentz's model and even claims – incorrectly – to contradict SR.
*   1913 – [Willem de Sitter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Willem_de_Sitter "Willem de Sitter") describes how the light of [double stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Double_star "Double star") contradicts Ritz’s ballistic theory of light.
*   1914 – [Ludwik Silberstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludwik_Silberstein "Ludwik Silberstein") gives the first description of [Thomas–Wigner rotation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wigner_rotation "Wigner rotation"),[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-28) then underappreciated.
*   1914 – [Günther Neumann](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=G%C3%BCnther_Neumann&action=edit&redlink=1 "Günther Neumann (page does not exist)") measures the mass–velocity dependence for electrons. His result favours the formula of Lorentz & Einstein over the one by Abraham.
*   1915 – [Charles-Eugène Guye](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles-Eug%C3%A8ne_Guye "Charles-Eugène Guye") and [Charles Lavanchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Charles_Lavanchy&action=edit&redlink=1 "Charles Lavanchy (page does not exist)") make their own measurements of the inertia of cathode rays, much more exact than the earlier research by Kaufmann. Their conclusion is opposite to Kaufmann’s – again, the formula of Lorentz and Einstein is correct and Abraham’s model is disproved.

*   1924 – [Hans Thirring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hans_Thirring "Hans Thirring") notices that ballistic theories of light contradict [spectroscopic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectroscopy "Spectroscopy") observations of the Sun.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-29)
*   1924 – [Anton Lampa](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Anton_Lampa&action=edit&redlink=1 "Anton Lampa (page does not exist)")[[de](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anton_Lampa "de:Anton Lampa")] predicts a relativistic effect later known as [Penrose–Terrell rotation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terrell_rotation "Terrell rotation").[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-Lampa1924-30)
*   1925 – the [Michelson–Gale–Pearson experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michelson%E2%80%93Gale%E2%80%93Pearson_experiment "Michelson–Gale–Pearson experiment") tests the [Sagnac effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagnac_effect "Sagnac effect") caused by the Earth’s rotation. The result disproves any aether drag; in combination with other experiments – disproving the stationary aether like the Michelson–Morley experiment – it proves the Lorentz transformations correct.
*   1925 – [Llewelyn Thomas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Llewelyn_Thomas "Llewelyn Thomas") discovers [Thomas precession](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_precession "Thomas precession"), which can be explained by the effect described earlier by Silberstein and later by Wigner.
*   1928 – [Paul Dirac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Dirac "Paul Dirac") describes the general [energy–momentum relation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy%E2%80%93momentum_relation "Energy–momentum relation"), extending the equivalence of mass and energy.
*   1932 – [Kennedy–Thorndike experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kennedy%E2%80%93Thorndike_experiment "Kennedy–Thorndike experiment") confirms the Lorentz transformations in a new way, complementary to the Michelson–Morley experiment.[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-KT-31) These two results, if combined, prove some form of time dilation.
*   1932 – [John Cockcroft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Cockcroft "John Cockcroft") and [Ernest Walton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernest_Walton "Ernest Walton") prove the mass–energy equivalence via a [nuclear reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_reaction "Nuclear reaction").
*   1933 – [Dayton Miller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dayton_Miller "Dayton Miller") conducts an improved form of the Michelson–Morley experiment, claiming to contradict special relativity.[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-32) It would be later explained consistently with SR in the 1950s.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-Shankland-33)
*   1935 – the [Hammar experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hammar_experiment "Hammar experiment") is another refutation of aether drag and evidence for special relativity.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-ham-34)[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-rob-35)
*   1938 – [Ives–Stilwell experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ives%E2%80%93Stilwell_experiment "Ives–Stilwell experiment") measures time dilation via the relativistic [Doppler effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doppler_effect "Doppler effect").[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-Ives1938-36) For the first time, the Lorentz transformations can be derived directly from empirical data, as would be noticed by Robertson in 1949.
*   1939 – [Eugene Wigner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_Wigner "Eugene Wigner") rediscovers that SR predicts the [Thomas–Wigner rotation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wigner_rotation "Wigner rotation").[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-37)

*   1940 – [Bruno Rossi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruno_Rossi "Bruno Rossi") and [D.B. Hall](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=D.B._Hall&action=edit&redlink=1 "D.B. Hall (page does not exist)") observe [time dilation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_dilation "Time dilation") in [cosmic rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_rays "Cosmic rays"), i.e. in the decay of [muons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muon "Muon").
*   1949 – [Howard P. Robertson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Howard_P._Robertson "Howard P. Robertson") notices that the Lorentz transformations can be deduced (extracted) from three key experiments: Michelson–Morley, Kennedy–Thorndike and Ives–Stillwell.[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-38)
*   1954 – [Gerhart Lüders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gerhart_L%C3%BCders "Gerhart Lüders") and [Wolfgang Pauli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfgang_Pauli "Wolfgang Pauli") prove that the [Lorentz invariance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lorentz_invariance "Lorentz invariance") in [quantum field theories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_field_theory "Quantum field theory") implies the [CPT symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CPT_symmetry "CPT symmetry"), allowing for new tests of special relativity.
*   1955 – [Robert S. Shankland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_S._Shankland "Robert S. Shankland") and others explain Miller’s experimental result from 1933 in a way consistent with special relativity.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-Shankland-33)
*   1959 – [Roger Penrose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roger_Penrose "Roger Penrose") and [James Terrell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Terrell_(physicist) "James Terrell (physicist)") independently publish their rediscovery that SR predicts the [Penrose–Terrell effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penrose%E2%80%93Terrell_effect "Penrose–Terrell effect").
*   1959 – E. Dewan and M. Beran publish the thought experiment known as [Bell's spaceship paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bell%27s_spaceship_paradox "Bell's spaceship paradox").[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-dewanberan-39)
*   1960 – [Vernon W. Hughes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vernon_W._Hughes "Vernon W. Hughes")_et al._ perform [a spectroscopic experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hughes%E2%80%93Drever_experiment "Hughes–Drever experiment"),[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-40) later interpreted as evidence for the Lorentz invariance of particle interactions.
*   1961 – [Ronald Drever](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ronald_Drever "Ronald Drever") independently conducts a similar experiment with the same conclusions.[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-41)
*   1961 – [Wolfgang Rindler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfgang_Rindler "Wolfgang Rindler") presents and solves the [ladder paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ladder_paradox "Ladder paradox").[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-42)
*   1967 – [Gerald Feinberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gerald_Feinberg "Gerald Feinberg") introduces the term _[tachyon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tachyon "Tachyon")_ for hypothetical particles with speeds higher than that of light in vacuum (_c_).[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-43)
*   1971 – The [Hafele–Keating experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hafele%E2%80%93Keating_experiment "Hafele–Keating experiment") confirms [time dilation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_dilation "Time dilation") predicted by special &[general relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity "General relativity").[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-paper1-44)[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_special_relativity_and_the_speed_of_light#cite_note-paper2-45)
*   1974 – [Stefan Marinov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stefan_Marinov "Stefan Marinov") claims to contradict special relativity by measuring a variation in _c_. His results are noted by the scientific community but rejected as incorrect.
*   1983 – the speed of light in vacuum (_c_) is used to define the [metre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metre "Metre") in the [SI system of units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_System_of_Units "International System of Units"); the definition does not mention any frame of reference, assuming this speed is universal, and implicitly that special relativity is correct.

*   2011 – [Faster-than-light neutrino anomaly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faster-than-light_neutrino_anomaly "Faster-than-light neutrino anomaly") is reported by [CERN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CERN "CERN").
*   2012 – the anomaly in neutrino speed is explained by a failure of the equipment; this reason is officially reported.
