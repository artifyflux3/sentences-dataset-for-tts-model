[![Image 1](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/P._Oxy._I_29.jpg/250px-P._Oxy._I_29.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:P._Oxy._I_29.jpg)

One of the oldest surviving fragments of Euclid's _Elements_, found at [Oxyrhynchus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxyrhynchus "Oxyrhynchus") and dated to c. 100 AD ([P. Oxy. 29](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papyrus_Oxyrhynchus_29 "Papyrus Oxyrhynchus 29")). The diagram accompanies Book II, Proposition 5.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_geometers#cite_note-1)

A **geometer** is a [mathematician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematician "Mathematician") whose area of study is the historical aspects that define [geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometry "Geometry"), instead of the analytical geometric studies conducted by [geometricians](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/geometrician).

Some notable geometers and their main fields of work, chronologically listed, are:

*   [Baudhayana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baudhayana "Baudhayana") (fl. c. 800 BC) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry")
*   [Manava](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manava "Manava") (c. 750 BC–690 BC) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry")
*   [Thales of Miletus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thales "Thales") (c. 624 BC – c. 546 BC) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry")
*   [Pythagoras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pythagoras "Pythagoras") (c. 570 BC – c. 495 BC) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry"), [Pythagorean theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pythagorean_theorem "Pythagorean theorem")
*   [Zeno of Elea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zeno_of_Elea "Zeno of Elea") (c. 490 BC – c. 430 BC) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry")
*   [Hippocrates of Chios](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hippocrates_of_Chios "Hippocrates of Chios") (born c. 470 – 410 BC) – first systematically organized _[Stoicheia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclid%27s\_Elements "Euclid's Elements") – Elements_ (geometry textbook)
*   [Mozi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozi "Mozi") (c. 468 BC – c. 391 BC)
*   [Plato](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plato "Plato") (427–347 BC)
*   [Theaetetus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theaetetus_(mathematician) "Theaetetus (mathematician)") (c. 417 BC – 369 BC)
*   [Autolycus of Pitane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autolycus_of_Pitane "Autolycus of Pitane") (360–c. 290 BC) – [astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy "Astronomy"), [spherical geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spherical_geometry "Spherical geometry")
*   [Euclid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclid "Euclid") (fl. 300 BC) – _[Elements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclid%27s\_Elements "Euclid's Elements")_, [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry") (sometimes called the "father of geometry")
*   [Apollonius of Perga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollonius_of_Perga "Apollonius of Perga") (c. 262 BC – c. 190 BC) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry"), [conic sections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conic_section "Conic section")
*   [Archimedes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes "Archimedes") (c. 287 BC – c. 212 BC) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry")
*   [Eratosthenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eratosthenes "Eratosthenes") (c. 276 BC – c. 195/194 BC) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry")
*   [Katyayana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katyayana "Katyayana") (c. 3rd century BC) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry")

*   [Hero of Alexandria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hero_of_Alexandria "Hero of Alexandria") (c. AD 10–70) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry")
*   [Pappus of Alexandria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pappus_of_Alexandria "Pappus of Alexandria") (c. AD 290–c. 350) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry"), [projective geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Projective_geometry "Projective geometry")
*   [Hypatia of Alexandria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypatia_of_Alexandria "Hypatia of Alexandria") (c. AD 370–c. 415) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry")
*   [Brahmagupta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brahmagupta "Brahmagupta") (597–668) – [Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_geometry "Euclidean geometry"), [cyclic quadrilaterals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclic_quadrilateral "Cyclic quadrilateral")
*   [Vergilius of Salzburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vergilius_of_Salzburg "Vergilius of Salzburg") (c.700–784) – Irish bishop of [Aghaboe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aghaboe "Aghaboe"), [Ossory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Ossory "Kingdom of Ossory") and later [Salzburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salzburg "Salzburg"), [Austria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austria "Austria"); [antipodes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antipodes "Antipodes"), and [astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy "Astronomy")
*   [Al-Abbās ibn Said al-Jawharī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Abb%C4%81s_ibn_Said_al-Jawhar%C4%AB "Al-Abbās ibn Said al-Jawharī") (c. 800–c. 860)
*   [Thabit ibn Qurra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thabit_ibn_Qurra "Thabit ibn Qurra") (826–901) – [analytic geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analytic_geometry "Analytic geometry"), [non-Euclidean geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Euclidean_geometry "Non-Euclidean geometry"), [conic sections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conic_section "Conic section")
*   [Abu'l-Wáfa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abul_W%C3%A1fa "Abul Wáfa") (940–998) – [spherical geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spherical_geometry "Spherical geometry"), [spherical triangles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spherical_triangles "Spherical triangles")
*   [Ibn al-Haytham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibn_al-Haytham "Ibn al-Haytham") (965–c. 1040)
*   [Omar Khayyam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omar_Khayyam "Omar Khayyam") (1048–1131) – [algebraic geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algebraic_geometry "Algebraic geometry"), [conic sections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conic_section "Conic section")
*   [Ibn Maḍāʾ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibn_Ma%E1%B8%8D%C4%81%CA%BE "Ibn Maḍāʾ") (1116–1196)
