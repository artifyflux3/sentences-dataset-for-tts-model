**Flora** (pl.: **floras** or **florae**) is all the [plant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plant "Plant") life present in a particular region or time, generally the naturally occurring ([indigenous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indigenous_(ecology) "Indigenous (ecology)")) [native plants.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Native_plant "Native plant") The corresponding term for [animals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animals "Animals") is _[fauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna "Fauna")_, and for [fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungi "Fungi"), it is _[funga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funga "Funga")_.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-1) Sometimes [bacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacteria "Bacteria") and [fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungi "Fungi") are also referred to as flora as in the terms _[gut flora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gut\_flora "Gut flora")_ or _[skin flora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skin\_flora "Skin flora")_ for purposes of specificity.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-4)

Etymology
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Flora&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Etymology")]

The word "flora" comes from the [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "Latin") name of [Flora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora_(mythology) "Flora (mythology)"), the [goddess](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goddess "Goddess") of [plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plant "Plant"), [flowers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flower "Flower"), and fertility in [Roman mythology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_mythology "Roman mythology").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-6) The technical term "flora" is then derived from a [metonymy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metonymy "Metonymy") of this goddess at the end of the sixteenth century. It was first used in poetry to denote the natural vegetation of an area, but soon also assumed the meaning of a work cataloguing such vegetation. Moreover, "Flora" was used to refer to the flowers of an artificial garden in the seventeenth century.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-berrens-7)

The distinction between [vegetation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vegetation "Vegetation") (the general appearance of a community) and flora (the taxonomic composition of a community) was first made by [Jules Thurmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jules_Thurmann "Jules Thurmann") (1849). Prior to this, the two terms were used interchangeably.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-Martins-9)

Classifications
---------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Flora&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Classifications")]

Plants are grouped into floras based on region ([floristic regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phytochorion "Phytochorion")), period, special environment, or climate. Regions can be distinct [habitats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habitat "Habitat") like mountain vs. flatland. Floras can mean plant life of a historic era as in _fossil flora_. Lastly, floras may be subdivided by special environments:

*   _Native flora_. The native and indigenous flora of an area.
*   _Agricultural and horticultural flora (garden flora)_. The plants that are deliberately grown by humans.
*   _Weed flora_. Traditionally this classification was applied to plants regarded as undesirable and studied in efforts to control or eradicate them. Today the designation is less often used as a classification of plant life since it includes three different types of plants: [weedy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weed "Weed") species, [invasive species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invasive_species "Invasive species") (that may or may not be weedy), and native and introduced non-weedy species that are agriculturally undesirable. Many native plants previously considered weeds have been shown to be beneficial or even necessary to various [ecosystems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecosystem "Ecosystem").

[![Image 11](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Floristic_regions_in_Europe_%28english%29.png/250px-Floristic_regions_in_Europe_%28english%29.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Floristic_regions_in_Europe_(english).png)

Floristic regions in Europe according to Wolfgang Frey and Rainer Lösch

The flora of a particular area or time period can be documented in a publication also known as a "[flora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora_(publication) "Flora (publication)")" (often capitalized as "Flora" to distinguish the two meanings when they might be confused). Floras may require specialist botanical knowledge to use with any effectiveness. Traditionally they are [books](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book "Book"), but some are now published on [CD-ROM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CD-ROM "CD-ROM") or [websites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Website "Website").

[Simon Paulli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simon_Paulli "Simon Paulli")'s _Flora Danica_ of 1648 is probably the first book titled "Flora" to refer to the plant world of a certain region. It mainly describes medicinal plants growing in Denmark. The _[Flora Sinensis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora\_Sinensis "Flora Sinensis")_ by the Polish [Jesuit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesuit "Jesuit")[Michał Boym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micha%C5%82_Boym "Michał Boym") is another early example of a book titled "Flora".[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-boym-10) However, despite its title it covered not only plants but also some animals of the region, that is China and India.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora#cite_note-berrens-7)

A published flora often contains diagnostic keys. Often these are [_dichotomous_ keys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Identification_key "Identification key"), which require the user to examine a plant repeatedly, and decide which one of two alternatives given best applies to the plant.
