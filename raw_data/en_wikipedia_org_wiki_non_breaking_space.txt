In [word processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word_processing "Word processing") and [digital typesetting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_typesetting "Digital typesetting"), a **non-breaking space** (), also called **NBSP**, **required space**,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-2)**hard space**, or **fixed space** (in most [typefaces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typeface "Typeface"), it is not of [fixed width](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monospaced_font "Monospaced font")), is a [space character](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whitespace_character "Whitespace character") that prevents an [automatic line break](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Line_wrap_and_word_wrap "Line wrap and word wrap") at its position. In some formats, including [HTML](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HTML "HTML"), it also prevents consecutive [whitespace characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whitespace_character "Whitespace character") from collapsing into a single space. Non-breaking space characters [with other widths](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#Variations) also exist.

Despite having layout and uses similar to those of [whitespace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whitespace_(computer_science) "Whitespace (computer science)"), it differs in contextual behavior.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-3)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-4)

### Non-breaking behavior

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Non-breaking_space&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Non-breaking behavior")]

Text-processing software typically assumes that an automatic line break may be inserted anywhere a space character occurs; a non-breaking space prevents this from happening (provided the software recognizes the character).

For example, if the text "100 km" will not quite fit at the end of a line, the software may break the line between "100" and "km". Using a non-breaking space between "100" and "km" will prevent this behaviour. This guarantees that the text "100 km" will not be broken—if it does not fit at the end of a line, it is moved in its entirety to the next line. For this reason, many style guides recommend using a non-breaking space between numbers and their associated units.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-5)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-6)

In [French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language "French language") typography, non-breaking spaces are used before "high punctuation" (`:`, `;`, `?`, and `!`), on the interior side of [guillemets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guillemet "Guillemet") (`«` and `»`), and before [footnotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Footnote "Footnote"). In the case of `;`, `?`, `!`, and footnotes (unless enclosed by parentheses), it is specifically the [narrow non-breaking space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#Narrow_non-breaking_space) that is used.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-IN_Group-7)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-Quebec-8)

In [German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_language "German language") typography, it is used between multi-part abbreviations (e.g., "_z. B._", "_d. h._", "_v. l. n. r._").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-9)

### Non-collapsing behavior

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Non-breaking_space&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Non-collapsing behavior")]

A second common application of non-breaking spaces is in [plain text](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plain_text "Plain text") file formats such as [SGML](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_Generalized_Markup_Language "Standard Generalized Markup Language"), [HTML](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HTML "HTML"), [TeX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TeX "TeX") and [LaTeX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LaTeX "LaTeX"), whose rendering engines are programmed to treat sequences of [whitespace characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whitespace_(computer_science) "Whitespace (computer science)") (space, newline, tab, [form feed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Form_feed "Form feed"), etc.) as if they were a single character (but this behavior can be overridden). Such "collapsing" of whitespace allows the author to neatly arrange the source text using line breaks, indentation and other forms of spacing without affecting the final [typeset](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typesetting "Typesetting") result.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-10)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-11)

In contrast, non-breaking spaces are not merged with neighboring whitespace characters when displayed. They can, therefore, be used by an author to simply insert additional visible space in the resulting output without using spans styled with peculiar values of the [CSS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cascading_Style_Sheets "Cascading Style Sheets") "white-space" property. Conversely, indiscriminate use (see the recommended use[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_] in [style guides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Style_guide "Style guide")), in addition to a normal space, gives extraneous space in the output.

### Narrow non-breaking space

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Non-breaking_space&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Narrow non-breaking space")]

U+202F NARROW NO-BREAK SPACE (`NNBSP`) is another non-breaking space, but with a smaller width than the standard non-breaking space (U+00A0). When used with [Mongolian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolian_language "Mongolian language"), its width is usually one third of the normal space;[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-12) in other contexts, its width is about 70% of the normal space, but may resemble that of the [thin space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thin_space "Thin space") (U+2009), at least with some fonts.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

It was introduced in 1999 in Unicode 3.0 for Mongolian,[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-13) to separate a suffix from the word stem without indicating a word boundary. It also triggers special shaping of those suffixes.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-Unicode_Standard_15_-_NNBSP-14) Starting in Unicode 16.0, it is no longer recommended for this purpose, with the Mongolian vowel separator (U+180E) being recommended for this purpose instead.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-Unicode_Standard_16_-_NNBSP-15)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-Unicode_Standard_16_-_Mongolian-16)

In [French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language "French language") typography, it is called _espace fine insécable_[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-Unicode_Standard_16_-_NNBSP-15) and is used before `;`, `?`, and `!`.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-IN_Group-7)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-Quebec-8) Additionally, footnotes are preceded by a narrow non-breaking space[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-Quebec-8) (unless they are enclosed by parentheses, in which case a regular non-breaking space is used).[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-IN_Group-7)

The narrow non-breaking space is used in numbers as a [group separator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decimal_separator#Digit_grouping "Decimal separator") in French (starting in Unicode CLDR 34[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-17)) and [Venetian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venetian_language "Venetian language") (starting in Unicode CLDR 44[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-18)). In Spanish, the [Royal Spanish Academy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Spanish_Academy "Royal Spanish Academy") and [Association of Academies of the Spanish Language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_of_Academies_of_the_Spanish_Language "Association of Academies of the Spanish Language")'s _[Diccionario panhispánico de dudas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diccionario\_panhisp%C3%A1nico\_de\_dudas "Diccionario panhispánico de dudas")_ prescribes the use of a small space as the number group separator,[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-19) although this is not the case in Unicode's [Common Locale Data Repository](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_Locale_Data_Repository "Common Locale Data Repository") (CLDR).[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-breaking_space#cite_note-20)

The narrow non-breaking space is used by some AI [chatbots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chatbot "Chatbot") to watermark their texts and indicate that the content was [AI-generated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_artificial_intelligence "Generative artificial intelligence").[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Other non-breaking variants [defined in Unicode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whitespace_character#Unicode "Whitespace character").
