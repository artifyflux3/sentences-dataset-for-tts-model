The Society for the History of Technology (SHOT) has awarded the Abbot Payson Usher Prize , named in his honor, annually since 1961.

[ 3 ] In the late 1920s Usher, the American historian Lewis Mumford and the Swiss art historian Sigfried Giedion began to systematically investigate the social consequences of technology.

[ 4 ] In A History of Mechanical Inventions Usher argued that technological innovation was a slow, collective process with many contributors, not relying on the genius of great inventors.

[ 5 ] In 1963 Usher was awarded the Leonardo da Vinci Medal by the Society for the History of Technology (SHOT). His daughter Miriam Usher Chrisman was a noted historian of the German Reformation.

[ 6 ] He earned his BA and PhD at Harvard University .
