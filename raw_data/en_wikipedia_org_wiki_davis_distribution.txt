Davis distributions are a family of continuous probability distributions . It is named after Harold T. Davis (1892–1974), who in 1941 proposed this distribution to model income sizes. ( The Theory of Econometrics and Analysis of Economic Time Series ). It is a generalization of the Planck's law of radiation from statistical physics .

Here μ, b , and n are parameters of the distribution, and n need not be an integer.
