_**Res extensa**_ is one of the two [substances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Substance_theory "Substance theory") described by [René Descartes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Descartes "René Descartes") in his [Cartesian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartesian_dualism "Cartesian dualism") ontology[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Res_extensa#cite_note-1) (often referred to as "radical [dualism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mind-body_dualism "Mind-body dualism")"), alongside _[res cogitans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mental\_substance "Mental substance")_. Translated from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "Latin"), "_res extensa_" means "extended thing" while the latter is described as "a thinking and unextended thing".[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Res_extensa#cite_note-:0-2) Descartes often translated _res extensa_ as "corporeal substance" but it is something that only God can create.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Res_extensa#cite_note-3)

_Res extensa_ vs. _res cogitans_
--------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Res_extensa&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Res extensa vs. res cogitans")]

_Res extensa_ and _res cogitans_ are mutually exclusive and this makes it possible to conceptualize the complete intellectual independence from the body.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Res_extensa#cite_note-:0-2)_Res cogitans_ is also referred to as the soul and is related by thinkers such as [Aristotle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aristotle "Aristotle") in his _De Anima_ to the indefinite realm of potentiality.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Res_extensa#cite_note-:2-4) On the other hand, _res extensa_, are entities described by the principles of logic and are considered in terms of definiteness. Due to the polarity of these two concepts, the natural science focused on _res extensa_.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Res_extensa#cite_note-:2-4)

In the Cartesian view, the distinction between these two concepts is a methodological necessity driven by a distrust of the senses and the _res extensa_ as it represents the entire material world.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Res_extensa#cite_note-5) The categorical separation of these two, however, caused a problem, which can be demonstrated in this question: How can a wish (a mental event), cause an arm movement (a physical event)?[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Res_extensa#cite_note-:1-6) Descartes has not provided any answer to this but [Gottfried Leibniz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gottfried_Wilhelm_Leibniz "Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz") proposed that it can be addressed by endowing each geometrical point in the _res extensa_ with mind.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Res_extensa#cite_note-:1-6) Each of these points is within _res extensa_ but they are also dimensionless, making them unextended.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Res_extensa#cite_note-:1-6)

In Descartes' substance–attribute–mode ontology, extension is the primary attribute of corporeal substance. He describes a piece of wax in the Second [Meditation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meditations_on_First_Philosophy "Meditations on First Philosophy") (see [Wax argument](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wax_argument "Wax argument")). A solid piece of wax has certain sensory qualities. However, when the wax is melted, it loses every single apparent quality it had in its solid form. Still, Descartes recognizes in the melted substance the idea of wax.
