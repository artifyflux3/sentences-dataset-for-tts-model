Indian mathematician and astronomer (1380–1460)

For the concept of God, see [Parameshwara (god)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parameshwara_(god) "Parameshwara (god)").

**Vatasseri Parameshvara Nambudiri** (c. 1380–1460)[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parameshvara#cite_note-Pingree-1) was a major [Indian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India")[mathematician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematician "Mathematician") and [astronomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomer "Astronomer") of the [Kerala school of astronomy and mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerala_school_of_astronomy_and_mathematics "Kerala school of astronomy and mathematics") founded by [Madhava of Sangamagrama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madhava_of_Sangamagrama "Madhava of Sangamagrama"). He was also an [astrologer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrologer "Astrologer"). Parameshvara was a proponent of [observational astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Observational_astronomy "Observational astronomy") in [medieval India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medieval_India "Medieval India") and he himself had made a series of [eclipse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eclipse "Eclipse") observations to verify the accuracy of the computational methods then in use. Based on his eclipse observations, Parameshvara proposed several corrections to the astronomical parameters which had been in use since the times of [Aryabhata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aryabhata "Aryabhata"). The computational scheme based on the revised set of parameters has come to be known as the _Drgganita_ or [Drig system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drig_system "Drig system"). Parameshvara was also a prolific writer on matters relating to astronomy. At least 25 manuscripts have been identified as being authored by Parameshvara.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parameshvara#cite_note-Pingree-1)

Biographical details
--------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Parameshvara_Nambudiri&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Biographical details")]

Parameshvara was a [Hindu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hindu "Hindu") of Bhrgu[gotra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gotra "Gotra") following the [Ashvalayanasutra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashvalayana "Ashvalayana") of the [Rigveda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rigveda "Rigveda"). Parameshvara's family name (_Illam_) was Vatasseri and his family resided in the village of Alathiyur (Sanskritised as _Asvatthagrama_) in [Tirur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tirur "Tirur"), [Kerala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerala "Kerala"). Alathiyur is situated on the northern bank of the river [Nila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nila_River "Nila River") (river [Bharathappuzha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bharathappuzha "Bharathappuzha")) at its mouth in Kerala. He was a grandson of a disciple of [Govinda Bhattathiri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Govinda_Bhattathiri "Govinda Bhattathiri") (1237–1295 CE), a legendary figure in the astrological traditions of [Kerala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerala "Kerala").

Parameshvara studied under teachers Rudra and Narayana, and also under [Madhava of Sangamagrama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madhava_of_Sangamagrama "Madhava of Sangamagrama") (c. 1350 – c. 1425) the founder of the [Kerala school of astronomy and mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerala_school_of_astronomy_and_mathematics "Kerala school of astronomy and mathematics"). [Damodara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damodara "Damodara"), another prominent member of the [Kerala school](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerala_school_of_astronomy_and_mathematics "Kerala school of astronomy and mathematics"), was his son and also his pupil. Parameshvara was also a teacher of [Nilakantha Somayaji](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nilakantha_Somayaji "Nilakantha Somayaji") (1444–1544) the author of the celebrated [Tantrasamgraha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tantrasamgraha "Tantrasamgraha").

Work
----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Parameshvara_Nambudiri&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Work")]

Parameshvara wrote commentaries on many mathematical and astronomical works such as those by [Bhāskara I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bh%C4%81skara_I "Bhāskara I") and [Aryabhata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aryabhata "Aryabhata"). He made a series of eclipse observations over a 55-year period. Constantly attempted to compare these with the theoretically computed positions of the planets. He revised planetary parameters based on his observations.

One of Parameshvara's more significant contributions was his [mean value type formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mean_value_theorem "Mean value theorem") for the inverse interpolation of the sine.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parameshvara#cite_note-2)

He was the first mathematician to give a formula for the [radius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radius "Radius") of the [circle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circle "Circle") circumscribing a cyclic quadrilateral.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parameshvara#cite_note-3) The expression is sometimes attributed to [Lhuilier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lhuilier "Lhuilier") [1782], 350 years later. With the sides of the [cyclic quadrilateral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclic_quadrilateral "Cyclic quadrilateral") being _a, b, c,_ and _d_, the radius _R_ of the circumscribed circle is:

R=(a b+c d)(a c+b d)(a d+b c)(−a+b+c+d)(a−b+c+d)(a+b−c+d)(a+b+c−d).{\displaystyle R={\sqrt {\frac {(ab+cd)(ac+bd)(ad+bc)}{(-a+b+c+d)(a-b+c+d)(a+b-c+d)(a+b+c-d)}}}.}![Image 5: {\displaystyle R={\sqrt {\frac {(ab+cd)(ac+bd)(ad+bc)}{(-a+b+c+d)(a-b+c+d)(a+b-c+d)(a+b+c-d)}}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8b93fef529798879eebd76c29399353c6cee596c)

Works by Parameshvara
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Parameshvara_Nambudiri&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Works by Parameshvara")]

The following works of Parameshvara are well-known.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parameshvara#cite_note-4) A complete list of all manuscripts attributed to Parameshvara is available in Pingree.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parameshvara#cite_note-Pingree-1)

*   _Bhatadipika_ – Commentary on [Āryabhaṭīya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%80ryabha%E1%B9%AD%C4%ABya "Āryabhaṭīya") of [Āryabhaṭa I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%80ryabha%E1%B9%ADa_I "Āryabhaṭa I")
*   _Karmadipika_ – Commentary on _Mahabhaskariya_ of Bhaskara I
*   _Paramesvari_– Commentary on _Laghubhaskariya_ of Bhaskara I
*   _Sidhantadipika_ – Commentary on _Mahabhaskariyabhashya_ of [Govindasvāmi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Govindasv%C4%81mi "Govindasvāmi")
*   _Vivarana_ – Commentary on [Surya Siddhanta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surya_Siddhanta "Surya Siddhanta") and [Lilāvati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lil%C4%81vati "Lilāvati")
*   _Drgganita_ – Description of the [Drig system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drig_system "Drig system") (composed in 1431 CE)
*   _Goladipika_ – Spherical geometry and astronomy (composed in 1443 CE)
*   _Grahanamandana_ – Computation of eclipses (Its epoch is 15 July 1411 CE.)
*   _Grahanavyakhyadipika_ – On the rationale of the theory of eclipses
*   _Vakyakarana_ – Methods for the derivation of several astronomical tables
