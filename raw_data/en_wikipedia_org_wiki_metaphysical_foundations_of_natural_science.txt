Summary [ edit ] The book is divided into four chapters. The chapters are concerned with the metaphysical foundations of phoronomy (now called kinematics ), dynamics , mechanics , and phenomenology .

Reception [ edit ] Kant's book was a basic influence on the rise of science departments of the universities in the German-speaking countries in the nineteenth century.

Hans Christian Ørsted wrote " Differential and integral calculus consist of nothing but ..

thought experiments and considerations of them. ... In his Metaphysical Foundations of Natural Science , Kant has given us the most beautiful examples of this kind of presentation, without, however, drawing attention to it himself." [ 1 ] Kurt Gödel was influenced by Metaphysische Anfangsgründe der Naturwissenschaft . Gödel studied it while a member of the Vienna Circle .
