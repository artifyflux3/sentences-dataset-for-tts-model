It was founded in 1921 as Verlag Chemie (meaning "Chemistry Press": VCH stands for Verlag Chemie [ 1 ] ) by two German learned societies . Later, it was merged into the German Chemical Society (GDCh). In 1991, VCH acquired Akademie Verlag . It has been owned by John Wiley & Sons since 1996.

[ 2 ] The humanities section of Akademie Verlag and the Akademie brand were sold in 1997 to R. Oldenbourg Verlag , while VCH retained the natural sciences catalog.
