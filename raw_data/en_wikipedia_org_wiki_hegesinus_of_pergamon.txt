He was the successor of Evander and the immediate predecessor of Carneades as the leader ( scholarch ) of the Platonic Academy , and served for a period around 160 BC.

[ 1 ] Nothing else is known about him.

Notes [ edit ] ^ Diogenes Laërtius , Lives and Opinions of Eminent Philosophers , iv. 60; Cicero , Academica , ii. 6.
