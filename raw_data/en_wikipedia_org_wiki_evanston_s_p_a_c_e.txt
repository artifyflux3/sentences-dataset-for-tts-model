**Evanston S.P.A.C.E.** (or **Evanston SPACE**) is a small [concert hall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concert_hall "Concert hall") and venue for music performance and live recording, and a [podcast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Podcast "Podcast") production facility, as well as serves similarly for the visual arts and literary events located at 1245 Chicago Avenue in [Evanston, Illinois](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evanston,_Illinois "Evanston, Illinois"), adjacent to [Chicago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago "Chicago"), in close proximity to the [CTA Purple Line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CTA_Purple_Line "CTA Purple Line")[mass transit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_transit "Mass transit")[elevated train](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elevated_train "Elevated train") station Dempster, just south of Dempster Avenue.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evanston_S.P.A.C.E.#cite_note-about-1) It opened in spring 2008, along with Union Pizzeria, an affiliated restaurant in the same building.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evanston_S.P.A.C.E.#cite_note-2)

It is a notable small concert space in the [Chicago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago "Chicago") area, that seats about 250 people at candle-lit small round tables and as standing spectators. The SPACE has over the years played host to the most renowned [folk music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Folk_music "Folk music") artists in the Western World; for example, only in 2014-2015 winter timeframe to [Suzanne Vega](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suzanne_Vega "Suzanne Vega"), [Leo Kottke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_Kottke "Leo Kottke"), [Justin Townes Earle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justin_Townes_Earle "Justin Townes Earle") and [Robyn Hitchcock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robyn_Hitchcock "Robyn Hitchcock"), among many tens of events.

S.P.A.C.E. is an [acronym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acronym "Acronym") for the Society for the Preservation of Art and Culture in Evanston. As of 2024, [Bruce Finkelman](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bruce_Finkelman&action=edit&redlink=1 "Bruce Finkelman (page does not exist)") and [Craig Golden](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Craig_Golden&action=edit&redlink=1 "Craig Golden (page does not exist)"), through their firm [16” on Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=16%E2%80%9D_on_Center&action=edit&redlink=1 "16” on Center (page does not exist)"), own, co-own, operate, and/or co-operate several music venues, including [The Empty Bottle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Empty_Bottle "The Empty Bottle"), [The Promontory](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=The_Promontory&action=edit&redlink=1 "The Promontory (page does not exist)"), Evanston S.P.A.C.E., Sonotheque (which closed in 2009), [Thalia Hall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thalia_Hall_(Chicago) "Thalia Hall (Chicago)"), and [The Salt Shed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Salt_Shed "The Salt Shed"), all in and near Chicago. Finkeleman and Golde are similarly affiliated with several other restaurants and bars, both at those music venues and free-standing, including [Bite Cafe](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bite_Cafe&action=edit&redlink=1 "Bite Cafe (page does not exist)"), [Dusek's](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Dusek%27s&action=edit&redlink=1 "Dusek's (page does not exist)"), and [Longman & Eagle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Longman_%26_Eagle "Longman & Eagle").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evanston_S.P.A.C.E.#cite_note-16OCabout-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evanston_S.P.A.C.E.#cite_note-EB_AssociatedVenues-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evanston_S.P.A.C.E.#cite_note-ChiReader_Sonotheque-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evanston_S.P.A.C.E.#cite_note-ThaliaHallHistory-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evanston_S.P.A.C.E.#cite_note-pork-7)

The listening room fits about 250 guests and shows often offer a mix of reserved tables, general admission seating and standing room tickets. Shows are open to all ages and the bar sells alcoholic and non-alcoholic drinks to patrons whose hands are stamped with water-soluble black stamp.

SPACE hosts local and national touring acts spanning rock, blues, jazz, folk, singer-songwriters, country and R&B. Past years' artists include [Brandi Carlile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brandi_Carlile "Brandi Carlile"), [Aaron Neville](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aaron_Neville "Aaron Neville"), [Graham Parker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graham_Parker "Graham Parker"), [Nick Lowe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Lowe "Nick Lowe"), [George Winston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Winston "George Winston"), [Alabama Shakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alabama_Shakes "Alabama Shakes"), [Marshall Crenshaw](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marshall_Crenshaw "Marshall Crenshaw"), [David Lindley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Lindley_(musician) "David Lindley (musician)"), [The Weepies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Weepies "The Weepies"), [Liz Phair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liz_Phair "Liz Phair"), [Herb Alpert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herb_Alpert "Herb Alpert") and [Lani Hall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lani_Hall "Lani Hall"), [Five For Fighting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Five_For_Fighting "Five For Fighting"), [Billy Bragg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billy_Bragg "Billy Bragg"), [Leon Russell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leon_Russell "Leon Russell"), [Terrence Blanchard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terrence_Blanchard "Terrence Blanchard"), [Dr. John](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dr._John "Dr. John"), [Zoë Keating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zo%C3%AB_Keating "Zoë Keating"), [The English Beat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_English_Beat "The English Beat"), [The Lone Bellow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lone_Bellow "The Lone Bellow"), [Martin Sexton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Sexton "Martin Sexton"), [Lucinda Williams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucinda_Williams "Lucinda Williams") and [The Lumineers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lumineers "The Lumineers").

The space is regularly[_[when?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual\_of\_Style/Dates\_and\_numbers#Chronological\_items "Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers")_] used for live broadcasts emitted on the local [National Public Radio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Public_Radio "National Public Radio") affiliate station [WBEZ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WBEZ "WBEZ") FM 91.5 [Chicago Public Radio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago_Public_Radio "Chicago Public Radio"). There is also a regular podcast series made available through [Apple, Inc.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple,_Inc. "Apple, Inc.")[iTunes Store](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ITunes_Store "ITunes Store").
