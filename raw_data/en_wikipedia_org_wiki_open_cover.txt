This article is about a family of subsets whose union is the whole set. For a type of continuous map, see [covering space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covering_space "Covering space").

In [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics"), and more particularly in [set theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set_theory "Set theory"), a **cover** (or **covering**)[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_cover#cite_note-willard-1) of a [set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set_(mathematics) "Set (mathematics)")![Image 1: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is a [family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Family_of_sets "Family of sets") of [subsets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subset "Subset") of ![Image 2: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) whose union is all of ![Image 3: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab). More formally, if ![Image 4: {\displaystyle C=\lbrace U_{\alpha }:\alpha \in A\rbrace }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/d355b464806f02bd2743b715bc795722d0a1f42c) is an [indexed family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indexed_family "Indexed family") of subsets ![Image 5: {\displaystyle U_{\alpha }\subset X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f72eb8307e494b171c24e3610948e81a75e06250) (indexed by the set ![Image 6: {\displaystyle A}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7daff47fa58cdfd29dc333def748ff5fa4c923e3)), then ![Image 7: {\displaystyle C}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4fc55753007cd3c18576f7933f6f089196732029) is a cover of ![Image 8: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) if ![Image 9: {\displaystyle \bigcup _{\alpha \in A}U_{\alpha }=X.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1a0405356ecd50eb505f5a3d1053ed943125bea1) Thus the collection ![Image 10: {\displaystyle \lbrace U_{\alpha }:\alpha \in A\rbrace }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/43d8866143ae5e05b23d5fb19d222c90447eeac9) is a cover of ![Image 11: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) if each element of ![Image 12: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) belongs to at least one of the subsets ![Image 13: {\displaystyle U_{\alpha }}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c507812c8cdaf4cea8d2e7e1705b495a3010a352).

Covers are commonly used in the context of [topology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topology "Topology"). If the set ![Image 14: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is a [topological space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topological_space "Topological space"), then a cover ![Image 15: {\displaystyle C}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4fc55753007cd3c18576f7933f6f089196732029) of ![Image 16: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is a collection of subsets ![Image 17: {\displaystyle \{U_{\alpha }\}_{\alpha \in A}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8ee60af71f3a9be7713c75ee88fa354228de34fe) of ![Image 18: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) whose union is the whole space ![Image 19: {\displaystyle X=\bigcup _{\alpha \in A}U_{\alpha }}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b2e6cdf822ddd4a1bc0589a9f14cdcbbf704c05f). In this case ![Image 20: {\displaystyle C}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4fc55753007cd3c18576f7933f6f089196732029) is said to cover ![Image 21: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab), or that the sets ![Image 22: {\displaystyle U_{\alpha }}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c507812c8cdaf4cea8d2e7e1705b495a3010a352) cover ![Image 23: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab).[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_cover#cite_note-willard-1)

If ![Image 24: {\displaystyle Y}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/961d67d6b454b4df2301ac571808a3538b3a6d3f) is a (topological) subspace of ![Image 25: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab), then a cover of ![Image 26: {\displaystyle Y}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/961d67d6b454b4df2301ac571808a3538b3a6d3f) is a collection of subsets ![Image 27: {\displaystyle C=\{U_{\alpha }\}_{\alpha \in A}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7902765f61f6e620da2d0b4a88a8ca3f0a41a21d) of ![Image 28: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) whose union contains ![Image 29: {\displaystyle Y}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/961d67d6b454b4df2301ac571808a3538b3a6d3f). That is, ![Image 30: {\displaystyle C}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4fc55753007cd3c18576f7933f6f089196732029) is a cover of ![Image 31: {\displaystyle Y}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/961d67d6b454b4df2301ac571808a3538b3a6d3f) if ![Image 32: {\displaystyle Y\subseteq \bigcup _{\alpha \in A}U_{\alpha }.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/40fc2c0f94e9c6367a4345f91d3193851ee137ce) Here, ![Image 33: {\displaystyle Y}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/961d67d6b454b4df2301ac571808a3538b3a6d3f) may be covered with either sets in ![Image 34: {\displaystyle Y}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/961d67d6b454b4df2301ac571808a3538b3a6d3f) itself or sets in the parent space ![Image 35: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab).

A cover of ![Image 36: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is said to be [locally finite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Locally_finite_collection "Locally finite collection") if every point of ![Image 37: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) has a [neighborhood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neighborhood_(topology) "Neighborhood (topology)") that intersects only [finitely](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finite_set "Finite set") many sets in the cover. Formally, ![Image 38: {\displaystyle C=\{U_{\alpha }\}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/2ba11fc8c6b3ccf65ea158a145563f4f1c911e3f) is locally finite if, for any ![Image 39: {\displaystyle x\in X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/3e580967f68f36743e894aa7944f032dda6ea01d), there exists some neighborhood ![Image 40: {\displaystyle N(x)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ca1ee5a5ef6497108b2f6d7f1fc8a224a52062fa) of ![Image 41: {\displaystyle x}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/87f9e315fd7e2ba406057a97300593c4802b53e4) such that the set ![Image 42: {\displaystyle \left\{\alpha \in A:U_{\alpha }\cap N(x)\neq \varnothing \right\}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5cc179bb7da928e515f943e6d970bc76ac9d0ee1) is finite. A cover of ![Image 43: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is said to be _point finite_ if every point of ![Image 44: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is contained in only finitely many sets in the cover.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_cover#cite_note-willard-1) A cover is point finite if locally finite, though the converse is not necessarily true.

Let ![Image 45: {\displaystyle C}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4fc55753007cd3c18576f7933f6f089196732029) be a cover of a topological space ![Image 46: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab). A _subcover_ of ![Image 47: {\displaystyle C}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4fc55753007cd3c18576f7933f6f089196732029) is a subset of ![Image 48: {\displaystyle C}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4fc55753007cd3c18576f7933f6f089196732029) that still covers ![Image 49: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab). The cover ![Image 50: {\displaystyle C}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4fc55753007cd3c18576f7933f6f089196732029) is said to be an _open cover_ if each of its members is an [open set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_set "Open set"). That is, each ![Image 51: {\displaystyle U_{\alpha }}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c507812c8cdaf4cea8d2e7e1705b495a3010a352) is contained in ![Image 52: {\displaystyle T}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ec7200acd984a1d3a3d7dc455e262fbe54f7f6e0), where ![Image 53: {\displaystyle T}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ec7200acd984a1d3a3d7dc455e262fbe54f7f6e0) is the topology on _X_.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_cover#cite_note-willard-1)

A simple way to get a subcover is to omit the sets contained in another set in the cover. Consider specifically open covers. Let ![Image 54: {\displaystyle {\mathcal {B}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/e5622de88a69f68340f8dcb43d0b8bd443ba9e13) be a topological basis of ![Image 55: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) and ![Image 56: {\displaystyle {\mathcal {O}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/d6ae2ed4058fb748a183d9ada8aea50a00d0c89f) be an open cover of ![Image 57: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab). First, take ![Image 58: {\displaystyle {\mathcal {A}}=\{A\in {\mathcal {B}}:{\text{ there exists }}U\in {\mathcal {O}}{\text{ such that }}A\subseteq U\}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9eb8e4a0dfac42a8c9ec8d09f8891146532f7304). Then ![Image 59: {\displaystyle {\mathcal {A}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/280ae03440942ab348c2ca9b8db6b56ffa9618f8) is a refinement of ![Image 60: {\displaystyle {\mathcal {O}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/d6ae2ed4058fb748a183d9ada8aea50a00d0c89f). Next, for each ![Image 61: {\displaystyle A\in {\mathcal {A}},}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ff68c10caaefaa39e2c1c6fc843ad51e7aacbf97) one may select a ![Image 62: {\displaystyle U_{A}\in {\mathcal {O}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7421ae5bbf043db27060b1d3fbac77fc5ffca51e) containing ![Image 63: {\displaystyle A}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7daff47fa58cdfd29dc333def748ff5fa4c923e3) (requiring the axiom of choice). Then ![Image 64: {\displaystyle {\mathcal {C}}=\{U_{A}\in {\mathcal {O}}:A\in {\mathcal {A}}\}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a92229c3e08655e60b99798990e306f39d35e1ac) is a subcover of ![Image 65: {\displaystyle {\mathcal {O}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b72c331152d4ea2b75e0ca22814f6e216e61b476) Hence the cardinality of a subcover of an open cover can be as small as that of any topological basis. Hence, second countability implies space is [Lindelöf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lindel%C3%B6f_space "Lindelöf space").

A **refinement** of a cover ![Image 66: {\displaystyle C}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4fc55753007cd3c18576f7933f6f089196732029) of a topological space ![Image 67: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is a new cover ![Image 68: {\displaystyle D}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f34a0c600395e5d4345287e21fb26efd386990e6) of ![Image 69: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) such that every set in ![Image 70: {\displaystyle D}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f34a0c600395e5d4345287e21fb26efd386990e6) is contained in some set in ![Image 71: {\displaystyle C}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4fc55753007cd3c18576f7933f6f089196732029). Formally,

![Image 72: {\displaystyle D=\{V_{\beta }\}_{\beta \in B}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f9bd87472e27d694125fba48d862a3bdb5b3e9d2) is a refinement of ![Image 73: {\displaystyle C=\{U_{\alpha }\}_{\alpha \in A}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7902765f61f6e620da2d0b4a88a8ca3f0a41a21d) if for all ![Image 74: {\displaystyle \beta \in B}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/597992ad6afd28605ae09e0b22b65690bcadb543) there exists ![Image 75: {\displaystyle \alpha \in A}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7d584ba1104d5f4e64c91027696802e38979ea02) such that ![Image 76: {\displaystyle V_{\beta }\subseteq U_{\alpha }.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/fde5cb79d3add7190ad57e8250c7997a812e5049)
In other words, there is a **refinement map**![Image 77: {\displaystyle \phi :B\to A}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c580546fb83e7e15882b31b65aba9575db231073) satisfying ![Image 78: {\displaystyle V_{\beta }\subseteq U_{\phi (\beta )}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0a8c82c92b43608689c41a7e06eb369b02763488) for every ![Image 79: {\displaystyle \beta \in B.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b5862c04ea9e98c8d143b652838eb7bee35f3ea0) This map is used, for instance, in the [Čech cohomology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%8Cech_cohomology "Čech cohomology") of ![Image 80: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab).[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_cover#cite_note-bott-2)

Every subcover is also a refinement, but the opposite is not always true. A subcover is made from the sets that are in the cover, but omitting some of them; whereas a refinement is made from any sets that are subsets of the sets in the cover.

The refinement relation on the set of covers of ![Image 81: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is [transitive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transitive_relation "Transitive relation") and [reflexive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflexive_relation "Reflexive relation"), i.e. a [Preorder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Preorder "Preorder"). It is never [asymmetric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asymmetric_relation "Asymmetric relation") for ![Image 82: {\displaystyle X\neq \emptyset }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a174e8fd37ae6a1cad4c0b9b522e87ad2fcf7fdd).

Generally speaking, a refinement of a given structure is another that in some sense contains it. Examples are to be found when partitioning an [interval](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interval_(mathematics) "Interval (mathematics)") (one refinement of ![Image 83: {\displaystyle a_{0}<a_{1}<\cdots <a_{n}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/bd617888ff25cff1332b5ef17634d07d6977d9ff) being ![Image 84: {\displaystyle a_{0}<b_{0}<a_{1}<a_{2}<\cdots <a_{n-1}<b_{1}<a_{n}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/00934f8ad1ce1543e7d5e5211c26da6a28b4f2dc)), considering [topologies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topology "Topology") (the [standard topology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_topology "Standard topology") in Euclidean space being a refinement of the [trivial topology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trivial_topology "Trivial topology")). When subdividing [simplicial complexes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplicial_complex "Simplicial complex") (the first [barycentric subdivision](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barycentric_subdivision "Barycentric subdivision") of a simplicial complex is a refinement), the situation is slightly different: every [simplex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplex "Simplex") in the finer complex is a face of some simplex in the coarser one, and both have equal underlying polyhedra.

Yet another notion of refinement is that of [star refinement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_refinement "Star refinement").

The language of covers is often used to define several topological properties related to compactness. A topological space ![Image 85: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is said to be:

*   [compact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compact_space "Compact space") if every open cover has a finite subcover, (or equivalently that every open cover has a finite refinement);
*   [Lindelöf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lindel%C3%B6f_space "Lindelöf space") if every open cover has a [countable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Countable "Countable") subcover, (or equivalently that every open cover has a countable refinement);
*   [metacompact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metacompact_space "Metacompact space"): if every open cover has a point-finite open refinement;
*   [paracompact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paracompact_space "Paracompact space"): if every open cover admits a locally finite open refinement; and
*   [orthocompact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthocompact_space "Orthocompact space"): if every open cover has an interior-preserving open refinement.

For some more variations see the above articles.

A topological space ![Image 86: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is said to be of [covering dimension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covering_dimension "Covering dimension")_![Image 87: {\displaystyle n}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest\_v1/media/math/render/svg/a601995d55609f2d9f5e233e36fbe9ea26011b3b)_ if every open cover of ![Image 88: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) has a point-finite open refinement such that no point of ![Image 89: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab) is included in more than _![Image 90: {\displaystyle n+1}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest\_v1/media/math/render/svg/2a135e65a42f2d73cccbfc4569523996ca0036f1)_ sets in the refinement and if _![Image 91: {\displaystyle n}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest\_v1/media/math/render/svg/a601995d55609f2d9f5e233e36fbe9ea26011b3b)_ is the minimum value for which this is true.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_cover#cite_note-munkres-3) If no such minimal _![Image 92: {\displaystyle n}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest\_v1/media/math/render/svg/a601995d55609f2d9f5e233e36fbe9ea26011b3b)_ exists, the space is said to be of infinite covering dimension.
