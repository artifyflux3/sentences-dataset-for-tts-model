Comparison of 1 square metre with some Imperial and metric units of area

The **square metre** ([international spelling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_and_British_English_spelling_differences#-re,_-er "American and British English spelling differences") as used by the [International Bureau of Weights and Measures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Bureau_of_Weights_and_Measures "International Bureau of Weights and Measures")) or **square meter** ([American spelling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_and_British_English_spelling_differences#-re,_-er "American and British English spelling differences")) is the unit of [area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Area "Area") in the [International System of Units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_System_of_Units "International System of Units") (SI) with symbol **m 2**.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square_metre#cite_note-1) It is the area of a [square](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square "Square") with sides one [metre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metre "Metre") in length.

Adding and subtracting [SI prefixes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SI_prefix "SI prefix") creates multiples and submultiples; however, as the unit is [exponentiated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exponentiate "Exponentiate"), the [quantities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantities "Quantities")[grow exponentially](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exponential_growth "Exponential growth") by the corresponding [power of 10](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_of_10 "Power of 10"). For example, 1 [kilometre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kilometre "Kilometre") is 10 3 (one [thousand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thousand "Thousand")) times the length of 1 metre, but 1 square kilometre is (10 3)2 (10 6, one [million](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Million "Million")) times the area of 1 square metre, and 1 cubic kilometre is (10 3)3 (10 9, one [billion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billion "Billion")) cubic metres.

Unicode characters
------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Square_metre&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Unicode characters")]

Unicode has several characters used to represent metric area units, but these are for compatibility with East Asian character encodings and are _not_ meant to be used in new documents.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square_metre#cite_note-Unicode-U3300-2)

*   U+33A1㎡SQUARE M SQUARED
*   U+33A2㎢SQUARE KM SQUARED
*   U+3378㍸SQUARE DM SQUARED
*   U+33A0㎠SQUARE CM SQUARED
*   U+339F㎟SQUARE MM SQUARED

Instead, the [Unicode superscript](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode_subscripts_and_superscripts "Unicode subscripts and superscripts")U+00B2 ² SUPERSCRIPT TWO can be used, as in m².
