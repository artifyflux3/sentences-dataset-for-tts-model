From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This timeline describes the major developments, both experimental and theoretical understanding of [fluid mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluid_mechanics "Fluid mechanics") and [continuum mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continuum_mechanics "Continuum mechanics"). This timeline includes developments in:

*   Theoretical models of [hydrostatics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrostatics "Hydrostatics"), [hydrodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluid_dynamics "Fluid dynamics") and [aerodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aerodynamics "Aerodynamics").
*   [Hydraulics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydraulics "Hydraulics")
*   [Elasticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elasticity_(physics) "Elasticity (physics)")
*   [Mechanical waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanical_wave "Mechanical wave") and [acoustics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acoustics "Acoustics")
*   Valves and [fluidics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluidics "Fluidics")
*   [Gas laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas_laws "Gas laws")
*   [Turbulence modeling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turbulence_modeling "Turbulence modeling")
*   [Plasticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasticity_(physics) "Plasticity (physics)") and [rheology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rheology "Rheology")
*   Quantum fluids like [Bose–Einstein condensates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bose%E2%80%93Einstein_condensate "Bose–Einstein condensate") and [superfluidity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superfluidity "Superfluidity")
*   [Microfluidics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microfluidics "Microfluidics")

Prehistory and antiquity
------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Prehistory and antiquity")]

[![Image 1](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Buoyancy.svg/250px-Buoyancy.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Buoyancy.svg)

[Free body diagram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_body_diagram "Free body diagram") of a ball floating on water. The principles of [buoyancy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buoyancy "Buoyancy") were known in [classical antiquity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_antiquity "Classical antiquity").

*   Before 3000 BC – [Civilization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civilization "Civilization") starts by settling around rivers, coast and lakes.
*   3000 BC – Irrigation techniques develop in [Mesopotamia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesopotamia "Mesopotamia") and [Ancient Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egypt "Ancient Egypt").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)[Indus Valley Civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilisation "Indus Valley Civilisation") develops city-wide drainage systems and toilet systems.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1) Egyptians develop [reed boats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reed_boat "Reed boat").
*   2300 BC – Construction of the [Nahrawan Canal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nahrawan_Canal "Nahrawan Canal").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   2000–1500 BC – First dams constructed in India to control water.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1700 BC – [Windmill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windmill "Windmill") are used in [Babylonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Babylonia "Babylonia") to pump water.
*   14th century BC – [Water clock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_clock "Water clock") are developed in Egypt under the reign of [Amenhotep III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amenhotep_III "Amenhotep III"). Clepsydra water clock design is developed in [ancient Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greece "Ancient Greece").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   6th century BC – [Theodorus of Samos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theodorus_of_Samos "Theodorus of Samos") invents the [water level](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_level "Water level"). [Ancient Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Rome "Ancient Rome")'s drainage system is designed during the reign of [Tarquinius Priscus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucius_Tarquinius_Priscus "Lucius Tarquinius Priscus"). Rome's [Cloaca Maxima](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloaca_Maxima "Cloaca Maxima") is constructed by lining a river bed with stone. [Tunnel of Eupalinos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunnel_of_Eupalinos "Tunnel of Eupalinos") is constructed in [Samos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samos "Samos").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   4th century BC – [Mencius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mencius "Mencius") describes how to measure an elephant using displacement of water. Development of [rain gauges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rain_gauge "Rain gauge") in India.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)[Aqua Appia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aqua_Appia "Aqua Appia") first [Roman aqueduct](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_aqueduct "Roman aqueduct") is built in Rome.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   3rd century BC – [Archimedes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes "Archimedes") published _[On Floating Bodies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/On\_Floating\_Bodies "On Floating Bodies")_ describing the general [principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes%27_principle "Archimedes' principle") for [buoyancy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buoyancy "Buoyancy") and [hydrostatics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrostatics "Hydrostatics"). [Archimedes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes "Archimedes") develops [Archimedes' screw](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes%27_screw "Archimedes' screw") for water extraction.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   2nd century BC – The aqueduct [Aqua Tepula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aqua_Tepula "Aqua Tepula") and [Aqua Marcia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aqua_Marcia "Aqua Marcia") aqueducts are completed in Rome.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)[Zhang Heng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhang_Heng "Zhang Heng") of [Han dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty") designs the first known [seismoscope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seismoscope "Seismoscope").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-needham-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-DeweyByerly-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-4)
*   1st century BC – [Frontinus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frontinus "Frontinus") publishes his treatise _[De aquaeductu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De\_aquaeductu "De aquaeductu")_ on Roman water engineering. [Hero of Alexandria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hero_of_Alexandria "Hero of Alexandria") makes a series of experiments and devices with fluids, including the [aeolipile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aeolipile "Aeolipile") steam device and wind harnessing devices.

*   8th–13th century – [Arab Agricultural Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Agricultural_Revolution "Arab Agricultural Revolution")
*   725 – [Northumbrian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Northumbria "Kingdom of Northumbria") monk [Bede](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bede "Bede") publishes _[The Reckoning of Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Reckoning\_of\_Time "The Reckoning of Time")_, which includes a quantitative description of the influence of the moon and the sun over the tides.
*   c. 850– [Abu Ma'shar al-Balkhi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abu_Ma%27shar_al-Balkhi "Abu Ma'shar al-Balkhi") (Albumasar) publishes his Kitab al-madkhal al-kabir recording the Moon position and tides, he recognizes that there are two tides in day.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-5)
*   850 – The _[Book of Ingenious Devices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book\_of\_Ingenious\_Devices "Book of Ingenious Devices")_ is published by the [Banū Mūsā brothers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ban%C5%AB_M%C5%ABs%C4%81_brothers "Banū Mūsā brothers"), describing a number of early [automatic controls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automatic_control "Automatic control") using fluid mechanics.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-Koetsier-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-7)
*   1206 – [Ismail al-Jazari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ismail_al-Jazari "Ismail al-Jazari") invented water-powered programmable automata/[robots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robot "Robot") and water music devices.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-Sharkey-8)

*   1432 – Portuguese develop [caravels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caravel "Caravel") for long-distance ocean travel.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1450 –[Nicholas of Cusa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicholas_of_Cusa "Nicholas of Cusa") publishes his experiments with fluids in _Idiota de staticis experimentis_, including the first proposal to measure [air moisture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_moisture "Air moisture") using wool.
*   1480-1510 – [Leonardo da Vinci](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci "Leonardo da Vinci") develops the first sophisticated [parachute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parachute "Parachute"), the first descriptions of [capillary action](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capillary_action "Capillary action"), and the first turbine water wheels designs.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1586 – [Simon Stevin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simon_Stevin "Simon Stevin") publishes _De Beghinselen des Waterwichts_ ("Principles on the weight of water") on hydrostatics. He first details the [hydrostatic paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrostatic_paradox "Hydrostatic paradox").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-9)
*   1596 – [Galileo Galilei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galileo_Galilei "Galileo Galilei") produces the first [(Galileo) thermometer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galileo_thermometer "Galileo thermometer").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)

*   1619 – [Benedetto Castelli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benedetto_Castelli "Benedetto Castelli") published _Della Misura dell'Acque Correnti_ ("On the Measurement of Running Waters"), one of the foundations of modern hydrodynamics.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-10)
*   1619 – [William Harvey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Harvey "William Harvey") provides first model of the human [circulatory system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circulatory_system "Circulatory system").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1624 – [Jan Baptist van Helmont](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jan_Baptist_van_Helmont "Jan Baptist van Helmont") coins the term "[gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas "Gas")".[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1631 – [René Descartes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Descartes "René Descartes") first describes the principle of the mercury [barometer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barometer "Barometer").
*   1643 – [Evangelista Torricelli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evangelista_Torricelli "Evangelista Torricelli") provides a relation between the speed of fluid flowing from an orifice to the height of fluid above the opening, given by [Torricelli's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torricelli%27s_law "Torricelli's law"). He also builds a mercury barometer and does a series of experiments on [vacuum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vacuum "Vacuum").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1650 – [Otto von Guericke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otto_von_Guericke "Otto von Guericke") invents the first [vacuum pump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vacuum_pump "Vacuum pump").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1653–1663 [Blaise Pascal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blaise_Pascal "Blaise Pascal") establishes [Pascal's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pascal%27s_law "Pascal's law") of hydrostatics.
*   1662-1678 – [Robert Boyle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Boyle "Robert Boyle") and [Edme Mariotte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edme_Mariotte "Edme Mariotte") independently discover a [gas law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas_laws "Gas laws") that describes the relationship between pressure and volume given by [Boyle's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boyle%27s_law "Boyle's law") (or Boyle-Mariotte's law).
*   1678 – [Robert Hooke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Hooke "Robert Hooke") publishes [Hooke's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hooke%27s_law "Hooke's law") describing linear deformation of a spring.
*   1687 – [Isaac Newton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Newton "Isaac Newton") publishes _[Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophi%C3%A6\_Naturalis\_Principia\_Mathematica "Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica")_ (_Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy_), introducing the [Newton's laws of motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%27s_laws_of_motion "Newton's laws of motion") of [classical mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_mechanics "Classical mechanics").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-11) He also introduces the concept of [Newtonian fluid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newtonian_fluid "Newtonian fluid").

[![Image 2](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Maquina_vapor_Watt_ETSIIM.jpg/250px-Maquina_vapor_Watt_ETSIIM.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Maquina_vapor_Watt_ETSIIM.jpg)

1832 [steam engine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steam_engine "Steam engine") based on [James Watt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Watt "James Watt")'s principles.

*   1713 – [Antoine Parent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoine_Parent "Antoine Parent") introduces the concept of [shear stress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shear_stress "Shear stress").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:1-12)
*   1714 – [Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Gabriel_Fahrenheit "Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit") develops the [mercury-in-glass thermometer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercury-in-glass_thermometer "Mercury-in-glass thermometer") along the [Fahrenheit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fahrenheit "Fahrenheit") temperature scale.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1718–1719 – [James Jurin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Jurin "James Jurin") writes the law of capillary action, known as [Jurin's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jurin%27s_law "Jurin's law").
*   1727 – [Leonhard Euler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonhard_Euler "Leonhard Euler") introduces [linear elasticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_elasticity "Linear elasticity") and the [Young's modulus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Young%27s_modulus "Young's modulus").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:1-12)
*   1732 – [Henri Pitot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henri_Pitot "Henri Pitot") discovers how to measure the pressure from the speed of a fluid using a [Pitot tube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pitot_tube "Pitot tube").[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-13)
*   1738 – [Daniel Bernoulli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli "Daniel Bernoulli") publishes _[Hydrodynamica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrodynamica "Hydrodynamica")_ discussing the mathematical relation between pressure and velocity of fluids according to [Bernoulli's principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernoulli%27s_principle "Bernoulli's principle").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1742 – [Anders Celsius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anders_Celsius "Anders Celsius") designs a thermometer with the [Celsius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celsius "Celsius") scale.
*   1744 – Euler introduces the concept of [deformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deformation_(physics) "Deformation (physics)") and strain.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:1-12)
*   1747 – [Jean le Rond d'Alembert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_le_Rond_d%27Alembert "Jean le Rond d'Alembert")'s [formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%27Alembert%27s_formula "D'Alembert's formula") for the solutions of the [wave equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wave_equation "Wave equation") in a string gets published.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-14)
*   1752 – D'Alembert show an inconsistency of treating fluids as inviscid incompressible fluids, known as [d'Alembert's paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%27Alembert%27s_paradox "D'Alembert's paradox").
*   1757 – Euler introduces the [Euler equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euler_equations_(fluid_dynamics) "Euler equations (fluid dynamics)") of fluid dynamics for incompressible and non-viscous flow. He also introduces the mathematical model for [buckling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buckling "Buckling").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:1-12)
*   1764 – [James Watt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Watt "James Watt") develops his steam water condenser leading to efficient [steam engines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steam_engine "Steam engine").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1765 – [Jean-Charles de Borda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Charles_de_Borda "Jean-Charles de Borda") experiments with whirling arm experiments. He corrects the available theories of air friction.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-15)
*   1766 – de Borda publishes "_Mémoire sur l’Écoulement des Fluides par les Orifices des Vases_" on hydraulics and resistance of fluid through orifices. He comes up with [Borda–Carnot equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borda%E2%80%93Carnot_equation "Borda–Carnot equation").
*   1768 – [Antoine de Chézy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoine_de_Ch%C3%A9zy "Antoine de Chézy") provides a semi-empirical formula for resistance of open channel flow, described by [Chézy formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ch%C3%A9zy_formula "Chézy formula").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1775 – [Pierre-Simon Girard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre-Simon_Girard "Pierre-Simon Girard") invents the [water turbine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_turbine "Water turbine").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1776 – [Charles Bossut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Bossut "Charles Bossut"), supervised by the [Marquis de Condorcet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marquis_de_Condorcet "Marquis de Condorcet") and d'Alembert, publishes _Nouvelles expériences sur la resistance de fluides,_ a report on a series experiments to test currents theories of hydraulics.
*   1775-76 – [Pierre-Simon Laplace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre-Simon_Laplace "Pierre-Simon Laplace") introduces the mathematical theory for [tidal forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tidal_force "Tidal force") on oceans.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-16)
*   1779 – [Pierre-Louis-Georges du Buat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre-Louis-Georges_du_Buat "Pierre-Louis-Georges du Buat") publishes _Principes de l'hydraulique_ ("Principles of hydraulics"), with semiempirical equations for the flow of water through pipes and open channels.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-17)[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-18)
*   1780 – [Jacques Charles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Charles "Jacques Charles") discover a gas law that describes the relationship between temperature and volume, given by [Charles's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles%27s_law "Charles's law").
*   1782 – The [Montgolfier brothers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montgolfier_brothers "Montgolfier brothers") invent the [hot air balloon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hot_air_balloon "Hot air balloon").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1785 – First theories of friction are introduced by [Charles-Augustin de Coulomb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles-Augustin_de_Coulomb "Charles-Augustin de Coulomb").[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-Popova_183%E2%80%93190-19)
*   1787 – [Ernst Chladni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernst_Chladni "Ernst Chladni"), publishes his experiments on vibrational modes of thin solid surfaces, describing the Chladni patterns created using a violin bow, based on previous experiments by Hooke.
*   1797 – [Giovanni Battista Venturi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giovanni_Battista_Venturi "Giovanni Battista Venturi") discovers the [Venturi effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venturi_effect "Venturi effect").[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-20)
*   1799 – [George Cayley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Cayley "George Cayley") introduces modern [fixed wing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fixed-wing_aircraft "Fixed-wing aircraft")-machines and identifies three important factors for flying machines: [thrust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thrust "Thrust"), [lift](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lift_(force) "Lift (force)"), [drag](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drag_(physics) "Drag (physics)"), and [weight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weight "Weight").

An [F/A-18C Hornet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McDonnell_Douglas_F/A-18_Hornet "McDonnell Douglas F/A-18 Hornet") breaks the [sound barrier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sound_barrier "Sound barrier") in the skies. Description of fluid at [supersonic speeds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supersonic_speed "Supersonic speed") were explored at the end of the 19th century before the development of manned airplanes.

*   1801 – [Robert Fulton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Fulton "Robert Fulton") develops the first submarine.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1805-1806 – The development of [Young–Laplace equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Young%E2%80%93Laplace_equation "Young–Laplace equation") by [Thomas Young](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Young_(scientist) "Thomas Young (scientist)") and improved by Laplace.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-21)
*   1808-1809 – [Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Louis_Gay-Lussac "Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac") describes the [law of combining gases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gay-Lussac%27s_law "Gay-Lussac's law").
*   1811–1812 – [Amedeo Avogadro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amedeo_Avogadro "Amedeo Avogadro") and [André-Marie Ampère](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andr%C3%A9-Marie_Amp%C3%A8re "André-Marie Ampère") independently discover a gas law relating volume and quantity of gas, given by [Avogadro's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avogadro%27s_law "Avogadro's law") (or Avogadro-Ampère's law).
*   1821 – [Claude-Louis Navier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Claude-Louis_Navier "Claude-Louis Navier") introduces [viscosity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viscosity "Viscosity") in to Euler equations of fluids.
*   1821 – [Sophie Germain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sophie_Germain "Sophie Germain") wins a contest of the [French Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Academy_of_Sciences "French Academy of Sciences") for providing a partial theory for the vibration of an elastic surfaces.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-22)
*   1827 – [Augustin-Louis Cauchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augustin-Louis_Cauchy "Augustin-Louis Cauchy") introduces the [Cauchy stress tensor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cauchy_stress_tensor "Cauchy stress tensor") and the concept of [stress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stress_(mechanics) "Stress (mechanics)") in [elasticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elasticity_(physics) "Elasticity (physics)").[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-23)[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:1-12)
*   1827 – [Robert Brown (botanist, born 1773)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Brown_(botanist,_born_1773) "Robert Brown (botanist, born 1773)"), identifies the [Brownian motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brownian_motion "Brownian motion") of [pollen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pollen "Pollen") grains suspended in water.
*   1831– [Michael Faraday](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Faraday "Michael Faraday") first describes vibrational modes in liquids, known as [Faraday waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faraday_wave "Faraday wave").[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-24)[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-25)
*   1831-1833– [Thomas Graham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Graham_(chemist) "Thomas Graham (chemist)") first studies the [diffusion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diffusion "Diffusion") in gases.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-26)
*   1834 – [Benoît Paul Émile Clapeyron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beno%C3%AEt_Paul_%C3%89mile_Clapeyron "Benoît Paul Émile Clapeyron") unifies many of the empirical gas laws into the [ideal gas law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ideal_gas_law "Ideal gas law").
*   1834 – [John Scott Russell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Scott_Russell "John Scott Russell") first describes the observation of solitary waves.
*   1837 – [George Green](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Green_(mathematician) "George Green (mathematician)") find the minimal number of [elastic moduli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elastic_modulus "Elastic modulus").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:1-12)
*   1838-40 – [Gotthilf Hagen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gotthilf_Hagen "Gotthilf Hagen") and [Jean Léonard Marie Poiseuille](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_L%C3%A9onard_Marie_Poiseuille "Jean Léonard Marie Poiseuille") study laminar flow, independently establishing [Hagen–Poiseuille equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hagen%E2%80%93Poiseuille_equation "Hagen–Poiseuille equation").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1841 – [George Biddell Airy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Biddell_Airy "George Biddell Airy") publishes the first correct formulation of [Airy wave theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Airy_wave_theory "Airy wave theory") of water waves.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-Craik2004-27)
*   1842 – [Christian Doppler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Doppler "Christian Doppler") introduces the [Doppler effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doppler_effect "Doppler effect").
*   1842-1850 – [Stokes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sir_George_Stokes,_1st_Baronet "Sir George Stokes, 1st Baronet") completes the equations of motions of fluids, now referred as [Navier–Stokes equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Navier%E2%80%93Stokes_equations "Navier–Stokes equations"). He also extends Airy wave theory to non-linear [Stokes wave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stokes_wave "Stokes wave") theory.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-Stokes1847-28)
*   1852 – [Heinrich Gustav Magnus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinrich_Gustav_Magnus "Heinrich Gustav Magnus") describes the [Magnus effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnus_effect "Magnus effect").[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-29)[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-30)
*   1855 – [Lord Kelvin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lord_Kelvin "Lord Kelvin") calculates the thermodynamics work and energy due to elastic deformation.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:1-12)
*   1855 – [Adolf Eugen Fick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adolf_Eugen_Fick "Adolf Eugen Fick") publishes [Fick's laws of diffusion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fick%27s_laws_of_diffusion "Fick's laws of diffusion").
*   1857 – [Henry Darcy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Darcy "Henry Darcy") studies flow through porous media, leading to the discovery of [Darcy's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Darcy%27s_law "Darcy's law").[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-31)
*   1857 – [Rudolf Clausius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudolf_Clausius "Rudolf Clausius") introduces the first model for the [kinetic theory of gases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_theory_of_gases "Kinetic theory of gases").[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-Maxwell-32)
*   1859 – [W. H. Besant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._H._Besant "W. H. Besant") introduces an equation for the dynamics of bubbles in an incompressible fluid.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-33)
*   1860 – [James Clerk Maxwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Clerk_Maxwell "James Clerk Maxwell") introduces the [Maxwell distribution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maxwell%E2%80%93Boltzmann_distribution "Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution") of velocity of classical gas molecules.
*   1863 –[Hermann von Helmholtz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermann_von_Helmholtz "Hermann von Helmholtz") publishes _[Sensations of Tone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sensations\_of\_Tone "Sensations of Tone")_ on the physics of sound perception.
*   1864 – [August Toepler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/August_Toepler "August Toepler") invents [Schlieren photography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schlieren_photography "Schlieren photography").
*   1865 – Lord Kelvin introduces the [Kelvin material](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelvin%E2%80%93Voigt_material "Kelvin–Voigt material") model for [viscoelasticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viscoelasticity "Viscoelasticity").[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-34)
*   1856 – [Carlo Marangoni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlo_Marangoni "Carlo Marangoni") studies the [tears of wine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tears_of_wine "Tears of wine"), now explained by the [Marangoni effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marangoni_effect "Marangoni effect").
*   1867 – Helmholtz works on [Helmholtz's theorems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helmholtz%27s_theorems "Helmholtz's theorems") for [vortex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vortex "Vortex") dynamics.
*   1867 – [James Clerk Maxwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Clerk_Maxwell "James Clerk Maxwell") introduces the [Maxwell material](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maxwell_material "Maxwell material") model for viscoelasticity.
*   1868–1871 – Helmholtz and Kelvin study and develop the theory of the [Kelvin–Helmholtz instability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelvin%E2%80%93Helmholtz_instability "Kelvin–Helmholtz instability").[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-35)
*   1870 – [William Rankine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Rankine "William Rankine") develops an equation for the study of [shock waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shock_wave "Shock wave").
*   1871 – [Francis Herbert Wenham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francis_Herbert_Wenham "Francis Herbert Wenham") designs and builds the first [wind tunnel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wind_tunnel "Wind tunnel").[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-36)[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-37)
*   1872-1877 – [Joseph Valentin Boussinesq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Valentin_Boussinesq "Joseph Valentin Boussinesq") introduces the concept of turbulence in forms of [eddy viscosity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turbulence_modeling "Turbulence modeling"), as well as [Boussinesq approximation for water waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boussinesq_approximation_(water_waves) "Boussinesq approximation (water waves)") and [Boussinesq approximation for buoyancy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boussinesq_approximation_(buoyancy) "Boussinesq approximation (buoyancy)").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1873 – [Johannes Diderik van der Waals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannes_Diderik_van_der_Waals "Johannes Diderik van der Waals") introduces the [Van der Waals equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_der_Waals_equation "Van der Waals equation").[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-38)
*   1883 – [Osborne Reynolds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Osborne_Reynolds "Osborne Reynolds") demonstrates the transition and differences between laminar and turbulent pipe flow.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1885 – [Lord Rayleigh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_William_Strutt,_3rd_Baron_Rayleigh "John William Strutt, 3rd Baron Rayleigh") predicts the existence of [Rayleigh surface waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rayleigh_wave "Rayleigh wave").[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-39)
*   1885 – Helmholtz describes the concept of [Helmholtz resonance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helmholtz_resonance "Helmholtz resonance").[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-Helmholtz1885-40)
*   1887 – [Pierre Henri Hugoniot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Henri_Hugoniot "Pierre Henri Hugoniot") based on the work of Rankine, introduces the [Rankine–Hugoniot conditions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rankine%E2%80%93Hugoniot_conditions "Rankine–Hugoniot conditions") to model shock waves.
*   1887 – First models of [supersonic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supersonic_speed "Supersonic speed") waves by [Ernst Mach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernst_Mach "Ernst Mach").[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-41) He introduces the concept of [Mach number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mach_number "Mach number").
*   1888 – First commercial [Venturi tube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venturi_tube "Venturi tube") by [Clemens Herschel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clemens_Herschel "Clemens Herschel").[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-42)
*   1888-1890 – Independently, Henry R. A. Mallock and [Maurice Couette](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurice_Couette "Maurice Couette") find the mathematical solution for the [Couette flow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Couette_flow "Couette flow").[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-43)
*   1889 – [Robert Manning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Manning_(engineer) "Robert Manning (engineer)") produces [Manning's formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manning_formula "Manning formula") for open channel flow.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1893 – [Carl Barus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Barus "Carl Barus") develops the theory of the [die swell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Die_swell "Die swell") in complex fluids.
*   1895 – [Diederik Korteweg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diederik_Korteweg "Diederik Korteweg") and [Gustav de Vries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gustav_de_Vries "Gustav de Vries")([1895](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#CITEREFKortewegde_Vries1895)) rediscover the [Korteweg–De Vries equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korteweg%E2%80%93De_Vries_equation "Korteweg–De Vries equation") first treated by Boussinesq and introduce the idea of [soliton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soliton "Soliton") solutions.[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-KdV-44)[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-45)

[![Image 4](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Laminar-turbulent_transition.jpg/250px-Laminar-turbulent_transition.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Laminar-turbulent_transition.jpg)

[Schlieren photograph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schlieren_photography "Schlieren photography") showing the [thermal convection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convection_(heat_transfer) "Convection (heat transfer)") plume rising from an ordinary candle in still air. Precise mathematical theories of [turbulence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turbulence "Turbulence") were not invented until the 20th century.

*   1902 – [Martin Kutta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Kutta "Martin Kutta") discusses the air flow through an [airfoil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Airfoil "Airfoil") using the [Kutta condition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kutta_condition "Kutta condition").
*   1903 – The [Wright brothers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wright_brothers "Wright brothers") carry the first successful manned airplane flight.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1903 – [Walther Ritz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walther_Ritz "Walther Ritz") introduces the [Ritz method](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ritz_method "Ritz method") to study beam theory and Chladni figures.[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-46)
*   1905 – First theory of [dislocations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dislocation "Dislocation") by [Vito Volterra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vito_Volterra "Vito Volterra").
*   1905-1906 – First successful theories of Brownian motion by [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein") and [Marian Smoluchowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marian_Smoluchowski "Marian Smoluchowski"), supporting the [atomic theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic_theory "Atomic theory") of matter.
*   1906 – [Richard Dixon Oldham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Dixon_Oldham "Richard Dixon Oldham") identifies the separate arrival of [p-waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/P-wave "P-wave"), s-waves and surface waves on [seismograms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seismogram "Seismogram") and found the first clear evidence that the Earth has a central core.[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-47)
*   1908 – [Paul Richard Heinrich Blasius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Richard_Heinrich_Blasius "Paul Richard Heinrich Blasius") introduces the concept of [boundary layer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boundary_layer "Boundary layer").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1908 – Experimental confirmation of the theories of Brownian motion by [Jean Baptiste Perrin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Baptiste_Perrin "Jean Baptiste Perrin").
*   1910: 
    *   [Harry Fielding Reid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Fielding_Reid "Harry Fielding Reid") put forward the [elastic rebound theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elastic-rebound_theory "Elastic-rebound theory") for [earthquakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earthquake "Earthquake").[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-48)
    *   Lord Rayleigh introduces the concept of [Rayleigh flow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rayleigh_flow "Rayleigh flow").[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-49)
    *   [Nikolay Zhukovsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolay_Zhukovsky_(scientist) "Nikolay Zhukovsky (scientist)") introduces the [Joukowsky transform](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joukowsky_transform "Joukowsky transform") and the [Kutta–Joukowski theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kutta%E2%80%93Joukowski_theorem "Kutta–Joukowski theorem") based on the work of Kutta.
    *   [Carl Wilhelm Oseen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Wilhelm_Oseen "Carl Wilhelm Oseen") solves the [Stokes' paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stokes%27_paradox "Stokes' paradox") by introducing [Oseen's approximation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oseen%27s_approximation "Oseen's approximation").[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-50)

*   1911 – [Augustus Edward Hough Love](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augustus_Edward_Hough_Love "Augustus Edward Hough Love") predicts the existence of [Love surface waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Love_wave "Love wave").[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-51)
*   1915–1916 – [Frederick W. Lanchester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_W._Lanchester "Frederick W. Lanchester") comes up with the [Lanchester's laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanchester%27s_laws "Lanchester's laws"), a set of differential equations that were practical for flying combat.
*   1915-1917 – [George Barker Jeffery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Barker_Jeffery "George Barker Jeffery")[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-52) and [Georg Hamel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georg_Hamel "Georg Hamel")[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-53) introduce the equations of [Jeffery–Hamel flow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeffery%E2%80%93Hamel_flow "Jeffery–Hamel flow").
*   1916 – [Horace Lamb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horace_Lamb "Horace Lamb") coins the term "[vorticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vorticity "Vorticity")".[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-54)
*   1916 – [Eugene C. Bingham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_C._Bingham "Eugene C. Bingham") studies [Bingham plastics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bingham_plastic "Bingham plastic")
*   1916-1923 – Lord Rayleigh, and later [G. I. Taylor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G._I._Taylor "G. I. Taylor") describe [Rayleigh–Taylor instability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rayleigh%E2%80%93Taylor_instability "Rayleigh–Taylor instability").
*   1917 – Lamb introduces [Lamb waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lamb_waves "Lamb waves"), generalizing Rayleigh's wave theory for thin metal plates.
*   1918 – [Ludwig Prandtl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludwig_Prandtl "Ludwig Prandtl") develops theory of flow over airplane wings.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1919 – [Jacob Bjerknes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacob_Bjerknes "Jacob Bjerknes") established the bases the [Norwegian cyclone model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norwegian_cyclone_model "Norwegian cyclone model").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1920 – [Nikola Tesla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikola_Tesla "Nikola Tesla") patents the [Tesla valve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tesla_valve "Tesla valve"), opening the field of [fluidics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluidics "Fluidics").[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-55)
*   1920 – Bingham coins the term [rheology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rheology "Rheology") from a suggestion by a colleague, [Markus Reiner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markus_Reiner "Markus Reiner").[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-56)
*   1921 – [Theodore von Kármán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theodore_von_K%C3%A1rm%C3%A1n "Theodore von Kármán") introduces the turbulence model of [Von Kármán swirling flow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Von_K%C3%A1rm%C3%A1n_swirling_flow "Von Kármán swirling flow"), and phenomena like [Kármán vortex street](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%A1rm%C3%A1n_vortex_street "Kármán vortex street").[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-Von_Karman_1921-57)
*   1921 – [Alan Arnold Griffith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Arnold_Griffith "Alan Arnold Griffith") develops his theory of [fracture mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fracture_mechanics "Fracture mechanics").[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-rupture-58)
*   1922 – [Supersonic wind tunnel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supersonic_wind_tunnel "Supersonic wind tunnel") is invented in [National Physical Laboratory (United Kingdom)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Physical_Laboratory_(United_Kingdom) "National Physical Laboratory (United Kingdom)").
*   1926 – Einstein solves the [tea leaf paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tea_leaf_paradox "Tea leaf paradox").
*   1925 – [Jakob Ackeret](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jakob_Ackeret "Jakob Ackeret") publishes the theory of supersonic airfoils.
*   1926 – [Erwin Madelung](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Madelung "Erwin Madelung") relates quantum mechanics with hydrodynamics through his [quantum hydrodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_hydrodynamics "Quantum hydrodynamics") equations, known as [Madelung equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madelung_equations "Madelung equations").
*   1931 – [Sylvia Skan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sylvia_Skan "Sylvia Skan") and Victor Montague Falkner introduce the equations for the [Falkner–Skan boundary layer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falkner%E2%80%93Skan_boundary_layer "Falkner–Skan boundary layer").[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-59)
*   1932 – The concept of quantum of sound ([phonons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phonon "Phonon")) is introduced by [Igor Tamm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Igor_Tamm "Igor Tamm").
*   1937 – [Superfluidity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superfluidity "Superfluidity") is discovered in [helium-4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helium-4 "Helium-4") by [Pyotr Kapitsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyotr_Kapitsa "Pyotr Kapitsa")[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-60) and independently by [John F. Allen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_F._Allen_(physicist) "John F. Allen (physicist)") and [Don Misener](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Misener "Don Misener").[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-61)
*   1938 – [Philip Saffman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_Saffman "Philip Saffman") and G. I. Taylor publish on [Saffman–Taylor instability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saffman%E2%80%93Taylor_instability "Saffman–Taylor instability").[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-62)
*   1937 – [Lev Landau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lev_Landau "Lev Landau") introduces [Landau theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landau_theory "Landau theory") of [phase transitions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phase_transition "Phase transition").
*   1940-1941 – [László Tisza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A1szl%C3%B3_Tisza "László Tisza") and Landau introduce the [two-fluid model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two-fluid_model "Two-fluid model") for [helium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helium "Helium").
*   1941 – Landau introduces the concept of [second sound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_sound "Second sound") in condensed matter.[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-63)
*   1942 – First [magnetohydrodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetohydrodynamics "Magnetohydrodynamics") descriptions of plasma by [Hannes Alfvén](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hannes_Alfv%C3%A9n "Hannes Alfvén"). He also introduced the idea of [Alfvén waves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfv%C3%A9n_wave "Alfvén wave").[[64]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-64)[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-65)
*   1948 – [Milton S. Plesset](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milton_S._Plesset "Milton S. Plesset") improves on Rayleigh and Bessant equations for the dynamics of bubbles by including surface tension according to [Rayleigh–Plesset equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rayleigh%E2%80%93Plesset_equation "Rayleigh–Plesset equation").[[66]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-Brennen-66)
*   1941 – [Andrey Kolmogorov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrey_Kolmogorov "Andrey Kolmogorov") introduces his detailed theory of [turbulence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turbulence "Turbulence").
*   1947– [Karl Weissenberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_Weissenberg "Karl Weissenberg") introduces the [Weissenberg effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weissenberg_effect "Weissenberg effect") in non-Newtonian fluids.
*   1950 – [James G. Oldroyd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_G._Oldroyd "James G. Oldroyd") introduces the [Oldroyd-B model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oldroyd-B_model "Oldroyd-B model") of viscoelasticity.[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-67)
*   1944 – [Lewis Ferry Moody](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lewis_Ferry_Moody "Lewis Ferry Moody") plots [Darcy–Weisbach friction factor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Darcy%E2%80%93Weisbach_equation#Darcy_friction_factor "Darcy–Weisbach equation") against [Reynolds number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reynolds_number "Reynolds number") for various values of relative [roughness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surface_roughness "Surface roughness"), leading to the first [Moody chart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moody_chart "Moody chart").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1961 – [Eugene P. Gross](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_P._Gross "Eugene P. Gross")[[68]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-68) and [Lev Pitaevskii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lev_Pitaevskii "Lev Pitaevskii")[[69]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-69) introduce [Gross–Pitaevskii equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross%E2%80%93Pitaevskii_equation "Gross–Pitaevskii equation") for the condensation of [bosons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boson "Boson").
*   1963 – Alex Kaye describes the [Kaye effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaye_effect "Kaye effect") in viscoelastic liquids.
*   1972 – [David Lee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Lee_(physicist) "David Lee (physicist)"), [Douglas Osheroff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douglas_Osheroff "Douglas Osheroff") and [Robert Coleman Richardson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Coleman_Richardson "Robert Coleman Richardson") discovered two phase transitions of [helium-3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helium-3 "Helium-3") along the melting curve, which were soon realized to be the two superfluid phases.
*   1995 – The first [Bose–Einstein condensate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bose%E2%80%93Einstein_condensate "Bose–Einstein condensate") is produced by [Eric Cornell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_Allin_Cornell "Eric Allin Cornell") and [Carl Wieman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Wieman "Carl Wieman") at the [University of Colorado at Boulder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Colorado_at_Boulder "University of Colorado at Boulder")[NIST](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Institute_of_Standards_and_Technology "National Institute of Standards and Technology")–[JILA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JILA "JILA") lab, in a gas of [rubidium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rubidium "Rubidium") atoms cooled to 170 [nanokelvins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelvin "Kelvin") (nK).[[70]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-:02-70) Shortly thereafter, [Wolfgang Ketterle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfgang_Ketterle "Wolfgang Ketterle") at MIT produced a Bose–Einstein condensate in a gas of [sodium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium "Sodium") atoms.

*   c. 2000 – Development on the field of [microfluidics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microfluidics "Microfluidics").[[71]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-71)
*   2003 – [Deborah S. Jin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deborah_S._Jin "Deborah S. Jin") and her collaboration produce the first [fermionic condensate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermionic_condensate "Fermionic condensate").[[72]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_fluid_and_continuum_mechanics#cite_note-NASA-72)

*   [History of aviation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_aviation "History of aviation")
