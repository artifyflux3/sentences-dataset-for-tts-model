A sorocarp (from the Greek word soros "a heap" + karpos "fruit") is the fruiting body characteristic of certain cellular slime moulds (e.g., Dictyosteliida ).  Each sorocarp consists of both a sorophore (stalk) and a sorus .

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] Sorocarps release spores .
