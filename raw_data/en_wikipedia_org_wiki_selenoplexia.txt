A Dictionary of Medical Science (1895), in its discussion of the conditions of sunstroke , states that the "morbid phenomena observable after death are generally those of nervous exhaustion, neuroparalysis.  Like effects have also been ascribed to the moon, selenoplexia, selenoplege, moonstroke; and to the stars, starstroke."
