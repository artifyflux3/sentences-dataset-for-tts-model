) PDF f ( μ μ , Λ Λ | μ μ 0 , λ λ , W , ν ν ) = N ( μ μ | μ μ 0 , ( λ λ Λ Λ ) − − 1 ) W ( Λ Λ | W , ν ν ) {\displaystyle f({\boldsymbol {\mu }},{\boldsymbol {\Lambda }}|{\boldsymbol {\mu }}_{0},\lambda ,\mathbf {W} ,\nu )={\mathcal {N}}({\boldsymbol {\mu }}|{\boldsymbol {\mu }}_{0},(\lambda {\boldsymbol {\Lambda }})^{-1})\ {\mathcal {W}}({\boldsymbol {\Lambda }}|\mathbf {W} ,\nu )} In probability theory and statistics , the normal-Wishart distribution (or Gaussian-Wishart distribution ) is a multivariate four-parameter family of continuous probability distributions . It is the conjugate prior of a multivariate normal distribution with unknown mean and precision matrix (the inverse of the covariance matrix ).

[ 1 ] Definition [ edit ] Suppose μ μ | μ μ 0 , λ λ , Λ Λ ∼ ∼ N ( μ μ 0 , ( λ λ Λ Λ ) − − 1 ) {\displaystyle {\boldsymbol {\mu }}|{\boldsymbol {\mu }}_{0},\lambda ,{\boldsymbol {\Lambda }}\sim {\mathcal {N}}({\boldsymbol {\mu }}_{0},(\lambda {\boldsymbol {\Lambda }})^{-1})} has a multivariate normal distribution with mean μ μ 0 {\displaystyle {\boldsymbol {\mu }}_{0}} and covariance matrix ( λ λ Λ Λ ) − − 1 {\displaystyle (\lambda {\boldsymbol {\Lambda }})^{-1}} , where Λ Λ | W , ν ν ∼ ∼ W ( Λ Λ | W , ν ν ) {\displaystyle {\boldsymbol {\Lambda }}|\mathbf {W} ,\nu \sim {\mathcal {W}}({\boldsymbol {\Lambda }}|\mathbf {W} ,\nu )} has a Wishart distribution . Then ( μ μ , Λ Λ ) {\displaystyle ({\boldsymbol {\mu }},{\boldsymbol {\Lambda }})} has a normal-Wishart distribution, denoted as ( μ μ , Λ Λ ) ∼ ∼ N W ( μ μ 0 , λ λ , W , ν ν ) .

The marginal distribution over μ μ {\displaystyle {\boldsymbol {\mu }}} is a multivariate t -distribution .
