An elliptical cone quadric surface

An **elliptical cone** is a [cone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cone "Cone") with an [elliptical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ellipse "Ellipse") base.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptic_cone#cite_note-:1-1) It is a generalization of the _[circular cone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circular\_cone "Circular cone")_ and a special case of the _[generalized cone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generalized\_cone "Generalized cone")_.

The term might refer to the [solid figure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solid_figure "Solid figure") bounded by the base or only to the lateral [conic surface](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conic_surface "Conic surface"), a [quadric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quadric "Quadric") called _conical quadric_ or _quadratic cone_.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptic_cone#cite_note-osg-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptic_cone#cite_note-young-3)

In a [three-dimensional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-dimensional "Three-dimensional")[Cartesian coordinate system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartesian_coordinate_system "Cartesian coordinate system"), an elliptic cone is the [locus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Locus_(mathematics) "Locus (mathematics)") of an equation of the form:[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptic_cone#cite_note-4)

![Image 2: {\displaystyle {\frac {x^{2}}{a^{2}}}+{\frac {y^{2}}{b^{2}}}=z^{2}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/14b603fddcb01a3ab642d0d432207e529ee30e2a)

It is an [affine image](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Affine_map "Affine map") of the unit [right circular cone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Right_circular_cone "Right circular cone") with equation ![Image 3: {\displaystyle x^{2}+y^{2}=z^{2}\ .}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/48ee56a974d71a87af039a9f89060fa04c288526) From the fact that the affine image of a [conic section](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conic_section "Conic section") is a conic section of the same type (ellipse, [parabola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parabola "Parabola"), etc.), any [plane section](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plane_section "Plane section") of an elliptic cone is a conic section (see [Circular section#Elliptic cone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circular_section#Elliptic_cone "Circular section")).

The intersection curve of an elliptic cone with a concentric sphere is a _[spherical conic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spherical\_conic "Spherical conic")_.
