**Eternity**, in [common parlance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_parlance "Common parlance"), is an [infinite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infinity "Infinity") amount of [time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time "Time") that never ends or the quality, condition or fact of being everlasting or eternal.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eternity#cite_note-1)[Classical philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_philosophy "Classical philosophy"), however, defines eternity as what is timeless or exists outside time, whereas **sempiternity** corresponds to infinite duration.

Philosophy
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eternity&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Philosophy")]

See also: [Eternalism (philosophy of time)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eternalism_(philosophy_of_time) "Eternalism (philosophy of time)") and [Philosophy of space and time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophy_of_space_and_time "Philosophy of space and time")

[Classical philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_philosophy "Classical philosophy") defines eternity as what exists outside time, as in describing timeless [supernatural](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supernatural "Supernatural") beings and forces, distinguished from sempiternity which corresponds to infinite time, as described in [requiem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requiem "Requiem") prayers for the dead.[_[which?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid\_weasel\_words "Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words")_] Some thinkers, such as [Aristotle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aristotle "Aristotle"), suggest the [eternity of the natural cosmos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eternity_of_the_world "Eternity of the world") in regard to both past and future eternal duration. [Boethius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boethius "Boethius") defined eternity as "simultaneously full and perfect possession of interminable life".[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eternity#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoedder1902book_2,_ch._2,_%22[httpsmaritainndedujmcetextnath34htm_The_Eternity_of_God]%22-2)[[a]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eternity#cite_note-3)[Thomas Aquinas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Aquinas "Thomas Aquinas") believed that God's eternity does not cease, as it is without either a beginning or an end; the concept of eternity is of [divine simplicity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divine_simplicity "Divine simplicity"), thus incapable of being defined or fully understood by humankind.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eternity#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHelm2010%C2%A7._6,_%22[httpsstanfordlibrarysydneyeduauarchivessum2010entrieseternity~textMedieval20thinkers-It20is20intextFor20Aquinas20God27s20eternity20isfully20grasped20by20a20creature_Medieval_thinkers]%22-4)

[Thomas Hobbes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Hobbes "Thomas Hobbes") (1588–1679) and many others in the [Age of Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment "Age of Enlightenment") drew on the classical distinction to put forward metaphysical hypotheses such as "eternity is a permanent now".[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eternity#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHobbes166250-5)

### Contemporary philosophy and physics

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eternity&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Contemporary philosophy and physics")]

Today cosmologists, philosophers, and others look towards analyses of the concept from across cultures and history. They debate, among other things, whether an absolute concept of eternity has real application for fundamental [laws of physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laws_of_physics "Laws of physics"); compare the issue of [entropy as an arrow of time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entropy_as_an_arrow_of_time "Entropy as an arrow of time").

Religion
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eternity&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Religion")]

See also: [God and eternity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/God_and_eternity "God and eternity") and [Unto the ages of ages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unto_the_ages_of_ages "Unto the ages of ages")

Eternity as infinite duration is an important concept in many lives and [religions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion "Religion"). [God](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/God "God") or gods are often said to endure eternally, or exist for all time, forever, without beginning or end. Religious views of an [afterlife](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afterlife "Afterlife") may speak of it in terms of eternity or [eternal life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immortality "Immortality").[[b]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eternity#cite_note-6) Christian theologians may regard [immutability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immutability_(theology) "Immutability (theology)"), like the eternal [Platonic forms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Platonic_realism "Platonic realism"), as essential to eternity.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eternity#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDeng2018%C2%A7._3.1,_%22[httpsplatostanfordeduentrieseternityLociClas_The_%3Cspan_title=%22Latin-language_text%22%3E%3Ci_lang=%22la%22%3ELoci_Classici%3C/i%3E%3C/span%3E[[Category:Articles_containing_Latin-language_text]]]%22-7)[[c]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eternity#cite_note-8)

Eternity is often symbolized by the endless snake, swallowing its own tail, the [ouroboros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ouroboros "Ouroboros"). The circle, band, or ring is also commonly used as a symbol for eternity, as is the mathematical [symbol of infinity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbol_of_infinity "Symbol of infinity"), ∞{\displaystyle \infty }![Image 12: {\displaystyle \infty }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c26c105004f30c27aa7c2a9c601550a4183b1f21). Symbolically these are reminders that eternity has no beginning or end.
