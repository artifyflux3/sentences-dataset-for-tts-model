The most common units in modern use are the metric units , used in every country globally. In the United States the U.S. customary units are also in use. British Imperial units are still used for some purposes in the United Kingdom and some other countries. The metric system is sub-divided into SI and non-SI units.

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ] History [ edit ] This section is an excerpt from History of measurement Â§ Units of length .

[ edit ] Before the establishment of the decimal metric system in France during the French Revolution in the late 18th century , [ 4 ] many units of length were based on parts of the human body .

[ 5 ] [ 6 ] The Nippur cubit was one of the oldest known units of length. The oldest known metal standard for length corresponds to this Sumerian unit and dates from 2650 BCE.

[ 7 ] [ 8 ] This copper bar was discovered in Nippur , on the banks of the Euphrates , and is kept in the Istanbul Archaeological Museum . Archaeologists consider that this 51.85 centimetres long unit was the origin of the Roman foot . Indeed, the Egyptians divided the Sumerian cubit into 28 fingers and 16 of these fingers gave a Roman foot of 29.633Â cm.

Other SI units are derived from the meter by adding prefixes , as in millimeter or kilometer, thus producing systematic decimal multiples and submultiples of the base unit that span many orders of magnitude. For example, a kilometer is 1000Â m .
