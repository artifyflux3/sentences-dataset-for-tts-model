31870435 Links Journal homepage Journal page at publisher's website Journal page at Project MUSE Diacritics is a quarterly peer-reviewed academic journal established in 1971 at Cornell University and published by the Johns Hopkins University Press . Articles serve to review recent literature in the field of literary criticism , and have covered topics in gender studies , political theory , psychoanalysis , queer theory , and other areas. The editor-in-chief is Andrea Bachner (Cornell University).

[ 1 ] History [ edit ] The journal was founded by David Grossvogel, who served as the Romance Studies chair at Cornell.

[ 2 ] Its first issue was published in the Fall of 1971, and Grossvogel served as editor until 1976.

[ 2 ] The name “Diacritics” was suggested by Romance Studies professor Emeritus Philip Lewis, who served from 1971 to 1987 as managing and general editor.
