Chemical compound

**Ethanimine** is an organonitrogen [compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_compound "Chemical compound") classified as an [imine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imine "Imine"). It is formed by reacting acetaldehyde and ammonia, but rapidly polymerizes to [acetaldehyde ammonia trimer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetaldehyde_ammonia_trimer "Acetaldehyde ammonia trimer").

It has two [tautomers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tautomer "Tautomer"): ethanimine, an imine, and ethenamine or aminoethylene, an [amine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amine "Amine"). Ethanimine has two hydrogens on the carbon, while ethenamine has two on the nitrogen atom.

Occurrence
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ethanimine&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Occurrence")]

It is not well known terrestrially, but has been detected in abundance toward [Sagittarius B2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagittarius_B2 "Sagittarius B2") (Sgr B2), a dense interstellar cloud between stars toward the [Galactic Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galactic_Center "Galactic Center") of the [Milky Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way "Milky Way"). The distance between the Sgr B2 cloud and center of galaxy is 100 pc (1 pc = 3.26 ly). Ethanimine is mainly found in hot cores of ISM clouds; in case of Sgr B2, the region would be the Sgr B2 N and Sgr B2 M.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanimine#cite_note-1) Radio telescopes such as the [Green Bank Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Bank_Observatory "Green Bank Observatory")'s [Green Bank Telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Bank_Telescope "Green Bank Telescope") and those operated by the [National Radio Astronomy Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Radio_Astronomy_Observatory "National Radio Astronomy Observatory") (measuring radio frequency light lambda ranging from 1–300 GHz) are able to detect organic molecules such as ethanimines because its internal [energy transition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_transition "Energy transition"), more specifically the rotational transition is within the radio frequency of 14085 MHz = 140.8 GHz.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanimine#cite_note-2)
