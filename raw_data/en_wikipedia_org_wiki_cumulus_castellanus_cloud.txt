It is a misnomer for cumulus congestus and correspondingly can be an indicator of forthcoming showers and thunderstorms.  The World Meteorological Organization and the American Meteorological Society do not recognize cumulus castellanus as a distinct species, but instead classify all towering cumulus clouds as Cumulus congestus .
