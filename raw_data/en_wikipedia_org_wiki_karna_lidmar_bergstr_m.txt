She is an emeritus professor of Stockholm University . In 2004 she was elected into the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences . In 2018 she was appointed an honorary doctor of the University of Gothenburg Faculty of Science.

Life [ edit ] Karna Lidmar-Bergström was born in 1940. She studied at Lund University under professor Karl-Erik Bergsten .

[ 1 ] Later Mats Åkesson served as her unofficial doctoral advisor, as the tutor system did not exist at that time.

[ 1 ] Lidmar-Bergström is a geologist and geomorphologist known for her study of Pre- Quaternary landforms in Sweden and Norway .

[ 2 ] Lidmar-Bergström studied the flint distribution and how the Swedish landmasses were formed, and was the discoverer of a previously unknown north–south current now called the Kattegatt current.

[ 2 ] Lidmar-Bergström also invented a method she called Stratigraphical Landscape Analyses, based on her research in southern Sweden.

[ 3 ] She was responsible for the preparation of geomorphological maps for the National Atlas of Sweden.

[ 2 ] In 2004 she was elected into the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences .

[ 2 ] She is an emeritus professor of Stockholm University .

[ 4 ] In 2018, when she had already been retired for several years, she was appointed an honorary doctor of the University of Gothenburg Faculty of Science.

[ 5 ] The award was presented by Professor Katarina Abrahamsson from the Department of Marine Sciences.
