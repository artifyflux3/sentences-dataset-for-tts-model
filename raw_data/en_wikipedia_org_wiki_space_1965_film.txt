_**Space**_ (1965) is an [underground film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Underground_film "Underground film") directed by [Andy Warhol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andy_Warhol "Andy Warhol"), written by [Ronald Tavel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ronald_Tavel "Ronald Tavel"), and starring [Edie Sedgwick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edie_Sedgwick "Edie Sedgwick"), [Gino Piserchio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gino_Piserchio "Gino Piserchio"), [Dorothy Dean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dorothy_Dean "Dorothy Dean"), Ed Hennessey, singer-songwriter [Eric Andersen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_Andersen "Eric Andersen"), and Norman Levine. Unlike many of Warhol's other films made at [The Factory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Factory "The Factory"), this film involved a moving camera, moving around the actors as they stood still.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_(1965_film)#cite_note-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_(1965_film)#cite_note-2)

Plot
----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Space_(1965_film)&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Plot")]

The film features a melange of casual talking, food fights, and folk singing. The film includes Eric Andersen with his guitar, singing his lines, and leading Edie Sedgwick and her friends in unscripted sing-alongs of popular songs including "[Puff, the Magic Dragon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puff,_the_Magic_Dragon "Puff, the Magic Dragon")" and "[The Battle Hymn of the Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_Hymn_of_the_Republic "Battle Hymn of the Republic")".
