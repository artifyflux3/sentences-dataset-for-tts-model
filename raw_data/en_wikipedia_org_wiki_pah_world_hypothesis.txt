Title: PAH world hypothesis

URL Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis

Published Time: 2006-02-15T16:44:22Z

Markdown Content:
From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The **PAH world hypothesis** is a speculative [hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypothesis "Hypothesis") that proposes that [polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon") (PAHs), known to be abundant in the [universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universe "Universe"),[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-SP-20051018-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-AJ-20051010-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-NASA-20110413-3) including in comets,[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-NASA-20150210-4) and assumed to be abundant in the [primordial soup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primordial_soup#%22Primordial_soup%22_theory "Primordial soup") of the early [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth"), played a major role in the [origin of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origin_of_life "Origin of life") by mediating the synthesis of [RNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNA "RNA") molecules, leading into the [RNA world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNA_world "RNA world"). However, as yet, the hypothesis is untested.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-snp1-5)

A PAH stack assembling

[![Image 2](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Miller-Urey_experiment-en.svg/250px-Miller-Urey_experiment-en.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Miller-Urey_experiment-en.svg)

The [Miller–Urey experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miller%E2%80%93Urey_experiment "Miller–Urey experiment") showed that organic compounds can be readily produced under the presumed conditions of the early [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth"). 

The 1952 [Miller–Urey experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miller%E2%80%93Urey_experiment "Miller–Urey experiment") demonstrated the synthesis of [organic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compounds "Organic compounds"), such as [amino acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acids "Amino acids"), [formaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formaldehyde "Formaldehyde") and [sugars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sugars "Sugars"), from the original [inorganic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inorganic "Inorganic") precursors the researchers presumed to have been present in the [primordial soup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primordial_soup "Primordial soup") (but is no longer considered likely). This experiment inspired many others. In 1961, [Joan Oró](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joan_Or%C3%B3 "Joan Oró") found that the [nucleotide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleotide "Nucleotide") base [adenine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adenine "Adenine") could be made from [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide") (HCN) and [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia") in a water solution.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-6) Experiments conducted later showed that the other [RNA and DNA nucleobases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleobase "Nucleobase") could be obtained through simulated prebiotic chemistry with a [reducing atmosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reducing_atmosphere "Reducing atmosphere").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-7)

The [RNA world hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNA_world_hypothesis "RNA world hypothesis") shows how RNA can become its own [catalyst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalyst "Catalyst") (a [ribozyme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ribozyme "Ribozyme")). In between there are some missing steps such as how the first [RNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNA "RNA") molecules could be formed. The PAH world hypothesis was proposed by Simon Nicholas Platts in May 2004 to try to fill in this missing step.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-astro-8) A more thoroughly elaborated idea has been published by [Ehrenfreund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pascale_Ehrenfreund "Pascale Ehrenfreund")_et al._[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-9)

Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons
--------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=PAH_world_hypothesis&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons")]

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/PIA22568-CatsPawNebula-Spitzer-20181023.jpg/330px-PIA22568-CatsPawNebula-Spitzer-20181023.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:PIA22568-CatsPawNebula-Spitzer-20181023.jpg)

The [Cat's Paw Nebula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cat%27s_Paw_Nebula "Cat's Paw Nebula") lies inside the [Milky Way Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way_Galaxy "Milky Way Galaxy") and is located in the [constellation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constellation "Constellation")[Scorpius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scorpius "Scorpius").

Green areas show regions where radiation from hot stars collided with large molecules and small dust grains called "[polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon")" (PAHs), causing them to [fluoresce](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluoresce "Fluoresce").

([Spitzer Space Telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spitzer_Space_Telescope "Spitzer Space Telescope"), 2018)

Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons are the most common and abundant of the known polyatomic molecules in the visible [universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universe "Universe"), and are considered a likely constituent of the [primordial sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primordial_sea "Primordial sea").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-SP-20051018-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-AJ-20051010-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-NASA-20110413-3) PAHs, along with [fullerenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullerenes "Fullerenes") (or "[buckyballs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buckyball_(molecule) "Buckyball (molecule)")"), have been recently detected in nebulae.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-10)[Buckminsterfullerene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buckminsterfullerene "Buckminsterfullerene") (C 60) has been identified in the [interstellar medium spaces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-SA-20190429-11)[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-AJL-20190422-12) ([Fullerenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullerenes "Fullerenes") are also implicated in the origin of life; according to astronomer Letizia Stanghellini, "It's possible that buckyballs from outer space provided seeds for life on Earth."[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-13)) PAHs, subjected to [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") (ISM) conditions, are transformed, through [hydrogenation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogenation "Hydrogenation"), [oxygenation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygenate "Oxygenate") and [hydroxylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroxylation "Hydroxylation"), to more complex [organics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") — "a step along the path toward [amino acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acids "Amino acids") and [nucleotides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleotides "Nucleotides"), the raw materials of [proteins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proteins "Proteins") and [DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA "DNA"), respectively".[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-Space-20120920-14)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-AJL-20120901-15) Further, as a result of these transformations, the PAHs lose their [spectroscopic signature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectroscopy "Spectroscopy") which could be one of the reasons "for the lack of PAH detection in [interstellar ice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_ice "Interstellar ice")[grains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_dust#Dust_grain_formation "Cosmic dust"), particularly the outer regions of cold, dense clouds or the upper molecular layers of [protoplanetary disks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protoplanetary_disks "Protoplanetary disks")."[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-Space-20120920-14)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-AJL-20120901-15)

In 2013, [polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbons "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons") were detected in the [upper atmosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_atmosphere "Upper atmosphere") of [Titan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titan_(moon) "Titan (moon)"), the largest [moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_satellite "Natural satellite") of the [planet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planet "Planet")[Saturn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturn "Saturn").[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-IAA-20130606-16)

Low-temperature chemical pathways from simple [organic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") to complex PAHs have been demonstrated. Such chemical pathways may help explain the presence of PAHs in the low-temperature atmosphere of [Saturn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturn "Saturn")'s moon [Titan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titan_(moon) "Titan (moon)"), and may be significant pathways, in terms of the PAH world hypothesis, in producing precursors to biochemicals related to life as we know it.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-DG-20181008-17)[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-NA-20181008-18)

PAHs are not normally very soluble in sea water, but when subject to ionizing radiation such as solar [UV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UV "UV") light, the outer [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen") atoms can be stripped off and replaced with a [hydroxyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroxyl "Hydroxyl") group, rendering the PAHs far more soluble.

These modified PAHs are [amphiphilic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amphiphilic "Amphiphilic"), which means that they have parts that are both [hydrophilic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrophilic "Hydrophilic") and [hydrophobic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrophobic "Hydrophobic"). When in solution, they assemble in [discotic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columnar_phase "Columnar phase")[mesogenic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesogen "Mesogen") ([liquid crystal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_crystal "Liquid crystal")) stacks which, like [lipids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipids "Lipids"), tend to organize with their hydrophobic parts protected.

In 2014, [NASA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASA "NASA") announced a database[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-NASA-20140221-19) for tracking [polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbons "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons") (PAHs) in the [universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universe "Universe"). More than 20% of the [carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon") in the universe may be associated with PAHs,[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-NASA-20140221-19)[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-STD-20140224-20) possible [starting materials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precursor_(chemistry) "Precursor (chemistry)") for the [formation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abiogenesis#PAH_world_hypothesis "Abiogenesis") of [life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life#Extraterrestrial "Life"). PAHs seem to have been formed as early as a couple of billion years after the [Big Bang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Bang "Big Bang"), are abundant in the universe,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-SP-20051018-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-AJ-20051010-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-NASA-20110413-3) and are associated with [new stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_formation "Star formation") and [exoplanets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exoplanets "Exoplanets").[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-NASA-20140221-19)

### Attachment of nucleobases to PAH scaffolding

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=PAH_world_hypothesis&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Attachment of nucleobases to PAH scaffolding")]

In the self-ordering PAH stack, the separation between adjacent rings is 0.34 nm. This is the same separation found between adjacent [nucleotides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleotide "Nucleotide") of [RNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNA "RNA") and [DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA "DNA"). Smaller molecules will naturally attach themselves to the PAH rings. However PAH rings, while forming, tend to swivel around on one another, which will tend to dislodge attached compounds that would collide with those attached to those above and below. Therefore, it encourages preferential attachment of flat molecules such as [pyrimidine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrimidine "Pyrimidine") and [purine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purine "Purine")[nucleobases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleobases "Nucleobases"), the key constituents (and information carriers) of RNA and DNA. These bases are similarly amphiphilic and so also tend to line up in similar stacks.

### Attachment of oligomeric backbone

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=PAH_world_hypothesis&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Attachment of oligomeric backbone")]

According to the hypothesis, once the nucleobases are attached (via [hydrogen bonds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_bonds "Hydrogen bonds")) to the PAH scaffolding, the inter-base distance would select for "linker" molecules of a specific size, such as small [formaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formaldehyde "Formaldehyde") ([methanal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanal "Methanal")) [oligomers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oligomer "Oligomer"), also taken from the prebiotic "soup", which will bind (via [covalent bonds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covalent_bonds "Covalent bonds")) to the nucleobases as well as each other to add a flexible structural backbone.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-snp1-5)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-astro-8)

### Detachment of the RNA-like strands

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=PAH_world_hypothesis&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Detachment of the RNA-like strands")]

A subsequent transient drop in the ambient [pH](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PH "PH") (increase in acidity), for example as a result of a [volcanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcano "Volcano") discharge of acidic gases such as [sulfur dioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfur_dioxide "Sulfur dioxide") or [carbon dioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide "Carbon dioxide"), would allow the bases to break off from their PAH scaffolding, forming RNA-like molecules (with the formaldehyde backbone instead of the ribose-phosphate backbone used by "modern" RNA, but the same 0.34 nm pitch).[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-snp1-5)

### Formation of ribozyme-like structures

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=PAH_world_hypothesis&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Formation of ribozyme-like structures")]

The hypothesis further speculates that once long RNA-like single strands are detached from the PAH stacks, and after ambient pH levels became less acidic, they would tend to fold back on themselves, with [complementary sequences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Base_pair "Base pair") of nucleobases preferentially seeking out each other and forming [hydrogen bonds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_bonds "Hydrogen bonds"), creating stable, at least partially double-stranded RNA-like structures, similar to [ribozymes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ribozymes "Ribozymes"). The formaldehyde oligomers would eventually be replaced with more stable ribose-phosphate molecules for the backbone material, resulting in a starting milestone for the [RNA world hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNA_world_hypothesis "RNA world hypothesis"), which speculates about further evolutionary developments from that point.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-snp1-5)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-astro-8)[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAH_world_hypothesis#cite_note-21)
