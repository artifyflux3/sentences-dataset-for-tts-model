Oedipodium is the only genus of moss in the family Oedipodiaceae . It contains the single species Oedipodium griffithianum , the gouty-moss [ 5 ] or Griffith's oedipodium moss .

[ 6 ] This species is distributed in cooler climates of Eurasia , as well as from Alaska , Washington state , British Columbia , Yukon , Greenland , Newfoundland , Tierra del Fuego and the Falkland Islands .

[ 7 ] The relationship of Oedipodium to other mosses has been much debated.  Previously, the taxon has been included with the Funariales or the Splachnales .  However, characteristics of the protonemata and asexual propagation, along with molecular evidence, point to a closer relationship with the Tetraphidaceae .
