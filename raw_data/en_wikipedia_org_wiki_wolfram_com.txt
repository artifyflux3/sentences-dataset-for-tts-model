**Wolfram Research, Inc.** ( [_WUUL-frəm_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key")) is an American [multinational company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multinational_corporation "Multinational corporation") that creates computational technology. Wolfram's flagship product is the [technical computing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_computing "Technical computing") program [Wolfram Mathematica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram_Mathematica "Wolfram Mathematica"), first released on June 23, 1988. Other products include [WolframAlpha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WolframAlpha "WolframAlpha"), [Wolfram System Modeler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram_SystemModeler "Wolfram SystemModeler"), Wolfram Workbench,[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-2)[gridMathematica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GridMathematica "GridMathematica"), Wolfram Finance Platform,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-3)[webMathematica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WebMathematica "WebMathematica"), the Wolfram Cloud, and the Wolfram Programming Lab.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-4) Wolfram Research founder [Stephen Wolfram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_Wolfram "Stephen Wolfram") is the [CEO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CEO "CEO"). The company is headquartered in [Champaign, Illinois](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Champaign,_Illinois "Champaign, Illinois"), United States.

The company launched [Wolfram Alpha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram_Alpha "Wolfram Alpha"), an [answer engine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Answer_engine "Answer engine") on May 16, 2009. It brings a new approach to knowledge generation and acquisition that involves large amounts of [curated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_curation "Digital curation") computable data in addition to semantic indexing of text.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-5)

Wolfram Research acquired MathCore Engineering AB on March 30, 2011.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-7)

On July 21, 2011, Wolfram Research launched the [Computable Document Format](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computable_Document_Format "Computable Document Format") (CDF). CDF is an electronic document format[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-8) designed to allow easy authoring[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-9) of dynamically generated interactive content.

In June 2014, Wolfram Research officially introduced the [Wolfram Language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram_Language "Wolfram Language") as a new general multi-paradigm programming language.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-10) It is the primary programming language used in [Mathematica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematica "Mathematica").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-11)

On April 15, 2020, Wolfram Research received $5,575,000 to help pay its employees during the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic") as part of the [U.S. government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States "Federal government of the United States")'s [Paycheck Protection Program](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paycheck_Protection_Program "Paycheck Protection Program") administered by the [Small Business Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Small_Business_Administration "Small Business Administration"). The loan was forgiven.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-12)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-13)

Products and resources
----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wolfram_Research&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Products and resources")]

Mathematica began as a software program for doing mathematics by computer, and has evolved to cover all domains of [technical computing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_computing "Technical computing") software, with features for [neural networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_neural_network "Artificial neural network"), [machine learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_learning "Machine learning"), [image processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Image_processing "Image processing"), [geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometry "Geometry"), [data science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_science "Data science"), and [visualizations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_visualization "Scientific visualization"). Central to Mathematica's mission is its ability to perform symbolic computation, for example, the ability to solve indefinite integrals symbolically. Mathematica includes a [notebook interface](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Notebook_interface "Notebook interface") and can produce slides for presentations. Mathematica is available in a desktop version, a grid computing version, and a cloud version.

Wolfram Alpha is a free online service that [answers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Question_answering "Question answering") factual queries directly by computing the answer from externally sourced curated data, rather than providing a list of documents or web pages that might contain the answer as a search engine might. Users submit queries and computation requests via a text field and Wolfram Alpha then computes answers and relevant visualizations. In 2012, a paid "Pro" level with additional features was released.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-14)

In 2016, Wolfram Alpha Enterprise, a business-focused analytics tool, was launched. The program combines data supplied by a corporation with the algorithms from Wolfram Alpha to answer questions related to that corporation.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-15)

### Wolfram System Modeler

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wolfram_Research&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Wolfram System Modeler")]

Wolfram System Modeler is a platform for engineering as well as life-science modeling and simulation based on the [Modelica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modelica "Modelica") language. It provides an interactive graphical modeling and simulation environment and a customizable set of component libraries. The primary interface, [ModelCenter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ModelCenter "ModelCenter"), is an interactive graphical environment including a customizable set of component libraries. The software also provides a tight integration with Mathematica. Users can develop, simulate, document, and analyze their models within Mathematica notebooks.

Wolfram Research publishes several free websites including the [MathWorld](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MathWorld "MathWorld") and **ScienceWorld** encyclopedias. ScienceWorld, which launched in 2002, is divided into sites on chemistry, physics, astronomy and scientific biography.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-16) In 2005, the physics site was deemed a "valuable resource" by _[American Scientist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American\_Scientist "American Scientist")_ magazine.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-17) However, by 2009, the astronomy site was said to suffer from outdated information, incomplete articles and [link rot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Link_rot "Link rot").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-18)

[The Wolfram Demonstrations Project](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram_Demonstrations_Project "Wolfram Demonstrations Project") is a collaborative site hosting interactive technical demonstrations powered by a free Mathematica Player runtime.

Wolfram Research publishes _The Mathematica Journal_.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-19) Wolfram has also published several books via Wolfram Media, Wolfram's publishing arm.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-20)[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-21) In addition, they have experimented with electronic textbook creation.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-22)

Wolfram Research served as the mathematical consultant for the [CBS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CBS "CBS") television series _[Numb3rs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numb3rs "Numb3rs")_, a show about the mathematical aspects of crime-solving.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfram.com#cite_note-23)
