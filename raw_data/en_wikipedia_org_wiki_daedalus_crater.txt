Closeup of the interior from [Apollo 11](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_11 "Apollo 11")

**Daedalus** is a prominent [crater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Impact_crater "Impact crater") located near the center of the [far side of the Moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Far_side_of_the_Moon "Far side of the Moon"). The inner wall is [terraced](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/terrace "wiktionary:terrace"), and there is a cluster of central peaks on the relatively flat floor. Because of its location (shielded from radio emissions from the [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth")), it has been proposed as the site of a future giant [radio telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_telescope "Radio telescope"), which would be scooped out of the crater itself, much like the [Arecibo radio telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arecibo_radio_telescope "Arecibo radio telescope"), but on a vastly larger scale.

The crater is named after [Daedalus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daedalus "Daedalus") of [Greek myth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_myth "Greek myth"). It is pictured in famous photographs taken by the [Apollo 11](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_11 "Apollo 11") astronauts. In contemporary sources it was called _Crater 308_ (this was a temporary [IAU](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union "International Astronomical Union") designation that preceded the establishment of far-side lunar nomenclature).[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daedalus_(crater)#cite_note-1)

Nearby craters of note include [Icarus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Icarus_(crater) "Icarus (crater)") to the east and [Racah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Racah_(crater) "Racah (crater)") to the south. Less than a crater diameter to the north-northeast is [Lipskiy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipskiy_(crater) "Lipskiy (crater)").
