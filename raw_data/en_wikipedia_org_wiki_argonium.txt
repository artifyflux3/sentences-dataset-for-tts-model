**Argonium** (also called the **argon hydride cation**, the **hydridoargon(1+) ion**, or **protonated argon**; chemical formula ArH+) is a [cation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cation "Cation") combining a [proton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydron_(chemistry) "Hydron (chemistry)") and an [argon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argon "Argon") atom. It can be made in an [electric discharge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_discharge "Electric discharge"), and was the first [noble gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noble_gas "Noble gas") molecular ion to be found in interstellar space.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-3)

Argonium is [isoelectronic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isoelectronic "Isoelectronic") with [hydrogen chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_chloride "Hydrogen chloride"). Its dipole moment is 2.18[D](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debye "Debye") for the ground state. The [binding energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Binding_energy "Binding energy") is 369 kJ mol−1[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5) (3.9 eV[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-barlow-6)). This is smaller than that of [H+ 3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trihydrogen_cation "Trihydrogen cation") and many other [protonated species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Onium_compounds "Onium compounds"), but more than that of [H+ 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dihydrogen_cation "Dihydrogen cation").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5)

But the reverse reaction happens:

*   Ar + H+

2 → ArH+ + H.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5)
*   Ar + H+

3 → *ArH+ + H 2[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5)

Ar+ + H 2 has a cross section of 10−18 m 2 for low energy. It has a steep drop off for energies over 100 eV[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Phelps-9) Ar + H+

2 has a cross sectional area of 6×10−19 m 2 for low energy H+

2, but when the energy exceeds 10 eV yield reduces, and more Ar+ and H 2 is produced instead.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Phelps-9)

Ar + H+

3 has a maximum yield of ArH+ for energies between 0.75 and 1 eV with a cross section of 5×10−20 m 2. 0.6 eV is needed to make the reaction proceed forward. Over 4 eV more Ar+ and H starts to appear.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Phelps-9)

Argonium is also produced from Ar+ ions produced by [cosmic rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_rays "Cosmic rays") and X-rays from neutral argon.

*   Ar+ + H 2 → *ArH+ + H[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5) 1.49 eV[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-barlow-6)

When ArH+ encounters an electron, dissociative recombination can occur, but it is extremely slow for lower energy electrons, allowing ArH+ to survive for a much longer time than many other similar protonated cations.

*   ArH+ + e− → Ar + H[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5)

Because [ionisation potential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionisation_potential "Ionisation potential") of argon atoms is lower than that of the hydrogen molecule (in contrast to that of helium or neon), the argon ion reacts with molecular hydrogen, but for helium and neon ions, they will strip an electron from a hydrogen molecule.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5)

*   Ar+ + H 2 → ArH+ + H[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5)
*   Ne+ + H 2 → Ne + H+ + H (dissociative charge transfer)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5)
*   He+ + H 2 → He + H+ + H[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5)

Artificial ArH+ made from earthly argon contains mostly the isotope 40 Ar rather than the cosmically abundant 36 Ar. Artificially it is made by an electric discharge through an argon–hydrogen mixture.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-brown-10) Brault and Davis were the first to detect the molecule using infrared spectroscopy to observe vibration–rotation bands.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-brown-10)

The UV spectrum has two absorption points resulting in the ion breaking up. The 11.2 eV conversion to the B 1 Π state has a low dipole and so does not absorb much. A 15.8 eV to a repulsive A 1 Σ+ state is at a shorter wavelength than the [Lyman limit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lyman_limit "Lyman limit"), and so there are very few photons around to do this in space.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5)

ArH+ occurs in interstellar diffuse [atomic hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic_hydrogen "Atomic hydrogen") gas. For argonium to form, the fraction of [molecular hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_hydrogen "Molecular hydrogen") H 2 must be in the range 0.0001 to 0.001. Different molecular ions form in correlation with different concentrations of H 2. Argonium is detected by its absorption lines at 617.525 GHz (_J_ = 1→0), and 1234.602 GHz (_J_ = 2→1). These lines are due to the isotopolog 36 Ar 1 H+ undergoing rotational transitions. The lines have been detected in the direction of the galactic centre [SgrB2](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=SgrB2&action=edit&redlink=1 "SgrB2 (page does not exist)")(M) and SgrB2(N), G34.26+0.15, [W31C](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=W31C&action=edit&redlink=1 "W31C (page does not exist)") (G10.62−0.39), [W49(N)](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=W49(N)&action=edit&redlink=1 "W49(N) (page does not exist)"), and [W51e](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=W51e&action=edit&redlink=1 "W51e (page does not exist)"), however where absorption lines are observed, argonium is not likely to be in the microwave source, but instead in the gas in front of it.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5) Emission lines are found in the [Crab Nebula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crab_Nebula "Crab Nebula").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-barlow-6)

In the Crab Nebula ArH+ occurs in several spots revealed by emission lines. The strongest place is in the Southern Filament. This is also the place with the strongest concentration of Ar+ and Ar 2+ ions.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-barlow-6) The column density of ArH+ in the Crab Nebula is between 10 12 and 10 13 atoms per square centimeter.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-barlow-6) Possible the energy required to excite the ions so that then can emit comes from collisions with electrons or hydrogen molecules.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-barlow-6) Towards the Milky Way centre the column density of ArH+ is around 2×10 13 cm−2.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Schilke-5)

Two isotopologs of argonium 36 ArH+ and 38 ArH+ are known to be in a distant unnamed galaxy with a redshift of _z_ = 0.88582 (7.5 billion light years away) which is on the line of sight to the [blazar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blazar "Blazar")[PKS 1830−211](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PKS_1830%E2%88%92211 "PKS 1830−211").

Electron neutralization and destruction of argonium outcompletes the formation rate in space if the H 2 concentration is below 1 in 10−4.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-Neufeld-11)

Using the McMath solar Fourier transform spectrometer at [Kitt Peak National Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kitt_Peak_National_Observatory "Kitt Peak National Observatory"), James W. Brault and Sumner P. Davis observed ArH+ vibration-rotation infrared lines for the first time.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-12) J. W. C. Johns also observed the infrared spectrum.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-13)

Argon facilitates the reaction of [tritium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tritium "Tritium") (T 2) with double bonds in fatty acids by forming an ArT+ (tritium argonium) intermediate.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-14) When gold is [sputtered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sputtering "Sputtering") with an argon-hydrogen plasma, the actual displacement of gold is done by ArH+.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonium#cite_note-15)
