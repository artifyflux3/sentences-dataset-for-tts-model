In physics, the Laplace expansion of potentials that are directly proportional to the inverse of the distance ( 1 / r {\displaystyle 1/r} ), such as Newton's gravitational potential or Coulomb's electrostatic potential , expresses them in terms of the spherical Legendre polynomials. In quantum mechanical calculations on atoms the expansion is used in the evaluation of integrals of the inter-electronic repulsion.
