Freeman and Company is an imprint of Macmillan Higher Education, a division of Macmillan Publishers . Macmillan publishes monographs and textbooks for the sciences under the imprint.

History [ edit ] The company, W. H. Freeman and Company Publishing [ 1 ] was founded in 1946 by William H. Freeman Jr.

, [ 2 ] who had been a salesman and editor at Macmillan Publishing .

Freeman later founded Freeman, Cooper and Company in San Francisco.

[ 3 ] [ 4 ] [ 5 ] [ 6 ] Works [ edit ] Titles published by W. H. Freeman include James Watson 's Recombinant DNA (1983), William J. Kaufmann III's The Universe (1985), Jon Rogawski's Calculus (2007), and Peter Atkins ’ Physical Chemistry (2014).
