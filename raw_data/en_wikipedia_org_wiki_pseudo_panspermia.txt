From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This article is about the mainstream hypothesis that the organic building-blocks of life originated in space. For the fringe theory that life permeates the universe and gave rise to life on Earth, see [Panspermia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panspermia "Panspermia").

**Pseudo-panspermia** (sometimes called **soft panspermia**, **molecular panspermia** or **quasi-panspermia**) is a well-supported hypothesis for a stage in the [origin of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origin_of_life "Origin of life"). The theory first asserts that many of the small [organic molecules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") used for life originated in [space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space "Outer space") (for example, being incorporated in the [solar nebula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nebular_hypothesis "Nebular hypothesis"), from which the [planets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planet "Planet") condensed). It continues that these organic molecules were distributed to planetary surfaces, where life then emerged on [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth") and [perhaps on other planets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life "Extraterrestrial life"). Pseudo-panspermia differs from the [fringe theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudoscience "Pseudoscience") of [panspermia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panspermia "Panspermia"), which asserts that life arrived on Earth from distant planets.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-May2019-1)

[![Image 1](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Origin_of_life_stages.svg/500px-Origin_of_life_stages.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Origin_of_life_stages.svg)

Some stages in the [origin of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origin_of_life "Origin of life") are well-understood, such as the [habitable Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_habitability "Planetary habitability") and the abiotic synthesis of simple molecules, whether in space or on Earth. Later stages remain more speculative.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-Walker_Packard_Cody_2017-2)

Theories of the origin of life have been recorded since the 5th century BC, when the Greek philosopher [Anaxagoras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anaxagoras "Anaxagoras") proposed an initial version of panspermia: life arrived on earth from the heavens.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-3) In modern times, full panspermia has little support amongst [mainstream scientists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainstream_science "Mainstream science").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-May2019-1) Pseudo-panspermia, in which molecules are formed and transported through space is, however, well-supported.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-Walker_Packard_Cody_2017-2)

Interstellar molecules are formed by chemical reactions within very sparse interstellar or circumstellar clouds of dust and gas. Usually this occurs when a molecule becomes [ionised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ion "Ion"), often as the result of an interaction with [cosmic rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_ray "Cosmic ray"). This positively charged molecule then draws in a nearby reactant by electrostatic attraction of the neutral molecule's electrons. Molecules can also be generated by reactions between neutral atoms and molecules, although this process is generally slower.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-pnas103-4) The dust plays a critical role of shielding the molecules from the ionizing effect of ultraviolet radiation emitted by stars.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-brown_pais95-5) The [Murchison meteorite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murchison_meteorite "Murchison meteorite") contains the organic molecules [uracil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uracil "Uracil") and [xanthine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xanthine "Xanthine"),[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-Murch_base-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-7) which must therefore already have been present in the early Solar System, where they could have played a role in the origin of life.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-Martins_Botta_Fogel_2008-8)

[Nitriles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrile "Nitrile"), key molecular precursors of the [RNA World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNA_world "RNA world") scenario, are among the most abundant chemical families in the universe and have been found in molecular clouds in the center of the Milky Way, protostars of different masses, meteorites and comets, and also in the atmosphere of Titan, the largest moon of Saturn.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-10)

Planetary distribution of organic molecules
-------------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pseudo-panspermia&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Planetary distribution of organic molecules")]

Organic molecules can then be distributed to planets including Earth both when the planets formed and later. If the materials from which planets formed contained organic molecules, and were not destroyed by heat or other processes, then these would be available for abiogenesis on those planets.

Later distribution is by means of bodies such as [comets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comet "Comet") and [asteroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroid "Asteroid"). These may fall to the planetary surface as [meteorites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meteorite "Meteorite"), releasing any molecules they are carrying as they vaporise on impact or later as they erode.

Studies of rock and dust from asteroid [Bennu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/101955_Bennu "101955 Bennu") delivered to Earth by NASA’s [OSIRIS-REx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OSIRIS-REx "OSIRIS-REx") have revealed molecules that, on Earth, are key to life, as well as a history of saltwater.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudo-panspermia#cite_note-27)
