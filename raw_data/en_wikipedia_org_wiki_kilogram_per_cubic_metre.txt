Imperial and US Customary units 0.06242796 lb/cu ft CGS units 0.001 g/cm 3 MTS units 0.001 t/m 3 The kilogram per cubic metre (symbol: kg·m −3 , or kg/m 3 ) is the unit of density in the International System of Units (SI). It is defined by dividing the SI unit of mass , the kilogram , by the SI unit of volume , the cubic metre .

[ 1 ] Conversions [ edit ] 1 kg/m 3 = 1 g/ L (exactly) [ 2 ] 1 kg/m 3 = 0.001 g/cm 3 (exactly) [ 3 ] 1 kg/m 3 ≈ 0.06243 lb / ft 3 (approximately) 1 kg/m 3 ≈ 0.1335 oz / US gal (approximately) 1 kg/m 3 ≈ 0.1604 oz/ imp gal (approximately) 1 g/cm 3 = 1000 kg/m 3 (exactly) 1 lb/ft 3 ≈ 16.02 kg/m 3 (approximately) 1 oz/(US gal) ≈ 7.489 kg/m 3 (approximately) 1 oz/(imp gal) ≈ 6.236 kg/m 3 (approximately) Relation to other measures [ edit ] The density of water is about 1000 kg/m 3 or 1 g/cm 3 , because the size of the gram was originally based on the mass of a cubic centimetre of water.

In chemistry , g/cm 3 is more commonly used.
