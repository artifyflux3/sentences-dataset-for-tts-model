[ 1 ] Johnson was educated at Trinity College, Dublin .

[ 2 ] He was Rector of Dungannon until 1756 when he was appointed Dean of Tuam .

[ 3 ] In 1759 he became Bishop of Cloyne , a post he held until his death in January 1767.
