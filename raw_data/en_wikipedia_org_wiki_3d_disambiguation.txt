### Relating to three-dimensionality

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=3D&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Relating to three-dimensionality")]

*   [3D computer graphics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_computer_graphics "3D computer graphics"), computer graphics that use a three-dimensional representation of geometric data
*   [3D display](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_display "3D display"), a type of information display that conveys depth to the viewer
*   [3D film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_film "3D film"), a motion picture that gives the illusion of three-dimensional perception
*   [3D modeling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_modeling "3D modeling"), developing a representation of any three-dimensional surface or object
*   [3D printing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_printing "3D printing"), making a three-dimensional solid object of a shape from a digital model
*   [3D television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_television "3D television"), television that conveys depth perception to the viewer
*   [3D projection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_projection "3D projection")
*   [3D rendering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_rendering "3D rendering")
*   [3D scanning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_scanning "3D scanning"), making a digital representation of three-dimensional objects
*   [3D video game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_game_graphics#3D "Video game graphics")
*   [Stereoscopy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stereoscopy "Stereoscopy"), any technique capable of recording three-dimensional visual information or creating the illusion of depth in an image
*   [Three-dimensional space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-dimensional_space "Three-dimensional space")
