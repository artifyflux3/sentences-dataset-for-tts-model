_**Physical Review Letters**_ (_**PRL**_), established in 1958, is a [peer-reviewed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peer_review "Peer review"), [scientific journal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_journal "Scientific journal") that is published 52 times per year by the [American Physical Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Physical_Society "American Physical Society"). The journal is considered one of the most prestigious in the field of [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics"). Over a quarter of [Physics Nobel Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics "Nobel Prize in Physics")-winning papers between 1995 and 2017 were published in it.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phys._Rev._Lett.#cite_note-1)

_PRL_ is published both online and as a print journal. Its focus is on short articles ("letters") intended for quick publication. The Lead Editor is Hugues Chat√©. The Managing Editor is Robert Garisto.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phys._Rev._Lett.#cite_note-about-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phys._Rev._Lett.#cite_note-editor-3)

History
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Physical_Review_Letters&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

The journal was created in 1958. [Samuel Goudsmit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samuel_Goudsmit "Samuel Goudsmit"), who was then the editor of _[Physical Review](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical\_Review "Physical Review")_, the [American Physical Society's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Physical_Society "American Physical Society") flagship journal, organized and published _Letters to the Editor of Physical Review_ into a new standalone journal _,_ which became _Physical Review Letters_. It was the first journal intended for the rapid publication of short articles, a format that eventually became popular in many other fields.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phys._Rev._Lett.#cite_note-4)

Scope
-----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Physical_Review_Letters&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Scope")]

_PRL_ covers all areas of physics. The journal is divided into the following sections:[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phys._Rev._Lett.#cite_note-about-2)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phys._Rev._Lett.#cite_note-toc2009-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phys._Rev._Lett.#cite_note-toc2010-9)

*   [General Physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics"): [Statistical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_mechanics "Statistical mechanics") and [Quantum mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_mechanics "Quantum mechanics"), [Quantum information](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_information "Quantum information"), etc.
*   [Gravitation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitation "Gravitation") and [astrophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrophysics "Astrophysics")
*   [Elementary particles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elementary_particle "Elementary particle") and [fields](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_field_theory "Quantum field theory")
*   [Nuclear physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_physics "Nuclear physics")
*   [Atomic, molecular, and optical physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic,_molecular,_and_optical_physics "Atomic, molecular, and optical physics")
*   [Nonlinear dynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonlinear_dynamics "Nonlinear dynamics"), [Fluid dynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluid_dynamics "Fluid dynamics"), [Classical optics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_optics "Classical optics"), etc.
*   [Plasma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasma_physics "Plasma physics") and [beam physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laser "Laser")
*   [Condensed matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Condensed_matter "Condensed matter"): Structure, etc.
*   Condensed Matter: [Semiconductor-Electronic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semiconductor "Semiconductor") properties, etc.
*   [Polymer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymer_physics "Polymer physics"), [Soft matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soft_matter "Soft matter"), Biological, and [Interdisciplinary physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics")

A section before the table of contents highlights a small number of particularly notable articles in each edition.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phys._Rev._Lett.#cite_note-toc2009-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phys._Rev._Lett.#cite_note-toc2010-9)
