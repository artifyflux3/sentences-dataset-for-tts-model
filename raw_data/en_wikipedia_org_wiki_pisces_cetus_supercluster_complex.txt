From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Galaxy filament containing the Virgo Supercluster

The **Pisces–Cetus Supercluster Complex** is a [galaxy filament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_filament "Galaxy filament"). It includes the [Laniakea Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laniakea_Supercluster "Laniakea Supercluster") which contains the [Virgo Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgo_Supercluster "Virgo Supercluster") lobe which in turn contains the [Local Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group "Local Group"), the [galaxy cluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_cluster "Galaxy cluster") that includes the [Milky Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way "Milky Way").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pisces%E2%80%93Cetus_Supercluster_Complex#cite_note-tully86-1) This filament is adjacent to the [Perseus–Pegasus Filament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perseus%E2%80%93Pegasus_Filament "Perseus–Pegasus Filament").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pisces%E2%80%93Cetus_Supercluster_Complex#cite_note-2) Astronomer [R. Brent Tully](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R._Brent_Tully "R. Brent Tully") of the [University of Hawaii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Hawaii "University of Hawaii")'s Institute of Astronomy identified the Complex in 1987.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pisces%E2%80%93Cetus_Supercluster_Complex#cite_note-3)

Extent
------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pisces%E2%80%93Cetus_Supercluster_Complex&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Extent")]

The Pisces–Cetus Supercluster Complex is estimated to be about 1.0 billion [light-years](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light-year "Light-year") (Gly) long and 150 million light years (Mly) wide. It is one of the [largest structures known](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_cosmic_structures "List of largest cosmic structures") in the [observable universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Observable_universe "Observable universe"), but is exceeded by the [Sloan Great Wall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sloan_Great_Wall "Sloan Great Wall") (1.3 Gly), [Clowes–Campusano LQG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clowes%E2%80%93Campusano_LQG "Clowes–Campusano LQG") (2.0 Gly), [U1.11](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U1.11 "U1.11") LQG (2.5 Gly), [Huge-LQG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huge-LQG "Huge-LQG") (4.0 Gly), and [Hercules–Corona Borealis Great Wall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hercules%E2%80%93Corona_Borealis_Great_Wall "Hercules–Corona Borealis Great Wall") (10 Gly), respectively.

Sixty clusters comprise the complex, which is estimated to have a total mass of 10 18[M☉](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_mass "Solar mass").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pisces%E2%80%93Cetus_Supercluster_Complex#cite_note-tully87-4) According to the discoverer, the complex is composed of 5 parts:

1.   The Pisces–Cetus Supercluster
2.   The Perseus–Pegasus chain, including the [Perseus–Pisces Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perseus%E2%80%93Pisces_Supercluster "Perseus–Pisces Supercluster")
3.   The Pegasus–Pisces chain
4.   The Sculptor region, including the [Sculptor Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sculptor_Supercluster "Sculptor Supercluster") and [Hercules Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hercules_Supercluster "Hercules Supercluster")
5.   The [Laniakea Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laniakea_Supercluster "Laniakea Supercluster"), which contains our [Virgo Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgo_Supercluster "Virgo Supercluster") (Local Supercluster) as well as the [Hydra–Centaurus Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydra%E2%80%93Centaurus_Supercluster "Hydra–Centaurus Supercluster").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pisces%E2%80%93Cetus_Supercluster_Complex#cite_note-tully87-4)

With its mass of 10 15[M☉](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_mass "Solar mass"), our [Virgo Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgo_Supercluster "Virgo Supercluster") accounts only for 0.1 percent of the total mass of the complex.

The complex was named after the Pisces–Cetus Superclusters, which are its richest superclusters.

Image
-----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Pisces%E2%80%93Cetus_Supercluster_Complex&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Image")]

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Superclusters_atlasoftheuniverse.gif/575px-Superclusters_atlasoftheuniverse.gif)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Superclusters_atlasoftheuniverse.gif)

The Pisces–Cetus Supercluster Complex is the very long chain of galaxies from the Pisces–Cetus Superclusters, Sculptor Superclusters, the Perseus–Pisces Supercluster and the [Laniakea Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laniakea_Supercluster "Laniakea Supercluster") (here shown as the former Virgo, Hydra, and Centaurus Superclusters)
