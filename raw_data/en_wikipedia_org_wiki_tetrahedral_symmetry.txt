A regular [tetrahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetrahedron "Tetrahedron"), an example of a solid with full tetrahedral symmetry

A regular [tetrahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetrahedron "Tetrahedron") has 12 rotational (or [orientation-preserving](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orientation-preserving "Orientation-preserving")) symmetries, and a [symmetry order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetry_order "Symmetry order") of 24 including transformations that combine a reflection and a rotation.

The group of all (not necessarily orientation preserving) symmetries is isomorphic to the group S 4, the [symmetric group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetric_group "Symmetric group") of permutations of four objects, since there is exactly one such symmetry for each permutation of the vertices of the tetrahedron. The set of orientation-preserving symmetries forms a group referred to as the [alternating subgroup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternating_group "Alternating group") A 4 of S 4.

**Chiral** and **full** (or **achiral tetrahedral symmetry** and **pyritohedral symmetry**) are [discrete point symmetries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point_groups_in_three_dimensions "Point groups in three dimensions") (or equivalently, [symmetries on the sphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_spherical_symmetry_groups "List of spherical symmetry groups")). They are among the [crystallographic point groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystal_system#Overview_of_point_groups_by_crystal_system "Crystal system") of the [cubic crystal system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cubic_(crystal_system) "Cubic (crystal system)").

Chiral tetrahedral symmetry
---------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tetrahedral_symmetry&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Chiral tetrahedral symmetry")]

_**T**_, **332**, [3,3]+, or **23**, of order 12 – **chiral** or **rotational tetrahedral symmetry**. There are three orthogonal 2-fold rotation axes, like chiral [dihedral symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dihedral_symmetry_in_three_dimensions "Dihedral symmetry in three dimensions")_D_ 2 or 222, with in addition four 3-fold axes, centered _between_ the three orthogonal directions. This group is [isomorphic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomorphic "Isomorphic") to _A_ 4, the [alternating group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternating_group "Alternating group") on 4 elements; in fact it is the group of [even permutations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Even_permutation "Even permutation") of the four 3-fold axes: e, (123), (132), (124), (142), (134), (143), (234), (243), (12)(34), (13)(24), (14)(23).

The [conjugacy classes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conjugacy_class "Conjugacy class") of T are:

*   identity
*   4 × rotation by 120° clockwise (seen from a vertex): (234), (143), (412), (321)
*   4 × rotation by 120° counterclockwise (ditto)
*   3 × rotation by 180°

The rotations by 180°, together with the identity, form a [normal subgroup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Normal_subgroup "Normal subgroup") of type Dih 2, with [quotient group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quotient_group "Quotient group") of type Z 3. The three elements of the latter are the identity, "clockwise rotation", and "anti-clockwise rotation", corresponding to permutations of the three orthogonal 2-fold axes, preserving orientation.

A 4 is the smallest group demonstrating that the converse of [Lagrange's theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange%27s_theorem_(group_theory) "Lagrange's theorem (group theory)") is not true in general: given a finite group _G_ and a divisor _d_ of |_G_|, there does not necessarily exist a subgroup of _G_ with order _d_: the group _G_ = A 4 has no subgroup of order 6. Although it is a property for the abstract group in general, it is clear from the isometry group of chiral tetrahedral symmetry: because of the chirality the subgroup would have to be C 6 or D 3, but neither applies.

Achiral tetrahedral symmetry
----------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tetrahedral_symmetry&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Achiral tetrahedral symmetry")]

[![Image 103](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Sphere_symmetry_group_td.png/250px-Sphere_symmetry_group_td.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Sphere_symmetry_group_td.png)

The full tetrahedral group T d with fundamental domain

**T d**, ***332**, [3,3] or 4 3m, of order 24 – **achiral** or **full tetrahedral symmetry**, also known as the (2,3,3) [triangle group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triangle_group "Triangle group"). This group has the same rotation axes as T, but with six mirror planes, each through two 3-fold axes. The 2-fold axes are now S 4 (4) axes. T d and O are isomorphic as abstract groups: they both correspond to S 4, the [symmetric group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetric_group "Symmetric group") on 4 objects. T d is the union of T and the set obtained by combining each element of O \ T with inversion. See also [the isometries of the regular tetrahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetrahedron#Isometries_of_irregular_tetrahedra "Tetrahedron").

The [conjugacy classes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conjugacy_class "Conjugacy class") of T d are:

*   identity
*   8 × rotation by 120° (C 3)
*   3 × rotation by 180° (C 2)
*   6 × reflection in a plane through two rotation axes (C s)
*   6 × rotoreflection by 90° (S 4)

Pyritohedral symmetry
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tetrahedral_symmetry&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Pyritohedral symmetry")]

[![Image 155](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Sphere_symmetry_group_th.png/250px-Sphere_symmetry_group_th.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Sphere_symmetry_group_th.png)

The pyritohedral group T h with fundamental domain

[![Image 156](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Volleyball_seams_diagram.png/250px-Volleyball_seams_diagram.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Volleyball_seams_diagram.png)

The seams of a [volleyball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volleyball_(ball) "Volleyball (ball)") have pyritohedral symmetry

**T h**, **3*2**, [4,3+] or m 3, of order 24 – **pyritohedral symmetry**.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetrahedral_symmetry#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKocaAl-MukhainiKocaAl_Qanobi2016-1) This group has the same rotation axes as T, with mirror planes through two of the orthogonal directions. The 3-fold axes are now [S 6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dihedral_symmetry "Dihedral symmetry") (3) axes, and there is a central inversion symmetry. T h is isomorphic to T × Z 2: every element of T h is either an element of T, or one combined with inversion. Apart from these two normal subgroups, there is also a normal subgroup D 2h (that of a [cuboid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuboid "Cuboid")), of type Dih 2 × Z 2 = Z 2 × Z 2 × Z 2. It is the direct product of the normal subgroup of T (see above) with [C _i_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point_reflection "Point reflection"). The [quotient group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quotient_group "Quotient group") is the same as above: of type Z 3. The three elements of the latter are the identity, "clockwise rotation", and "anti-clockwise rotation", corresponding to permutations of the three orthogonal 2-fold axes, preserving orientation.

It is the symmetry of a cube with on each face a line segment dividing the face into two equal rectangles, such that the line segments of adjacent faces do not meet at the edge. The symmetries correspond to the even permutations of the body diagonals and the same combined with inversion. It is also the symmetry of a [pyritohedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyritohedron "Pyritohedron"), which is extremely similar to the cube described, with each rectangle replaced by a pentagon with one symmetry axis and 4 equal sides and 1 different side (the one corresponding to the line segment dividing the cube's face); i.e., the cube's faces bulge out at the dividing line and become narrower there. It is a subgroup of the full [icosahedral symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Icosahedral_symmetry "Icosahedral symmetry") group (as isometry group, not just as abstract group), with 4 of the 10 3-fold axes.

The conjugacy classes of T h include those of T, with the two classes of 4 combined, and each with inversion:

*   identity
*   8 × rotation by 120° (C 3)
*   3 × rotation by 180° (C 2)
*   inversion (S 2)
*   8 × rotoreflection by 60° (S 6)
*   3 × reflection in a plane (C s)
