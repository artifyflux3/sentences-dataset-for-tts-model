From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

In [Euclidean space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_space "Euclidean space"), the **distance from a point to a plane** is the distance between a given point and its [orthogonal projection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthogonal_projection "Orthogonal projection") on the plane, the [perpendicular distance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perpendicular_distance "Perpendicular distance") to the nearest point on the plane.

It can be found starting with a [change of variables](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Change_of_variables "Change of variables") that moves the origin to coincide with the given point then finding the point on the shifted [plane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plane_(mathematics) "Plane (mathematics)")![Image 1: {\displaystyle ax+by+cz=d}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9fb9dc02a498445bc0cbd7e904fc0621b7a8c3a6) that is closest to the [origin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origin_(mathematics) "Origin (mathematics)"). The resulting point has [Cartesian coordinates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartesian_coordinate "Cartesian coordinate")![Image 2: {\displaystyle (x,y,z)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/22a8c93372e8f8b6e24d523bd5545aed3430baf4):

![Image 3: {\displaystyle \displaystyle x={\frac {ad}{a^{2}+b^{2}+c^{2}}},\quad \quad \displaystyle y={\frac {bd}{a^{2}+b^{2}+c^{2}}},\quad \quad \displaystyle z={\frac {cd}{a^{2}+b^{2}+c^{2}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9fd07ee731176f760eca051bc002648b1407d428).
The distance between the origin and the point ![Image 4: {\displaystyle (x,y,z)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/22a8c93372e8f8b6e24d523bd5545aed3430baf4) is ![Image 5: {\displaystyle {\sqrt {x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b322c45940093f06277e00b876bcd1afde77171c).

Converting general problem to distance-from-origin problem
----------------------------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Distance_from_a_point_to_a_plane&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Converting general problem to distance-from-origin problem")]

Suppose we wish to find the nearest point on a plane to the point (![Image 6: {\displaystyle X_{0},Y_{0},Z_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/e99b24f72a7e8162d202093c07d0c39859e48d12)), where the plane is given by ![Image 7: {\displaystyle aX+bY+cZ=D}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/523c3794ce264ce733e5854f8165c02e35209310). We define ![Image 8: {\displaystyle x=X-X_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a3dbc94f7907012afb39d791f709db1ca01e33e9), ![Image 9: {\displaystyle y=Y-Y_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6413c5827ad9c8159ab95893c0b856783e30c384), ![Image 10: {\displaystyle z=Z-Z_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6e8df8177e65280cf52a648012a6046f89bc418b), and ![Image 11: {\displaystyle d=D-aX_{0}-bY_{0}-cZ_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/57eae44a8fd10cd0eb9b8a198d7fe20184229716), to obtain ![Image 12: {\displaystyle ax+by+cz=d}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9fb9dc02a498445bc0cbd7e904fc0621b7a8c3a6) as the plane expressed in terms of the transformed variables. Now the problem has become one of finding the nearest point on this plane to the origin, and its distance from the origin. The point on the plane in terms of the original coordinates can be found from this point using the above relationships between ![Image 13: {\displaystyle x}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/87f9e315fd7e2ba406057a97300593c4802b53e4) and ![Image 14: {\displaystyle X}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/68baa052181f707c662844a465bfeeb135e82bab), between ![Image 15: {\displaystyle y}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b8a6208ec717213d4317e666f1ae872e00620a0d) and ![Image 16: {\displaystyle Y}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/961d67d6b454b4df2301ac571808a3538b3a6d3f), and between ![Image 17: {\displaystyle z}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/bf368e72c009decd9b6686ee84a375632e11de98) and ![Image 18: {\displaystyle Z}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1cc6b75e09a8aa3f04d8584b11db534f88fb56bd); the distance in terms of the original coordinates is the same as the distance in terms of the revised coordinates.

Restatement using linear algebra
--------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Distance_from_a_point_to_a_plane&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Restatement using linear algebra")]

The formula for the closest point to the origin may be expressed more succinctly using notation from [linear algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_algebra "Linear algebra"). The expression ![Image 19: {\displaystyle ax+by+cz}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f9a4e9ed7e6d7e1f764878d63901181ed52da54c) in the definition of a plane is a [dot product](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dot_product "Dot product")![Image 20: {\displaystyle (a,b,c)\cdot (x,y,z)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/edf40ef9091ec796e6f512ab581dd56d71fbb4b0), and the expression ![Image 21: {\displaystyle a^{2}+b^{2}+c^{2}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/369ff47d70ac6773d7b73b8576fedfaa8ecc4951) appearing in the solution is the squared [norm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norm_(mathematics) "Norm (mathematics)")![Image 22: {\displaystyle |(a,b,c)|^{2}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/331cfac83775b4043ec102607a1a705187baa983). Thus, if ![Image 23: {\displaystyle \mathbf {v} =(a,b,c)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/74f65a9a446f191e81ccb7725f274dff0a41ee57) is a given vector, the plane may be described as the set of vectors ![Image 24: {\displaystyle \mathbf {w} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/20795664b5b048744a2fd88977851104cc5816f8) for which ![Image 25: {\displaystyle \mathbf {v} \cdot \mathbf {w} =d}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/392616774acedb836012e7810eeeb83da0e53b37) and the closest point on this plane to the origin is the vector

![Image 26: {\displaystyle \mathbf {p} ={\frac {\mathbf {v} d}{|\mathbf {v} |^{2}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f5185b46ed27fb134246466215188c4dbade2550).[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distance_from_a_point_to_a_plane#cite_note-sb-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distance_from_a_point_to_a_plane#cite_note-sa-2)
The [Euclidean distance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_distance "Euclidean distance") from the origin to the plane is the norm of this point,

![Image 27: {\displaystyle {\frac {|d|}{|\mathbf {v} |}}={\frac {|d|}{\sqrt {a^{2}+b^{2}+c^{2}}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/efad05c7103ed9ef09c0672f4ae9d96c7236e0c5).

Why this is the closest point
-----------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Distance_from_a_point_to_a_plane&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Why this is the closest point")]

In either the coordinate or vector formulations, one may verify that the given point lies on the given plane by plugging the point into the equation of the plane.

To see that it is the closest point to the origin on the plane, observe that ![Image 28: {\displaystyle \mathbf {p} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/dd73e3862cb92b016721b8c492eadb4e8a577527) is a scalar multiple of the vector ![Image 29: {\displaystyle \mathbf {v} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/35c1866e359fbfd2e0f606c725ba5cc37a5195d6) defining the plane, and is therefore orthogonal to the plane. Thus, if ![Image 30: {\displaystyle \mathbf {q} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7be005a326b7ac3fe4c24bca391369f44c4c2876) is any point on the plane other than ![Image 31: {\displaystyle \mathbf {p} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/dd73e3862cb92b016721b8c492eadb4e8a577527) itself, then the line segments from the origin to ![Image 32: {\displaystyle \mathbf {p} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/dd73e3862cb92b016721b8c492eadb4e8a577527) and from ![Image 33: {\displaystyle \mathbf {p} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/dd73e3862cb92b016721b8c492eadb4e8a577527) to ![Image 34: {\displaystyle \mathbf {q} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7be005a326b7ac3fe4c24bca391369f44c4c2876) form a [right triangle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Right_triangle "Right triangle"), and by the [Pythagorean theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pythagorean_theorem "Pythagorean theorem") the distance from the origin to ![Image 35: {\displaystyle q}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/06809d64fa7c817ffc7e323f85997f783dbdf71d) is

![Image 36: {\displaystyle {\sqrt {|\mathbf {p} |^{2}+|\mathbf {p} -\mathbf {q} |^{2}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5417978c91e176c3d90399b9e96db47e576d1138).
Since ![Image 37: {\displaystyle |\mathbf {p} -\mathbf {q} |^{2}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4ae42ab1f7d92358a4d6612c545e5bf2f54975b8) must be a positive number, this distance is greater than ![Image 38: {\displaystyle |\mathbf {p} |}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6412e280b338030b7a5201182d0051f1b5b8bb25), the distance from the origin to ![Image 39: {\displaystyle \mathbf {p} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/dd73e3862cb92b016721b8c492eadb4e8a577527).[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distance_from_a_point_to_a_plane#cite_note-sa-2)

Alternatively, it is possible to rewrite the equation of the plane using dot products with ![Image 40: {\displaystyle \mathbf {p} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/dd73e3862cb92b016721b8c492eadb4e8a577527) in place of the original dot product with ![Image 41: {\displaystyle \mathbf {v} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/35c1866e359fbfd2e0f606c725ba5cc37a5195d6) (because these two vectors are scalar multiples of each other) after which the fact that ![Image 42: {\displaystyle \mathbf {p} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/dd73e3862cb92b016721b8c492eadb4e8a577527) is the closest point becomes an immediate consequence of the [Cauchy–Schwarz inequality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cauchy%E2%80%93Schwarz_inequality "Cauchy–Schwarz inequality").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distance_from_a_point_to_a_plane#cite_note-sb-1)
