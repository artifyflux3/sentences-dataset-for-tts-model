The **zodiacal light** (also called **false dawn**[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-5) when seen [before sunrise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dawn "Dawn")) is a faint glow of [diffuse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photon_diffusion "Photon diffusion")[sunlight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunlight "Sunlight")[scattered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light_scattering_by_particles "Light scattering by particles") by [interplanetary dust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interplanetary_dust "Interplanetary dust"). Brighter around the [Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun "Sun"), it appears in a particularly dark [night sky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Night_sky "Night sky") to extend from the Sun's direction in a roughly triangular shape along the [zodiac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiac "Zodiac"), and appears with less intensity and visibility along the whole [ecliptic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecliptic "Ecliptic") as the **zodiacal band**.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-6) Zodiacal light spans the entire sky and contributes[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-7) to the natural light of a clear and moonless night sky. A related phenomenon is _[gegenschein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gegenschein "Gegenschein")_ (or _counterglow_), sunlight [backscattered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backscatter "Backscatter") from the interplanetary dust, which appears [directly opposite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antisolar_point "Antisolar point") to the Sun as a faint but slightly brighter oval glow.

Zodiacal light contributes[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-8) to the natural light of the sky, though since zodiacal light is very faint, it is often outshone and rendered invisible by [moonlight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moonlight "Moonlight") or [light pollution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light_pollution "Light pollution").

The interplanetary dust in the [Solar System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_System "Solar System") forms a thick, pancake-shaped cloud called the zodiacal cloud which straddles the ecliptic plane. The [particle sizes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Particle_size "Particle size") range from 10 to 300 [micrometres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micrometre "Micrometre"), implying masses from one [nanogram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanogram "Nanogram") to tens of [micrograms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microgram "Microgram").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-McCracken1967-10)

The _[Pioneer 10](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pioneer\_10 "Pioneer 10")_ and [_Helios_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helios_Dust_Instrumentation "Helios Dust Instrumentation") spacecraft observations in the 1970s revealed zodiacal light to be scattered by the [interplanetary dust cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interplanetary_dust_cloud "Interplanetary dust cloud") in the Solar System.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-HannerEA1976-11)[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-12) Analysis of images of impact debris from the _[Juno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juno\_(spacecraft) "Juno (spacecraft)")_ spacecraft shows that the distribution of the dust extends from Earth's orbit to the 4:1 [orbital resonance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbital_resonance "Orbital resonance") with Jupiter at 2.06 AU, and suggests that the dust is from Mars.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-13) However, no other dedicated dust instrumentation on _[Pioneer 10](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pioneer\_10 "Pioneer 10")_, _[Pioneer 11](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pioneer\_11 "Pioneer 11")_, _[Galileo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galileo\_(spacecraft) "Galileo (spacecraft)")_, _[Ulysses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ulysses\_(spacecraft) "Ulysses (spacecraft)")_, nor _[Cassini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cassini\_(spacecraft) "Cassini (spacecraft)")_ found an indication that Mars is a significant source of dust besides comets and asteroids.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-HannerEA1976-11)[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-14)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-15)[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-SojaEA2019-16)

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Submillimeter_Array_Night.jpg/250px-Submillimeter_Array_Night.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Submillimeter_Array_Night.jpg)

Zodiacal light seen behind the [Submillimeter Array](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Submillimeter_Array "Submillimeter Array") from the summit of [Mauna Kea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauna_Kea "Mauna Kea")

In the mid-latitudes, the zodiacal light is best observed in the western sky in the spring after the evening twilight has completely disappeared, or in the eastern sky in the autumn just before the morning twilight appears. The zodiacal light appears as a column, brighter at the horizon and tilted at the angle of the ecliptic. The light scattered from extremely small dust particles is strongly [forward scattering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forward_scattering "Forward scattering"), although the zodiacal light actually extends all the way around the sky, hence it is brightest when observing at a small angle with the Sun. This is why it is most clearly visible near sunrise or sunset when the Sun is blocked, but the dust particles nearest the line of sight to the Sun are not. The dust band that causes the zodiacal light is uniform across the whole ecliptic.

The dust further from the ecliptic is almost undetectable except when viewed at a small angle with the Sun. Thus it is possible to see more of the width at small angles toward the Sun, and it appears wider near the horizon, closer to the Sun under the horizon.

[![Image 4](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Moonlight_and_Zodiacal_Light_Over_La_Silla_Observatory.jpg/250px-Moonlight_and_Zodiacal_Light_Over_La_Silla_Observatory.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Moonlight_and_Zodiacal_Light_Over_La_Silla_Observatory.jpg)

Moonlight and zodiacal light over [La Silla Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Silla_Observatory "La Silla Observatory")[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-17)

The source of the dust has been long debated. Until recently, it was thought that the dust originated from the tails of active [comets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comet "Comet") and from collisions between [asteroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroid "Asteroid") in the [asteroid belt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroid_belt "Asteroid belt").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-18) Many of our meteor showers have no known active comet parent bodies. Over 85 percent of the dust is attributed to occasional fragmentations of Jupiter-family comets that are nearly [dormant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extinct_comet "Extinct comet").[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-19) Jupiter-family comets have orbital periods of less than 20 years[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-20) and are considered dormant when not actively outgassing, but may do so in the future.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-21) The first fully dynamical model of the zodiacal cloud demonstrated that only if the dust was released in orbits that approach Jupiter, is it stirred up enough to explain the thickness of the zodiacal dust cloud. The dust in meteoroid streams is much larger, 300 to 10,000 [micrometres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micrometre "Micrometre") in diameter, and falls apart into smaller zodiacal dust grains over time.

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Romantic_Sunset_over_the_VLT.jpg/250px-Romantic_Sunset_over_the_VLT.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Romantic_Sunset_over_the_VLT.jpg)

Colorful center of the [Milky Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way "Milky Way") and the zodiacal light above the [Very Large Telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Very_Large_Telescope "Very Large Telescope")[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-22)

The [Poynting–Robertson effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poynting%E2%80%93Robertson_effect "Poynting–Robertson effect") forces the dust into more circular (but still elongated) orbits, while spiralling slowly into the Sun. Hence a continuous source of new particles is needed to maintain the zodiacal cloud. [Cometary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comet "Comet") dust and dust generated by collisions among the [asteroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroid "Asteroid") are believed to be mostly responsible for the maintenance of the dust cloud producing the zodiacal light and the [gegenschein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gegenschein "Gegenschein").

Particles can be reduced in size by collisions or by space weathering. When ground down to sizes less than 10 micrometres, the grains are removed from the inner Solar System by solar radiation pressure. The dust is then replenished by the infall from comets. Zodiacal dust around nearby stars is called [exozodiacal dust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exozodiacal_dust "Exozodiacal dust"); it is a potentially important source of noise in attempts to directly image [extrasolar planets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extrasolar_planets "Extrasolar planets"). It has been pointed out that this exozodiacal dust, or hot debris disks, can be an indicator of planets, as planets tend to scatter the comets to the inner Solar System.

In 2015, new results from the secondary ion dust spectrometer COSIMA on board the [_ESA/Rosetta_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosetta_(spacecraft) "Rosetta (spacecraft)") orbiter confirmed that the parent bodies of interplanetary dust are most probably Jupiter-family comets such as comet [67P/Churyumov–Gerasimenko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/67P/Churyumov%E2%80%93Gerasimenko "67P/Churyumov–Gerasimenko").[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-23) Data from the [Juno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juno_(spacecraft) "Juno (spacecraft)") mission indicate that the dust close to Earth has a local origin in the inner Solar System, best fitting the planet Mars as a source.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-24)

Zodiacal light is produced by sunlight reflecting off dust particles in the Solar System known as [cosmic dust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_dust "Cosmic dust"). Consequently, its [spectrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectroscopy "Spectroscopy") is the same as the [solar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun "Sun") spectrum. The material producing the zodiacal light is located in a lens-shaped volume of space centered on the sun and extending well out beyond the [orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbit "Orbit") of Earth. This material is known as the [interplanetary dust cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interplanetary_dust_cloud "Interplanetary dust cloud"). Since most of the material is located near the plane of the Solar System, the zodiacal light is seen along the [ecliptic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecliptic "Ecliptic"). The amount of material needed to produce the observed zodiacal light is quite small. If it were in the form of 1 mm particles, each with the same [albedo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albedo "Albedo") (reflecting power) as the [Moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon "Moon"), each particle would be 8 km from its neighbors. The [gegenschein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gegenschein "Gegenschein") may be caused by particles directly opposite the Sun as seen from Earth, which would be in full [phase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lunar_phase "Lunar phase").

According to Nesvorný and Jenniskens, when the dust grains are as small as about 150 micrometres in size, they will hit the Earth at an average speed of 14.5 km/s, many as slowly as 12 km/s. If so, they pointed out, this [comet dust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comet_dust "Comet dust") can survive entry in partially molten form, accounting for the unusual attributes of the [micrometeorites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micrometeorites "Micrometeorites") collected in Antarctica, which do not resemble the larger [meteorites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meteorites "Meteorites") known to originate from [asteroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroids "Asteroids"). In recent years, observations by a variety of spacecraft have shown significant structure in the zodiacal light including dust bands associated with debris from particular [asteroid families](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minor_planet "Minor planet") and several cometary trails.

Cultural significance
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Zodiacal_light&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Cultural significance")]

According to [Alexander von Humboldt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_von_Humboldt "Alexander von Humboldt")'s _[Kosmos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kosmos\_(Humboldt) "Kosmos (Humboldt)")_, Mesoamericans were aware of the zodiacal light before 1500.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-ley196104-25) It was perhaps first reported in print by [Joshua Childrey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joshua_Childrey "Joshua Childrey") in 1661. The phenomenon was investigated by the astronomer [Giovanni Domenico Cassini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giovanni_Domenico_Cassini "Giovanni Domenico Cassini") in 1683. According to some sources, he explained it by dust particles around the Sun.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-26)[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-27) Other sources state that it was first explained this way by [Nicolas Fatio de Duillier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolas_Fatio_de_Duillier "Nicolas Fatio de Duillier"), in 1684,[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-28) whom Cassini advised to study the zodiacal light.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-ley196104-25)

### Importance to Islam

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Zodiacal_light&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Importance to Islam")]

The [Islamic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam") prophet [Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad "Muhammad") described zodiacal light in reference to the timing of the [five daily prayers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salat "Salat"), calling it the "false dawn" (الفجر الكاذب _\_al-fajr al-kādhib\__). Muslim oral tradition preserves numerous sayings, or [hadith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadith "Hadith"), in which Muhammad describes the difference between the light of false dawn, appearing in the sky long after sunset, and the light of the first band of horizontal light at sunrise, the "true dawn" (الفجر الصادق _\_al-fajr al-sādiq\__). According to the vast majority of Muslim scholars, [navigation twilight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nautical_twilight "Nautical twilight") is considered the true dawn. Practitioners of Islam use Muhammad's descriptions of zodiacal light to avoid errors in determining the timing of fasting and daily prayers.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-29)[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-30)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-31)

In 2007, [Brian May](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_May "Brian May"), lead guitarist with the band [Queen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queen_(band) "Queen (band)"), completed his [thesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thesis "Thesis"), _A Survey of Radial Velocities in the Zodiacal Dust Cloud_, thirty-six years after abandoning it to pursue a career in music.[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-32) He was able to submit it only because of the minimal amount of research on the topic undertaken during the intervening years. May described the subject as being one that became "trendy" again in the 2000s.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiacal_light#cite_note-33)
