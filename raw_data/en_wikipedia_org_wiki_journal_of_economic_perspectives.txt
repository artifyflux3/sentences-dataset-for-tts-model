16474127 Links Journal homepage Online archive The Journal of Economic Perspectives ( JEP ) is an economic journal published by the American Economic Association . The journal was established in 1987.

[ 1 ] The JEP was founded by Joseph Stiglitz , Carl Shapiro , and Timothy Taylor .

[ 2 ] It is oriented around the twin goals of "providing perspective on current economic research, and explaining how economics provides perspective on questions of general interest." [ 2 ] [ 3 ] According to its editors its purpose is: to synthesize and integrate lessons learned from active lines of economic research; to provide economic analysis of public policy issues; to encourage cross-fertilization of ideas among the fields of thinking; to offer readers an accessible source for state-of-the-art economic thinking; to suggest directions for future research; to provide insights and readings for classroom use; and to address issues relating to the economics profession.

Its current editors are Heidi Williams and Jeffrey Kling , and its managing editor is Timothy Taylor .
