Chemical compound

**Imidogen** is an [inorganic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inorganic_compound "Inorganic compound") with the [chemical formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula") NH.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-2) Like other simple [radicals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_(chemistry) "Radical (chemistry)"), it is highly reactive and consequently short-lived except as a dilute gas. Its behavior depends on its [spin multiplicity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin_multiplicity "Spin multiplicity").

Production and properties
-------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Imidogen&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Production and properties")]

Imidogen can be generated by [electrical discharge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_discharge "Electrical discharge") in an atmosphere of [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-Campbell-3)

Imidogen has a large rotational splitting and a weak spin–spin interaction, therefore it will be less likely to undergo collision-induced [Zeeman transitions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zeeman_effect "Zeeman effect").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-Campbell-3) Ground-state imidogen can be magnetically trapped using [buffer-gas loading](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buffer-gas_loading "Buffer-gas loading") from a molecular beam.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-Campbell-3)

The [ground state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ground_state "Ground state") of imidogen is a [triplet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triplet_state "Triplet state"), with a [singlet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singlet_state "Singlet state") excited state only slightly higher in energy.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-Adams-4)

The first excited state (a 1 Δ) has a long lifetime as its relaxation to ground state (X 3 Σ−) is [spin-forbidden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin-forbidden_reactions "Spin-forbidden reactions").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-Hack-5) Imidogen undergoes collision-induced [intersystem crossing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intersystem_crossing "Intersystem crossing").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-Adams-4)

Reactivity
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Imidogen&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Reactivity")]

Ignoring hydrogen atoms, imidogen is [isoelectronic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isoelectronic "Isoelectronic") with [carbene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbene "Carbene") (CH 2) and oxygen (O) atoms, and it exhibits comparable reactivity.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-Hack-5) The first excited state can be detected by [laser-induced fluorescence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laser-induced_fluorescence "Laser-induced fluorescence") (LIF).[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-Hack-5) LIF methods allow for detection of depletion, production, and chemical products of NH. It reacts with [nitric oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitric_oxide "Nitric oxide") (NO):

NH + NO → N 2 + OH NH + NO → N 2 O + H
The former reaction is more favorable with a [Δ _H_ 0](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enthalpy "Enthalpy") of −408±2 kJ/mol compared to a Δ _H_ 0 of −147±2 kJ/mol for the latter reaction.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-6)

Nomenclature
------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Imidogen&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Nomenclature")]

The [trivial name](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trivial_name "Trivial name")_nitrene_ is the [preferred IUPAC name](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Preferred_IUPAC_name "Preferred IUPAC name"). The systematic names, _λ 1-azane_ and _hydridonitrogen_, valid [IUPAC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IUPAC "IUPAC") names, are constructed according to the substitutive and additive nomenclatures, respectively.

In appropriate contexts, imidogen can be viewed as ammonia with two hydrogen atoms removed, and as such, _azylidene_ may be used as a context-specific systematic name, according to substitutive nomenclature. By default, this name pays no regard to the radicality of the imidogen molecule. Although, in even more specific context, it can also name the non-radical state, whereas the diradical state is named _azanediyl_.

Astrochemistry
--------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Imidogen&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Astrochemistry")]

Interstellar NH was identified in the diffuse clouds toward [ζ Persei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%CE%96_Persei "Ζ Persei") and HD 27778 from high-resolution high-[signal-to-noise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Signal-to-noise "Signal-to-noise") spectra of the NH A 3 Π→X 3 Σ (0,0) absorption band near 3358 Å.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-apj376-7) A temperature of about 30 K (−243°C) favored an efficient production of CN from NH within the diffuse cloud.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-mnras260_2-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-apj209_1-9)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-apj376-7)

### Reactions relevant to astrochemistry

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Imidogen&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Reactions relevant to astrochemistry")]

Chemical reactions[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-10)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-11)| Reaction | Rate constant | Rate/[H 2]2 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| N + H− → NH + e− | 1×10−9 | 3.5×10−18 |
| NH 2 + O → NH + OH | 2.546×10−13 | 1.4×10−13 |
| NH+ 2 + e− → NH + H | 3.976×10−7 | 2.19×10−21 |
| NH+ 3 + e− → NH + H + H | 8.49×10−7 | 2.89×10−19 |
| NH + N → N 2 + H | 4.98×10−11 | 4.36×10−16 |
| NH + O → OH + N | 1.16×10−11 | 1.54×10−14 |
| NH + C+ → CN+ + H | 7.8×10−10 | 4.9×10−19 |
| NH + H+ 3 → NH+ 2 + H 2 | 1.3×10−9 | 3.18×10−19 |
| NH + H+ → NH+ + H | 2.1×10−9 | 4.05×10−20 |

Within diffuse clouds H− + N → NH + e− is a major formation mechanism. Near chemical equilibrium important NH formation mechanisms are recombinations of [NH+ 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrenium_ion "Nitrenium ion") and [NH+ 3](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Azaniumyl&action=edit&redlink=1 "Azaniumyl (page does not exist)") ions with electrons. Depending on the radiation field in the diffuse cloud, NH 2 can also contribute.

NH is destroyed in diffuse clouds by [photodissociation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation "Photodissociation") and [photoionization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photoionization "Photoionization"). In dense clouds NH is destroyed by reactions with atomic oxygen and nitrogen. O+ and N+ form OH and NH in diffuse clouds. NH is involved in creating N 2, OH, H, CN+, CH, N, NH+

2, NH+ for the interstellar medium.

NH has been reported in the diffuse [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") but not in dense [molecular clouds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-12) The purpose of detecting NH is often to get a better estimate of the rotational constants and vibrational levels of NH.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-13) It is also needed in order to confirm theoretical data which predicts N and NH abundances in [stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star "Star") which produce N and NH and other stars with leftover trace amounts of N and NH.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-14) Using current values for rotational constants and vibrations of NH as well as those of [OH](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroxyl_radical "Hydroxyl radical") and [CH](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylidyne "Methylidyne") permit studying the carbon, nitrogen and oxygen abundances without resorting to a full spectrum synthesis with a 3D model atmosphere.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imidogen#cite_note-15)
