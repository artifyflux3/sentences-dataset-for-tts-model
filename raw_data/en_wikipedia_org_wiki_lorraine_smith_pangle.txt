Her interests are ancient, early modern, and American political philosophy , ethics , the philosophy of education , and problems of justice and moral responsibility .

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ] [ 4 ] She has won fellowships from the Searle, Olin, and Earhart Foundations, the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada, and the National Endowment for the Humanities.
Pangle received her B.A. in history from Yale, a B.Ed. from the University of Toronto, and her PhD from University of Chicago in 1999. 
She is married to Thomas Pangle .
