The **Fauna** (pl.: **faunae** or **faunas**) is the whole of [animal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal "Animal")[life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life "Life") present in a particular region or time. The corresponding terms for [plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plant "Plant") and [fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungi "Fungi") are _[flora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora "Flora")_ and _[funga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funga "Funga")_, respectively. Flora, fauna, funga and other forms of life are collectively referred to as _[biota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biota\_(ecology) "Biota (ecology)")_. [Zoologists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoologist "Zoologist") and [paleontologists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleontologist "Paleontologist") use _fauna_ to refer to a typical collection of animals found in a specific time or place, e.g. the "[Sonoran Desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonoran_Desert "Sonoran Desert") fauna" or the "[Burgess Shale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burgess_Shale "Burgess Shale") fauna". [Paleontologists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleontology "Paleontology") sometimes refer to a sequence of [faunal stages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faunal_stage "Faunal stage"), which is a series of rocks all containing similar fossils. The study of animals of a particular region is called **faunistics**.

Etymology
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Etymology")]

_[Fauna](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/fauna "wikt:fauna")_ comes from the name [Fauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna_(deity) "Fauna (deity)"), a Roman goddess of earth and fertility, the Roman god [Faunus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faunus "Faunus"), and the related forest spirits called [Fauns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faun "Faun"). All three words are cognates of the name of the Greek god [Pan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pan_(god) "Pan (god)"), and _panis_ is the [Modern Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modern_Greek "Modern Greek") equivalent of fauna (πανίς or rather πανίδα). _Fauna_ is also the word for a book that catalogues the animals in such a manner. The term was first used by [Carl Linnaeus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus "Carl Linnaeus") from Sweden in the title of his 1745[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna#cite_note-1) work _Fauna Suecica_.

Subdivisions on the basis of region
-----------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Subdivisions on the basis of region")]

### Cryofauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Cryofauna")]

_Cryofauna_ refers to the animals that live in, or very close to, cold areas.

### Cryptofauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Cryptofauna")]

_Cryptofauna_ is the fauna that exists in protected or concealed [microhabitats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microhabitats "Microhabitats").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna#cite_note-2)

### Epifauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Epifauna")]

Epifauna, also called _epibenthos_, are aquatic animals that live on the bottom substratum as opposed to within it, that is, the [benthic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benthic "Benthic") fauna that live on top of the sediment surface at the seafloor.

### Infauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Infauna")]

This [time-lapse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time-lapse_photography "Time-lapse photography") movie shows images taken every hour during a two-week period. Worms, bacteria and fish are shown [disturbing the sediment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioturbation "Bioturbation") as they burrow and move through it.

_Infauna_ are [benthic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benthic_zone "Benthic zone") organisms that live within the bottom substratum of a water body, especially within the bottom-most oceanic sediments, the layer of small particles at the bottom of a body of water, rather than on its surface. [Bacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacteria "Bacteria") and [microalgae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microalgae "Microalgae") may also live in the interstices of bottom sediments. In general, infaunal animals become progressively smaller and less abundant with increasing water depth and distance from shore, whereas bacteria show more constancy in abundance, tending toward one million cells per milliliter of interstitial seawater.

Such creatures are found in the fossil record and include [lingulata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lingulata "Lingulata"), [trilobites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trilobite "Trilobite") and [worms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Worm "Worm"). They made burrows in the sediment as protection and may also have fed upon detritus or the mat of microbes which tended to grow on the surface of the sediment.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna#cite_note-3) Today, a variety of organisms live in and [disturb the sediment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioturbation "Bioturbation"). The deepest burrowers are the ghost shrimps (_[Thalassinidea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thalassinidea "Thalassinidea")_), which go as deep as 3 metres (10 ft) into the sediment at the bottom of the ocean.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna#cite_note-4)

### Limnofauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Limnofauna")]

_Limnofauna_ refers to the animals that live in fresh water.

### Macrofauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Macrofauna")]

_Macrofauna_ are [benthic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benthic "Benthic") or soil organisms which are retained on a 0.5 mm sieve. Studies in the deep sea define macrofauna as animals retained on a 0.3 mm sieve to account for the small size of many of the taxa.

### Megafauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Megafauna")]

Main article: [Megafauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megafauna "Megafauna")

[![Image 11](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Australisk_fauna%2C_Nordisk_familjebok.jpg/500px-Australisk_fauna%2C_Nordisk_familjebok.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Australisk_fauna,_Nordisk_familjebok.jpg)

[Papuan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna_of_New_Guinea "Fauna of New Guinea"), [Australian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australian_fauna "Australian fauna") and [New Zealand fauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna_of_New_Zealand "Fauna of New Zealand"). This image was likely first published in the first edition (1876–1899) of the _[Nordisk familjebok](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nordisk\_familjebok "Nordisk familjebok")_.

_Megafauna_ are large animals of any particular region or time. For example, [Australian megafauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australian_megafauna "Australian megafauna").

### Meiofauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Meiofauna")]

Main article: [Meiobenthos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meiobenthos "Meiobenthos")

_Meiofauna_ are small [benthic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benthic "Benthic")[invertebrates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invertebrate "Invertebrate") that live in both marine and freshwater [environments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecosystem "Ecosystem"). The term _meiofauna_ loosely defines a group of [organisms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organism "Organism") by their size, larger than microfauna but smaller than macrofauna, rather than a taxonomic grouping. One environment for meiofauna is between grains of damp sand (see [Mystacocarida](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mystacocarida "Mystacocarida")).

In practice these are metazoan animals that can pass unharmed through a 0.5–1 mm [mesh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesh "Mesh") but will be retained by a 30–45 μm mesh,[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna#cite_note-5) but the exact dimensions will vary from [researcher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Research "Research") to researcher. Whether an organism passes through a 1 mm mesh also depends upon whether it is alive or dead at the time of sorting.

### Mesofauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Mesofauna")]

Main article: [Soil mesofauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil_mesofauna "Soil mesofauna")

_Mesofauna_ are macroscopic soil animals such as [arthropods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthropod "Arthropod") or [nematodes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nematode "Nematode"). Mesofauna are extremely diverse; considering just the springtails ([Collembola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collembola "Collembola")), as of 1998, approximately 6,500 species had been identified.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna#cite_note-6)

### Microfauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=12 "Edit section: Microfauna")]

Main article: [Microfauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microfauna "Microfauna")

Microfauna are microscopic or very small animals (usually including [protozoans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protozoa "Protozoa") and very small animals such as [rotifers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotifer "Rotifer")). To qualify as part of the microfauna, an organism must exhibit animal-like characteristics, as opposed to [microflora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microflora "Microflora"), which are more plant-like.

### Stygofauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=13 "Edit section: Stygofauna")]

Main article: [Stygofauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stygofauna "Stygofauna")

Stygofauna is any fauna that lives in [groundwater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Groundwater "Groundwater") systems or aquifers, such as [caves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cave "Cave"), fissures and [vugs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vug "Vug"). Stygofauna and [troglofauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troglofauna "Troglofauna") are the two types of [subterranean fauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subterranean_fauna "Subterranean fauna") (based on life-history). Both are associated with subterranean environments – stygofauna is associated with water, and troglofauna with caves and spaces above the [water table](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_table "Water table"). Stygofauna can live within freshwater [aquifers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquifer "Aquifer") and within the [pore](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/pore "wiktionary:pore") spaces of [limestone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limestone "Limestone"), [calcrete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calcrete "Calcrete") or [laterite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laterite "Laterite"), whilst larger animals can be found in cave waters and wells. Stygofaunal animals, like troglofauna, are divided into three groups based on their life history - stygophiles, stygoxenes, and stygobites.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna#cite_note-Lopes-7)

### Troglofauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=14 "Edit section: Troglofauna")]

Main article: [Troglofauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troglofauna "Troglofauna")

[![Image 12](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/A_live_individual_of_Zospeum_tholussum.jpg/250px-A_live_individual_of_Zospeum_tholussum.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:A_live_individual_of_Zospeum_tholussum.jpg)

The microscopic cave snail _[Zospeum tholussum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zospeum\_tholussum "Zospeum tholussum")_, found at depths of 743 to 1,392 m (2,438 to 4,567 ft) in the [Lukina Jama–Trojama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lukina_jama "Lukina jama") cave system of [Croatia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croatia "Croatia"), is completely blind with a translucent shell

Troglofauna are small [cave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cave "Cave")-dwelling [animals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal "Animal") that have [adapted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adaptation "Adaptation") to their dark surroundings. Troglofauna and [stygofauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stygofauna "Stygofauna") are the two types of [subterranean fauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subterranean_fauna "Subterranean fauna") (based on life-history). Both are associated with subterranean environments – troglofauna is associated with caves and spaces above the water table and stygofauna with water. Troglofaunal species include [spiders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spider "Spider"), [insects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insect "Insect"), [myriapods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myriapods "Myriapods") and others. Some troglofauna lives permanently underground and cannot survive outside the cave environment. Troglofauna adaptations and characteristics include a heightened sense of hearing, touch and smell.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna#cite_note-Chapman-8) Loss of under-used senses is apparent in the lack of pigmentation as well as eyesight in most troglofauna. Troglofauna insects may exhibit a lack of [wings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insect_wing "Insect wing") and longer [appendages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Appendage "Appendage").

### Xenofauna

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fauna&action=edit&section=15 "Edit section: Xenofauna")]

Main article: [Extraterrestrial life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life "Extraterrestrial life")

_Xenofauna_, [theoretically](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drake_equation "Drake equation"), are [alien](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life "Extraterrestrial life") organisms that can be described as [animal analogues](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal "Animal"). While no alien life forms, animal-like or otherwise, are known definitively, the concept of alien life remains a subject of great interest in fields like [astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy "Astronomy"), [astrobiology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrobiology "Astrobiology"), [biochemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biochemistry "Biochemistry"), [evolutionary biology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evolutionary_biology "Evolutionary biology"), [science fiction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_fiction "Science fiction"), and [philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophy "Philosophy").
