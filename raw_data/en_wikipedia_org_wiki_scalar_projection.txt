[![Image 1](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Dot_Product.svg/300px-Dot_Product.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Dot_Product.svg)

If 0° ≤ _θ_ ≤ 90°, as in this case, the scalar projection of **a** on **b** coincides with the [length](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_norm "Euclidean norm") of the [vector projection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_projection "Vector projection").

[![Image 2](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Projection_and_rejection.svg/250px-Projection_and_rejection.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Projection_and_rejection.svg)

[Vector projection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_projection "Vector projection") of **a** on **b** (**a**1), and vector rejection of **a** from **b** (**a**2).

In [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics"), the **scalar projection** of a [vector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_(geometric) "Vector (geometric)")![Image 3: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1a957216653a9ee0d0133dcefd13fb75e36b8b9d) on (or onto) a vector ![Image 4: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} ,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a3654ae7aa05aad4aa7a3432e5d4169dfb0be078) also known as the **scalar resolute** of ![Image 5: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1a957216653a9ee0d0133dcefd13fb75e36b8b9d) in the [direction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direction_(geometry) "Direction (geometry)") of ![Image 6: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} ,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a3654ae7aa05aad4aa7a3432e5d4169dfb0be078) is given by:

![Image 7: {\displaystyle s=\left\|\mathbf {a} \right\|\cos \theta =\mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {\hat {b}} ,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f2d84231eaf0182d9a24047f0b9caa4d66d829a7)
where the operator ![Image 8: {\displaystyle \cdot }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ba2c023bad1bd39ed49080f729cbf26bc448c9ba) denotes a [dot product](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dot_product "Dot product"), ![Image 9: {\displaystyle {\hat {\mathbf {b} }}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b0bab98f69591338f95cb540eef6373f9963d176) is the [unit vector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unit_vector "Unit vector") in the direction of ![Image 10: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} ,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a3654ae7aa05aad4aa7a3432e5d4169dfb0be078)![Image 11: {\displaystyle \left\|\mathbf {a} \right\|}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ec49df2fa8265066b59f02d363bbc490ba023c23) is the [length](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_norm "Euclidean norm") of ![Image 12: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} ,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c0d76b79e18147442c016d8d5f7e8117aa922f0d) and ![Image 13: {\displaystyle \theta }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6e5ab2664b422d53eb0c7df3b87e1360d75ad9af) is the [angle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angle "Angle") between ![Image 14: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1a957216653a9ee0d0133dcefd13fb75e36b8b9d) and ![Image 15: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/13ebf4628a1adf07133a6009e4a78bdd990c6eb9).[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scalar_projection#cite_note-1)

The term **scalar component** refers sometimes to scalar projection, as, in [Cartesian coordinates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartesian_coordinates "Cartesian coordinates"), the [components of a vector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basis_(linear_algebra) "Basis (linear algebra)") are the scalar projections in the directions of the [coordinate axes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coordinate_axes "Coordinate axes").

The scalar projection is a [scalar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scalar_(mathematics) "Scalar (mathematics)"), equal to the [length](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_norm "Euclidean norm") of the [orthogonal projection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthogonal_projection "Orthogonal projection") of ![Image 16: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1a957216653a9ee0d0133dcefd13fb75e36b8b9d) on ![Image 17: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/13ebf4628a1adf07133a6009e4a78bdd990c6eb9), with a negative sign if the projection has an opposite direction with respect to ![Image 18: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/13ebf4628a1adf07133a6009e4a78bdd990c6eb9).

Multiplying the scalar projection of ![Image 19: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1a957216653a9ee0d0133dcefd13fb75e36b8b9d) on ![Image 20: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/13ebf4628a1adf07133a6009e4a78bdd990c6eb9) by ![Image 21: {\displaystyle \mathbf {\hat {b}} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/64cf170105d4377a6931a01b1d1e191f94318da7) converts it into the above-mentioned orthogonal projection, also called [vector projection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_projection "Vector projection") of ![Image 22: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1a957216653a9ee0d0133dcefd13fb75e36b8b9d) on ![Image 23: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/13ebf4628a1adf07133a6009e4a78bdd990c6eb9).

Definition based on angle _θ_
-----------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Scalar_projection&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Definition based on angle θ")]

If the [angle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angle "Angle")![Image 24: {\displaystyle \theta }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6e5ab2664b422d53eb0c7df3b87e1360d75ad9af) between ![Image 25: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1a957216653a9ee0d0133dcefd13fb75e36b8b9d) and ![Image 26: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/13ebf4628a1adf07133a6009e4a78bdd990c6eb9) is known, the scalar projection of ![Image 27: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1a957216653a9ee0d0133dcefd13fb75e36b8b9d) on ![Image 28: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/13ebf4628a1adf07133a6009e4a78bdd990c6eb9) can be computed using

![Image 29: {\displaystyle s=\left\|\mathbf {a} \right\|\cos \theta .}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/843db965135b5d9447589a149c1a892b9a787b26) (![Image 30: {\displaystyle s=\left\|\mathbf {a} _{1}\right\|}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/87441fba29e0dd9e2970a3f4676004d1bcea41d8) in the figure)
The formula above can be inverted to obtain the [angle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angle#Dot_product "Angle"), _θ_.

Definition in terms of a and b
------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Scalar_projection&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Definition in terms of a and b")]

When ![Image 31: {\displaystyle \theta }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6e5ab2664b422d53eb0c7df3b87e1360d75ad9af) is not known, the [cosine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosine "Cosine") of ![Image 32: {\displaystyle \theta }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6e5ab2664b422d53eb0c7df3b87e1360d75ad9af) can be computed in terms of ![Image 33: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1a957216653a9ee0d0133dcefd13fb75e36b8b9d) and ![Image 34: {\displaystyle \mathbf {b} ,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a3654ae7aa05aad4aa7a3432e5d4169dfb0be078) by the following property of the [dot product](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dot_product "Dot product")![Image 35: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {b} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/494aed3b5e94f1c0ee071debc707d2700c0e0390):

![Image 36: {\displaystyle {\frac {\mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {b} }{\left\|\mathbf {a} \right\|\left\|\mathbf {b} \right\|}}=\cos \theta }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/fd5016be1ec62745c882cd98e451546934826111)
By this property, the definition of the scalar projection ![Image 37: {\displaystyle s}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/01d131dfd7673938b947072a13a9744fe997e632) becomes:

![Image 38: {\displaystyle s=\left\|\mathbf {a} _{1}\right\|=\left\|\mathbf {a} \right\|\cos \theta =\left\|\mathbf {a} \right\|{\frac {\mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {b} }{\left\|\mathbf {a} \right\|\left\|\mathbf {b} \right\|}}={\frac {\mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {b} }{\left\|\mathbf {b} \right\|}}\,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/3c793da0f1adfe0fffc0f6f8284b602a01a3841d)
The scalar projection has a negative sign if ![Image 39: {\displaystyle 90^{\circ }<\theta \leq 180^{\circ }}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0ec19512a8be5e53bb53addea96a85bff5356d9b). It coincides with the [length](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_norm "Euclidean norm") of the corresponding [vector projection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_projection "Vector projection") if the angle is smaller than 90°. More exactly, if the vector projection is denoted ![Image 40: {\displaystyle \mathbf {a} _{1}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5220ec70acc24fa962b47d36289085080dbc3e79) and its length ![Image 41: {\displaystyle \left\|\mathbf {a} _{1}\right\|}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/2448e2fb1a4a2dacd945b9f21d15961871bfd31e):

![Image 42: {\displaystyle s=\left\|\mathbf {a} _{1}\right\|}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/87441fba29e0dd9e2970a3f4676004d1bcea41d8) if ![Image 43: {\displaystyle 0^{\circ }\leq \theta \leq 90^{\circ },}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/393fb2b356f40644c534ceed24ceb82d9604fa8f)![Image 44: {\displaystyle s=-\left\|\mathbf {a} _{1}\right\|}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6d5d8ce895ed89bca0c683cb344e5b70b2771e53) if ![Image 45: {\displaystyle 90^{\circ }<\theta \leq 180^{\circ }.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7ea37b3257af3990d071a6e1a1e4bf0174fe5ca9)
