This minimal coupling is in contrast to, for example, Pauli coupling , which includes the magnetic moment of an electron directly in the Lagrangian .

[ 1 ] Electrodynamics [ edit ] In electrodynamics , minimal coupling is adequate to account for all electromagnetic interactions. Higher moments of particles are consequences of minimal coupling and non-zero spin .

Inflation [ edit ] In studies of cosmological inflation , minimal coupling of a scalar field usually refers to minimal coupling to gravity. This means that the action for the inflaton field φ φ {\displaystyle \varphi } is not coupled to the scalar curvature . Its only coupling to gravity is the coupling to the Lorentz invariant measure g d 4 x {\displaystyle {\sqrt {g}}\,d^{4}x} constructed from the metric (in Planck units ): S = ∫ ∫ d 4 x g ( − − 1 2 R + 1 2 ∇ ∇ μ μ φ φ ∇ ∇ μ μ φ φ − − V ( φ φ ) ) {\displaystyle S=\int d^{4}x\,{\sqrt {g}}\,\left(-{\frac {1}{2}}R+{\frac {1}{2}}\nabla _{\mu }\varphi \nabla ^{\mu }\varphi -V(\varphi )\right)} where g := det g μ μ ν ν {\displaystyle g:=\det g_{\mu \nu }} , and utilizing the gauge covariant derivative .
