**Methylamine**, also known as **methanamine**, is an [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with a [formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula") of CH 3 NH 2. This colorless gas is a derivative of [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia"), but with one hydrogen atom being replaced by a [methyl group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_group "Methyl group"). It is the simplest primary [amine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amine "Amine").

Methylamine is sold as a solution in [methanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanol "Methanol"), [ethanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanol "Ethanol"), [tetrahydrofuran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetrahydrofuran "Tetrahydrofuran"), or [water](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water "Water"), or as the [anhydrous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anhydrous "Anhydrous") gas in pressurized metal containers. Industrially, methylamine is transported in its anhydrous form in pressurized railcars and tank trailers. It has a strong odor similar to rotten fish. Methylamine is used as a building block for the synthesis of numerous other commercially available compounds.

Industrial production
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methylamine&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Industrial production")]

Methylamine has been produced industrially since the 1920s (originally by [Commercial Solvents Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commercial_Solvents_Corporation "Commercial Solvents Corporation") for dehairing of animal skins).[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-:0-4) This was made possible by [Kazimierz Smoleński](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Kazimierz_Smole%C5%84ski&action=edit&redlink=1 "Kazimierz Smoleński (page does not exist)")[[pl](https://pl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kazimierz_Smole%C5%84ski "pl:Kazimierz Smoleński")] and his wife Eugenia who discovered amination of alcohols, including methanol, on alumina or kaolin catalyst after WWI, filed two patent applications in 1919[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-5) and published an article in 1921.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-:0-4)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-6)

It is now prepared commercially by the reaction of [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia") with [methanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanol "Methanol") in the presence of an [aluminosilicate catalyst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amorphous_silica-alumina "Amorphous silica-alumina"). [Dimethylamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethylamine "Dimethylamine") and [trimethylamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trimethylamine "Trimethylamine") are co-produced; the reaction kinetics and reactant ratios determine the ratio of the three products. The product most favored by the reaction kinetics is trimethylamine.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-:0-4)

CH 3 OH + NH 3 → CH 3 NH 2 + H 2 O
In this way, an estimated 115,000 tons were produced in 2005.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-Ullmann-7)

Methylamine was first prepared in 1849 by [Charles-Adolphe Wurtz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles-Adolphe_Wurtz "Charles-Adolphe Wurtz") via the hydrolysis of [methyl isocyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate "Methyl isocyanate") and related compounds.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-Ullmann-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-8) An example of this process includes the use of the [Hofmann rearrangement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hofmann_rearrangement "Hofmann rearrangement"), to yield methylamine from [acetamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide "Acetamide") and [bromine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bromine "Bromine").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-10)

In the laboratory, methylamine [hydrochloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrochloride "Hydrochloride") is readily prepared by various other methods. One method entails treating [formaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formaldehyde "Formaldehyde") with [ammonium chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonium_chloride "Ammonium chloride").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-11)

[NH 4]Cl + CH 2 O → [CH 2=NH 2]Cl + H 2 O[CH 2=NH 2]Cl + CH 2 O + H 2 O → [CH 3 NH 3]Cl + [HCOOH](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formic_acid "Formic acid")
The colorless hydrochloride salt can be converted to an amine by the addition of a strong base, such as [sodium hydroxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_hydroxide "Sodium hydroxide") (NaOH):

[CH 3 NH 3]Cl + NaOH → CH 3 NH 2 + NaCl + H 2 O
Another method entails reducing [nitromethane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitromethane "Nitromethane") with [zinc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zinc "Zinc") and hydrochloric acid.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-12)

Another method of methylamine production is spontaneous decarboxylation of [glycine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycine "Glycine") with a strong base in water.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-13)

Reactivity and applications
---------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methylamine&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Reactivity and applications")]

Methylamine is a good [nucleophile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleophile "Nucleophile") as it is an unhindered [amine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amine "Amine").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-14) As an amine it is considered a [weak base](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weak_base "Weak base"). Its use in [organic chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_chemistry "Organic chemistry") is pervasive. Some reactions involving simple reagents include: with [phosgene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phosgene "Phosgene") to [methyl isocyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate "Methyl isocyanate"), with [carbon disulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_disulfide "Carbon disulfide") and [sodium hydroxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_hydroxide "Sodium hydroxide") to the sodium methyldithiocarbamate, with [chloroform](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroform "Chloroform") and base to [methyl isocyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide "Methyl isocyanide") and with [ethylene oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethylene_oxide "Ethylene oxide") to [methylethanolamines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylethanolamine "Methylethanolamine"). Liquid methylamine has solvent properties analogous to those of [liquid ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia#Solvent "Ammonia").[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-15)

Representative commercially significant chemicals produced from methylamine include the pharmaceuticals [ephedrine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ephedrine "Ephedrine") and [theophylline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theophylline "Theophylline"), the pesticides [carbofuran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbofuran "Carbofuran"), [carbaryl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbaryl "Carbaryl"), and [metham sodium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metham_sodium "Metham sodium"), and the solvents [_N_-methylformamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-Methylformamide "N-Methylformamide") and [_N_-methylpyrrolidone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-Methyl-2-pyrrolidone "N-Methyl-2-pyrrolidone"). The preparation of some [surfactants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surfactant "Surfactant") and photographic developers require methylamine as a building block.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-Ullmann-7)

Biological chemistry
--------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methylamine&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Biological chemistry")]

Methylamine arises as a result of [putrefaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Putrefaction "Putrefaction") and is a substrate for [methanogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanogenesis "Methanogenesis").[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-16)

Additionally, methylamine is produced during [PADI4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PADI4 "PADI4")-dependent [arginine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arginine "Arginine")[demethylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demethylation "Demethylation").[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-17)

The [LD 50](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Median_lethal_dose "Median lethal dose") (mouse, [s.c.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subcutaneous_administration "Subcutaneous administration")) is 2.5 g/kg.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-18)

The [Occupational Safety and Health Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupational_Safety_and_Health_Administration "Occupational Safety and Health Administration") (OSHA) and [National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Institute_for_Occupational_Safety_and_Health "National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health") (NIOSH) have set occupational exposure limits at 10 ppm or 12 mg/m 3 over an eight-hour time-weighted average.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-19)

In the United States, methylamine is controlled as a [List 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DEA_list_of_chemicals "DEA list of chemicals")[precursor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drug_precursor "Drug precursor") chemical by the [Drug Enforcement Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drug_Enforcement_Administration "Drug Enforcement Administration")[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-20) due to its use in the illicit production of [methamphetamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methamphetamine "Methamphetamine").[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-21)

Fictional characters [Walter White](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_White_(Breaking_Bad) "Walter White (Breaking Bad)") and [Jesse Pinkman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesse_Pinkman "Jesse Pinkman") use [aqueous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aqueous_solution "Aqueous solution") methylamine as part of a process to synthesize [methamphetamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methamphetamine "Methamphetamine") in the [AMC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel) "AMC (TV channel)") series _[Breaking Bad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breaking\_Bad "Breaking Bad")_.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-22)[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine#cite_note-23)
