The following [outline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outline_(list) "Outline (list)") is provided as an overview and topical guide to space science:

**Space science** – field that encompasses all of the [scientific disciplines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Branches_of_science "Branches of science") that involve [space exploration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_exploration "Space exploration") and study [natural phenomena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_phenomena "Natural phenomena") and [physical bodies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomical_object "Astronomical object") occurring in [outer space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space "Outer space"), such as [space medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_medicine "Space medicine") and [astrobiology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrobiology "Astrobiology").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_science#cite_note-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_science#cite_note-2)

*   Subfields of astronomy: 
    *   [Astrophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrophysics "Astrophysics") – branch of [astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy "Astronomy") that deals with the [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics") of the [universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universe "Universe"), including the physical properties of [celestial objects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomical_object "Astronomical object"), as well as their interactions and behavior.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_science#cite_note-3) Among the objects studied are [galaxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy"), [stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star "Star"), [planets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planet "Planet"), [exoplanets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exoplanet "Exoplanet"), the [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") and the [cosmic microwave background](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_microwave_background "Cosmic microwave background"); and the properties examined include [luminosity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luminosity "Luminosity"), [density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Density "Density"), [temperature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature "Temperature"), and [chemical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemistry "Chemistry") composition. The subdisciplines of theoretical astrophysics are: 
        *   [Computational astrophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computational_astrophysics "Computational astrophysics") – The study of astrophysics using computational methods and tools to develop computational models.
        *   [Plasma astrophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasma_astrophysics "Plasma astrophysics") – studies properties of [plasma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasma_(physics) "Plasma (physics)") in outer space.
        *   [Space physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_physics "Space physics") – study of plasmas as they occur naturally in the Earth's upper atmosphere (aeronomy) and within the Solar System.
        *   [Solar physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_physics "Solar physics") – Sun and its interaction with the remainder of the Solar System and interstellar space.
        *   [Stellar astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy#Stellar_astronomy "Astronomy") – concerned with [Star formation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_formation "Star formation"), physical properties, [main sequence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Main_sequence "Main sequence") life span, [variability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variable_star "Variable star"), [stellar evolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_evolution "Stellar evolution") and extinction.
        *   [Galactic astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galactic_astronomy "Galactic astronomy") – deals with the structure and components of our galaxy and of other galaxies.
        *   [Extragalactic astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extragalactic_astronomy "Extragalactic astronomy") – study of objects (mainly galaxies) outside our galaxy, including [Galaxy formation and evolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_formation_and_evolution "Galaxy formation and evolution").

    *   [Cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmology "Cosmology")
        *   [Physical cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_cosmology "Physical cosmology") – origin and evolution of the universe as a whole. The study of cosmology is theoretical astrophysics at its largest scale.
        *   [Chemical cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmochemistry "Cosmochemistry") - study of the chemical composition of matter in the universe and the processes that led to those compositions.
        *   [Quantum cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_cosmology "Quantum cosmology") – the study of cosmology through the use of [quantum field theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_field_theory "Quantum field theory") to explain phenomena [general relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity "General relativity") cannot due to limitations in its framework.

    *   [Planetary Science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_Science "Planetary Science") – study of planets, moons, and planetary systems. 
        *   [Atmospheric science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmospheric_science "Atmospheric science") – study of atmospheres and weather.
        *   [Planetary geology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_geology "Planetary geology")
        *   [Planetary oceanography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_oceanography "Planetary oceanography")
        *   [Exoplanetology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exoplanetology "Exoplanetology") – various planets outside of the Solar System

    *   [Astrochemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrochemistry "Astrochemistry") – studies the abundance and reactions of molecules in the Universe, and their interaction with radiation.

*   Interdisciplinary studies of astronomy: 
    *   [Astrobiology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrobiology "Astrobiology") – studies the advent and [evolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evolution "Evolution") of [biological systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_system "Biological system") in the universe.
    *   [Space biology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_biology "Space biology") – studies to build a better understanding of how spaceflight affects living systems in spacecraft, or in ground-based experiments that mimic aspects of spaceflight[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_science#cite_note-4)
    *   [Space chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Space_chemistry&action=edit&redlink=1 "Space chemistry (page does not exist)") – Reactions of elements to form more complex compounds, such as amino acids, are key to the study of chemistry in space.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_science#cite_note-5)
        *   [Astrobotany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrobotany "Astrobotany") – Sub-discipline of botany that is the study of plants in space environments.

    *   [Archaeoastronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaeoastronomy "Archaeoastronomy") – studies ancient or traditional astronomies in their cultural context, utilizing archaeological and anthropological evidence.
    *   [Space archaeology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_archaeology "Space archaeology") – the study of human artifacts in outer space
    *   [Forensic astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forensic_astronomy "Forensic astronomy") – the use of astronomy, the scientific study of celestial objects, to determine the appearance of the sky at specific times in the past.

*   Techniques used in astronomical research: 
    *   [Theoretical astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theoretical_astronomy "Theoretical astronomy") – mathematical modelling of celestial entities and phenomena
    *   [Astrometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrometry "Astrometry") – study of the position of objects in the sky and their changes of position. Defines the system of coordinates used and the [kinematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinematics "Kinematics") of objects in our galaxy.
    *   [Photometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photometry_(astronomy) "Photometry (astronomy)") – study of how bright celestial objects are when passed through different filters
    *   [Spectroscopy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectroscopy "Spectroscopy") – study of the spectra of astronomical objects
    *   [Observational astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Observational_astronomy "Observational astronomy") – practice of observing celestial objects by using telescopes and other astronomical apparatus. [Observatories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Observatory "Observatory") on the ground as well as [space observatories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_observatory "Space observatory") take measurements of celestial entities and phenomena. It is concerned with recording data. The subdisciplines of [observational astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Observational_astronomy "Observational astronomy") are generally made by the specifications of the detectors: 
        *   [Radio astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_astronomy "Radio astronomy") – >300 μm
        *   [Submillimetre astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Submillimetre_astronomy "Submillimetre astronomy") – 200 μm to 1 mm
        *   [Infrared astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrared_astronomy "Infrared astronomy") – 0.7–350 μm
        *   [Optical astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_astronomy "Optical astronomy") – 380–750 nm
        *   [Ultraviolet astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet_astronomy "Ultraviolet astronomy") – 10–320 nm
        *   [High-energy astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High-energy_astronomy "High-energy astronomy")
            *   [Cosmic ray astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_ray_astronomy "Cosmic ray astronomy") - charged particles with very high kinetic energy
            *   [X-ray astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-ray_astronomy "X-ray astronomy") – 0.01–10 nm
            *   [Gamma-ray astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamma-ray_astronomy "Gamma-ray astronomy") – <0.01 nm
            *   [Neutrino astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neutrino_astronomy "Neutrino astronomy") – [Neutrinos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neutrino "Neutrino")

        *   [Gravitational wave astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitational_wave_astronomy "Gravitational wave astronomy") – [Gravitons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graviton "Graviton")
