[ 1 ] Families Endogonaceae Densosporaceae Endogonales is an order of fungi within the phylum of Zygomycota , [ 2 ] and in class Endogonomycetes .

[ 3 ] [ 4 ] It contains 2 families ; Endogonaceae , with 6 genera and 38 species and Densosporaceae , with one genera and 4 species.

[ 3 ] History [ edit ] The Endogales were originally formed to hold a single family, the Endogonaceae, which comprised 15 species in four genera ( Endogone , Peridiospora , Sclerogone , and Youngiomyces ). They were transferred to the phylum Glomeromycota to Zygomycetes .

[ 5 ] Then they were placed in Zygomycotina .

[ 6 ] They were placed in the subphylum Mucoromycotina in 2017 and genus Youngiomyces was synonymized with genus Endogone .

[ 7 ] They were then placed in the monotypic class Endogonomycetes.

[ 3 ] Life cycle [ edit ] The life cycle of the Endogonales is distinguished by their production of small sporocarps containing many zygospores , which are eaten by rodents and distributed by their faeces.

They also produce a fetid odour that attracts mammals and encourages them to eat their fruiting bodies, and so spread their spores.

[ 10 ] Food [ edit ] Like all fungi, they are heterotrophs with some being described as saprobes (with weak evidence).
