The **Local Interstellar Cloud** (**LIC**), also known as the **Local Fluff**, is an [interstellar cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_cloud "Interstellar cloud") roughly 30 [light-years](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light-year "Light-year") (9.2[pc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parsec "Parsec")) across, through which the [Solar System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_System "Solar System") is moving. This feature overlaps with a region around the Sun referred to as the **solar neighborhood**.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-2) It is unknown whether the [Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun "Sun") is embedded in the Local Interstellar Cloud, or is in the region where the Local Interstellar Cloud is interacting with the neighboring [G-Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G-Cloud "G-Cloud").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-Gilster-unknown-region-3) Like the G-Cloud and others, the LIC is part of the [Very Local Interstellar Medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") which begins where the [heliosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliosphere "Heliosphere") and [interplanetary medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interplanetary_medium "Interplanetary medium") end,[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-Linsky_p.-4) the furthest that probes have traveled.

Structure
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Local_Interstellar_Cloud&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Structure")]

The Solar System is located within a structure called the [Local Bubble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble "Local Bubble"), a low-density region of the galactic [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-JPL_interstellar-5) Within this region is the Local Interstellar Cloud (LIC), an area of slightly higher hydrogen density. It is estimated that the Solar System entered the LIC within the past 10,000 years.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-Frisch-2011-sec4-2-4-6) It is uncertain whether the Sun is still inside of the LIC or has already entered a transition zone between the LIC and the G cloud.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-Gilster-unknown-region-3)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-Frisch-2011-sec4-2-4-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-Linsky-2019-7) A recent analysis estimates the Sun will completely exit the LIC in no more than 1,900 years.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-Linsky-2020-8)

The cloud has a temperature of about 7,000 K (7,000°C; 12,000°F),[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-near-earth-9) about the same temperature as the surface of the Sun. However, its [specific heat capacity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Specific_heat_capacity "Specific heat capacity") is very low because it is not very dense, with 0.3 atoms per cubic centimetre (5/cu in). This is less dense than the average for the interstellar medium in the [Milky Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way "Milky Way") (0.5/cm 3 or 8/cu in), though six times denser than the gas in the hot, low-density Local Bubble (0.05/cm 3 or 0.8/cu in) which surrounds the local cloud.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-JPL_interstellar-5)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-Boulanger-10) In comparison, [Earth's atmosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmosphere_of_Earth "Atmosphere of Earth") at [the edge of space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%A1rm%C3%A1n_line "Kármán line") (i.e. 100 km above sea level) has around 1.2×10 13 molecules per cubic centimeter, dropping to around 50 million (5.0×10 7) at 450 km (280 mi).[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-11)

The cloud is flowing outwards from the [Scorpius–Centaurus association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scorpius%E2%80%93Centaurus_association "Scorpius–Centaurus association"), a [stellar association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_association "Stellar association") that is a star-forming region,[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-apod-1-12)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-apod-2-13) roughly perpendicular to the Sun's own direction.

In 2019, researchers found interstellar [iron-60](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron-60 "Iron-60") (60 Fe) in [Antarctica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica"), which they relate to the Local Interstellar Cloud.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-Koll2019-14)

Interaction with solar magnetic field
-------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Local_Interstellar_Cloud&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Interaction with solar magnetic field")]

[![Image 10](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Interstellar_medium_annotated.jpg/500px-Interstellar_medium_annotated.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Interstellar_medium_annotated.jpg)

The Solar System within the [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium"), with the different regions and their distances on a logarithmic scale (object sizes not to scale)

In 2009, _[Voyager 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voyager\_2 "Voyager 2")_ data suggested that the magnetic strength of the local interstellar medium was much stronger than expected (370 to 550 [pico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pico- "Pico-")[teslas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tesla_(unit) "Tesla (unit)") (pT), against previous estimates of 180 to 250 pT). The fact that the Local Interstellar Cloud is strongly magnetized could explain its continued existence despite the pressures exerted upon it by the winds that blew out the Local Bubble.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-15)

The Local Interstellar Cloud's potential effects on Earth are greatly diminished by the [solar wind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_wind "Solar wind") and the [Sun's magnetic field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_magnetic_field "Stellar magnetic field").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud#cite_note-near-earth-9) This interaction with the [heliosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliosphere "Heliosphere") is under study by the [Interstellar Boundary Explorer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_Boundary_Explorer "Interstellar Boundary Explorer") (IBEX), a [NASA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASA "NASA") satellite mapping the boundary between the Solar System and interstellar space.
