Albrecht Beutelspacher (2007)

**Albrecht Beutelspacher** (born 5 June 1950) is a German [mathematician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematician "Mathematician") and founder of the [Mathematikum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematikum "Mathematikum"). He is a professor emeritus at the [University of Giessen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Giessen "University of Giessen"), where he held the chair for [geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometry "Geometry") and [discrete mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discrete_mathematics "Discrete mathematics") from 1988 to 2018.

Beutelspacher studied from 1969 to 1973 math, physics and philosophy at the [University of Tübingen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_T%C3%BCbingen "University of Tübingen") and received his PhD 1976 from the [University of Mainz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannes_Gutenberg_University_of_Mainz "Johannes Gutenberg University of Mainz"). His PhD advisor was [Judita Cofman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judita_Cofman "Judita Cofman"). From 1982 to 1985 he was an associate professor at the University of Mainz and from 1985 to 1988 he worked at a research department of [Siemens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siemens "Siemens"). From 1988 to 2018 he was a tenured professor for geometry and discrete mathematics at the [University of Giessen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Giessen "University of Giessen"). He became a well-known popularizer of mathematics in Germany by authoring several books in the field of popular science and recreational math and by founding Germany's first math museum, the [Mathematikum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematikum "Mathematikum"). He received several awards for his contributions to popularizing mathematics. He had a math column in the German popular science magazine [Bild der Wissenschaft](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bild_der_Wissenschaft&action=edit&redlink=1 "Bild der Wissenschaft (page does not exist)") and moderated a popular math series for the TV Channel BR-![Image 2: {\displaystyle \alpha }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b79333175c8b3f0840bfb4ec41b8072c83ea88d3) (educational TV).
