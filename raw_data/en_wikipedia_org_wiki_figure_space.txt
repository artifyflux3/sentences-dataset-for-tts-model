Typographical space as wide as a numeral

A **figure space** or **numeric space**[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Figure_space#cite_note-1) is a [typographic unit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typographic_unit "Typographic unit") equal to the size of a single [numerical digit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numerical_digit "Numerical digit"). Its size can fluctuate somewhat depending on which [font](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Font "Font") is being used. This is the preferred space to use in numbers. It has the same width as a digit and keeps the number together for the purpose of line breaking.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Figure_space#cite_note-annex14-2)

Standard
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Figure_space&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Standard")]

In [Unicode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode "Unicode") it is assigned [U+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Punctuation#Block "General Punctuation")2007 FIGURE SPACE. Its HTML [character entity reference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_XML_and_HTML_character_entity_references "List of XML and HTML character entity references") is &numsp;.

[Baudot code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baudot_code "Baudot code") may include a figure space. It is character 23 on the [Hughes telegraph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hughes_telegraph "Hughes telegraph") typewheel.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Figure_space#cite_note-3)
