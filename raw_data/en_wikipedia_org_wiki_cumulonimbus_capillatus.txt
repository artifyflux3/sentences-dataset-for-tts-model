Symbol Altitude Ground to 23,000 m (75,000 ft) Appearance Dark-based storm cloud capable of impressive vertical growth, with a fibrous upper portion Precipitation Very often rain , snow , snow pellets , or hail ; heavy at times A cumulonimbus capillatus is a cumulonimbus cloud with dense cirrus clouds above it, making the cloud top appear to contain hair-like structures. The name comes from the Latin word capillatus , meaning "with hair".

[ 1 ] It is an intermediate stage between cumulonimbus calvus and cumulonimbus incus .

( October 2021 ) ( Learn how and when to remove this message ) A cumulonimbus capillatus is a mature and powerful cumulonimbus cloud and can produce multiple severe weather.

Lightning ; this is a strong thunderstorm cloud and it is capable of producing bursts of cloud to ground and cloud to cloud lightning .

Hail ; hailstones may fall from this cloud if it is in a highly unstable environment (which favors a more vigorous storm updraft ).

Heavy rain ; the cloud may drop several inches of rain in a short amount of time. This can cause flash flooding .

Strong wind ; gale-force winds from a downburst may occur under this cloud.

Strong to weak tornado can occur.
