In mathematics, the **radical symbol**, **radical sign**, **root symbol**, or **surd** is a [symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_notation "Mathematical notation") for the [square root](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square_root "Square root") or [higher-order root](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nth_root "Nth root") of a number. The square root of a number _x_ is written as

![Image 1: {\displaystyle {\sqrt {x}},}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b9763d9ff8638b882608213f6dc8e9fc8ddc79d3)
while the _n_ th root of _x_ is written as

![Image 2: {\displaystyle {\sqrt[{n}]{x}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8562e64a6bc6e408ddf67f055682c4dc9c9f957f)
It is also used for other meanings in more advanced mathematics, such as the [radical of an ideal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_of_an_ideal "Radical of an ideal").

In [linguistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linguistics "Linguistics"), the symbol is used to denote a [root word](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Root_word "Root word").

Principal square root
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Radical_symbol&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Principal square root")]

Each positive real number has two square roots, one positive and the other negative. The radical symbol refers to the [principal value](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Principal_value "Principal value") of the square root function called the principal square root, which is the positive one. The two square roots of a negative number are both [imaginary numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imaginary_number "Imaginary number"), and the square root symbol refers to the principal square root, the one with a positive imaginary part. For the definition of the principal square root of other [complex numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Complex_number "Complex number"), see [Square root §Principal square root of a complex number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Square_root#Principal_square_root_of_a_complex_number "Square root").

The origin of the root symbol √ is largely speculative. Some sources imply that the symbol was first used by Arab mathematicians. One of those mathematicians was [Abū al-Hasan ibn Alī al-Qalasādī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ab%C5%AB_al-Hasan_ibn_Al%C4%AB_al-Qalas%C4%81d%C4%AB "Abū al-Hasan ibn Alī al-Qalasādī") (1421–1486). Legend has it that it was taken from the [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic "Arabic") letter "ج" (_[ǧīm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gimel#Arabic\_%C7%A7%C4%ABm "Gimel")_), which is the first letter in the Arabic word "جذر" (_jadhir_, meaning "root").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-1) However, [Leonhard Euler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonhard_Euler "Leonhard Euler")[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-2) believed it originated from the letter "r", the first letter of the [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "Latin") word "[radix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radix "Radix")" (meaning "root"), referring to the same [mathematical operation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_(mathematics) "Operation (mathematics)").

The symbol was first seen in print without the [vinculum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinculum_(symbol) "Vinculum (symbol)") (the horizontal "bar" over the numbers inside the radical symbol) in the year 1525 in _Die Coss_ by [Christoff Rudolff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christoff_Rudolff "Christoff Rudolff"), a [German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_people "German people") mathematician. In 1637 [Descartes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renee_Descartes "Renee Descartes") was the first to unite the German radical sign √ with the vinculum to create the radical symbol in common use today.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-3)

The Unicode and HTML character codes for the radical symbols are:

*   U+221A√SQUARE ROOT (&radic;, &Sqrt;)
*   U+221B∛CUBE ROOT
*   U+221C∜FOURTH ROOT
*   U+23B7⎷RADICAL SYMBOL BOTTOM

However, these characters differ in appearance from most mathematical typesetting by omitting the [overline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overline "Overline") connected to the radical symbol, which surrounds the argument of the square root function. The [OpenType](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenType "OpenType") math table allows adding this overline following the radical symbol.

The Symbol font displays the character without any vinculum whatsoever; the overline may be a separate character at 0x60.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-4) The JIS,[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-5) Wansung[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-6) and CNS 11643[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-cnstwradic-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-8) code charts include a short overline attached to the radical symbol, whereas the GB 2312[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-9) and [GB 18030](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GB_18030 "GB 18030") charts do not.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-10)

Additionally a "Radical Symbol Bottom" (U+23B7, ⎷) is available in the [Miscellaneous Technical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miscellaneous_Technical "Miscellaneous Technical") block.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-11) This was used in contexts where [box-drawing characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Box-drawing_character "Box-drawing character") are used, such as in the [technical character set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DEC_Technical_Character_Set "DEC Technical Character Set") of [DEC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation "Digital Equipment Corporation") terminals, to join up with box drawing characters on the line above to create the vinculum.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-12)

In [LaTeX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LaTeX "LaTeX") the square root symbol may be generated by the `\sqrt` macro,[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-13) and the square root symbol without the overline may be generated by the `\surd` macro.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_symbol#cite_note-14)
