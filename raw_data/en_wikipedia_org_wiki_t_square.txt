[![Image 8](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Drafting_board_with_T_Square.jpg/250px-Drafting_board_with_T_Square.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Drafting_board_with_T_Square.jpg)

Drafting board with a T-square and [set square](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set_square "Set square")

A **T-square** is a technical [drawing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plan_(drawing) "Plan (drawing)")[instrument](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Measuring_instrument "Measuring instrument") used by [draftsmen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_drawing "Technical drawing") primarily as a guide for drawing horizontal lines on a [drafting table](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drafting_table "Drafting table"). The instrument is named after its resemblance to the letter T, with a long shaft called the "blade" and a short shaft called the "stock" or "head". T-squares are available in a range of sizes, with common lengths being 18 inches (460 mm), 24 inches (610 mm), 30 inches (760 mm), 36 inches (910 mm) and 42 inches (1,100 mm).[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

In addition to drawing horizontal lines, a T-square can be used with a [set square](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set_square "Set square") to draw vertical or diagonal lines.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T-square#cite_note-Drafting_1940-1) The T-square usually has a [transparent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transparency_and_translucency "Transparency and translucency") edge made of [plastic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plastic "Plastic") which should be free of nicks and cracks in order to provide smooth, straight lines.

T-squares are also used in various industries, such as construction. For example, drywall T-squares are typically made of aluminum and have a 48-inch-long (120 cm) tongue, allowing them to be used for measuring and cutting drywall. In woodworking, higher-end [table saws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Table_saw "Table saw") often have T-square [fences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fence_(woodworking) "Fence (woodworking)") attached to a rail on the front side of the table, providing improved accuracy and precision when cutting wood.
