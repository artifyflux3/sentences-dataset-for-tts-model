He was born in Oslo , but grew up in Helsinki and finished his secondary education in Finland before studying psychology at the University of Oslo . He took the mag.art.

degree in 1951 and the dr.philos.

degree in 1955. In the 1950s and 1960s, he was a guest scholar in Geneva and at the Center for Cognitive Studies , Harvard University , [ 1 ] and he was employed by the Norwegian Institute for Social Research .

[ 2 ] From 1964, he held a professorship of psychology at the University of Bergen , changing to the University of Oslo in 1966.

[ 3 ] He was inducted into the Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters in 1975.

[ 4 ] His later guest scholar stints include the University of Minnesota , the University of Kansas , Stanford University , Oxford University and Cambridge University .

[ 1 ] From 1977 to 2009, he was also a clinical psychologist with a private clinic. He resided in Oslo.
