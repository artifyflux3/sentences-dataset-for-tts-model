Find sources: "Eurymedon the Hierophant" – news · newspapers · books · scholar · JSTOR ( January 2017 ) ( Learn how and when to remove this message ) Eurymedon the Hierophant ( / j ʊəˈr ɪ m ɪ d ɒ n / ; Greek : Εὐρυμέδων ὁ ἱεροφάντης ) was a representative of the priestly clan overseeing the Eleusinian Mysteries . Together with Demophilus he reportedly brought a charge of impiety against Aristotle after Alexander the Great 's death in 323 BC.
