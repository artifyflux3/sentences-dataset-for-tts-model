Chemical compound

**Cyclopropenylidene**, or **_c_-C 3 H 2**, is a partially [aromatic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aromatic_hydrocarbon "Aromatic hydrocarbon") molecule belonging to a highly reactive class of [organic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound")[molecules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecules "Molecules") known as [carbenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbene "Carbene"). On Earth, cyclopropenylidene is only seen in the laboratory due to its reactivity. However, cyclopropenylidene is found in significant concentrations in the [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") (ISM) and on [Saturn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturn "Saturn")'s moon [Titan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titan_(moon) "Titan (moon)"). Its C 2v[symmetric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_symmetry "Molecular symmetry")[isomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomer "Isomer"), [propadienylidene](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Propadienylidene&action=edit&redlink=1 "Propadienylidene (page does not exist)") (CCCH 2) is also found in the ISM, but with abundances about an order of magnitude lower.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-1) A third C 2 symmetric isomer, propargylene (HCCCH), has not yet been detected in the ISM, most likely due to its low [dipole moment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_polarity "Chemical polarity").

History
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cyclopropenylidene&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

The [astronomical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomical "Astronomical") detection of _c_-C 3 H 2 was first confirmed in 1985.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-thad1-2) Four years earlier, several ambiguous lines had been observed in the [radio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_waves "Radio waves") region of [spectra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absorption_spectrum "Absorption spectrum") taken of the ISM,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-3) but the observed lines were not identified at the time. These lines were later matched with a spectrum of _c_-C 3 H 2 using an acetylene-helium [discharge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct_current_discharge "Direct current discharge"). Surprisingly, _c_-C 3 H 2 has been found to be ubiquitous in the ISM.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-lucas-4) Detections of _c_-C 3 H 2 in the [diffuse medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_cloud "Interstellar cloud") were particularly surprising because of the low densities.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-6) It had been believed that the [chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemistry "Chemistry") of the diffuse medium did not allow for the formation of larger molecules, but this discovery, as well as the discovery of other large molecules, continue to illuminate the complexity of the diffuse medium. More recently, observations of _c_-C 3 H 2 in [dense clouds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_cloud "Interstellar cloud") have also found concentrations that are significantly higher than expected. This has led to the hypothesis that the photodissociation of [polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbons "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons") (PAHs) enhances the formation of _c_-C 3 H 2.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-pety-7)

### Titan (Moon of Saturn)

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cyclopropenylidene&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Titan (Moon of Saturn)")]

On 15 October 2020, it was announced that small amounts of cyclopropenylidene had been found in the atmosphere of [Titan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titan_(moon) "Titan (moon)"), the largest moon of [Saturn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturn "Saturn")[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-8) using the [ALMA telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atacama_Large_Millimeter_Array "Atacama Large Millimeter Array"). Cyclopropenylidene became the fifth C 3 H n (three carbon) [hydrocarbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrocarbon "Hydrocarbon") molecule detected on Titan, after previous detections of CH 3 CCH ([propyne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne "Propyne")) and C 3 H 8 ([propane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propane "Propane"));[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-9) C 3 H 6 ([propene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propylene "Propylene"));[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-10) and CH 2 CCH 2 ([propadiene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propadiene "Propadiene")).[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-11)

Formation
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cyclopropenylidene&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Formation")]

The formation [reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_reaction "Chemical reaction") of _c_-C 3 H 2 has been speculated to be the [dissociative recombination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissociative_recombination "Dissociative recombination") of _c_-C

3 H+

3.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-mal-12)

C

3 H+

3 + e− → C 3 H 2 + H
_c_-C

3 H+

3 is a product of a long chain of carbon chemistry that occurs in the ISM. Carbon insertion reactions are crucial in this chain for forming C

3 H+

3. However, as for most [ion-molecule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas-phase_ion_chemistry "Gas-phase ion chemistry") reactions speculated to be important in interstellar environments, this pathway has not been verified by laboratory studies. The protonation of [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia") by _c_-C

3 H+

3 is another formation reaction. However, under typical dense cloud conditions, this reaction contributes less than 1% of the formation of C 3 H 2.

[Crossed molecular beam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crossed_molecular_beam "Crossed molecular beam") experiments indicate that the reaction of the [methylidyne radical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylidyne_radical "Methylidyne radical") (CH) with [acetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetylene "Acetylene") (C 2 H 2) forms cyclopropenylidene plus atomic hydrogen and also propadienylidene plus atomic hydrogen.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-13) The neutral–neutral reaction between atomic carbon and the [vinyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_group "Vinyl group") radical (C 2 H 3) also forms cyclopropenylidene plus atomic hydrogen.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-14) Both reactions are rapid at 10[K](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelvin "Kelvin") and have no [entrance barrier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Activation_energy "Activation energy") and provide efficient formation pathways in cold interstellar environments and [hydrocarbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrocarbon "Hydrocarbon")-rich atmospheres of planets and their moons.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-15)

[Matrix isolated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matrix_isolation "Matrix isolation") cyclopropenylidene has been prepared by [flash vacuum thermolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrolysis "Pyrolysis") of a [quadricyclane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quadricyclane "Quadricyclane") derivative in 1984.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-16)

Destruction
-----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cyclopropenylidene&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Destruction")]

Cyclopropenylidene is generally destroyed by reactions between [ions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ion "Ion") and neutral molecules. Of these, [protonation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protonation "Protonation") reactions are the most common. Any species of the type HX+ can react to convert the _c_-C 3 H 2 back to _c_-C

3 H+

3.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-mal-12) Due to [rate constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reaction_rate_constant "Reaction rate constant") and concentration considerations, the most important reactants for the destruction of _c_-C 3 H 2 are HCO+, [H+ 3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trihydrogen_cation "Trihydrogen cation"), and [H 3 O+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydronium "Hydronium").[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-17)

C 3 H 2 + HCO+ → C

3 H+

3 + CO
Notice that _c_-C 3 H 2 is mostly destroyed by converting it back to C

3 H+

3. Since the major destruction pathways only regenerate the major parent molecule, C 3 H 2 is essentially a dead end in terms of interstellar carbon chemistry. However, in diffuse clouds or in the [photodissociation region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region "Photodissociation region") (PDR) of dense clouds, the reaction with C+ becomes much more significant and C 3 H 2 can begin to contribute to the formation of larger [organic molecules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound").

Spectroscopy
------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cyclopropenylidene&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Spectroscopy")]

Detections of _c_-C 3 H 2 in the ISM rely on observations of molecular transitions using [rotational spectroscopy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotational_spectroscopy "Rotational spectroscopy"). Since _c_-C 3 H 2 is an asymmetric top, the rotational [energy levels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_level "Energy level") are split and the spectrum becomes complicated. Also, it should be noticed that C 3 H 2 has spin isomers much like the [spin isomers of hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin_isomers_of_hydrogen "Spin isomers of hydrogen"). These _ortho_ and _para_ forms exist in a 3:1 ratio and should be thought of as distinct molecules. Although the ortho and para forms look identical chemically, the energy levels are different, meaning that the molecules have different [spectroscopic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectroscopy "Spectroscopy") transitions.

When observing _c_-C 3 H 2 in the interstellar medium, there are only certain transitions that can be seen. In general, only a few lines are available for use in astronomical detection. Many lines are unobservable because they are absorbed by the Earth's [atmosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmosphere "Atmosphere"). The only lines that can be observed are those that fall in the [radio window](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_window "Radio window"). The more commonly observed lines are the 1 10 to 1 01 transition at 18 343 MHz and the 2 12 to 1 01 transition at 85 338 MHz of _ortho_-_c_-C 3 H 2.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-thad1-2)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-lucas-4)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenylidene#cite_note-pety-7)
