Chemical compound

**Magnesium cyanide** is a chemical compound with the formula Mg(CN)2. It is a toxic white solid. Unlike [calcium isocyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calcium_isocyanide "Calcium isocyanide"), the cyanide ligands prefer to coordinate at carbon, with a 0.3‑kcal/mol isomerization barrier.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium_cyanide#cite_note-cry-1) When this salt is heated to 500 °C, it decomposes to [magnesium nitride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium_nitride "Magnesium nitride").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium_cyanide#cite_note-pro-2)

Preparation
-----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Magnesium_cyanide&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Preparation")]

The first attempt to prepare magnesium cyanide was attempted in 1924. It was attempted by reacting a solution of [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide") in water with magnesium metal:[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium_cyanide#cite_note-pro-2)

HCN + Mg → Mg(CN)2 + H 2
However, no magnesium cyanide was observed, only [magnesium hydroxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium_hydroxide "Magnesium hydroxide") formed. To avoid this problem, instead of using water as the reaction medium, pure [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia") was used at -30 °C. This formed magnesium cyanide ammoniate, which in turn was heated to 180 °C to produce magnesium cyanide.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium_cyanide#cite_note-:)-3) Other methods are possible, such as the decomposition of [magnesium ferricyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Magnesium_ferricyanide&action=edit&redlink=1 "Magnesium ferricyanide (page does not exist)") in an electric carbon tube, which produces [iron carbide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron_carbide "Iron carbide") as a byproduct.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium_cyanide#cite_note-pro-2)

Complexes
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Magnesium_cyanide&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Complexes")]

Magnesium cyanide reacts with [silver nitrate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver_nitrate "Silver nitrate") to form magnesium silver cyanide, with the formula MgAg 2(CN)4. When this compound is heated it produces [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide") gas and magnesium hydroxide in the presence of water, which meant it could not be used as a pathway for the production of magnesium cyanide. When silver nitrate reacts with magnesium cyanide, it also produces another magnesium silver cyanide, with the formula MgAg(CN)3.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium_cyanide#cite_note-pro-2)

Toxicity
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Magnesium_cyanide&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Toxicity")]

Main article: [Cyanide poisoning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanide_poisoning "Cyanide poisoning")

Magnesium cyanide, like all cyanides, is extremely toxic. When it enters the body, it inhibits tissue respiration enzymes, and tissues lose the ability to absorb oxygen from the blood.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium_cyanide#cite_note-4)
