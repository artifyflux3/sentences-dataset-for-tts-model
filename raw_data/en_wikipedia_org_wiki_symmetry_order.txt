From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

(Redirected from [Symmetry order](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Symmetry_order&redirect=no "Symmetry order"))

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Octahedral_reflection_domains.png/250px-Octahedral_reflection_domains.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Octahedral_reflection_domains.png)

A sphere colored to show the 48 [fundamental domains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fundamental_domain "Fundamental domain") of [octahedral symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octahedral_symmetry "Octahedral symmetry").

The **symmetry number** or **symmetry order** of an object is the number of different but indistinguishable (or equivalent) arrangements (or views) of the object, that is, it is the [order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_(group_theory) "Order (group theory)") of its [symmetry group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetry_group "Symmetry group"). The object can be a molecule, crystal lattice, lattice, tiling, or in general any kind of mathematical object that admits symmetries.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetry_order#cite_note-1)

In [statistical thermodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_thermodynamics "Statistical thermodynamics"), the symmetry number corrects for any overcounting of equivalent molecular conformations in the [partition function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_function_(statistical_mechanics) "Partition function (statistical mechanics)"). In this sense, the symmetry number depends upon how the partition function is formulated. For example, if one writes the partition function of [ethane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethane "Ethane") so that the integral includes full [rotation of a methyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_group_rotation "Methyl group rotation"), then the 3-fold rotational symmetry of the methyl group contributes a factor of 3 to the symmetry number; but if one writes the partition function so that the integral includes only one rotational energy well of the methyl, then the methyl rotation does not contribute to the symmetry number. [[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetry_order#cite_note-2)
