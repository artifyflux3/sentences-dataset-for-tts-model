Mollivirus sibericum is a spherical DNA virus with a diameter of 500–600 nanometers (0.5–0.6 μm).

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] Mollivirus sibericum is the fourth ancient virus that scientists have found frozen in permafrost since 2003.

[ 3 ] It has a sister taxon , Mollivirus kamchatka .

[ 4 ] Description [ edit ] Mollivirus sibericum is an approximately spherical virion 0.6 μm in diameter. It encloses a 651 kb GC-rich genome encoding 523 proteins , of which 64% are ORFs .

[ 1 ] [ 5 ] The host's ribosomal proteins are packaged in the virion.
