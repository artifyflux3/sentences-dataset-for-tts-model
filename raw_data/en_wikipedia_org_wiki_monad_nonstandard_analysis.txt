[ 1 ] [ 2 ] Given a hyperreal number x in R ∗ , the monad of x is the set monad ( x ) = { y ∈ ∈ R ∗ ∗ ∣ ∣ x − − y is infinitesimal } .

{\displaystyle {\text{monad}}(x)=\{y\in \mathbb {R} ^{*}\mid x-y{\text{ is infinitesimal}}\}.} If x is finite (limited), the unique real number in the monad of x is called the standard part of x .
