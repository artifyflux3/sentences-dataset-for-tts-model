A domain wall is meant to represent an object of codimension one embedded into space (a defect in space localized in one spatial dimension). For example, D8-branes are domain walls in type II string theory . In M-theory , the existence of Horava–Witten domain walls , "ends of the world" that carry an E8 gauge theory , is important for various relations between superstring theory and M-theory .

If domain walls exist, their interactions are hypothesized to emit gravitational waves that would be detectable by LIGO and similar experiments.
