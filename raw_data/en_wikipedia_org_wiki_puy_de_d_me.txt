**Puy de Dôme** (,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-3)French:[[pɥi də dom]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/French "Help:IPA/French")[](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/3/36/Fr-Paris--Puy-de-D%C3%B4me.ogg/Fr-Paris--Puy-de-D%C3%B4me.ogg.mp3 "Play audio")[ⓘ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Fr-Paris--Puy-de-D%C3%B4me.ogg "File:Fr-Paris--Puy-de-Dôme.ogg"))[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-4) is a [lava dome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lava_dome "Lava dome") and one of the youngest [volcanoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcano "Volcano") in the _[Chaîne des Puys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cha%C3%AEne\_des\_Puys "Chaîne des Puys")_ region of [Massif Central](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massif_Central "Massif Central") in central [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France "France"). This chain of volcanoes including numerous [cinder cones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinder_cone "Cinder cone"), [lava domes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lava_dome "Lava dome") and [maars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maar "Maar") is far from the edge of any [tectonic plate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tectonic_plate "Tectonic plate").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-5)

Puy de Dôme was created by a [Peléan eruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pel%C3%A9an_eruption "Peléan eruption"), some 10,700 years ago. It is approximately 10 km (6 miles) from [Clermont-Ferrand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clermont-Ferrand "Clermont-Ferrand"). The [Puy-de-Dôme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy-de-D%C3%B4me "Puy-de-Dôme")_[département](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Departments\_of\_France "Departments of France")_ is named after the volcano.

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me_-_136.jpg/250px-Puy_de_D%C3%B4me_-_136.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Puy_de_D%C3%B4me_-_136.jpg)

Puy de Dôme

In pre-Christian [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe "Europe"), Puy de Dôme was an assembly place for spiritual ceremonies.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-6) Temples were built at the summit, including a [Gallo-Roman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gallo-Roman "Gallo-Roman")[temple of Mercury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temple_of_Mercury_(Puy_de_D%C3%B4me) "Temple of Mercury (Puy de Dôme)"), the ruins of which were discovered in 1872.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-7)

In 1648, [Florin Périer](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Florin_P%C3%A9rier&action=edit&redlink=1 "Florin Périer (page does not exist)")[[fr](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florin_P%C3%A9rier "fr:Florin Périer")], at the urging of his brother-in-law [Blaise Pascal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blaise_Pascal "Blaise Pascal"), confirmed [Evangelista Torricelli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evangelista_Torricelli "Evangelista Torricelli")'s theory that [barometric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barometer "Barometer") observations were caused by the weight of air by measuring the height of a column of [mercury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercury_(element) "Mercury (element)") at three elevations on Puy de Dôme.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-9)

In 1875, a physics laboratory was built at the summit. Since 1956, a TDF (_[Télédiffusion de France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T%C3%A9l%C3%A9diffusion\_de\_France "Télédiffusion de France")_) antenna is also located there. On the top of the mountain, there is a transmitter for FM radio and TV.

[![Image 6](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Puy_de_Dome%2C_Mercury_temple.jpg/250px-Puy_de_Dome%2C_Mercury_temple.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Puy_de_Dome,_Mercury_temple.jpg)

The [Temple of Mercury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temple_of_Mercury_(Puy_de_D%C3%B4me) "Temple of Mercury (Puy de Dôme)") before its reconstruction

A [UNESCO World Heritage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNESCO_World_Heritage "UNESCO World Heritage") site since 2018,[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-10) the Puy de Dôme is one of the most visited sites in the [Auvergne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auvergne_(r%C3%A9gion) "Auvergne (région)") region, attracting nearly 500,000 visitors a year. The summit offers expansive views of the [Chaîne des Puys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cha%C3%AEne_des_Puys "Chaîne des Puys") and [Clermont-Ferrand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clermont-Ferrand "Clermont-Ferrand"). It is a well-known centre for [paragliding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paragliding "Paragliding").

The summit can be reached by two [pedestrian paths](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trail "Trail"). The southern one, _Le sentier des muletiers_ ("The Mule Trail"), was formerly a [Roman road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_road "Roman road"). The northern one, _Le sentier des chèvres_ ("The Goat Trail"), runs past the _Nid de la Poule_ (Hen's Nest} crater. The [GR 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GR_footpath "GR footpath") long-distance trail includes both paths to cross the mountain.

Since May 2012, visitors can also go up the mountain by train with the _[Panoramique des Dômes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panoramique\_des\_D%C3%B4mes "Panoramique des Dômes")_, a [rack railway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rack_railway "Rack railway").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-11)

A road exists along the railway but it is closed to general traffic, except for the military, service vehicles or emergencies.

At the top of the mountain, restaurants and shops are available as well as a [visitor centre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visitor_centre "Visitor centre") giving information on the history and geology of the area.

### Geological heritage site

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Puy_de_D%C3%B4me&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Geological heritage site")]

In respect of its key role in the development of [volcanology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcanology "Volcanology") as a geoscience discipline, the Puy-de-Dôme and Petite-Puy-de-Dôme volcanoes were included by the [International Union of Geological Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Union_of_Geological_Sciences "International Union of Geological Sciences") (IUGS) in its assemblage of 100 "geological heritage sites" around the world in a listing published in October 2022.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-12)

In more recent times, Puy de Dôme has served as an occasional stage finish in the [Tour de France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tour_de_France "Tour de France"). It was here that in [1964](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1964_Tour_de_France "1964 Tour de France")[Raymond Poulidor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raymond_Poulidor "Raymond Poulidor") battled with [Jacques Anquetil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Anquetil "Jacques Anquetil") in one of the race's most famous moments, racing side by side up almost the entire climb; and that in [1975](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1975_Tour_de_France "1975 Tour de France")[Eddy Merckx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eddy_Merckx "Eddy Merckx") was punched in the kidney by a spectator.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-13)

Between the [1988](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1988_Tour_de_France "1988 Tour de France") and [2023](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Tour_de_France "2023 Tour de France") Tour de France summit finishes, only the first part of the ascent to the [Col de Ceyssat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Settlement_of_the_Col_de_Ceyssat "Roman Settlement of the Col de Ceyssat") was covered in the 13th stage of the [2020 Tour de France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Tour_de_France "2020 Tour de France"). The 2012 construction of the [Puy de Dôme rack railway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rack_railway_of_Puy_de_D%C3%B4me "Rack railway of Puy de Dôme") narrowed the already very narrow road to the top. Because of the increasing logistical demands of the Tour, many thought that the Tour would never return after 1988[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-14) but the 9th stage of the [2023 Tour de France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Tour_de_France "2023 Tour de France") had a summit finish, which was won by [Michael Woods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Woods_(cyclist) "Michael Woods (cyclist)"). However, spectators were not permitted on the climb.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-15)

Tourist centre

The road is open to cyclists only during very limited periods when other vehicles are prohibited. In 2006 this was 7–9am on Wednesdays and Sundays between 1 May and 30 September.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-16) In 2017, it was the first checkpoint in the [Transcontinental Race](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transcontinental_Race "Transcontinental Race"), a nonstop, unsupported bicycle race across Europe.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-17)

The [Circuit de Charade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circuit_de_Charade "Circuit de Charade") was a [motorsport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motorsport "Motorsport")[street circuit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Street_circuit "Street circuit") built in 1957 using pre-existing roads around the base of the Puy de Dôme.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-CharadeRush-18) The venue hosted the [French Grand Prix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Grand_Prix "French Grand Prix") as well as the [French motorcycle Grand Prix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_motorcycle_Grand_Prix "French motorcycle Grand Prix") several times in the 1960s and early 1970s.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-CharadeRush-18) In 1986, the track was shortened due to safety issues and was converted into a dedicated motorsport [race track](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Race_track "Race track") hosting [track days](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Track_day "Track day"), driving schools as well as [historic motorsport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Historic_motorsport "Historic motorsport") events.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-CharadeRush-18)[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puy_de_D%C3%B4me#cite_note-19)

While the lower areas of the mountain are firmly [oceanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oceanic_climate "Oceanic climate") ([Köppen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification"): _Cfb_), Puy de Dôme has a [humid continental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humid_continental_climate "Humid continental climate") (Köppen: _Dfb_) with borderline [subalpine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subalpine_climate "Subalpine climate") characteristics, thanks to its high elevation. Its classification is determined from its January average being well below the −3°C (27°F) threshold (with −5°C (23°F) as its usual lows), and from having over four months of average temperatures that exceed 10°C (50°F). (The requirement for this climate category is to have at least three such months.)
