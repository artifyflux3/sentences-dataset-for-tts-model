The Port of Lappeenranta on the Lake Saimaa in Lappeenranta , Finland An inland port is a port on an inland waterway , such as a river , lake , or canal , which may or may not be connected to the sea. The term "inland port" is also used to refer to a dry port .

Examples [ edit ] The United States Army Corps of Engineers publishes biannually a list of such locations and for this purpose states that "inland ports" are ports that are located on rivers and do not handle deep draft ship traffic . The list includes ports such as St. Louis , Cincinnati , Pittsburgh , Kansas City , and Memphis .
A dense network of inland waterways including ports exists also in Europe (France, Germany, Poland, Russia, the United Kingdom and the Benelux countries), as well as in China and Brazil.
