During 1968–1970, the metric system was adopted in Singapore.

[ 1 ] The metric system is used for most official purposes and only metric measures are permitted for trade.

[ 2 ] Despite these restrictions, fabric is still commonly sold by the square yard.

System during the later half of the 19th century [ edit ] Several units were used in Singapore under Straits Settlements .

Length units [ edit ] 1 cubit (aka. hasta) = 18 inches which is equal to 45.72 cm.

[ 3 ] Mass units [ edit ] 1 mace (aka. miam) = 52 grain equal to 3.36954 grams.

1 buncal (for gold) = 822 grain equal to 53.2647 grams.
