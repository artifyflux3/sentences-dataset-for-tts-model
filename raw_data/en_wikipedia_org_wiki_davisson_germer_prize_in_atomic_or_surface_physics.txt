Find sources: "Davisson–Germer Prize" – news · newspapers · books · scholar · JSTOR ( January 2020 ) The Davisson–Germer Prize in Atomic or Surface Physics is an annual prize that has been awarded by the American Physical Society since 1965. The recipient is chosen for " outstanding work in atomic physics or surface physics ". The prize is named after Clinton Davisson and Lester Germer , who first measured electron diffraction , and as of 2007 it is valued at $5,000.
