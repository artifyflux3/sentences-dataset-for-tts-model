In the stratosphere, the temperature increases with altitude , and the stratopause is the region where a maximum in the temperature occurs. This atmospheric feature is not exclusive to Earth, but also occurs on any other planet or moon with an atmosphere .

[ 1 ] According to James Kasting , planets whose atmospheres do not absorb shortwave sunlight, such as Venus and Mars , do not have a Stratosphere and thus have no Stratopause.

[ 2 ] On Earth , the stratopause is about 50 km (31 mi) above sea level .

[ 3 ] The atmospheric pressure is around 1 ⁄ 1000 of the pressure at sea level.

[ 4 ] The temperature in the stratopause is −2.5 °C (27.5 °F).
