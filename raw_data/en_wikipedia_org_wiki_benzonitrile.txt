**Benzonitrile** is the [chemical compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_compound "Chemical compound") with the formula C 6 H 5(CN), abbreviated [Ph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenyl "Phenyl")CN. This [aromatic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aromatic "Aromatic")[organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") is a colorless liquid with a [cherry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cherry "Cherry") or [almond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almond "Almond") like odour. It is mainly used industrially to synthesize the [melamine resin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melamine_resin "Melamine resin") precursor [benzoguanamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzoguanamine "Benzoguanamine").

Production and reactions
------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Benzonitrile&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Production and reactions")]

It is prepared by [ammoxidation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammoxidation "Ammoxidation") of [toluene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toluene "Toluene"), that is its reaction with [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia") and oxygen (or air) at 400 to 450°C (752 to 842°F).[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzonitrile#cite_note-1)

C 6 H 5 CH 3 + 3/2 O 2 + NH 3 → C 6 H 5(CN) + 3 H 2 O
In the laboratory it can be prepared by the dehydration of [benzamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzamide "Benzamide") or [benzaldehyde oxime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzaldehyde_oxime "Benzaldehyde oxime")[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzonitrile#cite_note-2) or by the [Rosenmund–von Braun reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosenmund%E2%80%93von_Braun_reaction "Rosenmund–von Braun reaction") using [cuprous cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuprous_cyanide "Cuprous cyanide") or [NaCN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_cyanide "Sodium cyanide")/[DMSO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_sulfoxide "Dimethyl sulfoxide") and [bromobenzene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bromobenzene "Bromobenzene").

[![Image 18: Rosenmund-von Braun synthesis](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Rosenmund-von_Braun_synthesis.svg/288px-Rosenmund-von_Braun_synthesis.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Rosenmund-von_Braun_synthesis.svg "Rosenmund-von Braun synthesis")
[Hydrogenation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogenation "Hydrogenation") of benzonitrile in principle gives [benzylamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzylamine "Benzylamine"), but owing to transamination, [dibenzylamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dibenzylamine "Dibenzylamine") and [tribenzylamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tribenzylamine "Tribenzylamine") are also produced.

Benzonitrile is a useful solvent and a versatile precursor to many derivatives. It reacts with amines to afford N-substituted benzamides after hydrolysis.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzonitrile#cite_note-3) It is a precursor to [diphenylmethanimine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzophenone_imine "Benzophenone imine") via reaction with [phenylmagnesium bromide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenylmagnesium_bromide "Phenylmagnesium bromide") followed by [methanolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanolysis "Methanolysis").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzonitrile#cite_note-4)

Benzonitrile forms [coordination complexes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coordination_complex "Coordination complex") with transition metals that are both soluble in organic solvents and conveniently labile. One example is [PdCl 2(PhCN)2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bis(benzonitrile)palladium_dichloride "Bis(benzonitrile)palladium dichloride"). The benzonitrile ligands are readily displaced by stronger ligands, making benzonitrile complexes useful synthetic intermediates.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzonitrile#cite_note-5)

Benzonitrile was reported by [Hermann Fehling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermann_von_Fehling "Hermann von Fehling") in 1844. He found the compound as a product from the thermal dehydration of [ammonium benzoate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonium_benzoate "Ammonium benzoate"). He deduced its structure from the already known analogue reaction of [ammonium formate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonium_formate "Ammonium formate") yielding [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide") ([formonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide")). He also coined the name benzonitrile which gave the name to all the group of [nitriles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrile "Nitrile").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzonitrile#cite_note-6)

In 2018, benzonitrile was reported to be detected in the [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzonitrile#cite_note-SC-20180112-7)
