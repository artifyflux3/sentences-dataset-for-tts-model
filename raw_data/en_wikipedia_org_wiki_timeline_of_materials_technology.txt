From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Major innovations in **[materials technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Materials_science "Materials science")**

*   28,000 BC – People wear beads, bracelets, and pendants[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)
*   14,500 BC – First [pottery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pottery "Pottery"), made by the [Jōmon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%C5%8Dmon_period "Jōmon period") people of Japan.
*   [6th millennium BC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/6th_millennium_BC "6th millennium BC") – [Copper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copper "Copper")[metallurgy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metallurgy "Metallurgy") is invented and copper is used for ornamentation (see [Pločnik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plo%C4%8Dnik_(archaeological_site) "Pločnik (archaeological site)") article)
*   [2nd millennium BC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2nd_millennium_BC "2nd millennium BC") – [Bronze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze "Bronze") is used for [weapons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weapon "Weapon") and [armor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armor "Armor")
*   16th century BC – The [Hittites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hittites "Hittites") develop crude [iron metallurgy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_ferrous_metallurgy "History of ferrous metallurgy")
*   13th century BC – Invention of [steel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steel "Steel") when [iron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron "Iron") and [charcoal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charcoal "Charcoal") are combined properly
*   10th century BC – [Glass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glass "Glass") production begins in [ancient Near East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Near_East "Ancient Near East")
*   [1st millennium BC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1st_millennium_BC "1st millennium BC") – [Pewter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pewter "Pewter") beginning to be used in China and [Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt "Egypt")
*   1000 BC – The [Phoenicians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenicia "Phoenicia") introduce dyes made from the purple murex.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)
*   3rd century BC – [Wootz steel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wootz_steel "Wootz steel"), the first [crucible steel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crucible_steel "Crucible steel"), is invented in [ancient India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_India "History of India")
*   50s BC – Glassblowing techniques flourish in [Phoenicia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenicia "Phoenicia")
*   20s BC – [Roman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rome "Rome")[architect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Architecture_(built_environment) "Architecture (built environment)")[Vitruvius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitruvius "Vitruvius") describes low-water-content method for mixing [concrete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concrete "Concrete")

*   3rd century – [Cast iron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cast_iron "Cast iron") widely used in [Han dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty") China
*   300 – Greek alchemist Zomius, summarizing the work of Egyptian alchemists, describes arsenic and lead acetate[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)
*   4th century – [Iron pillar of Delhi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron_pillar_of_Delhi "Iron pillar of Delhi") is the oldest surviving example of corrosion-resistant steel
*   8th century – [Porcelain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porcelain "Porcelain") is invented in [Tang dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tang_dynasty "Tang dynasty") China
*   8th century – [Tin-glazing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tin-glazing "Tin-glazing") of [ceramics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceramic "Ceramic") invented by [Muslim chemists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alchemy_and_chemistry_in_Islam "Alchemy and chemistry in Islam") and [potters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_pottery "Islamic pottery") in [Basra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basra "Basra"), [Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq "Iraq")[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-Mason-2): 1 
*   9th century – [Stonepaste ceramics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stoneware "Stoneware") invented in [Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq "Iraq")[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-Mason-2): 5 
*   900 – First systematic classification of chemical substances appears in the works attributed to [Jābir ibn Ḥayyān](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jabir_ibn_Hayyan "Jabir ibn Hayyan") (Latin: Geber) and in those of the Persian alchemist and physician [Abū Bakr al-Rāzī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abu_Bakr_al-Razi "Abu Bakr al-Razi") (c. 865–925, Latin: Rhazes)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-3)
*   900 – [Synthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_synthesis "Chemical synthesis") of [ammonium chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonium_chloride "Ammonium chloride") from [organic substances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") described in the works attributed to Jābir ibn Ḥayyān (Latin: Geber)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-4)
*   900 – Abū Bakr al-Rāzī describes the preparation of plaster of Paris and metallic antimony[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)
*   9th century – [Lustreware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lustreware "Lustreware") appears in Mesopotamia[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-5): 86–87 

*   1000 – Gunpowder is developed in China[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1340 – In [Liège, Belgium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge "Liège"), the first blast furnaces for the production of iron are developed[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)
*   1448 – [Johann Gutenberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Gutenberg "Johann Gutenberg") develops [type metal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Type_metal "Type metal") alloy
*   1450s – [Cristallo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cristallo "Cristallo"), a clear soda-based glass, is invented by [Angelo Barovier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angelo_Barovier "Angelo Barovier")
*   [1540](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Category:1540s_in_science "Category:1540s in science") – [Vannoccio Biringuccio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vannoccio_Biringuccio "Vannoccio Biringuccio") publishes first systematic book on [metallurgy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metallurgy "Metallurgy")
*   [1556](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Category:1550s_in_science "Category:1550s in science") – [Georg Agricola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georg_Agricola "Georg Agricola")'s influential book on [metallurgy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metallurgy "Metallurgy")
*   [1590](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1590_in_science "1590 in science") – Glass [lenses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lens_(optics) "Lens (optics)") are developed in the Netherlands and used for the first time in [microscopes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microscope "Microscope") and [telescopes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telescope "Telescope")
*   1664 – In the pipes supplying water to the gardens at Versailles, cast iron is used[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)

*   1717 – [Abraham Darby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abraham_Darby_I "Abraham Darby I") makes iron with coke, a derivative of coal[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)
*   [1738](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1738_in_science "1738 in science") – Metallic [zinc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zinc "Zinc") processed by [distillation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distillation "Distillation") from [calamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calamine_(mineral) "Calamine (mineral)") and charcoal patented by [William Champion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Champion_(metallurgist) "William Champion (metallurgist)")
*   [1740](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1740_in_science "1740 in science") – [Crucible steel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crucible_steel "Crucible steel") technique developed by [Benjamin Huntsman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Huntsman "Benjamin Huntsman")
*   1774 – 
    *   Joseph Priestley discovers oxygen[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)
    *   Johann Gottlieb Gahn discovers manganese[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)
    *   Karl Wilhelm Scheele discovers chlorine[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_materials_technology#cite_note-:0-1)

*   [1779](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1779_in_science "1779 in science") – Hydraulic cement ([stucco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stucco "Stucco")) patented by [Bryan Higgins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bryan_Higgins "Bryan Higgins") for use as an exterior [plaster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plaster "Plaster")
*   [1799](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1799_in_science "1799 in science") – [Acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acid "Acid")[battery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battery_(electricity) "Battery (electricity)") made from copper/zinc by [Alessandro Volta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alessandro_Volta "Alessandro Volta")

*   [1821](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1821_in_science "1821 in science") – [Thermocouple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermocouple "Thermocouple") invented by [Thomas Johann Seebeck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Johann_Seebeck "Thomas Johann Seebeck")
*   [1824](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1824_in_science "1824 in science") – [Portland cement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portland_cement "Portland cement") patent issued to [Joseph Aspdin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Aspdin "Joseph Aspdin")
*   [1825](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1825_in_science "1825 in science") – Metallic [aluminum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminum "Aluminum") produced by [Hans Christian Ørsted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hans_Christian_%C3%98rsted "Hans Christian Ørsted")
*   [1839](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1839_in_science "1839 in science") – [Vulcanized rubber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulcanization "Vulcanization") invented by [Charles Goodyear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Goodyear "Charles Goodyear")
*   [1839](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1839_in_science "1839 in science") – [Silver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver "Silver")-based [photographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photography "Photography") processes invented by [Louis Daguerre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Daguerre "Louis Daguerre") and [William Fox Talbot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Fox_Talbot "William Fox Talbot")
*   [1855](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1855_in_science "1855 in science") – [Bessemer process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bessemer_process "Bessemer process") for mass production of steel patented by [Henry Bessemer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Bessemer "Henry Bessemer")
*   [1861](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1861_in_science "1861 in science") – Color photography demonstrated by [James Clerk Maxwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Clerk_Maxwell "James Clerk Maxwell")
*   [1883](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1883_in_science "1883 in science") – First [solar cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_cell "Solar cell") using [selenium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selenium "Selenium") waffles made by [Charles Fritts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Fritts "Charles Fritts")
*   1893 – [Thermite Welding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exothermic_welding "Exothermic welding") developed and soon used to weld rails

*   [1902](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1902_in_science "1902 in science") – [Synthetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_synthesis "Chemical synthesis")[rubies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruby "Ruby") created by the [Verneuil process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verneuil_process "Verneuil process") developed by [Auguste Verneuil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auguste_Verneuil "Auguste Verneuil")
*   [1908](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1908_in_science "1908 in science") – [Cellophane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellophane "Cellophane") invented by [Jacques E. Brandenberger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_E._Brandenberger "Jacques E. Brandenberger")
*   [1909](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1909_in_science "1909 in science") – [Bakelite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bakelite "Bakelite") hard thermosetting [plastic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plastic "Plastic") presented by [Leo Baekeland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_Baekeland "Leo Baekeland")
*   [1911](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1911_in_science "1911 in science") – [Superconductivity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superconductivity "Superconductivity") discovered by [Heike Kamerlingh Onnes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heike_Kamerlingh_Onnes "Heike Kamerlingh Onnes")
*   [1912](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1912_in_science "1912 in science") – [Stainless steel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stainless_steel "Stainless steel") invented by [Harry Brearley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Brearley "Harry Brearley")
*   [1916](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1916_in_science "1916 in science") – Method for growing single [crystals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystal "Crystal") of [metals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal "Metal") invented by [Jan Czochralski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jan_Czochralski "Jan Czochralski")
*   1919 – The merchant ship [Fullagar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cammell_Laird "Cammell Laird") has the first all [welded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welding "Welding") hull.
*   [1924](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1924_in_science "1924 in science") – [Pyrex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrex "Pyrex") invented by scientists at [Corning Incorporated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corning_Glass_Works "Corning Glass Works"), a glass with a very low [coefficient of thermal expansion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coefficient_of_thermal_expansion "Coefficient of thermal expansion")
*   [1931](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1931_in_science "1931 in science") – synthetic [rubber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rubber "Rubber") called [neoprene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neoprene "Neoprene") developed by [Julius Nieuwland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julius_Nieuwland "Julius Nieuwland") (_see also: [E.K. Bolton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E.K.\_Bolton "E.K. Bolton"), [Wallace Carothers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wallace\_Carothers "Wallace Carothers")_)
*   [1931](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1931_in_science "1931 in science") – [Nylon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nylon "Nylon") developed by [Wallace Carothers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wallace_Carothers "Wallace Carothers")
*   [1935](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1935_in_science "1935 in science") – [Langmuir–Blodgett film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Langmuir%E2%80%93Blodgett_film "Langmuir–Blodgett film") coating of glass was developed by [Katharine Burr Blodgett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katharine_Burr_Blodgett "Katharine Burr Blodgett"), creating "invisible glass" which is >99% transmissive
*   [1938](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1938_in_science "1938 in science") – The process for making poly-tetrafluoroethylene, better known as [Teflon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polytetrafluoroethylene "Polytetrafluoroethylene") discovered by [Roy Plunkett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roy_J._Plunkett "Roy J. Plunkett")
*   [1939](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1939_in_science "1939 in science") – Dislocations in metals confirmed by [Robert W. Cahn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_W._Cahn "Robert W. Cahn")
*   [1947](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1947_in_science "1947 in science") – First [germanium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germanium "Germanium")[point-contact transistor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point-contact_transistor "Point-contact transistor") invented
*   [1947](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1947_in_science "1947 in science") – First commercial application of a [piezoelectric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piezoelectricity "Piezoelectricity")[ceramic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceramic "Ceramic"): [barium titanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barium_titanate "Barium titanate") used as a [phonograph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phonograph "Phonograph") pickup
*   [1951](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1951_in_science "1951 in science") – Individual [atoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atom "Atom") seen for the first time using the [field ion microscope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Field_ion_microscope "Field ion microscope")
*   [1953](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1953_in_science "1953 in science") – Metallic [catalysts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalyst "Catalyst") which greatly improve the strength of [polyethylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyethylene "Polyethylene")[polymers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymer "Polymer") discovered by [Karl Ziegler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_Ziegler "Karl Ziegler")
*   [1954](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1954_in_science "1954 in science") – [Silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon "Silicon")[solar cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_cell "Solar cell") with 6% efficiency made at [Bell Laboratories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bell_Labs "Bell Labs")
*   [1954](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1954_in_science "1954 in science") – [Argon oxygen decarburization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argon_oxygen_decarburization "Argon oxygen decarburization") (AOD) refining invented by scientists at the [Union Carbide Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_Carbide_Corporation "Union Carbide Corporation")
*   [1959](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1959_in_science "1959 in science") – [Float glass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Float_glass "Float glass") process patented by the [Pilkington Brothers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pilkington_Brothers "Pilkington Brothers")
*   [1962](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1962_in_science "1962 in science") – [SQUID](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SQUID "SQUID") superconducting quantum interference device invented
*   [1966](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1966_in_science "1966 in science") – [Stephanie Kwolek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephanie_Kwolek "Stephanie Kwolek") invented a fibre that would later become known as [Kevlar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kevlar "Kevlar")
*   [1968](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1968_in_science "1968 in science") – [Liquid crystal display](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_crystal_display "Liquid crystal display") developed by [RCA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RCA "RCA")
*   [1970](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1970_in_science "1970 in science") – Silica [optical fibers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_fiber "Optical fiber") grown by [Corning Incorporated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corning_Incorporated "Corning Incorporated")
*   [1980](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1980_in_science "1980 in science") – [Duplex stainless steels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duplex_stainless_steel "Duplex stainless steel") developed which resist oxidation in chlorides
*   [1984](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1984_in_science "1984 in science") – [Fold-forming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fold-forming "Fold-forming") system developed by Charles Lewton-Brain to produce complex three dimensional forms rapidly from sheet metal
*   [1985](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1985_in_science "1985 in science") – The first [fullerene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullerene "Fullerene") molecule discovered by scientists at [Rice University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rice_University "Rice University") (_see also:_[Timeline of carbon nanotubes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_carbon_nanotubes "Timeline of carbon nanotubes"))
*   [1986](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1986_in_science "1986 in science") – The first [high temperature superconductor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High_temperature_superconductor "High temperature superconductor") is discovered by [Georg Bednorz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georg_Bednorz "Georg Bednorz") and [K. Alex Müller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K._Alex_M%C3%BCller "K. Alex Müller")
