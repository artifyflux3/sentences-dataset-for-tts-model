Macauley Charles Raymond Macauley (March 19, 1871 – November 24, 1934) was an American cartoonist and illustrator. He was also involved in the film business.

[ 1 ] Born in Canton, Ohio , he worked as a freelance illustrator and as staff cartoonist for newspapers including the Cleveland World , New York World , New York Daily Mirror , New York Evening Graphic , and Brooklyn Daily Eagle . He received the 1930 Pulitzer Prize for Editorial Cartooning for his 1929 cartoon "Paying for a Dead Horse".

[ 2 ] [ 3 ] He got into filmmaking in support of Woodrow Wilson's campaigns in 1912 and 1916. He also made a film in support of his League of Nations plan. After leaving the New York World towards the end of World War I he again got into filmmaking launching his owm film company.

[ 4 ] In 1919, an advice column in United States Investor warned the company and filmmaking businesses in general were risky and highly speculative.
