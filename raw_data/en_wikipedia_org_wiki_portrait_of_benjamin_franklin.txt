Portrait of Benjamin Franklin is a 1767 portrait painting by the Scottish artist David Martin of the American politician and inventor Benjamin Franklin . It was painted during his lengthy residence in London when he was acting as colonial agent for Pennsylvania , Georgia , New Jersey , and Massachusetts .

[ 1 ] The work was commissioned by the Edinburgh merchant Robert Alexander, a friend of Franklin.

[ 2 ] He is shown seated next to a bust of the scientist Isaac Newton .

[ 3 ] The painting was displayed at the Exhibition of 1767 held by the Society of Artists held at Spring Gardens . Today it is part of the collection of the White House .
