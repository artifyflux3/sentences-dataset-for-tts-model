[ 1 ] He is currently (i.e., 2020) Professor Emeritus at the University of New South Wales .

[ 2 ] Education [ edit ] Sweller was educated at the University of Adelaide where he received a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1969 [ citation needed ] followed by a PhD from the department of psychology in 1972. His doctoral research investigated effects of discrimination training on subsequent shift learning in animals.

Career and research [ edit ] Sweller has authored more than eighty academic publications, mainly reporting research on cognitive factors in instructional design , [ 3 ] with specific emphasis on the instructional implications of working memory limitations and their consequences for instructional procedures.

[ 4 ] [ 5 ] [ 6 ] [ 7 ] Awards and honours [ edit ] Sweller was elected a Fellow of the Academy of the Social Sciences in Australia (FASSA) in 1993.
