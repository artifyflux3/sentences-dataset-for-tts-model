**Nader El-Bizri** ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language "Arabic language"): نادر البزري, _nādir al-bizrĩ_) served as the dean of the College of Arts, Humanities, and Social Sciences at the [University of Sharjah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Sharjah "University of Sharjah").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-1) He is currently a Senior Research Fellow at [The Warburg Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Warburg_Institute "The Warburg Institute") at the School of Advanced Study, University of London,[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-2) and an Affiliated Scholar with the Department of History and Philosophy of Science at the [University of Cambridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Cambridge "University of Cambridge").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-3) He was before that a tenured longstanding full professor of philosophy and civilization studies at the [American University of Beirut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_University_of_Beirut "American University of Beirut"), where he also acted as an associate dean of the Faculty of Arts and Sciences, and as the director of the General Education program.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-aubfas-4) El-Bizri specializes in [phenomenology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenomenology_(philosophy) "Phenomenology (philosophy)"), [Islamic science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world "Science in the medieval Islamic world") and [philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_philosophy "Arab philosophy"), and [architectural theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Architectural_theory "Architectural theory"). He is the author or editor of several books, including _The Phenomenological Quest between Avicenna and Heidegger_ (2000).

El-Bizri received a degree in architecture from the [Harvard Graduate School of Design](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_Graduate_School_of_Design "Harvard Graduate School of Design") and a PhD in philosophy from the [New School for Social Research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_School_for_Social_Research "New School for Social Research").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-5)

[![Image 2](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/AUB_1.jpg/330px-AUB_1.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:AUB_1.jpg)

[American University of Beirut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_University_of_Beirut "American University of Beirut")

El-Bizri is a professor of philosophy and civilization studies at the [American University of Beirut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_University_of_Beirut "American University of Beirut"), where he is also an associate dean of the faculty of arts and sciences, and the director of the general education program. He was formerly a director of the civilization studies program at the AUB and the coordinator of [Islamic studies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_studies "Islamic studies"),[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-aubfas-4) and also oversees the Anis Makdisi Program in Literature.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-6) He was previously a visiting professor of visual studies at the [University of Lincoln](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Lincoln "University of Lincoln") and a principal lecturer (reader) in architecture.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-7) He was an affiliated senior research fellow in philosophy at the [Institute of Ismaili Studies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institute_of_Ismaili_Studies "Institute of Ismaili Studies"), London, and is general editor (and managing editor _ex officio_) of an [Oxford University Press](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxford_University_Press "Oxford University Press") book series published in association with the Institute of Ismaili Studies, London, entitled: _Epistles of the [Brethren of Purity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brethren\_of\_Purity "Brethren of Purity") Series_ رسائل إخوان الصفاء .[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-8) He was a co-manager of a joint project between the Institute of Ismaili Studies and the [Institut Français du Proche Orient](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institut_fran%C3%A7ais_du_Proche-Orient "Institut français du Proche-Orient"), formerly based in [Damascus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damascus "Damascus") and now in [Beirut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beirut "Beirut").

El-Bizri was an affiliated research scholar in the [department of history and philosophy of science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Department_of_History_and_Philosophy_of_Science,_University_of_Cambridge "Department of History and Philosophy of Science, University of Cambridge") at the [University of Cambridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Cambridge "University of Cambridge") from 1999 to 2010, primarily lecturing on medieval Islamic sciences and philosophy.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-HPS2009-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-10) He has also been a visiting lecturer at the faculty of architecture at the University of Cambridge on Islamic architecture. He holds a _Chercheur associé_ position in history of science and philosophy at the [Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centre_National_de_la_Recherche_Scientifique "Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique") in Paris (Denis-Diderot, Paris VII, UMR 7219, SPHERE)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-11) since 2006, and he was previously a lecturer in architecture at the [University of Nottingham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Nottingham "University of Nottingham"), and taught at [Harvard University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_University "Harvard University").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-12) He is a member of the Ordered Universe research team on mediaeval science that is supported by the British [Arts and Humanities Research Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arts_and_Humanities_Research_Council "Arts and Humanities Research Council") (AHRC), which is based at the University of Durham and Oxford University.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-13)[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-autogenerated2-14) El-Bizri is an elected member of the Steering Committee of the Société Internationale d'Histoire des Sciences et des Philosophies Arabes et Islamiques (_Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique_, Paris).[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-chspam-15) 2022 Dean of College of Arts, Humanities & Social Sciences University of Sharjah

### Writing, television

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nader_El-Bizri&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Writing, television")]

El-Bizri is co-editor of a book series in phenomenology (Springer, formerly Kluwer Academic Publishers; Dordrecht, Netherlands),[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-springer1-16) and as section editor (Islam) of the _Encyclopedia of Sciences and Religions_ (SPRINGER, Dordrecht & Berlin). He is on the editorial boards of series and journals, such as the [Cambridge University Press](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambridge_University_Press "Cambridge University Press") journal _Arabic Sciences and Philosophy_, _Toposophia_ (series on philosophy and architecture; Lexington Books/Rowman & Littlefield),[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-17) Institute of Ismaili Studies _Texts and Translations Series_ (London: I. B. Tauris),[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-18) the _Encyclopaedia Islamica_ (Leiden: E. J. Brill),[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-19) the _Journal of World Philosophies_ (Indiana University Press),[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-20)[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-21) and the Fundamentals of Scientific Research at The Arab Organization for Translation in Beirut (linked to the Centre for Arab Unity Studies).[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-22) He is the European representative of the Equipe d’Etude et de Recherche sur le Patrimoine Scientifique Arabe, which is part of the Lebanese Centre national de la recherche scientifique (CNRS). He has written for _[Al-Hayat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Hayat "Al-Hayat")_.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-HPS2009-9)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-chspam-15)[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-23)

El-Bizri has made several contributions to BBC radio and television cultural programs, including a discussion about the Persian philosopher [Avicenna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avicenna "Avicenna") with [Melvin Bragg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melvin_Bragg "Melvin Bragg") and other guests on [BBC Radio 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Radio_4 "BBC Radio 4")'s _In Our Time_,[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-24) and an interview with [Jim Al-Khalili](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jim_Al-Khalili "Jim Al-Khalili") about [Ibn al-Haytham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibn_al-Haytham "Ibn al-Haytham") for [BBC Four](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Four "BBC Four")'s _Science and Islam_ series.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-25) He also recently took part in a panel on philosophy that was aired on [France Culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France_Culture "France Culture") in Paris in the series _[Les Chemins de la philosophie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Les\_Chemins\_de\_la\_philosophie "Les Chemins de la philosophie")_ that is moderated by [Adèle Van Reeth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ad%C3%A8le_Van_Reeth "Adèle Van Reeth").[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-26)[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-27)

El-Bizri has received several awards, including the Kuwait Prize 2014 from the Kuwait Foundation for the Advancement of Sciences.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-28) He has been also ranked as one of the "Thought Leaders" in the Arab world via the Berggruen Institute and its various partners.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-29)[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_El-Bizri#cite_note-30)
