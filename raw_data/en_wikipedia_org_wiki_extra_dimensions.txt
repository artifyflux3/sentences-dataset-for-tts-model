In [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics"), **extra dimensions** are proposed additional [space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space "Space") or [time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time "Time")[dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimension "Dimension") beyond the (3+1) typical of observed [spacetime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacetime "Spacetime"), such as the first attempts based on the [Kaluza–Klein theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaluza%E2%80%93Klein_theory "Kaluza–Klein theory"). Among theories proposing extra dimensions are:[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extra_dimensions#cite_note-Rizzo-1)

*   [Large extra dimension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_extra_dimension "Large extra dimension"), mostly motivated by the ADD model, by [Nima Arkani-Hamed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nima_Arkani-Hamed "Nima Arkani-Hamed"), [Savas Dimopoulos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Savas_Dimopoulos "Savas Dimopoulos"), and [Gia Dvali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gia_Dvali "Gia Dvali") in 1998, in an attempt to solve the [hierarchy problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hierarchy_problem "Hierarchy problem"). This theory requires that the fields of the [Standard Model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_Model "Standard Model") are confined to a four-dimensional [membrane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Membrane_(M-Theory) "Membrane (M-Theory)"), while gravity propagates in several additional spatial dimensions that are large compared to the [Planck scale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck_scale "Planck scale").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extra_dimensions#cite_note-2)
*   Warped extra dimensions, such as those proposed by the [Randall–Sundrum model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randall%E2%80%93Sundrum_model "Randall–Sundrum model") (RS), based on [warped geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warped_geometry "Warped geometry") where the [universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universe "Universe") is a five-dimensional [anti-de Sitter space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-de_Sitter_space "Anti-de Sitter space") and the [elementary particles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elementary_particles "Elementary particles") except for the [graviton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graviton "Graviton") are localized on a (3+1)-dimensional [brane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Membrane_(M-theory) "Membrane (M-theory)") or branes.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extra_dimensions#cite_note-RS-3)
*   [Universal extra dimension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_extra_dimension "Universal extra dimension"), proposed and first studied in 2000, assume, at variance with the ADD and RS approaches, that all fields propagate universally in extra dimensions.
*   [Multiple time dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_time_dimensions "Multiple time dimensions"), i.e. the possibility that there might be more than one dimension of [time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time "Time"), has occasionally been discussed in physics and philosophy, although those models have to deal with the problem of [causality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Causality "Causality").
