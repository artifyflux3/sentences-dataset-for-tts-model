Chemical compound

The **hexatriynyl radical**, C 6 H, is an organic [radical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_(chemistry) "Radical (chemistry)") molecule consisting of a [linear chain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_molecular_geometry "Linear molecular geometry") of six [carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon") atoms terminated by a [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen") (H‚àíC‚â°C‚àíC‚â°C‚àíC‚â°C‚Ä¢). The [unpaired electron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unpaired_electron "Unpaired electron") is located at the opposite end to the hydrogen atom, as indicated. Both experimental work and computer simulations on this species was done in the early 1990s.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexatriynyl_radical#cite_note-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexatriynyl_radical#cite_note-2)

Synthesis of the radical
------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hexatriynyl_radical&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Synthesis of the radical")]

The radical can be synthesized by [photolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photolysis "Photolysis"). Two different examples involve

*   Argon gas and [buta-1,3-diene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buta-1,3-diene "Buta-1,3-diene") in a mole ratio of 1500:1
*   Argon gas and [acetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetylene "Acetylene") in a mole ratio of 100:1

Hexatriyne anion
----------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hexatriynyl_radical&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Hexatriyne anion")]

In 2006 the negatively charged hexatriyne anion of this molecule, C 6 H‚àí, was the first negatively charged ion to be discovered to exist in the [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium"), using the [Green Bank Telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Bank_Telescope "Green Bank Telescope").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexatriynyl_radical#cite_note-3) Negative ions were thought to be unstable in this environment due to the prevalence of [ultraviolet light](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet_light "Ultraviolet light"), which dislodges extra electrons such as this.

### Synthesis of the anion

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hexatriynyl_radical&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Synthesis of the anion")]

The laboratory synthesis starts from [acetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetylene "Acetylene") C 2 H 2. The reaction takes place within a DC discharge at reduced pressure in a mixture with 15% [argon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argon "Argon"). The product is observed by millimeter-wave spectroscopy.

### Analogous species

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hexatriynyl_radical&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Analogous species")]

The two species C 4 H‚àí and [C 8 H](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octatetraynyl_radical "Octatetraynyl radical")‚àí have also been detected.
