A lump of [graphite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphite "Graphite"), a graphene [transistor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transistor "Transistor"), and a [tape dispenser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tape_dispenser "Tape dispenser"), a tool that was used for the exfolitation of single-layer graphene from graphite in 2004. Donated to the [Nobel Museum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Museum "Nobel Museum") in Stockholm by [Andre Geim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andre_Geim "Andre Geim") and [Konstantin Novoselov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Konstantin_Novoselov "Konstantin Novoselov") in 2010.

Single-layer **[graphene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphene "Graphene")** was first unambiguously produced and identified in 2004, by the group of [Andre Geim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andre_Geim "Andre Geim") and [Konstantin Novoselov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Konstantin_Novoselov "Konstantin Novoselov"), though they credit [Hanns-Peter Boehm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanns-Peter_Boehm "Hanns-Peter Boehm") and his co-workers for the experimental discovery of graphene in 1962; while it had been explored theoretically by [P. R. Wallace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/P._R._Wallace "P. R. Wallace") in 1947.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-credit-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-2) Boehm et al. introduced the term graphene in 1986.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-brit-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-name-4)

In 1859, [Benjamin Collins Brodie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sir_Benjamin_Collins_Brodie,_2nd_Baronet "Sir Benjamin Collins Brodie, 2nd Baronet") became aware of the highly [lamellar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lamella_(materials) "Lamella (materials)") structure of thermally reduced [graphite oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphite_oxide "Graphite oxide").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-6)

The structure of graphite was identified in 1916[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-7) by the related method of [powder diffraction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Powder_diffraction "Powder diffraction").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-8) It was studied in detail by Kohlschütter and Haenni in 1918, who described the properties of [graphite oxide paper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphene_oxide_paper "Graphene oxide paper").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-Kohlschuttler1918-9) Its structure was determined from single-crystal diffraction in 1924.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-10)

The theory of graphene was first explored by [P. R. Wallace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/P._R._Wallace "P. R. Wallace") in 1947 as a starting point for understanding the electronic properties of 3D graphite.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-brit-3)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-graph-11) The emergent massless Dirac equation was first pointed out by [Gordon W. Semenoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gordon_Walter_Semenoff "Gordon Walter Semenoff"), [David DiVincenzo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_DiVincenzo "David DiVincenzo") and [Eugene J. Mele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_J._Mele "Eugene J. Mele").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-devincenzo-12) Semenoff emphasized the occurrence in a magnetic field of an electronic [Landau level](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landau_level "Landau level") precisely at the Dirac point. This level is responsible for the anomalous integer [quantum Hall effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_Hall_effect "Quantum Hall effect").[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-2dgasDiracFermions-13)[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-Gusynin-14)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-Berry'sPhase-15)

The earliest TEM images of few-layer graphite were published by G. Ruess and F. Vogt in 1948.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-RuessTEM-16) Later, single graphene layers were observed directly by electron microscopy.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-Meyer07-17) Before 2004 intercalated graphite compounds were studied under a [transmission electron microscope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transmission_electron_microscopy "Transmission electron microscopy") (TEM). Researchers occasionally observed thin graphitic flakes ("few-layer graphene") and possibly even individual layers. An early, detailed study on few-layer graphite dates to 1962 when Boehm reported producing monolayer flakes of reduced graphene oxide.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-Boehm-18)[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-19)[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-GroxTEM-20)[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-21)

Starting in the 1970s single layers of graphite were grown epitaxially on top of other materials.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-Oshima97-22) This "epitaxial graphene" consists of a single-atom-thick hexagonal lattice of [sp 2-bonded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sp2_bond "Sp2 bond") carbon atoms, as in free-standing graphene. However, significant charge transfers from the substrate to the epitaxial graphene, and in some cases, the [d-orbitals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D-orbital "D-orbital") of the substrate atoms hybridize with the [π orbitals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pi_orbital "Pi orbital") of graphene, which significantly alters the electronic structure of epitaxial graphene.

Single layers of graphite were observed by TEM within bulk materials, in particular inside soot obtained by chemical exfoliation. Efforts to make thin films of graphite by mechanical exfoliation started in 1990,[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-SciAm-23) but nothing thinner than 50 to 100 layers was produced before 2004.

The term _graphene_ was introduced in 1986 by chemists [Hanns-Peter Boehm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanns-Peter_Boehm "Hanns-Peter Boehm"), [Ralph Setton](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Setton&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ralph Setton (page does not exist)") and [Eberhard Stumpp](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eberhard_Stumpp&action=edit&redlink=1 "Eberhard Stumpp (page does not exist)"). It is a combination of the word _graphite_ and the suffix _-ene_, referring to [polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-brit-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-name-4)

Initial attempts to make atomically thin graphitic films employed exfoliation techniques similar to the drawing method. Multilayer samples down to 10 nm in thickness were obtained. Earlier researchers tried to isolate graphene starting with intercalated compounds, producing very thin graphitic fragments (possibly monolayers).[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-GroxTEM-20) Neither of the earlier observations was sufficient to launch the "graphene gold rush" that awaited macroscopic samples of extracted atomic planes.

One of the first patents pertaining to the production of graphene was filed in October 2002 and granted in 2006.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-Patent_7071258-24) It detailed one of the first large scale graphene production processes. Two years later, in 2004 Geim and Novoselov extracted single-atom-thick crystallites from bulk graphite.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-:2-25) They pulled graphene layers from graphite and transferred them onto thin [silicon dioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon_dioxide "Silicon dioxide") (SiO

2) on a silicon wafer in a process called either micromechanical cleavage or the [Scotch tape](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scotch_tape "Scotch tape") technique.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-The_Story_of_Graphene-26) The SiO

2 electrically isolated the graphene and weakly interacted with it, providing nearly charge-neutral graphene layers. The silicon beneath the SiO

2 could be used as a "back gate" electrode to vary the charge density in the graphene over a wide range. [US patent](https://worldwide.espacenet.com/textdoc?DB=EPODOC&IDX=USpatent) 6667100, filed in 2002, describes how to process expanded graphite to achieve a graphite thickness of one hundred-thousandth of an inch (0.25 nm). The key to success was high-throughput visual recognition of graphene on a properly chosen substrate that provides a small but noticeable optical contrast.

The cleavage technique led directly to the first observation of the anomalous [quantum Hall effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_Hall_effect "Quantum Hall effect") in graphene,[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-2dgasDiracFermions-13)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-Berry'sPhase-15) which provided direct evidence of graphene's theoretically predicted [Berry's phase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berry%27s_phase "Berry's phase") of massless [Dirac fermions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dirac_fermion "Dirac fermion"). The effect was reported by Geim's group and by [Kim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_Kim_(physicist) "Philip Kim (physicist)") and [Zhang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuanbo_Zhang "Yuanbo Zhang"), whose papers[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-2dgasDiracFermions-13)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-Berry'sPhase-15) appeared in _[Nature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nature\_(journal) "Nature (journal)")_ in 2005. Before these experiments other researchers had looked for the quantum Hall effect[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-27) and Dirac fermions[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-28) in bulk graphite.

Geim and Novoselov received awards for their pioneering research on graphene, notably the 2010 [Nobel Prize in Physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics "Nobel Prize in Physics").[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-29)

In 2014, the [National Graphene Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Graphene_Institute "National Graphene Institute") was announced to support applied research and development in partnership with other research organizations and industry.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-30)

Commercialization of graphene proceeded rapidly once commercial scale production was demonstrated. In 2014 two [North East England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_East_England "North East England") commercial manufacturers, Applied Graphene Materials[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-31) and Thomas Swan Limited[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-32) (with Trinity College, Dublin researchers),[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-33) began manufacturing. In [East Anglia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Anglia "East Anglia")[Cambridge Nanosystems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambridge_Nanosystems "Cambridge Nanosystems")[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-34)[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-35)[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-36) operates a graphene powder production facility. By 2017, 13 years after creation of the first laboratory graphene electronic device, an integrated graphene electronics chip was produced commercially and marketed to pharmaceutical researchers by Nanomedical Diagnostics in San Diego.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_graphene#cite_note-:5-37)
