Type of application software

[![Image 6](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Xfig_screenshot.png/250px-Xfig_screenshot.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Xfig_screenshot.png)

A screenshot of the [xfig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xfig "Xfig") vector graphic editor

A **vector graphic editor** is a [computer program](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_program "Computer program") that enables its users to create, compose and edit images with the use of mathematical and geometrical commands rather than individual pixels. This software is used in creating high-definition [vector graphic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_graphics "Vector graphics")[images](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Image "Image") that can be scaled indefinitely without losing their quality. The output is saved in vector graphic formats, such as [EPS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Encapsulated_PostScript "Encapsulated PostScript"), [ODG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenDocument "OpenDocument"), or [SVG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scalable_Vector_Graphics "Scalable Vector Graphics").

Vector editors versus bitmap editors
------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vector_graphics_editor&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Vector editors versus bitmap editors")]

Vector editors are often contrasted with [bitmap editors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bitmap_graphics_editor "Bitmap graphics editor"), and their capabilities complement each other. Vector editors are often better for [page layout](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Page_layout "Page layout"), [typography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typography "Typography"), [logos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logo "Logo"), sharp-edged artistic [illustrations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illustration "Illustration") (e.g. [cartoons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartoons "Cartoons"), [clip art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clip_art "Clip art"), complex geometric patterns), [technical illustrations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_illustration "Technical illustration"), [diagramming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diagram "Diagram") and [flowcharting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flow_chart "Flow chart"). Bitmap editors are more suitable for retouching, photo processing, [photorealistic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photorealistic "Photorealistic") illustrations, [collage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collage "Collage"), and illustrations drawn by hand with a [pen tablet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pen_tablet "Pen tablet").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_graphics_editor#cite_note-1) Recent versions of bitmap editors such as [GIMP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GIMP "GIMP") and [Adobe Photoshop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photoshop "Photoshop") support vector tools (e.g. editable paths), and vector editors have adopted raster effects that were once limited to bitmap editors (e.g. blurring).[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_graphics_editor#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_graphics_editor#cite_note-3)

Specialized features
--------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vector_graphics_editor&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Specialized features")]

Some vector editors support [animation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animation "Animation"), while others (e.g. [Synfig Studio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synfig_Studio "Synfig Studio")) are specifically geared towards producing [animated graphics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animated_graphics "Animated graphics"). Generally, vector graphics are more suitable for animation, though there are raster-based animation tools as well.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_graphics_editor#cite_note-4)

[3D computer graphics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_computer_graphics "3D computer graphics") software such as [Maya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_(software) "Maya (software)"), [Blender](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blender_(software) "Blender (software)") or [Autodesk 3ds Max](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_Studio_Max "3D Studio Max") can also be thought of as an extension of the traditional 2D vector editors, as they share some common concepts and tools.
