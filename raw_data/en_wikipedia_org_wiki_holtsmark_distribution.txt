The Holtsmark distribution is a special case of a stable distribution with the index of stability or shape parameter α α {\displaystyle \alpha } equal to 3/2 and the skewness parameter β β {\displaystyle \beta } of zero. Since β β {\displaystyle \beta } equals zero, the distribution is symmetric, and thus an example of a symmetric alpha-stable distribution. The Holtsmark distribution is one of the few examples of a stable distribution for which a closed form expression of the probability density function is known. However, its probability density function is not expressible in terms of elementary functions ; rather, the probability density function is expressed in terms of hypergeometric functions .

The Holtsmark distribution has applications in plasma physics and astrophysics.

[ 1 ] In 1919, Norwegian physicist Johan Peter Holtsmark proposed the distribution as a model for the fluctuating fields in plasma due to the motion of charged particles.

[ 2 ] It is also applicable to other types of Coulomb forces, in particular to modeling of gravitating bodies, and thus is important in astrophysics.

[ 3 ] [ 4 ] Characteristic function [ edit ] The characteristic function of a symmetric stable distribution is: φ φ ( t ; μ μ , c ) = exp ⁡ ⁡ [ i t μ μ − − | c t | α α ] , {\displaystyle \varphi (t;\mu ,c)=\exp \left[~it\mu \!-\!\left|ct\right|^{\alpha }\right],} where α α {\displaystyle \alpha } is the shape parameter, or index of stability, μ μ {\displaystyle \mu } is the location parameter , and c is the scale parameter .

Since the Holtsmark distribution has α α = 3 / 2 , {\displaystyle \alpha =3/2,} its characteristic function is: [ 5 ] φ φ ( t ; μ μ , c ) = exp ⁡ ⁡ [ i t μ μ − − | c t | 3 / 2 ] .

{\displaystyle \varphi (t;\mu ,c)=\exp \left[~it\mu \!-\!\left|ct\right|^{3/2}\right].} Since the Holtsmark distribution is a stable distribution with α > 1 , μ μ {\displaystyle \mu } represents the mean of the distribution.

[ 6 ] [ 7 ] Since β = 0 , μ μ {\displaystyle \mu } also represents the median and mode of the distribution. And since α < 2 , the variance of the Holtsmark distribution is infinite.

[ 6 ] All higher moments of the distribution are also infinite.

[ 6 ] Like other stable distributions (other than the normal distribution), since the variance is infinite the dispersion in the distribution is reflected by the scale parameter , c. An alternate approach to describing the dispersion of the distribution is through fractional moments.
