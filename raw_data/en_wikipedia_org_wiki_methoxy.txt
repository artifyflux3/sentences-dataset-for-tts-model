From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

(Redirected from [Methoxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methoxy&redirect=no "Methoxy"))

Chemical group (–OCH3)

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Methoxy_group.svg/250px-Methoxy_group.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Methoxy_group.svg)

The structure of a typical methoxy group

In [organic chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_chemistry "Organic chemistry"), a **methoxy group** is the [functional group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_group "Functional group") consisting of a [methyl group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_group "Methyl group") bound to [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen"). This [alkoxy group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkoxy_group "Alkoxy group") has the formula R−O−CH 3.

On a [benzene ring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzene_ring "Benzene ring"), the [Hammett equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hammett_equation "Hammett equation") classifies a methoxy substituent at the _para_ position as an [electron-donating group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron-donating_group "Electron-donating group"), but as an [electron-withdrawing group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron-withdrawing_group "Electron-withdrawing group") if at the _meta_ position. At the _ortho_ position, [steric effects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steric_effects "Steric effects") are likely to cause a significant alteration in the Hammett equation prediction, which otherwise follows the same trend as that of the _para_ position.

Occurrence
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methoxy_group&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Occurrence")]

The simplest of methoxy compounds are [methanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanol "Methanol") and [dimethyl ether](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether "Dimethyl ether"). Other methoxy ethers include [anisole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anisole "Anisole") and [vanillin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanillin "Vanillin"). Many metal alkoxides contain methoxy groups, such as [tetramethyl orthosilicate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetramethyl_orthosilicate "Tetramethyl orthosilicate") and [titanium methoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium_methoxide "Titanium methoxide"). [Esters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ester "Ester") with a methoxy group can be referred to as methyl esters, and the —COOCH 3 substituent is called a methoxycarbonyl.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methoxy#cite_note-1)

### Biosynthesis

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methoxy_group&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Biosynthesis")]

In nature, methoxy groups are found on nucleosides subjected to [2′-_O_-methylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2%27-O-methylation "2'-O-methylation"), for example, in variations of the [5′-cap structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Five-prime_cap "Five-prime cap") known as cap-1 and cap-2. They are also common substituents in [_O_-methylated flavonoids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/O-methylated_flavonoid "O-methylated flavonoid"), whose formation is catalyzed by [O-methyltransferases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/O-methyltransferase "O-methyltransferase") that act on [phenols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenol "Phenol"), such as [catechol-_O_-methyl transferase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catechol-O-methyl_transferase "Catechol-O-methyl transferase") (COMT). Many natural products in plants, such as [lignins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lignin "Lignin"), are generated via catalysis by [caffeoyl-CoA _O_-methyltransferase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caffeoyl-CoA_O-methyltransferase "Caffeoyl-CoA O-methyltransferase").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methoxy#cite_note-2)

### Methoxylation

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methoxy_group&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Methoxylation")]

Organic methoxides are often produced by [methylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylation "Methylation") of alkoxides.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methoxy#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methoxy#cite_note-4) Some aryl methoxides can be synthesized by metal-catalyzed methylation of [phenols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenol "Phenol"), or by methoxylation of [aryl halides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aryl_halide "Aryl halide").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methoxy#cite_note-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methoxy#cite_note-6)
