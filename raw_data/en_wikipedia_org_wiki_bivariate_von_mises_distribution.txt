In probability theory and statistics , the bivariate von Mises distribution is a probability distribution describing values on a torus . It may be thought of as an analogue on the torus of the bivariate normal distribution . The distribution belongs to the field of directional statistics . The general bivariate von Mises distribution was first proposed by Kanti Mardia in 1975.

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] One of its variants is today used in the field of bioinformatics to formulate a probabilistic model of protein structure in atomic detail, [ 3 ] [ 4 ] such as backbone-dependent rotamer libraries .

Definition [ edit ] The bivariate von Mises distribution is a probability distribution defined on the torus , S 1 × × S 1 {\displaystyle S^{1}\times S^{1}} in R 3 {\displaystyle \mathbb {R} ^{3}} .

Two commonly used variants of the bivariate von Mises distribution are the sine and cosine variant.
