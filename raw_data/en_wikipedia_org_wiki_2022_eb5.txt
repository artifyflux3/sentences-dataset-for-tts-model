Discovery date 11 March 2022 Designations MPC designation 2022 EB 5 Alternative designations Sar2593 [ 3 ] Minor planet category NEO [ a ] · Apollo [ 6 ] [ 1 ] Orbital characteristics [ 6 ] Epoch 11 March 2022 ( JD 2459649.5) Uncertainty parameter 6 Observation arc 1.9 hours [ 1 ] Aphelion 4.772 AU Perihelion 0.888 AU Semi-major axis 2.830 AU Eccentricity 0.6863 Orbital period (sidereal) 4.76 yr (1,738 days) Mean anomaly 353.614 ° Mean motion 0° 12 m 25.472 s / day Inclination 10.422° Longitude of ascending node 350.992° Time of perihelion July 2017 (last perihelion) Argument of perihelion 222.416° Earth MOID 3717 km Jupiter MOID 0.661 AU Physical characteristics Mean diameter 2 m [ 4 ] Absolute magnitude (H) 31.33 ± 0.35 [ 6 ] 2022 EB 5 was a small, two-metre Apollo near-Earth asteroid that disintegrated in Earth's atmosphere at 21:22 UTC on 11 March 2022, over the Arctic Ocean southwest of the Norwegian island Jan Mayen . With an atmospheric entry speed of 18 km/s (11 mi/s), the asteroid's impact generated a 4-kiloton-equivalent fireball that was detected by infrasound from Greenland and Norway.

[ 7 ] [ 8 ] A bright flash possibly associated with the event was reported by observers from Northern Iceland .

[ 8 ] It was discovered by astronomer Krisztián Sárneczky at Konkoly Observatory 's Piszkéstető Station in Mátra Mountains , Hungary about two hours before impact.

[ 2 ] [ 8 ] 2022 EB 5 is the fifth asteroid discovered before impacting Earth.

[ 8 ] [ 4 ] It was briefly listed on the Minor Planet Center 's Near-Earth Object Confirmation Page under the temporary designation Sar2593 .
