From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

3D coordinate system used in mathematics

In [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics"), **6-sphere coordinates** are a [coordinate system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coordinate_system "Coordinate system") for three-dimensional space obtained by [inverting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inversive_geometry "Inversive geometry") the 3D [Cartesian coordinates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartesian_coordinate_system "Cartesian coordinate system") across the [unit 2-sphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unit_sphere "Unit sphere")x 2+y 2+z 2=1{\displaystyle x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}=1}![Image 8: {\displaystyle x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}=1}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a5b5c3e3525a1604f9425edb80835d236fe7af82). They are so named because the [loci](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Locus_(mathematics) "Locus (mathematics)") where one coordinate is constant form [spheres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sphere "Sphere") tangent to the [origin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origin_(mathematics) "Origin (mathematics)") from one of six sides (depending on which coordinate is held constant and whether its value is positive or negative). This coordinate system exists independently from and has no relation to the [6-sphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-sphere "N-sphere"). The three coordinates are

u=x x 2+y 2+z 2,v=y x 2+y 2+z 2,w=z x 2+y 2+z 2.{\displaystyle u={\frac {x}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}},\quad v={\frac {y}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}},\quad w={\frac {z}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}}.}![Image 9: {\displaystyle u={\frac {x}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}},\quad v={\frac {y}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}},\quad w={\frac {z}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/82be298085796a02439a038899d615e74aef0d02)
Since inversion is an [involution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Involution_(mathematics) "Involution (mathematics)"), the equations for _x_, _y_, and _z_ in terms of _u_, _v_, and _w_ are similar:

x=u u 2+v 2+w 2,y=v u 2+v 2+w 2,z=w u 2+v 2+w 2.{\displaystyle x={\frac {u}{u^{2}+v^{2}+w^{2}}},\quad y={\frac {v}{u^{2}+v^{2}+w^{2}}},\quad z={\frac {w}{u^{2}+v^{2}+w^{2}}}.}![Image 10: {\displaystyle x={\frac {u}{u^{2}+v^{2}+w^{2}}},\quad y={\frac {v}{u^{2}+v^{2}+w^{2}}},\quad z={\frac {w}{u^{2}+v^{2}+w^{2}}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c8627e9de24b9df99c7734442cc6f440ce92ac82)
This coordinate system is [R{\displaystyle R}![Image 11: {\displaystyle R}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4b0bfb3769bf24d80e15374dc37b0441e2616e33)-separable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Separable_partial_differential_equation "Separable partial differential equation") for the 3-variable Laplace equation.
