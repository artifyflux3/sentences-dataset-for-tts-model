From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Part of a star

A **circumstellar envelope** (**CSE**) is a part of a [star](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star "Star") that has a roughly spherical shape and is not gravitationally bound to the star core. Usually circumstellar envelopes are formed from the dense [stellar wind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_wind "Stellar wind"), or they are present before the formation of the star.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circumstellar_envelope#cite_note-1) Circumstellar envelopes of old stars ([Mira variables](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mira_variable "Mira variable") and [OH/IR stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OH/IR_star "OH/IR star")) eventually evolve into [protoplanetary nebulae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protoplanetary_nebula "Protoplanetary nebula"), and circumstellar envelopes of [young stellar objects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Young_stellar_object "Young stellar object") evolve into [circumstellar discs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circumstellar_disc "Circumstellar disc").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circumstellar_envelope#cite_note-2)
