[![Image 1](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Rosetta_Stone.JPG/330px-Rosetta_Stone.JPG)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Rosetta_Stone.JPG)

The [Rosetta Stone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosetta_Stone "Rosetta Stone") (196 BC) bears writing in three different scripts. [Hieroglyphs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hieroglyphs "Hieroglyphs") (top) and [Demotic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demotic_script "Demotic script") (middle) record the same text in the [Egyptian language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_language "Egyptian language"), while an equivalent passage in [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_language "Greek language") uses the [Greek alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_alphabet "Greek alphabet") (bottom). These correspondences were key to the [decipherment of Egyptian hieroglyphs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decipherment_of_Egyptian_hieroglyphs "Decipherment of Egyptian hieroglyphs") in the early 19th century.

**Writing** is the act of creating a persistent representation of [language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language "Language"). A [writing system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing_system "Writing system") includes a particular set of symbols called a _script_, as well as the rules by which they encode a particular spoken language. Every [written language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Written_language "Written language") arises from a corresponding spoken language; while the use of language is universal across human societies, most spoken languages are not written.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarris2000185-1)

Writing is a [cognitive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive "Cognitive") and [social](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social "Social") activity involving [neuropsychological](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neuropsychological "Neuropsychological") and [physical processes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing_process "Writing process"). The outcome of this activity, also called _writing_ (or a _[text](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Text\_(literary\_theory) "Text (literary theory)")_) is a series of [physically inscribed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Handwriting "Handwriting"), [mechanically transferred](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Printing_press "Printing press"), or [digitally represented](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_data "Digital data") symbols. [Reading](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reading "Reading") is the corresponding process of interpreting a written text, with the interpreter referred to as a _reader_.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmith2005[httpsarchiveorgdetailsinstitutionaleth0000smitpage105_105%E2%80%93108]-2)

In general, writing systems do not constitute languages in and of themselves, but rather a means of encoding language such that it can be read by others across time and space.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEOng1982-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHaas1996-4) While not all languages use a writing system, those that do can complement and extend the capacities of [spoken language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spoken_language "Spoken language") by creating durable forms of language that can be transmitted across space (e.g. [written correspondence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Letter_(message) "Letter (message)")) and stored over time (e.g. [libraries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libraries "Libraries")).[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchmandt-BesseratErard200721-5) Writing can also impact what knowledge people acquire, since it allows humans to externalize their thinking in forms that are easier to reflect on, elaborate on, reconsider, and revise.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEmig1994-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAdler-KassnerWardle2015-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWinsor1994227%E2%80%93250-8)

Tools, materials, and motivations to write
------------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Writing&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Tools, materials, and motivations to write")]

Any instance of writing involves a complex interaction among available tools, intentions, cultural customs, cognitive routines, genres, tacit and explicit knowledge, and the constraints and limitations of the systems used.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJakobsPerrin2014a8-9)[Writing implements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing_implement "Writing implement") used to make physical inscriptions include [fingers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finger "Finger"), [styluses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stylus "Stylus"), [ink brushes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ink_brush "Ink brush"), [pencils](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pencil "Pencil"), [pens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pen "Pen"), and many styles of [lithography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lithography "Lithography"); [writing surfaces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing_surface "Writing surface") on which inscriptions may be made include [stone tablets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stone_tablet "Stone tablet"), [clay tablets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clay_tablet "Clay tablet"), [bamboo slips](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bamboo_slips "Bamboo slips"), [papyrus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papyrus "Papyrus"), [wax tablets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wax_tablet "Wax tablet"), [vellum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vellum "Vellum"), [parchment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parchment "Parchment"), [paper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paper "Paper"), [copperplate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intaglio_printing "Intaglio printing"), and [slate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackboard "Blackboard").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMahlowDale2014209%E2%80%93210Condorelli202239%E2%80%9349-10)

The [typewriter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typewriter "Typewriter"), as well as the digital [word processor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word_processor "Word processor"), allow individual writers to produce visually consistent text mechanically via a [keyboard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Input_method "Input method").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTELindgrenSullivan2019-11)

Advancements in [natural language processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_language_processing "Natural language processing") and [natural language generation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_language_generation "Natural language generation") have resulted in software capable of producing certain forms of formulaic writing (e.g. weather forecasts and sports reporting) without the direct involvement of humans[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEReiterDale2000-12) after initial configuration or, more commonly, to be used to support writing processes such as generating initial drafts, producing feedback with the help of a rubric, copy-editing, and [helping translation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_translation "Machine translation").[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKatsnelson2022208%E2%80%93209-13)

### Motivations and purposes

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Writing&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Motivations and purposes")]

[![Image 2](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Olin-Warner-LoC-tympanum-Highsmith.jpeg/330px-Olin-Warner-LoC-tympanum-Highsmith.jpeg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Olin-Warner-LoC-tympanum-Highsmith.jpeg)

Bronze [tympanum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tympanum_(architecture) "Tympanum (architecture)") featuring the [personification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personification "Personification") of Writing– [Thomas Jefferson Building](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson_Building "Thomas Jefferson Building"), Washington, D.C.

Historically, writing emerged to address the needs of societies growing in economic and social complexity. Once developed, potential applications included tracking produce and other wealth, recording [history](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History "History"), maintaining [culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture "Culture"), codifying knowledge through [curricula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curricula "Curricula") as well as lists of texts deemed to contain foundational knowledge (e.g. _[The Canon of Medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Canon\_of\_Medicine "The Canon of Medicine")_) or artistic value (e.g. the [literary canon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Literary_canon "Literary canon")). Aids to administration included [legal codes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_codes "Legal codes"), [census](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Census "Census") records, [contracts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contract "Contract"), [deeds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deed "Deed") of ownership, [taxation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tax "Tax"), [trade agreements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade_agreement "Trade agreement"), and [treaties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaties "Treaties"). As [Charles Bazerman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Bazerman "Charles Bazerman") explains, the "marking of signs on stones, clay, paper, and now digital memories—each more portable and rapidly traveling than the previous—provided means for increasingly coordinated and extended action as well as memory across larger groups of people over time and space."[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBazerman2013193-14) Further innovations included more uniform, predictable, and widely dispersed legal systems, the distribution of accessible versions of [sacred texts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacred_texts "Sacred texts"), and furthering practices of [scientific inquiry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_inquiry "Scientific inquiry") and [knowledge management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge_management "Knowledge management"), all of which were largely reliant on portable and easily reproducible forms of inscribed language. The [history of writing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_writing "History of writing") is co-extensive with uses of writing and the elaboration of [activity systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soft_systems_methodology#Human_activity_system "Soft systems methodology") that give rise to and circulate writing.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAndersen2007214%E2%80%93231-15)

Individual motivations for writing include the ability to operate beyond the limitations of one's own [memory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memory "Memory")[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHutchins1995-16) (e.g. [to-do lists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/To-do_lists "To-do lists"), [recipes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recipe "Recipe"), reminders, [logbooks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logbook "Logbook"), [maps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Map "Map"), directions for complicated tasks or [rituals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ritual "Ritual")), dissemination of ideas and coordination (e.g. [essays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Essay "Essay"), [monographs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monograph "Monograph"), [broadsides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadside_(printing) "Broadside (printing)"), [plans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plan "Plan"), [petitions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petition "Petition"), [manifestos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifesto "Manifesto")), creativity and [storytelling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Storytelling "Storytelling"), maintaining [kinship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinship "Kinship") and other social networks,[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChristiansen2017135%E2%80%93164-17)[business correspondence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_correspondence "Business correspondence") regarding goods and services, and [life writing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_writing "Life writing") (e.g. a [diary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diary "Diary") or journal).[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTELindenmanDriscollEfthymiouPavesich202470%E2%80%93106-18)

The global spread of digital [communication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communication "Communication") systems such as [email](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Email "Email") and [social media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_media "Social media") has made writing an increasingly important feature of daily life, where these systems mix with older technologies like paper, pencils, whiteboards, printers, and copiers.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTESterponiZucchermaglioAlbyFatigante2017359%E2%80%93386-19) Substantial amounts of everyday writing characterize most workplaces in [developed countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_countries "Developed countries").[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBrandt20153-20) In many occupations (e.g. law, [accounting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accounting "Accounting"), [software design](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_design "Software design"), [human resources](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_resources "Human resources")), written documentation is not only the main deliverable but also the mode of work itself.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJakobsSpinuzzi2014360-21) Even in occupations not typically associated with writing, routine [records management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Records_management "Records management") has most employees writing at least some of the time.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBeaufort2007221%E2%80%93237-22)

Some professions are typically associated with writing, such as literary authors, journalists, and technical writers, but writing is pervasive in most modern forms of work, civic participation, household management, and leisure activities.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmith2001160-23)

### Business and finance

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Writing&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Business and finance")]

Writing permeates everyday commerce. For example, in the course of an afternoon, a wholesaler might receive a written inquiry about the availability of a product line, then communicate with suppliers and fabricators through work orders and purchase agreements, correspond via email to affirm shipping availability with a [drayage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drayage "Drayage") company, write an invoice, and request proof of receipt in the form of a written signature. At a larger scale, modern systems of finances, banking, and business rest on written documents– including regulations, policies, and procedures; the creation of reports and other monitoring documents to make, evaluate, and provide accountability for decisions and operations; the creation and maintenance of records; internal written communications within departments to coordinate work; written communications that comprise work products presented to other departments and to clients; and external communications to clients and the public.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEYates1989-24)[_[page needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing\_sources "Wikipedia:Citing sources")_][[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmart2006-25)[_[page needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing\_sources "Wikipedia:Citing sources")_] Business and financial organizations also rely on many written legal documents, such as contracts, reports to government agencies, tax records, and accounting reports.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDevitt1991336%E2%80%93357-26) Financial institutions and markets that hold, transmit, trade, insure, or regulate holdings for clients or other institutions are particularly dependent on written records (though now often in digital form) to maintain the integrity of their roles.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEYates2005-27)[_[page needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing\_sources "Wikipedia:Citing sources")_]

Many modern systems of government are organized and sanctified through written [constitutions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution "Constitution") at the national and sometimes state or other organizational levels. Written rules and procedures typically guide the operations of the various branches, departments, and other bodies of government, which regularly produce reports and other documents as work products and to account for their actions. In addition to [legislatures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legislature "Legislature") that draft and pass laws, these laws are administered by an [executive branch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_branch "Executive branch"), which can present further written regulations specifying the laws and how they are carried out.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKerwinFurlong2019-28) Governments at different levels also typically maintain written records on citizens concerning identities, life events such as births, deaths, marriages, and divorces, the granting of licenses for controlled activities, criminal charges, traffic offences, and other penalties small and large, and tax liability and payments.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBazermanRogers2007192%E2%80%93193-29)

### Science and scholarship

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Writing&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Science and scholarship")]

Research undertaken in [academic disciplines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academic_discipline "Academic discipline") is typically published as articles in journals or within book-length [monographs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monograph "Monograph"). Arguments, experiments, observational data, and other evidence collated in the course of research is represented in writing, and serves as the basis for later work. Data collection and drafting of [manuscripts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manuscript "Manuscript") may be supported by grants, which usually require proposals establishing the value of such work and the need for funding.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTETardy20037%E2%80%9336-30) The data and procedures are also typically collected in [lab notebooks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lab_notebook "Lab notebook") or other preliminary files.[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTELatourWoolgar1986-31)[_[page needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing\_sources "Wikipedia:Citing sources")_][Preprints](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Preprint "Preprint") of potential publications may also be presented at academic or disciplinary conferences or on publicly accessible web servers to gain peer feedback and build interest in the work. Prior to official publication, these documents are typically read and evaluated by [peer review](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peer_review "Peer review") from appropriate experts, who determine whether the work is of sufficient value and quality to be published.[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHyland20041%E2%80%9319-32)

Publication does not establish the claims or findings of work as being authoritatively true, only that they are worth the attention of other specialists. As the work appears in review articles, handbooks, textbooks, or other aggregations, and others cite it in the advancement of their own research, does it become codified as contingently reliable knowledge.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBazerman1988-33)

News and news reporting are central to citizen engagement and knowledge of many spheres of activity people may be interested in about the state of their community, including the actions and integrity of their governments and government officials, economic trends, natural disasters and responses to them, international geopolitical events, including conflicts, but also sports, entertainment, books, and other leisure activities. While news and newspapers have grown rapidly from the eighteenth to the twentieth centuries, the changing economics and ability to produce and distribute news have brought about radical and rapid challenges to journalism and the consequent organization of citizen knowledge and engagement.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEConboy2007201%E2%80%93216-34)[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPerrin2013-35)[_[page needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing\_sources "Wikipedia:Citing sources")_] These changes have also created challenges for [journalism ethics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Journalism_ethics "Journalism ethics") that have been developed over the past century.[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPavlik200182-36)

### Education and educational institutions

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Writing&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Education and educational institutions")]

Formal education is the social context most strongly associated with the learning of writing, and students may carry these particular associations long after leaving school.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWingate2012145%E2%80%93154-37) Alongside the writing that students read (in the forms of textbooks, assigned books, and other instructional materials as well as self-selected books) students do much writing within schools at all levels, on subject exams, in essays, in taking notes, in doing homework, and in [formative and summative assessments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing_assessment "Writing assessment"). Some of this is explicitly directed toward the learning of writing, but much is focused more on subject learning.[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKleinArconBaker2016245%E2%80%93246-38)[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilliamsBeam2019227%E2%80%93242-39)

[Writing systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing_system "Writing system") may be broadly classified according to what units of language are generally represented by its symbols:[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDanielsBright199656-40)[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTERogers200513%E2%80%9315-41)

*   _Phonographies_ represent sounds of speech– with [alphabets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphabet "Alphabet") and [syllabaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syllabaries "Syllabaries") using symbols for [phonemes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoneme "Phoneme") and [syllables](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syllable "Syllable") respectively.
*   _[Logographies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logographies "Logographies")_ represent a language's units of meaning ([words](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word "Word") or [morphemes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morpheme "Morpheme")), though still associated by readers with their given pronunciations in the corresponding spoken language.

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Comparative_evolution_of_Cuneiform%2C_Egyptian_and_Chinese_characters.svg/250px-Comparative_evolution_of_Cuneiform%2C_Egyptian_and_Chinese_characters.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Comparative_evolution_of_Cuneiform,_Egyptian_and_Chinese_characters.svg)

Comparative evolution from pictograms to abstract character shapes, in Mesopotamian [cuneiforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuneiform "Cuneiform"), [Egyptian hieroglyphs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_hieroglyph "Egyptian hieroglyph") and [Chinese characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_characters "Chinese characters")

A logography is written using [logograms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logogram "Logogram")– written characters which represent individual [words](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word "Word") or [morphemes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morpheme "Morpheme").[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDanielsBright199656-40) Many logograms have internal structures, with components potentially representing both phonographic and [ideographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ideographic "Ideographic") (e.g. [Chinese character radicals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_character_radicals "Chinese character radicals"), [hieroglyphic determinatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hieroglyphic_determinatives "Hieroglyphic determinatives")) aspects of the morpheme.[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTECondorelli202261%E2%80%9362-42)

The main logographic system in use is [Chinese characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_characters "Chinese characters"), used primarily to write the [Chinese languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_languages "Chinese languages") and [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "Japanese language"), and historically others from regions influenced by [Chinese culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_culture "Chinese culture"), such as [Korean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_language "Korean language") and [Vietnamese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_language "Vietnamese language"). Other logographic systems include [cuneiform](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuneiform "Cuneiform") and [Maya script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_script "Maya script").[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGnanadesikan200911Goody198718-43)

A [syllabary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syllabary "Syllabary") is a set of written symbols that represent [syllables](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syllable "Syllable"),[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDanielsBright199656-40) typically a consonant followed by a vowel, or just a vowel alone. In some scripts more complex syllables (e.g. consonant–vowel–consonant or consonant–consonant–vowel) may have dedicated glyphs. Phonetically similar syllables are not written similarly.[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDanielsBright199656-40)

Syllabaries are best suited to languages with a relatively simple syllable structure, such as Japanese. Other syllabic scripts include [Linear B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_B "Linear B") and the [Cherokee syllabary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cherokee_syllabary "Cherokee syllabary").[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTECushman2011255%E2%80%93281-44)

An [alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphabet "Alphabet") is a set of written symbols that represent [consonants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consonant "Consonant") and [vowels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vowel "Vowel").[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDanielsBright199656-40)

Alphabets that generally only have letters for consonants are called _[abjads](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abjad "Abjad")_ or _consonantaries_; though optional, abjads may also use diacritical marks to specify which vowels follow each consonant. The earliest alphabets were abjads, influenced by symbols representing specific consonants that originated in Egyptian hieroglyphs. Most abjads are likewise native to the Middle East, reflecting the relatively limited variation of vowels in the [morphology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morphology_(linguistics) "Morphology (linguistics)") of the [Semitic languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semitic_languages "Semitic languages") spoken in the region.[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDanielsBright199656-40)

In most of the alphabets of India and [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia"), vowels are indicated through diacritics or modification of the shape of the consonant. These are called _[abugidas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abugida "Abugida")_ or _alphasyllabaries_.[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDanielsBright199656-40) The term _abugida_ is derived from the names of the initial letters in the [Geʽez script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ge%CA%BDez_script "Geʽez script"), another prominent abugida used to write several languages in Ethiopia and Eritrea.[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTECruttenden2021222,_224-45)

History and origins
-------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Writing&action=edit&section=15 "Edit section: History and origins")]

Writing first emerged in the [Early Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_Bronze_Age "Early Bronze Age") to meet the growing economic needs of the city-states of [Sumeria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumeria "Sumeria"), located in southern [Mesopotamia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesopotamia "Mesopotamia"). During this time, the complexity of trade and administration outgrew the power of memory, with [Sumerian cuneiform](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumerian_cuneiform "Sumerian cuneiform") serving as a reliable means for recording transactions, maintaining financial accounts, and keeping historical records, among similar activities.[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobinson201936-46)

Cuneiform, used to write the [Sumerian language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumerian_language "Sumerian language"), was followed relatively quickly by [Egyptian hieroglyphs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_hieroglyphs "Egyptian hieroglyphs"), with both emerging from proto-writing systems between 3400 and 3100 BC, with the earliest coherent texts from c. 2600 BC.[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTECondorelli202221-47) The [Indus script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indus_script "Indus script") (c. 2600– c. 2000 BC), found on different types of artefacts produced by the [Indus Valley Civilization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilization "Indus Valley Civilization") on the [Indian subcontinent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_subcontinent "Indian subcontinent"), remains undeciphered, and whether it functioned as true writing is not agreed upon.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTECondorelli202220Sproat2010110Gnanadesikan2009120-48) While its origins are not visually obvious, the opportunity for Mesopotamian cultural diffusion to have introduced the concept of writing to the Indus peoples is clear.[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobinson200920%E2%80%9321-49)

[![Image 4](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Accountancy_clay_envelope_Louvre_Sb1932.jpg/250px-Accountancy_clay_envelope_Louvre_Sb1932.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Accountancy_clay_envelope_Louvre_Sb1932.jpg)

Globular envelope with a cluster of accountancy tokens, Uruk period, from [Susa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susa "Susa")– [Louvre Museum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louvre_Museum "Louvre Museum")

In the 1970s, archaeologist [Denise Schmandt-Besserat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denise_Schmandt-Besserat "Denise Schmandt-Besserat") presented a theory establishing a link between cuneiform and previously uncategorized clay "tokens", the oldest of which have been found in the Zagros region of Iran. Around 8000 BC, Mesopotamians began using clay tokens to count their agricultural and manufactured goods. Later they began placing these tokens inside large, hollow clay containers (bulla, or globular envelopes) which were then sealed. The quantity of tokens in each container came to be expressed by impressing, on the container's surface, one picture for each instance of the token inside. They next dispensed with the tokens, relying solely on symbols for the tokens, drawn on clay surfaces. To avoid making a picture for each instance of the same object (for example: 100 pictures of a hat to represent 100 hats), they counted the objects by using various small marks.[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWoodsEmberlingTeeter201015-50)

Cuneiform (from Latin _cunius_, lit.'wedge') emerged c. 3200 BC in the context of this technology for keeping accounts. By the end of the 4th millennium BC,[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKramer1981381%E2%80%93383-51) the Mesopotamians were using a triangular-shaped stylus pressed into soft clay to record numbers. This system was gradually augmented with using a sharp stylus to indicate what was being counted by means of [pictographs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pictographs "Pictographs"). Round and sharp styluses were gradually replaced with wedge-shaped styluses, at first only recording [logograms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logogram "Logogram")– with phonetic elements introduced by the 29th century BC to represent syllables in Sumerian, resulting in a general purpose writing system.[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGnanadesikan202337-52)[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchmandt-Besserat199255%E2%80%9371-53)

From the 26th century BC, cuneiform was adapted to write the East Semitic [Akkadian language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akkadian_language "Akkadian language") ([Assyrian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Assyrian_period "Old Assyrian period") and [Babylonian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Babylonia "Babylonia")) which had spread across southern Mesopotamia– and then to others such as [Elamite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elamite "Elamite"), [Hattian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hattian_language "Hattian language"), [Hurrian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurrian_language "Hurrian language") and [Hittite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hittite_language "Hittite language"). Scripts similar in appearance to this writing system include those for [Ugaritic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ugaritic "Ugaritic") and [Old Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Persian "Old Persian"). With the adoption of [Aramaic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aramaic "Aramaic") as the lingua franca of the [Neo-Assyrian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neo-Assyrian_Empire "Neo-Assyrian Empire") (911–609 BC), Old Aramaic was also adapted to Mesopotamian cuneiform. The latest cuneiform texts in Akkadian discovered thus far date from the 1st century AD.[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGeller1997-54)

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Narmer_Palette_serpopard_side.jpg/250px-Narmer_Palette_serpopard_side.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Narmer_Palette_serpopard_side.jpg)

The [Narmer Palette](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Narmer_Palette "Narmer Palette"), depicting two monstrous [serpopards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serpopard "Serpopard") representing unification of [Upper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_Egypt "Upper Egypt") and [Lower Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lower_Egypt "Lower Egypt"), c. 3100 BC

The earliest known [hieroglyphs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hieroglyphs "Hieroglyphs") (from Greek, lit.'sacred writing') are clay labels for the [Predynastic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Predynastic_Egypt "Predynastic Egypt") ruler "Scorpion I", dated c. the 33nd century BC and recovered at [Abydos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abydos,_Egypt "Abydos, Egypt") (modern Umm el-Qa'ab)– or otherwise the [Narmer Palette](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Narmer_Palette "Narmer Palette"), dated c. 3100 BC.[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMattessich2002-55) The hieroglyphic script was [logographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logographic "Logographic"), with phonetic adjuncts that included an effective [alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_hieroglyph#Script "Egyptian hieroglyph"). The oldest deciphered sentence is attested on a seal impression from the tomb of [Seth-Peribsen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seth-Peribsen "Seth-Peribsen") at Abydos, dating to the [Second Dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Dynasty "Second Dynasty") (28th or 27th century BC). Around 800 hieroglyphs were used during the Old, Middle, and New Kingdom periods (2686–1077 BC); by the Greco-Roman period (30 BC– 642 AD), more than 5,000 distinct glyphs are attested.[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTELoprieno199512-56)

Writing was important in maintaining the Egyptian empire, and literacy in the difficult system of hieroglyphs was concentrated among an educated elite of [scribes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scribe "Scribe") serving temple, pharaonic, and military authorities.[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTELipson20049Powell2009124-57)

Of several [pre-Columbian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pre-Columbian "Pre-Columbian") scripts in [Mesoamerica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesoamerica "Mesoamerica"), the one that appears to have been best developed, and the only one to be deciphered, is the [Maya script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_script "Maya script"). The earliest inscription identified as Maya dates to the 3rd century BC.[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTESaturnoStuartBeltr%C3%A1n20061281%E2%80%931283-58) Maya writing used around 800 distinct symbols– mainly logograms, complemented by a set of syllabograms used for affixes, disambiguation between different readings of a logogram, or the substitution of certain logograms entirely.[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTETrigger200451-59)

The earliest surviving examples of writing in China– inscriptions on [oracle bones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oracle_bone "Oracle bone"), usually tortoise [plastrons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plastron "Plastron") and ox [scapulae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scapula "Scapula") which were used for divination– date from c. 1200 BC, during the [Late Shang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Shang "Late Shang") period. A small number of bronze inscriptions from the same period have also survived.[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoltz199974%E2%80%93123-60)

The [Proto-Elamite script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Elamite_script "Proto-Elamite script"), in use c. 3200– c. 2900 BC, is attested on clay tablets found at different sites across modern-day Iran, with the majority having been excavated at [Susa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susa "Susa"), an ancient city located east of the [Tigris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tigris "Tigris").[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDahl2018383%E2%80%93396-61) The script is thought to have been partly [logographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logographic "Logographic"), to have developed from early cuneiform, and to have used more than 1,000 signs– though its inscriptions "have been, and will remain, highly problematic in a discussion of writing because they represent a very unclear period of literacy".[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEnglund2004104-62)

The [Elamite cuneiform](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elamite_cuneiform "Elamite cuneiform") script, used c. 2500– 331 BC, was adapted from cuneiform as was used to write Akkadian. At any given point during this period, Elamite cuneiform used around 130 symbols– with a total of 206 used across its entire lifespan, far fewer than in most other cuneiform scripts.[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDanielsBright199656-40)

Prior to the invention of the Greek alphabet during the Iron Age, [Cretan hieroglyphs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cretan_hieroglyphs "Cretan hieroglyphs") are attested on artefacts from [Crete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crete "Crete") during the early-to-mid 2nd millennium BC. [Linear B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_B "Linear B"), the writing system of the [Mycenaean Greeks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mycenaean_Greeks "Mycenaean Greeks"), was used in [Knossos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knossos "Knossos") on Crete as well as the [Greek mainland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_mainland "Greek mainland")c. 1450−1200 BC.[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEOlivier1986377%E2%80%93389-63)[Linear A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_A "Linear A"), yet to be deciphered, was used in the [Aegean Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegean_Islands "Aegean Islands") and the mainland c. 1800–1450 BC.[[64]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTESalgarella2023396-64)

### Development of the alphabet

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Writing&action=edit&section=22 "Edit section: Development of the alphabet")]

The alphabet is only known to have been invented once in human history, by a community of Canaanite turquoise miners in the [Sinai Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula "Sinai Peninsula")c. 1800 BC to write [West Semitic languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Semitic_languages "West Semitic languages"),[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGoldwasser2010-65) "in the context of cultural exchanges between Semitic-speaking people from the Levant and communities in Egypt".[[66]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDrucker2022188-66) This earliest attested form is known as the _[Proto-Sinaitic script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Sinaitic\_script "Proto-Sinaitic script")_, and it adapted concepts and at least some of its written letterforms from Egyptian hieroglyphic writing; it adopted wholly West Semitic sound values for its letters, as opposed to adapting existing Egyptian ones.[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDrucker2022187%E2%80%93189Fischer200184%E2%80%9385-67) Precise dating of its origin, as well as the graphical origins of many letterforms (if any) remain unclear, and the script remains undeciphered.[[68]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFischer200185Haring202053%E2%80%9354-68) Around 30 crude inscriptions have been found at a mountainous Egyptian mining site known as Serabit el-Khadem, with symbols that stood for single consonant sounds rather than whole words or concepts– the basis of an alphabetic system. It was not until between the 12th and 9th centuries BC that use of the alphabet became widespread.[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGoldwasser2010-65)

The [Phoenician alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenician_alphabet "Phoenician alphabet") (c. 1050 BC) is a direct descendant of Proto-Sinaitic. Proto-Sinaitic and Phoenician were [abjads](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abjad "Abjad") which only had letters representing consonantal sounds; Phoenician was ultimately adapted into the [Greek alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_alphabet "Greek alphabet") (c. 800 BC), the first to represent vowel sounds, which it did by re-purposing unused Phoenician consonantal signs.[[69]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFischer200184%E2%80%9386-69) The [Cumae alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cumae_alphabet "Cumae alphabet"), a variant of the early Greek alphabet, gave rise to the [Etruscan alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Etruscan_alphabet "Etruscan alphabet") and its own descendants, such as the [Latin alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_alphabet "Latin alphabet").[[70]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFischer2001137%E2%80%93144-70) Other descendants from the Greek alphabet include [Cyrillic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyrillic "Cyrillic"), used to write languages such as [Bulgarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgarian_language "Bulgarian language") and [Russian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_language "Russian language").[[71]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTETrigger200458-71) The Phoenician alphabet was also adapted into the [Aramaic script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aramaic_script "Aramaic script"), from which the West Asian [Square Hebrew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_script "Hebrew script"), [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_script "Arabic script"),[[72]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTECoulmas2002114-72) and South Asian [Brahmic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brahmic "Brahmic") scripts are descended.[[73]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDaniels201881%E2%80%9388Fischer2001106Trigger200460Gnanadesikan2009173-73)

In the history of writing, [religious texts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_texts "Religious texts") or writing have played a special role. For example, some religious text compilations have been some of the earliest popular texts, or even the only written texts in some languages, and in some cases are still highly popular around the world.[[74]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMartin1994-74)[_[page needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing\_sources "Wikipedia:Citing sources")_][[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohnston2007133-75)[[76]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPowell200912-76)
