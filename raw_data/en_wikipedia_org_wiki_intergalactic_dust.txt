From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Cosmic dust in between galaxies in intergalactic space

**Intergalactic dust** is [cosmic dust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_dust "Cosmic dust") in between [galaxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy") in [intergalactic space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space#Intergalactic_space "Outer space").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergalactic_dust#cite_note-dust1-1) Evidence for intergalactic dust has been suggested as early as 1949, and study of it grew throughout the late 20th century.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergalactic_dust#cite_note-dust1-1) There are large variations in the distribution of intergalactic dust.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergalactic_dust#cite_note-dust1-1) Dust may affect intergalactic distance measurements, such as supernovae and quasars in other galaxies.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergalactic_dust#cite_note-2) Partially due to the dust's absorption and re-emission of visible light, observations of more distant [astronomical objects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomical_object "Astronomical object") have greater [apparent magnitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apparent_magnitude "Apparent magnitude") when conducted in [infrared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrared "Infrared").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergalactic_dust#cite_note-3)

Intergalactic dust can form **intergalactic dust clouds**, known since the 1960s to exist around some galaxies.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergalactic_dust#cite_note-dust1-1) By the 1980s, at least four intergalactic dust clouds had been discovered within several [megaparsecs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megaparsec "Megaparsec") of the [Milky Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way "Milky Way") galaxy,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergalactic_dust#cite_note-dust1-1) exemplified by the [Okroy Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Okroy_Cloud "Okroy Cloud").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intergalactic_dust#cite_note-dust1-1)
