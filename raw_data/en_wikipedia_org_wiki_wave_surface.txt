In mathematics, Fresnel's wave surface , found by Augustin-Jean Fresnel in 1822, is a quartic surface describing the propagation of light in an optically biaxial crystal . Wave surfaces are special cases of tetrahedroids which are in turn special cases of Kummer surfaces .

In projective coordinates ( w : x : y : z ) the wave surface is given by a 2 x 2 x 2 + y 2 + z 2 − − a 2 w 2 + b 2 y 2 x 2 + y 2 + z 2 − − b 2 w 2 + c 2 z 2 x 2 + y 2 + z 2 − − c 2 w 2 = 0 {\displaystyle {\frac {a^{2}x^{2}}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}-a^{2}w^{2}}}+{\frac {b^{2}y^{2}}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}-b^{2}w^{2}}}+{\frac {c^{2}z^{2}}{x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}-c^{2}w^{2}}}=0} They are used in the treatment of conical refractions .
