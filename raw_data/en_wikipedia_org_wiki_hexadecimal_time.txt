**Hexadecimal time** is the representation of the [time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time "Time") of [day](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Day "Day") as a [hexadecimal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexadecimal "Hexadecimal")[number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_number "Real number") in the [interval](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interval_(mathematics) "Interval (mathematics)") [0,1].

The day is divided into 10 16 (16 10) hexadecimal hours, each hour into 100 16 (256 10) hexadecimal minutes, and each minute into 10 16 (16 10) hexadecimal seconds.

History
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hexadecimal_time&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

This time format was proposed by the Swedish-American engineer [John W. Nystrom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_W._Nystrom "John W. Nystrom") in 1863 as part of his _[tonal system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tonal\_system\_(Nystrom) "Tonal system (Nystrom)")_.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexadecimal_time#cite_note-1)

In 1997, the American Mark Vincent Rogers of [Intuitor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intuitor "Intuitor") proposed a similar system of hexadecimal time and implemented it in [JavaScript](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JavaScript "JavaScript") as the Hexclock.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexadecimal_time#cite_note-2)

Implementation
--------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hexadecimal_time&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Implementation")]

A day is unity, or **1**, and any fraction thereof can be shown with digits to the right of the hexadecimal [separator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radix_point "Radix point"). So the day begins at midnight with **.0000** and one hexadecimal second after midnight is **.0001**. Noon is **.8000** (one half), one hexadecimal second before was **.7FFF** and one hexadecimal second before next midnight will be **.FFFF**.

Intuitor-hextime may also be formatted with an underscore separating hexadecimal hours, minutes and seconds. For example:

### Clock

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hexadecimal_time&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Clock")]

| Hex | Hex (Boardman) | [ISO 8601](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO_8601 "ISO 8601") | Comment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| .0100 | 0_10_0 | 00:05:37.5 |  |
| .0200 | 0_20_0 | 00:11:15 |  |
| .0400 | 0_40_0 | 00:22:30 |  |
| .0800 | 0_80_0 | 00:45:00 |  |
| .1000 | 1_00_0 | 01:30:00 | 1.5:24 = 1:16 = 0.1 |
| .8000 | 8_00_0 | 12:00:00 | 12:24 = 8:16 = 0.8 |
| .F000 | F_00_0 | 22:30:00 | 22.5:24 = 15:16 = 0.F |
| .F800 | F_80_0 | 23:15:00 |  |

### Conversions

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hexadecimal_time&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Conversions")]

| Hex |  | hexsec base 16 |  | hexsec base 10 |  | Traditional |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 day | = | 10000 | = | 65536 | = | 24 h |
| 1 hexadecimal hour | = | 1000 | = | 4096 | = | 1 h 30 min |
| 1 hexadecimal maxime | = | 100 | = | 256 | = | 5 min 37.5 s |
| 1 hexadecimal minute | = | 10 | = | 16 | = | 21.09375 s |
| 1 hexadecimal second | = | 1 | = | 1 | = | 1.318359375 s |
| 1 second | = | 0.C22E4 | = | 0.75851 | = | 1 s |
