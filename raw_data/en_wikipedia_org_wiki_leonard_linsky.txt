Died August 27, 2012 (2012-08-27) (aged 89) Nationality American Education Alma mater University of California, Berkeley Thesis A Study in Meaning: the Interchangeability of Expressions in Non-extensional Contexts (1949) Philosophical work Institutions University of Chicago Leonard Linsky (November 13, 1922 – August 27, 2012) [ 1 ] was an American philosopher of language. He was an Emeritus Professor of the University of Chicago .

Philosophical work [ edit ] Linsky was best known for work on the theory of reference , and also as a historian of early analytical philosophy .

[ 2 ] He is often cited as an example of the "orthodox view" in the theory of reference.

[ 3 ] He questioned the "intensional isomorphism" concept of Rudolf Carnap .
