**Methyl isocyanate** (**MIC**) is an [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the molecular formula CH 3 NCO. Synonyms are **isocyanatomethane** and **methyl carbylamine**. Methyl isocyanate is an intermediate chemical in the production of [carbamate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbamate "Carbamate")[pesticides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pesticide "Pesticide") and Haffmann Bromamide Degradation (such as [carbaryl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbaryl "Carbaryl"), [carbofuran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbofuran "Carbofuran"), [methomyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methomyl "Methomyl"), and [aldicarb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aldicarb "Aldicarb")). It has also been used in the production of [rubbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rubber "Rubber") and [adhesives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adhesive "Adhesive"). As an extremely [toxic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toxic "Toxic") and irritating compound, it is very hazardous to human health. MIC was the principal toxicant involved in the [Bhopal gas disaster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhopal_gas_disaster "Bhopal gas disaster"), which short-term killed 4,000–8,000 people and caused permanent injury and premature deaths to approximately 15,000-20,000.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-irs-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-Eckerman2013-10) It is also a very potent [lachrymatory agent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lachrymatory_agent "Lachrymatory agent").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-Kimmerle-11)

Physical properties
-------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methyl_isocyanate&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Physical properties")]

Methyl isocyanate is a colorless, poisonous, [lachrymatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lachrymatory_agent "Lachrymatory agent") ([tearing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tears "Tears") agent), flammable liquid.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-Union_Carbide_1967-12) It is soluble in water to 6–10 parts per 100 parts, but it also reacts with water (see [Reactions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#Reactions) below).

It has a refractive index of 1.363 with a wavelength of 589 nm at a temperature of 20°C [[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-13)

Methyl isocyanate is usually manufactured by the reaction of [monomethylamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylamine "Methylamine") and [phosgene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phosgene "Phosgene"). For large-scale production it is advantageous to combine these reactants at higher temperature in the gas phase. A mixture of methyl isocyanate and two moles of [hydrogen chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_chloride "Hydrogen chloride") is formed, but N-methylcarbamoyl chloride (MCC) forms as the mixture is condensed, leaving one [mole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mole_(unit) "Mole (unit)") of [hydrogen chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_chloride "Hydrogen chloride") as a gas.

[![Image 20](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/MMA_plus_Phosgene_diagram.svg/512px-MMA_plus_Phosgene_diagram.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:MMA_plus_Phosgene_diagram.svg)

The methyl isocyanate is obtained by treating the MCC with a [tertiary amine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tertiary_amine "Tertiary amine"), such as [_N_,_N_-dimethylaniline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethylaniline "Dimethylaniline"), or with [pyridine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyridine "Pyridine"),[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-14) or by separating it by using [distillation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distillation "Distillation") techniques.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-15)

[![Image 21](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/MCC_to_MIC_%26_HCl.svg/512px-MCC_to_MIC_%26_HCl.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:MCC_to_MIC_%26_HCl.svg)

Methyl isocyanate is also manufactured from [_N_-methylformamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-Methylformamide "N-Methylformamide") and air. In the latter process, it is immediately consumed in a closed-loop process to make [methomyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methomyl "Methomyl").[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-16) Other manufacturing methods have been reported.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-17)[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-18)

Methyl isocyanate reacts readily with many substances that contain N-H or O-H groups. With water, it forms [1,3-dimethylurea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1,3-dimethylurea "1,3-dimethylurea") and [carbon dioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide "Carbon dioxide") with the evolution of heat (1358.5 joules, or 325 calories, per gram of MIC): It is relatively slow to react at below 68°F, but will increase its rate with elevated temperatures or in the presence of acid or base.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-19)

[![Image 22](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d7/MIC_%26_water_to_DMU_%26_TMB.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:MIC_%26_water_to_DMU_%26_TMB.png)

At 25°C, in excess water, half of the MIC is consumed in 9 min.;[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-20) if the heat is not efficiently removed from the reacting mixture, the rate of the reaction will increase and rapidly cause the MIC to boil. Such a reaction triggered the [Bhopal disaster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhopal_disaster "Bhopal disaster") after a large amount of water was introduced to a MIC storage tank. The consequence of the out of control [exothermic process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exothermic_reaction "Exothermic reaction") was a [runaway reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Runaway_reaction "Runaway reaction") and the direct release of 42 tons of MIC to the atmosphere.

If MIC is in excess, [1,3,5-trimethylbiuret](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biuret "Biuret") is formed along with [carbon dioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide "Carbon dioxide").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-Union_Carbide_1967-12)[Alcohols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcohols "Alcohols") and [phenols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenols "Phenols"), which contain an O-H group, react slowly with MIC, but the reaction can be catalyzed by trialkylamines or dialkyltin dicarboxylate. [Oximes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxime "Oxime"), [hydroxylamines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroxylamine "Hydroxylamine"), and [enols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enol "Enol") also react with MIC to form methylcarbamates.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-Union_Carbide_1967-12) These reactions produce the products described below ([Uses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#Uses)).

[![Image 23](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/MIC_plus_1-naphthol_to_carbaryl.svg/512px-MIC_plus_1-naphthol_to_carbaryl.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:MIC_plus_1-naphthol_to_carbaryl.svg)

[Ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia"), primary, and secondary [amines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amine "Amine") rapidly react with MIC to form substituted [ureas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urea "Urea"). Other N-H compounds, such as amides and [ureas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urea "Urea"), react much more slowly with MIC.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-21)

It also reacts with itself to form a trimer or higher-molecular-weight polymers. In the presence of [catalysts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalysts "Catalysts"), MIC reacts with itself to form a solid trimer, trimethyl isocyanurate, or a higher-molecular-weight polymer:

[![Image 24](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/MIC_to_trimer.svg/424px-MIC_to_trimer.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:MIC_to_trimer.svg)

[Sodium methoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkoxide "Alkoxide"), [triethyl phosphine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phosphine "Phosphine"), [ferric chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferric_chloride "Ferric chloride") and certain other metal compounds catalyze the formation of the MIC-trimer, while the high-molecular-weight polymer formation is catalyzed by certain trialkylamines. Since the formation of the MIC trimer is [exothermic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exothermic "Exothermic") (1246 joules, or 298 calories, per gram of MIC), the reaction can lead to violent boiling of the MIC. The high-molecular-weight polymer hydrolyzes in hot water to form the trimethyl [isocyanurate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanuric_acid "Cyanuric acid"). Since [catalytic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalysis "Catalysis") metal salts can be formed from impurities in commercial grade MIC and steel, this product must not be stored in steel drums or tanks.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-Union_Carbide_1967-12)

Methyl isocyanate is extremely [toxic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toxic "Toxic"). There is no known [antidote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antidote "Antidote"). The threshold limit value set by the American Conference of Governmental Industrial Hygienists is 0.02 [ppm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parts_per_million "Parts per million"). MIC is toxic by inhalation, ingestion and contact in quantities as low as 0.4 [ppm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parts_per_million "Parts per million"). Exposure symptoms include coughing, [chest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chest "Chest") pain, [dyspnea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dyspnea "Dyspnea"), [asthma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asthma "Asthma"), irritation of the [eyes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_eye "Human eye"), [nose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_nose "Human nose") and [throat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Throat "Throat"), as well as [skin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skin "Skin") damage. Higher levels of exposure, over 21 ppm, can result in pulmonary or lung [edema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edema "Edema"), [emphysema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emphysema "Emphysema") and [hemorrhages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemorrhage "Hemorrhage"), bronchial [pneumonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pneumonia "Pneumonia") and [death](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death "Death"). Although the odor of methyl isocyanate cannot be detected at 5 ppm by most people, its potent [lachrymal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lachrymatory_agent "Lachrymatory agent") properties provide an excellent warning of its presence (at a concentration of 2–4 parts per million (ppm) subjected to eyes are irritated, while at 21 ppm, subjects could not tolerate the presence of methyl isocyanate in air).[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-Kimmerle-11) The irritant effects of methyl isocyanate are mediated by the irritant and tear gas receptor [TRPA1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TRPA1 "TRPA1") in pain-sensing nerve endings in the eye.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-22)

Proper care must be taken to store methyl isocyanate because of its ease of exothermically polymerizing (see [Reactions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#Reactions)) and its similar sensitivity to water. Only [stainless steel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stainless_steel "Stainless steel") or [glass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glass "Glass") containers may be safely used; the MIC must be stored at temperatures below 40°C (104°F) and preferably at 4°C (39°F).[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

The toxic effect of the compound was apparent in the 1984 [Bhopal disaster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhopal_disaster "Bhopal disaster"), when around 42,000 kilograms (93,000 lb) of methyl isocyanate and other gases were released from the underground reservoirs of the [Union Carbide India Limited](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_Carbide_India_Limited "Union Carbide India Limited") (UCIL) factory, over a populated area on 3 December 1984, killing about 3,500 people immediately, 8,000 people in the first 48 hours and 15,000 more over the next several years. 200,000 people had lasting health effects from the disaster.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-23)[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-convictions-24)

During structural fires, natural materials can contribute to releasing isocyanates including methyl isocyanate.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-25)

### Mechanism of action

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methyl_isocyanate&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Mechanism of action")]

Until recent decades, the mechanism of methyl isocyanate toxicity in humans was largely unknown or unclear.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-26)[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-27) Methyl isocyanate and other [isocyanates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanate "Isocyanate") are [electrophiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrophile "Electrophile") and are currently thought to cause toxicity by the [alkylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkylation "Alkylation") of biomolecules.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-28) The mechanism of methyl isocyanate was previously suspected to be the [carbamylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbamylation "Carbamylation") of [hemoglobin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemoglobin "Hemoglobin"), thus interfering with its oxygen-binding capability and causing [hypoxia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypoxia_(medical) "Hypoxia (medical)"). However, experiments showed that when [rats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rat "Rat") and [guinea pigs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinea_pig "Guinea pig") were exposed to methyl isocyanate at [concentrations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concentration "Concentration") above the [median lethal concentration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Median_lethal_dose "Median lethal dose") ([LC 50](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Median_lethal_dose "Median lethal dose"), the concentration sufficient to kill 50% of the tested population), only 2% of hemoglobin molecules were carbamylated, suggesting that this is probably not the mechanism of toxicity.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-29)[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-30)

[![Image 25](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/ALMA_detects_methyl_isocyanate_around_young_Sun-like_stars.jpg/250px-ALMA_detects_methyl_isocyanate_around_young_Sun-like_stars.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:ALMA_detects_methyl_isocyanate_around_young_Sun-like_stars.jpg)

A photographic montage of methyl isocyanate around young Sun-like stars, as detected by the [ALMA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atacama_Large_Millimeter_Array "Atacama Large Millimeter Array")[interferometer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interferometer "Interferometer") (northern [Chile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chile "Chile")).[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-ALMA-31)

On 30 July 2015, scientists reported that upon the first touchdown of the _[Philae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philae\_(spacecraft) "Philae (spacecraft)")_ lander on [comet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comet "Comet")[67/P](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/67/P "67/P")'s surface, measurements by the [COSAC and Ptolemy instruments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philae_(spacecraft)#Instruments "Philae (spacecraft)") revealed sixteen [organic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound"), four of which were seen for the first time on a [comet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comet "Comet"), including [acetamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide "Acetamide"), [acetone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetone "Acetone"), methyl isocyanate and [propionaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde "Propionaldehyde").[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-wapo20150730-32)[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-esa20150730-33)[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-SCI-20150731-34)

In 2017, two teams of astronomers using the [Atacama Large Millimeter Array](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atacama_Large_Millimeter_Array "Atacama Large Millimeter Array") (ALMA) [interferometer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interferometer "Interferometer") made of 66 radio telescopes in the [Atacama Desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atacama_Desert "Atacama Desert") (northern [Chile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chile "Chile")) have discovered the presence of MIC around young Sun-like stars.[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-ALMA-31)

MIC is considered a [prebiotic molecule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prebiotic_(chemistry) "Prebiotic (chemistry)") as explained by the discoverers of the ALMA findings in [IRAS 16293-2422](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IRAS_16293-2422 "IRAS 16293-2422"), a multiple system of very young stars: "This family of organic molecules is involved in the synthesis of [peptides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peptide "Peptide") and [amino acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acid "Amino acid"), which, in the form of [proteins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein "Protein"), are the biological basis for life as we know it".[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate#cite_note-ALMA-31)
