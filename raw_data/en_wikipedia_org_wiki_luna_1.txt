_**Luna 1**_, also known as _**Mechta**_ ([Russian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_language "Russian language"): Мечта[[mʲɪt͡ɕˈta]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Russian "Help:IPA/Russian"), _[lit.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Literal\_translation "Literal translation")_: _Dream_), _**E-1 No.4**_ and _First Lunar Rover_,[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarvey2007a26-4) was the first [spacecraft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacecraft "Spacecraft") to reach the vicinity of Earth's [Moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon "Moon"), the first spacecraft to leave Earth's orbit, and the first to be placed in [heliocentric orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliocentric_orbit "Heliocentric orbit"). Intended as a Moon impactor, _Luna 1_ was launched as part of the [Soviet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union")[Luna programme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_programme "Luna programme") in 1959.

A malfunction in the ground-based control system caused an error in the upper stage rocket's burn time, and the spacecraft missed the Moon by 5,900 km (more than three times the Moon's radius). _Luna 1_ became the first human-made object to reach [heliocentric orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliocentric_orbit "Heliocentric orbit") and was dubbed "Artificial Planet 1"[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc.gsfc.nasa.gov-5) and renamed _Mechta_ (_Dream_).[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTECormack2012342-6)_Luna 1_ was also referred to as the "First Cosmic Ship",[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc.gsfc.nasa.gov-5) in reference to its achievement of Earth [escape velocity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Escape_velocity "Escape velocity").

The satellite and rocket carrying _Luna 1_ was originally referred to as the Soviet Space Rocket by the Soviet Press.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi201811-1) Pravda writer [Alexander Kazantsev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Kazantsev "Alexander Kazantsev") called it _Mechta_ ([Russian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_language "Russian language"): Мечта, meaning 'dream').[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-alkaz-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDarling2003244-8) Citizens of Moscow unofficially deemed it _Lunik_, a combination of Luna (Moon) and [Sputnik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sputnik "Sputnik").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-alkaz-7) It was renamed to _Luna 1_ in 1963.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi201811-1)

The spherical satellite was powered by [mercury-oxide batteries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercury_battery "Mercury battery") and [silver-zinc accumulators](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver_zinc_battery "Silver zinc battery").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2) There were five antennas on one hemisphere, four whip-style and one rigid, for communication purposes. The spacecraft also contained [radio equipment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_equipment "Radio equipment") including a [tracking transmitter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tracking_transmitter "Tracking transmitter") and [telemetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telemetry "Telemetry") system.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2) There was no propulsion system.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2)

_Luna 1_ was designed to impact the Moon, delivering two metallic pennants with the [Soviet coat of arms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_coat_of_arms "Soviet coat of arms") that were included into its payload package.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2) It also had six instruments to study the Moon upon its suicidal approach. The flux-gate [magnetometer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetometer "Magnetometer") was triaxial and could measure ± 3000 [gammas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tesla_(unit)#Conversion_to_non-SI_units "Tesla (unit)"). It was designed to detect lunar magnetic fields.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarvey2007b26-10) Two micrometeorite detectors, developed by Tatiana Nazarova of the Vernadsky Institute, were installed on the spacecraft. They each consisted of a metal plate with springs and could detect small impacts.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarvey2007b26-10) Four ion traps, used to measure solar wind and plasma, were included. They were developed by Konstantin Gringauz.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarvey2007b26-10) The scientific payload also included two gas-discharge [Geiger counters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geiger_counter "Geiger counter"), a sodium-iodide [scintillation counter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scintillation_counter "Scintillation counter"), and a [Cherenkov detector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cherenkov_detector "Cherenkov detector"). The upper stage of the rocket contained a scintillation counter and 1 kilogram (2.2 lb) of sodium for a gas-dispersion experiment.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2)[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi201811-1)

The spacecraft weighed 361.3 kilograms (797 lb) at launch.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi201811-1) It was about 1.22 metres (4.0 ft) in diameter.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-Smithsonian-11)

Route of Luna 1 on a Soviet stamp

_Luna 1_ was launched at 16:41 GMT (22:41 [local time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UTC%2B6 "UTC+6")) on 2 January 1959 from [Site 1/5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome_Site_1 "Baikonur Cosmodrome Site 1") at the [Baikonur Cosmodrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome "Baikonur Cosmodrome") by a [Luna 8K72](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_8K72 "Luna 8K72") rocket.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi201811-1) The first three stages operated nominally. The Soviet engineers did not trust automated systems for controlling the engine burns, so they communicated to the rocket via radio. The signal to stop firing the engine Block E stage was delayed,[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEReichl201920-12) and the prolonged burn imparted an extra 175 m/s to _Luna 1_.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi201811-1)

Consequently _Luna 1_ missed its target by 5,995 kilometers (3,725 mi). The spacecraft passed within 5,995–6,400 kilometers (3,725–3,977 mi) of the Moon's surface on 4 January after 34 hours of flight, and then became the first human made object to leave Earth's orbit on January 6, 1959.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2)[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi201811-1)_Luna 1_ ran out of battery power on 5 January, when it was 597,000 kilometers (371,000 mi) from Earth, making it impossible to track further.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi201811-1)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEReichl201921-13) The batteries were designed for a minimum of 40 hours but lasted for 62.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEReichl201921-13)

_Luna 1_ became the first artificial object to reach the [escape velocity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Escape_velocity "Escape velocity") of the Earth,[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi20181-14) along with its carrier rocket's 1,472-kilogram (3,245 lb)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2) upper stage, which it separated from after being the first spacecraft to reach [heliocentric orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliocentric_orbit "Heliocentric orbit").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi201811-1) It remains in orbit around the Sun, between the orbits of Earth and Mars.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2)

At 00:57 GMT on 3 January 1959, at a distance of 113,000 kilometres (70,000 mi) from Earth,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTESiddiqi201811-1) 1 kilogram (2.2 lb) of [sodium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium "Sodium") gas was released by the spacecraft, forming a cloud behind it to serve as an artificial [comet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comet "Comet"). The cloud was released for two purposes: to allow visual tracking of the spacecraft's trajectory[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-16) and to observe the behavior of gas in space.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2) This glowing orange trail of gas, visible over the [Indian Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean "Indian Ocean") with the brightness of a sixth-magnitude star for a few minutes, was photographed by Mstislav Gnevyshev at the Mountain Station of the Main Astronomical Observatory of the [Academy of Sciences of the USSR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academy_of_Sciences_of_the_USSR "Academy of Sciences of the USSR") near [Kislovodsk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kislovodsk "Kislovodsk").[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-BSE59-17)

While traveling through the outer [Van Allen radiation belt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_Allen_radiation_belt "Van Allen radiation belt"), the spacecraft's [scintillator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scintillator "Scintillator") made observations indicating that a small number of high-energy [particles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subatomic_particle "Subatomic particle") exist in the outer belt. The measurements obtained during this mission provided new data on the Earth's radiation belt and [outer space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space "Outer space"). The craft was unable to detect a lunar [magnetic field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetic_field "Magnetic field") which placed an upper limit on its strength of 1/10,000th of Earth's.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2)[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-Huntress235-18) The first-ever direct observations and measurements of [solar wind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_wind "Solar wind"),[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarvey2007a26-4)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2) a strong flow of [ionized plasma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasma_(physics) "Plasma (physics)") emanating from the Sun and streaming through interplanetary space, were performed.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-nssdc-2) The ionized plasma concentration was measured to be some 700 particles per cm 3 at altitudes of 20,000–25,000 km and 300 to 400 particles per cm 3 at altitudes of 100,000–150,000 km.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-BSE59-17) The spacecraft also marked the first instance of radio communication at the half-million-kilometer distance.

Some doubted the veracity of the Soviets' claim of mission success. [Lloyd Mallan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lloyd_Mallan "Lloyd Mallan") wrote about it in a book called _The Big Red Lie_. Many in the West did not receive transmissions from the spacecraft even though the Soviets publicized them before the flight. By the time the Earth rotated so that scientists in the United States could pick up signals from the spacecraft, it was already 171,000 kilometers (106,000 mi) away.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarvey2007b28%E2%80%9329-19) In May 1959 several hearings _Soviet Space Technology_ before the _Committee on Science and Astronautics and Special Subcommittee on Lunik Probe_ of the [United States House of Representatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives "United States House of Representatives") attested the achievements of the Soviet mission and its sophisticated guidance technology.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-20)

The Soviet Union issued stamps to commemorate their success.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luna_1#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHarvey2007b30-21)
