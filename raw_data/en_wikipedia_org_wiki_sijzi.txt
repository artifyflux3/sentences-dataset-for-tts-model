Persian astronomer and mathematician (c.945-c.1020)

This article is about the Iranian scientist astronomer. For the Indo-Persian Sufi poet, see [Hasan Sijzi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hasan_Sijzi "Hasan Sijzi").

For other people named Al-Sijistani, see [Al-Sijistani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Sijistani "Al-Sijistani").

**Abu Sa'id Ahmed ibn Mohammed ibn Abd al-Jalil al-Sijzi** (c. 945 - c. 1020, also known as **al-Sinjari** and **al-Sijazi**; [Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_language "Persian language"): ابوسعید سجزی; **Al-Sijzi** is short for "[Al-Sijistani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Sijistani "Al-Sijistani")") was an [Iranian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_peoples "Iranian peoples")[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sijzi#cite_note-3)[Muslim astronomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_astronomy "Islamic astronomy"), [mathematician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_mathematics "Islamic mathematics"), and [astrologer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_astrology "Islamic astrology"). He is notable for his correspondence with [al-Biruni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Biruni "Al-Biruni") and for proposing that the [Earth rotates around its axis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth%27s_rotation "Earth's rotation") in the 10th century.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sijzi#cite_note-Scientia-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sijzi#cite_note-young-5)

He dedicated work to ['Adud al-Daula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%27Adud_al-Daula "'Adud al-Daula"), who was probably his patron, and to the prince of [Balkh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkh "Balkh"). He also worked in [Shiraz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shiraz "Shiraz") making astronomical observations from 969 to 970.

Mathematics
-----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Al-Sijzi&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Mathematics")]

Al-Sijzi studied intersections of conic sections and circles. He replaced the old kinematical [trisection of an angle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trisection_of_an_angle "Trisection of an angle") by a purely geometric solution (intersection of a circle and an equilateral hyperbola.)

Earth's rotation
----------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Al-Sijzi&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Earth's rotation")]

[Al-Biruni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Biruni "Al-Biruni") tells us that Al-Sijzi invented an [astrolabe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrolabe "Astrolabe"), called "al-zūraqī", whose design was based on the idea that the Earth rotates:[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sijzi#cite_note-6)

> I have seen the astrolabe called Zuraqi invented by Abu Sa'id Sijzi. I liked it very much and praised him a great deal, as it is based on the idea entertained by some to the effect that the motion we see is due to the Earth's movement and not to that of the sky. By my life, it is a problem difficult of solution and refutation. [...] For it is the same whether you take it that the Earth is in motion or the sky. For, in both cases, it does not affect the Astronomical Science. It is just for the physicist to see if it is possible to refute it.

Al-Biruni also referred to Al-Sijzi as a prominent astronomer who defended the theory that the Earth rotates in _al-Qānūn al-Masʿūdī_.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sijzi#cite_note-Scientia-4)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sijzi#cite_note-7)

The fact that some people did believe that the Earth is moving on its own axis is further confirmed by a reference from the 13th century which states:

> "According to the geometers [or engineers] (_muhandisīn_), the earth is in constant circular motion, and what appears to be the motion of the heavens is actually due to the motion of the earth and not the stars."[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sijzi#cite_note-young-5)
