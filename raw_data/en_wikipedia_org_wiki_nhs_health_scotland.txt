A Special Health Board of NHS Scotland , its goal was to improve the health of the nation, via research, planning, programme implementation and evaluation.

[ 1 ] NHS Health Scotland provided leadership and helped coordinate the work of other bodies, principally the 14 regional NHS Boards, in improving the health of the population and reducing health inequality .

NHS Health Scotland was established on 1 April 2003, by the merger of the Health Education Board for Scotland ( HEBS ) and the Public Health Institute of Scotland ( PHIS ).

[ 2 ] It was dissolved by the establishment of Public Health Scotland on 1 April 2020. It employed about 280 staff.

[ 3 ] The work of the agency was focussed on: child oral health child healthy weight alcohol brief interventions suicide prevention smoking cessation cardiovascular health NHS Health Scotland was dissolved and succeeded by Public Health Scotland on 1 April 2020. This new national Special Health Board is a collaborative approach by both the Scottish Government and COSLA to give effect to the recommendations of the 2015 Review of Public Health .
