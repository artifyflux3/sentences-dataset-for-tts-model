[Alternation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternation_(geometry) "Alternation (geometry)") of the _n_-cube yields one of two _n_-demicubes, as in this 3-dimensional illustration of the two [tetrahedra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetrahedra "Tetrahedra") that arise as the 3-demicubes of the 3-cube.

In [geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometry "Geometry"), **demihypercubes** (also called _n-demicubes_, _n-hemicubes_, and _half measure polytopes_) are a class of _n_-[polytopes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polytopes "Polytopes") constructed from [alternation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternation_(geometry) "Alternation (geometry)") of an _n_-[hypercube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypercube "Hypercube"), labeled as _hγ n_ for being _half_ of the hypercube family, _γ n_. Half of the vertices are deleted and new facets are formed. The 2 _n_ facets become 2 _n_**(_n_−1)-demicubes**, and 2 _n_**(_n_−1)-simplex** facets are formed in place of the deleted vertices.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demihypercube#cite_note-1)

They have been named with a _demi-_ prefix to each [hypercube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypercube "Hypercube") name: demi[cube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cube "Cube"), demi[tesseract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tesseract "Tesseract"), etc. The demicube is identical to the regular [tetrahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetrahedron "Tetrahedron"), and the demitesseract is identical to the regular [16-cell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/16-cell "16-cell"). The [demipenteract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demipenteract "Demipenteract") is considered _semiregular_ for having only regular facets. Higher forms do not have all regular facets but are all [uniform polytopes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uniform_polytope "Uniform polytope").

The [vertex-edge graph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graph_of_a_polytope "Graph of a polytope") of the demihypercube is the [halved cube graph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halved_cube_graph "Halved cube graph").

An _n_-demicube has [inversion symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point_reflection "Point reflection") if _n_ is [even](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parity_(mathematics) "Parity (mathematics)").

[Thorold Gosset](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thorold_Gosset "Thorold Gosset") described the demipenteract in his 1900 publication listing all of the regular and semiregular figures in _n_-dimensions above three. He called it a _5-ic semi-regular_. It also exists within the [semiregular _k_ 21 polytope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semiregular_k_21_polytope "Semiregular k 21 polytope") family.

The demihypercubes can be represented by extended [Schläfli symbols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schl%C3%A4fli_symbol "Schläfli symbol") of the form h{4,3,...,3} as half the vertices of {4,3,...,3}. The [vertex figures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertex_figure "Vertex figure") of demihypercubes are [rectified](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rectification_(geometry) "Rectification (geometry)")_n_-[simplexes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplex "Simplex").

In general, a demicube's elements can be determined from the original _n_-cube: (with C _n_,_m_ = _m th_-face count in [_n_-cube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypercube "Hypercube") = 2 _n_−_m_ _n_!/(_m_!(_n_−_m_)!))

*   **Vertices:** D _n_,0 = 1/2 C _n_,0 = 2 _n_−1 (Half the _n_-cube vertices remain)
*   **Edges:** D _n_,1 = C _n_,2 = 1/2 _n_(_n_−1) 2 _n_−2 (All original edges lost, each square faces create a new edge)
*   **Faces:** D _n_,2 = 4 * C _n_,3 = 2/3 _n_(_n_−1)(_n_−2) 2 _n_−3 (All original faces lost, each cube creates 4 new triangular faces)
*   **Cells:** D _n_,3 = C _n_,3 + 2 3 C _n_,4 (tetrahedra from original cells plus new ones)
*   **Hypercells:** D _n_,4 = C _n_,4 + 2 4 C _n_,5 (16-cells and 5-cells respectively)
*   ...
*   [For _m_ = 3,...,_n_−1]: D _n_,_m_ = C _n_,_m_ + 2 _m_ C _n_,_m_+1 (_m_-demicubes and _m_-simplexes respectively)
*   ...
*   **Facets:** D _n_,_n_−1 = 2 _n_ + 2 _n_−1 ((_n_−1)-demicubes and (_n_−1)-simplices respectively)

The stabilizer of the demihypercube in the [hyperoctahedral group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperoctahedral_group "Hyperoctahedral group") (the [Coxeter group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coxeter_group "Coxeter group")![Image 362: {\displaystyle BC_{n}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7919e662fe0b68f70e6f1121f86f4dbd1318d195) [4,3 _n_−1]) has index 2. It is the Coxeter group ![Image 363: {\displaystyle D_{n},}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f791ccfe467d310fef894c66d25763afacfb438e) [3 _n_−3,1,1] of order ![Image 364: {\displaystyle 2^{n-1}n!}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/fb4af342fccc32ab4e0de63f5b384229a97deb7b), and is generated by permutations of the coordinate axes and reflections along _pairs_ of coordinate axes.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demihypercube#cite_note-2)

Orthotopic constructions
------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Demihypercube&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Orthotopic constructions")]

[![Image 365](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Rhombic_disphenoid.png/250px-Rhombic_disphenoid.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Rhombic_disphenoid.png)

The rhombic disphenoid inside of a [cuboid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuboid "Cuboid")

Constructions as alternated [orthotopes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthotope "Orthotope") have the same topology, but can be stretched with different lengths in _n_-axes of symmetry.

The [rhombic disphenoid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhombic_disphenoid "Rhombic disphenoid") is the three-dimensional example as alternated cuboid. It has three sets of edge lengths, and [scalene triangle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scalene_triangle "Scalene triangle") faces.
