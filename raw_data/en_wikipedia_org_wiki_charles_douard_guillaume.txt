**Charles-Édouard Guillaume** (French:[[ʃaʁl edwaʁ gijom]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/French "Help:IPA/French"); 15 February 1861 – 13 June 1938)[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-nobelprize.org-1) was a Swiss [physicist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physicist "Physicist") who received the [Nobel Prize in Physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics "Nobel Prize in Physics") in 1920 "for the service he had rendered to precision measurements in physics by his discovery of anomalies in nickel steel [alloys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alloy "Alloy")".[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-nobelprize.org-1) In 1919, he gave the fifth [Guthrie Lecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institute_of_Physics_Michael_Faraday_Medal_and_Prize#Guthrie_lecturers "Institute of Physics Michael Faraday Medal and Prize") at the [Institute of Physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institute_of_Physics "Institute of Physics") in [London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London "London") with the title "The Anomaly of the Nickel-Steels".[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-4)

Personal life
-------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Charles-%C3%89douard_Guillaume&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Personal life")]

Charles-Edouard Guillaume was born in [Fleurier, Switzerland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fleurier,_Switzerland "Fleurier, Switzerland"), on 15 February 1861.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-nobelprize.org-1) Guillaume received his early education in [Neuchâtel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neuch%C3%A2tel "Neuchâtel"), and obtained a doctoral degree in Physics at [ETH Zurich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ETH_Zurich "ETH Zurich") in 1883.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-nobelprize.org-1)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-5)

Guillaume was married in 1888 to A.M. Taufflieb, with whom he had three children.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-nobelprize.org-1)

He died on 13 June 1938 in [Sèvres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%A8vres "Sèvres"), aged 77.

Scientific career
-----------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Charles-%C3%89douard_Guillaume&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Scientific career")]

Guillaume was head of the [International Bureau of Weights and Measures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Bureau_of_Weights_and_Measures "International Bureau of Weights and Measures").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-Gould-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-7) He also worked with [Kristian Birkeland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristian_Birkeland "Kristian Birkeland"), serving at the [Observatoire de Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Observatoire_de_Paris "Observatoire de Paris") – Section de Meudon. He conducted several experiments with [thermostatic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermostatics "Thermostatics") measurements at the observatory.

### Nickel–steel alloy

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Charles-%C3%89douard_Guillaume&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Nickel–steel alloy")]

[![Image 8](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/1918_Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume.jpg/250px-1918_Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:1918_Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume.jpg)

1918 [Autochrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autochrome_Lumi%C3%A8re "Autochrome Lumière") by [Auguste Léon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Archives_of_the_Planet "The Archives of the Planet")

Guillaume is known for his discovery of nickel–steel alloys he named [invar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invar "Invar"),[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-8)[elinvar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elinvar "Elinvar") and [platinite](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Platinite&action=edit&redlink=1 "Platinite (page does not exist)")[[it](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/platinite "it:platinite")], also known as red platinum. Invar has a near-zero [coefficient of thermal expansion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coefficient_of_thermal_expansion "Coefficient of thermal expansion"), making it useful in constructing precision instruments whose dimensions need to remain constant in spite of varying temperature. Elinvar has a near-zero thermal coefficient of the [modulus of elasticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modulus_of_elasticity "Modulus of elasticity"), making it useful in constructing instruments with springs that need to be unaffected by varying temperature, such as the [marine chronometer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_chronometer "Marine chronometer"). Elinvar is also non-magnetic, which is a secondary useful property for [antimagnetic watches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antimagnetic_watch "Antimagnetic watch").

### Space radiation

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Charles-%C3%89douard_Guillaume&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Space radiation")]

Guillaume is also known for the _earliest_ estimation of the "radiation of the stars” in his 1896 article _"La Température de L'Espace"_ ("The Temperature of Space"). This publication made him a pioneer in [plasma cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasma_cosmology "Plasma cosmology"), the study of conditions far from any particular star.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-9) The concept was later known as the [Cosmic microwave background](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_microwave_background "Cosmic microwave background").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-lanaturecited-10) He was one of the first people in history to estimate the temperature of space, as 5–6[K](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelvin "Kelvin").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-lanaturecited-10)

[![Image 9](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Charles_Edouard_Guillaume.jpg/250px-Charles_Edouard_Guillaume.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Charles_Edouard_Guillaume.jpg)

1922 pastel portraiture by [Marie-Louise Catherine Breslau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marie-Louise_Catherine_Breslau "Marie-Louise Catherine Breslau")

### Horology

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Charles-%C3%89douard_Guillaume&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Horology")]

As the son of a Swiss [horologist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horologist "Horologist"), Guillaume took an interest in [marine chronometers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_chronometer "Marine chronometer"). For use as the [compensation balance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compensation_balance "Compensation balance") he developed a slight variation of the invar alloy which had a negative quadratic coefficient of expansion. The purpose of doing this was to eliminate the "middle-temperature" error of the balance wheel.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-Gould-6) The _Guillaume balance_ (a type of [balance wheel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balance_wheel "Balance wheel")) in [horology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horology "Horology") is named after him.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-11)[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_%C3%89douard_Guillaume#cite_note-12)
