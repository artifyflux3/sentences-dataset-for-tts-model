It forms as a result of atmospheric waves [ 1 ] caused by wind passing over obstacles; for example, a mountain or a building. They are more common in locations that are hilly, or places where foehn winds are common [ 2 ] They tend to look more well-defined when a foehn wind is causing their formation. They also sometimes show iridescence .

[ 3 ] It is a type of lenticular cloud .
