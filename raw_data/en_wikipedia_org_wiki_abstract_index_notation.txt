From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

**Abstract index notation** (also referred to as slot-naming index notation)[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abstract_index_notation#cite_note-Thorne2017-1) is a mathematical notation for [tensors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor "Tensor") and [spinors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spinor "Spinor") that uses indices to indicate their types, rather than their components in a particular basis.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abstract_index_notation#cite_note-Penrose2007-2) The indices are mere placeholders, not related to any basis and, in particular, are non-numerical. Thus it should not be confused with the [Ricci calculus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ricci_calculus "Ricci calculus"). The notation was introduced by [Roger Penrose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roger_Penrose "Roger Penrose") as a way to use the formal aspects of the [Einstein summation convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_summation_convention "Einstein summation convention") to compensate for the difficulty in describing [contractions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_contraction "Tensor contraction") and [covariant differentiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covariant_derivative "Covariant derivative") in modern abstract tensor notation, while preserving the explicit [covariance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covariance_and_contravariance_of_vectors "Covariance and contravariance of vectors") of the expressions involved.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abstract_index_notation#cite_note-Penrose1984-3)

Let ![Image 1: {\displaystyle V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/af0f6064540e84211d0ffe4dac72098adfa52845) be a [vector space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_space "Vector space"), and ![Image 2: {\displaystyle V^{*}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5910e6a94f4f7ee2ee85ceed9dacef3eff7a6242) its [dual space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_space "Dual space"). Consider, for example, an order-2 [covariant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covariance_and_contravariance_of_vectors "Covariance and contravariance of vectors") tensor ![Image 3: {\displaystyle h\in V^{*}\otimes V^{*}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/07b12887bcd1b62326e123fce4f43818ffd05b51). Then ![Image 4: {\displaystyle h}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b26be3e694314bc90c3215047e4a2010c6ee184a) can be identified with a [bilinear form](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilinear_form "Bilinear form") on ![Image 5: {\displaystyle V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/af0f6064540e84211d0ffe4dac72098adfa52845). In other words, it is a function of two arguments in ![Image 6: {\displaystyle V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/af0f6064540e84211d0ffe4dac72098adfa52845) which can be represented as a pair of _slots_:

![Image 7: {\displaystyle h=h(-,-).}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/3a45f9c6a14512f4cc3763415875973572373b32)
Abstract index notation is merely a _labelling_ of the slots with Latin letters, which have no significance apart from their designation as labels of the slots (i.e., they are non-numerical):

![Image 8: {\displaystyle h=h_{ab}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/087ea2986068f35f3381084fb982870108fede7f)
A [tensor contraction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_contraction "Tensor contraction") (or trace) between two tensors is represented by the repetition of an index label, where one label is contravariant (an _upper index_ corresponding to the factor ![Image 9: {\displaystyle V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/af0f6064540e84211d0ffe4dac72098adfa52845)) and one label is covariant (a _lower index_ corresponding to the factor ![Image 10: {\displaystyle V^{*}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5910e6a94f4f7ee2ee85ceed9dacef3eff7a6242)). Thus, for instance,

![Image 11: {\displaystyle t_{ab}{}^{b}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f3e720ad4094d562f4b0259590fa058d211329b2)
is the trace of a tensor ![Image 12: {\displaystyle t=t_{ab}{}^{c}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/91228a617a200192bdbbdc1ee7cfe5f214da17cb) over its last two slots. This manner of representing tensor contractions by repeated indices is formally similar to the [Einstein summation convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_summation_convention "Einstein summation convention"). However, as the indices are non-numerical, it does not imply summation: rather it corresponds to the abstract basis-independent trace operation (or [natural pairing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_pairing "Natural pairing")) between tensor factors of type ![Image 13: {\displaystyle V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/af0f6064540e84211d0ffe4dac72098adfa52845) and those of type ![Image 14: {\displaystyle V^{*}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5910e6a94f4f7ee2ee85ceed9dacef3eff7a6242).

Abstract indices and tensor spaces
----------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Abstract_index_notation&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Abstract indices and tensor spaces")]

A general homogeneous tensor is an element of a [tensor product](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_product "Tensor product") of copies of ![Image 15: {\displaystyle V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/af0f6064540e84211d0ffe4dac72098adfa52845) and ![Image 16: {\displaystyle V^{*}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5910e6a94f4f7ee2ee85ceed9dacef3eff7a6242), such as

![Image 17: {\displaystyle V\otimes V^{*}\otimes V^{*}\otimes V\otimes V^{*}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/fad0f977ecd7445c389e3d80fb2096bba3f258a0)
Label each factor in this tensor product with a Latin letter in a raised position for each contravariant ![Image 18: {\displaystyle V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/af0f6064540e84211d0ffe4dac72098adfa52845) factor, and in a lowered position for each covariant ![Image 19: {\displaystyle V^{*}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5910e6a94f4f7ee2ee85ceed9dacef3eff7a6242) position. In this way, write the product as

![Image 20: {\displaystyle V^{a}V_{b}V_{c}V^{d}V_{e}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7e2efd71bbc92bfb0d711b18fc762c55477f9ea8)
or, simply

![Image 21: {\displaystyle V^{a}{}_{bc}{}^{d}{}_{e}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/005b56f79ca6ecbe7cb01ddcf40c40778854314f)
The last two expressions denote the same object as the first. Tensors of this type are denoted using similar notation, for example:

![Image 22: {\displaystyle h^{a}{}_{bc}{}^{d}{}_{e}\in V^{a}{}_{bc}{}^{d}{}_{e}=V\otimes V^{*}\otimes V^{*}\otimes V\otimes V^{*}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/e19ffec9f4f72d3f0346e5f3d0c963b75df6f91c)
In general, whenever one contravariant and one covariant factor occur in a tensor product of spaces, there is an associated _contraction_ (or _trace_) map. For instance,

![Image 23: {\displaystyle \mathrm {Tr} _{12}:V\otimes V^{*}\otimes V^{*}\otimes V\otimes V^{*}\to V^{*}\otimes V\otimes V^{*}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/80f889d29715c04844f6dd32fcab9b3e5a522d66)
is the trace on the first two spaces of the tensor product.![Image 24: {\displaystyle \mathrm {Tr} _{15}:V\otimes V^{*}\otimes V^{*}\otimes V\otimes V^{*}\to V^{*}\otimes V^{*}\otimes V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a04a7d21eac99e09a599f0a894cfc528b9faa903) is the trace on the first and last space.

These trace operations are signified on tensors by the repetition of an index. Thus the first trace map is given by

![Image 25: {\displaystyle \mathrm {Tr} _{12}:h{}^{a}{}_{b}{}_{c}{}^{d}{}_{e}\mapsto h{}^{a}{}_{a}{}_{c}{}^{d}{}_{e}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/2bfff5eafd27de5c3b159ceb6ce057c69562a7ba)
and the second by

![Image 26: {\displaystyle \mathrm {Tr} _{15}:h{}^{a}{}_{b}{}_{c}{}^{d}{}_{e}\mapsto h{}^{a}{}_{b}{}_{c}{}^{d}{}_{a}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0344ba2be2e008f7d3c9291acb127f9c441ff1f6)
To any tensor product on a single vector space, there are associated [braiding maps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_product#Tensor_powers_and_braiding "Tensor product"). For example, the braiding map

![Image 27: {\displaystyle \tau _{(12)}:V\otimes V\rightarrow V\otimes V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5e299c1a5846e2de48bc04f661bb1c8f203e1720)
interchanges the two tensor factors (so that its action on simple tensors is given by ![Image 28: {\displaystyle \tau _{(12)}(v\otimes w)=w\otimes v}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/30a01db1ebdd0838858d6af1c30df0c78d08f166)). In general, the braiding maps are in one-to-one correspondence with elements of the [symmetric group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetric_group "Symmetric group"), acting by permuting the tensor factors. Here, ![Image 29: {\displaystyle \tau _{\sigma }}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ea9d51dc2f1ee30df36e314a43ff5584b7df6e8b) denotes the braiding map associated to the permutation ![Image 30: {\displaystyle \sigma }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/59f59b7c3e6fdb1d0365a494b81fb9a696138c36) (represented as a product of disjoint [cyclic permutations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclic_permutation "Cyclic permutation")).

Braiding maps are important in [differential geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_geometry "Differential geometry"), for instance, in order to express the [Bianchi identity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bianchi_identity "Bianchi identity"). Here let ![Image 31: {\displaystyle R}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4b0bfb3769bf24d80e15374dc37b0441e2616e33) denote the [Riemann tensor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riemann_curvature_tensor "Riemann curvature tensor"), regarded as a tensor in ![Image 32: {\displaystyle V^{*}\otimes V^{*}\otimes V^{*}\otimes V}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/2c23718e38ddf25a996b3efc68701fa117749cae). The first Bianchi identity then asserts that

![Image 33: {\displaystyle R+\tau _{(123)}R+\tau _{(132)}R=0.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/2726ae30234d12035dd13d9da9337d7e679ffba1)
Abstract index notation handles braiding as follows. On a particular tensor product, an ordering of the abstract indices is fixed (usually this is a [lexicographic ordering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lexicographic_order "Lexicographic order")). The braid is then represented in notation by permuting the labels of the indices. Thus, for instance, with the Riemann tensor

![Image 34: {\displaystyle R=R_{abc}{}^{d}\in V_{abc}{}^{d}=V^{*}\otimes V^{*}\otimes V^{*}\otimes V,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/43a3ca1f98e4e5577ca83a8cb26e8524ac40be60)
the Bianchi identity becomes

![Image 35: {\displaystyle R_{abc}{}^{d}+R_{cab}{}^{d}+R_{bca}{}^{d}=0.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a865d2f0eb304bc0a53829abd113290896e258c6)

Antisymmetrization and symmetrization
-------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Abstract_index_notation&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Antisymmetrization and symmetrization")]

A general tensor may be antisymmetrized or symmetrized, and there is according notation.

We demonstrate the notation by example. Let's antisymmetrize the type-(0,3) tensor ![Image 36: {\displaystyle \omega _{abc}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/bfeef3f0b5e1004ee088b37d523838efae99e57a), where ![Image 37: {\displaystyle \mathrm {S} _{3}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f7739a4f0628592d6e923ae31dac49b5ccd71d3d) is the symmetric group on three elements.

![Image 38: {\displaystyle \omega _{[abc]}:={\frac {1}{3!}}\sum _{\sigma \in \mathrm {S} _{3}}{{\text{sgn}}(\sigma )}\omega _{\sigma (a)\sigma (b)\sigma (c)}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ead5c0dc4d732e8c36e8c9d97ac0cb38b2b36346)
Similarly, we may symmetrize:
