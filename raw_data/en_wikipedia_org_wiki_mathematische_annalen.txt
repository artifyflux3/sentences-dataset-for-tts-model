or, formerly, Math. Annal.

) is a German mathematical research journal founded in 1868 by Alfred Clebsch and Carl Neumann . Subsequent managing editors were Felix Klein , David Hilbert , Otto Blumenthal , Erich Hecke , Heinrich Behnke , Hans Grauert , Heinz Bauer , Herbert Amann , Jean-Pierre Bourguignon , Wolfgang Lück , Nigel Hitchin , and Thomas Schick .

[ 1 ] Currently, the managing editor of Mathematische Annalen is Yoshikazu Giga ( University of Tokyo ).

Volumes 1–80 (1869–1919) were published by Teubner . Since 1920 ( vol.

81), the journal has been published by Springer . In the late 1920s, under the editorship of Hilbert, the journal became embroiled in controversy over the participation of L. E. J. Brouwer on its editorial board, a spillover from the foundational Brouwer–Hilbert controversy .

[ 2 ] Between 1945 and 1947, the journal briefly ceased publication.
