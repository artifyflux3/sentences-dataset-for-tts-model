**Marcel Berger** (14 April 1927 – 15 October 2016) was a French [mathematician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematician "Mathematician"), doyen of French [differential geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_geometry "Differential geometry"), and a former director of the [Institut des Hautes Études Scientifiques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institut_des_Hautes_%C3%89tudes_Scientifiques "Institut des Hautes Études Scientifiques") (IHÉS), France.

After studying from 1948 to 1951 at the [École normale supérieure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89cole_normale_sup%C3%A9rieure_(Paris) "École normale supérieure (Paris)") in Paris, Berger obtained in 1954 his PhD from the [University of Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Paris "University of Paris"), with thesis written under the direction of [André Lichnerowicz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Lichnerowicz "André Lichnerowicz").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcel_Berger#cite_note-1) From 1958 to 1964 he taught at the [University of Strasbourg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Strasbourg "University of Strasbourg") and had visiting positions at the [Massachusetts Institute of Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") and the [University of California, Berkeley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley "University of California, Berkeley"). From 1964 to 1966 he taught at the [University of Nice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_Nice-Sophia-Antipolis "Université Nice-Sophia-Antipolis"), after which he joined the [University of Paris VII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_Diderot_University "Paris Diderot University"). From 1985 to 1993 he served as director of the IHÉS.

Formerly residing in Le Castera in [Lasseube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lasseube "Lasseube"), Berger was instrumental in [Mikhail Gromov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mikhail_Gromov_(mathematician) "Mikhail Gromov (mathematician)")'s accepting positions both at the University of Paris and at the IHÉS.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcel_Berger#cite_note-2)
