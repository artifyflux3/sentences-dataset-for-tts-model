From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Special character in text processing

The **zero-width space** (rendered: ​; [HTML entity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HTML_entity "HTML entity"): &ZeroWidthSpace; or &#8203;), abbreviated **ZWSP**, is a [non-printing character](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Control_character "Control character") used in computerized [typesetting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typesetting "Typesetting") to indicate where the word boundaries are, without actually displaying a visible space in the rendered text. This enables text-processing systems for scripts that do not use explicit spacing to recognize where word boundaries are for the purpose of handling [line breaks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Line_wrap_and_word_wrap "Line wrap and word wrap") appropriately.

The zero-width space is [Unicode character](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode_character "Unicode character")`U+200B`, and is located in the Unicode [General Punctuation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Punctuation "General Punctuation") block. In HTML, it can be represented by the [character entity reference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Character_entity_reference "Character entity reference")`&ZeroWidthSpace;`.

Purpose
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Zero-width_space&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Purpose")]

The zero-width space marks a potential line break without [hyphenation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syllabification "Syllabification"). Its semantics and [HTML](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HyperText_Markup_Language "HyperText Markup Language") implementation are similar to the [soft hyphen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soft_hyphen "Soft hyphen"), but soft hyphens display a hyphen character at the point where the line is broken.

The zero-width space can be used to mark word breaks in languages without visible space between words, such as [Thai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thai_language "Thai language"), [Myanmar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar_language "Myanmar language"), [Khmer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khmer_language "Khmer language"), and [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "Japanese language").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-width_space#cite_note-Unicode_Standard-1)

In [justified](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justification_(typesetting) "Justification (typesetting)") text, the rendering engine may add inter-character spacing, also known as letter spacing, between letters separated by a zero-width space, unlike around fixed-width spaces.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-width_space#cite_note-Unicode_Standard-1)

### Example

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Zero-width_space&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Example")]

To show the effect of the zero-width space in text, the following words have been separated with zero-width spaces:

> Lorem​Ipsum​Dolor​Sit​Amet​Consectetur​Adipiscing​Elit​Sed​Do​Eiusmod​Tempor​Incididunt​Ut​Labore​Et​Dolore​Magna​Aliqua​Ut​Enim​Ad​Minim​Veniam​Quis​Nostrud​Exercitation​Ullamco​Laboris​Nisi​Ut​Aliquip​Ex​Ea​Commodo​Consequat​Duis​Aute​Irure​Dolor​In​Reprehenderit​In​Voluptate​Velit​Esse​Cillum​Dolore​Eu​Fugiat​Nulla​Pariatur​Excepteur​Sint​Occaecat​Cupidatat​Non​Proident​Sunt​In​Culpa​Qui​Officia​Deserunt​Mollit​Anim​Id​Est​Laborum

By contrast, the following words have not been separated:

> LoremIpsumDolorSitAmetConsecteturAdipiscingElitSedDoEiusmodTemporIncididuntUtLaboreEtDoloreMagnaAliquaUtEnimAdMinimVeniamQuisNostrudExercitationUllamcoLaborisNisiUtAliquipExEaCommodoConsequatDuisAuteIrureDolorInReprehenderitInVoluptateVelitEsseCillumDoloreEuFugiatNullaPariaturExcepteurSintOccaecatCupidatatNonProidentSuntInCulpaQuiOfficiaDeseruntMollitAnimIdEstLaborum

The first text is broken into lines but only at word boundaries, and resizing the browser window will [re-break](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflowable_document "Reflowable document") the text accordingly, while the second text is not broken at all.

Usage
-----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Zero-width_space&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Usage")]

### HTML

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Zero-width_space&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: HTML")]

In [HTML](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HTML "HTML") pages, the HTML element `<wbr>` functions as a zero-width space. In [Internet Explorer 6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_Explorer_6 "Internet Explorer 6"), the zero-width space was not supported in some fonts.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-width_space#cite_note-2)

### Prohibition in domain names

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Zero-width_space&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Prohibition in domain names")]

[ICANN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ICANN "ICANN") rules prohibit [domain names](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domain_names "Domain names") from containing non-displayed characters, including the zero-width space, and most browsers prohibit their use within domain names because they can be used to create a [homograph attack](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IDN_homograph_attack#Non-displayable_characters "IDN homograph attack"), where a malicious URL is visually indistinguishable from a legitimate one.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-width_space#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-width_space#cite_note-4)

Encoding
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Zero-width_space&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Encoding")]

The zero-width space character is encoded in [Unicode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode "Unicode") as U+200B ZERO WIDTH SPACE.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-width_space#cite_note-5)

In HTML, it can be referenced as `&ZeroWidthSpace;`, `&#8203;` or `&#x200B;`. Additionally, the character entities `&NegativeThickSpace;`, `&NegativeMediumSpace;`, `&NegativeThinSpace;`, and `&NegativeVeryThinSpace;` all also refer to the zero-width space, contrary to what their names suggest.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-width_space#cite_note-6)

In HTML `mailto:`tags[_[clarify](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Please\_clarify "Wikipedia:Please clarify")_], %E2%80%8B renders a zero-width space (but may interfere with correctly copying the email link).[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

The [TeX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TeX "TeX") representation is `\hskip0pt`; the [LaTeX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LaTeX "LaTeX") representation is `\hspace{0pt}`;[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-width_space#cite_note-7) and the [groff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Groff_(software) "Groff (software)") representation is `\:`.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-width_space#cite_note-8)
