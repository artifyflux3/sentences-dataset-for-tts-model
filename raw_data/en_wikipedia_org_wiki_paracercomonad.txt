[ 2 ] Taxonomically, they comprise the family Paracercomonadidae , order Paracercomonadida and subclass Paracercomonada . Due to their morphological similarities to the cercomonads , members of this family were grouped with Cercomonas and similar taxa from the beginning. However, their similarities are due to convergent evolution .
