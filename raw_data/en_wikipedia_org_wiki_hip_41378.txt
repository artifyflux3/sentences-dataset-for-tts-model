The star has an apparent magnitude of 8.92. This F-type main sequence dwarf has a mass of 1.15 M â˜‰ and a radius of 1.25 R â˜‰ . It has a surface temperature of about 6,251 K .

Planetary system [ edit ] In 2016, the K2 Kepler mission discovered five planets around HIP 41378, with sizes ranging from 2 times the size of Earth to the size of Jupiter , out to about 1 AU for the outermost planet.

[ 9 ] The semi-major axes were not known until K2 Haute-Provence Observatory radial velocity data was obtained in 2019. Also, a sixth non-transiting planet, HIP 41378 g, was discovered, along with speculation that additional planets may exist between HIP 41378 g and HIP 41378 d. The planet HIP 41378 f was also found to likely have optically-thick rings or a highly extended atmosphere.
