Straus Francis Joseph Murray (February 3, 1911 – March 15, 1996) was a mathematician , known for his foundational work (with John von Neumann ) on functional analysis , and what subsequently became known as von Neumann algebras . He received his BA from Columbia College in 1932 and PhD from Columbia University in 1936.

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] He taught at Duke University .

In 1967 he was awarded the Outstanding Civilian Service Medal by the U. S. Army.
