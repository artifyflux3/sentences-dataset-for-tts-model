The institute manages two ocean museums (in Monaco and Paris) and lobbies globally for the preservation of the oceans' ecology.

History [ edit ] The Institut océanographique was founded in 1906 by Albert I, Prince of Monaco [ 1 ] (the International Hydrographic Organization was launched in Monaco in 1921).

In 1957, Jacques Cousteau was appointed director of the Institut océanographique.

[ 2 ] In 1961, the Institut océanographique reached an agreement with the International Atomic Energy Agency to relocate the International Laboratory of Marine Radioactivity in Monaco.

[ 1 ] In 1998, the Institut océanographique organized the second International Aquariology Congress (the first edition was held in 1958). The European Union of Aquarium Curators was created during this event.

[ 3 ] The institute implemented the "Monaco Blue Initiative", a global initiative focused on deep sea biodiversity and large marine species, and backed by UNESCO .

[ 4 ] In 2016, hundreds of cubic meters of archives belonging to the Institut were found in the Schœlcher campus of the University of the French West Indies ( Martinique ).

[ 5 ] In 2019, the Institut océanographique invested 5 million euros in the opening of a care center for marine species, especially sick or injured turtles.
