**Hemolithin** (sometimes confused with the similar space polymer **[hemoglycin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemoglycin "Hemoglycin")**) is a proposed [protein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein "Protein") containing [iron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron "Iron") and lithium, of [extraterrestrial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life "Extraterrestrial life") origin, according to an unpublished [preprint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Preprint "Preprint").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-ARX-20200222-1)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-SN-20200326-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-PHYS-20200303-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-ES-20200317-8) The result has not been published in any [peer-reviewed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peer-review "Peer-review")[scientific journal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_journal "Scientific journal"). The protein was purportedly found inside two [CV3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbonaceous_chondrite#CV_group "Carbonaceous chondrite")[meteorites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meteorite "Meteorite"), [Allende](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allende_meteorite "Allende meteorite") and Acfer-086,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-ARX-20200222-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-VC-20200228-2)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-TMS-20200303-4) by a team of scientists led by [Harvard University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_University "Harvard University") biochemist Julie McGeoch.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-ARX-20200222-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-VC-20200228-2) The report of the discovery was met with some skepticism and suggestions that the researchers had extrapolated too far from incomplete data.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-NS-20200303-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-SPC-20200303-10)

Sources
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hemolithin&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Sources")]

The detected hemolithin protein was reported to have been found inside two CV3 meteorites Allende and Acfer 086.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-TMS-20200303-4) Acfer-086, where the complete molecule was detected rather than fragments (Allende), was discovered in [Agemour, Algeria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria "Algeria") in 1990.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-VC-20200228-2)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-MB-19910901-5)

Structure
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hemolithin&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Structure")]

According to the researchers' [mass spectrometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_spectrometry "Mass spectrometry"), hemolithin is largely composed of [glycine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycine "Glycine") and [hydroxyglycine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-proteinogenic_amino_acids#Chalcogen "Non-proteinogenic amino acids")[amino acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acid "Amino acid").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-SPC-20200303-10) The researchers noted that the protein was related to "very high extraterrestrial" ratios of [Deuterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deuterium "Deuterium")/Hydrogen (D/H);[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-VC-20200228-2) such high D/H ratios are not found anywhere on Earth, but are "consistent with long-period comets"[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-SA-20200302-3) and suggest, as reported, "that the protein was formed in the [proto-solar disc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protoplanetary_disk "Protoplanetary disk") or perhaps even earlier, in [interstellar molecular clouds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud") that existed long before the Sun's birth".[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-VC-20200228-2)

A natural development of hemolithin may have started with glycine forming first, and then later linking with other glycine molecules into [polymer chains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymer#Chain_length "Polymer"), and later still, combining with iron and oxygen atoms. The iron and oxygen atoms reside at the end of the newly found molecule. The researchers speculate that the [iron oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron_oxide "Iron oxide") grouping formed at the end of the molecule may be able to absorb [photons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photon "Photon"), thereby enabling the molecule to split water (H 2 O) into hydrogen and [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen") and, as a result, produce a source of energy that might be useful to the [development of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abiogenesis "Abiogenesis").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-VC-20200228-2)

[Exobiologist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrobiology "Astrobiology") and chemist [Jeffrey Bada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeffrey_L._Bada "Jeffrey L. Bada") expressed concerns about the possible protein discovery commenting, "The main problem is the occurrence of [hydroxyglycine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-proteinogenic_amino_acids#Chalcogen "Non-proteinogenic amino acids"), which, to my knowledge, has never before been reported in meteorites or in [prebiotic experiments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abiogenesis "Abiogenesis"). Nor is it found in any proteins. ... Thus, this amino acid is a strange one to find in a meteorite, and I am highly suspicious of the results."[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-SPC-20200303-10) Likewise, Lee Cronin of the [University of Glasgow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Glasgow "University of Glasgow") stated, "The structure makes no sense."[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-NS-20200303-9)

Hemolithin is the name given to a protein molecule isolated from two [CV3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbonaceous_chondrite#CV_group "Carbonaceous chondrite") meteorites, Allende and Acfer-086. Its deuterium to hydrogen ratio is 26 times terrestrial which is consistent with it having formed in an [interstellar molecular cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_cloud "Interstellar cloud"), or later in the [protoplanetary disk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protoplanetary_disk "Protoplanetary disk") at the start of the [Solar System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_System "Solar System") 4.567 billion years ago. The elements hydrogen, lithium, [carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon"), oxygen, [nitrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen "Nitrogen") and iron that it is composed of, were all available for the first time 13 billion years ago after the first generation of [massive stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star#Massive_stars "Star") ended in [nucleosynthetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleosynthetic "Nucleosynthetic") events.

The research leading to the discovery of Hemolithin started in 2007 when [another protein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ATP_synthase_subunit_C "ATP synthase subunit C"), one of the first to form on Earth, was observed to entrap water.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-JRSI-20070917-11) That property being useful to chemistry before biochemistry on earth developed, theoretical [enthalpy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enthalpy "Enthalpy") calculations on the condensation of [amino acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acids "Amino acids") were performed in gas phase space asking: "whether amino acids could polymerize to protein in space?" - they could, and their water of condensation aided their polymerization.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-PLOS-20140721-12) This led to several manuscripts of isotope and mass information on Hemolithin.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-ARX-20200222-1)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-MPS-2015-13)[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-ARX-20170728-14)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemolithin#cite_note-ARX-20181115-15)

A comment from the Harvard research leader on Hemolithin/Hemoglycin JEMMc – Hemolithin is now termed hemoglycin. Hemoglycin, a space polymer of glycine and iron has been extensively characterized [1-11] and it does contain lithium in some samples [5]. The research and now needs to be considered in the context of 4 areas of astronomy and planetary science: 1st in astronomy, the period between Pop III and Pop II stars, when the constituent elements of hemoglycin first formed even as early as 500My into cosmic time [1]. 2nd in molecular clouds and protoplanetary disks where the polymer is likely to form and function in accretion [6,9,10]. Thus, the polymer could be a major player in solar system formation throughout the Universe. 3rd after in-fall to planets like Earth, where on Earth it could have kick-started "The Great Oxygenation Event" (GOE) [9]. 4th on exo-planets that evolve biochemistry like Earth, it could be asked whether the formation of DNA involves hemoglycin as a template. Guanine and cytosine nucleotide bases could form and bind to the 5 nm glycine rods of in-fall hemoglycin to start the coding of glycine [12].

Hemoglycin is not a biological molecule, being outside of biochemistry, that is, abiotic. It may have first formed 500 million years into cosmic time as a structure that could absorb photons from 0.2-15 μm [7,8,9,10], be available throughout the Universe, and provide energy to drive adjacent space chemistry. On its in-fall to exo-planets like Earth it could absorb solar ultraviolet and donate energy to early chemical systems. Hemoglycin could therefore be thought of as an abiotic absorber of light, a supplier of energy and an accretor of matter. Synthetic hemoglycin synthesis will be attempted in 2025 to aid acquisition of a refined x-ray diffraction set for its structure. Hemoglycin crystals from meteorites, and stromatolites, to date are fiber-like or multiple [6,8,9]. A comparison of the MALDI mass spectrometry fragmentation patterns [5,11] of synthetic and extracted hemoglycin will be informative.
