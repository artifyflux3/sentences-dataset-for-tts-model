**Christmas on the International Space Station** covers the celebration of [Christmas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas "Christmas") on the [International Space Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Space_Station "International Space Station"). Christmas is celebrated each year by the [International Space Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Space_Station "International Space Station") crew, their families, and ground-staff. Crew are given time off duty according to their respective culture, religion/faith and ethnicity. The [Russian Orthodox Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Orthodox_Church "Russian Orthodox Church") celebrates Christmas according to the [Julian calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_calendar "Julian calendar"), whilst the [Catholic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church "Catholic Church") and the various [Protestant denominations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protestantism "Protestantism") use the [Gregorian calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gregorian_calendar "Gregorian calendar") so the crew may celebrate Christmas more than once on the station choosing between 25 December or 6, 7 or 19 January.

[Expedition 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expedition_1 "Expedition 1") arrived on the ISS on 2 November 2000, celebrating their first Christmas on board the station later that year.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-2) Other celebrations included [Expedition 30](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expedition_30 "Expedition 30"), with the arrival of [Donald Pettit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Pettit "Donald Pettit"), [Oleg Kononenko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oleg_Kononenko "Oleg Kononenko"), and [André Kuipers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Kuipers "André Kuipers").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-4) The station has been continuously occupied since 2000, so every Christmas has been experienced by a crew.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-geekwire.com-5) The holiday is popular enough that one of the traditions that has developed is having a Christmas dinner.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-geekwire.com-5)

On 25 December 2011 the crew of [Expedition 30](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expedition_30 "Expedition 30") took a break on Christmas to take a crew photo.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-nasa.gov-6) This included Dan Burbank, Oleg Kononenko, Don Pettit, Anatoly Ivanishin, Andre Kuipers, and Anton Shkaplerov.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-nasa.gov-6)

On 24 December 2013, astronauts made a rare Christmas Eve [extravehicular activity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extravehicular_activity "Extravehicular activity"), installing a new ammonia pump for the station's cooling system. The faulty cooling system had failed earlier in the month, halting many of the station's science experiments. Astronauts had to brave a "mini blizzard" of noxious ammonia while installing the new pump. It was only the second Christmas Eve spacewalk in NASA history.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-7)

On 25 December 2016 the crew celebrated Christmas by floating in micro-gravity and opening Christmas presents recently delivered on a Japanese cargo spacecraft.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-space.com-8) One astronaut wore a Santa hat in orbit.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-space.com-8) The French astronaut [Thomas Pesquet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Pesquet "Thomas Pesquet") shared special French food with station crew.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-space.com-8)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-geekwire.com-5) Pesquet also made a Christmas-time special video for the ESA.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station#cite_note-geekwire.com-5)
