The **trademark symbol**‚Ñ¢ is a [symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbol "Symbol") to indicate that the preceding mark is a [trademark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark "Trademark"), specifically an [unregistered trademark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unregistered_trademark "Unregistered trademark"). It complements the [registered trademark symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol "Registered trademark symbol")¬Æ which is reserved for trademarks registered with an appropriate government agency.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark_symbol#cite_note-1)

In Canada, an equivalent _marque de commerce_ symbol, (U+1F16AüÖ™RAISED MC SIGN) is used in French.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark_symbol#cite_note-2) Canada also has an [official mark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Official_mark "Official mark") symbol, ‚ü®‚ìÇ‚ü©, to indicate that a name or design used by Canadian public authorities is protected.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark_symbol#cite_note-3) Some German publications, especially dictionaries, also use a _Warenzeichen_ grapheme, (U+1F12EüÑÆCIRCLED WZ), which is informative and independent of the actual protection status of the name.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark_symbol#cite_note-4)

Use
---

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Trademark_symbol&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Use")]

Use of the trademark symbol indicates an assertion that a word, image, or other sign is a trademark; it does not indicate registration or impart enhanced protections. Registered trademarks are indicated using the [registered trademark symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registered_trademark_symbol "Registered trademark symbol"), ¬Æ, and in many jurisdictions it is unlawful or illegal to use the registered trademark symbol with a mark that has not been registered.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark_symbol#cite_note-5)

The [service mark symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service_mark_symbol "Service mark symbol"), ‚Ñ†, is used to indicate the assertion of a [service mark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service_mark "Service mark") (a trademark for the provision of services). The service mark symbol is less commonly used than the trademark sign, especially outside the United States.

On [social media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_media "Social media"), the trademark symbol has seen use in an [ironic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ironic "Ironic") fashion, highlighting a concept as if it were important enough to warrant its own trademark, for example, "Official Bisexual Haircut‚Ñ¢".[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark_symbol#cite_note-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trademark_symbol#cite_note-7) This is a non-standard usage of the symbol, which confers no special legal rights.

Non-standard substitutions
--------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Trademark_symbol&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Non-standard substitutions")]

The letters ‚ü®T‚ü© and ‚ü®M‚ü© are sometimes seen paired in an attempt to emulate the trademark symbol. Methods include

*   (TM), the letters in normal form, enclosed in parentheses
*   TM, the letters written in [small caps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Small_caps "Small caps"), on the [baseline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baseline_(typography) "Baseline (typography)")
*   TM, the letters written as [superscripts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superscript "Superscript"), as in mathematical [exponentiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exponentiation "Exponentiation")
*   ·µÄ·¥π, using symbols from the [Phonetic Extensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phonetic_Extensions "Phonetic Extensions") block in [Unicode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode "Unicode")
