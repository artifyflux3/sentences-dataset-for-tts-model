Chemical compound

**Methyl isocyanide** or **isocyanomethane** is an [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") and a member of the [isocyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanide "Isocyanide") family. This colorless liquid is isomeric and isoelectronic to methyl cyanide ([acetonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetonitrile "Acetonitrile")), but its reactivity is very different. In contrast to the faintly sweet, ethereal odor of acetonitrile, the smell of methyl isocyanide, like that of other simple volatile isocyanides, is distinctly penetrating and vile.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide#cite_note-1) Methyl isocyanide is mainly used for making 5-membered [heterocyclic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heterocyclic "Heterocyclic") rings. The C-N distance in methyl isocyanide is very short, 1.158 Å as is characteristic of isocyanides.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide#cite_note-2)

Preparation and uses
--------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methyl_isocyanide&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Preparation and uses")]

Methyl isocyanide was first prepared by [A. Gautier](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=A._Gautier&action=edit&redlink=1 "A. Gautier (page does not exist)") by reaction of [silver cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver_cyanide "Silver cyanide") with [methyl iodide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_iodide "Methyl iodide").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide#cite_note-4) The common method for preparing methyl isocyanides is the dehydration of [_N_-methylformamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-Methylformamide "N-Methylformamide").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide#cite_note-5) Many metal cyanides react with methylating agents to give complexes of methyl isocyanide.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide#cite_note-6) This kind of reactivity has been invoked as being relevant to the origin of life.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide#cite_note-7)

Methyl isocyanide is useful for the preparation of diverse heterocycles. It is often used to prepare [transition metal isocyanide complexes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transition_metal_isocyanide_complexes "Transition metal isocyanide complexes").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide#cite_note-8)

Safety
------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methyl_isocyanide&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Safety")]

Methyl isocyanide is very endothermic (Δ f _H_⦵(g) = +150.2 kJ/mol, 3.66 kJ/g) and can isomerize explosively to acetonitrile.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide#cite_note-9) A sample exploded when heated in a sealed ampoule, and during redistillation at 59°C and 1 bar, a drop of liquid fell back into the dry boiler flask and exploded violently. The explosive decomposition of methyl isocyanide has been studied in detail.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanide#cite_note-10)
