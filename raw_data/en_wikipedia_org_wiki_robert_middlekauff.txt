Robert Lawrence Middlekauff (July 5, 1929 – March 10, 2021) was a professor of colonial and early United States history at the University of California, Berkeley .

[ 1 ] Career [ edit ] In 1983, Middlekauff became the President of Huntington Library, Art Collections and Botanical Gardens in San Marino, California , until 1987.

[ 2 ] He was elected to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences in 1984.

[ 3 ] In 1987, Middlekauff became a professor at UC Berkeley .

[ 4 ] Middlekauff is best known for The Glorious Cause , a history of the American Revolutionary War , which was a finalist for the Pulitzer Prize in 1983.

[ 5 ] He was the Harold Vyvyan Harmsworth Professor of American History in 1996–97. In 1997, he was elected to the American Philosophical Society in 1997.

[ 6 ] Personal life [ edit ] He was born in Yakima, Washington .

[ 5 ] Middlekauff died at the age of 91 from complications of a stroke on March 10, 2021, in Pleasanton, California .
