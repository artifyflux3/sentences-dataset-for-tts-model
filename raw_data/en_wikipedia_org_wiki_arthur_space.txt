**Charles Arthur Space** (October 12, 1908[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Space#cite_note-1) – January 13, 1983) was an American film, television and stage actor. Today's audiences know him as the eccentric inventor opposite [Laurel and Hardy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laurel_and_Hardy "Laurel and Hardy") in _[The Big Noise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Big\_Noise\_(1944\_film) "The Big Noise (1944 film)")_ (1944), and as veterinarian Doc Weaver in 39 episodes of the [CBS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CBS "CBS") television series _[Lassie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lassie\_(1954\_TV\_series) "Lassie (1954 TV series)")_.

Born in [New Brunswick, New Jersey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Brunswick,_New_Jersey "New Brunswick, New Jersey"), Space first delved into acting at [Douglass College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douglass_College "Douglass College").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Space#cite_note-upi-2)

Space began his career in [summer stock theater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Summer_stock_theater "Summer stock theater") and eventually began appearing on Broadway.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Space#cite_note-nytimes-3) His [Broadway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadway_theatre "Broadway theatre") credits include _Three Men on a Horse_ and _Awake and Sing_.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Space#cite_note-upi-2)

Producer [Edward Finney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Finney "Edward Finney") cast Space as an urbane hoodlum in the 1941 [crime drama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Police_procedural "Police procedural")_Riot Squad_, starring [Richard Cromwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Cromwell_(actor) "Richard Cromwell (actor)") and released by [PRC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Producers_Releasing_Corporation "Producers Releasing Corporation"). He jumped from the PRC company to the [Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer "Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer") studio. Under contract to MGM, Space appeared alongside [Abbott and Costello](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbott_and_Costello "Abbott and Costello") in _[Rio Rita](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rio\_Rita\_(1942\_film) "Rio Rita (1942 film)")_, and had roles in _[Tortilla Flat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tortilla\_Flat\_(film) "Tortilla Flat (film)")_, _[Grand Central Murder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand\_Central\_Murder "Grand Central Murder")_, _[Andy Hardy's Double Life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andy\_Hardy%27s\_Double\_Life "Andy Hardy's Double Life")_, and others. Space remained with MGM but took assignments at other studios, including [Twentieth Century-Fox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twentieth_Century-Fox "Twentieth Century-Fox"), [Columbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columbia_Pictures "Columbia Pictures"), and [Universal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_Pictures "Universal Pictures"). At Fox, he appeared in the [Laurel and Hardy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laurel_and_Hardy "Laurel and Hardy") comedy _The Big Noise_, in which Space played the second lead to the starring team.

Space appeared in three [Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_Pictures "Republic Pictures") chapter plays: _[Government Agents vs. Phantom Legion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government\_Agents\_vs.\_Phantom\_Legion "Government Agents vs. Phantom Legion")_, _[Canadian Mounties vs. Atomic Invaders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canadian\_Mounties\_vs.\_Atomic\_Invaders "Canadian Mounties vs. Atomic Invaders")_, and _[Panther Girl of the Kongo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panther\_Girl\_of\_the\_Kongo "Panther Girl of the Kongo")_.

In 1953, Space played Lt. Col. [William Barrett Travis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Barrett_Travis "William Barrett Travis"), the commander during the siege at the Alamo, in _[The Man from the Alamo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Man\_from\_the\_Alamo "The Man from the Alamo")_ (1953). Space was unbilled.

In 1954, Space played the bandit [Black Bart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Bolles "Charles Bolles"), or Charles Bolles, in an episode of the [syndicated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Television_syndication "Television syndication")[Western television series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westerns_on_television "Westerns on television")_[Stories of the Century](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stories\_of\_the\_Century "Stories of the Century")_.

Throughout the mid-1950s, Space continued appearing in films such as _[The Spirit of St. Louis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Spirit\_of\_St.\_Louis\_(film) "The Spirit of St. Louis (film)")_ with [James Stewart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Stewart "James Stewart") while guest starring on various television series. He appeared four times as Col. Tomkin in the [ABC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company "American Broadcasting Company")[Western](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_(genre) "Western (genre)") series, _[Colt .45](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colt\_.45\_(TV\_series) "Colt .45 (TV series)")_, starring [Wayde Preston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wayde_Preston "Wayde Preston"). During this time, Space had a recurring role as veterinarian Dr. Frank Weaver on _Lassie (Jeff’s Collie_ in syndication).

Space was cast as Ben Hudson in the 1959 episode "Hang 'Em High", on the [syndicated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Television_syndication "Television syndication")[anthology series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthology_series "Anthology series"), _[Death Valley Days](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death\_Valley\_Days "Death Valley Days")_, hosted by [Stanley Andrews](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanley_Andrews "Stanley Andrews"). The dramatization focuses on the completion in 1861 of the [first transcontinental telegraph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_transcontinental_telegraph "First transcontinental telegraph") line. Hudson determines that Confederates have attempted to [sabotage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabotage "Sabotage") construction because the telegraph would most benefit the Union government. [Paul Birch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Birch_(actor) "Paul Birch (actor)") and [William Schallert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Schallert "William Schallert") were cast in the episode as Mike Walsh and Ellis Higby, respectively.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Space#cite_note-4)

In 1960, Space landed the role of the practical farmer Herbert Brown in the 58-episode [NBC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NBC "NBC") television series _[National Velvet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National\_Velvet\_(TV\_series) "National Velvet (TV series)")_, with [Lori Martin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lori_Martin "Lori Martin") as his equestrian daughter, Velvet Brown, and [Ann Doran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ann_Doran "Ann Doran") as his wife, Martha.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Space#cite_note-etvs-5) After the series ended in 1962, Space continued acting in both television and films. Among his roles were four _[Perry Mason](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perry\_Mason\_(1957\_TV\_series) "Perry Mason (1957 TV series)")_ appearances between 1958 and 1964. In his first appearance he played murder suspect Willard Scott in "The Case of the Rolling Bones," and his final role was as murder suspect Edgarton Cartwell in "The Case of the Paper Bullets." In 1969 Space appeared as Sam Foster on the TV series _[The Virginian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Virginian\_(TV\_series) "The Virginian (TV series)")_ in the episode titled "A Woman of Stone."

In 1978, he appeared in an episode of [The Waltons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Waltons "The Waltons") titled "The Beau", playing Grandma Walton's former boyfriend Marcus Dane. His last role was in a 1981 episode of the television series, _[Walking Tall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walking\_Tall\_(TV\_series) "Walking Tall (TV series)")_.

Space died of cancer at his home in [Hollywood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollywood,_Los_Angeles,_California "Hollywood, Los Angeles, California") on January 13, 1983, at the age of 74.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Space#cite_note-nytimes-3)
