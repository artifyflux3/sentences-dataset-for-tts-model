**Basic research**, also called **pure research**, **fundamental research**, **basic science**, or **pure science**, is a type of [scientific research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_research "Scientific research") with the aim of improving scientific [theories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theory "Theory") for better understanding and [prediction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prediction#Science "Prediction") of natural or other phenomena.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Research2014avg2-1) In contrast, [applied research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_research "Applied research") uses scientific theories to develop [technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology "Technology") or techniques, which can be used to intervene and _alter_ natural or other phenomena. Though often driven simply by [curiosity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curiosity "Curiosity"),[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-NIGMS-2) basic research often fuels the [technological innovations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technological_innovation "Technological innovation") of [applied science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_science "Applied science").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-3) The two aims are often practiced simultaneously in coordinated [research and development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Research_and_development "Research and development").

In addition to innovations, basic research serves to provide insights and public support of nature, possibly improving [conservation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_biology "Conservation biology") efforts.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-5) Technological innovations may influence [engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Engineering "Engineering") concepts, such as the beak of a [kingfisher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingfisher "Kingfisher") influencing the design of a high-speed bullet train.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-6)

> **Despite smart people working on this problem for 50 years, we're still discovering surprisingly basic things about the earliest history of our world. It's quite humbling.** — Matija Ćuk, scientist at the [SETI Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SETI_Institute "SETI Institute") and lead researcher, November 2016[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-7)

Basic research advances fundamental knowledge about the world. It focuses on creating and [refuting or supporting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falsifiability "Falsifiability")[theories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_theory "Scientific theory") that explain observed phenomena. Pure research is the source of most new scientific ideas and ways of thinking about the world. It can be [exploratory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exploratory_research "Exploratory research"), [descriptive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Descriptive_research "Descriptive research"), or explanatory; however, explanatory research is the most common.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Basic research generates new ideas, principles, and theories, which may not be immediately utilized but nonetheless form the basis of progress and development in different fields. Today's computers, for example, could not exist without research in pure mathematics conducted over a century ago, for which there was no known practical application at the time. Basic research rarely helps practitioners directly with their everyday concerns; nevertheless, it stimulates new ways of thinking that have the potential to revolutionize and dramatically improve how practitioners deal with a problem in the future.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

In the United States, basic research is funded mainly by the federal government and done mainly at universities and institutes.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-8) As government funding has diminished in the 2010s, however, private funding is increasingly important.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-NYT31514-9)

Basic versus applied science
----------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Basic_research&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Basic versus applied science")]

Applied science focuses on the development of technology and techniques. In contrast, basic science develops scientific knowledge and predictions, principally in [natural sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_science "Natural science") but also in other empirical sciences, which are used as the scientific foundation for applied science. Basic science develops and establishes _information_ to predict phenomena and perhaps to understand nature, whereas [applied science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_science "Applied science") uses portions of basic science to develop _interventions_ via technology or technique to alter events or outcomes.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Davis-10)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-McCormick-11) Applied and basic sciences can interface closely in [research and development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Research_and_development "Research and development").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Piel-12)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-13) The interface between basic research and applied research has been studied by the National Science Foundation.

> A worker in basic scientific research is motivated by a driving curiosity about the unknown. When his explorations yield new knowledge, he experiences the satisfaction of those who first attain the summit of a mountain or the upper reaches of a river flowing through unmapped territory. Discovery of truth and understanding of nature are his objectives. His professional standing among his fellows depends upon the originality and soundness of his work. Creativeness in science is of a cloth with that of the poet or painter.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Research2014avg-14)

It conducted a study in which it traced the relationship between basic scientific research efforts and the development of major innovations, such as oral contraceptives and videotape recorders. This study found that basic research played a key role in the development in all of the innovations. The number of basic science research[_[clarification needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Please\_clarify "Wikipedia:Please clarify")_] that assisted in the production of a given innovation peaked between 20 and 30 years before the innovation itself. While most innovation takes the form of applied science and most innovation occurs in the private sector, basic research is a necessary precursor to almost all applied science and associated instances of innovation. Roughly 76% of basic research is conducted by universities.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-15)

A distinction can be made between basic science and disciplines such as medicine and technology.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Davis-10)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-McCormick-11)[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Smith-16)[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-17)[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-18) They can be grouped as _STM_ (science, technology, and medicine; not to be confused with _STEM_ [science, technology, engineering, and mathematics]) or _[STS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science,\_technology,\_and\_society "Science, technology, and society")_ (science, technology, and society). These groups are interrelated and influence each other,[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Holtzman-19)[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Strong-McPherson-20)[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Karhausen-21)[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Bayertz-Nevers-22)[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Pickstone-Worboys-23) although they may differ in the specifics such as methods and standards.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-McCormick-11)[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Smith-16)[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-Pickstone-Worboys-23)[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-24)[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-25)[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-26)[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-27)[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-28)[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-29)[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-30)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-31)[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-32)[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-33)[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-34)[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-35)[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-36)[_[excessive citations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing\_sources#Bundling\_citations "Wikipedia:Citing sources")_]

The [Nobel Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize "Nobel Prize") mixes basic with applied sciences for its award in [Physiology or Medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physiology_or_Medicine "Physiology or Medicine"). In contrast, the [Royal Society of London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Society_of_London "Royal Society of London") awards distinguish natural science from applied science.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research#cite_note-37)
