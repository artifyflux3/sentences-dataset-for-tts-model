c.

1260 Academic work Era Islamic Golden Age Main interests Astronomy Fakhr al-Din al-Akhlati ( Kurdish : Fexredînê Exlatî , Fakhr al-Din al-Kurdi al-Akhlati ; Arabic : فخر الدين الأخلاتي ; flourished c.

1260 ), was a Kurdish and Islamic astronomer from Anatolia , who worked at the Maragha observatory .

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] He was one of the first elites that the Persian polymath Nasir al-Din al-Tusi recruited to work in there. Al-Akhlati's life was in a period contemporaneous with the fall of Baghdad at the hands of the Mongols in 1258.
