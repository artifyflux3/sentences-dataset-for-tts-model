Handwritten notice in Japanese. Note the _komejirushi_ at the bottom of each page, preceding the footnotes.

The **reference mark** or **reference symbol** "**※**" is a [typographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typographic "Typographic") mark or word used in [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"), [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "Japanese language") and [Korean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_language "Korean language") (CJK) writing.

The symbol was used historically to call attention to an important sentence or idea, such as a prologue or footnote.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reference_mark#cite_note-1) As an indicator of a note, the mark serves the same purpose as the [asterisk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asterisk#Typography "Asterisk") in English. However, in Japanese usage, the note text is placed directly into the main text immediately after the reference mark, rather than at the bottom of the page or end of chapter as is the case in English writing.

Names
-----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Reference_mark&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Names")]

The Japanese name, **_komejirushi_** ([Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "Japanese language"): こめじるし; 米印, pronounced[[komedʑiꜜɾɯɕi]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Japanese "Help:IPA/Japanese"), lit.'rice symbol'), refers to the symbol's visual similarity to the _[kanji](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kanji "Kanji")_ for "rice" ([米](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%B1%B3 "米")).[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reference_mark#cite_note-2)

In Korean, the symbol's name, **_chamgopyo_** ([Korean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_language "Korean language"): 참고표; 参考表), simply means "reference mark". Informally, the symbol is often called **_danggujangpyo_** (당구장표; lit.'[billiard hall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billiard_hall "Billiard hall") mark'), as it is often used to indicate the presence of pool halls, due to its visual similarity to two crossed [cue sticks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cue_stick "Cue stick") and four [billiard balls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billiard_ball "Billiard ball").

In Chinese, the symbol is called _cānkǎo biāojì_ ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 参考标记; lit. 'reference mark') or _mǐ xīnghào_ ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 米星号; lit. 'rice asterisk' due to its visual similarity to [米](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%B1%B3 "米") "rice"). It is not often used in Chinese writing.

Unicode
