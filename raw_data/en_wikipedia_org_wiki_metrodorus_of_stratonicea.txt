His defection from the Epicurean school is almost unique. It is explained by Cicero as being due to his theory that the scepticism of Carneades was merely a means of attacking the Stoics on their own ground. Metrodorus held that Carneades was in reality a loyal follower of Plato . Cicero speaks of him as an orator of great fire and volubility.

[ 1 ] He flourished about 110 BC.
