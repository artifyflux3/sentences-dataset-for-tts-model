Exponential-Logarithmic (EL) distribution is a family of lifetime distributions with
decreasing failure rate , defined on the interval [0, ∞). This distribution is parameterized by two parameters p ∈ ∈ ( 0 , 1 ) {\displaystyle p\in (0,1)} and β β > 0 {\displaystyle \beta >0} .

Introduction [ edit ] The study of lengths of the lives of organisms, devices, materials, etc., is of major importance in the biological and engineering sciences. In general, the lifetime of a device is expected to exhibit decreasing failure rate (DFR) when its behavior over time is characterized by 'work-hardening' (in engineering terms) or 'immunity' (in biological terms).

The exponential-logarithmic model, together with its various properties, are studied by  Tahmasbi and Rezaei (2008).

[ 1 ] This model is obtained under the concept of population heterogeneity (through the process of
compounding).
