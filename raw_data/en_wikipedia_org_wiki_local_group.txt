The **Local Group** is the [galaxy group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy_group "Galaxy group") that includes the [Milky Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way "Milky Way"), where [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth") is located. It has a total diameter of roughly 3 [megaparsecs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parsec#Megaparsecs_and_gigaparsecs "Parsec") (10 million [light-years](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light-year "Light-year"); 9×10 19[kilometres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kilometre "Kilometre")),[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-1) and a total mass of the order of 2×10 12[solar masses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_mass "Solar mass") (4×10 42 kg).[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-2) It consists of two collections of galaxies in a "[dumbbell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dumbbell "Dumbbell")" shape; the Milky Way and its satellites form one lobe, and the [Andromeda Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andromeda_Galaxy "Andromeda Galaxy") and its satellites constitute the other. The two collections are separated by about 800 kiloparsecs (3×10 6 ly; 2×10 19 km) and are moving toward one another with a velocity of 123 km/s.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-Karachentsevetal2006-3) The group itself is a part of the larger [Virgo Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgo_Supercluster "Virgo Supercluster"), which may be a part of the [Laniakea Supercluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laniakea_Supercluster "Laniakea Supercluster"). The exact number of galaxies in the Local Group is unknown as some are occluded by the Milky Way; however, at least 80 members are known, most of which are [dwarf galaxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dwarf_galaxies "Dwarf galaxies").

The two largest members, the Andromeda and the Milky Way galaxies, are both [spiral galaxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spiral_galaxies "Spiral galaxies") with masses of about 10 12 solar masses each. Each has its own system of [satellite galaxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satellite_galaxies "Satellite galaxies"):

*   The [Andromeda Galaxy's satellite system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Andromeda%27s_satellite_galaxies "List of Andromeda's satellite galaxies") consists of [Messier 32](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Messier_32 "Messier 32") (M32), [Messier 110](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Messier_110 "Messier 110") (M110), [NGC 147](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NGC_147 "NGC 147"), [NGC 185](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NGC_185 "NGC 185"), [Andromeda I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andromeda_I "Andromeda I") (And I), [And II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_II "And II"), [And III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_III "And III"), [And V](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_V "And V"), [And VI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_VI "And VI") (also known as the Pegasus Dwarf Spheroidal Galaxy, or Pegasus dSph), [And VII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_VII "And VII") (a.k.a. the Cassiopeia Dwarf Galaxy), [And VIII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_VIII "And VIII"), [And IX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_IX "And IX"), [And X](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_X "And X"), [And XI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andromeda_XI "Andromeda XI"), [And XIX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_XIX "And XIX"), [And XXI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_XXI "And XXI") and [And XXII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/And_XXII "And XXII"), plus several additional [ultra-faint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dwarf_galaxy#Ultra-faint_dwarf_galaxies "Dwarf galaxy")[dwarf spheroidal galaxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dwarf_spheroidal_galaxies "Dwarf spheroidal galaxies").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-4)
*   The [Milky Way's satellite galaxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way%27s_satellite_galaxies "Milky Way's satellite galaxies") system comprises the [Sagittarius Dwarf Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagittarius_Dwarf_Spheroidal_Galaxy "Sagittarius Dwarf Spheroidal Galaxy"), [Large Magellanic Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_Magellanic_Cloud "Large Magellanic Cloud"), [Small Magellanic Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Small_Magellanic_Cloud "Small Magellanic Cloud"), [Canis Major Dwarf Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canis_Major_Dwarf_Galaxy "Canis Major Dwarf Galaxy") (disputed, considered by some not a galaxy), [Ursa Minor Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursa_Minor_Dwarf "Ursa Minor Dwarf") Galaxy, [Draco Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Draco_Dwarf "Draco Dwarf") Galaxy, [Carina Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carina_Dwarf "Carina Dwarf") Galaxy, [Sextans Dwarf Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sextans_Dwarf_Galaxy "Sextans Dwarf Galaxy"), [Sculptor Dwarf Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sculptor_Dwarf_Galaxy "Sculptor Dwarf Galaxy"), [Fornax Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fornax_Dwarf "Fornax Dwarf") Galaxy, [Leo I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_I_(dwarf_galaxy) "Leo I (dwarf galaxy)") (a dwarf galaxy), [Leo II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_II_(dwarf_galaxy) "Leo II (dwarf galaxy)") (a dwarf galaxy), [Ursa Major I Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursa_Major_I_Dwarf "Ursa Major I Dwarf") Galaxy and [Ursa Major II Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursa_Major_II_Dwarf "Ursa Major II Dwarf") Galaxy, plus several additional ultra-faint dwarf spheroidal galaxies.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-Koposov2015-5)

The [Triangulum Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triangulum_Galaxy "Triangulum Galaxy") (M33) is the third-largest member of the Local Group, with a mass of approximately 5×10 10 _M_☉ (1×10 41 kg), and is the third spiral galaxy.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-6) It is unclear whether the Triangulum Galaxy is a companion of the Andromeda Galaxy; the two galaxies are 750,000 light years apart,[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-7) and experienced a close passage 2–4 billion years ago which triggered star formation across Andromeda's disk. The [Pisces Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pisces_Dwarf "Pisces Dwarf") Galaxy is equidistant from the Andromeda Galaxy and the Triangulum Galaxy, so it may be a satellite of either.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-apj562_2-8)

The other members of the group are likely gravitationally secluded from these large subgroups: [IC 10](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IC_10 "IC 10"), [IC 1613](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IC_1613 "IC 1613"), [Phoenix Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenix_Dwarf "Phoenix Dwarf") Galaxy, [Leo A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_A "Leo A"), [Tucana Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tucana_Dwarf "Tucana Dwarf") Galaxy, [Cetus Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cetus_Dwarf "Cetus Dwarf") Galaxy, [Pegasus Dwarf Irregular Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pegasus_Dwarf_Irregular_Galaxy "Pegasus Dwarf Irregular Galaxy"), [Wolf–Lundmark–Melotte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolf%E2%80%93Lundmark%E2%80%93Melotte "Wolf–Lundmark–Melotte"), [Aquarius Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquarius_Dwarf "Aquarius Dwarf") Galaxy, and [Sagittarius Dwarf Irregular Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagittarius_Dwarf_Irregular_Galaxy "Sagittarius Dwarf Irregular Galaxy").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-McConnachie-9)

The membership of [NGC 3109](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NGC_3109 "NGC 3109"), with its companions [Sextans A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sextans_A "Sextans A") and the [Antlia Dwarf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antlia_Dwarf "Antlia Dwarf") Galaxy as well as [Sextans B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sextans_B "Sextans B"), [Leo P](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_P "Leo P"), [Antlia B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antlia_B "Antlia B") and possibly [Leo A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_A "Leo A"), is uncertain due to extreme distances from the center of the Local Group.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-Karachentsevetal2006-3) The [Antlia-Sextans Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antlia-Sextans_Group "Antlia-Sextans Group") is unlikely to be gravitationally bound to the Local Group due to probably lying outside the Local Group's [zero-velocity surface](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero-velocity_surface "Zero-velocity surface")—which would make it a true galaxy group of its own rather than a subgroup within the Local Group.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-10) This possible independence may, however, disappear as the Milky Way continues coalescing with Andromeda due to the increased mass, and density thereof, plausibly widening the radius of the zero-velocity surface of the Local Group.

The term "The Local Group" was introduced by [Edwin Hubble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edwin_Hubble "Edwin Hubble") in Chapter VI of his 1936 book _The Realm of the Nebulae_.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-Hubble-1936-11) There, he described it as "a typical small group of nebulae which is isolated in the general field" and delineated, by decreasing luminosity, its members to be [M31](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andromeda_Galaxy "Andromeda Galaxy"), [Milky Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way "Milky Way"), [M33](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triangulum_Galaxy "Triangulum Galaxy"), [Large Magellanic Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_Magellanic_Cloud "Large Magellanic Cloud"), [Small Magellanic Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Small_Magellanic_Cloud "Small Magellanic Cloud"), [M32](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Messier_32 "Messier 32"), [NGC 205](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NGC_205 "NGC 205"), [NGC 6822](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NGC_6822 "NGC 6822"), [NGC 185](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NGC_185 "NGC 185"), [IC 1613](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IC_1613 "IC 1613") and [NGC 147](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NGC_147 "NGC 147"). He also identified [IC 10](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IC_10 "IC 10") as a possible part of the Local Group.

Overview of the structure and trajectory of the Local Group

*   [Magellanic Stream](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magellanic_Stream "Magellanic Stream"), a stream of gas being stripped off the Magellanic Clouds due to their interaction with the Milky Way
*   [Monoceros Ring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monoceros_Ring "Monoceros Ring"), a ring of stars around the Milky Way that is proposed to consist of a [stellar stream](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_stream "Stellar stream") torn from the Canis Major Dwarf Galaxy
*   Virgo Strem, a steam formed from a dwarf galaxy.
*   Helni Stream

The galaxies of the Local Group are likely to merge together under their own mutual gravitational attractions over a timescale of [tens of billions of years](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_the_far_future "Timeline of the far future") into a single [elliptical galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptical_galaxy "Elliptical galaxy"), with the [coalescence of Andromeda and the Milky Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andromeda%E2%80%93Milky_Way_collision "Andromeda–Milky Way collision") being the predominant event in this process.[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group#cite_note-41) There is debate over whether ellipticity might be the immediate structure of the combined galaxy right after the collision or whether ellipticity might only emerge after a theoretical intermediate period of retaining a spiralling structure directly following the collision. Some even theorise a permanent superspiral or a transition toward a more [Lenticular galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lenticular_galaxy "Lenticular galaxy"), rather than a more elliptical or spiralled distribution, as the future of the Local Group's galactic merger.
