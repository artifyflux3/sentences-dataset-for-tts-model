[ 1 ] It combines elements of Gothic and the emerging romantic art trend. It is also known as A Philosopher in a Moonlit Churchyard .

[ 2 ] Inspired by the work Night-Thoughts by the English poet Edward Young , it depicts a young man in the ruins of Tintern Abbey in the middle of the night. The ivy -covered ruins shown by Loutherbourg are a capriccio , Today the painting is in the Yale Center for British Art in Connecticut as part of the Paul Mellon Collection .
