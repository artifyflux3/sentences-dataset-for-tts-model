In mathematics, the Ernst equation [ 1 ] is an integrable non-linear partial differential equation , named after the American physicist Frederick J. Ernst [ Wikidata ] .

[ 2 ] The Ernst equation [ edit ] The equation reads: ℜ ℜ ( u ) ( u r r + u r / r + u z z ) = ( u r ) 2 + ( u z ) 2 .

{\displaystyle \Re (u)(u_{rr}+u_{r}/r+u_{zz})=(u_{r})^{2}+(u_{z})^{2}.} where ℜ ℜ ( u ) {\textstyle \Re (u)} is the real part of u {\textstyle u} .  For its Lax pair and other features see e.g.

[ 3 ] [ 4 ] and references therein.

Usage [ edit ] The Ernst equation is employed in order to produce exact solutions of the Einstein's equations in the general theory of relativity .
