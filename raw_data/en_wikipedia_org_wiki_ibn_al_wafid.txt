He was the vizier of Al-Mamun of Toledo . His main work is Kitāb al-Adwiya al-Mufrada ( كتاب الأدوية المفردة ), translated into Latin as De Medicamentis Simplicibus .

[ 3 ] Ibn al-Wafid was mainly a pharmacist in Toledo , and he used the techniques and methods available in alchemy to extract at least 520 different kinds of medicines from various plants and herbs .

One of his students was Ibn al-Lūnquh .
