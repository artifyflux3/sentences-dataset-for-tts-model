Ahmad b. Muhammad Ibn Ghazi al-'Utmani al-Miknasi ( Arabic : ابن غازي المكناسي ) (1437–1513) was a Moroccan scholar in the field of history , Islamic law , Arabic philology and mathematics . He was born in Meknes from Banu Uthman, a clan in the Berber kutama tribe, [ 1 ] but spent his life in Fez .

[ 2 ] Ibn Ghazi wrote a three-volume history of Meknes and a commentary to the treatise of Ibn al-Banna , Munyat al-hussab . Ibn Ghazi wrote another treatise based on his commentary titled Bughyat al-tulab fi sharh munyat al-hussab ("The desire of students for an explanation of the calculator's craving"). He included sections on arithmetic and algebraic methods. He is also the author of Kulliyat , a short work on legal questions and judgements in the Maliki madhab.
