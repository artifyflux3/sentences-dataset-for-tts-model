There is also a generalization to infinite-dimensional spaces. Gaussian measures are named after the German mathematician Carl Friedrich Gauss . One reason why Gaussian measures are so ubiquitous in probability theory is the central limit theorem . Loosely speaking, it states that if a random variable X {\displaystyle X} is obtained by summing a large number N {\displaystyle N} of independent random variables with variance 1, then X {\displaystyle X} has variance N {\displaystyle N} and its law is approximately Gaussian.
