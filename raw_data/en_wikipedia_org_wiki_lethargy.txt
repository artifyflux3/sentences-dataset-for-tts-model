From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

State of tiredness, weariness, fatigue, or lack of energy

Not to be confused with [Fatigue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatigue "Fatigue").

For the 1990s band, see [Lethargy (band)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lethargy_(band) "Lethargy (band)").

**Lethargy** is a state of tiredness, sleepiness, weariness, [fatigue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatigue_(medical) "Fatigue (medical)"), sluggishness, or lack of energy. It can be accompanied by [depression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Depression_(mood) "Depression (mood)"), decreased motivation, or [apathy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apathy "Apathy"). Lethargy can be a normal response to inadequate sleep, overexertion, overworking, stress, lack of exercise, improper nutrition, drug abuse, [boredom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boredom "Boredom"), or a symptom of an underlying illness or disorder. It may also be a side effect of medication or caused by an interaction between medications and [alcohol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcoholic_drink "Alcoholic drink"). It may also be an [altered level of consciousness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Altered_level_of_consciousness "Altered level of consciousness").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lethargy#cite_note-1)

When part of a normal response, lethargy often resolves with rest, adequate sleep, decreased stress, physical exercise, and good nutrition.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lethargy#cite_note-2) Its symptoms, however, can last days or even months,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lethargy#cite_note-3) so it can be a sign of a recent underlying illness or infection if it does not resolve normally.
