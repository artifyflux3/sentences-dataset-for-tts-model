Find sources: "Binomial approximation" – news · newspapers · books · scholar · JSTOR ( February 2016 ) ( Learn how and when to remove this message ) The binomial approximation is useful for approximately calculating powers of sums of 1 and a small number x .  It states that ( 1 + x ) α α ≈ ≈ 1 + α α x .

{\displaystyle (1+x)^{\alpha }\approx 1+\alpha x.} It is valid when | x | < 1 {\displaystyle |x|<1} and | α α x | ≪ ≪ 1 {\displaystyle |\alpha x|\ll 1} where x {\displaystyle x} and α α {\displaystyle \alpha } may be real or complex numbers .

The benefit of this approximation is that α α {\displaystyle \alpha } is converted from an exponent to a multiplicative factor.  This can greatly simplify mathematical expressions (as in the example below ) and is a common tool in physics.

[ 1 ] The approximation can be proven several ways, and is closely related to the binomial theorem .  By Bernoulli's inequality , the left-hand side of the approximation is greater than or equal to the right-hand side whenever x > − − 1 {\displaystyle x>-1} and α α ≥ ≥ 1 {\displaystyle \alpha \geq 1} .
