A **spacer** can refer to something used to separate two parts in an assembly.

**Spacer** can also refer to:

Arts and entertainment
----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Spacer&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Arts and entertainment")]

*   [_Spacer_ (album)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacer_(album) "Spacer (album)"), a 2011 album by Jason Adasiewicz
*   "Spacer", a song by Sheila and B. Devotion from the 1980 album _[King of the World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King\_of\_the\_World\_(album) "King of the World (album)")_
*   "Spacer", a 1938 short story by [Sydor Rey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sydor_Rey "Sydor Rey")
*   [Spacers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robot_series#Overview_of_the_Robot_Novels "Robot series"), a fictional sociocultural group in Isaac Asimov's _Robot_ series of novels and short stories

### Other uses in science and technology

*   [Spacers and standoffs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacers_and_standoffs "Spacers and standoffs"), unthreaded pieces of rigid tubing, often used in electronic equipment
*   [Rebar spacer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rebar_spacer "Rebar spacer"), in concrete construction

Oil and gas industry
--------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Spacer&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Oil and gas industry")]

*   Spacer, a viscous fluid used to remove [drilling fluids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drilling_fluid "Drilling fluid") ahead of cement slurry. The spacer is made up with specific fluid attributes, such as viscosity and density that are designed to prohibit the interaction between the mud and cement slurry.
