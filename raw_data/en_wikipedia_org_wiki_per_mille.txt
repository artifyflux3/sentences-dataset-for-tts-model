The phrase **per mille** (from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_language "Latin language")_\_[per](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/per#Latin "wikt:per")\_ \_[mīlle](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/mille#Latin "wikt:mille")\__'in each thousand')[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-oed-1) indicates [parts per thousand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parts_per_thousand "Parts per thousand").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-cam-2) The associated symbol is ‰, similar to a [per cent sign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_cent_sign "Per cent sign")% but with an extra zero in the [divisor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Division_(mathematics) "Division (mathematics)").

Major dictionaries do not agree on the spelling,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-oed-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-cam-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-oad-3) giving other options of **per mil**,[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-cam-2)**per mill**,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-oed-1)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-oad-3)**permil**,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-oed-1)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-solubility-4)**permill**,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-oed-1)**permille**.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-uni-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-6)

The word **_promille_** is the [cognate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognate "Cognate") in Dutch, German, Finnish and Swedish, and is sometimes seen as a [loanword](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loanword "Loanword") in English with the same meaning as _per mille_.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-7)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-solubility-4)

The symbol is included in the [General Punctuation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Punctuation_(Unicode_block) "General Punctuation (Unicode block)") block of [Unicode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode "Unicode") at U+2030‰PER MILLE SIGN.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-uni-5) There is also an Arabic-Indic per mille sign at U+0609؉ARABIC-INDIC PER MILLE SIGN.

### Blood alcohol concentration

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Per_mille&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Blood alcohol concentration")]

Promille and permille are likely best known as shorthand terms for [Blood Alcohol Concentration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blood_Alcohol_Concentration "Blood Alcohol Concentration"), where 1 promille corresponds to 1 gram of alcohol per liter of blood.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-8) Promille is used in the legal limits of [blood-alcohol content](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BAC_by_volume "BAC by volume") for driving a road vehicle in some countries: for example: 0.5‰ or 0.8‰.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-HRB_BAL-9)

#### Sales and marketing

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Per_mille&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Sales and marketing")]

[Cost per mille](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cost_per_mille "Cost per mille") (CPM), the price of 1000 units, may be used for views of banner and display advertising, and for emails delivered by [email service providers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Email_service_provider_(marketing) "Email service provider (marketing)").

In UK insurance usage, the cost per mille is the rate per £1000 of insured value.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-10) In India, the premium per mille is the rate expressed as thousandths of the sum assured.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-11)

[![Image 1: A railroad distance and gradient sign in Gdańsk, Poland. The 50‰ grade is equivalent to 5%.](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/50_per_mille.png/250px-50_per_mille.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:50_per_mille.png)

A tram and gradient sign in [Gdańsk, Poland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gda%C5%84sk,_Poland "Gdańsk, Poland"). The 50‰ [grade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grade_(slope) "Grade (slope)") is equivalent to 50 m/km or 5%.

Gradients (in some countries in [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe "Europe")) may be expressed as mm/m or m/km, and written with the per mille symbol.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-12)

Per mille may be used to express [stable-isotope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stable_isotope "Stable isotope") ratios—for example: "δ 13 C was measured at −3.5‰"

In Italy, it is possible to nominate a charity or charities to receive "five per mille" (5‰) of personal taxation payments.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-13)

[Property taxation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Property_tax "Property tax") rates may be expressed as the _[millage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Millage "Millage") rate_ ([U.S.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States")) or _mill rate_ ([Canada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada "Canada")), from [mill (currency)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mill_(currency) "Mill (currency)"), one thousandth of a dollar.

Permille may also be used to express a [1000-quantile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantile#permille "Quantile") ("999th permille"),[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-walker-14)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-15) but this usage is rare and largely obsolete. Modern publications instead use fractional percentiles ("99.9th percentile").[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-16)

Seawater [salinity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salinity "Salinity") is often expressed per mille. Average marine salinity is about 35 parts per thousand or 35‰ (3.5%).[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Per_mille#cite_note-17)
