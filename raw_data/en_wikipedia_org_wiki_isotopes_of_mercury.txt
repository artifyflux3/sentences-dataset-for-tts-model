The longest-lived radioisotopes are 194 Hg with a half-life of 447 years, and 203 Hg with a half-life of 46.61 days. Most of the remaining 40 radioisotopes have half-lives that are less than a day. The odd natural isotopes 199 Hg and 201 Hg are NMR-active , having spin of 1/2 and 3/2 respectively; as NMR is best with spin 1/2, the former is normally used.

[ 4 ] All isotopes of mercury are either radioactive or observationally stable , meaning that they are predicted to be radioactive but no actual decay has been observed. These isotopes are predicted to undergo either alpha decay or double beta decay .
