Terministic screen Toulmin model Wooden iron v t e A talking point is a pre-established message or formula used in the field of political communication, sales and commercial or advertising communication. The message is coordinated a priori to remain more or less invariable regardless of which stakeholder brings the message in the media.

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ] [ 4 ] Such statements can either be free standing or created as retorts to the opposition's talking points and are frequently used in public relations , particularly in areas heavy in debate such as politics and marketing .
