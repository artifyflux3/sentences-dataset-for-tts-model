Chemical compound

**Titanium(II) oxide** ([Ti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium "Titanium")[O](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen")) is an [inorganic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inorganic "Inorganic")[chemical compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_compound "Chemical compound") of titanium and oxygen. It can be prepared from [titanium dioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium_dioxide "Titanium dioxide") and titanium metal at 1500°C.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-Wiberg&Holleman-1) It is [non-stoichiometric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-stoichiometric "Non-stoichiometric") in a range TiO 0.7 to TiO 1.3 and this is caused by vacancies of either Ti or O in the defect rock salt structure.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-Wiberg&Holleman-1) In pure TiO 15% of both Ti and O sites are vacant,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-Wiberg&Holleman-1) as the vacancies allow metal-metal bonding between adjacent Ti centres. Careful annealing can cause ordering of the vacancies producing a monoclinic form which has 5 TiO units in the primitive cell that exhibits lower resistivity.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-2) A high temperature form with titanium atoms with trigonal prismatic coordination is also known.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-3) Acid solutions of TiO are stable for a short time then decompose to give hydrogen:[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-Wiberg&Holleman-1)

2 Ti 2+(aq) + 2 H+(aq) → 2 Ti 3+(aq) + H 2(g)
Gas-phase TiO shows strong bands in the optical spectra of cool ([M-type](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_classification#Spectral_types "Stellar classification")) stars.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-Jorgensen1994-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-5) In 2017, TiO was claimed to be detected in an [exoplanet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exoplanet "Exoplanet") atmosphere for the first time; a result which is still debated in the literature.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-Sedaghati2017-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-Espinoza2019-7) Additionally, evidence has been obtained for the presence of the diatomic molecule TiO in the interstellar medium.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium(II)_oxide#cite_note-8)
