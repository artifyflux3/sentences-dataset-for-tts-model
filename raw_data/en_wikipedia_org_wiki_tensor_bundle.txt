From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

In [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics"), the **tensor bundle** of a [manifold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifold "Manifold") is the [direct sum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct_sum_of_vector_bundles "Direct sum of vector bundles") of all tensor products of the [tangent bundle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tangent_bundle "Tangent bundle") and the [cotangent bundle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cotangent_bundle "Cotangent bundle") of that manifold. To do [calculus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calculus "Calculus") on the tensor bundle a [connection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Connection_(mathematics) "Connection (mathematics)") is needed, except for the special case of the [exterior derivative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exterior_derivative "Exterior derivative") of antisymmetric tensors.

Definition
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tensor_bundle&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Definition")]

See also: [Tensor field §Tensor bundles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_field#Tensor_bundles "Tensor field")

A tensor bundle is a [fiber bundle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiber_bundle "Fiber bundle") where the fiber is a tensor product of any number of copies of the [tangent space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tangent_space "Tangent space") and/or [cotangent space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cotangent_space "Cotangent space") of the base space, which is a manifold. As such, the fiber is a [vector space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_space "Vector space") and the tensor bundle is a special kind of [vector bundle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_bundle "Vector bundle").
