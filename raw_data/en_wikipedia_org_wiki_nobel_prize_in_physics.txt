The **Nobel Prize in Physics** ([Swedish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swedish_language "Swedish language"): _Nobelpriset i fysik_) is an annual award given by the [Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Academy_of_Sciences "Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences") for those who have made the most outstanding contributions to mankind in the field of [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics"). It is one of the five [Nobel Prizes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize "Nobel Prize") established by the will of [Alfred Nobel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_Nobel "Alfred Nobel") in 1895 and awarded since 1901, the others being the [Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Chemistry "Nobel Prize in Chemistry"), [Nobel Prize in Literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Literature "Nobel Prize in Literature"), [Nobel Peace Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize "Nobel Peace Prize"), and [Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physiology_or_Medicine "Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine"). Physics is traditionally the first award presented in the Nobel Prize ceremony.

The prize consists of [a medal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_medal "Nobel Prize medal") along with a diploma and a certificate for the monetary award. The [front side](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obverse "Obverse") of the medal displays the same profile of Alfred Nobel depicted on the medals for Physics, Chemistry, and Literature.

The first Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to German physicist [Wilhelm R√∂ntgen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilhelm_R%C3%B6ntgen "Wilhelm R√∂ntgen") in recognition of the extraordinary services he rendered by the discovery of [X-rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-ray "X-ray"). This award is administered by the [Nobel Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Foundation "Nobel Foundation") and is widely regarded as the most prestigious award that a scientist can receive in physics. It is presented in [Stockholm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stockholm "Stockholm") at an annual ceremony on the 10th of December, the anniversary of Nobel's death. As of 2024[[update]](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nobel_Prize_in_Physics&action=edit), a total of 226 [individuals have been awarded the prize.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Nobel_laureates_in_Physics "List of Nobel laureates in Physics")[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-nobelOrg-2)

Background
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nobel_Prize_in_Physics&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Background")]

[![Image 26](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Alfred_Nobel3.jpg/250px-Alfred_Nobel3.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Alfred_Nobel3.jpg)

[Alfred Nobel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_Nobel "Alfred Nobel") (1833‚Äì1896), who established the [Nobel Prizes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize "Nobel Prize").

[Alfred Nobel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_Nobel "Alfred Nobel"), in his last will and testament, stated that his wealth should be used to create a series of prizes for those who confer the "greatest benefit on mankind" in the fields of [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics"), [chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemistry "Chemistry"), [peace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace "Peace"), [physiology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physiology "Physiology") or medicine, and literature.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-BBCHistory-3) Though Nobel wrote several wills during his lifetime, the last one was written a year before he died and was signed at the Swedish-Norwegian Club in Paris on 27 November 1895.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-5) Nobel bequeathed 94% of his total assets, 31 million [Swedish _kronor_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swedish_krona "Swedish krona"), to establish and endow the five Nobel Prizes.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-6) Owing to the level of skepticism surrounding the will, it was not until 26 April 1897 that it was approved by the [Storting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliament_of_Norway "Parliament of Norway") (Norwegian Parliament).[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-nobelprize1-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-Levinovitz13-8) The executors of his will were [Ragnar Sohlman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ragnar_Sohlman "Ragnar Sohlman") and Rudolf Lilljequist, who formed the Nobel Foundation to take care of Nobel's fortune and organise the prizes.

The members of the Norwegian Nobel Committee who were to award the Peace Prize were appointed soon after the will was approved. The other prize-awarding organisations followed: [Karolinska Institutet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karolinska_Institutet "Karolinska Institutet") on 7 June, the [Swedish Academy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swedish_Academy "Swedish Academy") on 9 June, and the [Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Academy_of_Sciences "Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences") on 11 June.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-10) The Nobel Foundation then established guidelines for awarding the prizes. In 1900, the Nobel Foundation's newly created statutes were promulgated by King [Oscar II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oscar_II_of_Sweden "Oscar II of Sweden").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-Levinovitz13-8)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-BritannicaWill-11) According to Nobel's will, the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences would award the Prize in Physics.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-BritannicaWill-11)

Millikan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Andrews_Millikan "Robert Andrews Millikan") (1923 laureate).

A maximum of three [Nobel laureates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_laureate "Nobel laureate") and two different works may be selected for the Nobel Prize in Physics.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-12) Compared with other Nobel Prizes, the nomination and selection process for the prize in physics is long and rigorous. This is a key reason why it has grown in importance over the years to become the most important prize in Physics.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-13)

The Nobel laureates are selected by the [Nobel Committee for Physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Committee_for_Physics "Nobel Committee for Physics"), a [Nobel Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Committee "Nobel Committee") that consists of five members elected by [The Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Royal_Swedish_Academy_of_Sciences "The Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences"). During the first stage which begins in September, a group of about 3,000 selected university professors, Nobel Laureates in Physics and Chemistry, and others are sent confidential nomination forms. The completed forms must arrive at the Nobel Committee by 31 January of the following year. The nominees are scrutinized and discussed by experts and are narrowed to approximately fifteen names. The committee submits a report with recommendations on the final candidates to the Academy, where, in the Physics Class, it is further discussed. The Academy then makes the final selection of the Laureates in Physics by a majority vote.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-Nomination-14)

[![Image 28](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Abdus_Salam_1987.jpg/250px-Abdus_Salam_1987.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Abdus_Salam_1987.jpg)

[Abdus Salam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdus_Salam "Abdus Salam") ‚Äì 1979 laureate

The names of the nominees are never publicly announced, and neither are they told that they have been considered for the Prize. Nomination records are sealed for fifty years.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-fiftyyearsrule-15) While posthumous nominations are not permitted, awards can be made if the individual died in the months between the decision of the committee (typically in October) and the ceremony in December. Prior to 1974, [posthumous awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Posthumous_award "Posthumous award") were permitted if the candidate had died after being nominated.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-16)

The rules for the Nobel Prize in Physics require that the significance of achievements being recognized has been "tested by time". In practice, that means that the lag between the discovery and the award is typically on the order of 20 years and can be much longer. For example, half of the 1983 Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to [Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subrahmanyan_Chandrasekhar "Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar") for his work on stellar structure and evolution that was done during the 1930s. As a downside of this tested-by-time rule, not all scientists live long enough for their work to be recognized. Some important scientific discoveries are never considered for a prize, as the discoverers have died by the time the impact of their work is appreciated.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-17)[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-18)

Prizes
------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nobel_Prize_in_Physics&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Prizes")]

A Physics Nobel Prize laureate is awarded a gold medal, a diploma bearing a citation, and a sum of money.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-19)

### Medals

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nobel_Prize_in_Physics&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Medals")]

Main article: [Nobel Prize medal ¬ßPhysics and Chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_medal#Physics_and_Chemistry "Nobel Prize medal")

The medal for the Nobel Prize in Physics is identical in design to the [Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Chemistry "Nobel Prize in Chemistry") medal.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-NobelUnique-20)[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-NobelPhysicsChem-21) The reverse of the physics and chemistry medals depicts the [Goddess of Nature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physis "Physis") in the form of [Isis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isis "Isis") as she emerges from clouds holding a [cornucopia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cornucopia "Cornucopia"). The Genius of Science holds the veil which covers Nature's "cold and austere face".[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-NobelPhysicsChem-21) It was designed by [Erik Lindberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erik_Lindberg "Erik Lindberg") and is manufactured by Svenska Medalj in [Eskilstuna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eskilstuna "Eskilstuna").[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-NobelPhysicsChem-21) It is inscribed "_Inventas vitam iuvat excoluisse per artes_" ("It is beneficial to have improved (human) life through discovered arts"), an adaptation of "_inventas aut qui vitam excoluere per artes_" from line 663 of book 6 of the _[Aeneid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aeneid "Aeneid")_ by the Roman poet [Virgil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgil "Virgil").[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-NobelMedicine-22) A plate below the figures is inscribed with the name of the recipient. The text "_REG. ACAD. SCIENT. SUEC._" denoting the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences is inscribed on the reverse.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-NobelPhysicsChem-21)

### Diplomas

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nobel_Prize_in_Physics&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Diplomas")]

[![Image 29](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Nobel_Pierre_et_Marie_Curie_1.jpg/330px-Nobel_Pierre_et_Marie_Curie_1.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Nobel_Pierre_et_Marie_Curie_1.jpg)

1903 Nobel Prize diploma, awarded to [Marie Curie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marie_Curie "Marie Curie") and [Pierre Curie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Curie "Pierre Curie")

Nobel laureates receive a diploma directly from the hands of the [King of Sweden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchy_of_Sweden "Monarchy of Sweden"). Each diploma is uniquely designed by the prize-awarding institutions for the laureate who receives it.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-nobelprize5-23) The diploma contains a picture with the name of the laureate and a citation explaining their accomplishments.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-nobelprize5-23)

### Award money

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nobel_Prize_in_Physics&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Award money")]

At the awards ceremony, the laureate is given a document indicating the award sum. The amount of the cash award may differ from year to year, based on the funding available from the [Nobel Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Foundation "Nobel Foundation"). For example, in 2009 the total cash awarded was 10 million [Swedish Kronor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swedish_Krona "Swedish Krona") (SEK) (US$1.4 million),[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-nobelprize2-24) but in 2012 following the [Great Recession](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Recession "Great Recession"), the amount was 8 million SEK, or US$1.1 million.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-25) If there are two laureates in a particular category, the award grant is divided equally between the recipients, but if there are three, the awarding committee may opt to divide the grant equally, or award half to one recipient and a quarter to each of the two others.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-26)[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-27)[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-28)[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-29)

### Ceremony

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nobel_Prize_in_Physics&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Ceremony")]

The committee and institution serving as the selection board for the prize typically announce the names of the laureates during the first week of October. The prize is then awarded at formal ceremonies held annually in [Stockholm Concert Hall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stockholm_Concert_Hall "Stockholm Concert Hall") on 10 December, the anniversary of Nobel's death. The laureates receive a diploma, a medal, and a document confirming the prize amount.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics#cite_note-awardceremony-30)
