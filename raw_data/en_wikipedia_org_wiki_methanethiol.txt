**Methanethiol** ( [_METH-ayn-THY-ol_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key")), also called **methyl mercaptan**, is an [organosulfur compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organosulfur_chemistry "Organosulfur chemistry") with the [chemical formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula")CH 3 SH. It is a colorless [flammable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combustibility_and_flammability "Combustibility and flammability") gas with a distinctive putrid smell. In small amounts, it is pervasive in nature and found in certain foods, such as some [nuts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nut_(fruit) "Nut (fruit)") and [cheese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cheese "Cheese"). It contributes to many odors, including the emissions from [pulp mills](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pulp_mill "Pulp mill"), [bad breath](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halitosis "Halitosis"), and [flatus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flatulence "Flatulence"). Methanethiol is the simplest [thiol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiol "Thiol") and is sometimes abbreviated as **MeSH**.

Structure and reactions
-----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methanethiol&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Structure and reactions")]

The molecule is tetrahedral at the carbon atom, like [methanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanol "Methanol"). It is a [weak acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weak_acid "Weak acid"), with a [p _K_ a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PKa "PKa") of ~10.4, but is about a hundred thousand times more acidic than methanol. The colorless salt can be obtained by treatment with [sodium methoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_methoxide "Sodium methoxide"):

CH 3 SH + CH 3 ONa → CH 3 SNa + CH 3 OH
The thiolate anion in [sodium methanethiolate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_methanethiolate "Sodium methanethiolate") is a strong [nucleophile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleophile "Nucleophile").

It can be methylated to [dimethyl disulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_disulfide "Dimethyl disulfide"):

2CH 3 SH + [O] → CH 3 SSCH 3 + H 2 O
Further oxidation takes the [disulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disulfide "Disulfide") to two molecules of [methanesulfonic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanesulfonic_acid "Methanesulfonic acid"), which is odorless. [Bleach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bleach "Bleach") deodorizes methanethiol in this way.

Methanethiol (MeSH) is released as a by-product of [kraft pulping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kraft_pulping "Kraft pulping") in [pulp mills](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pulp_(paper) "Pulp (paper)"). In kraft pulping, [lignin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lignin "Lignin") is [depolymerized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Depolymerization "Depolymerization") by nucleophilic attack with the strongly nucleophilic hydrosulfide ion (HS−) in a highly alkaline medium. However, in a side reaction, HS− attacks methoxyl groups (OMe) in [lignin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lignin "Lignin"), demethylating them to give free [phenolate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenolate "Phenolate") groups (PhO−) and releasing MeSH. Due to alkalinity, MeSH is readily deprotonated ([MeSNa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_methanethiolate "Sodium methanethiolate")), and the formed MeS− ion is also a strong nucleophile, reacting further to [dimethyl sulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_sulfide "Dimethyl sulfide"). The compounds remain in the liquor and are burned in the [recovery boiler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recovery_boiler "Recovery boiler"), where the sulfur is recovered as [sodium sulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_sulfide "Sodium sulfide").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-4)

Methanethiol is released from decaying organic matter in [marshes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marsh "Marsh") and is present in the [natural gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_gas "Natural gas") of certain regions, in [coal tar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coal_tar "Coal tar"), and in some [crude oils](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crude_oil "Crude oil"). It occurs in various plants and vegetables, such as radishes.

In surface [seawater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seawater "Seawater"), methanethiol is the primary breakdown product of the [algal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algae "Algae")[metabolite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metabolite "Metabolite")[dimethylsulfoniopropionate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethylsulfoniopropionate "Dimethylsulfoniopropionate") (DMSP). [Marine bacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_bacteria "Marine bacteria") appear to obtain most of the sulfur in their proteins by the breakdown of DMSP and incorporation of methanethiol, despite the fact that methanethiol is present in seawater at much lower concentrations than sulfate (~0.3 nM vs. 28 mM).[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-5) Bacteria in environments both with and without oxygen can also convert methanethiol to [dimethyl sulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_sulfide "Dimethyl sulfide") (DMS), although most DMS in surface seawater is produced by a separate pathway.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-6) Both DMS and methanethiol can be used by certain microbes as substrates for [methanogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanogenesis "Methanogenesis") in some anaerobic soils.

Methanethiol is a byproduct of the metabolism of [asparagus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asparagus "Asparagus").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-7) The production of methanethiol in [urine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urine "Urine") after eating asparagus was once thought to be a [genetic trait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetics "Genetics"). More recent research suggests that the peculiar odor is in fact produced by all humans after consuming asparagus, while the ability to detect it (methanethiol being one of many components in "asparagus pee") is in fact the genetic trait.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-8) The chemical components responsible for the change in the odor of urine show as soon as 15 minutes after eating asparagus.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-9)

Methanethiol is prepared commercially by the reaction of [methanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanol "Methanol") with [hydrogen sulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_sulfide "Hydrogen sulfide") gas over an [aluminium oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_oxide "Aluminium oxide") catalyst:[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-John_Wiley_&_Sons,_Inc-10)

CH 3 OH + H 2 S → CH 3 SH + H 2 O
Although impractical, it can be prepared by the reaction of [methyl iodide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_iodide "Methyl iodide") with [thiourea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiourea "Thiourea").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-Reid-11)

[![Image 18](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Methanethiol_cylinder.jpg/250px-Methanethiol_cylinder.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Methanethiol_cylinder.jpg)

Cylinder of methanethiol gas

Methanethiol is mainly used to produce the essential amino acid [methionine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methionine "Methionine"), which is used as a dietary component in poultry and animal feed.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-John_Wiley_&_Sons,_Inc-10) Methanethiol is also used in the [plastic industry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plastics_industry "Plastics industry") as a moderator for [free-radical polymerizations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_polymerization "Radical polymerization")[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-John_Wiley_&_Sons,_Inc-10) and as a precursor in the manufacture of [pesticides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pesticide "Pesticide") including [isomalathion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomalathion "Isomalathion").

This chemical is also used in the [natural gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_gas "Natural gas") industry as an [odorant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odorant "Odorant"), as it mixes well with [methane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methane "Methane"). The characteristic rotting vegetation smell of the mix is widely known by natural gas customers as an indicator of a possible [gas leak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas_leak "Gas leak"), even a minor one.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-12)

The [safety data sheet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Safety_data_sheet "Safety data sheet") (SDS) lists methanethiol as a colorless, flammable gas with an extremely strong and repulsive smell. At very high concentrations it is highly toxic and affects the central nervous system. Its penetrating odor provides warning at dangerous concentrations. An odor threshold of 1[ppb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parts_per_billion "Parts per billion") has been reported.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-Devos-13) The United States [OSHA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupational_Safety_and_Health_Administration "Occupational Safety and Health Administration") Ceiling Limit is listed as 10 ppm.

In 2001 a rail car fire of 25,000 US gallons (95,000 L) near [Trenton, Michigan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trenton,_Michigan "Trenton, Michigan") left three people dead and nine injured.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-14)

On November 15, 2014, at [DuPont](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DuPont "DuPont")'s [La Porte, Texas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Porte,_Texas "La Porte, Texas") facility, 24,000 lb (10,886 kg) of methyl mercaptan were released and travelled downwind into surrounding areas, killing four and injuring one other.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-15)[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-CSB_Video-16) In 2023, DuPont pleaded guilty to criminal negligence for its role in the leak. The company was ordered to pay a $12 million fine and donate an additional $4 million to the [National Fish and Wildlife Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Fish_and_Wildlife_Foundation "National Fish and Wildlife Foundation").[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-17)[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-18)

On July 14, 2022, an accidental release in [Charlotte, North Carolina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina "Charlotte, North Carolina") led to the temporary closure of local government offices.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-19)

On April 10, 2024, an accidental release[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanethiol#cite_note-20) of a higher-than expected level of methyl mercaptan into the natural gas supply was attributed to an "upstream supplier" for [Columbia Gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columbia_Gas "Columbia Gas"). This release was noticed by residents in at least [Richland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richland_County,_Ohio "Richland County, Ohio"), [Ashland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashland_County,_Ohio "Ashland County, Ohio"), and [Lorain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lorain_County,_Ohio "Lorain County, Ohio") counties in Ohio. Numerous schools cancelled their school days and numerous evacuations took place.
