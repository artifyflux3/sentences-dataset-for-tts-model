Chemical compound

In [organic chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_chemistry "Organic chemistry"), **methenium** (also called **methylium**, **carbenium**,[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_cation#cite_note-2)**methyl cation**, or **protonated methylene**) is a [cation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cation "Cation") with the formula CH+

3. It can be viewed as a [methylene radical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methylene_radical "Methylene radical") (**:**CH

2) with an added [proton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proton "Proton") (H+

), or as a [methyl radical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_radical "Methyl radical") (•CH

3) with one [electron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron "Electron") removed. It is a [carbocation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbocation "Carbocation") and an [enium ion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enium_ion "Enium ion"), making it the simplest of the [carbenium ions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbenium_ion "Carbenium ion").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_cation#cite_note-Golob-3)

Structure
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methenium&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Structure")]

Experiments and calculations generally agree that the methenium ion is planar, with threefold [symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_symmetry "Molecular symmetry").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_cation#cite_note-Golob-3) The carbon atom is a prototypical (and exact) example of sp 2 hybridization.

Preparation and reactions
-------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methenium&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Preparation and reactions")]

For [mass spectrometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_spectrometry "Mass spectrometry") studies at low pressure, methenium can be obtained by [ultraviolet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet "Ultraviolet")[photoionization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photoionization "Photoionization") of methyl radical,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_cation#cite_note-Golob-3) or by collisions of monatomic cations such as C+

 and Kr+

 with neutral methane.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_cation#cite_note-Sharma-4) In such conditions, it will react with [acetonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetonitrile "Acetonitrile")CH

3 CN to form the ion (CH

3)

2 CN+

.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_cation#cite_note-McEwan-5)

Upon capture of a low-energy electron (less than 1[eV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronvolt "Electronvolt")), it will spontaneously dissociate.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_cation#cite_note-Bahati-6)

It is seldom encountered as an intermediate in the condensed phase. It is proposed as a reactive intermediate that forms upon protonation or hydride abstraction of methane with [FSO 3 H-SbF 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magic_acid "Magic acid"). The methenium ion is very reactive, even towards [alkanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkane "Alkane").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_cation#cite_note-7)

---------

### Origins of life

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methenium&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Origins of life")]

Main article: [Abiogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abiogenesis "Abiogenesis")

In June 2023, astronomers [detected](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_interstellar_and_circumstellar_molecules "List of interstellar and circumstellar molecules"), for the first time outside the Solar System, methyl cation, CH 3+ (and/or [carbon cation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_cation "Carbon cation"), C+), the known basic ingredients of [life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life "Life"), in [interstellar space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_space "Interstellar space").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_cation#cite_note-MSH-20230627-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_cation#cite_note-NAT-20230626-9)
