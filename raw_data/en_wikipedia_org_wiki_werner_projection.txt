Like other heart -shaped projections, [ specify ] it is also categorized as cordiform .

Stab-Werner refers to two originators: Johannes Werner (1466–1528), a parish priest in Nuremberg , refined and promoted this projection that had been developed earlier by Johannes Stabius (Stab) of Vienna around 1500.

The projection is a limiting form of the Bonne projection , having its standard parallel at one of the poles (90°N/S).

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] Distances along each parallel and along the central meridian are correct, as are all distances from the north pole.
