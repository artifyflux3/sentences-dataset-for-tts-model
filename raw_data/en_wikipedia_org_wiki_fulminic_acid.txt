Chemical compound

**Fulminic acid** is an [acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acid "Acid") with the formula HCNO, more specifically H−C≡N+−O−. It is an [isomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomer "Isomer") of [isocyanic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid "Isocyanic acid") (H−N=C=O) and of its elusive [tautomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tautomer "Tautomer"), [cyanic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanic_acid "Cyanic acid") (H−O−C≡N), and also of isofulminic acid (H−O−N+≡C−).[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminic_acid#cite_note-beck1966-1)

[Fulminate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminate "Fulminate") is the [anion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anion "Anion")[C−≡N+−O−] of any of its [salts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salt_(chemistry) "Salt (chemistry)"). For historical reasons, the fulminate [functional group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_group "Functional group") is understood to be −O−N+≡C− as in isofulminic acid;[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminic_acid#cite_note-went1981-2) whereas the group −C≡N+O− is called [nitrile oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrile_oxide "Nitrile oxide").

History
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fulminic_acid&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

This chemical was known since the early 1800s through its salts and via the products of reactions in which it was proposed to exist,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminic_acid#cite_note-JChemEd-history-3) but the acid itself was not detected until 1966.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminic_acid#cite_note-beck1966-1)

Structure
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fulminic_acid&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Structure")]

Fulminic acid was long believed to have a structure of H–O–N+≡C−. It wasn't until the 1966 isolation and analysis of a pure sample of fulminic acid that this structural idea was conclusively disproven.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminic_acid#cite_note-JChemEd-history-3) The chemical that actually has that structure, isofulminic acid (a [tautomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tautomer "Tautomer") of the actual fulminic acid structure) was eventually detected in 1988.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminic_acid#cite_note-JChemEd-history-3)

The structure of the molecule has been determined by [microwave spectroscopy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microwave_spectroscopy "Microwave spectroscopy") with the following bond-lengths - C-H: 1.027(1) [Å](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angstrom "Angstrom"), C-N: 1.161(15) [Å](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angstrom "Angstrom"), N-O: 1.207(15) [Å](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angstrom "Angstrom").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminic_acid#cite_note-4)

Synthesis
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fulminic_acid&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Synthesis")]

A convenient synthesis involves [flash pyrolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flash_pyrolysis "Flash pyrolysis") of certain [oximes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxime "Oxime"). In contrast to earlier syntheses, this method avoids the use of highly explosive metal fulminates.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminic_acid#cite_note-5)
