Miller, Jr.

) American philosopher (born 1944) Fred Dycus Miller Jr.

(born 1944) [ 1 ] is an American philosopher who specializes in Aristotelian philosophy , with additional interests in political philosophy, business ethics , metaphysics, and philosophy in science fiction. He is a professor emeritus at Bowling Green State University .

[ 2 ] Education and career [ edit ] Miller is a 1966 graduate of Portland State University .

[ 3 ] and earned a Ph.D. in 1971 at the University of Washington .

[ 1 ] He took a faculty position at Bowling Green State University in 1972, was promoted to associate professor in 1977, and to full professor in 1982. He was executive director of the university's Social Philosophy and Policy Center from 1981 to 2012. He retired as a professor emeritus in 2013.

[ 3 ] He served as president of the Society for Ancient Greek Philosophy from 1998 to 2004.

[ 3 ] Books [ edit ] Miller is the author of Nature, Justice, and Rights in Aristotle's Politics (Clarendon Press, 1995).

[ 4 ] He is the translator of Aristotle 's On the Soul: and Other Psychological Works (Oxford World's Classics, Oxford University Press, 2018), [ 5 ] and of ' Alexander': On Aristotle Metaphysics 12 (Bloomsbury, 2021).
