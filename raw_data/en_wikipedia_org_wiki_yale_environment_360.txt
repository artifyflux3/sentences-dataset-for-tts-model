It includes original reporting, opinion, analysis, and interviews.

Yale Environment 360 is published by the Yale School of the Environment at Yale University , but it is editorially independent.

[ 1 ] History [ edit ] James Gustave Speth , then dean of the Yale School of Forestry & Environmental Studies, first proposed the idea of a Yale-sponsored environmental website in 2007.

Roger Cohn , formerly the editor-in-chief of Mother Jones and executive editor of Audubon , was hired to be e360 's editor.

[ 2 ] Cohn described his vision for e360 at the time as "a publication that straddles the line between more academic and specialized environmental sites and more general green lifestyle sites." [ 3 ] Yale E360 officially launched on June 3, 2008 with articles by Bill McKibben , Elizabeth Kolbert , Fred Pearce , and Carl Zimmer .

[ 4 ] The website was redesigned in 2017.

[ 5 ] Funders and partnerships [ edit ] Yale Environment 360 is funded using a non-profit journalism model by private foundations and donors. Major donors include the BAND Foundation, the William Penn Foundation , the Heinz Endowments , and the Climate and Land Use Alliance.

[ 6 ] The magazine is part of Climate Desk, a journalistic collaboration between Yale Environment 360 , Wired , Mother Jones , Slate , and others.

[ 7 ] Awards and accolades [ edit ] "Leveling Appalachia," a video produced by e360 , was awarded best video at the 2010 National Magazine Awards . It was the first video ever to receive a National Magazine Award.

[ 8 ] Yale E360 was also honored by the Online News Association in 2009, 2010, and 2011, [ 9 ] and has received multiple Webby Award nominations.

[ 10 ] The Warriors of Qiugang , a short film directed by Ruby Yang and produced by Thomas Lennon , was co-produced and hosted by Yale E360 . It was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Documentary (Short Subject) at the 83rd Academy Awards .
