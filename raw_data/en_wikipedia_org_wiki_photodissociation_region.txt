From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Gaseous parts of the interstellar medium which are heated by UV photons

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/STScI-01F686BHJ60D8GR8VVRMA21V8J.png/250px-STScI-01F686BHJ60D8GR8VVRMA21V8J.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:STScI-01F686BHJ60D8GR8VVRMA21V8J.png)

The image shows the 4 primary zones of a photodissociation region: the molecular zone, the dissociation front, the ionization front, and the fully ionized flow of gas.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-1)

In [astrophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrophysics "Astrophysics"), **photodissociation regions** (or **photon-dominated regions**, **PDRs**) are predominantly neutral regions of the [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") in which far [ultraviolet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet "Ultraviolet")[photons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photon "Photon") strongly influence the gas chemistry and act as the most important source of heat.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-ht99-2) They constitute a sort of shell around sources of far-UV photons at a distance where the interstellar gas is dense enough, and the flux from the photon source is no longer strong enough, to strip electrons from the neutral constituent atoms.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-3) Despite being composed of denser gas, PDRs still have too low a [column density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Column_density "Column density") to prevent the penetration of far-UV photons from distant, massive [stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star "Star"). PDRs are also composed of a cold molecular zone that has the potential for star formation.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-4) They achieve this cooling by far-infrared fine line emissions of neutral oxygen and ionized carbon.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-5) It is theorized that PDRs are able to maintain their shape by trapped magnetic fields originating from the far-UV source.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-6) A typical and well-studied example is the gas at the boundary of a [giant molecular cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giant_molecular_cloud "Giant molecular cloud").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-ht99-2) PDRs are also associated with [HII regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HII_regions "HII regions"), [reflection nebulae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflection_nebulae "Reflection nebulae"), [active galactic nuclei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Active_galactic_nuclei "Active galactic nuclei"), and [Planetary nebulae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_nebulae "Planetary nebulae").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-7) All of a galaxy's [atomic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atom "Atom") gas and most of its [molecular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular "Molecular") gas is found in PDRs.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-review-8)

The closest PDRs to the Sun are [IC 59](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IC_59 "IC 59") and [IC 63](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IC_63 "IC 63"), near the bright [Be star](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Be_star "Be star")[Gamma Cassiopeiae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamma_Cassiopeiae "Gamma Cassiopeiae").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-Eiermann_et_al_2024-9)

History
-------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Photodissociation_region&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")]

The study of photodissociation regions began from early observations of the [star-forming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_formation "Star formation") regions [Orion A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orion_Nebula "Orion Nebula") and [M17](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omega_Nebula "Omega Nebula") which showed neutral areas bright in [infrared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrared "Infrared") radiation lying outside ionised [HII regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HII_regions "HII regions").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation_region#cite_note-review-8)
