They are orthogonal with respect to the weight ( 1 − − x ) α α ( 1 + x ) β β {\displaystyle (1-x)^{\alpha }(1+x)^{\beta }} on the interval [ − − 1 , 1 ] {\displaystyle [-1,1]} . The Gegenbauer polynomials , and thus also the Legendre , Zernike and Chebyshev polynomials , are special cases of the Jacobi polynomials.

[ 1 ] The Jacobi polynomials were introduced by Carl Gustav Jacob Jacobi .

Fix an electric particle at +1 with charge 1 + α α 2 {\displaystyle {\frac {1+\alpha }{2}}} , and another particle at -1 with charge 1 + β β 2 {\displaystyle {\frac {1+\beta }{2}}} . Then, place n {\displaystyle n} electric particles with charge + 1 {\displaystyle +1} . The first relation states that the zeroes of P n ( α α , β β ) {\displaystyle P_{n}^{(\alpha ,\beta )}} are the equilibrium positions of the particles. This equilibrium is stable and unique.

[ 15 ] Other relations, such as ∑ ∑ 1 ≤ ≤ j ≤ ≤ n , i ≠ ≠ j 1 ( x i − − x j ) 2 , ∑ ∑ 1 ≤ ≤ j ≤ ≤ n , i ≠ ≠ j 1 ( x i − − x j ) 3 {\displaystyle \sum _{1\leq j\leq n,i\neq j}{\frac {1}{(x_{i}-x_{j})^{2}}},\sum _{1\leq j\leq n,i\neq j}{\frac {1}{(x_{i}-x_{j})^{3}}}} , are known in closed form.

[ 14 ] As the zeroes specify the polynomial up to scaling, this provides an alternative way to uniquely characterize the Jacobi polynomials.

The electrostatic interpretation allows many relations to be intuitively seen. For example: the symmetry relation between P n ( α α , β β ) {\displaystyle P_{n}^{(\alpha ,\beta )}} and P n ( β β , α α ) {\displaystyle P_{n}^{(\beta ,\alpha )}} ; the roots monotonically decrease when α α {\displaystyle \alpha } increases; Since the Stieltjes relation also exists for the Hermite polynomials and the Laguerre polynomials, by taking an appropriate limit of α α , β β {\displaystyle \alpha ,\beta } , the limit relations are derived. For example, for the Hermite polynomials, the zeros satisfy − − x i + ∑ ∑ 1 ≤ ≤ j ≤ ≤ n , i ≠ ≠ j 1 x i − − x j = 0 {\displaystyle -x_{i}+\sum _{1\leq j\leq n,i\neq j}{\frac {1}{x_{i}-x_{j}}}=0} Thus, by taking α α = β β → → ∞ ∞ {\displaystyle \alpha =\beta \to \infty } limit, all the electric particles are forced into an infinitesimal neighborhood of the origin, where the field strength is linear. Then after scaling up the line, we obtain the same electrostatic configuration for the zeroes of Hermite polynomials.
