_**Bacillus subtilis**_ (),[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-4) known also as the **hay bacillus** or **grass bacillus**, is a [gram-positive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gram-positive "Gram-positive"), [catalase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalase "Catalase")-positive [bacterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacterium "Bacterium"), found in soil and the [gastrointestinal tract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gastrointestinal_tract "Gastrointestinal tract") of [ruminants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruminant "Ruminant"), humans and marine sponges.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Errington_2020-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Paul_2021-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Rahman_2020-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-8) As a member of the [genus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genus "Genus")_[Bacillus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus "Bacillus")_, _B.subtilis_ is rod-shaped, and can form a tough, protective [endospore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endospore "Endospore"), allowing it to tolerate extreme environmental conditions. _B.subtilis_ has historically been classified as an [obligate aerobe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obligate_aerobe "Obligate aerobe"), though evidence exists that it is a [facultative anaerobe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Facultative_anaerobe "Facultative anaerobe"). _B.subtilis_ is considered the best studied Gram-positive bacterium and a [model organism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Model_organism "Model organism") to study bacterial chromosome replication and cell differentiation. It is one of the bacterial champions in secreted [enzyme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enzyme "Enzyme") production and used on an industrial scale by biotechnology companies.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Errington_2020-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Paul_2021-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Rahman_2020-7)

_Bacillus subtilis_ is a [Gram-positive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gram-positive "Gram-positive") bacterium, [rod-shaped](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rod-shaped "Rod-shaped") and [catalase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalase "Catalase")-positive. It was originally named _Vibrio subtilis_ by [Christian Gottfried Ehrenberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Gottfried_Ehrenberg "Christian Gottfried Ehrenberg"),[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Ehrenberg_1835-9) and renamed _Bacillus subtilis_ by [Ferdinand Cohn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_Cohn "Ferdinand Cohn") in 1872[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Cohn_1872-10) (subtilis being the Latin for "fine, thin, slender"). _B.subtilis_ cells are typically rod-shaped, and are about 4–10 micrometers (μm) long and 0.25–1.0 μm in diameter, with a cell volume of about 4.6 fL at stationary phase.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Paul_2021-6)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Yu_2013-11)

As with other members of the [genus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genus "Genus")_[Bacillus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus "Bacillus")_, it can form an [endospore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endospore "Endospore"), to survive extreme environmental conditions of temperature and desiccation.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Brock-12)_B.subtilis_ is a [facultative anaerobe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Facultative_anaerobe "Facultative anaerobe")[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Paul_2021-6)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Nakano_1998-13) and had been considered as an [obligate aerobe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obligate_aerobe "Obligate aerobe") until 1998. _B.subtilis_ is heavily [flagellated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flagellate "Flagellate"), which gives it the ability to move quickly in liquids.

_B. subtilis_ has proven highly amenable to [genetic manipulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetic_manipulation "Genetic manipulation"), and has become widely adopted as a [model organism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Model_organism "Model organism") for laboratory studies, especially of [sporulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sporulation "Sporulation"), which is a simplified example of [cellular differentiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellular_differentiation "Cellular differentiation"). In terms of popularity as a laboratory model organism, _B.subtilis_ is often considered as the [Gram-positive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gram-positive "Gram-positive") equivalent of _[Escherichia coli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Escherichia\_coli "Escherichia coli")_, an extensively studied [Gram-negative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gram-negative "Gram-negative") bacterium.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-14)

This species is commonly found in the upper layers of the soil and _B.subtilis_ is thought to be a [normal gut commensal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gut_flora "Gut flora") in humans. A 2009 study compared the density of spores found in soil (about 10 6 spores per gram) to that found in human feces (about 10 4 spores per gram). The number of spores found in the human gut was too high to be attributed solely to consumption through food contamination.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-15) In some bee habitats, _B.subtilis_ appears in the gut flora of [honey bees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honey_bee "Honey bee").[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-16)_B.subtilis_ can also be found in marine environments.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Paul_2021-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Rahman_2020-7)

There is evidence that _B.subtilis_ is [saprophytic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saprophytic "Saprophytic") in nature. Studies have shown that the bacterium exhibits vegetative growth in soil rich in organic matter, and that spores were formed when nutrients were depleted.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Tan_2014-17) Additionally, _B.subtilis_ has been shown to form [biofilms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biofilm "Biofilm") on plant roots, which might explain why it is commonly found in gut microbiomes.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Tan_2014-17) Perhaps animals eating plants with _B.subtilis_ biofilms can foster growth of the bacterium in their gastrointestinal tract. It has been shown that the entire lifecycle of _B.subtilis_ can be completed in the gastrointestinal tract, which provides credence to the idea that the bacterium enters the gut via plant consumption and stays present as a result of its ability to grow in the gut.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Tan_2014-17)

Sporulating _B.subtilis_

[![Image 4](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Bacillus_subtilis_endospore_stain.png/250px-Bacillus_subtilis_endospore_stain.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Bacillus_subtilis_endospore_stain.png)

Another endospore stain of _B.subtilis_

_Bacillus subtilis_ can divide symmetrically to make two [daughter cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daughter_cell "Daughter cell") (binary fission), or asymmetrically, producing a single [endospore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endospore "Endospore") that can remain viable for decades and is resistant to unfavourable environmental conditions such as [drought](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drought "Drought"), [salinity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salinity "Salinity"), extreme [pH](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PH "PH"), [radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiation "Radiation"), and [solvents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solvent "Solvent"). The endospore is formed at times of nutritional stress and through the use of hydrolysis, allowing the organism to persist in the environment until conditions become favourable. Prior to the process of sporulation the cells might become [motile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motility "Motility") by producing [flagella](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flagella "Flagella"), take up DNA from the environment, or produce [antibiotics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antibiotic "Antibiotic").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Paul_2021-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Rahman_2020-7) These responses are viewed as attempts to seek out nutrients by seeking a more favourable environment, enabling the cell to make use of new beneficial genetic material or simply by killing off competition. [_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Under stressful conditions, such as nutrient deprivation, _B.subtilis_ undergoes the process of [sporulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sporulation_in_Bacillus_subtilis "Sporulation in Bacillus subtilis"). This process has been very well studied and has served as a model organism for studying sporulation.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-18)

Although sporulation in _B.subtilis_ is induced by starvation, the sporulation developmental program is not initiated immediately when growth slows due to nutrient limitation. A variety of alternative responses can occur, including the activation of [flagellar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flagellum "Flagellum") motility to seek new food sources by [chemotaxis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemotaxis "Chemotaxis"), the production of [antibiotics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antibacterial "Antibacterial") to destroy competing soil microbes, the secretion of hydrolytic [enzymes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enzyme "Enzyme") to scavenge extracellular proteins and [polysaccharides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polysaccharide "Polysaccharide"), or the induction of '[competence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Competence_(biology) "Competence (biology)")' for uptake of exogenous [DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA "DNA") for consumption, with the occasional side-effect that new genetic information is stably integrated. Sporulation is the last-ditch response to starvation and is suppressed until alternative responses prove inadequate. Even then, certain conditions must be met such as [chromosome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromosome "Chromosome") integrity, the state of chromosomal replication, and the functioning of the [Krebs cycle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citric_acid_cycle "Citric acid cycle").[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-19)

Once _B.subtilis_ commits to sporulation, the [sigma factor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sigma_factor "Sigma factor") sigma F is secreted.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-20) This factor promotes sporulation. A sporulation septum is formed and a chromosome is slowly moved into the forespore. When a third of one chromosome copy is in the forespore and the remaining two thirds is in the mother cell, the chromosome fragment in the forespore contains the locus for sigma F, which begins to be expressed in the forespore.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-21) In order to prevent sigma F expression in the mother cell, an anti-sigma factor, which is encoded by spoIIAB,[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Slonczewski_J-22) is expressed. Any residual anti-sigma factor in the forespore (which would otherwise interfere with sporulation) is inhibited by an anti-anti-sigma factor, which is encoded by spoIIAA.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Slonczewski_J-22) SpoIIAA is located near the locus for the sigma factor, so it is consistently expressed in the forespore. Since the spoIIAB locus is not located near the sigma F and spoIIAA loci, it is expressed only in the mother cell and therefore repress sporulation in that cell, allowing sporulation to continue in the forespore. Residual spoIIAA in the mother cell represses spoIIAB, but spoIIAB is constantly replaced so it continues to inhibit sporulation. When the full chromosome localizes to the forespore, spoIIAB can repress sigma F. Therefore, the genetic asymmetry of the _B.subtilis_ chromosome and expression of sigma F, spoIIAB and spoIIAA dictate spore formation in _B.subtilis._

[![Image 5: Diagram of bacterium with labeled forespore, mother cell and asymmetrically partitioned chromosomal loci](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/B._subtilis_sporulation_reg.png/250px-B._subtilis_sporulation_reg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:B._subtilis_sporulation_reg.png)

Regulation of sporulation in _B.subtilis_

Sporulation requires a great deal of time and also a lot of energy,[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-23) making it crucial for a cell to monitor its surroundings efficiently and ensure that sporulation is embarked upon at only the most appropriate times. The wrong decision can be catastrophic: a vegetative cell will die if the conditions are too harsh, while bacteria forming spores in an environment which is conducive to vegetative growth will be out competed.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-24) In short, initiation of sporulation is a very tightly [regulated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulation_of_gene_expression "Regulation of gene expression")[network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_network "Biological network") with numerous checkpoints for efficient control.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Chromosomal replication
-----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bacillus_subtilis&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Chromosomal replication")]

_Bacillus subtilis_ is a [model organism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Model_organism "Model organism") used to study bacterial chromosome replication. Replication of the single [circular chromosome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circular_chromosome "Circular chromosome") initiates at a single locus, the origin (_[oriC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origin\_of\_replication "Origin of replication")_). Replication proceeds bidirectionally and two [replication forks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Replication_fork "Replication fork") progress in clockwise and counterclockwise directions along the chromosome. Chromosome replication is completed when the forks reach the terminus region, which is positioned opposite to the origin on the [chromosome map](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromosome_map "Chromosome map"). The terminus region contains several short DNA sequences (_Ter_ sites) that promote replication arrest. Specific proteins mediate all the steps in DNA replication. Comparison between the proteins involved in chromosomal DNA replication in _B.subtilis_ and in _[Escherichia coli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Escherichia\_coli "Escherichia coli")_ reveals similarities and differences. Although the basic components promoting initiation, elongation, and termination of replication are well-[conserved](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conserved_sequence "Conserved sequence"), some important differences can be found (such as one bacterium missing proteins essential in the other). These differences underline the diversity in the mechanisms and strategies that various bacterial species have adopted to carry out the duplication of their genomes.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Graumann-25)

_Bacillus subtilis_ has about 4,100 genes. Of these, only 192 were shown to be indispensable; another 79 were predicted to be essential, as well. A vast majority of essential genes were categorized in relatively few domains of cell metabolism, with about half involved in information processing, one-fifth involved in the synthesis of cell envelope and the determination of cell shape and division, and one-tenth related to cell energetics.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-26)

The complete genome sequence of _B.subtilis_ sub-strain QB928 has 4,146,839 DNA base pairs and 4,292 genes. The QB928 strain is widely used in genetic studies due to the presence of various markers [aroI(aroK)906 purE1 dal(alrA)1 trpC2].[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-27)

Several noncoding RNAs have been characterized in the _B.subtilis_ genome in 2009, including [Bsr RNAs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis_BSR_sRNAs "Bacillus subtilis BSR sRNAs").[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-28) Microarray-based comparative genomic analyses have revealed that _B.subtilis_ members show considerable genomic diversity.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Earl-29)

FsrA is a [small RNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Small_RNA "Small RNA") found in _Bacillus subtilis_. It is an [effector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effector_(biology) "Effector (biology)") of the iron sparing response, and acts to down-regulate iron-containing proteins in times of poor iron bioavailability.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-30)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-31)

A promising fish probiotic, _B.subtilis_ strain WS1A, that possesses antimicrobial activity against _Aeromonas veronii_ and suppressed motile _Aeromonas_ septicemia in _Labeo rohita_. The de novo assembly resulted in an estimated chromosome size of 4,148,460 bp, with 4,288 open reading frames.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Paul_2021-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Rahman_2020-7)_B.subtilis_ strain WS1A genome contains many potential genes, such as those encoding proteins involved in the biosynthesis of [riboflavin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riboflavin "Riboflavin"), [vitamin B6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitamin_B6 "Vitamin B6"), and [amino acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acid "Amino acid") (_[ilvD](https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P51785)_) and in carbon utilization (_[pta](https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P39646)_).[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Paul_2021-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Rahman_2020-7)

[Natural bacterial transformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transformation_(genetics) "Transformation (genetics)") involves the transfer of DNA from one bacterium to another through the surrounding medium. In _B.subtilis_ the length of transferred DNA is greater than 1,271 kb (more than 1 million bases).[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-32) The transferred DNA is likely double-stranded DNA and is often more than a third of the total chromosome length of 4,215 kb.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-33) It appears that about 7–9% of the recipient cells take up an entire chromosome.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-34)

In order for a recipient bacterium to bind, take up exogenous DNA from another bacterium of the same species and recombine it into its chromosome, it must enter a special physiological state called [competence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Competence_(biology) "Competence (biology)"). Competence in _B.subtilis_ is induced toward the end of logarithmic growth, especially under conditions of amino-acid limitation.[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-35) Under these stressful conditions of semistarvation, cells typically have just one copy of their chromosome and likely have increased DNA damage. To test whether transformation is an adaptive function for _B.subtilis_ to repair its DNA damage, experiments were conducted using UV light as the damaging agent.[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Hoelzer1991-36)[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-37)[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-38) These experiments led to the conclusion that competence, with uptake of DNA, is specifically induced by DNA-damaging conditions, and that transformation functions as a process for recombinational repair of DNA damage.[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Michod-39)

While the natural competent state is common within laboratory _B.subtilis_ and field isolates, some industrially relevant strains, e.g. _B.subtilis_ (natto), are reluctant to DNA uptake due to the presence of restriction modification systems that degrade exogenous DNA. _B.subtilis_ (natto) mutants, which are defective in a type I restriction modification system endonuclease, are able to act as recipients of conjugative plasmids in mating experiments, paving the way for further genetic engineering of this particular _B.subtilis_ strain.[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-40)

By adopting Green Chemistry[_[buzzword](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Use\_plain\_English#Buzzwords "Wikipedia:Use plain English")_] in the use of less hazardous materials, while saving cost, researchers[_[who?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual\_of\_Style/Words\_to\_watch#Unsupported\_attributions "Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch")_] have been mimicking nature's methods of synthesizing chemicals that can be useful for the food and drug industry, by "piggybacking molecules on shorts strands of DNA" before they are zipped together during their complementary base pairing between the two strands. Each strand will carry a particular molecule of interest that will undergo a specific chemical reaction simultaneously when the two corresponding strands of DNA pairs hold together like a zipper, allowing another molecule of interest, to react with one another in controlled and isolated reaction between those molecules being carried into these DNA complementary attachments. By using this method with certain bacteria that naturally follow a process replication in a multi-step fashion, the researchers can simultaneously carry on the interactions of these added molecules to interact with enzymes and other molecules used for a secondary reaction by treating it like a capsule, which is similar to how the bacteria performs its own DNA replication processes.[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-41)

Gram-stained _B.subtilis_

Cultures of _B.subtilis_ were popular worldwide, before the introduction of [antibiotics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antibiotics "Antibiotics"), as an immunostimulatory agent to aid treatment of [gastrointestinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gastrointestinal "Gastrointestinal") and [urinary tract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urinary_tract "Urinary tract") diseases. It was used throughout the 1950s as an [alternative medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternative_medicine "Alternative medicine"), which upon digestion has been found to significantly stimulate [broad-spectrum immune activity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broad-spectrum_therapeutic "Broad-spectrum therapeutic") including activation of secretion of specific [antibodies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antibodies "Antibodies")[IgM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IgM "IgM"), [IgG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immunoglobulin_G "Immunoglobulin G") and [IgA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IgA "IgA")[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Ciprandi-42) and release of [CpG dinucleotides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CpG_dinucleotide "CpG dinucleotide") inducing [interferon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interferon "Interferon")[IFN-α](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IFN-%CE%B1 "IFN-α")/[IFNγ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IFN%CE%B3 "IFNγ") producing activity of [leukocytes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leukocytes "Leukocytes") and [cytokines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytokines "Cytokines") important in the development of [cytotoxicity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytotoxicity "Cytotoxicity") towards [tumor cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tumor_cells "Tumor cells").[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Shylakhovenko-43) It was marketed throughout America and Europe from 1946 as an immunostimulatory aid in the treatment of gut and urinary tract diseases such as [Rotavirus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotavirus "Rotavirus") and [Shigellosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shigellosis "Shigellosis"). In 1966, the U.S. Army dumped _Bacillus subtilis_ onto the grates of New York City subway stations for five days in order to observe how a biological agent dispensed around the subway trains would disperse and potentially affect unsuspecting passengers.[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-44) Due to its ability to survive, it is thought to still be present there.[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-45)

The antibiotic [bacitracin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacitracin "Bacitracin") was first isolated from a variety of _[Bacillus licheniformis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus\_licheniformis "Bacillus licheniformis")_ named "Tracy I"[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-46) in 1945, then considered part of the _B.subtilis_ species. It is still commercially manufactured by growing the variety in a container of liquid [growth medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Growth_medium "Growth medium"). Over time, the bacteria synthesize bacitracin and secrete the antibiotic into the medium. The bacitracin is then extracted from the medium using chemical processes.[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-pmid17770204-47)

Since the 1960s _B.subtilis_ has had a history as a test species in spaceflight experimentation. Its [endospores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endospore "Endospore") can survive up to 6 years in space if coated by dust particles protecting it from solar UV rays.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-horneck-48) It has been used as an [extremophile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extremophile "Extremophile") survival indicator in [outer space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space "Outer space") such as [Exobiology Radiation Assembly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exobiology_Radiation_Assembly "Exobiology Radiation Assembly"),[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Dose-49)[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Horneck-50)[EXOSTACK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EXOSTACK "EXOSTACK"),[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Clancy-51)[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Gerda_Horneck-52) and [EXPOSE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EXPOSE "EXPOSE") orbital missions.[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-53)[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Brandstatter2008-54)[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-55)

Wild-type natural isolates of _B.subtilis_ are difficult to work with compared with laboratory strains that have undergone domestication processes of [mutagenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mutagenesis "Mutagenesis") and selection. These strains often have improved capabilities of transformation (uptake and integration of environmental DNA), growth, and loss of abilities needed "in the wild". And, while dozens of different strains fitting this description exist, the strain designated '168' is the most widely used. Strain 168 is a [tryptophan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tryptophan "Tryptophan")[auxotroph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auxotroph "Auxotroph") isolated after X-ray mutagenesis of _B.subtilis_ Marburg strain and is widely used in research due to its high transformation efficiency.[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-56)

[![Image 7](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Bacillus_subtilis_colonies.jpg/250px-Bacillus_subtilis_colonies.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Bacillus_subtilis_colonies.jpg)

Colonies of _B.subtilis_ grown on a [culture dish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_dish "Culture dish") in a [molecular biology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_biology "Molecular biology")[laboratory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laboratory "Laboratory")

_Bacillus globigii_, a closely related but [phylogenetically](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phylogenetic "Phylogenetic") distinct species now known as _[Bacillus atrophaeus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus\_atrophaeus "Bacillus atrophaeus")_[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-57)[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-58) was used as a biowarfare [simulant](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/simulant "wiktionary:simulant") during [Project SHAD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_SHAD "Project SHAD") (aka _Project 112_).[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-59) Subsequent genomic analysis showed that the strains used in those studies were products of deliberate enrichment for strains that exhibited abnormally high rates of [sporulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sporulation "Sporulation").[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-60)

A strain of _B.subtilis_ formerly known as _Bacillus natto_ is used in the commercial production of the Japanese food _[nattō](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natt%C5%8D "Nattō")_, as well as the similar Korean food _[cheonggukjang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cheonggukjang "Cheonggukjang")_.

*   As a [model organism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Model_organism "Model organism"), _B.subtilis_ is commonly used in laboratory studies directed at discovering the fundamental properties and characteristics of Gram-positive spore-forming bacteria.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-Earl-29) In particular, the basic principles and mechanisms underlying formation of the durable endospore have been deduced from studies of spore formation in _B.subtilis_.
*   Its surface-binding properties play a role in safe radionuclide waste [e.g. thorium (IV) and plutonium (IV)] disposal.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]
*   Due to its excellent fermentation properties, with high product yields (20 to 25 gram per litre) it is used to produce various enzymes, such as [amylase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amylase "Amylase") and [proteases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protease "Protease").[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-mcf2013-61)
*   _B. subtilis_ is used as a [soil inoculant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil_inoculant "Soil inoculant") in [horticulture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horticulture "Horticulture") and [agriculture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture "Agriculture").[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-62)[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-63)[[64]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-64)
*   It may provide some benefit to [saffron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saffron "Saffron") growers by speeding corn growth and increasing stigma biomass yield.[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-65)
*   It is used as an "indicator organism" during gas sterilization procedures, to ensure a sterilization cycle has completed successfully. Specifically _B.subtilis_ endospores are used to verify that a cycle has reached spore-destroying conditions.[[66]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-66)[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-67)
*   _B. subtilis_ has been found to act as a useful bioproduct fungicide that prevents the growth of _[Monilinia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monilinia "Monilinia") vaccinii-corymbosi_, a.k.a. the mummy berry fungus, without interfering with pollination or fruit qualities.[[68]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-68)
*   Both metabolically active and non-metabolically active _B.subtilis_ cells have been shown to reduce gold (III) to gold (I) and gold (0) when oxygen is present. This biotic reduction plays a role in gold cycling in geological systems and could potentially be used to recover solid gold from said systems.
*   Pairing bacillus subtilis and with chitosan has been shown to reduce green mold decay in citrus fruits.[[69]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-69)

### Novel and artificial substrains

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bacillus_subtilis&action=edit&section=12 "Edit section: Novel and artificial substrains")]

*   Novel strains of _B.subtilis_ that could use 4-fluorotryptophan (4FTrp) but not canonical tryptophan (Trp) for propagation were isolated. As [Trp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trp_operon "Trp operon") is only coded by a single codon, there is evidence that Trp can be displaced by 4FTrp in the genetic code. The experiments showed that the canonical genetic code can be mutable.[[70]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-70)
*   Recombinant strains pBE2C1 and pBE2C1AB were used in production of [polyhydroxyalkanoates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyhydroxyalkanoates "Polyhydroxyalkanoates") (PHA), and malt waste can be used as their carbon source for lower-cost PHA production.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]
*   It is used to produce [hyaluronic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyaluronic_acid "Hyaluronic acid"), which is used in the joint-care sector in healthcare[[71]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-71) and cosmetics.
*   [Monsanto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsanto "Monsanto") has isolated a gene from _B.subtilis_ that expresses cold shock protein B and spliced it into their drought-tolerant corn hybrid MON 87460, which was approved for sale in the US in November 2011.[[72]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-72)[[73]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-73)
*   A new strain has been modified to convert nectar into [honey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honey "Honey") by secreting enzymes.[[74]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-74)

_Bacillus subtilis_ was reviewed by the US FDA [Center for Veterinary Medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Center_for_Veterinary_Medicine "Center for Veterinary Medicine") and found to present no safety concerns when used in direct-fed microbial products, so the [Association of American Feed Control Officials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_of_American_Feed_Control_Officials "Association of American Feed Control Officials") has listed it approved for use as an [animal feed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal_feed "Animal feed") ingredient under Section 36.14 "Direct-fed Microorganisms".[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_] The [Canadian Food Inspection Agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canadian_Food_Inspection_Agency "Canadian Food Inspection Agency") Animal Health and Production Feed Section has classified _Bacillus_ culture dehydrated approved feed ingredients as a [silage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silage "Silage") additive under Schedule IV-Part 2-Class 8.6 and assigned the International Feed Ingredient number IFN 8-19-119.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_] On the other hand, several feed additives containing viable spores of _B.subtilis_ have been positively evaluated by the [European Food Safety Authority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Food_Safety_Authority "European Food Safety Authority"), regarding their safe use for weight gaining in animal production.

_Bacillus subtilis_ spores can survive the extreme heat generated during cooking. Some _B.subtilis_ strains are responsible for causing ropiness or rope spoilage– a sticky, stringy consistency caused by bacterial production of long-chain [polysaccharides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polysaccharide "Polysaccharide")– in spoiled bread dough and baked goods.[[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-75) For a long time, bread ropiness was associated uniquely with _B.subtilis_ species by biochemical tests. Molecular assays (randomly amplified polymorphic DNA PCR assay, [denaturing gradient gel electrophoresis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denaturing_gradient_gel_electrophoresis "Denaturing gradient gel electrophoresis") analysis, and sequencing of the V3 region of [16S ribosomal DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/16S_ribosomal_DNA "16S ribosomal DNA")) revealed greater _Bacillus_ species variety in ropy breads, which all seems to have a positive amylase activity and high heat resistance.[[76]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-76)

_B. subtilis_ CU1 (2 billion spores per day) was evaluated in a 16-week study (10 days administration of probiotic, followed by 18 days wash-out period per each month; repeated same procedure for total 4 months) to healthy subjects. _B.subtilis_ CU1 was found to be safe and well tolerated in the subjects without any side effects.[[77]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-77)

_Bacillus subtilis_ and substances derived from it have been evaluated by different authoritative bodies for their safe and beneficial use in food. In the United States, an opinion letter issued in the early 1960s by the [Food and Drug Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration "Food and Drug Administration") (FDA) designated some substances derived from microorganisms as [generally recognized as safe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generally_recognized_as_safe "Generally recognized as safe") (GRAS), including [carbohydrase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbohydrase "Carbohydrase") and protease enzymes from _B.subtilis_. The opinions were predicated on the use of nonpathogenic and nontoxicogenic strains of the respective organisms and on the use of current good manufacturing practices.[[78]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-78) The FDA stated that the enzymes derived from the _B.subtilis_ strain were in common use in food prior to January 1, 1958, and that nontoxigenic and nonpathogenic strains of _B.subtilis_ are widely available and have been safely used in a variety of food applications. This includes consumption of Japanese fermented soy bean, in the form of _[Natto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natto "Natto")_, which is commonly consumed in Japan, and contains as many as 10 8 viable cells per gram. The fermented beans are recognized for their contribution to a healthy gut flora and [vitamin K 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitamin_K2 "Vitamin K2") intake; during this long history of widespread use, _natto_ has not been implicated in adverse events potentially attributable to the presence of _B.subtilis_.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_] The natto product and the _B.subtilis_ natto as its principal component are FOSHU (Foods for Specified Health Use) approved by the Japanese [Ministry of Health, Labour, and Welfare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Health,_Labour,_and_Welfare "Ministry of Health, Labour, and Welfare") as effective for preservation of health.[[79]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-79)

_Bacillus subtilis_ has been granted "Qualified Presumption of Safety" status by the [European Food Safety Authority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Food_Safety_Authority "European Food Safety Authority").[[80]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis#cite_note-80)
