Rouse Ball , was a British mathematician , lawyer, and fellow at Trinity College, Cambridge , from 1878 to 1905. He was also a keen amateur magician, and the founding president of the Cambridge Pentacle Club in 1919, one of the world's oldest magic societies.

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] Life [ edit ] Born 14 August 1850 in Hampstead , London , Ball was the son and heir of Walter Frederick Ball, of 3, St John's Park Villas, South Hampstead, London. Educated at University College School , he entered Trinity College, Cambridge , in 1870, became a scholar and first Smith's Prizeman , and gained his BA in 1874 as second Wrangler . He became a Fellow of Trinity in 1875, and remained one for the rest of his life.

[ 3 ] He died on 4 April 1925 in Elmside, Cambridge , [ 2 ] and is buried at the Parish of the Ascension Burial Ground in Cambridge.

[ 4 ] He is commemorated in the naming of the small pavilion, now used as changing rooms and toilets, on Jesus Green in Cambridge.
