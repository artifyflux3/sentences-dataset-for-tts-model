**Acetamide** (systematic name: **ethanamide**) is an [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the formula CH 3 CONH 2. It is an [amide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amide "Amide") derived from [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia") and [acetic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetic_acid "Acetic acid"). It finds some use as a [plasticizer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasticizer "Plasticizer") and as an industrial solvent.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-ullmann-5) The related compound [_N_,_N_-dimethylacetamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethylacetamide "Dimethylacetamide") (DMA) is more widely used, but it is not prepared from acetamide. Acetamide can be considered an intermediate between [acetone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetone "Acetone"), which has two methyl (CH 3) groups either side of the carbonyl (CO), and [urea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urea "Urea") which has two amide (NH 2) groups in those locations. Acetamide is also a naturally occurring mineral[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-6) with the [IMA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Mineralogical_Association "International Mineralogical Association")[symbol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_mineral_symbols "List of mineral symbols"): Ace.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-7)

[![Image 19](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/CSD_CIF_ACEMID06.jpg/330px-CSD_CIF_ACEMID06.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:CSD_CIF_ACEMID06.jpg)

Structure of acetamide [hydrogen-bonded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen-bond "Hydrogen-bond") dimer from [X-ray crystallography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-ray_crystallography "X-ray crystallography"). Selected distances: C-O: 1.243, C-N, 1.325, N---O, 2.925 Å. Color code: red = O, blue = N, gray = C, white = H.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-8)

Acetamide can be produced in the laboratory from [ammonium acetate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonium_acetate "Ammonium acetate") by [dehydration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dehydration_reaction "Dehydration reaction"):[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-9)

[NH 4][CH 3 CO 2] → CH 3 C(O)NH 2 + H 2 O
Alternatively acetamide can be obtained in excellent yield via [ammonolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonolysis "Ammonolysis") of [acetylacetone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetylacetone "Acetylacetone") under conditions commonly used in [reductive amination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reductive_amination "Reductive amination").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-10)

It can also be made from anhydrous acetic acid, acetonitrile and very well dried hydrogen chloride gas, using an ice bath, alongside more valuable reagent [acetyl chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetyl_chloride "Acetyl chloride"). Yield is typically low (up to 35%), and the acetamide made this way is generated as a salt with HCl.

In a similar fashion to some laboratory methods, acetamide is produced by [dehydrating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dehydration_reaction "Dehydration reaction") ammonium acetate or via the [hydration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydration_reaction "Hydration reaction") of [acetonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetonitrile "Acetonitrile"), a byproduct of the production of [acrylonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrylonitrile "Acrylonitrile"):[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-ullmann-5)

CH 3 CN + H 2 O → CH 3 C(O)NH 2
Acetamide is used as a plasticizer and an industrial solvent.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-ullmann-5) Molten acetamide is good solvent with a broad range of applicability. Notably, its [dielectric constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dielectric_constant "Dielectric constant") is higher than most organic solvents, allowing it to dissolve [inorganic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inorganic_compound "Inorganic compound") with solubilities closely analogous to that of water.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-11) Acetamide has uses in electrochemistry and the [organic synthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_synthesis "Organic synthesis") of pharmaceuticals, pesticides, and antioxidants for plastics.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-12) It is a precursor to [thioacetamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioacetamide "Thioacetamide").[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-13)

Acetamide has been detected near the center of the [Milky Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way "Milky Way") galaxy.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-14) This finding is potentially significant because acetamide has an amide bond, similar to the essential bond between amino acids in proteins. This finding lends support to the theory that organic molecules that can lead to life (as we know it on [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth")) can form in space.

On 30 July 2015, scientists reported that upon the first touchdown of the _[Philae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philae\_(spacecraft) "Philae (spacecraft)")_ lander on [comet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comet "Comet")[67/P](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/67/P "67/P")'s surface, measurements by the COSAC and Ptolemy instruments revealed sixteen [organic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound"), four of which – acetamide, [acetone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetone "Acetone"), [methyl isocyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_isocyanate "Methyl isocyanate"), and [propionaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionaldehyde "Propionaldehyde")[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-wapo20150730-15)[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-esa20150730-16)[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-SCI-20150731-17) – were seen for the first time on a comet.

In addition, acetamide is found infrequently on burning coal dumps, as a mineral of the same name.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-18)[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetamide#cite_note-19)
