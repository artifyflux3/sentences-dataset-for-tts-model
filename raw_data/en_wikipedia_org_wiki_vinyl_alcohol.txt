Chemical compound

**Vinyl alcohol**, also called **ethenol** (IUPAC name; not ethanol) or **ethylenol**, is the simplest [enol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enol "Enol"). With the [formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula")[C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon")[H](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen")2 CH[O](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen")H, it is a labile compound that converts to [acetaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetaldehyde "Acetaldehyde") immediately upon isolation near room temperature.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-March-1) It is not a practical precursor to any compound.

Synthesis
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vinyl_alcohol&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Synthesis")]

Vinyl alcohol can be formed by the pyrolytic [elimination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elimination_reaction "Elimination reaction") of water from [ethylene glycol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethylene_glycol "Ethylene glycol") at a temperature of 900°C and low pressure. Such processes are of no practical importance.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-2)

Tautomerization of vinyl alcohol to acetaldehyde
------------------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vinyl_alcohol&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Tautomerization of vinyl alcohol to acetaldehyde")]

Under normal conditions, vinyl alcohol converts ([tautomerizes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tautomer "Tautomer")) to [acetaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetaldehyde "Acetaldehyde"):

[![Image 32](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Ethenol%E2%80%93ethanal_tautom%C3%A9risation.svg/250px-Ethenol%E2%80%93ethanal_tautom%C3%A9risation.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Ethenol%E2%80%93ethanal_tautom%C3%A9risation.svg)
At room temperature, acetaldehyde (H 3 CC(O)H) is more stable than vinyl alcohol (H 2 C=CHOH) by 42.7 kJ/mol.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-3) Vinyl alcohol gas isomerizes to the aldehyde with a half-life of 30 min at room temperature.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-March-1)

H 2 C=CHOH → H 3 CC(O)H[![Image 33](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/WackerCycleKeith%26HenryImp.png/250px-WackerCycleKeith%26HenryImp.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:WackerCycleKeith%26HenryImp.png)

The industrial synthesis of [acetaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetaldehyde "Acetaldehyde") ([Wacker process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wacker_process "Wacker process")) proceeds via the intermediacy of a vinyl alcohol complex.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-K&H-4)

The uncatalyzed [keto–enol tautomerism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keto%E2%80%93enol_tautomerism "Keto–enol tautomerism") by a 1,3-hydrogen migration is forbidden by the [Woodward–Hoffmann rules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woodward%E2%80%93Hoffmann_rules "Woodward–Hoffmann rules") and therefore has a high activation barrier and is not a significant pathway at or near room temperature. However, even trace amounts of acids or bases (including water) can catalyze the reaction. Even with rigorous precautions to minimize adventitious moisture or proton sources, vinyl alcohol can only be stored for minutes to hours before it isomerizes to acetaldehyde. ([Carbonic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbonic_acid "Carbonic acid") is another example of a substance that is stable when rigorously pure, but decomposes rapidly due to catalysis by trace moisture.)

The tautomerization can also be catalyzed via photochemical process. These findings suggest that the keto–enol tautomerization is a viable route under atmospheric and stratospheric conditions, relevant to a role for vinyl alcohol in the production of organic acids in the atmosphere.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-6)

Vinyl alcohol can be stabilized by controlling the water concentration in the system and utilizing the kinetic favorability of the [deuterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deuterium "Deuterium")-produced [kinetic isotope effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_isotope_effect "Kinetic isotope effect") (_k_ H+/_k_ D+ = 4.75, _k_ H 2 O/_k_ D 2 O = 12). Deuterium stabilization can be accomplished through hydrolysis of a [ketene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ketene "Ketene") precursor in the presence of a slight stoichiometric excess of [heavy water](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heavy_water "Heavy water") (D 2 O). Studies show that the tautomerization process is significantly inhibited at ambient temperatures ( _k_ t ≈ 10−6 M/s), and the [half-life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Half-life "Half-life") of the enol form can easily be increased to _t_ 1/2 = 42 minutes for first-order hydrolysis kinetics.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-7)

Relationship to poly(vinyl alcohol)
-----------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vinyl_alcohol&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Relationship to poly(vinyl alcohol)")]

Because of the instability of vinyl alcohol, the [thermoplastic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermoplastic "Thermoplastic")[polyvinyl alcohol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyvinyl_alcohol "Polyvinyl alcohol") (PVA or PVOH) is made indirectly by polymerization of [vinyl acetate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_acetate "Vinyl acetate") followed by [hydrolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrolysis "Hydrolysis") of the [ester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ester "Ester") bonds (Ac = acetyl; HOAc = acetic acid):[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-Ullmann-8)

_n_ CH 2=CHOAc → (CH 2−CHOAc)_n_(CH 2−CHOAc)_n_ + _n_ H 2 O → (CH 2−CHOH)_n_ + _n_ HOAc

As a ligand
-----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vinyl_alcohol&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: As a ligand")]

Several [metal complexes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coordination_complex "Coordination complex") are known that contain vinyl alcohol as a [ligand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ligand "Ligand"). One example is Pt(acac)(η 2-C 2 H 3 OH)Cl.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-9)

Occurrence in interstellar medium

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vinyl_alcohol&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Occurrence in interstellar medium")]

Vinyl alcohol was detected in the [molecular cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud")[Sagittarius B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagittarius_B2 "Sagittarius B2") in 2001, the last of the three stable [isomers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomer "Isomer") of C

2 H

4 O (after acetaldehyde and [ethylene oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethylene_oxide "Ethylene oxide")) to be detected in space.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-10)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-SpaceVA-11) Its stability in the (dilute) [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") shows that its tautomerization does not happen [unimolecularly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecularity "Molecularity"),[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-SpaceVA-11) a fact attributed to the size of the [activation energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Activation_energy "Activation energy") barrier to the rearrangement being insurmountable at temperatures present in interstellar space.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-ChemPhysChem2021-12) The vinyl alcohol to acetaldehyde rearrangement is the only keto-enol tautomerisation to have been detected in deep space, induced by the provision of secondary electrons from [galactic cosmic rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galactic_cosmic_rays "Galactic cosmic rays").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinyl_alcohol#cite_note-ChemPhysChem2021-12)
