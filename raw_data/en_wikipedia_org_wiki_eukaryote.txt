The **eukaryotes** ( [_yoo-KARR-ee-ohts, -⁠əts_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key"))[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-3) comprise the [domain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domain_(biology) "Domain (biology)") of **Eukaryota** or **Eukarya**, [organisms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organism "Organism") whose [cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_(biology) "Cell (biology)") have a membrane-bound [nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_nucleus "Cell nucleus"). All [animals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal "Animal"), [plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plant "Plant"), [fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungus "Fungus"), [seaweeds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seaweed "Seaweed"), and many [unicellular organisms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicellular_organism "Unicellular organism") are eukaryotes. They constitute a major group of [life forms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outline_of_life_forms "Outline of life forms") alongside the two groups of [prokaryotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote "Prokaryote"): the [Bacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacteria "Bacteria") and the [Archaea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaea "Archaea"). Eukaryotes represent a small minority of the number of organisms, but given their generally much larger size, their collective [global biomass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_biomass "Global biomass") is much larger than that of prokaryotes.

The eukaryotes emerged within the archaeal [kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_(biology) "Kingdom (biology)")[Promethearchaeati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asgard_(Archaea) "Asgard (Archaea)"), near or inside the [class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Class_(biology) "Class (biology)") "[_Candidatus_ Heimdallarchaeia](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Candidatus_Heimdallarchaeia&action=edit&redlink=1 "Candidatus Heimdallarchaeia (page does not exist)")".[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Zhang-2025-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-5) This implies that there are only [two domains of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two-domain_system "Two-domain system"), Bacteria and Archaea, with eukaryotes incorporated among the Archaea. Eukaryotes first emerged during the [Paleoproterozoic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleoproterozoic "Paleoproterozoic"), likely as [flagellated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flagellated_cell "Flagellated cell") cells. The leading [evolutionary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evolutionary "Evolutionary") theory is they were created by [symbiogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbiogenesis "Symbiogenesis") between an anaerobic Promethearchaeati archaean and an aerobic [proteobacterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proteobacterium "Proteobacterium"), which formed the [mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrion "Mitochondrion"). A second episode of symbiogenesis with a [cyanobacterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanobacteria "Cyanobacteria") created the plants, with [chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast "Chloroplast").

Eukaryotic cells contain [membrane-bound organelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organelle "Organelle") such as the [nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_nucleus "Cell nucleus"), the [endoplasmic reticulum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endoplasmic_reticulum "Endoplasmic reticulum"), and the [Golgi apparatus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golgi_apparatus "Golgi apparatus"). Eukaryotes may be either [unicellular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicellular_organism "Unicellular organism") or [multicellular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multicellular_organism "Multicellular organism"). In comparison, prokaryotes are typically unicellular. Unicellular eukaryotes are sometimes called [protists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protist "Protist"). Eukaryotes can reproduce both [asexually](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asexual_reproduction "Asexual reproduction") through [mitosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitosis "Mitosis") and [sexually](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sexual_reproduction "Sexual reproduction") through [meiosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meiosis "Meiosis") and [gamete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamete "Gamete") fusion ([fertilization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fertilization "Fertilization")).

The word _eukaryote_ is derived from the [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_language "Greek language") words "eu" (εὖ) meaning "true" or "good" and "karyon" (κάρυον) meaning "nut" or "kernel", referring to the [nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_nucleus "Cell nucleus") of a [cell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_(biology) "Cell (biology)").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-6)

Eukaryotes are [organisms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organism "Organism") that range from microscopic single [cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_(biology) "Cell (biology)"), such as [picozoans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Picozoa "Picozoa") under 3 micrometres across,[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Seenivasan_Sausen_Medlin_Melkonian_2013_p=e59565-7) to [animals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal "Animal") like the [blue whale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blue_whale "Blue whale"), weighing up to 190 [tonnes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tonne "Tonne") and measuring up to 33.6 metres (110 ft) long,[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Wood-8) or [plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plant "Plant") like the [coast redwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sequoia_sempervirens "Sequoia sempervirens"), up to 120 metres (390 ft) tall.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-9) Many eukaryotes are unicellular; the informal grouping called [protists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protist "Protist") includes many of these, with some multicellular forms like the [giant kelp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macrocystis "Macrocystis") up to 200 feet (61 m) long.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Hoek_95-10) The multicellular eukaryotes include the animals, plants, and [fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungus "Fungus"), but again, these groups too contain many unicellular [species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Species "Species").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-burki2014-11) Eukaryotic cells are typically much larger than those of [prokaryotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote "Prokaryote")—the [bacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacteria "Bacteria") and the [archaea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaea "Archaea")—having a volume of around 10,000 times greater.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-DeRennaux_2001_pp._329%E2%80%93332-12)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-13) Eukaryotes represent a small minority of the number of [organisms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organism "Organism"), but, as many of them are much larger, their collective global [biomass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomass_(ecology) "Biomass (ecology)") (468 gigatons) is far larger than that of prokaryotes (77 gigatons), with plants alone accounting for over 81% of the total biomass of [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-The_biomass_of_Earth-14)

The eukaryotes are a diverse lineage, consisting mainly of [microscopic organisms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microorganism "Microorganism").[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Burki_Roger_Brown_Simpson_2020_pp._43%E2%80%9355-15) Multicellularity in some form has [evolved independently](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evolved_independently "Evolved independently") at least 25 times within the eukaryotes.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Grosberg2007-16)[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-17) Complex multicellular organisms, not counting the aggregation of [amoebae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amoeba_(genus) "Amoeba (genus)") to form [slime molds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slime_mold "Slime mold"), have evolved within only six eukaryotic lineages: [animals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animals "Animals"), [symbiomycotan fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungi#Phylogeny "Fungi"), [brown algae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brown_algae "Brown algae"), [red algae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_algae "Red algae"), [green algae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_algae "Green algae"), and [land plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embryophyte "Embryophyte").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-18) Eukaryotes are grouped by genomic similarities, so that groups often lack visible shared characteristics.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Burki_Roger_Brown_Simpson_2020_pp._43%E2%80%9355-15)

Distinguishing features
-----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eukaryote&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Distinguishing features")]

The defining feature of eukaryotes is that [their cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_(biology)#Eukaryotic_cells "Cell (biology)") have a well-defined, membrane-bound [nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleus_(cell) "Nucleus (cell)"), distinguishing them from [prokaryotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote "Prokaryote") that lack such a structure. Eukaryotic cells have a variety of internal membrane-bound structures, called [organelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organelle "Organelle"), and a [cytoskeleton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytoskeleton "Cytoskeleton") which defines the cell's organization and shape. The nucleus stores the cell's [DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA "DNA"), which is divided into linear bundles called [chromosomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromosome "Chromosome");[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-19) these are separated into two matching sets by a [microtubular spindle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spindle_apparatus "Spindle apparatus") during nuclear division, in the distinctively eukaryotic process of [mitosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitosis "Mitosis").[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-20)

Eukaryotes differ from prokaryotes in multiple ways, with unique biochemical pathways such as [sterane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sterane "Sterane") synthesis.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-sterane-21) The eukaryotic signature [proteins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein "Protein") have no homology to proteins in other domains of life, but appear to be universal among eukaryotes. They include the proteins of the cytoskeleton, the complex [transcription](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transcription_(biology) "Transcription (biology)") machinery, the membrane-sorting systems, the [nuclear pore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_pore "Nuclear pore"), and some [enzymes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enzyme "Enzyme") in the biochemical pathways.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-pmid11805300-22)

[![Image 14](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Prokaryote_cell.svg/60px-Prokaryote_cell.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Prokaryote_cell.svg)

[![Image 15](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Endomembrane_system_diagram_en.svg/330px-Endomembrane_system_diagram_en.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Endomembrane_system_diagram_en.svg)

Eukaryote cells include a variety of membrane-bound structures, together forming the endomembrane system.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-23) Simple compartments, called [vesicles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vesicle_(biology) "Vesicle (biology)") and [vacuoles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vacuole "Vacuole"), can form by budding off other membranes. Many cells ingest food and other materials through a process of [endocytosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endocytosis "Endocytosis"), where the outer membrane [invaginates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invagination "Invagination") and then pinches off to form a vesicle.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-24) Some cell products can leave in a vesicle through [exocytosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exocytosis "Exocytosis").[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-25)

The nucleus is surrounded by a double membrane known as the [nuclear envelope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_envelope "Nuclear envelope"), with [nuclear pores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_pore "Nuclear pore") that allow material to move in and out.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-TheNuclearEnvelope-NCBI-26) Various tube- and sheet-like extensions of the nuclear membrane form the [endoplasmic reticulum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endoplasmic_reticulum "Endoplasmic reticulum"), which is involved in [protein transport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein_transport "Protein transport") and maturation. It includes the rough endoplasmic reticulum, covered in [ribosomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ribosome "Ribosome") which synthesize proteins; these enter the interior space or lumen. Subsequently, they generally enter vesicles, which bud off from the smooth endoplasmic reticulum.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-27) In most eukaryotes, these protein-carrying vesicles are released and their contents further modified in stacks of flattened vesicles ([cisternae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cisterna "Cisterna")), the [Golgi apparatus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golgi_apparatus "Golgi apparatus").[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-28)

Vesicles may be specialized; for instance, [lysosomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lysosome "Lysosome") contain [digestive enzymes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digestive_enzyme "Digestive enzyme") that break down [biomolecules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomolecule "Biomolecule") in the cytoplasm.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-29)

[![Image 16](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Mitochondrion_structure.svg/500px-Mitochondrion_structure.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Mitochondrion_structure.svg)

Mitochondria are essentially universal in the eukaryotes, and with their own [DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA "DNA") somewhat resemble prokaryotic cells.

Mitochondria are organelles in eukaryotic cells. The mitochondrion is commonly called "the powerhouse of the cell",[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-30) for its function providing energy by oxidising sugars or fats to produce the energy-storing molecule [ATP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adenosine_triphosphate "Adenosine triphosphate").[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Voet-31)[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-32) Mitochondria have two surrounding [membranes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrion#Structure "Mitochondrion"), each a [phospholipid bilayer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipid_bilayer "Lipid bilayer"), the [inner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inner_mitochondrial_membrane "Inner mitochondrial membrane") of which is folded into invaginations called [cristae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cristae "Cristae") where [aerobic respiration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aerobic_respiration "Aerobic respiration") takes place.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-33)

Mitochondria contain [their own DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrial_DNA "Mitochondrial DNA"), which has close structural similarities to [bacterial DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacterial_DNA "Bacterial DNA"), from which it originated, and which encodes [rRNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RRNA "RRNA") and [tRNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TRNA "TRNA") genes that produce RNA which is closer in structure to bacterial RNA than to eukaryote RNA.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-MolecGene-34)

Some eukaryotes, such as the [metamonads](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metamonad "Metamonad")_[Giardia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giardia "Giardia")_ and _[Trichomonas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trichomonas "Trichomonas")_, and the amoebozoan _[Pelomyxa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pelomyxa "Pelomyxa")_, appear to lack mitochondria, but all contain mitochondrion-derived organelles, like [hydrogenosomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogenosome "Hydrogenosome") or [mitosomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitosome "Mitosome"), having lost their mitochondria secondarily.[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Karn-35) They obtain energy by enzymatic action in the cytoplasm.[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-36)[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Karn-35) It is thought that mitochondria developed from [prokaryotic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote "Prokaryote") cells which became [endosymbionts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endosymbiont "Endosymbiont") living inside eukaryotes.[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-37)

[![Image 17](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Chloroplast_II_%28cropped%29.svg/330px-Chloroplast_II_%28cropped%29.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Chloroplast_II_(cropped).svg)

The most common type of plastid is the [chloroplast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast "Chloroplast"), which contains [chlorophyll](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chlorophyll "Chlorophyll") and produces organic compounds by [photosynthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photosynthesis "Photosynthesis").

Plants and various groups of [algae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algae "Algae") have plastids as well as mitochondria. Plastids, like mitochondria, have [their own DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast_DNA "Chloroplast DNA") and are developed from [endosymbionts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endosymbionts "Endosymbionts"), in this case [cyanobacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanobacteria "Cyanobacteria"). They usually take the form of [chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast "Chloroplast") which, like cyanobacteria, contain [chlorophyll](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chlorophyll "Chlorophyll") and produce organic compounds (such as [glucose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glucose "Glucose")) through [photosynthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photosynthesis "Photosynthesis"). Others are involved in storing food. Although plastids probably had a single origin, not all plastid-containing groups are closely related. Instead, some eukaryotes have obtained them from other eukaryotes through [secondary endosymbiosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secondary_endosymbiosis "Secondary endosymbiosis") or ingestion.[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-38) The capture and sequestering of photosynthetic cells and chloroplasts, [kleptoplasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kleptoplasty "Kleptoplasty"), occurs in many types of modern eukaryotic organisms.[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-pmid18518896-39)[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Kleptoplastidy_Hypothesis-40)

### Cytoskeletal structures

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eukaryote&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Cytoskeletal structures")]

[![Image 18](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/FluorescentCells.jpg/250px-FluorescentCells.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:FluorescentCells.jpg)

The cytoskeleton. [Actin filaments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actin_filaments "Actin filaments") are shown in red, [microtubules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microtubules "Microtubules") in green. (The nucleus is in blue.)

The cytoskeleton provides stiffening structure and points of attachment for motor structures that enable the cell to move, change shape, or transport materials. The motor structures are [microfilaments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microfilament "Microfilament") of [actin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actin "Actin") and [actin-binding proteins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actin-binding_protein "Actin-binding protein"). These include α-[actinin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actinin "Actinin"), [fimbrin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fimbrin "Fimbrin"), and [filamin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Filamin "Filamin") in submembranous [cortical layers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_cortex "Cell cortex") and bundles. [Motor proteins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motor_protein "Motor protein") of microtubules, [dynein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynein "Dynein") and [kinesin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinesin "Kinesin"), and [myosin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myosin "Myosin") of actin filaments, make the network dynamic.[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-41)[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Sweeney-42)

Many eukaryotes have long slender motile cytoplasmic projections, called [flagella](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flagellum "Flagellum"), or multiple shorter structures called [cilia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cilium "Cilium"). [These organelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Undulipodium "Undulipodium") are variously involved in movement, feeding, and sensation. They are composed mainly of [tubulin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tubulin "Tubulin"), and are entirely distinct from prokaryotic flagella. They are supported by a bundle of [microtubules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microtubule "Microtubule") arising from a [centriole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centriole "Centriole"), characteristically arranged as nine doublets surrounding two singlets. Flagella may have hairs ([mastigonemes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mastigoneme "Mastigoneme")), as in many [stramenopiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stramenopile "Stramenopile"). Their interior is continuous with the cell's [cytoplasm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytoplasm "Cytoplasm").[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Bardy_Ng_Jarrell_2003-43)[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Silflow_Lefebvre_2001-44)

Centrioles are often present, even in cells and groups that do not have flagella, but [conifers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conifer "Conifer") and [flowering plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flowering_plant "Flowering plant") have neither. They generally occur in groups that give rise to various microtubular roots. These form a primary component of the cytoskeleton, and are often assembled over the course of several cell divisions, with one flagellum retained from the parent and the other derived from it. Centrioles produce the spindle during nuclear division.[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-45)

The cells of plants, algae, fungi and most [chromalveolates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromalveolata "Chromalveolata"), but not animals, are surrounded by a cell wall. This is a layer outside the [cell membrane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_membrane "Cell membrane"), providing the cell with structural support, protection, and a filtering mechanism. The cell wall also prevents [over-expansion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytolysis "Cytolysis") when water enters the cell.[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Howland_2000-46)

The major [polysaccharides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polysaccharides "Polysaccharides") making up the primary cell wall of [land plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embryophyte "Embryophyte") are [cellulose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellulose "Cellulose"), [hemicellulose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemicellulose "Hemicellulose"), and [pectin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pectin "Pectin"). The cellulose [microfibrils](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microfibril "Microfibril") are linked together with hemicellulose, embedded in a pectin matrix. The most common hemicellulose in the primary cell wall is [xyloglucan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xyloglucan "Xyloglucan").[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Fry1989-47)

### Sexual reproduction

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eukaryote&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Sexual reproduction")]

[![Image 19](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Sexual_cycle_N-2N.svg/250px-Sexual_cycle_N-2N.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Sexual_cycle_N-2N.svg)

[Sexual reproduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sexual_reproduction "Sexual reproduction") requires a [life cycle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_life_cycle "Biological life cycle") that alternates between a [haploid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haploid "Haploid") phase, with one copy of each [chromosome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromosome "Chromosome") in the cell, and a [diploid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diploid "Diploid") phase, with two copies. In eukaryotes, haploid [gametes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamete "Gamete") are produced by [meiosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meiosis "Meiosis"); two gametes fuse to form a diploid [zygote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zygote "Zygote").

Eukaryotes have a life cycle that involves [sexual reproduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sexual_reproduction "Sexual reproduction"), alternating between a [haploid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haploid "Haploid") phase, where only one copy of each chromosome is present in each cell, and a [diploid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diploid "Diploid") phase, with two copies of each chromosome in each cell. The diploid phase is formed by fusion of two haploid gametes, such as [eggs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egg_cell "Egg cell") and [spermatozoa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spermatozoa "Spermatozoa"), to form a [zygote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zygote "Zygote"); this may grow into a body, with its cells dividing by [mitosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitosis "Mitosis"), and at some stage produce haploid gametes through [meiosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meiosis "Meiosis"), a division that reduces the number of chromosomes and creates [genetic variability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetic_variability "Genetic variability").[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-48) There is considerable variation in this pattern. Plants have both [haploid and diploid multicellular phases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternation_of_generations "Alternation of generations").[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-49) Eukaryotes have lower metabolic rates and longer generation times than prokaryotes, because they are larger and therefore have a smaller surface area to volume ratio.[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Lane2011-50)

The [evolution of sexual reproduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evolution_of_sexual_reproduction "Evolution of sexual reproduction") may be a primordial characteristic of eukaryotes. Based on a phylogenetic analysis, Dacks and [Roger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrew_J._Roger "Andrew J. Roger") have proposed that facultative sex was present in the group's common ancestor.[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-51) A core set of genes that function in meiosis is present in both _[Trichomonas vaginalis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trichomonas\_vaginalis "Trichomonas vaginalis")_ and _[Giardia intestinalis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giardia\_intestinalis "Giardia intestinalis")_, two organisms previously thought to be asexual.[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Ramesh-52)[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Malik-53) Since these two species are descendants of lineages that diverged early from the eukaryotic evolutionary tree, core meiotic genes, and hence sex, were likely present in the common ancestor of eukaryotes.[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Ramesh-52)[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Malik-53) Species once thought to be asexual, such as _[Leishmania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leishmania "Leishmania")_ parasites, have a sexual cycle.[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-54) Amoebae, previously regarded as asexual, may be anciently sexual; while present-day asexual groups could have arisen recently.[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-55)

### History of classification

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eukaryote&action=edit&section=13 "Edit section: History of classification")]

In [antiquity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_history "Ancient history"), the two lineages of [animals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal "Animal") and [plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plant "Plant") were recognized by [Aristotle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aristotle "Aristotle") and [Theophrastus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theophrastus "Theophrastus"). The lineages were given the [taxonomic rank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taxonomic_rank "Taxonomic rank") of kingdom by [Linnaeus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus "Carl Linnaeus") in the 18th century. Though he included the [fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungi "Fungi") with plants with some reservations, it was later realized that they are quite distinct and warrant a separate kingdom.[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Moore1980-56) The various single-cell eukaryotes were originally placed with plants or animals when they became known. In 1818, the German biologist [Georg A. Goldfuss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georg_A._Goldfuss "Georg A. Goldfuss") coined the word _[Protozoa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protozoa "Protozoa")_ to refer to organisms such as [ciliates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ciliate "Ciliate"),[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-57) and this group was expanded until [Ernst Haeckel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernst_Haeckel "Ernst Haeckel") made it a kingdom encompassing all single-celled eukaryotes, the [Protista](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protista "Protista"), in 1866.[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Scamardella1999-58)[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Rothschild1989-59)[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Whittaker-60) The eukaryotes thus came to be seen as four kingdoms:

*   Kingdom [Protista](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protista "Protista")
*   Kingdom [Plantae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plantae "Plantae")
*   Kingdom [Fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungi "Fungi")
*   Kingdom [Animalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animalia "Animalia")

The protists were at that time thought to be "primitive forms", and thus an [evolutionary grade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evolutionary_grade "Evolutionary grade"), united by their primitive unicellular nature.[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Rothschild1989-59) Understanding of the oldest branchings in the [tree of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tree_of_life_(biology) "Tree of life (biology)") only developed substantially with [DNA sequencing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleic_acid_sequence "Nucleic acid sequence"), leading to a system of [domains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domain_(biology) "Domain (biology)") rather than kingdoms as top level rank being put forward by [Carl Woese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Woese "Carl Woese"), [Otto Kandler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otto_Kandler "Otto Kandler"), and [Mark Wheelis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Wheelis "Mark Wheelis") in 1990, uniting all the eukaryote kingdoms in the domain "Eucarya", stating, however, that "'eukaryotes' will continue to be an acceptable common synonym".[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-w1990-1)[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Knoll_1992-61) In 1996, the evolutionary biologist [Lynn Margulis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lynn_Margulis "Lynn Margulis") proposed to replace kingdoms and domains with "inclusive" names to create a "symbiosis-based phylogeny", giving the description "Eukarya (symbiosis-derived nucleated organisms)".[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Margulis_1996-2)

[![Image 20](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Openly_available_illustrations_as_tools_to_describe_eukaryotic_microbial_diversity_-_Journal.pbio.3002395.g001.tif/lossy-page1-340px-Openly_available_illustrations_as_tools_to_describe_eukaryotic_microbial_diversity_-_Journal.pbio.3002395.g001.tif.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Openly_available_illustrations_as_tools_to_describe_eukaryotic_microbial_diversity_-_Journal.pbio.3002395.g001.tif)

Tree of eukaryotes showing major subgroups and thumbnail diagrams of representative members of each group, based on 2023 phylogenomic reconstructions.[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-62)

By 2014, a rough consensus started to emerge from the phylogenomic studies of the previous two decades.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-burki2014-11)[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-UntanglingEarlyEukaryotes-63) The majority of eukaryotes can be placed in one of two large clades dubbed [Amorphea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amorphea "Amorphea") (similar in composition to the [unikont](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unikont "Unikont") hypothesis) and the [Diphoda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diphoda "Diphoda") (formerly bikonts), which includes plants and most algal lineages. A third major grouping, the [Excavata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Excavata "Excavata"), has been abandoned as a formal group as it was found to be [paraphyletic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paraphyletic "Paraphyletic").[[64]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-AdlBass2019-64) The proposed phylogeny below includes two groups of excavates ([Discoba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discoba "Discoba") and [Metamonada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metamonad "Metamonad")),[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Brown_2018-65) and incorporates the 2021 proposal that [picozoans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Picozoa "Picozoa") are close relatives of rhodophytes.[[66]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Sch%C3%B6nZlatogursky2021-66) The [Provora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provora "Provora") are a group of microbial predators discovered in 2022.[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Provora-67)[TSAR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SAR_supergroup#Internal_phylogeny "SAR supergroup") is a possible clade that would contain [Telonemia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telonemia "Telonemia") and the [SAR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SAR_supergroup "SAR supergroup")[supergroup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supergroup_(biology) "Supergroup (biology)").[[68]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-68)[[69]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-69)[[70]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-70)

One view of the great kingdoms and their stem groups.[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Brown_2018-65)[[71]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Picozoa_2021-71)[[72]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Provora_2022-72)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Burki_Roger_Brown_Simpson_2020_pp._43%E2%80%9355-15) The [Metamonada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metamonada "Metamonada") are hard to place, being sister possibly to [Discoba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discoba "Discoba") or to [Malawimonada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malawimonada "Malawimonada")[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Burki_Roger_Brown_Simpson_2020_pp._43%E2%80%9355-15) or being a paraphyletic group external to [all other eukaryotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metakaryota "Metakaryota").[[73]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Al_Jewari_Baldauf_20232-73)

### Origin of eukaryotes

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eukaryote&action=edit&section=15 "Edit section: Origin of eukaryotes")]

[![Image 21](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Symbiogenesis_2_mergers.svg/500px-Symbiogenesis_2_mergers.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Symbiogenesis_2_mergers.svg)

In the theory of [symbiogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbiogenesis "Symbiogenesis"), a merger of an [archaean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaea "Archaea") and an aerobic bacterium created the eukaryotes, with aerobic [mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrion "Mitochondrion"); a second merger added [chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast "Chloroplast"), creating the [green plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viridiplantae "Viridiplantae").[[74]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-latorre-74)

The origin of the eukaryotic cell, or _eukaryogenesis_, is a milestone in the evolution of life, since eukaryotes include all complex cells and almost all multicellular organisms. The [last eukaryotic common ancestor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Last_eukaryotic_common_ancestor "Last eukaryotic common ancestor") (LECA) is the hypothetical origin of all living eukaryotes,[[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Gabald%C3%B3n-75) and was most likely a [biological population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population "Population"), not a single individual.[[76]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-O'Malley_Leger_Wideman_Ruiz-Trillo_pp._338%E2%80%93344-76) The LECA is believed to have been a protist with a nucleus, at least one [centriole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centriole "Centriole") and [flagellum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flagellum "Flagellum"), facultatively aerobic mitochondria, sex ([meiosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meiosis "Meiosis") and [syngamy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syngamy "Syngamy")), a dormant [cyst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microbial_cyst "Microbial cyst") with a cell wall of [chitin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chitin "Chitin") or [cellulose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellulose "Cellulose"), and [peroxisomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peroxisome "Peroxisome").[[77]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-77)[[78]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-RedAlgalDerivedPlastids-78)[[79]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Koumandou_Wickstead_Ginger_van_der_Giezen_2013-79)

An [endosymbiotic union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbiogenesis "Symbiogenesis") between a motile [anaerobic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anaerobic_organism "Anaerobic organism") archaean and an aerobic [alphaproteobacterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphaproteobacteria "Alphaproteobacteria") gave rise to the LECA and all eukaryotes with [mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrion "Mitochondrion"). A second, much later endosymbiosis with a cyanobacterium gave rise to the ancestor of plants, with [chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast "Chloroplast").[[74]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-latorre-74)

The presence of eukaryotic biomarkers in archaea points towards an archaeal origin. The genomes of Promethearchaeati archaea have plenty of [eukaryotic signature protein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryotic_signature_protein "Eukaryotic signature protein") genes, which play a crucial role in the development of the [cytoskeleton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytoskeleton "Cytoskeleton") and complex cellular structures characteristic of eukaryotes. In 2022, [cryo-electron tomography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryo_Electron_Tomography "Cryo Electron Tomography") demonstrated that Promethearchaeati archaea have a complex [actin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actin "Actin")-based cytoskeleton, providing the first direct visual evidence of the archaeal ancestry of eukaryotes.[[80]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Rodrigues-Oliveira_et_al_2023-80)

The timing of the origin of eukaryotes is hard to determine, but the discovery of _Qingshania magnificia_, the earliest multicellular eukaryote from North China which lived 1.635 billion years ago, suggests that the crown group eukaryotes originated from the late [Paleoproterozoic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleoproterozoic "Paleoproterozoic") ([Statherian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statherian "Statherian")). The earliest unequivocal unicellular eukaryotes, _[Tappania plana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tappania "Tappania")_, _[Shuiyousphaeridium macroreticulatum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shuiyousphaeridium "Shuiyousphaeridium")_, _Dictyosphaera macroreticulata_, _Germinosphaera alveolata_, and _Valeria lophostriata_ from North China, lived approximately 1.65 billion years ago.[[81]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-81)

Some [acritarchs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acritarch "Acritarch") are known from at least 1.65 billion years ago, and a fossil, _[Grypania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grypania "Grypania")_, which may be an alga, is as much as 2.1 billion years old.[[82]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Han-82)[[83]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-83) The ["problematic"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incertae_sedis "Incertae sedis")[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Retallack_2013-84) fossil _[Diskagma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diskagma "Diskagma")_ has been found in [paleosols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleosol "Paleosol") 2.2 billion years old.[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Retallack_2013-84)

[![Image 22](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Diskagma_butonii.jpg/250px-Diskagma_butonii.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Diskagma_butonii.jpg)

Reconstruction of the problematic[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Retallack_2013-84)_[Diskagma buttonii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diskagma\_buttonii "Diskagma buttonii")_, a terrestrial fossil less than 1mm high, from rocks around 2.2 billion years old

The [Neoarchean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neoarchean "Neoarchean") fossil _[Thuchomyces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thuchomyces "Thuchomyces")_ shares similarities with eukaryotes, specifically fungi. It especially resembles the problematic fossil _Diskagma_,[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Retallack_2013-84) with hyphae and multiple differentiated layers.[[85]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-85) However, it is over 600 million years older than all other possible eukaryotes, and many of its "eukaryote features" are not specific to the clade, meaning it is almost certainly a microbial mat instead.[[86]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-86)

Structures proposed to represent "large colonial organisms" have been found in the [black shales](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_shale "Black shale") of the [Palaeoproterozoic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palaeoproterozoic "Palaeoproterozoic") such as the [Francevillian B Formation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francevillian_B_Formation "Francevillian B Formation"), in [Gabon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabon "Gabon"), dubbed the "[Francevillian biota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francevillian_biota "Francevillian biota")" which is dated at 2.1 billion years old.[[87]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-El_Albani_Bengtson_Canfield_2010-87)[[88]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-El_Albani_2023-88) However, the status of these structures as fossils is contested, with other authors suggesting that they might represent [pseudofossils](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudofossil "Pseudofossil").[[89]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-Ossa_Ossa_Pons_Bekker_Hofmann_2023-89) The oldest fossils that can unambiguously be assigned to eukaryotes are from the Ruyang Group of China, dating to approximately 1.8-1.6 billion years ago.[[90]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-90) Fossils that are clearly related to modern groups start appearing an estimated 1.2 billion years ago, in the form of [red algae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_algae#Fossil_record "Red algae"), though recent work suggests the existence of fossilized [filamentous algae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Filamentous_algae "Filamentous algae") in the [Vindhya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vindhya "Vindhya") basin dating back perhaps to 1.6 to 1.7 billion years ago.[[91]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-91)

The presence of [steranes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sterane "Sterane"), eukaryotic-specific [biomarkers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomarker_(petroleum) "Biomarker (petroleum)"), in [Australian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australia "Australia")[shales](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shale "Shale") previously indicated that eukaryotes were present in these rocks dated at 2.7 billion years old,[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-sterane-21)[[92]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-92) but these Archaean biomarkers have been rebutted as later contaminants.[[93]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-93) The oldest valid biomarker records are only around 800 million years old.[[94]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-94) In contrast, a molecular clock analysis suggests the emergence of sterol biosynthesis as early as 2.3 billion years ago.[[95]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-95) The nature of steranes as eukaryotic biomarkers is further complicated by the production of [sterols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sterol "Sterol") by some bacteria.[[96]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-96)[[97]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-97)

Whenever their origins, eukaryotes may not have become ecologically dominant until much later; a massive increase in the [zinc composition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abiogenesis#Zinc_world "Abiogenesis") of marine sediments [800](https://geoltime.github.io/?Ma=800)million years ago has been attributed to the rise of substantial populations of eukaryotes, which preferentially consume and incorporate [zinc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zinc "Zinc") relative to prokaryotes, approximately a billion years after their origin (at the latest).[[98]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote#cite_note-pmid29869832-98)
