Chemical compound

**Cyclopropenone** is an [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with [molecular formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_formula "Molecular formula")C 3 H 2 O consisting of a [cyclopropene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropene "Cyclopropene") carbon framework with a [ketone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ketone "Ketone")[functional group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_group "Functional group"). It is a colorless, volatile liquid that boils near room temperature.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenone#cite_note-1) Neat cyclopropenone polymerizes upon standing at room temperature,[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenone#cite_note-2) and chemical vendors typically supply it as an [acetal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetal "Acetal").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenone#cite_note-3) The chemical properties of the compound are dominated by the strong [polarization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dielectric "Dielectric") of the [carbonyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbonyl "Carbonyl") group, which gives a partial positive charge with [aromatic stabilization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aromaticity "Aromaticity") on the ring and a partial negative charge on oxygen. It is an aromatic compound.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenone#cite_note-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclopropenone#cite_note-5)
