In [anatomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatomy "Anatomy"), a **spatium** or **anatomic space** is a space (cavity or gap). Anatomic spaces are often landmarks to find other important structures. When they fill with gases (such as air) or liquids (such as blood) in pathological ways, they can suffer conditions such as [pneumothorax](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pneumothorax "Pneumothorax"), [edema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edema "Edema"), or [pericardial effusion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pericardial_effusion "Pericardial effusion"). Many anatomic spaces are [potential spaces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potential_space "Potential space"), which means that they are potential rather than realized (with their realization being dynamic according to [physiologic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physiology "Physiology") or [pathophysiologic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pathophysiology "Pathophysiology") events). In other words, they are like an empty plastic bag that has not been opened (two walls collapsed against each other; no interior [volume](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volume "Volume") until opened) or a balloon that has not been inflated.
