**Multi-index notation** is a [mathematical notation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_notation "Mathematical notation") that simplifies formulas used in [multivariable calculus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multivariable_calculus "Multivariable calculus"), [partial differential equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partial_differential_equation "Partial differential equation") and the theory of [distributions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distribution_(mathematics) "Distribution (mathematics)"), by generalising the concept of an integer [index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_notation "Index notation") to an ordered [tuple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuple "Tuple") of indices.

The proof follows from the [power rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_rule "Power rule") for the [ordinary derivative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_calculus "Differential calculus"); if _α_ and _β_ are in ![Image 41: {\textstyle \{0,1,2,\ldots \}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/d340630e2d63e1f9bc0056e7195d981f35729ed9), then

Suppose ![Image 43: {\displaystyle \alpha =(\alpha _{1},\ldots ,\alpha _{n})}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/a94e85af78356a1f61786ae37197b9db2a163b85), ![Image 44: {\displaystyle \beta =(\beta _{1},\ldots ,\beta _{n})}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/702931598aacda47b9ae005779234164483e05cb), and ![Image 45: {\displaystyle x=(x_{1},\ldots ,x_{n})}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/e9cca790655fd95015b4a019b694f390c55a0032). Then we have that ![Image 46: {\displaystyle {\begin{aligned}\partial ^{\alpha }x^{\beta }&={\frac {\partial ^{\vert \alpha \vert }}{\partial x_{1}^{\alpha _{1}}\cdots \partial x_{n}^{\alpha _{n}}}}x_{1}^{\beta _{1}}\cdots x_{n}^{\beta _{n}}\\&={\frac {\partial ^{\alpha _{1}}}{\partial x_{1}^{\alpha _{1}}}}x_{1}^{\beta _{1}}\cdots {\frac {\partial ^{\alpha _{n}}}{\partial x_{n}^{\alpha _{n}}}}x_{n}^{\beta _{n}}.\end{aligned}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/3c5a4a78a610adf197ab62f8f42c3a9924a92b2e)

For each ![Image 47: {\textstyle i}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5b0f327332497b21a059c479e7b2ce098baa1a7e) in ![Image 48: {\textstyle \{1,\ldots ,n\}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c56fca6d0a1a9443ffb3f3c051aa8dd8c7cbf0af), the function ![Image 49: {\displaystyle x_{i}^{\beta _{i}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4b2f48e0abed7bfcbc5a87aac7382c290f824a46) only depends on ![Image 50: {\displaystyle x_{i}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/e87000dd6142b81d041896a30fe58f0c3acb2158). In the above, each partial differentiation ![Image 51: {\displaystyle \partial /\partial x_{i}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/2b838ea755d9b444bb40d1cc461c8ae97a02488f) therefore reduces to the corresponding ordinary differentiation ![Image 52: {\displaystyle d/dx_{i}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8e475dea6ceab4ddd7d602d1e5d428fc188d2b85). Hence, from equation (**[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-index_notation#math_1)**), it follows that ![Image 53: {\displaystyle \partial ^{\alpha }x^{\beta }}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/12ed2a2f32f14c4621cdeb7412eaafff41464ef3) vanishes if ![Image 54: {\textstyle \alpha _{i}>\beta _{i}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/e3eb86272b58b5bb91e49ed413de791d789db353) for at least one ![Image 55: {\textstyle i}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/5b0f327332497b21a059c479e7b2ce098baa1a7e) in ![Image 56: {\textstyle \{1,\ldots ,n\}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c56fca6d0a1a9443ffb3f3c051aa8dd8c7cbf0af). If this is not the case, i.e., if ![Image 57: {\textstyle \alpha \leq \beta }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/27564029d3fbe571dda8b98933f4803f32de4338) as multi-indices, then ![Image 58: {\displaystyle {\frac {d^{\alpha _{i}}}{dx_{i}^{\alpha _{i}}}}x_{i}^{\beta _{i}}={\frac {\beta _{i}!}{(\beta _{i}-\alpha _{i})!}}x_{i}^{\beta _{i}-\alpha _{i}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/71dc5027a5bbd718f053ca6514950f6e600c6087) for each ![Image 59: {\displaystyle i}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/add78d8608ad86e54951b8c8bd6c8d8416533d20) and the theorem follows.
