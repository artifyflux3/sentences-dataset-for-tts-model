In [abstract algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abstract_algebra "Abstract algebra"), the **sedenions** form a 16-[dimensional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimension_of_a_vector_space "Dimension of a vector space")[noncommutative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commutative_property "Commutative property") and [nonassociative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Associative_property "Associative property")[algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algebra_over_a_field "Algebra over a field") over the [real numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_number "Real number"), usually represented by the capital letter S, boldface **S** or [blackboard bold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackboard_bold "Blackboard bold")![Image 2: {\displaystyle \mathbb {S} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9f9d5874c5d7f68eba1cec9da9ccbe53903303bb).

The sedenions are obtained by applying the [Cayley–Dickson construction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cayley%E2%80%93Dickson_construction "Cayley–Dickson construction") to the [octonions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octonion "Octonion"), which can be mathematically expressed as ![Image 3: {\displaystyle \mathbb {S} ={\mathcal {CD}}(\mathbb {O} ,1)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6468ad565a6b5b5236a884df9e5379a03aca8ca3).[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sedenion#cite_note-Ensembles-1) As such, the octonions are [isomorphic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomorphism "Isomorphism") to a [subalgebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subalgebra#Subalgebras_for_algebras_over_a_ring_or_field "Subalgebra") of the sedenions. Unlike the octonions, the sedenions are not an [alternative algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternative_algebra "Alternative algebra"). Applying the Cayley–Dickson construction to the sedenions yields a 32-dimensional algebra, called the [trigintaduonions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trigintaduonion "Trigintaduonion") or sometimes the 32-nions.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sedenion#cite_note-2)

The term _sedenion_ is also used for other 16-dimensional algebraic structures, such as a tensor product of two copies of the [biquaternions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biquaternion "Biquaternion"), or the algebra of 4 × 4 [matrices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matrix_(mathematics) "Matrix (mathematics)") over the real numbers, or that studied by [Smith (1995)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sedenion#CITEREFSmith1995).

A visualization of a 4D extension to the cubic [octonion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octonion#Fano_plane_mnemonic "Octonion"),[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sedenion#cite_note-3) showing the 35 triads as [hyperplanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperplane "Hyperplane") through the real ![Image 5: {\displaystyle (e_{0})}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4d37e78a54299afe36763797b6ebcc23db95f907) vertex of the sedenion example given

Every sedenion is a [linear combination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_combination "Linear combination") of the unit sedenions ![Image 6: {\displaystyle e_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/51703931b2ce836f3a377076756262596e4348e3), ![Image 7: {\displaystyle e_{1}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6e81caf3d4bcb929315801cbabc83543829484ee), ![Image 8: {\displaystyle e_{2}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4045b5c7cee9bd0681153bbb077489b13269355e), ![Image 9: {\displaystyle e_{3}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1686693c185b6df4a5f57f34f7b61c4a0e057ad9), ..., ![Image 10: {\displaystyle e_{15}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/49f87afc339369e782535a8e83c83030a90d81a9), which form a [basis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basis_(linear_algebra) "Basis (linear algebra)") of the [vector space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_space "Vector space") of sedenions. Every sedenion can be represented in the form

![Image 11: {\displaystyle x=x_{0}e_{0}+x_{1}e_{1}+x_{2}e_{2}+\cdots +x_{14}e_{14}+x_{15}e_{15}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1706297c73e5b49816bfb964821338bda3fe62d2)
Addition and subtraction are defined by the addition and subtraction of corresponding coefficients and multiplication is [distributive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributive_property "Distributive property") over addition.

Like other algebras based on the [Cayley–Dickson construction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cayley%E2%80%93Dickson_construction "Cayley–Dickson construction"), the sedenions contain the algebra they were constructed from. So they contain the octonions (generated by ![Image 12: {\displaystyle e_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/51703931b2ce836f3a377076756262596e4348e3) to ![Image 13: {\displaystyle e_{7}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/23f1a9ce80c1a8bd4a9813628bb793c6757cbf46) in the table below), and therefore also the [quaternions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quaternion "Quaternion") (generated by ![Image 14: {\displaystyle e_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/51703931b2ce836f3a377076756262596e4348e3) to ![Image 15: {\displaystyle e_{3}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1686693c185b6df4a5f57f34f7b61c4a0e057ad9)), [complex numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Complex_number "Complex number") (generated by ![Image 16: {\displaystyle e_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/51703931b2ce836f3a377076756262596e4348e3) and ![Image 17: {\displaystyle e_{1}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6e81caf3d4bcb929315801cbabc83543829484ee)) and real numbers (generated by ![Image 18: {\displaystyle e_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/51703931b2ce836f3a377076756262596e4348e3)).

Like [octonions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octonion "Octonion"), [multiplication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiplication "Multiplication") of sedenions is neither [commutative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commutative "Commutative") nor [associative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Associative "Associative"). However, in contrast to the octonions, the sedenions do not even have the property of being [alternative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternative_algebra "Alternative algebra"). They do, however, have the property of [power associativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_associativity "Power associativity"), which can be stated as that, for any element ![Image 19: {\displaystyle x}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/87f9e315fd7e2ba406057a97300593c4802b53e4) of ![Image 20: {\displaystyle \mathbb {S} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9f9d5874c5d7f68eba1cec9da9ccbe53903303bb), the power ![Image 21: {\displaystyle x^{n}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/150d38e238991bc4d0689ffc9d2a852547d2658d) is well defined. They are also [flexible](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flexible_algebra "Flexible algebra").

The sedenions have a multiplicative [identity element](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Identity_element "Identity element")![Image 22: {\displaystyle e_{0}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/51703931b2ce836f3a377076756262596e4348e3) and multiplicative inverses, but they are not a [division algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Division_algebra "Division algebra") because they have [zero divisors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zero_divisors "Zero divisors"): two nonzero sedenions can be multiplied to obtain zero, for example ![Image 23: {\displaystyle (e_{3}+e_{10})(e_{6}-e_{15})}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/dd67f791107477ac968fcdb05edfbc44b524105b). All [hypercomplex number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypercomplex_number "Hypercomplex number") systems after sedenions that are based on the Cayley–Dickson construction also contain zero divisors.

### Sedenion properties

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sedenion&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Sedenion properties")]

[![Image 315](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/PG%283%2C2%29_g005.png/250px-PG%283%2C2%29_g005.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:PG(3,2)_g005.png)

An illustration of the structure of [PG(3,2)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PG(3,2) "PG(3,2)") that provides the multiplication law for sedenions, as shown by [Saniga, Holweck & Pracna (2015)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sedenion#CITEREFSanigaHolweckPracna2015). Any three points (representing three sedenion imaginary units) lying on the same line are such that the product of two of them yields the third one, sign disregarded.

From the above table, we can see that:

![Image 316: {\displaystyle e_{0}e_{i}=e_{i}e_{0}=e_{i}\,{\text{for all}}\,i,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/da260d079a05dd42d7cae20cee875b31d9cd98ab)![Image 317: {\displaystyle e_{i}e_{i}=-e_{0}\,\,{\text{for}}\,\,i\neq 0,}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4438a09498ac4bf199045fb5b3a7c85452ab7208) and![Image 318: {\displaystyle e_{i}e_{j}=-e_{j}e_{i}\,\,{\text{for}}\,\,i\neq j\,\,{\text{with}}\,\,i,j\neq 0.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/de023fa5ea9105391c3e356e698f300de0ab1bf7)
The sedenions are not fully anti-associative. Choose any four generators, ![Image 319: {\displaystyle i,j,k}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9550e66fe7e601c4f58bbc9c19ba226301149cde) and ![Image 320: {\displaystyle l}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/829091f745070b9eb97a80244129025440a1cfac). The following 5-cycle shows that these five relations cannot all be anti-associative.

![Image 321: {\displaystyle (ij)(kl)=-((ij)k)l=(i(jk))l=-i((jk)l)=i(j(kl))=-(ij)(kl)=0}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9ae8ae5fb4a99ee2193d674008797ed3854a87cb)

[Moreno (1998)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sedenion#CITEREFMoreno1998) showed that the space of pairs of norm-one sedenions that multiply to zero is [homeomorphic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homeomorphism "Homeomorphism") to the compact form of the exceptional [Lie group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lie_group "Lie group")[G 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G2_(mathematics) "G2 (mathematics)"). (Note that in his paper, a "zero divisor" means a _pair_ of elements that multiply to zero.)

[Guillard & Gresnigt (2019)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sedenion#CITEREFGuillardGresnigt2019) demonstrated that the three generations of [leptons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lepton "Lepton") and [quarks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quark "Quark") that are associated with unbroken [gauge symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauge_symmetry "Gauge symmetry")![Image 328: {\displaystyle \mathrm {SU(3)_{c}\times U(1)_{em}} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4b9306c8262204ee229ba73882a8fc0ab0856685) can be represented using the algebra of the complexified sedenions ![Image 329: {\displaystyle \mathbb {C\otimes S} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/25b87e25677a13c842aaf4c4b836f4a257434992). Their reasoning follows that a primitive [idempotent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idempotent "Idempotent")[projector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Projection_(linear_algebra) "Projection (linear algebra)")![Image 330: {\displaystyle \rho _{+}=1/2(1+ie_{15})}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/86fd574d8d84e15d0d17a876e05a0d7924ea4884) — where ![Image 331: {\displaystyle e_{15}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/49f87afc339369e782535a8e83c83030a90d81a9) is chosen as an [imaginary unit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imaginary_unit "Imaginary unit") akin to ![Image 332: {\displaystyle e_{7}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/23f1a9ce80c1a8bd4a9813628bb793c6757cbf46) for ![Image 333: {\displaystyle \mathbb {O} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c1ed2664a4fe515e6fbed25a7193ce663b82920c) in the [Fano plane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fano_plane "Fano plane") — that [acts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_action "Group action") on the [standard basis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_basis "Standard basis") of the sedenions uniquely divides the algebra into three sets of [split basis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split-complex_number "Split-complex number") elements for ![Image 334: {\displaystyle \mathbb {C\otimes O} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c29dc40369330bc529dc69ba90364d896a6d86d4), whose adjoint [left actions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_action#Left_group_action "Group action")_on themselves_ generate three copies of the [Clifford algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clifford_algebra "Clifford algebra")![Image 335: {\displaystyle \mathrm {C} l(6)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/d1dbdf7475d2c7aaee52e74c968296c2e5a88474) which in-turn contain [minimal left ideals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ideal_(ring_theory)#Types_of_ideals "Ideal (ring theory)") that describe a single generation of [fermions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermion "Fermion") with unbroken ![Image 336: {\displaystyle \mathrm {SU(3)_{c}\times U(1)_{em}} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/4b9306c8262204ee229ba73882a8fc0ab0856685) gauge symmetry. In particular, they note that [tensor products](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor_product "Tensor product") between normed division algebras generate zero divisors akin to those inside ![Image 337: {\displaystyle \mathbb {S} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/9f9d5874c5d7f68eba1cec9da9ccbe53903303bb), where for ![Image 338: {\displaystyle \mathbb {C\otimes O} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c29dc40369330bc529dc69ba90364d896a6d86d4) the lack of alternativity and associativity does not affect the construction of minimal left ideals since their underlying split basis requires only two basis elements to be multiplied together, in-which associativity or alternativity are uninvolved. Still, these ideals constructed from an adjoint algebra of left actions of the algebra on itself remain associative, alternative, and [isomorphic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomorphic "Isomorphic") to a Clifford algebra. Altogether, this permits three copies of ![Image 339: {\displaystyle (\mathbb {C\otimes O} )_{L}\cong \mathrm {Cl(6)} }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/17e25dc44e5152ed07b3357601e470a93d7b6057) to exist inside ![Image 340: {\displaystyle \mathbb {(C\otimes S)} _{L}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ccffb220c5b545fd6665e5b3692307cd80077ced). Furthermore, these three complexified octonion subalgebras are not independent; they share a common ![Image 341: {\displaystyle \mathrm {C} l(2)}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/bcffe33d9e75d46816c63dd1460720766f1ec813) subalgebra, which the authors note could form a theoretical basis for [CKM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabibbo%E2%80%93Kobayashi%E2%80%93Maskawa_matrix "Cabibbo–Kobayashi–Maskawa matrix") and [PMNS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontecorvo%E2%80%93Maki%E2%80%93Nakagawa%E2%80%93Sakata_matrix "Pontecorvo–Maki–Nakagawa–Sakata matrix") matrices that, respectively, describe [quark mixing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quark_mixing "Quark mixing") and [neutrino oscillations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neutrino_oscillation "Neutrino oscillation").

Sedenion neural networks provide[_[further explanation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Please\_clarify "Wikipedia:Please clarify")_] a means of efficient and compact expression in machine learning applications and have been used in solving multiple time-series and traffic forecasting problems.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sedenion#cite_note-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sedenion#cite_note-5)
