**Typographical symbols and punctuation marks** are marks and symbols used in [typography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typography "Typography") with a variety of purposes such as to help with legibility and accessibility, or to identify special cases. This list gives those most commonly encountered with [Latin script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_script "Latin script"). For a far more comprehensive list of symbols and signs, see [List of Unicode characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Unicode_characters "List of Unicode characters"). For other languages and symbol sets (especially in mathematics and science), see [below](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_typographical_symbols_and_punctuation_marks#See_also).

In this table,

*   The first cell in each row gives a symbol;
*   The second is a link to the article that details that symbol, using its Unicode standard name or common alias. (Holding the mouse pointer on the hyperlink will pop up a summary of the symbol's function.);
*   The third gives symbols listed elsewhere in the table that are similar to it in meaning or appearance, or that may be confused with it;
*   The fourth (if present) links to the related article(s) or adds a clarification note.
