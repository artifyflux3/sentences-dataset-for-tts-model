Floating kelp ( Macrocystis pyrifera ) with many pneumatocysts In phycology , a pneumatocyst is a floating structure that contains gas found on brown seaweed . A seaweed's thallus may have more than one. They provide buoyancy to lift the blades toward the surface, allowing them to receive more sunlight for photosynthesis .

The proportion of gases in the pneumatocysts varies depending on the physiological status of the alga and the partial pressure of gases in the surrounding air or water.

[ 1 ] The pneumatocyst can hold O 2 , CO 2 , N 2 , [ 1 ] and CO .
