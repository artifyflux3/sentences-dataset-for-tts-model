Albin Haller Albin Haller (7 March 1849, Fellering – 1 May 1925) was a French chemist .

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] Haller founded the École Nationale Supérieure des Industries Chimiques in Nancy and in 1917 won the Davy Medal of the Royal Society " On the ground of his important researches in the domain of organic chemistry ".

[ 3 ] Appointed to the French Academy of Sciences in 1900, he served as its president beginning in 1923.  He was also a member of the French Académie Nationale de Médecine .

[ 4 ] Haller was married to a cousin of Henri Poincaré . Haller and Poincaré were close friends.
