**Applied physics** is the application of [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics") to solve scientific or engineering problems. It is usually considered a bridge or a connection between physics and [engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Engineering "Engineering"). "Applied" is distinguished from "pure" by a subtle combination of factors, such as the motivation and attitude of researchers and the nature of the relationship to the [technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology "Technology") or [science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science "Science") that may be affected by the work. Applied physics is rooted in the fundamental truths and basic concepts of the [physical sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_science "Physical science") but is concerned with the utilization of scientific principles in practical devices and systems and with the application of physics in other areas of science and [high technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High_technology "High technology").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_physics#cite_note-1)
