Chemical compound

**Sodium iodide** ([chemical formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula")**NaI**) is an [ionic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionic_compound "Ionic compound") formed from the [chemical reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_reaction "Chemical reaction") of [sodium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium "Sodium") metal and [iodine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iodine "Iodine"). Under standard conditions, it is a white, water-soluble [solid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solid "Solid") comprising a 1:1 mix of sodium [cations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cation "Cation") (Na+) and [iodide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iodide "Iodide")[anions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anion "Anion") (I−) in a [crystal lattice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystal_lattice "Crystal lattice"). It is used mainly as a nutritional supplement and in [organic chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_chemistry "Organic chemistry"). It is produced industrially as the [salt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salt_(chemistry) "Salt (chemistry)") formed when acidic iodides react with [sodium hydroxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_hydroxide "Sodium hydroxide").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_iodide#cite_note-Ullmann-11) It is a [chaotropic salt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chaotropic_agent "Chaotropic agent").

Uses
----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sodium_iodide&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Uses")]

### Food supplement

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sodium_iodide&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Food supplement")]

Sodium iodide, as well as [potassium iodide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potassium_iodide "Potassium iodide"), is commonly used to treat and prevent [iodine deficiency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iodine_deficiency "Iodine deficiency"). Iodized table salt contains 10[ppm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parts_per_million "Parts per million")[iodide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iodide "Iodide").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_iodide#cite_note-Ullmann-11)

### Organic synthesis

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sodium_iodide&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Organic synthesis")]

[![Image 43](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/NaI%40DWNT.png/250px-NaI%40DWNT.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:NaI@DWNT.png)

Monatomic NaI chains grown inside double-wall [carbon nanotubes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_nanotube "Carbon nanotube").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_iodide#cite_note-chains-12)

Sodium iodide is used for conversion of [alkyl chlorides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkyl_chloride "Alkyl chloride") into [alkyl iodides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkyl_iodide "Alkyl iodide"). This method, the [Finkelstein reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finkelstein_reaction "Finkelstein reaction"),[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_iodide#cite_note-13) relies on the insolubility of [sodium chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_chloride "Sodium chloride") in [acetone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetone "Acetone") to drive the reaction:[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_iodide#cite_note-14)

R–Cl + NaI → R–I + NaCl

### Nuclear medicine

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sodium_iodide&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Nuclear medicine")]

Some radioactive iodide salts of sodium, including Na[125 I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iodine-125 "Iodine-125") and Na[131 I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iodine-131 "Iodine-131"), have [radiopharmaceutical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiopharmaceutical "Radiopharmaceutical") uses for [thyroid cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thyroid_cancer "Thyroid cancer") and [hyperthyroidism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperthyroidism "Hyperthyroidism") or as [radioactive tracer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radioactive_tracer "Radioactive tracer") in imaging (see [Isotopes of iodine > Radioiodines I-123, I-124, I-125, and I-131 in medicine and biology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isotopes_of_iodine#Radioiodines_123I,_124I,_125I,_and_131I_in_medicine_and_biology "Isotopes of iodine")).

### Thallium-doped NaI(Tl) scintillators

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sodium_iodide&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Thallium-doped NaI(Tl) scintillators")]

Sodium iodide [activated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Activator_(phosphor) "Activator (phosphor)") with [thallium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thallium "Thallium"), NaI(Tl), when subjected to [ionizing radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionizing_radiation "Ionizing radiation"), emits [photons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photon "Photon") (i.e., [scintillate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scintillation_(physics) "Scintillation (physics)")) and is used in [scintillation detectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scintillation_detector "Scintillation detector"), traditionally in [nuclear medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_medicine "Nuclear medicine"), [geophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geophysics "Geophysics"), [nuclear physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_physics "Nuclear physics"), and environmental measurements. NaI(Tl) is the most widely used scintillation material. The crystals are usually coupled with a [photomultiplier tube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photomultiplier_tube "Photomultiplier tube"), in a [hermetically sealed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermetically_sealed "Hermetically sealed") assembly, as sodium iodide is [hygroscopic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hygroscopic "Hygroscopic"). Fine-tuning of some parameters (i.e., [radiation hardness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiation_hardness "Radiation hardness"), [afterglow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamma-ray_burst#Afterglow "Gamma-ray burst"), [transparency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transparency_and_translucency "Transparency and translucency")) can be achieved by varying the conditions of the [crystal growth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystal_growth "Crystal growth"). Crystals with a higher level of [doping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dopant "Dopant") are used in [X-ray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-ray "X-ray") detectors with high spectrometric quality. Sodium iodide can be used both as [single crystals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Single_crystal "Single crystal") and as [polycrystals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycrystals "Polycrystals") for this purpose. The wavelength of maximum emission is 415 nm.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_iodide#cite_note-15)

### Radiocontrast

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sodium_iodide&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Radiocontrast")]

[António Egas Moniz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_Egas_Moniz "António Egas Moniz") searched for a [radiocontrast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiocontrast "Radiocontrast") agent for [cerebral angiography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cerebral_angiography "Cerebral angiography").[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_iodide#cite_note-jama-16) After experiments on [rabbits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit") and [dogs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dog "Dog") he settled upon sodium iodide as the best medium.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium_iodide#cite_note-jama-16)

Stability
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sodium_iodide&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Stability")]

Iodides (including sodium iodide) are detectably oxidized by atmospheric [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen") (O 2) to molecular [iodine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iodine "Iodine") (I 2). I 2 and I− complex to form the [triiodide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triiodide "Triiodide") complex, which has a yellow color, unlike the white color of sodium iodide. Water accelerates the oxidation process, and iodide can also produce I 2 by photooxidation, therefore for maximum stability sodium iodide should be stored under dark, low temperature, low humidity conditions.
