Star density maps of the Gaia Catalogue of Nearby Stars.The Sun is located at the centre of both maps. The regions with higher density of stars are shown; these correspond with known star clusters (Hyades and Coma Berenices) and moving groups.

This is a **list of nearby stellar associations and moving groups**. A [stellar association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_association "Stellar association") is a very loose [star cluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_cluster "Star cluster"), looser than an [open cluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_cluster "Open cluster"). A [moving group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_kinematics#Moving_groups "Stellar kinematics") is the remnant of such a stellar association.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_nearby_stellar_associations_and_moving_groups#cite_note-nao-cas-1) Members of stellar associations and moving groups share similar kinematic properties, as well as similar ages and chemical composition.

_The list (below) is sorted by the distance to the [Solar System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar\_System "Solar System")._
