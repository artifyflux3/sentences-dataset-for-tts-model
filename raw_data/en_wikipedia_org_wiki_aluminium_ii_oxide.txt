Chemical compound

**Aluminium(II) oxide** or **aluminium monoxide** is a compound of [aluminium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium "Aluminium") and [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen") with the [chemical formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula") AlO. It has been detected in the gas phase after explosion of aluminized grenades in the upper atmosphere[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium(II)_oxide#cite_note-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium(II)_oxide#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium(II)_oxide#cite_note-3) and in stellar absorption spectra.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium(II)_oxide#cite_note-4)

**Aluminium(II) oxide** is one of the [aluminium oxides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_oxides "Aluminium oxides") (the most common is [Aluminium oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium_oxide "Aluminium oxide") Al 2 O 3), as it was the rare example of aluminium(II) compound since aluminium usually exists in its +3 [oxidation state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxidation_state "Oxidation state").
