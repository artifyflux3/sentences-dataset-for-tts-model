Renaissance Allegorical interpretations of Plato Plato's unwritten doctrines Pseudo-Platonica Related categories Plato Philosophy portal v t e In Platonism , dianoia ( Greek : διάνοια ) is the human cognitive faculty associated with the BC portion of the analogy of the divided line and related to discursive thinking about mathematical and technical subjects.

[ 1 ] It stands in contrast to the immediate, cognitive process of intuitive apprehension or noesis .

[ 2 ] In Aristotle, dianoia is subdivided into theoretical knowledge ( technē ) and practical knowledge ( phronēsis ) .
