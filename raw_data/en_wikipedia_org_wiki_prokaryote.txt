Diagram of a prokaryotic cell, a [bacterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacteria "Bacteria") with a [flagellum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flagellum "Flagellum")

A **prokaryote** (; less commonly spelled **procaryote**)[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-1) is a [single-celled organism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicellular_organism "Unicellular organism") whose [cell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_(biology) "Cell (biology)") lacks a [nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_nucleus "Cell nucleus") and other [membrane-bound organelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Membrane-bound_organelle "Membrane-bound organelle").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-NCSU-2) The word _prokaryote_ comes from the [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "Ancient Greek")[πρό](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%80%CF%81%CF%8C#Ancient_Greek "wikt:πρό") (_pró_), meaning 'before', and [κάρυον](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BA%CE%AC%CF%81%CF%85%CE%BF%CE%BD#Ancient_Greek "wikt:κάρυον") (_káruon_), meaning 'nut' or 'kernel'.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-OnlineEtDict-3) In the earlier [two-empire system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two-empire_system "Two-empire system") arising from the work of [Édouard Chatton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89douard_Chatton "Édouard Chatton"), prokaryotes were classified within the empire **Prokaryota**. However, in the [three-domain system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-domain_system "Three-domain system"), based upon [molecular phylogenetics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_phylogenetics "Molecular phylogenetics"), prokaryotes are divided into two [domains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domain_(biology) "Domain (biology)"): [Bacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacteria "Bacteria") and [Archaea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaea "Archaea"). A third domain, [Eukaryota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote "Eukaryote"), consists of organisms with nuclei.

Prokaryotes [evolved](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evolution "Evolution") before eukaryotes, and lack nuclei, [mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondria "Mitochondria"), and most of the other distinct organelles that characterize the eukaryotic cell. Some unicellular prokaryotes, such as [cyanobacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanobacteria "Cyanobacteria"), form [colonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colony_(biology) "Colony (biology)") held together by [biofilms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biofilm "Biofilm"), and large colonies can create multilayered [microbial mats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microbial_mat "Microbial mat"). Prokaryotes are [asexual](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asexual_reproduction "Asexual reproduction"), reproducing via [binary fission.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fission_(biology) "Fission (biology)")[Horizontal gene transfer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horizontal_gene_transfer "Horizontal gene transfer") is common as well.

Molecular phylogenetics has provided insight into the interrelationships of the three domains of life. The division between prokaryotes and eukaryotes reflects two very different levels of cellular organization; only eukaryotic cells have an [enclosed nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_membrane "Nuclear membrane") that contains its [DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA "DNA"), and other membrane-bound organelles including mitochondria. More recently, the primary division has been seen as that between Archaea and Bacteria, since eukaryotes may be part of the archaean clade and have multiple [homologies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homology_(biology) "Homology (biology)") with other Archaea.

Prokaryotic cells are generally smaller and similar than eukaryotic cells. Prokaryotic cells do not enclose their genetic material within a nucleus.

The cellular components of prokaryotes are not enclosed in membranes within the [cytoplasm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytoplasm "Cytoplasm"), like eukaryotic organelles. Bacteria have [microcompartments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacterial_microcompartment "Bacterial microcompartment"), quasi-organelles enclosed in [protein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein "Protein") shells such as [encapsulin protein cages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Encapsulin_nanocompartment "Encapsulin nanocompartment"),[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-pref-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-5) while both bacteria and some archaea have [gas vesicles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas_vesicle "Gas vesicle").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-6)

Prokaryotes have simple [cell skeletons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryotic_cytoskeleton "Prokaryotic cytoskeleton"). These are highly diverse, and contain [homologues](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homology_(biology) "Homology (biology)") of the eukaryote proteins [actin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actin "Actin") and [tubulin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tubulin "Tubulin"). The cytoskeleton provides the capability for movement within the cell.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-7)

Most prokaryotes are between 1 and 10 μm, but they vary in size from 0.2 μm in _[Thermodiscus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermodiscus "Thermodiscus")_ spp. and _[Mycoplasma genitalium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mycoplasma\_genitalium "Mycoplasma genitalium")_ to 750 μm in _[Thiomargarita namibiensis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiomargarita\_namibiensis "Thiomargarita namibiensis")_.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Schulz_Jorgensen_2001-9)

Bacterial cells have [various shapes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacterial_cellular_morphologies "Bacterial cellular morphologies"), including spherical or ovoid [cocci](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cocci "Cocci"), e.g., _[Streptococcus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Streptococcus "Streptococcus")_; cylindrical [bacilli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacillus_(shape) "Bacillus (shape)"), e.g., _Lactobacillus_; [spiral bacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spiral_bacteria "Spiral bacteria"), e.g., _[Helicobacter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helicobacter "Helicobacter")_; or comma-shaped, e.g., _[Vibrio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vibrio "Vibrio")_.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-10) Archaea are mainly simple ovoids, but _[Haloquadratum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haloquadratum "Haloquadratum")_ is flat and square.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-11)

Reproduction and DNA transfer
-----------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Prokaryote&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Reproduction and DNA transfer")]

Bacteria and archaea reproduce through [asexual reproduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asexual_reproduction "Asexual reproduction"), usually by [binary fission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Binary_fission "Binary fission"). Genetic exchange and recombination occur by [horizontal gene transfer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horizontal_gene_transfer "Horizontal gene transfer"), not involving [replication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA_replication "DNA replication").[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Bobay_2020-13) DNA transfer between prokaryotic cells occurs in bacteria[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Chiang_Penad%C3%A9s_2019-14) and archaea.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Rosenshine_1989-15)

In bacteria, gene transfer occurs by three processes. These are virus-mediated [transduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transduction_(genetics) "Transduction (genetics)");[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Chiang_Penad%C3%A9s_2019-14)[conjugation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacterial_conjugation "Bacterial conjugation");[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Patkowski_2023-16) and [natural transformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetic_transformation#Natural_transformation "Genetic transformation").[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Chen_Dubnau_2004-17)

Transduction of bacterial genes by [bacteriophage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacteriophage "Bacteriophage") viruses appears to reflect occasional errors during intracellular assembly of [virus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virus "Virus") particles, rather than an [adaptation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adaptation "Adaptation") of the host bacteria. There are at least three ways that it can occur, all involving the incorporation of some bacterial DNA in the virus, and from there to another bacterium.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Chiang_Penad%C3%A9s_2019-14)

[![Image 3: Black and white image of F-pili bacteria](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Bacterial_conjugation.png/500px-Bacterial_conjugation.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Bacterial_conjugation.png)

[Bacterial conjugation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacterial_conjugation "Bacterial conjugation") using F-pili to exchange DNA[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Patkowski_2023-16)

Conjugation involves [plasmids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasmid "Plasmid"), allowing plasmid DNA to be transferred from one bacterium to another. Infrequently, a plasmid may integrate into the host bacterial chromosome, and subsequently transfer part of the host bacterial DNA to another bacterium.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Cabez%C3%B3n_2014-18)

Natural bacterial transformation involves the transfer of DNA from one bacterium to another through the water around them. This is a bacterial adaptation for DNA transfer, because it depends on the interaction of numerous bacterial gene products.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Chen_Dubnau_2004-17)

The bacterium must first enter the physiological state called [competence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_competence "Natural competence"); in _Bacillus subtilis_, the process involves 40 genes.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-19) The amount of DNA transferred during transformation can be as much as a third of the whole chromosome.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-20)[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-21) Transformation is common, occurring in at least 67 species of bacteria.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-22)

Among archaea, _[Haloferax volcanii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haloferax\_volcanii "Haloferax volcanii")_ forms cytoplasmic bridges between cells that transfer DNA between cells,[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Rosenshine_1989-15) while _[Sulfolobus solfataricus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfolobus\_solfataricus "Sulfolobus solfataricus")_ transfers DNA between cells by direct contact. Exposure of _S. solfataricus_ to agents that damage DNA induces cellular aggregation, perhaps enhancing homologous recombination to increase [the repair of damaged DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA_repair "DNA repair").[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-23)

Colonies and biofilms
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Prokaryote&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Colonies and biofilms")]

[![Image 4: photograph of cave biofilm](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/A114%2C_Lava_Beds_National_Monument%2C_California%2C_USA%2C_Golden_Dome_Lava_Tube_Cave%2C_2004.jpg/250px-A114%2C_Lava_Beds_National_Monument%2C_California%2C_USA%2C_Golden_Dome_Lava_Tube_Cave%2C_2004.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:A114,_Lava_Beds_National_Monument,_California,_USA,_Golden_Dome_Lava_Tube_Cave,_2004.jpg)

Biofilm of golden hydrophobic bacteria in a cave[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-24)

Prokaryotes are strictly [unicellular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicellular_organism "Unicellular organism"), but most can form stable aggregate communities in [biofilms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biofilm "Biofilm").[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-brock-25) Bacterial biofilms are formed by the secretion of [extracellular polymeric substance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extracellular_polymeric_substance "Extracellular polymeric substance") (EPS).[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-costerton2007-26)[Myxobacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myxobacteria "Myxobacteria") have multicellular stages in their [life cycles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_life_cycle "Biological life cycle").[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-27) Biofilms may be structurally complex and may attach to solid surfaces, or exist at liquid-air interfaces. Bacterial biofilms are often made up of [microcolonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microcolony "Microcolony") (dome-shaped masses of bacteria and matrix) separated by channels through which water may flow easily.

Microcolonies may join above the substratum to form a continuous layer. This structure functions as a simple [circulatory system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circulatory_system "Circulatory system") by moving water through the biofilm, helping to provide cells with oxygen which is often in short supply.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-costerton1995-28) The result approaches a multicellular organisation.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-shapiro1998-29) Differential cell expression, collective behavior, signaling ([quorum sensing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quorum_sensing "Quorum sensing")), [programmed cell death](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Programmed_cell_death "Programmed cell death"), and discrete [biological dispersal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_dispersal "Biological dispersal") events all seem to point in this direction.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-30)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-balaban2008-31) Bacterial biofilms may be 100 times more resistant to antibiotics than free-living unicells, making them difficult to remove from surfaces they have colonized.[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-costerton1999-32)

[![Image 5: photograph of multi-colored spring water](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Aerial_image_of_Grand_Prismatic_Spring_%28view_from_the_south%29.jpg/250px-Aerial_image_of_Grand_Prismatic_Spring_%28view_from_the_south%29.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Aerial_image_of_Grand_Prismatic_Spring_(view_from_the_south).jpg)

The bright colors of [Grand Prismatic Spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Prismatic_Spring "Grand Prismatic Spring"), [Yellowstone National Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellowstone_National_Park "Yellowstone National Park") are produced by [thermophilic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermophile "Thermophile") bacteria.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Brock_2005-33)

Prokaryotes have diversified greatly throughout their long existence. Their metabolism is far more varied than that of eukaryotes, leading to many highly distinct types. For example, prokaryotes may obtain energy by [chemosynthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemosynthesis "Chemosynthesis").[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-34) Prokaryotes live nearly everywhere on Earth, including in [environments as cold as](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryobiology "Cryobiology") soils in [Antarctica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica"),[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Tribelli_L%C3%B3pez_2018-35) or as hot as undersea [hydrothermal vents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrothermal_vent "Hydrothermal vent") and land-based [hot springs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hot_spring "Hot spring").[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Brock_2005-33) Some bacteria are [pathogenic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pathogen "Pathogen"), causing [disease](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disease "Disease") in organisms including humans.[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Peterson-1996-36) Some archaea and bacteria are [extremophiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extremophile "Extremophile"), thriving in harsh conditions, such as high temperatures ([thermophiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermophile "Thermophile")) or high salinity ([halophiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halophile "Halophile")).[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-37) Some [archaeans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaea "Archaea") are [methanogens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanogen "Methanogen"), living in anoxic environments and releasing [methane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methane "Methane").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-NCSU-2) Many archaea grow as [plankton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plankton "Plankton") in the oceans. [Symbiotic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbiotic "Symbiotic") prokaryotes live in or on the bodies of other organisms, including humans. Prokaryotes have high populations in the [soil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil "Soil"), in the sea, and in undersea sediments. Soil prokaryotes are still heavily undercharacterized despite their easy proximity to humans and their tremendous [economic importance to agriculture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agricultural_microbiology "Agricultural microbiology").[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Cobian-Guemes-et-al-2016-38)

The first organisms
-------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Prokaryote&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: The first organisms")]

[![Image 6](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Symbiogenesis_2_mergers.svg/500px-Symbiogenesis_2_mergers.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Symbiogenesis_2_mergers.svg)

In the theory of [symbiogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbiogenesis "Symbiogenesis"), a merger of two prokaryotes, an [archaean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaea "Archaea") and an [aerobic bacterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphaproteobacteria "Alphaproteobacteria"), created the eukaryotes, with aerobic [mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrion "Mitochondrion"); a second merger added [chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast "Chloroplast") from a third prokaryote, a photosynthetic [cyanobacterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanobacteria "Cyanobacteria"), creating the [green plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viridiplantae "Viridiplantae").[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-latorre-39)

A theoretical model of the [origin of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origin_of_life "Origin of life") is that the first organisms were prokaryotes. These are hypothesized to have evolved out of [protocells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protocell "Protocell"), while [the eukaryotes appeared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryogenesis "Eukaryogenesis") later in the history of life,[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-40) by [symbiogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbiogenesis "Symbiogenesis"): a merger of two prokaryotes, an [archaean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaea "Archaea") and an aerobic bacterium, created the first eukaryote, with aerobic [mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrion "Mitochondrion"). A second merger added [chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast "Chloroplast"), from a photosynthetic [cyanobacterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanobacteria "Cyanobacteria"), creating the [green plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viridiplantae "Viridiplantae").[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-latorre-39)

The oldest [fossilized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fossil "Fossil") prokaryotes were laid down approximately 3.5 billion years ago, only about 1 billion years after the formation of the Earth's crust. Eukaryotes only appear in the fossil record later. The oldest fossil eukaryotes are about 1.7 billion years old.[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-41)

### Taxonomy and phylogeny

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Prokaryote&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Taxonomy and phylogeny")]

The distinction between prokaryotes and [eukaryotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote "Eukaryote") was established by the microbiologists [Roger Stanier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roger_Stanier "Roger Stanier") and [C. B. van Niel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C._B._van_Niel "C. B. van Niel") in their 1962 paper _The concept of a bacterium_ (though spelled procaryote and eucaryote there).[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Stanier_Van_Niel_1962-42) That paper cites [Édouard Chatton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89douard_Chatton "Édouard Chatton")'s 1937 book _Titres et Travaux Scientifiques_[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-43) for using those terms and recognizing the distinction.[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-sapp2005-44) One reason for this classification was so that the group then often called **blue-green algae** (now [cyanobacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanobacteria "Cyanobacteria")) would not be classified as [plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plant "Plant") but grouped with bacteria.[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Stanier_Van_Niel_1962-42)

In 1977, [Carl Woese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Woese "Carl Woese") proposed dividing prokaryotes into the [Bacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacteria "Bacteria") and [Archaea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaea "Archaea") (originally Eubacteria and Archaebacteria) because of the major differences in the structure and genetics between the two groups of organisms. Archaea were originally thought to be extremophiles, living only in inhospitable conditions such as extremes of [temperature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature "Temperature"), [pH](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PH "PH"), and [radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiation "Radiation") but have since been found in all types of [habitats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habitat "Habitat"). The resulting arrangement of Eukaryota (also called "Eucarya"), Bacteria, and Archaea is called the [three-domain system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-domain_system "Three-domain system"), replacing the traditional [two-empire system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two-empire_system "Two-empire system").[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-45)[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-46)

Knowledge of prokaryote taxonomy is rapidly changing in the 21st century with the [sequencing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whole_genome_sequencing "Whole genome sequencing") of large numbers of genomes, many of these without the isolation of cultures of the organisms involved. As of 2021, consensus had not been reached among taxonomists to rely exclusively on genomes as opposed to existing practices, describing species from cultures.[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-47)

According to the 2016 phylogenetic analysis of Laura Hug and colleagues, using genomic data on over 1,000 organisms, the relationships among prokaryotes are as shown in the tree diagram. Bacteria dominate the diversity of organisms, shown at left, top, and right in the diagram; the archaea are shown bottom centre, and the eukaryotes in the small green area at bottom right. As represented by red dots on the diagram, there are multiple major lineages where no representative has been isolated: such lineages are common in both bacteria (such as [Omnitrophica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omnitrophica "Omnitrophica") and [Wirthbacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wirthbacteria "Wirthbacteria")) and archaea (such as [Parvarchaeota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parvarchaeota "Parvarchaeota") and [Lokiarchaeota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lokiarchaeota "Lokiarchaeota")). At the lower levels (species to class) and up to the level of phylum, the data provide strong support for the groupings, but the deepest (oldest) branches of the phylogeny are more uncertain.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Hug-48)

The large diversity of bacterial lineages shown in purple on the right of the diagram. These represent the so-called "candidate phyla radiation of bacteria", namely those with a combination of small genomes and reduced metabolic capabilities: none of them have been found to be able to carry out the whole of the [citric acid cycle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citric_acid_cycle "Citric acid cycle") by which many cells [release usable energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellular_respiration "Cellular respiration"), and few can synthesise [amino acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acid "Amino acid") and [nucleotides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleotide "Nucleotide"), building blocks of [proteins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein "Protein") and [nucleic acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleic_acid "Nucleic acid"). This may represent an ancient condition, or a loss of capabilities of symbiotic organisms.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Hug-48)

### As distinct from eukaryotes

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Prokaryote&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: As distinct from eukaryotes")]

[![Image 8](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Prokaryote_cell.svg/60px-Prokaryote_cell.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Prokaryote_cell.svg)

Prokaryote, to same scale

[![Image 9](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Endomembrane_system_diagram_en.svg/330px-Endomembrane_system_diagram_en.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Endomembrane_system_diagram_en.svg)

Part of a eukaryotic cell

Eukaryotic cells are some 10,000 times larger than prokaryotic cells by volume, have their DNA organised in a nucleus, and contain [membrane-bound organelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Membrane-bound_organelle "Membrane-bound organelle").[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-DeRennaux_2001_pp._329%E2%80%93332-49)

The division between prokaryotes and eukaryotes has been considered the most important distinction or difference among organisms. The distinction is that eukaryotic cells have a "true" [nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_nucleus "Cell nucleus") containing their [DNA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA "DNA"), whereas prokaryotic cells do not have a nucleus.[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Cot%C3%A9_De_Tullio_2010-50) Eukaryotic cells are some 10,000 times larger than prokaryotic cells by volume, and contain [membrane-bound organelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Membrane-bound_organelle "Membrane-bound organelle").[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-DeRennaux_2001_pp._329%E2%80%93332-49)

Both eukaryotes and prokaryotes contain [ribosomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ribosome "Ribosome") which [produce proteins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Translation_(biology) "Translation (biology)") as specified by the cell's DNA. Prokaryote ribosomes are smaller than those in eukaryote cytoplasm, but similar to those inside [mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondria "Mitochondria") and [chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast "Chloroplast"), one of several lines of evidence that those organelles derive from bacteria incorporated by [symbiogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbiogenesis "Symbiogenesis").[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Alberts-51)[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Latorre_Durban_2011-52)

The [genome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genome "Genome") in a prokaryote is held within a DNA/protein complex in the [cytosol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytosol "Cytosol") called the [nucleoid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleoid "Nucleoid"), which lacks a [nuclear envelope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_envelope "Nuclear envelope"). The complex contains a single [circular chromosome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circular_chromosome "Circular chromosome"), a cyclic, double-stranded molecule of stable chromosomal DNA, in contrast to the multiple linear, compact, highly organized [chromosomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromosome "Chromosome") found in eukaryotic cells.[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-53) In addition, many important genes of prokaryotes are stored in separate circular DNA structures called plasmids.[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Helinski_2022-54) Like eukaryotes, prokaryotes may partially duplicate genetic material, and can have a [haploid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haploid "Haploid") chromosomal composition that is [partially replicated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merodiploid "Merodiploid").[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-55)

Prokaryotes vs Eukaryotes[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Cot%C3%A9_De_Tullio_2010-50)[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Harold_1972-56)| Domain | [Nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_nucleus "Cell nucleus") | [Organelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organelle "Organelle") | [Reproduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reproduction "Reproduction") |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Prokaryotes | None, DNA is free in cytoplasm | Few | [Asexual](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asexual_reproduction "Asexual reproduction"), with [horizontal gene transfer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horizontal_gene_transfer "Horizontal gene transfer") |
| Eukaryotes | DNA in nucleus | Membrane-bound organelles, inc. [endoplasmic reticulum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endoplasmic_reticulum "Endoplasmic reticulum"), [mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrion "Mitochondrion"), [chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast "Chloroplast") | [Sexual reproduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sexual_reproduction "Sexual reproduction") with [haploid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haploid "Haploid")[gametes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamete "Gamete") |

Prokaryotes lack [mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrion "Mitochondrion") and chloroplasts. Instead, processes such as [oxidative phosphorylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxidative_phosphorylation "Oxidative phosphorylation") and photosynthesis take place across the prokaryotic cell membrane.[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Harold_1972-56) Prokaryotes possess some internal structures, such as [prokaryotic cytoskeletons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryotic_cytoskeleton "Prokaryotic cytoskeleton").[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-57)[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-58) It was previously suggested that the bacterial phylum [Planctomycetota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planctomycetota "Planctomycetota") has a membrane around the nucleoid and contains other membrane-bound cellular structures.[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-59) Further investigation revealed that Planctomycetota cells are not compartmentalized or nucleated and, like other bacterial membrane systems, are interconnected.[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-60)

Prokaryotic cells are usually much smaller than eukaryotic cells. This causes prokaryotes to have a larger [surface-area-to-volume ratio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surface-area-to-volume_ratio "Surface-area-to-volume ratio"), giving them a higher [metabolic rate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metabolic_rate "Metabolic rate"), a higher growth rate, and as a consequence, a shorter generation time than eukaryotes.[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Kempes_Dutkiewicz_2012-61)

### Eukaryotes as Archaea

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Prokaryote&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Eukaryotes as Archaea")]

[![Image 10](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Phylogenetic_Tree_of_Prokaryota.png/500px-Phylogenetic_Tree_of_Prokaryota.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Phylogenetic_Tree_of_Prokaryota.png)

Phylogenetic tree showing the diversity of prokaryotes.[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Castelle2018-62) This 2018 proposal shows eukaryotes within the archaean [Asgard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asgard_(archaea) "Asgard (archaea)") group which represents a modern version of the [eocyte hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eocyte_hypothesis "Eocyte hypothesis"). In this view, the division between bacteria and the rest is what groups organisms into the two major domains.

There is increasing evidence that the roots of the eukaryotes are to be found in the archaean [Asgard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asgard_(archaea) "Asgard (archaea)") group, perhaps [Heimdallarchaeota](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Heimdallarchaeota&action=edit&redlink=1 "Heimdallarchaeota (page does not exist)").[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Castelle2018-62) For example, [histones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Histone#Conservation_across_species "Histone"), which usually package DNA in eukaryotic nuclei, are found in several archaean groups, giving evidence for homology.[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Mattiroli_Bhattacharyya_2017-63) A proposed non-bacterial group comprising Archaea and Eukaryota was called [Neomura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neomura "Neomura") by [Thomas Cavalier-Smith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Cavalier-Smith "Thomas Cavalier-Smith") in 2002.[[64]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-pmid11931142-64)

Eukaryotes as Archaea[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-NLane_Funke_2015-65)[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Mattiroli_Bhattacharyya_2017-63)[[66]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Barry_Bell_2006-66)| Domain | [Histone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Histone "Histone") proteins | [ATP synthase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ATP_synthase "ATP synthase") | [DNA replication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA_replication "DNA replication") |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Archaea, inc. Eukaryota | All are similar in these two groups, implying [homology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homology_(biology) "Homology (biology)") and relatedness |
| Bacteria | (missing) | Present in a very different form |

Another view is that the most important difference between [biota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biota_(taxonomy) "Biota (taxonomy)") may be the division between Bacteria and the rest (Archaea and Eukaryota).[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Castelle2018-62)[DNA replication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA_replication "DNA replication") differs fundamentally between the Bacteria and Archaea (including that in eukaryotic nuclei), and it may not be homologous between these two groups.[[66]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Barry_Bell_2006-66)

Further, [ATP synthase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ATP_synthase "ATP synthase"), though homologous in all organisms, differs greatly between bacteria (including eukaryotic [organelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organelle "Organelle") such as mitochondria and chloroplasts) and the archaea/eukaryote nucleus group. The last common ancestor of all life (called [LUCA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LUCA#Location_of_the_root "LUCA")) should have possessed an early version of this protein complex. As ATP synthase is obligate membrane bound, this supports the assumption that LUCA was a cellular organism. The [RNA world hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNA_world_hypothesis "RNA world hypothesis") might clarify this scenario, as LUCA might have lacked DNA, but had an RNA genome built by ribosomes as [suggested by Woese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woese%27s_dogma#Ribosomes_as_primordial_self-replicating_entities "Woese's dogma").[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-NLane_Funke_2015-65)

A [ribonucleoprotein world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RNP_world "RNP world") has been proposed based on the idea that [oligopeptides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oligopeptide "Oligopeptide") may have been built together with primordial nucleic acids at the same time, which supports the concept of a [ribocyte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ribocyte "Ribocyte") as LUCA. The feature of DNA as the material base of the genome might have then been adopted separately in bacteria and in archaea (and later eukaryote nuclei), presumably with the help of some viruses (possibly [retroviruses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retroviruses "Retroviruses") as they could [reverse transcribe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reverse_transcription "Reverse transcription") RNA to DNA).[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote#cite_note-Forterre2006-67)
