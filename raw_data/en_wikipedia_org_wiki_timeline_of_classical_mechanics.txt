The following is a **timeline of the [history of classical mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_classical_mechanics "History of classical mechanics")**:

*   4th century BC – [Aristotle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aristotle "Aristotle") invents the system of [Aristotelian physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aristotelian_physics "Aristotelian physics"), which is later largely disproved
*   4th century BC – [Babylonian astronomers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Babylonian_astronomy "Babylonian astronomy") calculate Jupiter's position using the [Trapezoidal rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trapezoidal_rule "Trapezoidal rule")[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-1)
*   260 BC – [Archimedes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes "Archimedes") works out the principle of the [lever](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lever "Lever") and [connects buoyancy to weight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes%27_principle "Archimedes' principle")
*   60 – [Hero of Alexandria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hero_of_Alexandria "Hero of Alexandria") writes _Metrica, Mechanics_ (on means to lift heavy objects), and _Pneumatics_ (on machines working on pressure)
*   350 – [Themistius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Themistius "Themistius") states, that [static friction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Static_friction "Static friction") is larger than [kinetic friction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_friction "Kinetic friction")[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-2)

*   6th century – [John Philoponus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Philoponus "John Philoponus") introduces the concept of [impetus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theory_of_impetus "Theory of impetus")[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-3) and the theory was modified by [Avicenna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avicenna "Avicenna") in the 11th century and [Ibn Malka al-Baghdadi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibn_Malka_al-Baghdadi "Ibn Malka al-Baghdadi") in the 12th century
*   6th century – [John Philoponus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Philoponus "John Philoponus") says that by observation, two balls of very different weights will fall at nearly the same speed. He therefore tests the [equivalence principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equivalence_principle "Equivalence principle")
*   1021 – [Al-Biruni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Biruni "Al-Biruni") uses three [orthogonal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthogonal "Orthogonal") coordinates to describe point in space[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-MacTutor-4)
*   1100–1138 – [Avempace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibn_Bajjah "Ibn Bajjah") develops the concept of a fatigue, which according to Shlomo Pines is precursor to Leibnizian idea of force[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-5)
*   1100–1165 – [Hibat Allah Abu'l-Barakat al-Baghdaadi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hibat_Allah_Abu%27l-Barakat_al-Baghdaadi "Hibat Allah Abu'l-Barakat al-Baghdaadi") discovers that [force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Force "Force") is proportional to acceleration rather than speed, a fundamental law in classical mechanics[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-6)

*   1340–1358 – [Jean Buridan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Buridan "Jean Buridan") develops the [theory of impetus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theory_of_impetus "Theory of impetus")
*   14th century – [Oxford Calculators](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxford_Calculators "Oxford Calculators") and French collaborators prove the [mean speed theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mean_speed_theorem "Mean speed theorem")
*   14th century – [Nicole Oresme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicole_Oresme "Nicole Oresme") derives the times-squared law for uniformly accelerated change.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-7) Oresme, however, regarded this discovery as a purely intellectual exercise having no relevance to the description of any natural phenomena, and consequently failed to recognise any connection with the motion of accelerating bodies[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-8)
*   1500–1528 – [Al-Birjandi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Birjandi "Al-Birjandi") develops the theory of "circular [inertia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inertia "Inertia")" to explain [Earth's rotation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth%27s_rotation "Earth's rotation")[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-Ragep-9)
*   16th century – [Francesco Beato](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Francesco_Beato&action=edit&redlink=1 "Francesco Beato (page does not exist)") and [Luca Ghini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luca_Ghini "Luca Ghini") experimentally contradict Aristotelian view on free fall.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-10)
*   16th century – [Domingo de Soto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domingo_de_Soto "Domingo de Soto") suggests that bodies falling through a homogeneous medium are uniformly accelerated.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-11)[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-12) Soto, however, did not anticipate many of the qualifications and refinements contained in Galileo's theory of falling bodies. He did not, for instance, recognise, as Galileo did, that a body would fall with a strictly uniform acceleration only in a vacuum, and that it would otherwise eventually reach a uniform terminal velocity
*   1581 – [Galileo Galilei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galileo_Galilei "Galileo Galilei") notices the timekeeping property of the [pendulum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pendulum "Pendulum")
*   1589 – Galileo Galilei uses balls rolling on inclined planes to show that different weights fall with the same acceleration
*   1638 – Galileo Galilei publishes _[Dialogues Concerning Two New Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dialogues\_Concerning\_Two\_New\_Sciences "Dialogues Concerning Two New Sciences")_ (which were [materials science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Materials_science "Materials science") and [kinematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinematics "Kinematics")) where he develops, amongst other things, [Galilean transformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galilean_transformation "Galilean transformation")
*   1644 – [René Descartes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Descartes "René Descartes") suggests an early form of the law of [conservation of momentum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_of_momentum "Conservation of momentum")
*   1645 – [Ismaël Bullialdus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isma%C3%ABl_Bullialdus "Ismaël Bullialdus") argues that "gravity" weakens as the inverse square of the distance[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-13)
*   1651 – [Giovanni Battista Riccioli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giovanni_Battista_Riccioli "Giovanni Battista Riccioli") and [Francesco Maria Grimaldi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francesco_Maria_Grimaldi "Francesco Maria Grimaldi") discover the [Coriolis effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coriolis_effect "Coriolis effect")
*   1658 – [Christiaan Huygens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christiaan_Huygens "Christiaan Huygens") experimentally discovers that balls placed anywhere inside an inverted [cycloid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cycloid "Cycloid") reach the lowest point of the cycloid in the same time and thereby experimentally shows that the cycloid is the [tautochrone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tautochrone "Tautochrone")
*   1668 – [John Wallis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Wallis "John Wallis") suggests the law of conservation of momentum
*   1673 – [Christiaan Huygens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christiaan_Huygens "Christiaan Huygens") publishes his _[Horologium Oscillatorium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horologium\_Oscillatorium "Horologium Oscillatorium")_. Huygens describes in this work the first two [laws of motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%27s_laws_of_motion "Newton's laws of motion").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-14) The book is also the first modern treatise in which a physical problem (the accelerated motion of a falling body) is idealized by a set of parameters and then analyzed mathematically.
*   1676–1689 – [Gottfried Leibniz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gottfried_Leibniz "Gottfried Leibniz") develops the concept of [vis viva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vis_viva "Vis viva"), a limited theory of [conservation of energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_of_energy "Conservation of energy")
*   1677 – [Baruch Spinoza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baruch_Spinoza "Baruch Spinoza") puts forward a primitive notion of [Newton's first law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%27s_first_law "Newton's first law")

Newtonian mechanics
-------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_classical_mechanics&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Newtonian mechanics")]

*   1687 – [Isaac Newton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Newton "Isaac Newton") publishes his _[Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophi%C3%A6\_Naturalis\_Principia\_Mathematica "Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica")_, in which he formulates [Newton's laws of motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%27s_laws_of_motion "Newton's laws of motion") and [Newton's law of universal gravitation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%27s_law_of_universal_gravitation "Newton's law of universal gravitation")
*   1690 – [James Bernoulli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Bernoulli "James Bernoulli") shows that the [cycloid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cycloid "Cycloid") is the solution to the tautochrone problem
*   1691 – [Johann Bernoulli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Bernoulli "Johann Bernoulli") shows that a chain freely suspended from two points will form a [catenary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catenary "Catenary")
*   1691 – James Bernoulli shows that the catenary curve has the lowest [center of gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Center_of_gravity "Center of gravity") of any chain hung from two fixed points
*   1696 – Johann Bernoulli shows that the cycloid is the solution to the [brachistochrone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brachistochrone "Brachistochrone") problem
*   1710 – [Jakob Hermann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jakob_Hermann "Jakob Hermann") shows that [Laplace–Runge–Lenz vector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laplace%E2%80%93Runge%E2%80%93Lenz_vector "Laplace–Runge–Lenz vector") is conserved for a case of the inverse-square [central force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_force "Central force")[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-15)
*   1714 – [Brook Taylor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brook_Taylor "Brook Taylor") derives the [fundamental frequency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fundamental_frequency "Fundamental frequency") of a stretched vibrating string in terms of its tension and mass per unit length by solving an ordinary [differential equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_equation "Differential equation")
*   1733 – [Daniel Bernoulli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli "Daniel Bernoulli") derives the fundamental frequency and [harmonics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harmonic "Harmonic") of a hanging chain by solving an ordinary differential equation
*   1734 – Daniel Bernoulli solves the ordinary differential equation for the vibrations of an elastic bar clamped at one end
*   1739 – [Leonhard Euler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonhard_Euler "Leonhard Euler") solves the ordinary differential equation for a [forced harmonic oscillator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harmonic_oscillator#Driven_harmonic_oscillators "Harmonic oscillator") and notices the [resonance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resonance "Resonance")
*   1742 – [Colin Maclaurin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colin_Maclaurin "Colin Maclaurin") discovers his [uniformly rotating self-gravitating spheroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maclaurin_spheroid "Maclaurin spheroid")
*   1743 – [Jean le Rond d'Alembert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_le_Rond_d%27Alembert "Jean le Rond d'Alembert") publishes his _Traite de Dynamique_, in which he introduces the concept of [generalized forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generalized_forces "Generalized forces") and [D'Alembert's principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%27Alembert%27s_principle "D'Alembert's principle")
*   1747 – D'Alembert and [Alexis Clairaut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexis_Clairaut "Alexis Clairaut") publish first approximate solutions to the [three-body problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-body_problem "Three-body problem")
*   1749 – [Leonhard Euler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonhard_Euler "Leonhard Euler") derives equation for [Coriolis acceleration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coriolis_acceleration "Coriolis acceleration")
*   1759 – Leonhard Euler solves the partial differential equation for the vibration of a rectangular drum
*   1764 – Leonhard Euler examines the partial differential equation for the vibration of a circular drum and finds one of the [Bessel function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bessel_function "Bessel function") solutions
*   1776 – [John Smeaton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Smeaton "John Smeaton") publishes a paper on experiments relating [power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_(physics) "Power (physics)"), [work](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanical_work "Mechanical work"), [momentum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Momentum "Momentum") and [kinetic energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_energy "Kinetic energy"), and supporting the conservation of energy

Analytical mechanics
--------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_classical_mechanics&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Analytical mechanics")]

*   1788 – [Joseph-Louis Lagrange](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph-Louis_Lagrange "Joseph-Louis Lagrange") presents [Lagrange's equations of motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrangian_mechanics "Lagrangian mechanics") in the _Méchanique Analytique_
*   1798 – [Pierre-Simon Laplace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre-Simon_Laplace "Pierre-Simon Laplace") publishes his [Traité de mécanique céleste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trait%C3%A9_de_m%C3%A9canique_c%C3%A9leste "Traité de mécanique céleste") vol.1 and lasts vol.5 in 1825. In this, he summarized and extended the work of his predecessors
*   1803 – [Louis Poinsot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Poinsot "Louis Poinsot") develops idea of [angular momentum conservation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angular_momentum#Conservation_of_angular_momentum "Angular momentum") (this result was previously known only in the case of conservation of [areal velocity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Areal_velocity "Areal velocity"))
*   1813 – [Peter Ewart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Ewart "Peter Ewart") supports the idea of the conservation of energy in his paper "On the measure of moving force"
*   1821 – [William Hamilton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Rowan_Hamilton "William Rowan Hamilton") begins his analysis of [Hamilton's characteristic function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamilton%27s_characteristic_function "Hamilton's characteristic function") and [Hamilton–Jacobi equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamilton%E2%80%93Jacobi_equation "Hamilton–Jacobi equation")
*   1829 – [Carl Friedrich Gauss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Friedrich_Gauss "Carl Friedrich Gauss") introduces [Gauss's principle of least constraint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauss%27s_principle_of_least_constraint "Gauss's principle of least constraint")
*   1834 – [Carl Jacobi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Gustav_Jacob_Jacobi "Carl Gustav Jacob Jacobi") discovers his [uniformly rotating self-gravitating ellipsoids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacobi_ellipsoid "Jacobi ellipsoid")
*   1834 – [Louis Poinsot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Poinsot "Louis Poinsot") notes an instance of the [intermediate axis theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intermediate_axis_theorem "Intermediate axis theorem")[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-16)
*   1835 – William Hamilton states [Hamilton's canonical equations of motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamiltonian_mechanics "Hamiltonian mechanics")
*   1838 – Liouville begins work on [Liouville's theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liouville%27s_theorem_(Hamiltonian) "Liouville's theorem (Hamiltonian)")
*   1841 – [Julius von Mayer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julius_von_Mayer "Julius von Mayer"), an [amateur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amateur "Amateur") scientist, writes a paper on the conservation of energy but his lack of academic training leads to a [priority dispute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanical_equivalent_of_heat "Mechanical equivalent of heat").
*   1847 – [Hermann von Helmholtz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermann_von_Helmholtz "Hermann von Helmholtz") formally states the law of [conservation of energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_of_energy "Conservation of energy")
*   first half of the 19th century – [Cauchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cauchy "Cauchy") develops [his momentum equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cauchy_momentum_equation "Cauchy momentum equation") and [his stress tensor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cauchy_stress_tensor "Cauchy stress tensor")
*   1851 – [Léon Foucault](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Foucault "Léon Foucault") shows the Earth's rotation with a huge [pendulum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pendulum "Pendulum") ([Foucault pendulum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foucault_pendulum "Foucault pendulum"))
*   1870 – [Rudolf Clausius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudolf_Clausius "Rudolf Clausius") deduces [virial theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virial_theorem "Virial theorem")
*   1890 – [Henri Poincaré](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henri_Poincar%C3%A9 "Henri Poincaré") discovers the sensibility of initial conditions in the [three-body problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-body_problem "Three-body problem").[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-17)
*   1898 – [Jacques Hadamard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Hadamard "Jacques Hadamard") discusses the [Hadamard billiards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadamard_billiards "Hadamard billiards").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-:0-18)

Modern developments
-------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_classical_mechanics&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Modern developments")]

*   1900 – [Max Planck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Planck "Max Planck") introduces the idea of [quanta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum "Quantum"), introducing [quantum mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_mechanics "Quantum mechanics")
*   1902 – [James Jeans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Jeans "James Jeans") finds the length scale required for gravitational perturbations to grow in a static nearly homogeneous medium
*   1905 – [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein") first mathematically describes [Brownian motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brownian_motion "Brownian motion") and introduces [relativistic mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relativistic_mechanics "Relativistic mechanics")
*   1915 – [Emmy Noether](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmy_Noether "Emmy Noether") proves [Noether's theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noether%27s_theorem "Noether's theorem"), from which conservation laws are deduced
*   1915 – [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein") introduces [general relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity "General relativity")
*   1923 – [Élie Cartan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89lie_Cartan "Élie Cartan") introduces the [geometrized Newtonian gravitation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton%E2%80%93Cartan_theory "Newton–Cartan theory"), treating Newtonian gravitation in terms of spacetime.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-19)
*   1931–1932 – [Bernard Koopman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernard_Koopman "Bernard Koopman") and [John von Neumann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_von_Neumann "John von Neumann") papers led to the development of [Koopman–von Neumann classical mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koopman%E2%80%93von_Neumann_classical_mechanics "Koopman–von Neumann classical mechanics").[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-20)
*   1952 – Parker develops a [tensor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensor "Tensor") form of the virial theorem[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-21)
*   1954 – [Andrey Kolmogorov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrey_Kolmogorov "Andrey Kolmogorov") publishes the first version of the [Kolmogorov–Arnold–Moser theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kolmogorov%E2%80%93Arnold%E2%80%93Moser_theorem "Kolmogorov–Arnold–Moser theorem").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-:0-18)
*   1961 – [Edward Norton Lorenz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Norton_Lorenz "Edward Norton Lorenz") discovers [Lorenz systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lorenz_system "Lorenz system") and establishes the field of [chaos theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chaos_theory "Chaos theory").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-:0-18)
*   1978 – [Vladimir Arnold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Arnold "Vladimir Arnold") states precise form of [Liouville–Arnold theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liouville%E2%80%93Arnold_theorem "Liouville–Arnold theorem")[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_classical_mechanics#cite_note-22)
*   1983 – [Mordehai Milgrom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mordehai_Milgrom "Mordehai Milgrom") proposes [modified Newtonian dynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modified_Newtonian_dynamics "Modified Newtonian dynamics") as an alternative to the [dark matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dark_matter "Dark matter") hypothesis
*   1992 – Udwadia and Kalaba create [Udwadia–Kalaba equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Udwadia%E2%80%93Kalaba_equation "Udwadia–Kalaba equation")
*   2003 – [John D. Norton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_D._Norton "John D. Norton") introduces [Norton's dome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norton%27s_dome "Norton's dome")
