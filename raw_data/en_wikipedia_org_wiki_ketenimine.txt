Class of chemical compounds

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Ketenimine.png/120px-Ketenimine.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Ketenimine.png)

General structure of a ketenimine

**Ketenimines** are a group of [organic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") sharing a common [functional group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_group "Functional group") with the general structure R 1 R 2 C=C=NR 3. A ketenimine is a cumulated [alkene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkene "Alkene") and [imine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imine "Imine") and is related to an [allene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allene "Allene") and a [ketene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ketene "Ketene").

The parent compound is ketenimine or CH 2 CNH. The most recent work by Bane _et al._ investigates the rovibrational structure of the ν 8 and ν 12 bands in the high-resolution FTIR spectrum, complementing the earlier analysis of the pure rotational spectrum. This pair of Coriolis coupled bands provide a rare example where intensity sharing between bands yields sufficient intensity for an otherwise invisible band (ν 12).[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ketenimine#cite_note-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ketenimine#cite_note-2)
