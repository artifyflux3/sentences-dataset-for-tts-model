**Dimethyl ether** (**DME**; also known as **methoxymethane**) is the [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the formula CH 3 OCH 3, (sometimes ambiguously simplified to C 2 H 6 O as it is an [isomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomer "Isomer") of [ethanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanol "Ethanol")). The simplest [ether](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ether "Ether"), it is a colorless gas that is a useful precursor to other organic compounds and an aerosol propellant that is currently being demonstrated for use in a variety of fuel applications.

Dimethyl ether was first synthesised by [Jean-Baptiste Dumas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Dumas "Jean-Baptiste Dumas") and [Eugene Péligot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_P%C3%A9ligot "Eugene Péligot") in 1835 by distillation of methanol and sulfuric acid.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-5)

Approximately 50,000 tons were produced in 1985 in Western Europe by [dehydration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dehydration_reaction "Dehydration reaction") of [methanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanol "Methanol"):[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-Ullmann-6)

2 CH 3 OH → (CH 3)2 O + H 2 O
The required methanol is obtained from synthesis gas ([syngas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syngas "Syngas")).[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-ChemSystems-7) Other possible improvements call for a dual catalyst system that permits both methanol synthesis and dehydration in the same process unit, with no methanol isolation and purification.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-ChemSystems-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-8) Both the one-step and two-step processes above are commercially available. The two-step process is relatively simple and start-up costs are relatively low. A one-step liquid-phase process is in development.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-ChemSystems-7)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-9)

Dimethyl ether is a synthetic [second generation biofuel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_generation_biofuel "Second generation biofuel") (BioDME), which can be produced from [lignocellulosic biomass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lignocellulosic_biomass "Lignocellulosic biomass").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-10) The EU is considering BioDME in its potential biofuel mix in 2030;[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-11) It can also be made from [biogas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biogas "Biogas") or [methane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methane "Methane") from animal, food, and agricultural waste,[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-12)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-13) or even from [shale gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shale_gas "Shale gas") or [natural gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_gas "Natural gas").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-14)

The [Volvo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volvo "Volvo") Group is the coordinator for the [European Community](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Community "European Community")[Seventh Framework Programme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seventh_Framework_Programme "Seventh Framework Programme") project BioDME[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-15)[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-16) where [Chemrec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemrec "Chemrec")'s BioDME pilot plant is based on [black liquor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_liquor "Black liquor")[gasification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gasification "Gasification") in [Piteå](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pite%C3%A5 "Piteå"), [Sweden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweden "Sweden").[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-17)

The largest use of dimethyl ether is as the feedstock for the production of the methylating agent, [dimethyl sulfate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_sulfate "Dimethyl sulfate"), which entails its reaction with [sulfur trioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfur_trioxide "Sulfur trioxide"):

CH 3 OCH 3 + SO 3 → (CH 3)2 SO 4
Dimethyl ether can also be converted into [acetic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetic_acid "Acetic acid") using [carbonylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbonylation "Carbonylation") technology related to the [Monsanto acetic acid process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsanto_acetic_acid_process "Monsanto acetic acid process"):[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-Ullmann-6)

(CH 3)2 O + 2 CO + H 2 O → 2 CH 3 CO 2 H

### Laboratory reagent and solvent

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Dimethyl_ether&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Laboratory reagent and solvent")]

Dimethyl ether is a low-temperature solvent and extraction agent, applicable to specialised laboratory procedures. Its usefulness is limited by its low [boiling point](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boiling_point "Boiling point") (−23°C (−9°F)), but the same property facilitates its removal from reaction mixtures. Dimethyl ether is the precursor to the useful [alkylating agent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkylating_agent "Alkylating agent"), [trimethyloxonium tetrafluoroborate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trimethyloxonium_tetrafluoroborate "Trimethyloxonium tetrafluoroborate").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-18)

A mixture of dimethyl ether and [propane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propane "Propane") is used in some over-the-counter "[freeze spray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freeze_spray "Freeze spray")" products to treat [warts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wart "Wart") by [freezing them](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryosurgery "Cryosurgery").[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-19)[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-20) In this role, it has supplanted halocarbon compounds ([Freon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freon "Freon")).

Dimethyl ether is also a component of certain high temperature ["Map-Pro"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MAPP_gas "MAPP gas") blowtorch gas blends, supplanting the use of [methyl acetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_acetylene "Methyl acetylene") and [propadiene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propadiene "Propadiene") mixtures.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-MAP-Plus-21)

Dimethyl ether is also used as a propellant in aerosol products. Such products include hair spray, bug spray and some aerosol glue products.

Installation of BioDME synthesis towers at Chemrec's pilot facility

A potentially major use of dimethyl ether is as substitute for [propane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propane "Propane") in [LPG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquefied_petroleum_gas "Liquefied petroleum gas") used as fuel in household and industry.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-22) Dimethyl ether can also be used as a blendstock in propane [autogas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autogas "Autogas").[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-23)

It is also a promising fuel in [diesel engines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diesel_engines "Diesel engines"),[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-24) and [gas turbines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas_turbines "Gas turbines"). For diesel engines, an advantage is the high [cetane number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cetane_number "Cetane number") of 55, compared to that of [diesel fuel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diesel_fuel "Diesel fuel") from petroleum, which is 40–53.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-25) Only moderate modifications are needed to convert a diesel engine to burn dimethyl ether. The simplicity of this short carbon chain compound leads to very low emissions of particulate matter during combustion. For these reasons as well as being sulfur-free, dimethyl ether meets even the most stringent emission regulations in Europe ([EURO5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_emission_standards "European emission standards")), U.S. (U.S. 2010), and Japan (2009 Japan).[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-26)

At the [European Shell Eco Marathon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shell_Eco-marathon "Shell Eco-marathon"), an unofficial World Championship for mileage, vehicle running on 100% dimethyl ether drove 589 km/L (169.8 cm 3/100 km), fuel equivalent to gasoline with a 50 cm 3 displacement 2-stroke engine. As well as winning they beat the old standing record of 306 km/liter (326.8 cm 3/100 km), set by the same team in 2007.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-27)

To study the dimethyl ether for the combustion process a chemical kinetic mechanism[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-28) is required which can be used for Computational fluid dynamics calculation.

Dimethyl ether is a [refrigerant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Refrigerant "Refrigerant") with [ASHRAE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASHRAE "ASHRAE") refrigerant designation R-E170. It is also used in refrigerant blends with e.g. [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia"), [carbon dioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide "Carbon dioxide"), [butane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Butane "Butane") and [propene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propene "Propene"). Dimethyl ether was the first refrigerant. In 1876, the French engineer [Charles Tellier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Tellier "Charles Tellier") bought the ex-Elder-Dempster a 690 tons cargo ship _Eboe_ and fitted a methyl-ether [refrigerating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reefer_ship "Reefer ship") plant of his design. The ship was renamed _Le Frigorifique_ and successfully imported a cargo of refrigerated meat from [Argentina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argentina "Argentina"). However the machinery could be improved and in 1877 another refrigerated ship called _Paraguay_ with a refrigerating plant improved by [Ferdinand Carré](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_Carr%C3%A9 "Ferdinand Carré") was put into service on the South American run.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-29)[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-ASHRAE_refrigerants-30)

Unlike other alkyl ethers, dimethyl ether resists [autoxidation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autoxidation "Autoxidation").[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-31) Dimethyl ether is also relatively non-toxic, although it is highly flammable. On July 28, 1948, a [BASF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BASF "BASF") factory in [Ludwigshafen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludwigshafen "Ludwigshafen") suffered an explosion after 30 tonnes of dimethyl ether leaked from a tank and ignited in the air. 200 people died, and a third of the industrial plant was destroyed.[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_ether#cite_note-32)
