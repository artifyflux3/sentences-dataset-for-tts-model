Not to be confused with [List of freedom indices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_freedom_indices "List of freedom indices").

Number of independent parameters of a system

In many scientific fields, the **degrees of freedom** of a system is the number of [parameters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parameter "Parameter") of the system that may vary independently. For example, a point in the plane has two degrees of freedom for [translation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Translation_(geometry) "Translation (geometry)"): its two [coordinates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coordinates "Coordinates"); a non-infinitesimal object on the plane might have additional degrees of freedoms related to its [orientation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orientation_(geometry) "Orientation (geometry)").

In [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics"), this notion is formalized as the [dimension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimension_(mathematics_and_physics) "Dimension (mathematics and physics)") of a [manifold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifold "Manifold") or an [algebraic variety](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algebraic_variety "Algebraic variety"). When _degrees of freedom_ is used instead of _dimension_, this usually means that the manifold or variety that models the system is only implicitly defined. See:

*   [Degrees of freedom (mechanics)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Degrees_of_freedom_(mechanics) "Degrees of freedom (mechanics)"), number of independent motions that are allowed to the body or, in case of a [mechanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanism_(engineering) "Mechanism (engineering)") made of several bodies, number of possible independent relative motions between the pieces of the mechanism
*   [Degrees of freedom (physics and chemistry)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Degrees_of_freedom_(physics_and_chemistry) "Degrees of freedom (physics and chemistry)"), a term used in explaining dependence on parameters, or the dimensions of a phase space
*   [Degrees of freedom (statistics)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Degrees_of_freedom_(statistics) "Degrees of freedom (statistics)"), the number of values in the final calculation of a statistic that are free to vary
*   [Degrees of freedom problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Degrees_of_freedom_problem "Degrees of freedom problem"), the problem of controlling motor movement given abundant degrees of freedom
