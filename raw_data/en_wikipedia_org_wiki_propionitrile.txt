**Propionitrile**, also known as **ethyl cyanide** and **propanenitrile**, is an [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the formula CH 3 CH 2 CN. It is a simple aliphatic [nitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrile "Nitrile"). The compound is a colourless, water-soluble liquid. It is used as a solvent and a precursor to other organic compounds.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionitrile#cite_note-Ullmann-7)

The main industrial route to this nitrile is the hydrogenation of [acrylonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrylonitrile "Acrylonitrile"). It is also prepared by the [ammoxidation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammoxidation "Ammoxidation") of [propanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1-propanol "1-propanol") (propionaldehyde can also be used instead):[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionitrile#cite_note-Ullmann-7)

CH 3 CH 2 CH 2 OH + O 2 + NH 3 → CH 3 CH 2 C≡N + 3 H 2 O
Propionitrile is a byproduct of the electrodimerisation of acrylonitrile to [adiponitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adiponitrile "Adiponitrile").

In the laboratory propanenitrile can also be produced by the [dehydration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dehydration_reaction "Dehydration reaction") of [propionamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionamide "Propionamide"), by catalytic reduction of [acrylonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrylonitrile "Acrylonitrile"), or by distilling [ethyl sulfate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethyl_sulfate "Ethyl sulfate") and [potassium cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potassium_cyanide "Potassium cyanide").

Propionitrile is a solvent similar to [acetonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetonitrile "Acetonitrile") but with a slightly higher boiling point. It is a precursor to propylamines by hydrogenation. It is a C-3 building block in the preparation of the drug [flopropione](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flopropione "Flopropione") by the [Houben-Hoesch reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Houben-Hoesch_reaction "Houben-Hoesch reaction").

The nitrile aldol reaction with [benzophenone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benzophenone "Benzophenone"), followed by reduction of the nitrile with lithium aluminium hydride gives [2-MDP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2-MDP "2-MDP"). This agent possesses [appetite suppressant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Appetite_suppressant "Appetite suppressant") and antidepressant properties.

[![Image 18](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/2-MDP.svg/250px-2-MDP.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:2-MDP.svg)

 Chemical structure of [2-MDP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2-MDP "2-MDP")

The toxicity [LD 50](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Median_lethal_dose "Median lethal dose") of propionitrile is listed as 39 mg/kg[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionitrile#cite_note-NIOSH-8) and as 230 my (both rats, oral).[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionitrile#cite_note-Ullmann-7)

In 1979, the Kalama (Vega) plant in [Beaufort](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beaufort,_South_Carolina "Beaufort, South Carolina"), [South Carolina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Carolina "South Carolina") experienced an explosion during the production of propionitrile by nickel-catalyzed hydrogenation of acrylonitrile.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionitrile#cite_note-EPA-9) This site is now one of the two [Superfund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superfund "Superfund") cleanup sites in South Carolina.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionitrile#cite_note-EPA-9)
