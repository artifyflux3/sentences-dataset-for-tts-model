Jean Meeus (born 12 December 1928) is a Belgian meteorologist and amateur astronomer specializing in celestial mechanics , spherical astronomy , and mathematical astronomy.

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] Meeus studied mathematics at the University of Leuven in Belgium , where he received the Degree of Licentiate in 1953. From then until his retirement in 1993, he was a meteorologist at Brussels Airport .

[ 2 ] Awards and honors [ edit ] In 1986, he won the Amateur Achievement Award of the Astronomical Society of the Pacific .

[ 3 ] The main belt asteroid 2213 Meeus was named after him by the International Astronomical Union in 1981 for his contributions to the field.
