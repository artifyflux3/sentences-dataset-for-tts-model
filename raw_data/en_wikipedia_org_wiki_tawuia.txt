Fossil specimen, Geological Museum of China Scientific classification Domain: Eukaryota Kingdom: incertae sedis Genus: † Tawuia H.J.Hofmann , 1979 Reconstruction as a slime mold-like organism A reconstruction of Tawuia as a coenocytic macroalgae with epibionts.

Tawuia is a millimetric disc or sausage-shaped, most likely multicellular (although coenocytic ) macrofossil from the Proterozoic .

Stratigraphically , they range from 1,630 million years ago [ 1 ] to the early Cambrian .

[ 2 ] The fossils are often preserved as organic compressions. They are sometimes considered to represent microbial structures; [ 3 ] some authors affiliate them with slime molds .

[ 3 ] However, Tawuia is instead likely an alga , as sub-cellular structures supporting this have been preserved.
