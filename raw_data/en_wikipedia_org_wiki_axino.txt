Peccei–Quinn theory attempts to explain the observed phenomenon known as the strong CP problem by introducing a hypothetical real scalar particle called the axion .  Adding supersymmetry to the model predicts the existence of a fermionic superpartner for the axion, the axino, and a bosonic superpartner, the saxion . They are all bundled up in a chiral superfield .

The axino has been predicted to be the lightest supersymmetric particle in such a model.

[ 1 ] In part due to this property, it is considered a candidate for the composition of dark matter .

[ 2 ] The supermultiplet containing an axion and axino has been suggested as the origin of supersymmetry breaking, where the supermultiplet gains an F-term expectation value.
