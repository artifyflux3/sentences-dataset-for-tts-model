**Rebecca Claire Hill** (born 14 February 1994)[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-facebook-1) is an English singer and songwriter. She rose to fame after appearing on the first series of _[The Voice UK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Voice\_UK "The Voice UK")_, auditioning with [John Legend](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Legend "John Legend")'s "[Ordinary People](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordinary_People_(John_Legend_song) "Ordinary People (John Legend song)")". She joined [Jessie J](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jessie_J "Jessie J")'s team and reached the semi-final of the competition.

In June 2014, she became the first (and only thus far) _The Voice UK_ contestant to score a UK number one single when the song "[Gecko (Overdrive)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gecko_(song)#Gecko_(Overdrive) "Gecko (song)")" with [Oliver Heldens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oliver_Heldens "Oliver Heldens") topped the [UK Singles Chart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart "UK Singles Chart").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-:0-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-5) In 2022, she received two [Brit Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/42nd_Brit_Awards "42nd Brit Awards") nominations, including [British Song of the Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brit_Award_for_British_Single "Brit Award for British Single") for "[Remember](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remember_(Becky_Hill_and_David_Guetta_song) "Remember (Becky Hill and David Guetta song)")" with [David Guetta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Guetta "David Guetta"), and [Best Dance Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brit_Award_for_British_Dance_Act "Brit Award for British Dance Act"), which she won two years in a row in 2022 and 2023.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-6)

Becky Hill was born on 14 February 1994. She began making music at the age of 14 and started performing in a band called Shaking Trees ‚Äì receiving [BBC Introducing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Introducing "BBC Introducing") support from Andrew Marston at [BBC Hereford & Worcester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Hereford_%26_Worcester "BBC Hereford & Worcester"), who selected her band to perform at the Nozstock Festival of Performing Arts,[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-7) as well as a live session for Adam Green and Josh Withey at [BBC Radio Shropshire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Radio_Shropshire "BBC Radio Shropshire").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-8)

Hill has appeared in a number of live performances for YouTube, including a web series called _The Rebelations_. She auditioned for the [first series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Voice_UK_(series_1) "The Voice UK (series 1)") of _[The Voice UK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Voice\_UK "The Voice UK")_, and at the blind auditions she performed "[Ordinary People](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordinary_People_(John_Legend_song) "Ordinary People (John Legend song)")". Judges [Jessie J](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jessie_J "Jessie J") and [will.i.am](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will.i.am "Will.i.am"), the only coaches with an available space on their teams, turned around for her. She opted to join Jessie. In the battle rounds she competed against Indie and Pixie performing the song "[Irreplaceable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irreplaceable "Irreplaceable")", she won the battle and progressed to the live shows. She got to the semi-final stage of the competition where she was eliminated.

### 2013‚Äì2017: Breakthrough and _Eko EP_

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Becky_Hill&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: 2013‚Äì2017: Breakthrough and Eko EP")]

Since appearing on _The Voice UK_, versions of [David Guetta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Guetta "David Guetta")'s "[She Wolf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/She_Wolf_(Falling_to_Pieces) "She Wolf (Falling to Pieces)")" and "[Not Giving In](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Not_Giving_In "Not Giving In")" by [Rudimental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudimental "Rudimental") have been published, as have covers of [Paolo Nutini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paolo_Nutini "Paolo Nutini")'s "[Last Request](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Last_Request "Last Request")" and [Alex Clare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alex_Clare "Alex Clare")'s "[Too Close](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Too_Close_(Alex_Clare_song) "Too Close (Alex Clare song)")". Hill featured on [Rudimental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudimental "Rudimental")'s track "Powerless", taken from their debut album _[Home](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home\_(Rudimental\_album) "Home (Rudimental album)")_. She also provided cover for [Ella Eyre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ella_Eyre "Ella Eyre")'s vocals for Rudimental's set at Glastonbury 2013, Bestival 2013, Wakestock 2013 and Lovebox 2013. Hill provided vocals for and co-wrote [Wilkinson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilkinson_(musician) "Wilkinson (musician)")'s song [Afterglow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afterglow_(Wilkinson_song) "Afterglow (Wilkinson song)"),[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-selfblown-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-datatransmission-10) which peaked at number 8 on the [UK Singles Chart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart "UK Singles Chart") in October 2013,[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-officialcharts-11) the song also peaked at number 1 on the [UK Dance Chart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UK_Dance_Chart "UK Dance Chart"). "[Powerless](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Powerless_(Rudimental_song) "Powerless (Rudimental song)")" received a full single release on 23 February 2014. The song peaked at number 73 on the UK Singles Chart. Hill appeared on and co-wrote a vocal version of [Oliver Heldens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oliver_Heldens "Oliver Heldens")' "[Gecko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gecko_(song) "Gecko (song)")", renamed "[Gecko (Overdrive)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gecko_(song)#Gecko_(Overdrive) "Gecko (song)")", alongside [MNEK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MNEK "MNEK"). The song topped the UK Singles Chart on 29 June 2014 thus making her the first _The Voice UK_ contestant to achieve a number 1.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-12)

On 9 November 2014 she released a single, "[Losing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Losing_(Becky_Hill_song) "Losing (Becky Hill song)")", which was produced by [MNEK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MNEK "MNEK") and intended to be the lead single from her solo album with then label [Parlophone Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parlophone_Records "Parlophone Records"). The single debuted at number 56 on the [UK Singles Chart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart "UK Singles Chart").[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-13) Hill co-wrote Australian recording artist [Reigan Derry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reigan_Derry "Reigan Derry")'s debut single "[All of the Pieces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/All_of_the_Pieces "All of the Pieces")".[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-14) In July 2015, [French house](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_house "French house") producer [Waterm√§t](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waterm%C3%A4t "Waterm√§t") released a vocal version of his track "Frequency" with Hill as joint lead artist. The track was renamed to "All My Love".[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-:0-4)

In early 2016, Hill announced that she was songwriting with [CocknBullKid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CocknBullKid "CocknBullKid"). In May 2016 she released her first proper single in two years, entitled "Back to My Love", featuring vocals from rapper [Little Simz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Little_Simz "Little Simz"). It was said to be the lead single off of her debut album, but was later included in the _Eko EP_, released in August 2017. During the summer, she collaborated with a number of artists. Her first was with DJ and record producer [MK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marc_Kinchen "Marc Kinchen") on his single "[Piece of Me](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piece_of_Me_(MK_and_Becky_Hill_song) "Piece of Me (MK and Becky Hill song)")". The second was with [Matoma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matoma "Matoma") on his breakthrough single "[False Alarm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/False_Alarm_(Matoma_and_Becky_Hill_song) "False Alarm (Matoma and Becky Hill song)")". In the late months of 2016, she announced a second single from her EP, entitled "Warm". It was co-written with [MNEK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MNEK "MNEK") and produced by [Shift K3Y](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shift_K3Y "Shift K3Y"). In the end of March 2017, she released a third single, entitled "Rude Love". The song was co-written and produced by MNEK. The music video was released in April alongside the launch of her official VEVO channel on [YouTube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/YouTube "YouTube").

In May 2017, Hill signed a worldwide publishing deal with Sony/ATV and also guest presented an episode of [CBBC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CBBC_(TV_channel) "CBBC (TV channel)")'s _[The Playlist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Playlist "The Playlist")_. Following from this, Hill released a fourth single "Unpredictable" from the _Eko EP_, along with the EP itself in August 2017 as the final release from her own independent label Eko Music Limited.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-Polydor-15) "Unpredictable" was co-written by [Karen Poole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karen_Poole "Karen Poole") and MNEK, who also produced the track.

### 2017‚Äì2019: _Get to Know_

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Becky_Hill&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: 2017‚Äì2019: Get to Know")]

In August 2017 she signed a worldwide record deal with [Polydor Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polydor_Records "Polydor Records") and expected to release her debut album in 2019.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-Polydor-15) A single, "Sunrise in the East", released in 2018, was treated as the lead cut from her supposedly rejuvenated debut project.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-16) Not long after, she appeared on songs including "[Back & Forth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Back_%26_Forth_(MK,_Jonas_Blue_and_Becky_Hill_song) "Back & Forth (MK, Jonas Blue and Becky Hill song)")", alongside MK and [Jonas Blue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonas_Blue "Jonas Blue"). This continued in the following year with "[I Could Get Used to This](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I_Could_Get_Used_to_This "I Could Get Used to This")", with Weiss, and finally "[Wish You Well](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wish_You_Well_(Sigala_and_Becky_Hill_song) "Wish You Well (Sigala and Becky Hill song)")", with [Sigala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sigala "Sigala"), which became her first top 10 hit in the UK Charts since "Gecko (Overdrive)". She was one of seven co-writers of the [Europa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europa_(musical_duo) "Europa (musical duo)") song "[All Day and Night](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/All_Day_and_Night "All Day and Night")", which was her composer credit to make it to the top 10 in the UK Charts. On 6 July 2019, Hill performed at [Pride in London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pride_in_London "Pride in London") on the Trafalgar Square stage. A "mini-album", _[Get to Know](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Get\_to\_Know "Get to Know")_, with four new tracks, was released in September 2019.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-17) The four tracks were from an intended EP, _Fickle Emotions_, that had not been released.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-18)

On 6 December 2019, Hill released a cover of the [Yazoo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yazoo_(band) "Yazoo (band)") song "[Only You](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Only_You_(Yazoo_song) "Only You (Yazoo song)")", dedicated to her grandfather, who had introduced her to [The Flying Pickets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Flying_Pickets "The Flying Pickets")' cover version of the song when she was young.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-19)

### 2020‚Äì2022: _Only Honest on the Weekend_

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Becky_Hill&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: 2020‚Äì2022: Only Honest on the Weekend")]

At the beginning of 2020, Hill supported Irish pop rock band [The Script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Script "The Script") on their Sunsets & Full Moons tour in the UK and Ireland and performed some of her songs from _Get to Know_. In late March of that year, the rest of the dates were cancelled due to the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic").

She released the next singles from her debut album _[Only Honest on the Weekend](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Only\_Honest\_on\_the\_Weekend "Only Honest on the Weekend")_; "[Better Off Without You](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Better_Off_Without_You_(Becky_Hill_and_Shift_K3Y_song) "Better Off Without You (Becky Hill and Shift K3Y song)")" with [Shift K3Y](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shift_K3Y "Shift K3Y") and "[Heaven on My Mind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heaven_on_My_Mind_(song) "Heaven on My Mind (song)")" with Sigala, both of which reached number fourteen on the UK Singles Chart. Follow up single "[Space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_(Becky_Hill_song) "Space (Becky Hill song)")" peaked at seventy-nine, though this later did not make the final tracklist.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-iTunes-20)

In April, she collaborated with [Tiesto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiesto "Tiesto") on the song "[Nothing Really Matters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nothing_Really_Matters_(Becky_Hill_and_Ti%C3%ABsto_song) "Nothing Really Matters (Becky Hill and Ti√´sto song)")" which was later released through Tiesto's seventh album _[The London Sessions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_London\_Sessions\_(Ti%C3%ABsto\_album) "The London Sessions (Ti√´sto album)")_, where she featured on another song called "Over You". In June, she premiered a Spotify podcast called "The Art of Rave" with features such as [Pete Tong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pete_Tong "Pete Tong"), [Sister Bliss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sister_Bliss "Sister Bliss") and [DJ Zinc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DJ_Zinc "DJ Zinc").

In November, she covered [Alphaville's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphaville_(band) "Alphaville (band)") "[Forever Young](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forever_Young_(Alphaville_song)#Becky_Hill_version "Forever Young (Alphaville song)")" for the UK's [McDonald's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McDonald%27s "McDonald's") Christmas advert.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-21) The track became her first song without a joint credit to reach the UK Top 40, when it peaked at number thirty-five.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-22)

Beginning in 2021, Hill released the singles "Last Time" and "[Remember](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remember_(Becky_Hill_and_David_Guetta_song) "Remember (Becky Hill and David Guetta song)")" with French DJ [David Guetta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Guetta "David Guetta").[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-23) They peaked at number thirty-nine and number three respectively, the latter becoming Hill's fourth top-ten hit.

On 15 July 2021, Hill teased the release of her debut album, _Only Honest on the Weekend_, with a trailer on her Instagram featuring previously unreleased songs "[My Heart Goes (La Di Da)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/My_Heart_Goes_(La_Di_Da) "My Heart Goes (La Di Da)")" with [Topic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topic_(DJ) "Topic (DJ)") and "Through the Night" with [220 Kid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/220_Kid "220 Kid"). The tracklist was later confirmed in full by [Official Charts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Official_Charts "Official Charts").[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-24) The album was originally planned to be released on 20 August 2021. Hill explained that the album would be delayed 1 week, and released on 27 August 2021 to let "[My Heart Goes (La Di Da)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/My_Heart_Goes_(La_Di_Da) "My Heart Goes (La Di Da)")" shine on the UK charts. Released as the final single from the album, the song spent four weeks at number 11 becoming Hill's ninth top-20 single.

Additionally, on 25 August 2021, days before the release of _Only Honest on the Weekend_, a "[Kiss My (Uh Oh)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kiss_My_(Uh_Oh) "Kiss My (Uh Oh)")" _Girl Power Remix_ was released that Hill featured on along with [Anne-Marie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anne-Marie "Anne-Marie"), [Little Mix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Little_Mix "Little Mix"), [Raye](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raye "Raye") and [Stefflon Don](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stefflon_Don "Stefflon Don").

On 25 January 2022, Hill announced that she will release a song with Wilkinson 9 years after their previous hit "Afterglow". The song is called "Here For You" and it was released on 11 February 2022. She later released "Run", a collaboration with Swedish EDM duo [Galantis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galantis "Galantis") was released on 25 February 2022.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-25) This was followed by a deluxe edition of her debut album _Only Honest on the Weekend_. It included singles "Space", "Run" with Galantis, "Hold On" with Netsky and "Here For You" with Wilkinson, the song "Personally" and the acoustic version of "Remember".

On 8 April 2022, Hill collaborated with David Guetta and [Ella Henderson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ella_Henderson "Ella Henderson") on the song "[Crazy What Love Can Do](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crazy_What_Love_Can_Do "Crazy What Love Can Do")".[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-26) In July 2022, she performed at the closing ceremony of [UEFA Women's Euro 2022](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA_Women%27s_Euro_2022 "UEFA Women's Euro 2022") at [Wembley Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wembley_Stadium "Wembley Stadium"), after which she was [trolled](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_troll "Internet troll") online due to the silver leotard she wore for the performance.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-27) She also performed during the [beach volleyball events](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beach_volleyball_at_the_2022_Commonwealth_Games "Beach volleyball at the 2022 Commonwealth Games") at the [2022 Commonwealth Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_Commonwealth_Games "2022 Commonwealth Games") in Birmingham.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-28)

On 5 August 2022, Hill collaborated with British DJ [Joel Corry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joel_Corry "Joel Corry") on the song "History".

### 2023‚Äìpresent: _Believe Me Now?_

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Becky_Hill&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: 2023‚Äìpresent: Believe Me Now?")]

On 2 May 2023, Hill announced the lead single "Side Effects" released on 12 May 2023, a collaboration with Lewis Thompson from her upcoming second studio album; the song reached 35 on the UK Singles Chart. She later confirmed the album was slated for release in 2024.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-auto-29)

On 14 July 2023, Hill released "[Disconnect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disconnect_(Becky_Hill_and_Chase_%26_Status_song) "Disconnect (Becky Hill and Chase & Status song)")" with English DJ duo [Chase & Status](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chase_%26_Status "Chase & Status") as the second single from her upcoming second album.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-30) The song became her sixth top ten hit in the UK.

In January 2024, Hill toured New Zealand and Australia for the first time, performing shows in Auckland, Melbourne and Sydney. While on tour, she released the single "[Never Be Alone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Never_Be_Alone_(Becky_Hill_and_Sonny_Fodera_song) "Never Be Alone (Becky Hill and Sonny Fodera song)")", which features Australian-born DJ [Sonny Fodera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonny_Fodera "Sonny Fodera").[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-31)[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-32)

In April 2021, Hill came out as [queer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queer "Queer") on her Twitter account, stating "I've definitely felt uncomfortable branding myself as straight, or anything else for that matter, but queer seems to be the most fitting identity for who I am".[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-33) In January 2022, Hill announced her engagement to her long-term boyfriend.[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-Engagement-34)

She is a supporter of [Walsall F.C.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walsall_F.C. "Walsall F.C.")[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Becky_Hill#cite_note-35)
