SI units 41 840 J/m 2 The langley (Ly) is a unit of heat transmission, especially used to express the rate of solar radiation (or insolation ) received by the earth. The unit was proposed by Franz Linke in 1942 [ 1 ] and named after Samuel Langley (1834–1906) in 1947.
