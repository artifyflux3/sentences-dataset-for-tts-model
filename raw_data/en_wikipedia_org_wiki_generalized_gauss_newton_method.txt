Find sources: "Generalized Gauss–Newton method" – news · newspapers · books · scholar · JSTOR ( January 2017 ) ( Learn how and when to remove this message ) The generalized Gauss–Newton method is a generalization of the least-squares method originally described by Carl Friedrich Gauss and of Newton's method due to Isaac Newton to the case of constrained nonlinear least-squares problems.

[ 1 ] References [ edit ] ^ Golub, G. H.; Pereyra, V. (1973), "The differentiation of pseudo-inverses and nonlinear least squares problems whose variables separate", SIAM Journal on Numerical Analysis , 10 (2): 413– 432, doi : 10.1137/0710036 , MR 0336980 .
