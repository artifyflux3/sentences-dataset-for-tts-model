Take a circle of radius 1.

Inscribe a regular triangle in this circle. Inscribe a circle in this triangle. Inscribe a square in it. Inscribe a circle, regular pentagon , circle, regular hexagon and so forth. 
The radius of the limiting circle is called the Kepler–Bouwkamp constant.

[ 1 ] It is named after Johannes Kepler and Christoffel Jacob Bouwkamp [ de ] , and is the inverse of the polygon circumscribing constant .
