[ 1 ] With background allele frequency p the allele frequencies, in sub-populations separated by Wright's F ST F , are distributed according to independent draws from B ( 1 − − F F p , 1 − − F F ( 1 − − p ) ) {\displaystyle B\left({\frac {1-F}{F}}p,{\frac {1-F}{F}}(1-p)\right)} where B is the Beta distribution . This distribution has mean p and variance Fp (1 – p ).

[ 2 ] The model is due to David Balding and Richard Nichols and is widely used in the forensic analysis of DNA profiles and in population models for genetic epidemiology .
