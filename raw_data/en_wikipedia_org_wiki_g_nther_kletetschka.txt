**Günther Kletetschka** is a multidisciplinary scientist specializing in [geophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geophysics "Geophysics"), [astrophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrophysics "Astrophysics"), and [fundamental interactions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fundamental_interaction "Fundamental interaction"). He is a Senior Research Professor at the [Geophysical Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geophysical_Institute "Geophysical Institute") of the [University of Alaska Fairbanks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Alaska_Fairbanks "University of Alaska Fairbanks") and an Assistant Professor at the Institute of Hydrogeology of [Charles University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_University "Charles University") in [Prague](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prague "Prague"), [Czech Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czech_Republic "Czech Republic").

In November 2024, Dr. Kletetschka propounded that time exists in three [dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimension "Dimension"), and that our [three spatial dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_spatial_dimensions "Three spatial dimensions") emerge from those [three temporal dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_temporal_dimensions "Three temporal dimensions"), thus creating our [universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universe "Universe") of [six-dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six-dimensions "Six-dimensions") with [matter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matter "Matter"), [energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy "Energy"), and their [fundamental interactions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fundamental_interactions "Fundamental interactions").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-3DT-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-CTP-2) The Kletetschka mathematical framework provides a possible way to reconcile the [quantum physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_physics "Quantum physics") of [subatomic particles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subatomic_particle "Subatomic particle"), the [Newtonian physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newtonian_physics "Newtonian physics") of our everyday lives, and the [relativistic physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relativistic_physics "Relativistic physics") of the [cosmos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmos "Cosmos").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-Phys-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-Visegr%C3%A1d-4)

Early life and career
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Early life and career")]

Kletetschka was born in [Litoměřice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Litom%C4%9B%C5%99ice "Litoměřice"), [Czechoslovakia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czechoslovakia "Czechoslovakia"), in 1964. In 1989, he received the Bc degree in geophysics from [Charles University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_University "Charles University") in Prague, Czechoslovakia. During his studies from 1987 to 1991, he worked as a research and teaching assistant. Immigrating to the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States") in 1991, Kletetschka received the MS degree in 1994 and the PhD degree in geology and geophysics in 1998 from the [University of Minnesota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Minnesota "University of Minnesota"). During his studies from 1991 to 1998, he served as a teaching and research assistant.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-CV-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-UK-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-ORCID-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-EC-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-AC-9)

Following his doctoral studies, Kletetschka pursued a wide variety of activities around the world in [climate science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_science "Climate science"), geophysics, [hydrogeology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogeology "Hydrogeology"), [aerospace engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aerospace_engineering "Aerospace engineering"), [magnetics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetics "Magnetics"), astrophysics, and [medical research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medical_research "Medical research"). From 1998 to 2000, he was named a [National Research Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Research_Council_(United_States) "National Research Council (United States)") Postdoctoral Research Associate. Kletetschka served as a contractor for the [Goddard Space Flight Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goddard_Space_Flight_Center "Goddard Space Flight Center") Laboratory of Terrestrial Physics from 1998 to 2002, and the Laboratory of Extraterrestrial Physics until 2004. From 2000 to 2002, he served as a Research Associate at the [Catholic University of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_University_of_America "Catholic University of America"). In 2002, Kletetschka was a Lecturer at [Montgomery College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montgomery_College "Montgomery College"), and from 2002 to 2003, he served as a Postdoctoral Research Associate at [Howard University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Howard_University "Howard University").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-CV-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-UK-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-ORCID-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-EC-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-AC-9)

From 2003 to 2023, Kletetschka was named a Researcher at the Institute of Geology of the [Academy of Sciences of the Czech Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academy_of_Sciences_of_the_Czech_Republic "Academy of Sciences of the Czech Republic"). In 2023, he was also named a Research Scholar at the [University of Delaware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Delaware "University of Delaware")[Bartol Research Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bartol_Research_Institute "Bartol Research Institute"). From 2003 to 2011, Kletetschka was appointed a Research Professor at the Catholic University of America Institute of Astrophysics and Computational Sciences. From 2005 to 2011, he served as a contractor for the Goddard Space Flight Center Solar System Exploration team. From 2011 to 2015, Kletetschka was a Guest Researcher for the [National Institute of Standards and Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Institute_of_Standards_and_Technology "National Institute of Standards and Technology") Magnetic group in [Gaithersburg, Maryland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaithersburg,_Maryland "Gaithersburg, Maryland"). From 2013 to 2015, he was a Guest Researcher at the [Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lawrence_Berkeley_National_Laboratory "Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory") Nuclear Science group in [Berkeley, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berkeley,_California "Berkeley, California").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-CV-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-UK-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-ORCID-7)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-RG-10)

In June 2011, Kletetschka was appointed Research Scientist at the Charles University Institute of Hydrogeology, a position he continues to hold. In August 2016, he was appointed Associate Research Professor at the Geophysical Institute of the University of Alaska Fairbanks.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-CV-5)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-ORCID-7)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-RG-10)

Dr. Kleteschka is widely published.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-ORCID-7)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-RG-10)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-GS-11)

Six-dimensional universe
------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Six-dimensional universe")]

In November 2024, Dr. Kletetschka propounded his [six-dimensional universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six-dimensional_universe "Six-dimensional universe") of [three temporal dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_temporal_dimensions "Three temporal dimensions") and [three spatial dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_spatial_dimensions "Three spatial dimensions") in a paper entitled _Three-Dimensional Time: A Mathematical Framework for Fundamental Physics_.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-3DT-1) Kletetschka proposed numerous experiments to test his mathematical framework.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-3DT-1) Although other scientists had previously proposed [six-dimensional universes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six-dimensional_universe "Six-dimensional universe"), Kletetschka's mathematical framework maintains [causality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Causality "Causality"), accurately calculates [fermion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermion "Fermion") masses, and makes specific predictions for the discovery of [quantum gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_gravity "Quantum gravity").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-Phys-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-Visegr%C3%A1d-4)

In March 2025, Kletetschka further propounded that electrical charge is a [topological property](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topological_property "Topological property") of [three-dimensional time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-dimensional_time "Three-dimensional time") in a paper entitled _Charge as a Topological Property in Three-Dimensional Time_.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Kletetschka#cite_note-CTP-2) This paper further elaborates on three-dimensional time and [electromagnetic fields](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetic_field "Electromagnetic field").
