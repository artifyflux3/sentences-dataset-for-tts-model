The **International Standard Book Number** (**ISBN**) is a numeric commercial [book](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book "Book")[identifier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Identifier "Identifier") that is intended to be unique.[[a]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-2)[[b]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-3) Publishers purchase or receive ISBNs from an affiliate of the International ISBN Agency.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-4)

A different ISBN is assigned to each separate edition and variation of a publication, but not to a simple reprinting of an existing item. For example, an [e-book](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E-book "E-book"), a [paperback](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paperback "Paperback") and a [hardcover](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hardcover "Hardcover") edition of the same [book](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book "Book") must each have a different ISBN, but an unchanged reprint of the hardcover edition keeps the same ISBN. The ISBN is ten digits long if assigned before 2007, and thirteen digits long if assigned on or after 1 January 2007.[[c]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-conversion-6) The method of assigning an ISBN is nation-specific and varies between countries, often depending on how large the publishing industry is within a country.

The first version of the ISBN identification format was devised in 1967, based upon the 9-digit **Standard Book Numbering** (**SBN**) created in 1966. The 10-digit ISBN format was developed by the [International Organization for Standardization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization "International Organization for Standardization") (ISO) and was published in 1970 as international standard ISO 2108 (any 9-digit SBN can be converted to a 10-digit ISBN by prefixing it with a zero).

Privately published books sometimes appear without an ISBN. The International ISBN Agency sometimes assigns ISBNs to such books on its own initiative.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-7)

A separate identifier code of a similar kind, the [International Standard Serial Number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Standard_Serial_Number "International Standard Serial Number") (ISSN), identifies periodical publications such as [magazines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magazine "Magazine") and [newspapers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newspaper "Newspaper"). The [International Standard Music Number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Standard_Music_Number "International Standard Music Number") (ISMN) covers [musical scores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheet_music "Sheet music").

History
-------

The Standard Book Number (SBN) is a commercial system using nine-digit [code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Code "Code") numbers to identify books. In 1965, British bookseller and stationers [WHSmith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WHSmith "WHSmith") announced plans to implement a standard numbering system for its books.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-history-1) They hired consultants to work on their behalf, and the system was devised by [Gordon Foster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gordon_Foster "Gordon Foster"), emeritus professor of statistics at [Trinity College Dublin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trinity_College_Dublin "Trinity College Dublin").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-From_Archive.org-4646971-8) The [International Organization for Standardization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization "International Organization for Standardization") (ISO) Technical Committee on Documentation sought to adapt the British SBN for international use. The ISBN identification format was conceived in 1967 in the United Kingdom by [David Whitaker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Whitaker_(publisher) "David Whitaker (publisher)")[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-ktieb-9)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-10) (regarded as the "Father of the ISBN")[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-niso.org-11) and in 1968 in the United States by Emery Koltay[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-ktieb-9) (who later became director of the U.S. ISBN agency [R. R. Bowker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R._R._Bowker "R. R. Bowker")).[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-niso.org-11)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-12)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-13)

The 10-digit ISBN format was developed by the ISO and was published in 1970 as international standard ISO 2108.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-history-1)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-ktieb-9) The United Kingdom continued to use the nine-digit SBN code until 1974. ISO has appointed the International ISBN Agency as the [registration authority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Registration_authority "Registration authority") for ISBN worldwide and the ISBN Standard is developed under the control of ISO Technical Committee 46/Subcommittee 9 [TC 46/SC 9](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TC_46/SC_9 "TC 46/SC 9"). The ISO on-line facility only refers back to 1978.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-14)

[![Image 2](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Total_Number_of_Registration_ISBN_2020.png/250px-Total_Number_of_Registration_ISBN_2020.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Total_Number_of_Registration_ISBN_2020.png)

Total number of ISBN registrations. 2020.

An SBN may be converted to an ISBN by prefixing the digit "0". For example, the second edition of _[Mr. J. G. Reeder Returns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Guv%27nor\_and\_Other\_Short\_Stories "The Guv'nor and Other Short Stories")_, published by Hodder in 1965, has "SBN 340 01381 8", where "340" indicates the [publisher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Publishing "Publishing"), "01381" is the serial number assigned by the publisher, and "8" is the [check digit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Check_digit "Check digit"). By prefixing a zero, this can be converted to [ISBN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier) "ISBN (identifier)")[0-340-01381-8](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:BookSources/0-340-01381-8 "Special:BookSources/0-340-01381-8"); the check digit does not need to be re-calculated. Some publishers, such as [Ballantine Books](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ballantine_Books "Ballantine Books"), would sometimes use 12-digit SBNs where the last three digits indicated the price of the book;[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-15) for example, _Woodstock Handmade Houses_ had a 12-digit Standard Book Number of 345-24223-8-595 (valid SBN: 345-24223-8, ISBN: 0-345-24223-8),[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-16) and it cost US$5.95.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-17)

Since 1 January 2007, ISBNs have contained thirteen digits, a format that is compatible with "[Bookland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bookland "Bookland")" [European Article Numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Article_Number "European Article Number"), which have 13 digits.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-LAC_FAQ-5) Since 2016, ISBNs have also been used to identify [mobile games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mobile_game "Mobile game") by China's [Administration of Press and Publication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Administration_of_Press_and_Publication "General Administration of Press and Publication").[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-18)

The [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States"), with 3.9 million registered ISBNs in 2020, was by far the biggest user of the ISBN identifier in 2020, followed by the [Republic of Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Korea "South Korea") (329,582), [Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germany "Germany") (284,000), [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") (263,066), the [UK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom "United Kingdom") (188,553) and [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia") (144,793). Lifetime ISBNs registered in the United States are over 39 million as of 2020.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-19)

Overview
--------

A separate ISBN is assigned to each edition and variation (except reprintings) of a publication. For example, an ebook, [audiobook](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Audiobook "Audiobook"), paperback, and hardcover edition of the same book must each have a different ISBN assigned to it.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-7th_edition_Users'_Manual-20): 12  The ISBN of an electronic publication is often colloquially called "[eISBN](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/eISBN "wikt:eISBN")",[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-21)[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-22) however no such notation is present in the documentation. Whether a publication is electronic or print cannot be inferred from the ISBN only. The ISBN is thirteen digits long if assigned on or after 1 January 2007, and ten digits long if assigned before 2007.[[c]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-conversion-6)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-LAC_FAQ-5) An International Standard Book Number consists of four parts (if it is a 10-digit ISBN) or five parts (for a 13-digit ISBN).

Section 5 of the International ISBN Agency's official user manual[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-7th_edition_Users'_Manual-20): 11  describes the structure of the 13-digit ISBN, as follows:

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/ISBN_Details.svg/250px-ISBN_Details.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:ISBN_Details.svg)

The parts of a 10-digit ISBN and the corresponding EAN‑13 and barcode. Note the different check digits in each. The part of the EAN‑13 labeled "EAN" is the [Bookland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bookland "Bookland") country code.

1.   for a 13-digit ISBN, a prefix element – a _[GS1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GS1 "GS1") prefix_: so far 978 or 979 have been made available by GS1,
2.   the _registration group element_ (language-sharing country group, individual country or territory),[[d]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-23)
3.   the _registrant_ element,
4.   the _publication element_, and
5.   a _[checksum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Checksum "Checksum") character_ or [check digit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Check_digit "Check digit").

A 13-digit ISBN can be separated into its parts (_prefix element_, _registration group_, _registrant_, _publication_ and _check digit_), and when this is done it is customary to separate the parts with [hyphens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyphen "Hyphen") or spaces. Separating the parts (_registration group_, _registrant_, _publication_ and _check digit_) of a 10-digit ISBN is also done with either hyphens or spaces. Figuring out how to correctly separate a given ISBN is complicated, because most of the parts do not use a fixed number of digits.[[e]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-25) Online tools such the ISBN Converter provided by the [Library of Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library_of_Congress "Library of Congress") and ISBNBarcode.org can assist with hyphenation and generate standard barcodes.[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-26)[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-27)

### Issuing process

ISBN issuance is country-specific, in that ISBNs are issued by the ISBN registration agency that is responsible for that country or territory regardless of the publication language. The ranges of ISBNs assigned to any particular country are based on the publishing profile of the country concerned, and so the ranges will vary depending on the number of books and the number, type, and size of publishers that are active. Some ISBN registration agencies are based in national libraries or within ministries of culture and thus may receive direct funding from the government to support their services. In other cases, the ISBN registration service is provided by organisations such as bibliographic data providers that are not government funded.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-28)

### Registration group element

The ISBN [registration group element](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_ISBN_identifier_groups "List of ISBN identifier groups") is a 1-to-5-digit number that is valid within a single prefix element (i.e. one of 978 or 979),[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-7th_edition_Users'_Manual-20): 11  and can be separated between hyphens, such as "978-1-...". Registration groups have primarily been allocated within the 978 prefix element.[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-ISBN_Ranges-50) The single-digit registration groups within the 978-prefix element are: 0 or 1 for English-speaking countries; 2 for French-speaking countries; 3 for German-speaking countries; 4 for Japan; 5 for Russian-speaking countries; and 7 for People's Republic of China. Example 5-digit registration groups are 99936 and 99980, for Bhutan. The allocated registration groups are: 0–5, 600–631, 65, 7, 80–94, 950–989, 9910–9989, and 99901–99993.[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-51) Books published in rare languages typically have longer group elements.[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-52)

Within the 979 prefix element, the registration group 0 is reserved for compatibility with [International Standard Music Numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Standard_Music_Number "International Standard Music Number") (ISMNs), but such material is not actually assigned an ISBN.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-6th_edition_Users'_Manual-53) The registration groups within prefix element 979 that have been assigned are 8 for the United States of America, 10 for France, 11 for the Republic of Korea, and 12 for Italy.[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-C4WDefault-5691881-54)

The original 9-digit standard book number (SBN) had no registration group identifier, but prefixing a zero to a 9-digit SBN creates a valid 10-digit ISBN.

### Registrant element

The national ISBN agency assigns the registrant element ([cf.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cf. "Cf.")[Category:ISBN agencies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Category:ISBN_agencies "Category:ISBN agencies")) and an accompanying series of ISBNs within that registrant element to the publisher; the publisher then allocates one of the ISBNs to each of its books. In most countries, a book publisher is not legally required to assign an ISBN, although most large bookstores only handle publications that have ISBNs assigned to them.[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-55)[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-56)[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-57)

The International ISBN Agency maintains the details of over one million ISBN prefixes and publishers in the [Global Register of Publishers](https://grp.isbn-international.org/).[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-58) This database is freely searchable over the internet.

Publishers receive blocks of ISBNs, with larger blocks allotted to publishers expecting to need them; a small publisher may receive ISBNs of one or more digits for the registration group identifier, several digits for the registrant, and a single digit for the publication element. Once that block of ISBNs is used, the publisher may receive another block of ISBNs, with a different registrant element. Consequently, a publisher may have different allotted registrant elements. There also may be more than one registration group identifier used in a country. This might occur once all the registrant elements from a particular registration group have been allocated to publishers.

By using variable block lengths, registration agencies are able to customise the allocations of ISBNs that they make to publishers. For example, a large publisher may be given a block of ISBNs where fewer digits are allocated for the registrant element and many digits are allocated for the publication element; likewise, countries publishing many titles have few allocated digits for the registration group identifier and many for the registrant and publication elements.[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-59) Here are some sample ISBN-10 codes, illustrating block length variations.

Check digits
------------

A [check digit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Check_digit "Check digit") is a form of redundancy check used for [error detection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Error_detection "Error detection"), the decimal equivalent of a binary [check bit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Check_bit "Check bit"). It consists of a single digit computed from the other digits in the number. The method for the 10-digit ISBN is an extension of that for SBNs, so the two systems are compatible; an SBN prefixed with a zero (the 10-digit ISBN) will give the same check digit as the SBN without the zero. The check digit is base eleven, and can be an integer between 0 and 9, or an 'X'. The system for 13-digit ISBNs is not compatible with SBNs and will, in general, give a different check digit from the corresponding 10-digit ISBN, so does not provide the same protection against transposition. This is because the 13-digit code was required to be compatible with the [EAN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Article_Number "European Article Number") format, and hence could not contain the letter 'X'.

### ISBN-10 check digits

According to the 2001 edition of the International ISBN Agency's official user manual,[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-60) the ISBN-10 check digit (which is the last digit of the 10-digit ISBN) must range from 0 to 10 (the symbol 'X' is used for 10), and must be such that the sum of the ten digits, each multiplied by its (integer) weight, descending from 10 to 1, is a multiple of 11. That is, if x i is the i th digit, then x 10 must be chosen such that:

![Image 4: {\displaystyle \sum _{i=1}^{10}(11-i)x_{i}\equiv 0{\pmod {11}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/d84ad16f69d83c0e7c0aa8704d2dd27288fc650b)

For example, for an ISBN-10 of 0-306-40615-2:

![Image 5: {\displaystyle {\begin{aligned}s&=(0\times 10)+(3\times 9)+(0\times 8)+(6\times 7)+(4\times 6)+(0\times 5)+(6\times 4)+(1\times 3)+(5\times 2)+(2\times 1)\\&=0+27+0+42+24+0+24+3+10+2\\&=132=12\times 11.\end{aligned}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/98964d3f60b0fed1f302298b39c224295bc00eed)

Formally, using [modular arithmetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modular_arithmetic "Modular arithmetic"), this is rendered

![Image 6: {\displaystyle (10x_{1}+9x_{2}+8x_{3}+7x_{4}+6x_{5}+5x_{6}+4x_{7}+3x_{8}+2x_{9}+x_{10})\equiv 0{\pmod {11}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/d26f3565c29f48292f653bcc0d6fb729528d18c1)

It is also true for ISBN 10s that the sum of all ten digits, each multiplied by its weight in _ascending_ order from 1 to 10, is a multiple of 11. For this example:

![Image 7: {\displaystyle {\begin{aligned}s&=(0\times 1)+(3\times 2)+(0\times 3)+(6\times 4)+(4\times 5)+(0\times 6)+(6\times 7)+(1\times 8)+(5\times 9)+(2\times 10)\\&=0+6+0+24+20+0+42+8+45+20\\&=165=15\times 11.\end{aligned}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/8c4fe8374d8f3820ab1e34cf651376955361c4b0)

Formally, this is rendered

![Image 8: {\displaystyle (x_{1}+2x_{2}+3x_{3}+4x_{4}+5x_{5}+6x_{6}+7x_{7}+8x_{8}+9x_{9}+10x_{10})\equiv 0{\pmod {11}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/da98464b376c0a9398076342abdc3865e6963d92)

The two most common errors in handling an ISBN (e.g. when typing it or writing it down) are a single altered digit or the transposition of adjacent digits. It can be proven mathematically that all pairs of valid ISBN 10s differ in at least two digits. It can also be proven that there are no pairs of valid ISBN 10s with eight identical digits and two transposed digits (these proofs are true because the ISBN is less than eleven digits long and because 11 is a [prime number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_number "Prime number")). The ISBN check digit method therefore ensures that it will always be possible to detect these two most common types of error, i.e., if either of these types of error has occurred, the result will never be a valid ISBN—the sum of the digits multiplied by their weights will never be a multiple of 11. However, if the error were to occur in the publishing house and remain undetected, the book would be issued with an invalid ISBN.[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-61)

In contrast, it is possible for other types of error, such as two altered non-transposed digits, or three altered digits, to result in a valid ISBN (although it is still unlikely).

### ISBN-10 check digit calculation

Each of the first nine digits of the 10-digit ISBN—excluding the check digit itself—is multiplied by its (integer) weight, descending from 10 to 2, and the sum of these nine products found. The value of the check digit is simply the one number between 0 and 10 which, when added to this sum, means the total is a multiple of 11.

For example, the check digit for an ISBN-10 of 0-306-40615-_?_ is calculated as follows:

![Image 9: {\displaystyle {\begin{aligned}s&=(0\times 10)+(3\times 9)+(0\times 8)+(6\times 7)+(4\times 6)+(0\times 5)+(6\times 4)+(1\times 3)+(5\times 2)\\&=130.\end{aligned}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/fa18e800f8a57d90ff6cba4dfc097ad942e56134)

Adding 2 to 130 gives a multiple of 11 (because 132 = 12×11)—this is the only number between 0 and 10 which does so. Therefore, the check digit has to be 2, and the complete sequence is ISBN 0-306-40615-2. If the value of ![Image 10: {\displaystyle x_{10}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/c6f81393481a94be9cca20430d468620e63cdb75) required to satisfy this condition is 10, then an 'X' should be used.

Alternatively, [modular arithmetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modular_arithmetic "Modular arithmetic") is convenient for calculating the check digit using modulus 11. The [remainder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remainder "Remainder") of this sum when it is divided by 11 (i.e. its value modulo 11), is computed. This remainder plus the check digit must equal either 0 or 11. Therefore, the check digit is (11 minus the remainder of the sum of the products modulo 11) modulo 11. Taking the remainder modulo 11 a second time accounts for the possibility that the first remainder is 0. Without the second modulo operation, the calculation could result in a check digit value of 11 − 0 = 11, which is invalid. (Strictly speaking, the _first_ "modulo 11" is not needed, but it may be considered to simplify the calculation.)

For example, the check digit for the ISBN of 0-306-40615-_?_ is calculated as follows:

![Image 11: {\displaystyle {\begin{aligned}s&=(11-(((0\times 10)+(3\times 9)+(0\times 8)+(6\times 7)+(4\times 6)+(0\times 5)+(6\times 4)+(1\times 3)+(5\times 2))\,{\bmod {\,}}11))\,{\bmod {\,}}11\\&=(11-((0+27+0+42+24+0+24+3+10)\,{\bmod {\,}}11))\,{\bmod {\,}}11\\&=(11-((130)\,{\bmod {\,}}11))\,{\bmod {\,}}11\\&=(11-(9))\,{\bmod {\,}}11\\&=2\,{\bmod {\,}}11\\&=2\end{aligned}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/1f9879648e1c7a74242708957e8d346ec25e59ef)

Thus the check digit is 2.

It is possible to avoid the multiplications in a software implementation by using two accumulators. Repeatedly adding `t` into `s` computes the necessary multiples:

// Returns ISBN error syndrome, zero for a valid ISBN, non-zero for an invalid one.
// digits[i] must be between 0 and 10.
int CheckISBN(int const digits[10]) {
 int i, s = 0, t = 0;

 for (i = 0; i < 10; ++i) {
 t += digits[i];
 s += t;
 }
 return s % 11;
}

The modular reduction can be done once at the end, as shown above (in which case `s` could hold a value as large as 496, for the invalid ISBN 99999-999-9-X), or `s` and `t` could be reduced by a conditional subtract after each addition.

### ISBN-13 check digit calculation

Appendix 1 of the International ISBN Agency's official user manual[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-7th_edition_Users'_Manual-20): 33  describes how the 13-digit ISBN check digit is calculated. The ISBN-13 check digit, which is the last digit of the ISBN, must range from 0 to 9 and must be such that the sum of all the thirteen digits, each multiplied by its (integer) weight, alternating between 1 and 3, is a multiple of [10](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/10_(number) "10 (number)"). As ISBN-13 is a subset of [EAN-13](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Article_Number#Check_digit "International Article Number"), the algorithm for calculating the check digit is exactly the same for both.

Formally, using [modular arithmetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modular_arithmetic "Modular arithmetic"), this is rendered:

![Image 12: {\displaystyle (x_{1}+3x_{2}+x_{3}+3x_{4}+x_{5}+3x_{6}+x_{7}+3x_{8}+x_{9}+3x_{10}+x_{11}+3x_{12}+x_{13})\equiv 0{\pmod {10}}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/bc96dc5786774a9f0ccc50e52e63e4baeff61572)

The calculation of an ISBN-13 check digit begins with the first twelve digits of the 13-digit ISBN (thus excluding the check digit itself). Each digit, from left to right, is alternately multiplied by 1 or 3, then those products are summed [modulo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modular_arithmetic "Modular arithmetic") 10 to give a value ranging from 0 to 9. Subtracted from 10, that leaves a result from 1 to 10. A zero replaces a ten, so, in all cases, a single check digit results.

For example, the ISBN-13 check digit of 978-0-306-40615-_?_ is calculated as follows:

s = 9×1 + 7×3 + 8×1 + 0×3 + 3×1 + 0×3 + 6×1 + 4×3 + 0×1 + 6×3 + 1×1 + 5×3
  =   9 +  21 +   8 +   0 +   3 +   0 +   6 +  12 +   0 +  18 +   1 +  15
  = 93
93 / 10 = 9 remainder 3
10 –  3 = 7

Thus, the check digit is 7, and the complete sequence is ISBN 978-0-306-40615-7.

In general, the ISBN check digit is calculated as follows.

Let

![Image 13: {\displaystyle r=10-{\big (}{\big (}x_{1}+3x_{2}+x_{3}+3x_{4}+\cdots +x_{11}+3x_{12}{\big )}{\bmod {1}}0{\big )}.}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f481fc859d2a2cc8ac806490b53b7453f8c4278f)

Then

![Image 14: {\displaystyle x_{13}={\begin{cases}r,&r<10,\\0,&r=10.\end{cases}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/27cbad9a7a0447520bea37c0e1482e30021fcf0f)

This check system—similar to the [UPC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_Product_Code "Universal Product Code") check digit formula—does not catch all errors of adjacent digit transposition. Specifically, if the difference between two adjacent digits is 5, the check digit will not catch their transposition. For instance, the above example allows this situation with the 6 followed by a 1. The correct order contributes 3 × 6 + 1 × 1 = 19 to the sum; while, if the digits are transposed (1 followed by a 6), the contribution of those two digits will be 3 × 1 + 1 × 6 = 9. However, 19 and 9 are congruent modulo 10, and so produce the same, final result: both ISBNs will have a check digit of 7. The ISBN-10 formula uses the [prime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_number "Prime number") modulus 11 which avoids this blind spot, but requires more than the digits 0–9 to express the check digit.

Additionally, if the sum of the 2nd, 4th, 6th, 8th, 10th, and 12th digits is tripled then added to the remaining digits (1st, 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, 11th, and 13th), the total will always be divisible by 10 (i.e., end in 0).

### ISBN-10 to ISBN-13 conversion

A 10-digit ISBN is converted to a 13-digit ISBN by prepending "978" to the ISBN-10 and recalculating the final checksum digit using the ISBN-13 algorithm. The reverse process can also be performed, but not for numbers commencing with a prefix other than 978, which have no 10-digit equivalent.

### Errors in usage

[Publishers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Publishing "Publishing") and [libraries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library "Library") have varied policies about the use of the ISBN check digit. Publishers sometimes fail to check the correspondence of a book title and its ISBN before publishing it; that failure causes book identification problems for libraries, booksellers, and readers.[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-62) For example, [ISBN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier) "ISBN (identifier)")[0-590-76484-5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:BookSources/0-590-76484-5 "Special:BookSources/0-590-76484-5") is shared by two books—_Ninja gaiden: a novel based on the best-selling game by Tecmo_ (1990) and _Wacky laws_ (1997), both published by [Scholastic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scholastic_Corporation "Scholastic Corporation").

Most libraries and booksellers display the book record for an invalid ISBN issued by the publisher. The Library of Congress catalogue contains books published with invalid ISBNs, which it usually tags with the phrase "Cancelled ISBN".[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-63) The International Union Library Catalog ([WorldCat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WorldCat "WorldCat")[OCLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OCLC "OCLC")—Online Computer Library Center system) often indexes by invalid ISBNs, if the book is indexed in that way by a member library.[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-64)

EAN format used in barcodes, and upgrading
------------------------------------------

The [barcodes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barcode "Barcode") on a book's back cover (or inside a mass-market [paperback](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paperback "Paperback") book's front cover) are [EAN-13](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Article_Number "European Article Number"); they may have a separate barcode encoding five digits called an [EAN-5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EAN-5 "EAN-5") for the [currency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Currency "Currency") and the [recommended retail price](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recommended_retail_price "Recommended retail price").[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-65) For 10-digit ISBNs, the number "978", the [Bookland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bookland "Bookland") "country code", is prefixed to the ISBN in the barcode data, and the check digit is recalculated according to the EAN-13 formula (modulo 10, 1× and 3× weighting on alternating digits).

Partly because of an expected shortage in certain ISBN categories, the [International Organization for Standardization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization "International Organization for Standardization") (ISO) decided to migrate to a 13-digit ISBN (ISBN-13). The process began on 1 January 2005 and was planned to conclude on 1 January 2007.[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-66) As of 2011, all the 13-digit ISBNs began with 978. As the 978 ISBN supply is exhausted, the 979 prefix was introduced. Part of the 979 prefix is reserved for use with the [Musicland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bookland "Bookland") code for musical scores with an [ISMN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISMN "ISMN"). The 10-digit ISMN codes differed visually as they began with an "M" letter; the bar code represents the "M" as a zero, and for checksum purposes it counted as a 3. All ISMNs are now thirteen digits commencing 979-0; 979-1 to 979-9 will be used by ISBN.

Publisher identification code numbers are unlikely to be the same in the 978 and 979 ISBNs, likewise, there is no guarantee that language area code numbers will be the same. Moreover, the 10-digit ISBN check digit generally is not the same as the 13-digit ISBN check digit. Because the GTIN-13 is part of the [Global Trade Item Number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Trade_Item_Number "Global Trade Item Number") (GTIN) system (that includes the GTIN-14, the GTIN-12, and the GTIN-8), the 13-digit ISBN falls within the 14-digit data field range.[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-67)

Barcode format compatibility is maintained, because (aside from the group breaks) the ISBN-13 barcode format is identical to the EAN barcode format of existing 10-digit ISBNs. So, migration to an EAN-based system allows booksellers the use of a single numbering system for both books and non-book products that is compatible with existing ISBN based data, with only minimal changes to [information technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_technology "Information technology") systems. Hence, many [booksellers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bookstore "Bookstore") (e.g., [Barnes & Noble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barnes_%26_Noble "Barnes & Noble")) migrated to EAN barcodes as early as March 2005. Although many American and Canadian booksellers were able to read EAN-13 barcodes before 2005, most general retailers could not read them. The upgrading of the [UPC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_Product_Code "Universal Product Code")[barcode system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barcode_system "Barcode system") to full EAN-13, in 2005, eased migration to the ISBN in North America.

Finding publisher codes for English and German, say, with this effect would amount to solving a linear equation in modular arithmetic.[_[original research?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:No\_original\_research "Wikipedia:No original research")_]
5.   **[^](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_ref-25)**The International ISBN Agency's _ISBN User's Manual_ says: "The ten-digit number is divided into four parts of variable length, which must be separated clearly, by hyphens or spaces", although omission of separators is permitted for internal data processing. If present, hyphens must be correctly placed.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISBN_(identifier)#cite_note-range_file_generation-24) The actual definition for hyphenation contains more than 220 different registration group elements with each one broken down into a few to several ranges for the length of the registrant element (more than 1,000 total). The document defining the ranges, listed by agency, is 29 pages.
