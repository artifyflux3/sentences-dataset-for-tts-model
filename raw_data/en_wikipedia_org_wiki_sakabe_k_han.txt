**Sakabe Kōhan** (坂部 廣胖; 1759 – September 16, 1824) was a Japanese [mathematician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematician "Mathematician") in the [Edo period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edo_period "Edo period").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-1)

Sakabe served for a time in the Fire Department of the shogunate, but he resigned that position to become a _[rōnin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R%C5%8Dnin "Rōnin")_ or masterless samurai. He spent the rest of this life in study, in teaching, and in promoting mathematics education in Japan.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-smith208-2)

Sakabe was a student of [Ajima Naonobu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ajima_Naonobu "Ajima Naonobu").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-hayashi120-4)

Sakabe investigated some European and Chinese works which had appeared in Japan, but his general method was later construed to be innovative, clarified and thus improved.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-5) Foreign influence shows itself indirectly some of his published work.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-smith266-6)

Sakabe's _Sampo Tenzan Shinan-roku_ (_Treatise on Tenzan Algebra_) in 1810 was the first published work in Japan proposing the use of [logarithmic tables](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_logarithm "Common logarithm"). He explained that "these tables save much labor, [but] they are but little known for the reason that they have never been printed in our country."[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-smith268-7) Sakabe's proposal would not be realized until twenty years after his death when the first extensive logarithmic table was published in 1844 by [Koide Shuke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koide_Shuke "Koide Shuke").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-smith270-8)

In Sakabe's _Treatise on Tenzan Algebra_, mathematical problems are arranged in order from easy problems to difficult ones. The text presents a method for finding the length of a circumference and the length an arc of an [ellipse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ellipse "Ellipse"). This was the first appearance of the problems pertaining to ellipses in printed books in Japan.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-9)

In a statistical overview derived from writings by and about Harry Smith Parkes, [OCLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OCLC "OCLC")/[WorldCat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WorldCat "WorldCat") encompasses roughly 10+ works in 10+ publications in 1 language and 10+ library holdings.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-10)

*   1795 — _Shinsen Tetsujutsu_[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-smith208-2)
*   1802 — _Kaiujutsu-keima_ (_Considerations on the theory of the polygon_)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-smith208-2)
*   1803 — _Rippō-eijiku_, method for finding cube root[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-hayashi120-4)
*   1810 — _Tenzan Shinan-roku_ (點竄指南錄)[OCLC 22057236896](http://www.worldcat.org/search?q=no:%22057236896%22), _Treatise on Tenzan Algebra_[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-smith268-7)
*   1812 — _Kwanki-kodo-shōhō_, measurement of spherical arcs and trigonometrical tables[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-11)
*   1816 — _Kairo Anshin-roku_ (海路安心錄)[OCLC 122810576](http://www.worldcat.org/search?q=no:%22122810576%22), theory of navigation applying the spherical astronomy of the West[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakabe_K%C5%8Dhan#cite_note-smith266-6)

*   [Sangaku](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sangaku "Sangaku"), the custom of presenting mathematical problems, carved in wood tablets, to the public in [shinto shrines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shinto_shrines "Shinto shrines")
*   [Soroban](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soroban "Soroban"), a Japanese [abacus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abacus "Abacus")
*   [Japanese mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_mathematics "Japanese mathematics")
