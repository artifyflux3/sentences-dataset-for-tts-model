[ 1 ] He was born in Wath-upon-Dearne and educated at Saltburn Primary School, Sir William Turner's Grammar School, London University (Philosophy) and then Selwyn College, Cambridge for his Doctorate.

[ 2 ] From 1969 until his retirement in 2001, Woolhouse worked in the Department of Philosophy at the University of York .

[ 2 ] Cambridge University Press requested Woolhouse write a biography of Locke, the last major biography being Maurice Cranston 's 1957 work. Woolhouse's biography appeared in 2007.

After his death, York's Department of Philosophy founded the Roger Woolhouse Prize, an annual £500 prize awarded to MA Philosophy students.
