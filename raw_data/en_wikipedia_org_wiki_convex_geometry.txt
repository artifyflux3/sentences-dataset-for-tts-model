This article is about the study of convex sets. For the combinatorial set systems, see [antimatroid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antimatroid "Antimatroid").

In [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics"), **convex geometry** is the branch of [geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometry "Geometry") studying [convex sets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convex_set "Convex set"), mainly in [Euclidean space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_space "Euclidean space"). Convex sets occur naturally in many areas: [computational geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computational_geometry "Computational geometry"), [convex analysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convex_analysis "Convex analysis"), [discrete geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discrete_geometry "Discrete geometry"), [functional analysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_analysis "Functional analysis"), [geometry of numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometry_of_numbers "Geometry of numbers"), [integral geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integral_geometry "Integral geometry"), [linear programming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_programming "Linear programming"), [probability theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probability_theory "Probability theory"), [game theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Game_theory "Game theory"), etc.

According to the [Mathematics Subject Classification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics_Subject_Classification "Mathematics Subject Classification") MSC2010,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convex_geometry#cite_note-1) the mathematical discipline _Convex and Discrete Geometry_ includes three major branches:[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convex_geometry#cite_note-2)

*   general convexity
*   polytopes and polyhedra
*   discrete geometry

(though only portions of the latter two are included in convex geometry).

General convexity is further subdivided as follows:[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convex_geometry#cite_note-3)

*   axiomatic and generalized convexity
*   convex sets without dimension restrictions
*   convex sets in topological vector spaces
*   convex sets in 2 dimensions (including convex curves)
*   convex sets in 3 dimensions (including convex surfaces)
*   convex sets in _n_ dimensions (including convex hypersurfaces)
*   finite-dimensional Banach spaces
*   random convex sets and integral geometry
*   asymptotic theory of convex bodies
*   approximation by convex sets
*   variants of convex sets (star-shaped, (_m, n_)-convex, etc.)
*   Helly-type theorems and geometric transversal theory
*   other problems of combinatorial convexity
*   length, area, volume
*   [mixed volumes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mixed_volume "Mixed volume") and related topics
*   [valuations on convex bodies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valuation_(geometry) "Valuation (geometry)")
*   inequalities and extremum problems
*   convex functions and convex programs
*   spherical and hyperbolic convexity

Convex geometry is a relatively young mathematical discipline. Although the first known contributions to convex geometry date back to antiquity and can be traced in the works of [Euclid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclid "Euclid") and [Archimedes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes "Archimedes"), it became an independent branch of mathematics at the turn of the 20th century, mainly due to the works of [Hermann Brunn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermann_Brunn "Hermann Brunn") and [Hermann Minkowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermann_Minkowski "Hermann Minkowski") in dimensions two and three. A big part of their results was soon generalized to spaces of higher dimensions, and in 1934 [T. Bonnesen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tommy_Bonnesen "Tommy Bonnesen") and [W. Fenchel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Werner_Fenchel "Werner Fenchel") gave a comprehensive survey of convex geometry in [Euclidean space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_space "Euclidean space")**R**n. Further development of convex geometry in the 20th century and its relations to numerous mathematical disciplines are summarized in the _Handbook of convex geometry_ edited by P. M. Gruber and J. M. Wills.
