Chemical compound

**Dicarbon monoxide** (C 2 O) is a [molecule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecule "Molecule") that contains two [carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon")[atoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atom "Atom") and one [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen") atom. It is a [linear molecule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_molecule "Linear molecule") that, because of its simplicity, is of interest in a variety of areas. It is, however, so extremely [reactive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reactivity_(chemistry) "Reactivity (chemistry)") that it is not encountered in everyday life. It is classified as a [carbene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbene "Carbene"), [cumulene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cumulene "Cumulene") and an [oxocarbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxocarbon "Oxocarbon").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dicarbon_monoxide#cite_note-1)

Occurrence
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Dicarbon_monoxide&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Occurrence")]

Dicarbon monoxide is a product of the [photolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation "Photodissociation") of [carbon suboxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_suboxide "Carbon suboxide"):[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dicarbon_monoxide#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dicarbon_monoxide#cite_note-3)

C 3 O 2 → CO + C 2 O
It is stable enough to observe reactions with [NO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitric_oxide "Nitric oxide") and [NO 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen_dioxide "Nitrogen dioxide").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dicarbon_monoxide#cite_note-4)

Called **ketenylidene** in [organometallic chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organometallic_chemistry "Organometallic chemistry"), it is a [ligand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ligand "Ligand") observed in [metal carbonyl clusters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_carbonyl_cluster "Metal carbonyl cluster"), e.g. [OC 2 Co 3(CO)9]+. Ketenylidenes are proposed as intermediates in the chain growth mechanism of the [Fischer-Tropsch Process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fischer-Tropsch_Process "Fischer-Tropsch Process"), which converts [carbon monoxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_monoxide "Carbon monoxide") and [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen") to hydrocarbon fuels.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dicarbon_monoxide#cite_note-5)

The [organophosphorus compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organophosphorus_compound "Organophosphorus compound") (C 6 H 5)3 PCCO (CAS# 15596-07-3) contains the C 2 O functionality. Sometimes called Bestmann's Ylide, it is a yellow solid.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dicarbon_monoxide#cite_note-6)
