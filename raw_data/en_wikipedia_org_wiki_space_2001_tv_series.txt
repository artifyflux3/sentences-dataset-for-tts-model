_**Space**_ (_**Hyperspace**_ in the United States) is a 2001 [BBC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC "BBC")[documentary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Documentary_film "Documentary film") which ran for six episodes covering a number of topics in relation to [outer space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space "Outer space"). The series is hosted and narrated by actor [Sam Neill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Neill "Sam Neill").

Episodes
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Space_(2001_TV_series)&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Episodes")]

| No. | Title | Original release date |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | "Star Stuff" | 22 July 2001(2001-07-22) |
| The first episode covers the origins of life and how everything is produced by the process in which stars burn their fuel. |
| 2 | "Staying Alive" | 29 July 2001(2001-07-29) |
| This episode analyses the chances of Earth being destroyed by a [black hole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_hole "Black hole") or [asteroid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroid "Asteroid"). |
| 3 | "Black Holes" | 5 August 2001(2001-08-05) |
| Episode three looks at how black holes are formed and how they behave, with potential to destroy the [Solar System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_System "Solar System"). |
| 4 | "Are We Alone?" | 12 August 2001(2001-08-12) |
| Number four looks for potential homes of extraterrestrial life and the chances that humans could make contact. |
| 5 | "New Worlds" | 19 August 2001(2001-08-19) |
| The fifth episode covers the possibility of colonising and [terraforming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terraforming "Terraforming") planets both in the Solar System and beyond into deep space. |
| 6 | "Boldly Go" | 26 August 2001(2001-08-26) |
| The final episode looks at the technologies that are being developed to further enable humans ventures into space. |
