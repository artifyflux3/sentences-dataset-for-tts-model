**Acharya Virasena** (750–825CE),[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJaini1991111-1) also spelt as **Veerasena**, was a Jain monk and belonged to the lineage of Acharya [Kundakunda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kundakunda "Kundakunda").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-Indranandi-2) He was an [Indian mathematician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_mathematician "Indian mathematician") and Jain philosopher and scholar of Digambar sect. He was also known as a famous orator and an accomplished poet.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-Jinasena-3) He is renowned for his work on the monumental Jain commentary series on the ''Shatkhandagama'', known as the ''_[Dhavala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhavala "Dhavala")_'' texts.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-4) The late Dr. Hiralal Jain places the completion of this treatise in 816.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-5) He played a critical role in the development of Digambara scholasticism and is considered one of the greatest Jain scholars of early medieval India.

Virasena was a noted mathematician. He gave the derivation of the [volume](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volume "Volume") of a [frustum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frustum "Frustum") by a sort of infinite procedure. He worked with the concept of _ardhachheda_: the number of times a number can be divided by 2. This coincides with the [binary logarithm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Binary_logarithm "Binary logarithm") when applied to [powers of two](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Powers_of_two "Powers of two"), but gives the [2-adic order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/P-adic_order "P-adic order") rather than the logarithm for other integers.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-7)

Virasena gave the approximate formula _C_=3 _d_+(16 _d_+16)/113 to relate the circumference of a circle, _C_, to its diameter, _d_. For large values of _d_, this gives the approximation π≈355/113=3.14159292..., which is more accurate than the approximation π≈3.1416 given by [Aryabhata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aryabhata "Aryabhata") in the _[Aryabhatiya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aryabhatiya "Aryabhatiya")_.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-8)

Life
----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Virasena&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Life")]

Virasena was a disciple of the Jain acharya [Aryanandi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aryanandi_(8th_century) "Aryanandi (8th century)") and flourished during the [Rashtrakuta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashtrakuta "Rashtrakuta") King [Dhruva Dharavarsha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhruva_Dharavarsha "Dhruva Dharavarsha") and [Govinda III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Govinda_III "Govinda III").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-jaini1979-9) He was based in the Deccan region and attracted several prominent disciples, including Jinasena I and Gunasena, later known as Jinasena II.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-upadhye-10)

Virasena was proficient in astrology, grammar, logic, mathematics and prosody. He wrote _[Dhavala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhavala "Dhavala")_, a commentary on Jain canon _[Shatakhandagama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shatakhandagama "Shatakhandagama")_. He also started the work on _Jayadhavalaa_ which was completed by his disciples. He was among the jewels of [Rashtrakuta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashtrakuta "Rashtrakuta") Emperor Dhruva dharavarsha and Govinda III, it is also says that he also present at the time of [Amoghavarsha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amoghavarsha "Amoghavarsha").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-FOOTNOTENatubhai_Shah200431-11)

His lineage started with Chandrasena who initiated Aryanandi.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPannalal_Jain195130%E2%80%9331-12) Aryanandi initiated Virasena and Jayasena.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPannalal_Jain195130%E2%80%9331-12) Virasena initiated six disciples who were Dasharayguru, [Jinasena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jinasena "Jinasena"), Vinayasena, Shripal, Padmasena and Devasena.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPannalal_Jain195130%E2%80%9331-12) Dasharayguru and Jinasena initiated Gunabhadra who later initiated Lokasena.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPannalal_Jain195130%E2%80%9331-12) Vinayasena initiated Kumarasena who started the [Kashtha Sangha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kashtha_Sangha "Kashtha Sangha").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPannalal_Jain195130%E2%80%9331-12)

### Dhavala and Related Texts

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Virasena&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Dhavala and Related Texts")]

Virasena is primarily known for initiating the commentary project on the _Shatkhandagama_, one of the most revered canonical texts in Digambara [Jainism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jainism "Jainism"). This massive commentary is collectively referred to as the _[Dhavala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhavala "Dhavala")_ series, and it includes:

•⁠ ⁠_Dhavala_

•⁠ ⁠_Jayadhavala_

•⁠ ⁠_Mahadhavala_

While Virasena began the _Dhavala_, he was unable to complete it in his lifetime. The work was carried forward by his disciples Jinasena II (Gunasena) and others.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-cort2010-13)

These texts are not only commentaries but also rich repositories of Jain philosophy, cosmology, and metaphysics.

### Contributions to Mathematics

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Virasena&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Contributions to Mathematics")]

Virasena is also credited with contributions to Jain mathematics and logic. He was among the early thinkers to use concepts of infinitesimals and logarithmic reasoning, although in a proto-mathematical form embedded in religious cosmology.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virasena#cite_note-14)

Disciples
---------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Virasena&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Disciples")]

Virasena mentored several disciples who carried forward his intellectual legacy: •⁠ ⁠**[Jinasena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jinasena "Jinasena")**: Author of the _Adi Purana_, an epic Jain text on Rishabhanatha and Bharata. •⁠ ⁠**Gunasena (Jinasena II)**: Successor of Jinasena I, and co-author of the _Dhavala_ commentary series.

Legacy
------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Virasena&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Legacy")]

Acharya Virasena is revered as a pioneering figure in the Digambara tradition for his scholarship and rigorous analysis of Jain doctrine. His contributions to Jain literature and mathematics mark him as a significant figure in early medieval Indian intellectual history. The _Dhavala_ series continues to be studied by Jain scholars and remains a cornerstone of Digambara exegesis.
