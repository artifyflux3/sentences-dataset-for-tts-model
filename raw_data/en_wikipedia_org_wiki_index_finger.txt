The **index finger** (also referred to as **forefinger**,[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-1)**first finger**,[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-2)**second finger**,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-3)**pointer finger**, **trigger finger**, **digitus secundus,****digitus II**, and [many other terms](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Wikisaurus:index_finger "wikt:Wikisaurus:index finger")) is the second [digit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finger "Finger") of a human [hand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hand "Hand"). It is located between the [thumb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thumb "Thumb") and the [middle finger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_finger "Middle finger"). It is usually the most [dextrous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fine_motor_skill "Fine motor skill") and sensitive digit of the hand, though not the longest. It is shorter than the middle finger, and may be shorter or longer than the [ring finger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ring_finger "Ring finger") (see [digit ratio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digit_ratio "Digit ratio")).

"[Index finger](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/index_finger "wikt:index finger")" literally means "pointing finger", from the same Latin source as _[indicate](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/indicate "wikt:indicate");_ its anatomical names are "index finger" and "second digit".

The index finger has three [phalanges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phalanges "Phalanges"). It does not contain any muscles, but is controlled by muscles in the hand by attachments of [tendons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tendon "Tendon") to the bones.

[![Image 2](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Anger_during_a_protest_by_David_Shankbone.jpg/250px-Anger_during_a_protest_by_David_Shankbone.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Anger_during_a_protest_by_David_Shankbone.jpg)

A man [pointing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pointing "Pointing") at a woman during an argument

A lone index finger held vertically is often used to represent the [number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Number "Number")[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1_(number) "1 (number)") (but [finger counting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finger_counting "Finger counting") differs across cultures), or when held up or moved side to side (finger-wagging), it can be an admonitory gesture. If held upward, it can also mean "wait a minute" or "one moment". With the hand held palm out and the thumb and middle fingers touching, it represents the letter _d_ in the [American Sign Language alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Sign_Language_alphabet "American Sign Language alphabet").

Pointing with the pointer finger may be used to indicate or identify an item, person, place or object.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-Imai-4)

Around age one, babies begin pointing to communicate relatively complex thoughts, including interest, desire, and information. Pointing in human babies can demonstrate the [theory of mind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theory_of_mind "Theory of mind"), or ability to understand what other people are thinking. This gesture may form one basis for the development of human language.

Non-human primates, lacking the ability to formulate ideas about what others are thinking, use pointing in much less complex ways.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-Day-5) However, [corvids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corvid "Corvid"), [dogs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dog "Dog")[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-pmid22347411-6) and [elephants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elephant "Elephant")[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-7) do understand finger pointing.

In some cultures, particularly the [Malays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_people "Malay people") and [Javanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Javanese_people "Javanese people")[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-8) in [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia"), pointing using the index finger is considered rude, hence the [thumb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thumb "Thumb") is used instead.

### Index finger in Islam

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Index_finger&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Index finger in Islam")]

In Islam raising the index finger signifies the [Tawhīd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tawhid "Tawhid") (تَوْحِيد), which denotes the indivisible oneness of [God](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/God_in_Islam "God in Islam"). It is used to express the unity of God ("there is no god but God").

In [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic "Arabic"), the index or fore finger is called musabbiḥa (مُسَبِّحة), mostly used with the definite article: al-musabbiḥa (الْمُسَبِّحة). Sometimes also as-sabbāḥa (السَّبّاحة) is used.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-:0-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-10) The Arabic verb سَبَّحَ - which shares the same root as the Arabic word for index finger - means to praise or glorify God by saying: "[Subḥāna Allāh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subhan_Allah "Subhan Allah")" (سُبْحانَ الله).

### Index finger in archaeoastronomy

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Index_finger&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Index finger in archaeoastronomy")]

Before the advent of GPS and compass, early humans used index finger for pointing direction of objects with the help of stellar objects during night time.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-11)

The index finger pointing up is a sign of teaching authority. This is shown in the depiction of [Plato](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plato "Plato") in the [School of Athens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/School_of_Athens "School of Athens") by [Raphael](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raphael "Raphael").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Index_finger#cite_note-12)

As a modern artistic convention, the index finger pointing at the viewer is in the form of a command or summons. Two famous examples of this are [recruiting posters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_recruitment#Recruitment_posters "Military recruitment") used during [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I") by the [United Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom "United Kingdom") and the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States").
