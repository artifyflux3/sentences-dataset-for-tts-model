1910 Sir Charles Raymond Beazley (3 April 1868 – 1 February 1955) was a British historian.

[ 1 ] He was Professor of History at the University of Birmingham from 1909 to 1933.

[ 2 ] Born in Blackheath , he was the son of Rev. Joseph and Louisa Beazley.

[ 3 ] [ 4 ] He was educated at St Paul's School , King's College London and Balliol College, Oxford . His academic career was as a Fellow of Merton College, Oxford , [ 5 ] until his chair at Birmingham.

Associated with a pro-German tendency within the British political and intellectual establishment in the inter-war years, [ 6 ] Beazley was a regular contributor to the Anglo-German Review , established in 1936.

[ 7 ] He subsequently sat on the National Council of the Link , a pro-German organisation.
