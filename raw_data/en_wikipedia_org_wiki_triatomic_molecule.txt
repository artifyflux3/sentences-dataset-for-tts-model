Molecules made of three atoms

[![Image 11](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Carbon-dioxide-3D-vdW.svg/250px-Carbon-dioxide-3D-vdW.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Carbon-dioxide-3D-vdW.svg)

Carbon dioxide

**Triatomic molecules** are [molecules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecule "Molecule") composed of three [atoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atom "Atom"), of either the same or different [chemical elements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_element "Chemical element"). Examples include [H 2 O](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water "Water"), [CO 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide "Carbon dioxide") (pictured), [HCN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide"), O 3 ([ozone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozone "Ozone")) and [NO 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen_dioxide "Nitrogen dioxide").

Molecular vibrations
--------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Triatomic_molecule&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Molecular vibrations")]

The [vibrational modes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_vibration "Molecular vibration") of a triatomic molecule can be determined in specific cases.

### Symmetric linear molecules

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Triatomic_molecule&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Symmetric linear molecules")]

A symmetric linear molecule ABA can perform:

*   Antisymmetric longitudinal vibrations with frequency

ω a=k 1 M m A m B{\displaystyle \omega _{a}={\sqrt {\frac {k_{1}M}{m_{A}m_{B}}}}}![Image 12: {\displaystyle \omega _{a}={\sqrt {\frac {k_{1}M}{m_{A}m_{B}}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/b0f731fdc9ed9d54158c3dd999213140659df5f7)
*   Symmetric longitudinal vibrations with frequency

ω s 1=k 1 m A{\displaystyle \omega _{s1}={\sqrt {\frac {k_{1}}{m_{A}}}}}![Image 13: {\displaystyle \omega _{s1}={\sqrt {\frac {k_{1}}{m_{A}}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/f7c03b417fd97b2b663312afba5bb99ab5da3c1c)
*   Symmetric transversal vibrations with frequency

ω s 2=2 k 2 M m A m B{\displaystyle \omega _{s2}={\sqrt {\frac {2k_{2}M}{m_{A}m_{B}}}}}![Image 14: {\displaystyle \omega _{s2}={\sqrt {\frac {2k_{2}M}{m_{A}m_{B}}}}}](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/e7ea5e84f2a6aa7517126bf3d0a16ef616b0379a)
In the previous formulas, _M_ is the total mass of the molecule, _m A_ and _m B_ are the masses of the elements A and B, _k_ 1 and _k_ 2 are the spring constants of the molecule along its axis and perpendicular to it.

Types
-----

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Triatomic_molecule&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Types")]

### Homonuclear

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Triatomic_molecule&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Homonuclear")]

[![Image 15](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Ozone-elpot-3D-vdW.png/250px-Ozone-elpot-3D-vdW.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Ozone-elpot-3D-vdW.png)

Ozone, O 3

[![Image 16](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Trihydrogen-cation-3D-vdW.png/250px-Trihydrogen-cation-3D-vdW.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Trihydrogen-cation-3D-vdW.png)

Trihydrogen cation, H 3+

Homonuclear triatomic molecules contain three of the same kind of atom. That molecule will be an [allotrope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allotrope "Allotrope") of that [element](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_element "Chemical element").

[Ozone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ozone "Ozone"), O 3 is an example of a triatomic molecule with all atoms the same. [Triatomic hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triatomic_hydrogen "Triatomic hydrogen"), H 3, is unstable and breaks up spontaneously. H 3+, the [trihydrogen cation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trihydrogen_cation "Trihydrogen cation") is stable by itself and is symmetric. 4 He 3, the [helium trimer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helium_trimer "Helium trimer") is only weakly bound by [van der Waals force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_der_Waals_force "Van der Waals force") and is in an [Efimov state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Efimov_state "Efimov state").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triatomic_molecule#cite_note-Kunitski15-1)[Trisulfur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trisulfur "Trisulfur") (S 3) is analogous to ozone.

Geometry
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Triatomic_molecule&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Geometry")]

All triatomic molecules may be classified as possessing either a [linear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_molecular_geometry "Linear molecular geometry"), [bent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bent_molecular_geometry "Bent molecular geometry"), or [cyclic](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cyclic_molecular_geometry&action=edit&redlink=1 "Cyclic molecular geometry (page does not exist)") geometry.[_[further explanation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Please\_clarify "Wikipedia:Please clarify")_]

### Linear

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Triatomic_molecule&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Linear")]

Linear triatomic molecules owe their geometry to their _**sp**_ or _**sp 3 d**_ hybridised central atoms. Well-known linear triatomic molecules include [carbon dioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide "Carbon dioxide") (CO 2) and [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide") (HCN).

[Xenon difluoride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xenon_difluoride "Xenon difluoride") (XeF 2) is one of the rare examples of a linear triatomic molecule possessing non-bonded pairs of electrons on the central atom.
