Chemical compound

**Hydroxyaluminium(I)**, also known as **Aluminium(I) hydroxide**, is an [inorganic chemical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inorganic_chemical "Inorganic chemical") with [molecular formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_formula "Molecular formula") AlOH. It consists of [aluminium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium "Aluminium") in the +1 [oxidation state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxidation_state "Oxidation state") paired with a single [hydroxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroxide "Hydroxide"). It has been detected as a [molecular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular "Molecular") substance in the envelope of an [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen")-rich [red supergiant star](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_supergiant_star "Red supergiant star"), a place where substances containing metals or hydroxides are thought to be rare.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium(I)_hydroxide#cite_note-2)

Production
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Aluminium_monohydroxide&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Production")]

In the laboratory AlOH can be made by heating aluminium, so that it vapourises into low pressure hydrogen peroxide vapour.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium(I)_hydroxide#cite_note-app-3) Another method is to condense a mixture of aluminium vapour, hydrogen and oxygen with argon into a solid at 10K. Along with AlOH, there are also Al(OH)2, Al(OH)3, HAl(OH)2, _cyc_-AlO 2 and AlOAl molecules formed.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium(I)_hydroxide#cite_note-4)

Properties
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Aluminium_monohydroxide&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Properties")]

The bond lengths are, Al-O 1.682 Å, and for O-H 0.878 Å.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium(I)_hydroxide#cite_note-app-3) The rotational constants are B 0=15,740.2476 MHz and D 0=0.02481 MHz.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium(I)_hydroxide#cite_note-app-3)
