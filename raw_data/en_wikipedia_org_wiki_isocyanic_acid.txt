**Isocyanic acid** is a [chemical compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_compound "Chemical compound") with the [structural formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Structural_formula "Structural formula") HNCO, which is often written as H−N=C=O. It is a colourless, [volatile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volatility_(chemistry) "Volatility (chemistry)") and [poisonous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poisonous "Poisonous") gas, [condensing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boiling_point "Boiling point") at 23.5°C. It is the predominant [tautomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tautomer "Tautomer") and an [isomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomer "Isomer") of cyanic acid _(aka. cyanol)_ (H−O−C≡N), and the monomer of [cyanuric acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanuric_acid "Cyanuric acid").

The derived [anion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anion "Anion") of isocyanic acid is the same as the derived anion of cyanic acid, and that anion is [N=C=O]−, which is called [cyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanate "Cyanate"). The related [functional group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_group "Functional group")−N=C=O is [isocyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanate "Isocyanate"); it is distinct from [cyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanate "Cyanate") (−O−C≡N), [fulminate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminate "Fulminate") (−O−N+≡C−), and [nitrile oxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrile_oxide "Nitrile oxide") (−C≡N+−O−).[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-mart2019-6)

Isocyanic acid was discovered in 1830 by [Justus von Liebig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justus_von_Liebig "Justus von Liebig") and [Friedrich Wöhler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedrich_W%C3%B6hler "Friedrich Wöhler").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-7)

Isocyanic acid is the simplest stable [chemical compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_compound "Chemical compound") that contains [carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon"), [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen"), [nitrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen "Nitrogen"), and [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen"), the [four most commonly found elements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CHON "CHON") in [organic chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_chemistry "Organic chemistry") and biology. It is the only fairly stable one of the four linear isomers with [molecular formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_formula "Molecular formula") HOCN that have been synthesized, the others being cyanic acid (cyanol, H−O−C≡N) and the elusive [fulminic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fulminic_acid "Fulminic acid") (H−C≡N+−O−)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-8) and [isofulminic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isofulminic_acid "Isofulminic acid")H−O−N+≡C−.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-mart2019-6)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-quan2010-9)

### Isocyanic acid (HNCO)

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Isocyanic_acid&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Isocyanic acid (HNCO)")]

Although the electronic structure according to [valence bond theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valence_bond_theory "Valence bond theory") can be written as H−N=C=O, the [vibrational spectrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vibrational_spectroscopy "Vibrational spectroscopy") has a band at 2268.8 cm−1 in the gas phase, which some say indicates a carbon–nitrogen triple bond.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-10)[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-Isom-11) If so, then the canonical form H−N+≡C−O− is the major [resonance structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resonance_structure "Resonance structure").

However, classic vibrational analysis would indicate that the 2268.8 cm−1 is the asymmetric N=C=O stretch, as per Colthup et al.,[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-12) as well as the NIST Chemistry WebBook,[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-13) which also reports the corresponding symmetric N=C=O stretch (weak in infrared, but strong in Raman) to be 1327 cm−1. Based on these classic assignments, there is no need to invoke a full charged state for the N and O atoms, to explain the vibrational spectral data.

The [tautomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tautomer "Tautomer"), known as cyanic acid, HOCN, in which the oxygen atom is protonated exists in equilibrium with isocyanic acid to the extent of about 3%.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_][_[dubious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy\_dispute#Disputed\_statement "Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute") – [discuss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Talk:Isocyanic\_acid#Dubious "Talk:Isocyanic acid")_] The vibrational spectrum is indicative of the presence of a [triple bond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triple_bond "Triple bond") between the nitrogen and carbon atoms.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-Isom-11)

In aqueous solution it is a [weak acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weak_acid "Weak acid"), having a [p _K_ a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acid_dissociation_constant "Acid dissociation constant") of 3.7:[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-scdb-4)

HNCO ⇌ H+ + NCO−
Isocyanic acid [hydrolyses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrolysis "Hydrolysis") to [carbon dioxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_dioxide "Carbon dioxide") and [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia"):

HNCO + H 2 O → CO 2 + NH 3
Dilute solutions of isocyanic acid are stable in inert [solvents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solvent "Solvent"), e.g. [ether](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ether "Ether") and [chlorinated hydrocarbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organochloride "Organochloride").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-eEROS-2)

At high concentrations, isocyanic acid oligomerizes to give the [trimer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trimer_(chemistry) "Trimer (chemistry)")[cyanuric acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanuric_acid "Cyanuric acid") and [cyamelide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyamelide "Cyamelide"), a polymer. These species usually are easily separated from liquid- or gas-phase reaction products.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-eEROS-2)

Isocyanic acid reacts with [amines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amine "Amine") to give [ureas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urea "Urea") (carbamides):

HNCO + RNH 2 → RNHC(O)NH 2
This reaction is called **carbamylation**. Excess isocyanic acid can react with the ureas to give [allophanates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allophanate "Allophanate").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-eEROS-2)

HNCO adds across electron-rich double bonds, such as [vinylethers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enol_ether "Enol ether"), to give the corresponding isocyanates.

Isocyanic acid, HNCO, is a [Lewis acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lewis_acids_and_bases "Lewis acids and bases") whose [free energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gibbs_free_energy "Gibbs free energy"), [enthalpy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enthalpy "Enthalpy") and [entropy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entropy "Entropy") changes for its 1:1 association with a number of bases in [carbon tetrachloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_tetrachloride "Carbon tetrachloride") solution at 25°C have been reported.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-14) The acceptor properties of HNCO are compared with other Lewis acid in the [ECW model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ECW_model "ECW model").

Low-temperature [photolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photodissociation "Photodissociation") of solids containing HNCO creates the tautomer cyanic acid H−O−C≡N, also called hydrogen cyanate.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-15) Pure cyanic acid has not been isolated, and isocyanic acid is the predominant form in all solvents.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-eEROS-2) Sometimes information presented for cyanic acid in reference books is actually for isocyanic acid.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Isocyanic acid can be made by protonation of the [cyanate anion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanate_anion "Cyanate anion"), such as from salts like [potassium cyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potassium_cyanate "Potassium cyanate"), by either gaseous [hydrogen chloride](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_chloride "Hydrogen chloride") or acids such as [oxalic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxalic_acid "Oxalic acid").[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-16)

H+ + NCO− → HNCO
HNCO also can be made by the high-temperature thermal decomposition of the trimer [cyanuric acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanuric_acid "Cyanuric acid"):

C 3 H 3 N 3 O 3 → 3 HNCO
In all cases the resulting acid must be kept very cold or in gaseous form, as concentrated isocyanic acid polymerizes rapidly above −20°C.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-eEROS-2)

In the reverse of the famous synthesis of [urea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urea "Urea") by [Friedrich Wöhler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedrich_W%C3%B6hler "Friedrich Wöhler"),

CO(NH 2)2 → HNCO + NH 3
isocyanic acid is produced and rapidly trimerizes to cyanuric acid.

Isocyanic acid has been detected in many kinds of [interstellar environments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-quan2010-9)

Isocyanic acid is also present in various forms of [smoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smoke "Smoke"), including [smog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smog "Smog") and [cigarette smoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cigarette_smoke "Cigarette smoke"). It was detected using [mass spectrometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_spectrometry "Mass spectrometry"), and easily [dissolves in water](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Properties_of_water#Water_as_a_solvent "Properties of water"), posing a health risk to the lungs.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isocyanic_acid#cite_note-17)
