Among elements with a known stable isotope, only indium and tellurium similarly occur with a stable isotope in lower abundance than the long-lived radioactive isotope.

There are 36 other unstable isotopes recognized, the longest-lived of which are 183 Re with a half-life of 70 days, 184 Re with a half-life of 35.4 days, 186 Re with a half-life of 3.7185 days, 182 Re with a half-life of 64.2 hours, and 189 Re with a half-life of 24.3 hours. There are also numerous isomers , the longest-lived of which are 186m Re with a half-life of 200,000 years and 184m Re with a half-life of 177.25 days.

[ 4 ] All others have half-lives less than a day.
