**Formyl cyanide** is a simple [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the [formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula") HCOCN and [structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_structure "Chemical structure")HC(=O)−C≡N. It is simultaneously a [nitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrile "Nitrile") (R−C≡N) and an [aldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aldehyde "Aldehyde") (R−CH=O). Formyl cyanide is the simplest member of the [acyl cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acyl_cyanide "Acyl cyanide") family. It is known to occur in space in the [Sgr B2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sgr_B2 "Sgr B2")[molecular cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanoformaldehyde#cite_note-1)

Formyl cyanide was first made through [methoxyacetonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Methoxyacetonitrile&action=edit&redlink=1 "Methoxyacetonitrile (page does not exist)")[flash vacuum pyrolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flash_vacuum_pyrolysis "Flash vacuum pyrolysis") at 600°C. The same technique with cinnamyloxyacetonitrile[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanoformaldehyde#cite_note-lb-2) or allyloxyacetonitrile also generates formyl cyanide.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanoformaldehyde#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanoformaldehyde#cite_note-4)

In molecular clouds, formation of formyl cyanide is speculated to result from [formaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formaldehyde "Formaldehyde") and the [cyanide radical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanide_radical "Cyanide radical"):[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanoformaldehyde#cite_note-5)

CH 2 O + CN• → HCOCN + H•
In Earth's atmosphere, the pollutant [acrylonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrylonitrile "Acrylonitrile") reacts with [hydroxyl radical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroxyl_radical "Hydroxyl radical") forming formyl cyanide, [hydroperoxyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroperoxyl "Hydroperoxyl") and formaldehyde:[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanoformaldehyde#cite_note-6)

CH 2=CHCN + HO• +3⁄2 O 2 → HOO• + HCOCN + CH 2 O
Formyl cyanide reacts rapidly with trace quantities of water to form [formic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formic_acid "Formic acid") and [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanoformaldehyde#cite_note-lb-2)

By formally substituting the hydrogen atom, cyanoformyl chloride, ClC(O)CN, and cyanoformyl bromide, BrC(O)CN are obtained.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanoformaldehyde#cite_note-7)
