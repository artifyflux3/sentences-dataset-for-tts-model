Scientific classification Domain: Eukaryota Clade : Sar Clade : Stramenopiles Clade : Gyrista Cavalier-Smith 1998 [ 1 ] Subgroups [ 2 ] [ 3 ] Pseudofungi Oomycetes Hyphochytrea Ochrophyta (=Heterokontophyta) Chrysista Diatomista Developea Pirsonea Gyrista is a clade of stramenopile protists containing three diverse groups: the mostly photosynthetic Ochrophyta , the parasitic Pseudofungi , and the recently described group of nanoflagellates known as Bigyromonada .

[ 2 ] Members of this clade are characterized by the presence of a helix or a double helix/ring system in the ciliary transition region .

[ 1 ] Systematics [ edit ] Taxonomic history [ edit ] Gyrista was first described in 1998 by protistologist Thomas Cavalier-Smith in his work A revised six-kingdom system of life , originally as a superphylum containing two phyla : Ochrophyta , the heterokont algae ; and Bigyra , which then contained the pseudofungi and bigyromonads together with the opalines .

[ 1 ] Later, the name Bigyra was modified to contain opalines, bicosoecids and labyrinthulomycetes , while the Ochrophyta, Pseudofungi and Bigyromonada remained as groups within Gyrista.

[ 2 ] Molecular phylogenetics [ edit ] Gyrista was seen in 2017 as the sister group to phylum Bigyra , which contains the Sagenista and Opalozoa . Together, Gyrista and Bigyra form the clade Stramenopiles or Heterokonta .
