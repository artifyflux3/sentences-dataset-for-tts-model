From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The survival of some [microorganisms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microorganisms "Microorganisms") exposed to [outer space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space "Outer space") has been studied using both simulated facilities and [low Earth orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low_Earth_orbit "Low Earth orbit") exposures. Bacteria were some of the first organisms investigated, when in 1960 a Russian satellite carried _[Escherichia coli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Escherichia\_coli "Escherichia coli")_, _[Staphylococcus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Staphylococcus "Staphylococcus")_, and _[Enterobacter aerogenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enterobacter\_aerogenes "Enterobacter aerogenes")_ into orbit.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_microorganisms_tested_in_outer_space#cite_note-:0-1) Many kinds of microorganisms have been selected for exposure experiments since, as listed in the table below.

Experiments of the adaption of microbes in space have yielded unpredictable results. While sometimes the microorganism may weaken, they can also increase in their disease-causing potency.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_microorganisms_tested_in_outer_space#cite_note-:0-1)

It is possible to classify these microorganisms into two groups, the [human-borne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medical_microbiology "Medical microbiology") and the [extremophiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extremophiles "Extremophiles"). Studying the human-borne microorganisms is significant for human welfare and future [crewed missions in space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_spaceflight "Human spaceflight"), whilst the extremophiles are vital for studying the physiological requirements of survival in space.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_microorganisms_tested_in_outer_space#cite_note-Experimental_methods-2)[NASA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASA "NASA") has pointed out that normal adults have ten times as many microbial cells as human cells in their bodies.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_microorganisms_tested_in_outer_space#cite_note-bio-3) They are also nearly everywhere in the environment and, although normally invisible, can form slimy [biofilms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biofilm "Biofilm").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_microorganisms_tested_in_outer_space#cite_note-bio-3)

Extremophiles have adapted to live in some of the most extreme environments on Earth. This includes [hypersaline lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypersaline_lake "Hypersaline lake"), [arid regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desert "Desert"), [deep sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_sea "Deep sea"), acidic sites, cold and dry polar regions and [permafrost](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Permafrost "Permafrost").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_microorganisms_tested_in_outer_space#cite_note-4) The existence of extremophiles has led to the speculation that microorganisms could survive the harsh conditions of extraterrestrial environments and be used as model organisms to understand the fate of biological systems in these environments. The focus of many experiments has been to investigate the possible survival of organisms inside rocks ([lithopanspermia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lithopanspermia "Lithopanspermia")),[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_microorganisms_tested_in_outer_space#cite_note-Experimental_methods-2) or their survival on [Mars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mars "Mars") for understanding the likelihood of past or present life on that planet.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_microorganisms_tested_in_outer_space#cite_note-Experimental_methods-2) Because of their ubiquity and resistance to spacecraft decontamination, bacterial [spores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spore "Spore") are considered likely potential [forward contaminants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_protection "Planetary protection") on robotic missions to [Mars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mars "Mars"). Measuring the resistance of such organisms to space conditions can be applied to develop adequate decontamination procedures.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_microorganisms_tested_in_outer_space#cite_note-5)

Research and testing of microorganisms in outer space could eventually be applied for [directed panspermia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Directed_panspermia "Directed panspermia") or [terraforming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terraforming "Terraforming").
