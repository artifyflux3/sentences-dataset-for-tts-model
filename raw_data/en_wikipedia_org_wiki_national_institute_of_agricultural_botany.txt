National Institute of Agricultural Botany Company type Agribusiness Industry Agriculture Founded 1919 Headquarters Cambridge , England Products Trials and valuation Seed certification Plant breeders rights Laboratory services Research and training courses Seed testing Subscription services Publications Number of employees Approx. 150 Website www .niab .com The National Institute of Agricultural Botany ( NIAB ) is a plant science research company based in Cambridge , UK .

NIAB group [ edit ] The NIAB group consists of: NIAB NIAB EMR – a horticultural and agricultural research institute at East Malling, Kent, with a specialism in fruit and clonally propagated crop production. Joined the NIAB Group in 2016.

[ 1 ] NIAB CUF – a potato agronomy unit. Joined the NIAB Group in 2013.

[ 2 ] NIAB TAG – the arable group that joined in 2009 [ 3 ] BCPC – promotes the use of science and technology in the understanding and application of effective, sustainable crop production. Acquired by NIAB in 2018.

[ 4 ] History [ edit ] NIAB was founded in 1919 by Sir Lawrence Weaver . The original Huntingdon Road headquarters building was opened in 1921, by King George V and Queen Mary.
