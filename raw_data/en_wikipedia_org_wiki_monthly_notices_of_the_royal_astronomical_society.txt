_**Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society**_ (**MNRAS**) is a [peer-reviewed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peer_review "Peer review")[scientific journal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_journal "Scientific journal") in [astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy "Astronomy"), [astrophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrophysics "Astrophysics") and related fields. It publishes [original research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Original_research "Original research") in two formats: [papers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_paper "Scientific paper") (of any length) and letters (limited to five pages). MNRAS publishes more articles per year than any other astronomy journal.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-number_papers-3)

The [learned society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learned_society "Learned society") journal has been in continuous existence since 1827 and became [online only](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_journal "Online journal") in 2020. It operates as a partnership between the [Royal Astronomical Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Astronomical_Society "Royal Astronomical Society") (RAS), who select and peer-review the contents, and [Oxford University Press](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxford_University_Press "Oxford University Press") (OUP), who publish and market the journal. Despite its name, MNRAS is no longer monthly, nor does it carry the notices of the RAS.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-ag2008-4) In 2024 MNRAS became a purely [gold open access](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold_open_access "Gold open access") journal.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-gold_oa-2)

The first issue of MNRAS was published on 9 February 1827 as _Monthly Notices of the Astronomical Society of London_ and it has been in continuous publication ever since. It took its current name from the second volume, after the [Astronomical Society of London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomical_Society_of_London "Astronomical Society of London") became the [Royal Astronomical Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Astronomical_Society "Royal Astronomical Society") (RAS). Until 1960 it carried the monthly notices of the RAS, at which time these were transferred to the newly established _[Quarterly Journal of the Royal Astronomical Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quarterly\_Journal\_of\_the\_Royal\_Astronomical\_Society "Quarterly Journal of the Royal Astronomical Society")_ (1960–1996) and then to its successor journal _[Astronomy & Geophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy\_%26\_Geophysics "Astronomy & Geophysics")_ (since 1997). Until 1965, MNRAS was published in-house by the society; from 1965 to 2012 it was published by [Blackwell Publishing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wiley-Blackwell "Wiley-Blackwell") (later part of [Wiley-Blackwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wiley-Blackwell "Wiley-Blackwell")) on behalf of the RAS. From 2013, MNRAS is published by [Oxford University Press](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxford_University_Press "Oxford University Press") (OUP).[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-oup-1)

The journal is no longer monthly, with thirty-six issues a year divided into nine volumes. The Letters section had originally appeared on pink paper in the print edition, but moved online only in the early 2000s. Print publication ceased after the April 2020 volume, during the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic"), with the [journal becoming online-only](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_journal "Online journal").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-online-5)

MNRAS publishes peer-reviewed articles on original research in astronomy and astrophysics. Two sorts of article are carried by MNRAS: papers, which can be of any length, and letters, which are published more quickly but are limited to five pages in length. Editorial control of the journal is exercised by the RAS through an [editorial board](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Editorial_board "Editorial board") of professional [astronomers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomers "Astronomers"); since July 2012, the [editor-in-chief](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Editor-in-chief "Editor-in-chief") has been [David Flower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Flower "David Flower") ([University of Durham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Durham "University of Durham")).[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-new_eic_2012-6)

In 2022 MNRAS published 3441 articles, more than any other astronomy journal.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-number_papers-3)

In January 2024 MNRAS became a [gold open access](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold_open_access "Gold open access") journal, making all articles free to read online as soon as they are published, under a [Creative Commons Attribution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creative_Commons_license "Creative Commons license") (CC-BY) licence.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-gold_oa-2) There are no subscription fees for readers, instead the costs of publication are met by an [article processing charge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Article_processing_charge "Article processing charge") (APC) on the authors. As of June 2024, the APCs are £2310 for a standard article and £1100 for a letter; RAS Fellows receive a 20% discount on these fees.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-7) In many cases the APCs are paid for by a [read and publish agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Read_and_publish_agreement&action=edit&redlink=1 "Read and publish agreement (page does not exist)") between the author's institution and OUP,[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-8) and authors based in [developing countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_countries "Developing countries") are entitled to an APC waiver.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-:0-9) If an author is not from a developing country but is unable to pay the APC, MNRAS provides partial or full waivers on a case-by-case basis.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-:0-9)

Prior to 2024, MNRAS used a combination of [green open access](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_open_access "Green open access"), [delayed open access](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delayed_open_access "Delayed open access") (36 months) and optional [hybrid open access](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hybrid_open_access "Hybrid open access"). There were no fees for authors, from the founding of the journal in 1927 until the end of 2023, with all costs of publications being met by subscriptions. Green open access was encouraged through [self-archiving](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-archiving "Self-archiving") by authors on personal webpages, in [institutional repositories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institutional_repository "Institutional repository"), and on the [arXiv](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ArXiv "ArXiv") preprint server. Fellows of the RAS were provided with free online access to the RAS journals as part of their membership benefits.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly_Notices_of_the_Royal_Astronomical_Society#cite_note-10)
