Chemical compound

**Aminoacetonitrile** is the [organic compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_compound "Organic compound") with the formula H 2 N−CH 2−C≡N. The compound is a colorless liquid. It is unstable at room temperature, owing to the incompatibility of the amine nucleophile and the nitrile electrophile. For this reason it is usually encountered as the chloride and bisulfate salts of the [ammonium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonium "Ammonium") derivative, i.e., [NCCH 2 NH 3]+Cl− and [NCCH 2 NH 3]+HSO 4−.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aminoacetonitrile#cite_note-eros-3)

Production and applications
---------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Aminoacetonitrile&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Production and applications")]

Industrially aminoacetonitrile is produced from [glycolonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycolonitrile "Glycolonitrile") by reaction with ammonia:

HOCH 2 CN + NH 3 → H 2 NCH 2 CN + H 2 O
The aminoacetonitrile can be hydrolysed to give [glycine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycine "Glycine"):[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aminoacetonitrile#cite_note-Ullmann-4) Being bifunctional, it is useful in the synthesis of diverse nitrogen-containing [heterocycles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heterocycle "Heterocycle").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aminoacetonitrile#cite_note-eros-3)

Aminoacetonitrile derivatives are useful antihelmintics. They act as nematode specific ACh agonists[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aminoacetonitrile#cite_note-5) causing a spastic paralysis and rapid expulsion from the host.

Occurrence in the interstellar medium
-------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Aminoacetonitrile&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Occurrence in the interstellar medium")]

Using [radio astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Planck_Institute_for_Radio_Astronomy "Max Planck Institute for Radio Astronomy"), aminoacetonitrile was discovered in the [Large Molecule Heimat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_Molecule_Heimat "Large Molecule Heimat"), a giant gas cloud near the [Galactic Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galactic_Center "Galactic Center") in the constellation [Sagittarius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagittarius_(constellation) "Sagittarius (constellation)").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aminoacetonitrile#cite_note-6) This discovery is significant to the debate on whether glycine exists widely in the universe.
