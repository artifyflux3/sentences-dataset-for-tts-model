Like several of his family he addicted himself partly to the study of Pythagorean philosophy, partly to the science of medicine . He wrote a treatise upon the works of Epicharmus, in which, on the authority of Epicharmus and Pythagoras himself, he maintained that the Doric was the proper dialect of the Orphic hymns.
