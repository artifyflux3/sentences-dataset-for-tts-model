He studied at Sidney Sussex College, Cambridge , and was later appointed as Assistant Director of the university's Solar Physics Observatory, a position that he held from 1950 to 1960.

[ 1 ] During his career, he visited several countries to carry out astronomical investigations, including Fiji , Bolivia (twice) and Canada .  He was President of the Royal Astronomical Society from 1973 to 1975.  He held his Oxford professorship in conjunction with a fellowship of New College, Oxford .
