**Georges Henri Joseph Édouard Lemaître** ( [_lə-MET-rə_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key"); French:[[ʒɔʁʒ ləmɛːtʁ]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/French "Help:IPA/French")[](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/9/96/Fr-Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre.oga/Fr-Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre.oga.mp3 "Play audio")[ⓘ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Fr-Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre.oga "File:Fr-Georges Lemaître.oga"); 17 July 1894 – 20 June 1966) was a Belgian [Catholic priest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Priesthood_in_the_Catholic_Church "Priesthood in the Catholic Church"), theoretical physicist, and mathematician who made major contributions to [cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_cosmology "Physical cosmology") and [astrophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrophysics "Astrophysics").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-1) He was the first to argue that the recession of [galaxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy") is evidence of an [expanding universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metric_expansion_of_space "Metric expansion of space") and to connect the observational [Hubble–Lemaître law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hubble%E2%80%93Lema%C3%AEtre_law "Hubble–Lemaître law")[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-namechange-2) with the solution to the [Einstein field equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_field_equations "Einstein field equations") in the [general theory of relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity "General relativity") for a homogenous and isotropic universe.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELema%C3%AEtre1927a49-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEReich2011-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELivio2011171%E2%80%93173-5) That work led Lemaître to propose what he called the "hypothesis of the primeval atom", now regarded as the first formulation of the [Big Bang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Bang "Big Bang") theory of the origin of the universe.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELema%C3%AEtre1931b706-6)[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-7)

Lemaître studied engineering, mathematics, physics, and philosophy at the [Catholic University of Louvain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_University_of_Leuven_(1834%E2%80%931968) "Catholic University of Leuven (1834–1968)") and was ordained as a priest of the [Archdiocese of Mechelen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Mechelen%E2%80%93Brussels "Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Mechelen–Brussels") in 1923. His ecclesiastical superior and mentor, [Cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholic_Church) "Cardinal (Catholic Church)")[Désiré-Joseph Mercier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%C3%A9sir%C3%A9-Joseph_Mercier "Désiré-Joseph Mercier"), encouraged and supported his scientific work, allowing Lemaître to travel to England, where he worked with the astrophysicist [Arthur Eddington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Eddington "Arthur Eddington") at the [University of Cambridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Cambridge "University of Cambridge") in 1923–1924, and to the United States, where he worked with [Harlow Shapley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harlow_Shapley "Harlow Shapley") at the [Harvard College Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_College_Observatory "Harvard College Observatory") and at the [Massachusetts Institute of Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") (MIT) in 1924–1925.

Lemaître was a professor of physics at Louvain from 1927 until his retirement in 1964. A pioneer in the use of [computers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer "Computer") in physics research, in the 1930s he showed, with [Manuel Sandoval Vallarta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manuel_Sandoval_Vallarta "Manuel Sandoval Vallarta") of MIT, that [cosmic rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_ray "Cosmic ray") are deflected by the [Earth's magnetic field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth%27s_magnetic_field "Earth's magnetic field") and must therefore carry [electric charge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_charge "Electric charge"). Lemaître also argued in favor of including a positive [cosmological constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmological_constant "Cosmological constant") in the Einstein field equations, both for conceptual reasons and to help reconcile the [age of the universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_the_universe "Age of the universe") inferred from the Hubble–Lemaître law with the ages of the oldest stars and the abundances of [radionuclides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radionuclide "Radionuclide").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeebles202072%E2%80%9373-8)

Father Lemaître remained until his death a [secular priest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secular_clergy "Secular clergy") of the Archdiocese of Mechelen (after 1961, the "Archdiocese of Mechelen-Brussels"). In 1935, he was made an honorary [canon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canon_(priest) "Canon (priest)") of [St. Rumbold's Cathedral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._Rumbold%27s_Cathedral "St. Rumbold's Cathedral"). In 1960, [Pope John XXIII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope_John_XXIII "Pope John XXIII") appointed him as [Domestic Prelate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prelate_of_Honour_of_His_Holiness "Prelate of Honour of His Holiness"), entitling him to be addressed as "[Monsignor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsignor "Monsignor")". In that same year he was appointed as president of the [Pontifical Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontifical_Academy_of_Sciences "Pontifical Academy of Sciences"), a post that he occupied until his death.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-Georges_Lemaitre-9) Among other awards, Lemaître received the first [Eddington Medal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eddington_Medal "Eddington Medal") of the [Royal Astronomical Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Astronomical_Society "Royal Astronomical Society") in 1953, "for his work on the expansion of the universe".[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELema%C3%AEtre19532-10)

Georges Lemaître was born in [Charleroi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charleroi "Charleroi"), Belgium, the eldest of four children of Joseph Lemaître, a prosperous industrialist who owned a glassworks factory,[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitton2017-11) and Marguerite _née_ Lannoy, who was the daughter of a brewer.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert201533-12) Georges was educated at the _Collège du Sacré-Cœur_, a grammar school in Charleroi run by the [Jesuits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society_of_Jesus "Society of Jesus").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitton2017-11) In 1910, after a fire destroyed the glassworks, the family moved to [Brussels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City_of_Brussels "City of Brussels"), where Joseph had found a new position as manager for the French bank _[Société Générale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9\_G%C3%A9n%C3%A9rale "Société Générale")_. Georges then became a pupil at another Jesuit school, [St. Michael's College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._Michael%27s_College,_Brussels "St. Michael's College, Brussels").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitton2017-11) Although he had expressed his interest in pursuing a religious vocation, his father convinced him to attend university first and to train as a [mining engineer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mining_engineering "Mining engineering").[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

### University studies and military service

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: University studies and military service")]

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Lema%C3%AEtre_1914.jpg/250px-Lema%C3%AEtre_1914.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Lema%C3%AEtre_1914.jpg)

Georges Lemaître (left) and his younger brother Jacques (right) in uniform after they volunteered for the Belgian Army on 7 August 1914

In 1911, Lemaître began to study engineering at the [Catholic University of Louvain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_University_of_Leuven_(1834%E2%80%931968) "Catholic University of Leuven (1834–1968)"). In 1914, after the outbreak of [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I"), Lemaître interrupted his studies to volunteer for the [Belgian army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgian_Land_Component "Belgian Land Component"). He participated in the [Battle of the Yser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Yser "Battle of the Yser"), in which the Belgians succeeded in halting the [German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Empire "German Empire") advance. When the army transferred him from the infantry to artillery, Lemaître was sent to complete a course on [ballistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ballistics "Ballistics"). His prospects of promotion to [officer rank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Officer_(armed_forces) "Officer (armed forces)") were dashed after he was marked down for [insubordination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insubordination "Insubordination") as a result of pointing out to the instructor a mathematical error in the official artillery manual.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitton2017-11) However, at the end of hostilities he received the Belgian [War Cross](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_Cross_(Belgium) "War Cross (Belgium)") with bronze palm,[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-13) one of only five rank-and-file troops to receive that award from the hands of King [Albert I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_I_of_Belgium "Albert I of Belgium").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitton2017-11)

Lemaître was an admirer of the French Catholic writer [Léon Bloy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Bloy "Léon Bloy").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert201558-14) During a leave from his military service in World War I, Lemaître visited Bloy in [Bourg-la-Reine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bourg-la-Reine "Bourg-la-Reine"), near Paris, where Bloy was living in a house that had belonged to his late friend and fellow writer [Charles Péguy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_P%C3%A9guy "Charles Péguy").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert201558-14) On that occasion, Lemaître shared with Bloy an essay entitled _Les trois premières paroles de Dieu_ ("The First Three Words of God"), in which he attempted to reconcile the [Genesis creation narrative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genesis_creation_narrative "Genesis creation narrative") with modern science.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert201558-14) Bloy, however, was unimpressed and advised Lemaître to grow more familiar with the works of the [Church Fathers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_Fathers "Church Fathers").[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-15) This experience may have contributed to Lemaître's abandonment of the "concordist" effort to reconcile theological and scientific knowledge at a common intellectual level.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert2016-16)

After the war, Lemaître abandoned engineering for the study of [physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physics "Physics") and [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics"). In 1919 he also completed the course taught at the [Higher Institute of Philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Higher_Institute_of_Philosophy "Higher Institute of Philosophy"), established by [Cardinal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholic_Church) "Cardinal (Catholic Church)")[Désiré-Joseph Mercier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%C3%A9sir%C3%A9-Joseph_Mercier "Désiré-Joseph Mercier") to promote [neo-Thomism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neo-scholasticism "Neo-scholasticism").[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHolderMitton201310-17) Lemaître obtained his [doctorate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doctorate "Doctorate") in science in 1920 with a thesis entitled _L'approximation des fonctions de plusieurs variables réelles_ ("The approximation of functions of several real variables"), written under the direction of mathematician [Charles de la Vallée-Poussin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_de_la_Vall%C3%A9e-Poussin "Charles de la Vallée-Poussin").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-18)

Lemaître had considered joining the Jesuits or the [Benedictines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benedictines "Benedictines"), but finally decided to prepare instead for the [diocesan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diocesan_priest "Diocesan priest")[priesthood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Priesthood_(Catholic_Church) "Priesthood (Catholic Church)").[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFarrell2008-19) Between 1920 and 1923 he was a student at the _Maison Saint-Rombaut_, the [seminary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seminary "Seminary") for "late vocations" (i.e., mature students for the priesthood) of the [Archdiocese of Mechelen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Mechelen%E2%80%93Brussels "Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Mechelen–Brussels"). It was during his spare time at the seminary that Lemaître learned the [general theory of relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_theory_of_relativity "General theory of relativity"). He was [ordained](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordination "Ordination") as a priest on 22 September 1923 by Cardinal Mercier.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert1996309%E2%80%93343-20)[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert199728%E2%80%9353-21) As a diocesan priest in [French-speaking Belgium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Community_of_Belgium "French Community of Belgium"), he was known as "[Abbé](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abb%C3%A9 "Abbé") Lemaître".

At the seminary, Lemaître joined the _Fraternité sacerdotale des Amis de Jésus_ ("Priestly fraternity of the Friends of Jesus"), which had been created by Cardinal Mercier to promote the spiritual life of select diocesan priests and which was established canonically by his successor, Cardinal [Jozef-Ernest van Roey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jozef-Ernest_van_Roey "Jozef-Ernest van Roey"). As a member of the fraternity, Lemaître took [vows](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solemn_vow "Solemn vow") of [chastity, poverty, and obedience](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evangelical_counsels "Evangelical counsels"), as well as special _votum immolationis_ ("vow of immolation") promising complete submission to the person of Christ. In the spirit of the fraternity, Lemaître did not discuss his involvement with the _Amis de Jésus_ outside of the group, but he regularly made silent retreats in a house called _Regina Pacis_ ("Queen of Peace") in [Schilde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schilde "Schilde"), near [Antwerp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antwerp "Antwerp"), and also undertook translations of the [mystical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mysticism "Mysticism") works of [John of Ruusbroec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_of_Ruusbroec "John of Ruusbroec").[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert201569%E2%80%9375-22)

### Voyage to Britain and the US

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Voyage to Britain and the US")]

[![Image 4](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Lemaitre-1.jpg/250px-Lemaitre-1.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Lemaitre-1.jpg)

A young Georges Lemaître in clerical dress, early 1920s

In 1922, Lemaître applied to the Belgian Ministry of Sciences and Arts for a travel bursary. As part of that application, he submitted a thesis on the astronomical implications of general relativity that included a demonstration that the most general form of the [Einstein field equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_field_equations "Einstein field equations") included a [cosmological constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmological_constant "Cosmological constant") term. The jury awarded Lemaître a prize of 8,000 [Belgian francs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgian_franc "Belgian franc").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitton2017-11)

Cardinal Mercier supported Lemaître's scientific work and helped him to obtain further financial support for a two-year visit to Great Britain and the United States.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitton2017-11) Only ten days after his ordination, Lemaître left Belgium to take up residence at St Edmund's House, then a community of Catholic priests studying for degrees at the [University of Cambridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Cambridge "University of Cambridge") and which would later become [St Edmund's College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_Edmund%27s_College,_Cambridge "St Edmund's College, Cambridge"). At Cambridge, Lemaître was a research associate in astronomy and worked with the eminent astrophysicist [Arthur Eddington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Eddington "Arthur Eddington"), who introduced Lemaître to modern [cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_cosmology "Physical cosmology"), [stellar astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy#Stellar_astronomy "Astronomy"), and [numerical analysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numerical_analysis "Numerical analysis").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitton2017-11)

Lemaître then spent the following year at the [Harvard College Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_College_Observatory "Harvard College Observatory"), in [Cambridge, Massachusetts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts "Cambridge, Massachusetts"), working with [Harlow Shapley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harlow_Shapley "Harlow Shapley"), a leading expert in the study of what were then called "spiral nebulae" (now identified as [spiral galaxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spiral_galaxy "Spiral galaxy")).[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitton2017-11) Lemaître also registered at that time in the doctoral program in science at the [Massachusetts Institute of Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") (MIT), with the Belgian engineer Paul Heymans as his official advisor.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert201599-23)

On his return to Belgium in 1925, Lemaître became a part-time lecturer at the [Catholic University of Louvain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_University_of_Leuven_(1834%E2%80%931968) "Catholic University of Leuven (1834–1968)") and began working on a report that was finally published in 1927 in the _Annales de la Société Scientifique de Bruxelles_ ("Annals of the Scientific Society of Brussels"), under the title _Un Univers homogène de masse constante et de rayon croissant rendant compte de la vitesse radiale des nébuleuses extragalactiques_ ("A homogeneous Universe of constant mass and growing radius accounting for the radial velocity of extragalactic nebulae").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELema%C3%AEtre1927a49-3) There he developed (independently of the earlier work of [Alexander Friedmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Friedmann "Alexander Friedmann")) the argument that the equations of [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein")'s [general theory of relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_relativity "General relativity") implied that the Universe is not static (see [Friedmann equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedmann_equations "Friedmann equations")). Lemaître connected this prediction to what he argued was a simple relation of proportionality between the average [recessional velocity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recessional_velocity "Recessional velocity") of galaxies and their distance to the Earth.

This 1927 paper had little impact because the _Annales de la Société Scientifique de Bruxelles_ were not widely read by astronomers or physicists outside of Belgium. Moreover, the initial state that Lemaître proposed for the Universe in this 1927 paper was Einstein's model of a static universe with a [cosmological constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmological_constant "Cosmological constant"),[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBelenkiy201238-24) but at this time, Einstein insisted that only a static picture of the universe was physically acceptable. Lemaître later recalled Einstein saying to him: "_vos calculs sont corrects, mais votre physique est abominable_" ("your calculations are correct, but your physics is atrocious").[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDeprit1984370-25)

Also in 1927, Lemaître returned to MIT to defend his doctoral dissertation on _The gravitational field in a fluid sphere of uniform invariant density according to the theory of relativity_.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELema%C3%AEtre1927b-26) Upon obtaining that second doctorate, Lemaître's was appointed [ordinary professor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordinary_professor "Ordinary professor") at the Catholic University of Louvain.

### Hubble-Lemaître law

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Hubble-Lemaître law")]

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Universe_expansion-en.svg/250px-Universe_expansion-en.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Universe_expansion-en.svg)

According to the [Big Bang theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Bang_theory "Big Bang theory"), the [universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universe "Universe") emerged from an extremely dense and hot state ([singularity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitational_singularity "Gravitational singularity")). [Space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space "Space") itself has been expanding ever since, carrying [galaxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galaxy "Galaxy") with it, like raisins in a rising loaf of bread. The graphic scheme above is an artist's conception illustrating the expansion of a portion of a flat universe.

In 1929, the US astronomer [Edwin Hubble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edwin_Hubble "Edwin Hubble") published a paper in the _[Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proceedings\_of\_the\_National\_Academy\_of\_Sciences\_of\_the\_United\_States\_of\_America "Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America")_ showing, based on better and more abundant data than what Lemaître had had at his disposal in 1927, that, in the average, galaxies recede at a velocity proportional to their distance from the observer. Although Hubble himself did not interpret that result in terms of an expanding Universe, his work attracted widespread attention and soon convinced many experts, including Einstein, that the Universe is not static. The proportionality between distance and recessional velocity for galaxies has since been commonly known as "[Hubble's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hubble%27s_law "Hubble's law")", but in 2018 the [International Astronomical Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union "International Astronomical Union") (IAU) adopted a resolution recommending that it be referred to as the "Hubble-Lemaître law".[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-namechange-2)

In 1931, an English translation of Lemaître's 1927 report appeared in the _[Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monthly\_Notices\_of\_the\_Royal\_Astronomical\_Society "Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society")_, with a commentary by [Arthur Eddington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Eddington "Arthur Eddington") that characterized Lemaître's work as a "brilliant solution" to the outstanding problems of cosmology and a response by Lemaître to Eddington's comments.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELema%C3%AEtre1931a490%E2%80%93501-27) This English translation, however, omitted Lemaître's estimate of the "Hubble constant" for reasons that remained unclear for many years.[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWayNussbaumer20118-28) The issue was clarified in 2011 by [Mario Livio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mario_Livio "Mario Livio"): Lemaître himself removed those paragraphs when he prepared the English translation, opting instead to cite the stronger results that Hubble had published in 1929.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELivio2011171%E2%80%93173-5)

### Hypothesis of the primeval atom

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Hypothesis of the primeval atom")]

In March 1931 Lemaître wrote a brief report in which he proposed that the universe expanded from a single initial [quantum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum "Quantum"), which he called the "primeval atom". This was published in _[Nature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nature\_(journal) "Nature (journal)")_,[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELema%C3%AEtre1931b706-6) and later that year Lemaître participated in a public colloquium on "The Evolution of the Universe" held in London on 29 September 1931 to mark the centenary of the [British Association for the Advancement of Science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Science_Association "British Science Association").[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert2015151-29)

Lemaître's theory was first presented to a general audience in the December 1932 issue of _[Popular Science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popular\_Science "Popular Science")_, in a piece written by the astronomer [Donald Howard Menzel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Howard_Menzel "Donald Howard Menzel") of [Harvard University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_University "Harvard University").[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMenzel193252-30) In 1933–1934, Lemaître was a guest professor at the [Catholic University of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_University_of_America "Catholic University of America"), in [Washington, D.C.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington,_D.C. "Washington, D.C.")[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert2015180-31) At that time he also presented his work on the "Evolution of the Expanding Universe" before the US [National Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Academy_of_Sciences "National Academy of Sciences").[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELema%C3%AEtre193412%E2%80%9317-32) Lemaître became a scientific celebrity and newspapers around the world referred to him as the leader of a new physical cosmology.[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert2015179%E2%80%93184-33)

[![Image 6](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/MillikanLemaitreEinstein.jpg/330px-MillikanLemaitreEinstein.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:MillikanLemaitreEinstein.jpg)

[Robert Millikan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Andrews_Millikan "Robert Andrews Millikan"), Lemaître and [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein") after Lemaître's lecture at the [California Institute of Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Institute_of_Technology "California Institute of Technology") in January 1933.

Lemaître and Einstein met on four occasions: in 1927 in [Brussels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brussels "Brussels"), at the time of a [Solvay Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solvay_Conference "Solvay Conference"); in 1932 in Belgium, at the time of a cycle of conferences in Brussels; in California in January 1933;[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambertn.d.-34) and in 1935 at [Princeton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Princeton_University "Princeton University"). In 1933 at the [California Institute of Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Institute_of_Technology "California Institute of Technology"), after Lemaître presented his theory, Einstein stood up, applauded, and is reported to have said, "This is the most beautiful and satisfactory explanation of creation to which I have ever listened."[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKragh199955-35) However, there is disagreement over the reporting of this quote in the newspapers of the time, and it may be that Einstein was not referring to the "primordial atom" theory as a whole, but only to Lemaître's proposal that [cosmic rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_ray "Cosmic ray") could be "fossils" of the primordial decay.

Lemaître argued that cosmic rays could be a "fossil radiation" produced by the decay of the primeval atom. Much of his work in the 1930s was focused on cosmic rays. In 1946, Lemaître published a book on _L'Hypothèse de l'Atome Primitif_ ("The Primeval Atom Hypothesis"), which was translated into Spanish in the same year and into English in 1950. The astronomer [Fred Hoyle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fred_Hoyle "Fred Hoyle") introduced the term "[Big Bang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Bang "Big Bang")" in a 1949 BBC radio broadcast to refer to cosmological theories such as Lemaître's, according to which the Universe has a beginning in time.[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-36)[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-37) Hoyle remained throughout his life an opponent of such "Big Bang" theories, advocating instead a [steady-state model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steady-state_model "Steady-state model") of an eternal Universe.

In 1948, theoreticians [Ralph Alpher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ralph_Alpher "Ralph Alpher"), [Robert Herman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Herman "Robert Herman"), and [George Gamow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Gamow "George Gamow") predicted a different form of "fossil radiation" based on the Big Bang model, now known as the [cosmic microwave background](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_microwave_background "Cosmic microwave background") (CMB). The CMB was produced when the contents of the expanding Universe cooled sufficiently that they became transparent to [electromagnetic radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetic_radiation "Electromagnetic radiation"). In 1965, shortly before his death, Lemaître learned from his assistant [Odon Godart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odon_Godart "Odon Godart") of the [recent discovery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_cosmic_microwave_background_radiation "Discovery of cosmic microwave background radiation") of the CMB by radio astronomers [Arno Penzias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arno_Penzias "Arno Penzias") and [Robert Wilson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Woodrow_Wilson "Robert Woodrow Wilson"). That discovery convinced most experts of the scientific validity of the Big Bang.

### Cosmological constant

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Cosmological constant")]

Lemaître maintained throughout his career that the [Einstein field equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_field_equations "Einstein field equations") should incorporate a positive [cosmological constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmological_constant "Cosmological constant") (![Image 7: {\displaystyle \Lambda }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0ac0a4a98a414e3480335f9ba652d12571ec6733)) term. His reasoning was based on both theoretical and empirical considerations. Lemaître argued in 1958 that "if some extension of relativity towards a broader field, such as [quantum theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_mechanics "Quantum mechanics"), has to be achieved the superfluous ![Image 8: {\displaystyle \Lambda }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0ac0a4a98a414e3480335f9ba652d12571ec6733) term shall be very much welcomed".[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHolderMitton201331-38) He also held that the [accelerating expansion of the universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accelerating_expansion_of_the_universe "Accelerating expansion of the universe") induced by ![Image 9: {\displaystyle \Lambda }](https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/0ac0a4a98a414e3480335f9ba652d12571ec6733) could help to reconcile the [age of the universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_the_universe "Age of the universe") deduced from the [Hubble-Lemaître law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hubble%27s_law "Hubble's law") with the ages of the oldest stars and the observed abundances of [radionuclides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radionuclide "Radionuclide").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeebles202072%E2%80%9373-8) Lemaître argued for a positive cosmological constant both in print and in correspondence with Einstein, who was skeptical about the reality of such a term after abandoning his [model of a static universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein%27s_static_universe "Einstein's static universe") in the early 1930s.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeebles202072%E2%80%9373-8) The [Nobel Prize in Physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Physics "Nobel Prize in Physics") for 2011 was awarded to [Saul Perlmutter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saul_Perlmutter "Saul Perlmutter"), [Brian P. Schmidt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_P._Schmidt "Brian P. Schmidt"), and [Adam G. Riess](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_G._Riess "Adam G. Riess") for establishing the reality of the universe's accelerating expansion, based on extensive surveys of [Type Ia supernovae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Type_Ia_supernova "Type Ia supernova") used as astronomical "[standard candles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_distance_ladder "Cosmic distance ladder")". In the scientific background for that prize, the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences credited Lemaître with the idea that such an acceleration is driven by [vacuum energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vacuum_energy "Vacuum energy"), also modernly called in this context [dark energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dark_energy "Dark energy").[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEClass_for_Physics201113-39)

### Views on relation between science and faith

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Views on relation between science and faith")]

[![Image 10](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre_and_Arthur_Eddington_1938.jpg/330px-Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre_and_Arthur_Eddington_1938.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre_and_Arthur_Eddington_1938.jpg)

Lemaître and [Arthur Eddington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Eddington "Arthur Eddington") in discussion while sailing back from the 6th General Assembly of the [International Astronomical Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union "International Astronomical Union"), held in Stockholm in 1938

Lemaître viewed his work as a scientist as neither supporting nor contradicting any truths of the Catholic faith, and he was strongly opposed to making any arguments that mixed science with religion,[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert199728%E2%80%9353-21) although he held that the two were not in conflict.[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-40) He was always anxious that his work on cosmology should be judged on purely scientific criteria.

In 1951, [Pope Pius XII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope_Pius_XII "Pope Pius XII") gave an address to the [Pontifical Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontifical_Academy_of_Sciences "Pontifical Academy of Sciences"), with Lemaître in the audience, in which he drew a parallel between the new Big Bang cosmology and the Christian doctrine of _[creatio ex nihilo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creatio\_ex\_nihilo "Creatio ex nihilo")_:

> Indeed, it seems that the science of today, by going back in one leap millions of centuries, has succeeded in being a witness to that primordial _[Fiat Lux](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Let\_there\_be\_light "Let there be light")_, when, out of nothing, there burst forth with matter a sea of light and radiation [... Thus modern science has confirmed] with the concreteness of physical proofs the contingency of the universe and the well-founded deduction that about that time the cosmos issued from the hand of the Creator.[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-41)

Lemaître was reportedly horrified by that intervention and was later able, with the assistance of Father [Daniel O’Connell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Joseph_Kelly_O%27Connell "Daniel Joseph Kelly O'Connell"), the director of the [Vatican Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vatican_Observatory "Vatican Observatory"), to convince the Pope not make any further public statements on religious or philosophical interpretations of matters concerning [physical cosmology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_cosmology "Physical cosmology").[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHolderMitton201371-42)

According to the theoretical physicist and Nobel laureate [Paul Dirac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Dirac "Paul Dirac"),

> Once when I was talking with Lemaître about [his cosmological theory] and feeling stimulated by the grandeur of the picture that he has given us, I told him that I thought cosmology was the branch of science that lies closest to religion. However Lemaître did not agree with me. After thinking it over he suggested psychology as lying closest to religion.[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDirac1968-43)

Other scientific work
---------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Other scientific work")]

[![Image 11](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Lemaitre_Burroughs.jpg/330px-Lemaitre_Burroughs.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Lemaitre_Burroughs.jpg)

Georges Lemaître and Andrée Bartholomé using a Burroughs E101 computer in the numerical research laboratory of the Catholic University of Louvain, May 1959 (_Archives de l'Université catholique de Louvain_)

With [Manuel Sandoval Vallarta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manuel_Sandoval_Vallarta "Manuel Sandoval Vallarta"), whom he had met at MIT, Lemaître showed that the intensity of [cosmic rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_rays "Cosmic rays") varies with latitude because they are composed of [charged particles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charged_particle "Charged particle") and therefore are deflected by the [Earth's magnetic field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth%27s_magnetic_field "Earth's magnetic field").[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-44) In their calculations, Lemaître and Vallarta made use of MIT's new [differential analyzer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_analyzer "Differential analyzer") computer, developed by [Vannevar Bush](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vannevar_Bush "Vannevar Bush").[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert2015250-45) That work disproved the view, advocated among others by the Nobel laureate [Robert Millikan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Andrews_Millikan "Robert Andrews Millikan"), that cosmic rays were composed of high-energy photons. Lemaître and Vallarta also worked on a theory of primary cosmic radiation and applied it to their investigations of the Sun's magnetic field and the effects of the galaxy's rotation.

In 1933, Lemaître found an important inhomogeneous solution of [Einstein's field equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_field_equations "Einstein field equations") describing a spherical dust cloud, the [Lemaître–Tolman metric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lema%C3%AEtre%E2%80%93Tolman_metric "Lemaître–Tolman metric"). He became increasingly interested in problems of numerical computation and in the 1930s began to use the most powerful [calculator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calculator "Calculator") available at the time, the mechanical [Mercedes-Euklid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercedes-Euklid "Mercedes-Euklid"). In his only work in [physical chemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_chemistry "Physical chemistry"), Lemaître collaborated in the numerical calculation of the energy levels of monodeuteroethyelene (a molecule of [ethylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethylene "Ethylene") with one of its [hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen") atoms replaced by [deuterium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deuterium "Deuterium")).[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert2015175%E2%80%93176-46)

In 1948 Lemaître published a mathematical essay titled _Quaternions et espace elliptique_ ("[Quaternions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quaternion "Quaternion") and [elliptic space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptic_space "Elliptic space")").[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-47)[William Kingdon Clifford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Kingdon_Clifford "William Kingdon Clifford") had introduced the concept of elliptic space in 1873. Lemaître developed the theory of quaternions from first principles, in the spirit of the [Erlangen program](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erlangen_program "Erlangen program").[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Lemaître also worked on the [three-body problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-body_problem "Three-body problem"), introducing a new method of [regularization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regularization_(mathematics) "Regularization (mathematics)") to avoid [singularities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singularity_(mathematics) "Singularity (mathematics)") associated with the collisions of two bodies. In the 1950s he worked out an early version of the [fast Fourier transform](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_Fourier_transform "Fast Fourier transform"), later developed independently by [James Cooley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Cooley "James Cooley") and [John Tukey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Tukey "John Tukey").[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert2015312-48) He introduced the [Burroughs E101](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burroughs_Corporation "Burroughs Corporation") electromechanical computer to his university in the late 1950s. In his later years he collaborated with his nephew Gilbert Lemaître on a new [programming language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Programming_language "Programming language") called "Velocode", a precursor of [BASIC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BASIC "BASIC").[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHolderMitton201315-49)

During the 1950s, Lemaître gradually gave up part of his teaching workload, ending it completely when he took [emeritus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89m%C3%A9ritat "Éméritat") status in 1964. In 1960 he was named [domestic prelate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honorary_Prelate "Honorary Prelate") (with the treatment of "[Monsignor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsignor "Monsignor")") by [Pope John XXIII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope_John_XXIII "Pope John XXIII").[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-:0-50) Following the death of the physician and [Capuchin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_of_Friars_Minor_Capuchin "Order of Friars Minor Capuchin") friar [Agostino Gemelli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agostino_Gemelli "Agostino Gemelli"), Lemaître was appointed to succeed him as the second president of the [Pontifical Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontifical_Academy_of_Sciences "Pontifical Academy of Sciences").

During the [Second Vatican Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vatican_II "Vatican II") of 1962–65, the pope asked Lemaître to serve on the 4th session of the [Pontifical Commission on Birth Control](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontifical_Commission_on_Birth_Control "Pontifical Commission on Birth Control").[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcClory1998205-51) However, since his health made it impossible for him to travel to Rome —he suffered a heart attack in December 1964— Lemaître demurred. He told a [Dominican](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominican_Order "Dominican Order") colleague, Père Henri de Riedmatten, that he thought it was dangerous for a mathematician to venture outside of his area of expertise.[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELambert2015364-52) Lemaître died on 20 June 1966, shortly after having learned of the discovery of [cosmic microwave background radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_microwave_background_radiation "Cosmic microwave background radiation"), which provided solid experimental support for his theory of the Big Bang.[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-53)

Lemaître was strongly opposed to the _Leuven Vlaams_ ("Flemish Leuven") movement that sought to make instruction at the Catholic University of Leuven monolingual in [Dutch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_language "Dutch language"). With the historian [Gérard Garitte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%A9rard_Garitte "Gérard Garitte"), in 1962 Lemaître established the _Association du corps académique et du personnel scientifique de l’Université de Louvain_ (ACAPSUL, "Association of the faculty and scientific personnel of the University of Louvain") to advocate for the continued use of the French language in that institution.[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-54) After Lemaître's death, the [university was separated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split_of_the_Catholic_University_of_Leuven "Split of the Catholic University of Leuven") into a Dutch-speaking institution, [KU Leuven](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KU_Leuven "KU Leuven"), and a French-speaking institution, [UCLouvain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UCLouvain "UCLouvain"), based in the planned town of [Louvain-la-Neuve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louvain-la-Neuve "Louvain-la-Neuve") ("New Leuven") that was built for that purpose just across the [language border](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language_border "Language border") in [Walloon Brabant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walloon_Brabant "Walloon Brabant").

Honours and recognition
-----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre&action=edit&section=12 "Edit section: Honours and recognition")]

On 27 July 1935 Lemaître was appointed as an [honorary canon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cathedral_chapter "Cathedral chapter") of [St. Rumbold's Cathedral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._Rumbold%27s_Cathedral "St. Rumbold's Cathedral") by Cardinal [Jozef-Ernest van Roey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jozef-Ernest_van_Roey "Jozef-Ernest van Roey").[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-55) He was elected a member of the [Pontifical Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontifical_Academy_of_Sciences "Pontifical Academy of Sciences") in 1936, and took an active role there, serving as its president from March 1960 until his death.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-Georges_Lemaitre-9) In 1941, he was elected a member of the [Royal Academy of Sciences and Arts of Belgium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Sciences_and_Arts_of_Belgium "Royal Academy of Sciences and Arts of Belgium").[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-:0-50)

On 17 March 1934, Lemaître received the [Francqui Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francqui_Prize "Francqui Prize"), the highest Belgian scientific distinction, from [King Leopold III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leopold_III_of_Belgium "Leopold III of Belgium").[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-:0-50) His proposers were [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein"), Charles de la Vallée-Poussin and Alexandre de Hemptinne. The members of the international jury were Eddington, [Langevin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Langevin "Paul Langevin"), [Théophile de Donder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Th%C3%A9ophile_de_Donder "Théophile de Donder") and Marcel Dehalu. The same year he received the Mendel Medal of the [Villanova University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Villanova_University "Villanova University").[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-56)

In 1936, Lemaître received the [Prix Jules Janssen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prix_Jules_Janssen "Prix Jules Janssen"), the highest award of the [Société astronomique de France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_astronomique_de_France "Société astronomique de France"), the French astronomical society.[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-57) Another distinction that the Belgian government reserves for exceptional scientists was allotted to him in 1950: the decennial prize for applied sciences for the period 1933–1942.[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-:0-50) Lemaître was elected to the [American Philosophical Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Philosophical_Society "American Philosophical Society") in 1945.[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-58) In 1953, he was given the inaugural [Eddington Medal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eddington_Medal "Eddington Medal") by the [Royal Astronomical Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Astronomical_Society "Royal Astronomical Society").[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTELema%C3%AEtre19532-10)[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-59)

In 2005, Lemaître was voted to the 61st place of _[De Grootste Belg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De\_Grootste\_Belg "De Grootste Belg")_ ("The Greatest Belgian"), a [Flemish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flanders "Flanders") television program on the [VRT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vlaamse_Radio-_en_Televisieomroeporganisatie "Vlaamse Radio- en Televisieomroeporganisatie"). In the same year he was voted to the 78th place by the audience of the _[Les plus grands Belges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Les\_plus\_grands\_Belges "Les plus grands Belges")_ ("The Greatest Belgians"), a television show of the [RTBF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RTBF "RTBF"). Later, in December 2022, VRT recovered in its archives a lost 20-minute interview with Georges Lemaître in 1964, "a gem," says cosmologist [Thomas Hertog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Hertog "Thomas Hertog").[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-BRT_interview_1964-60)[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-61) On 17 July 2018, [Google Doodle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Doodle "Google Doodle") celebrated Georges Lemaître's 124th birthday.[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-62) On 26 October 2018, an electronic vote among all members of the [International Astronomical Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union "International Astronomical Union") voted 78% to recommend changing the name of the Hubble law to the [Hubble–Lemaître law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hubble%E2%80%93Lema%C3%AEtre_law "Hubble–Lemaître law").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-namechange-2)[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georges_Lema%C3%AEtre#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGibney2018-63)
