Chemical compound

**Thiocyanic acid** is a [chemical compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_compound "Chemical compound") with the [formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula")[H](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen")[S](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfur "Sulfur")[C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon")[N](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen "Nitrogen") and [structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Structural_formula "Structural formula")H−S−C≡N, which exists as a [tautomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tautomer "Tautomer") with isothiocyanic acid (H−N=C=S).[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiocyanic_acid#cite_note-8) The isothiocyanic acid tautomer tends to dominate with the compound being about 95% isothiocyanic acid in the [vapor phase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vapor_phase "Vapor phase").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiocyanic_acid#cite_note-9)

[![Image 37](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Thiocyanic_Acid_Tautomerism_V.1.svg/250px-Thiocyanic_Acid_Tautomerism_V.1.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Thiocyanic_Acid_Tautomerism_V.1.svg)

Tautomerism between thiocyanic acid (left) and isothiocyanic acid (right)

It is a moderately [strong acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strong_acid "Strong acid"),[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiocyanic_acid#cite_note-10) with a [p _K_ a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PKa "PKa") of 1.1 at 20°C and extrapolated to zero [ionic strength](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionic_strength "Ionic strength").[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiocyanic_acid#cite_note-11)

One of the thiocyanic acid tautomers, HSCN, is predicted to have a [triple bond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triple_bond "Triple bond") between [carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon "Carbon") and [nitrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen "Nitrogen"). Thiocyanic acid has been observed spectroscopically.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiocyanic_acid#cite_note-12)

The [salts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salt_(chemistry) "Salt (chemistry)") and [esters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ester "Ester") of thiocyanic acid are known as [thiocyanates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiocyanate "Thiocyanate"). The salts are composed of the thiocyanate ion ([SCN]−) and a suitable cation (e.g., [potassium thiocyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potassium_thiocyanate "Potassium thiocyanate"), KSCN). The esters of thiocyanic acid have the general structure R−S−C≡N, where R stands for an [organyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organyl "Organyl") group.

Isothiocyanic acid, HNCS, is a [Lewis acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lewis_acid "Lewis acid") whose free energy, enthalpy and entropy changes for its 1:1 association with a variety of Lewis bases in carbon tetrachloride solution at 25°C have been reported.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiocyanic_acid#cite_note-13)< HNCS acceptor properties are discussed in the [ECW model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ECW_model "ECW model"). The salts are composed of the thiocyanate ion ([SCN]−) and a suitable cation (e.g., [ammonium thiocyanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonium_thiocyanate "Ammonium thiocyanate"), [NH 4]+[SCN]−). Isothiocyanic acid forms isothiocyanates R−N=C=S, where R stands for an [organyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organyl "Organyl") group.

[Thiocyanuric acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thiocyanuric_acid "Thiocyanuric acid") is a stable trimer of thiocyanic acid.
