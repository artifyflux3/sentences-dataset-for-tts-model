In [orbital mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbital_mechanics "Orbital mechanics"), a **transfer orbit** is an intermediate [elliptical orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliptical_orbit "Elliptical orbit") that is used to move a [spacecraft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spacecraft "Spacecraft") in an [orbital maneuver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbital_maneuver "Orbital maneuver") from one [circular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circular_orbit "Circular orbit"), or largely circular, [orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbit "Orbit") to another.

There are several types of transfer orbits, which vary in their [energy efficiency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_conversion_efficiency "Energy conversion efficiency") and speed of transfer. These include:

*   [Hohmann transfer orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hohmann_transfer_orbit "Hohmann transfer orbit"), an elliptical orbit used to transfer a spacecraft between two circular orbits of different altitudes in the same plane
*   [Bi-elliptic transfer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bi-elliptic_transfer "Bi-elliptic transfer"), a slower method of transfer, but one that may be more efficient than a Hohmann transfer orbit
*   [Geostationary transfer orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geostationary_transfer_orbit "Geostationary transfer orbit") or [geosynchronous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geosynchronous "Geosynchronous") transfer orbit is usually also a Hohmann transfer orbit
*   _Lunar transfer orbit_ is an orbit that touches [Low Earth orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low_Earth_orbit "Low Earth orbit") and a [lunar orbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lunar_orbit "Lunar orbit").
