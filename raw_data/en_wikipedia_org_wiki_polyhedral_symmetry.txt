In [geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometry "Geometry"), the **polyhedral groups** are the [symmetry groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetry_group "Symmetry group") of the [Platonic solids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Platonic_solid "Platonic solid").

There are three polyhedral groups:

*   The [tetrahedral group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetrahedral_symmetry "Tetrahedral symmetry") of order 12, rotational symmetry group of the [regular tetrahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetrahedron "Tetrahedron"). It is isomorphic to [A 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternating_group "Alternating group"). 
    *   The [conjugacy classes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conjugacy_class "Conjugacy class") of T are: 
        *   identity

*   The [octahedral group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octahedral_symmetry "Octahedral symmetry") of order 24, rotational symmetry group of the [cube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cube "Cube") and the [regular octahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octahedron "Octahedron"). It is isomorphic to [S 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetric_group "Symmetric group"). 
    *   The conjugacy classes of O are:

*   The [icosahedral group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Icosahedral_symmetry "Icosahedral symmetry") of order 60, rotational symmetry group of the [regular dodecahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dodecahedron "Dodecahedron") and the [regular icosahedron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Icosahedron "Icosahedron"). It is isomorphic to [A 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternating_group "Alternating group").

These symmetries double to 24, 48, 120 respectively for the full reflectional groups. The reflection symmetries have 6, 9, and 15 mirrors respectively. The octahedral symmetry, [4,3] can be seen as the union of 6 tetrahedral symmetry [3,3] mirrors, and 3 mirrors of [dihedral symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dihedral_symmetry "Dihedral symmetry") Dih 2, [2,2]. [Pyritohedral symmetry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyritohedral_symmetry "Pyritohedral symmetry") is another doubling of tetrahedral symmetry.
