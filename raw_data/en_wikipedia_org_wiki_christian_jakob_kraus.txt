Biography [ edit ] A native of Osterode ( East Prussia ), Kraus studied at the universities of Königsberg and Göttingen . In 1782 he became a professor of practical philosophy and cameralism in Königsberg. A student of Immanuel Kant , Kraus was famous for importing the ideas of Adam Smith into the German academic scene. He was also a librarian of the Königsberg Public Library from 1786 to 1804. Kraus encouraged the East Prussian officials and nobility to improve rural conditions in the province; some of his ideas were later adapted in the era of Prussian reforms . Kraus died in Königsberg in 1807.
