When the jawbone , the only known fossil of this creature, was uncovered in Latvia , it was mistaken as a lobe-fin fish. However, when it was analyzed, it proved to hold many similarities to Elginerpeton , from Scotland . It was then declared belonging to the earliest group of tetrapods.
