Thomas Bunbury Gough (13 June 1777 – 8 May 1860) was an Anglo-Irish priest who was Dean of Derry in the Church of Ireland for four decades.

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] Gough was born in County Limerick into an Anglo-Irish family that had moved from Wiltshire to Ireland in the 17th century. His younger brother was Field-Marshal Hugh Gough, 1st Viscount Gough . He was educated at Trinity College, Dublin .

[ 3 ] He married Hon. Charlotte Bloomfield, daughter of 2nd Viscount Jocelyn , granddaughter of Lord Chancellor Robert Jocelyn, 1st Viscount Jocelyn , and sister of Benjamin Bloomfield, 1st Baron Bloomfield . His sons were Gen. Sir John Bloomfield Gough of the Indian Army , George Gough of the Bengal Civil Service, and Col. Thomas Bunbury Gough, who was killed in Battle of the Great Redan in the Crimean War ; and Rev.

Benjamin Gough , who was also Dean of Derry.

[ 1 ] He was Chancellor of Ardfert from 1811 until 1815; [ 4 ] and Dean of Derry from 1820 until his death at the deanery 40 years later.
