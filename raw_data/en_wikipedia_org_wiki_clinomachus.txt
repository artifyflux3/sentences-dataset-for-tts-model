He is said by Diogenes Laërtius to have been the first who composed treatises on the fundamental principles of dialectics , [ 1 ] and is described as the founder of the Dialectical school.

[ 2 ] According to the Suda , he was the disciple of Euclid of Megara , [ 3 ] and he taught Bryson , the teacher of Pyrrho .

[ 4 ] He thus lived towards the earlier half of the 4th century BC.
