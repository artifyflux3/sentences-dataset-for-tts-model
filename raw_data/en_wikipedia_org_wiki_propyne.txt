Chemical compound

**Propyne** (**methylacetylene**) is an [alkyne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkyne "Alkyne") with the [chemical formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_formula "Chemical formula")CH 3 C≡CH. It is a component of [MAPD gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MAPP_gas "MAPP gas")—along with its [isomer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isomer "Isomer")[propadiene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propadiene "Propadiene") (allene), which was commonly used in [gas welding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas_welding "Gas welding"). Unlike [acetylene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetylene "Acetylene"), propyne can be safely [condensed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Condensation "Condensation").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-Ullmann-4)

Production and equilibrium with propadiene
------------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Propyne&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Production and equilibrium with propadiene")]

Propyne exists in equilibrium with [propadiene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propadiene "Propadiene"), the mixture of propyne and propadiene being called MAPD:

H 3 C−C≡CH ⇌ H 2 C=C=CH 2
The [coefficient of equilibrium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_equilibrium "Chemical equilibrium")_K_ eq is 0.22 at 270°C or 0.1 at 5°C. MAPD is produced as a side product, often an undesirable one, by [cracking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cracking_(chemistry) "Cracking (chemistry)")[propane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propane "Propane") to produce [propene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propene "Propene"), an important [feedstock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feedstock "Feedstock") in the [chemical industry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_industry "Chemical industry").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-Ullmann-4) MAPD interferes with the [catalytic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ziegler%E2%80%93Natta_catalyst "Ziegler–Natta catalyst")[polymerization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymerization "Polymerization") of propene.

### Laboratory methods

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Propyne&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Laboratory methods")]

Propyne can also be synthesized on laboratory scale by reducing [1-propanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1-propanol "1-propanol"),[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-5)[allyl alcohol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allyl_alcohol "Allyl alcohol") or [acetone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetone "Acetone")[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-6) vapors over magnesium.

Use as a rocket fuel
--------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Propyne&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Use as a rocket fuel")]

European space companies have researched using light [hydrocarbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrocarbons "Hydrocarbons") with [liquid oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_oxygen "Liquid oxygen"), a relatively high performing [liquid rocket propellant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_rocket_propellant "Liquid rocket propellant") combination that would also be less toxic than the commonly used MMH/NTO ([monomethylhydrazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monomethylhydrazine "Monomethylhydrazine")/[nitrogen tetroxide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen_tetroxide "Nitrogen tetroxide")). Their research showed[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_] that propyne would be highly advantageous as a [rocket fuel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocket_fuel "Rocket fuel") for craft intended for [low Earth orbital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low_Earth_orbit "Low Earth orbit") operations. They reached this conclusion based upon a [specific impulse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Specific_impulse "Specific impulse") expected to reach 370 [s](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second "Second") with oxygen as the oxidizer, a high [density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Density "Density") and [power density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_density "Power density")—and the moderate [boiling point](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boiling_point "Boiling point"), which makes the chemical easier to store than [cryogenic fuels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryogenic_fuel "Cryogenic fuel") that must be kept at extremely low temperatures.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-7)

Organic chemistry
-----------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Propyne&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Organic chemistry")]

Propyne is a convenient three-carbon building block for [organic synthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organic_synthesis "Organic synthesis"). Deprotonation with [_n_-butyllithium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-Butyllithium "N-Butyllithium") gives [propynyllithium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propynyllithium "Propynyllithium"). This [nucleophilic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nucleophilic "Nucleophilic") reagent adds to [carbonyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbonyl "Carbonyl") groups, producing [alcohols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcohol_(chemistry) "Alcohol (chemistry)") and [esters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ester "Ester").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-8) Whereas purified propyne is expensive, MAPP gas could be used to cheaply generate large amounts of the reagent.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-9)

Propyne, along with [2-butyne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2-butyne "2-butyne"), is also used to synthesize [alkylated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkylated "Alkylated")[hydroquinones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroquinone "Hydroquinone") in the [total synthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Total_synthesis "Total synthesis") of [vitamin E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitamin_E "Vitamin E").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-Cyclization-10)

The chemical shift of an alkynyl proton and propargylic proton generally occur in the same region of the [1 H NMR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proton_nuclear_magnetic_resonance "Proton nuclear magnetic resonance") spectrum. In propyne, these two signals have almost exactly the same chemical shifts, leading to overlap of the signals, and the 1 H NMR spectrum of propyne, when recorded in deuteriochloroform on a 300 MHz instrument, consists of a single signal, a sharp singlet resonating at 1.8 ppm.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-11)

In Astrophysics
---------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Propyne&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: In Astrophysics")]

Propyne has been detected in multiple astrophysical objects following its first observation in 1973 in the [galactic center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galactic_Center "Galactic Center")[giant molecular cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud")[Sgr B2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagittarius_B2 "Sagittarius B2") using [radio astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_astronomy "Radio astronomy") techniques.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-12) Propyne has been proposed to act as a precursor molecule to the formation of [PAHs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbon "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon") in space, such as [indene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indene "Indene").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-13)

Propyne has been detected by [infrared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrared "Infrared")[spectroscopy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spectroscopy "Spectroscopy") in the [chemically reducing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redox "Redox")[atmospheres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmosphere "Atmosphere") of the [outer planets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_System "Solar System") in the [Solar System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_System "Solar System"), including on [Jupiter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jupiter "Jupiter") in 2000 [[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-14) and on [Saturn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturn "Saturn") in 1997,[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-15) both using the [Infrared Space Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrared_Space_Observatory "Infrared Space Observatory"); on Titan in 1981 using [Voyager's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voyager_program "Voyager program")[IRIS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrared_interferometer_spectrometer_and_radiometer "Infrared interferometer spectrometer and radiometer") instrument;[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-16) and on the [ice giants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice_giant "Ice giant")[Uranus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uranus "Uranus") in 2006 [[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-17) and on [Neptune](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neptune "Neptune") in 2008 [[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propyne#cite_note-18) using the [Spitzer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spitzer_Space_Telescope "Spitzer Space Telescope") space telescope.
