Asteroid with which Earth shares its orbit around the Sun

[![Image 19](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Animation_of_2010_TK7.gif/250px-Animation_of_2010_TK7.gif)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Animation_of_2010_TK7.gif)

[![Image 20](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Lagrange_points2.svg/250px-Lagrange_points2.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Lagrange_points2.svg)

The orbit of [2010 TK 7](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2010_TK7 "2010 TK7"), the first Earth trojan to be discovered (left). [Lagrange points](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange_point "Lagrange point") L 4 and L 5. Lines around the blue triangles represent [tadpole orbits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horseshoe_orbit#Tadpole_orbit "Horseshoe orbit")_(right)_

An **Earth trojan** is an [asteroid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroid "Asteroid") that orbits the [Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun "Sun") in the vicinity of the [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth")–Sun [Lagrange points](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange_point "Lagrange point")[L 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange_point#L4_and_L5 "Lagrange point") (leading 60°) or [L 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange_point#L4_and_L5 "Lagrange point") (trailing 60°), thus having an orbit similar to Earth's. Only two Earth trojans have so far been discovered. The name ["trojan"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trojan_(celestial_body) "Trojan (celestial body)") was first used in 1906 for the [Jupiter trojans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jupiter_trojan "Jupiter trojan"), the asteroids that were observed near the Lagrangian points of [Jupiter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jupiter "Jupiter")'s orbit.

### [L 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange_point#L4_and_L5 "Lagrange point") (trailing)

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Earth_trojan&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: L5 (trailing)")]

*   No known objects are currently thought to be [L 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange_point#L4_and_L5 "Lagrange point") trojans of Earth.

Searches
--------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Earth_trojan&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Searches")]

An Earth-based search for [L 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange_point#L4_and_L5 "Lagrange point") objects was conducted in 1994, covering 0.35 square degrees of sky, under poor observing conditions.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-Whitley+Tholen1998-5) That search failed to detect any objects:

"The limiting sensitivity of this search was magnitude~22.8, corresponding to [C-type asteroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C-type_asteroid "C-type asteroid") ~350 m in diameter, or [S-type asteroids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S-type_asteroid "S-type asteroid") ~175 m in diameter."[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-Whitley+Tholen1998-5)
In February 2017, the [OSIRIS-REx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OSIRIS-REx "OSIRIS-REx") spacecraft performed a search from within the L 4 region on its way to asteroid[Bennu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/101955_Bennu "101955 Bennu").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-osirisrex-6) No additional Earth trojans were discovered.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-osirisrexresults-7)

In April 2017, the _[Hayabusa2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hayabusa2 "Hayabusa2")_ spacecraft searched the [L 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange_point#L4_and_L5 "Lagrange point") region while proceeding to [asteroid Ryugu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/162173_Ryugu "162173 Ryugu"),[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-hayabusa2-8) but did not find any asteroids there.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-hayabusa2ms-9)

Giant-impact hypothesis
-----------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Earth_trojan&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Giant-impact hypothesis")]

A hypothetical [planet-sized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planetary_mass_objects "Planetary mass objects") Earth trojan the size of [Mars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mars "Mars"), given the name [Theia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theia_(planet) "Theia (planet)"), is thought by proponents of the [giant-impact hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giant-impact_hypothesis "Giant-impact hypothesis") to be the origin of the [Moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon "Moon"). The hypothesis states that the Moon formed after Earth and Theia collided,[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-10) showering material from the two planets into space. This material eventually [accreted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accretion_disk "Accretion disk") around Earth and into a single orbiting body, the Moon.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-TheiaHypothesis-11)

At the same time, material from Theia mixed and combined with Earth's mantle and core. Supporters of the giant-impact hypothesis theorise that Earth's large core in relation to its overall volume is as a result of this combination.

Continuing interest in near-Earth asteroids
-------------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Earth_trojan&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Continuing interest in near-Earth asteroids")]

Astronomy continues to retain interest in the subject. A publication[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-Malhoutra2019-12) describes these reasons thus:

> The survival to the present day of an ancient [Earth Trojan] population is reasonably assured provided Earth's orbit itself was not strongly perturbed since its formation. It is therefore pertinent to consider that modern theoretical models of planet formation find strongly chaotic orbital evolution during the final stages of assembly of the terrestrial planets and the Earth–Moon system.
> 
> 
> Such chaotic evolution may at first sight appear unfavorable to the survival of a primordial population of [Earth trojans]. However, during and after the chaotic assembly of the terrestrial planets, it is likely that a residual planetesimal population, of a few percent of Earth's mass, was present and helped to damp the orbital eccentricities and inclinations of the terrestrial planets to their observed low values, as well as to provide the so-called "late veneer" of accreting planetesimals to account for the abundance patterns of the highly siderophile elements in Earth's mantle.
> 
> 
> Such a residual planetesimal population would also naturally lead to a small fraction trapped in the Earth's Trojan zones as Earth's orbit circularized. In addition to potentially hosting an ancient, long-term stable population of asteroids, Earth's Trojan regions also provide transient traps for NEOs that originate from more distal reservoirs of small bodies in the solar system like the main asteroid belt.

Other companions of Earth
-------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Earth_trojan&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Other companions of Earth")]

Several other small objects have been found on an orbital path associated with Earth. Although these objects are in 1:1 orbital resonance, they are not Earth trojans, because they do not [librate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libration "Libration") around a definite Sun–Earth Lagrangian point, neither [L 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange_point#L4_and_L5 "Lagrange point") nor [L 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lagrange_point#L4_and_L5 "Lagrange point").

Earth has another noted companion, asteroid [3753 Cruithne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3753_Cruithne "3753 Cruithne"). About 5 km across, it has a peculiar type of orbital resonance called an [overlapping horseshoe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horseshoe_orbit "Horseshoe orbit"), and is probably only a temporary liaison.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-13)

[469219 Kamoʻoalewa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/469219_Kamo%CA%BBoalewa "469219 Kamoʻoalewa"), an [asteroid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroid "Asteroid") discovered on 27 April 2016, is possibly the most stable [quasi-satellite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quasi-satellite "Quasi-satellite") of [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_trojan#cite_note-NASA-20160615-14)
