Chemical compound

**Thioxoethenylidene**, is a reactive [hetero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heteroatom "Heteroatom")[allene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allene "Allene") molecule with formula **CCS**.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-maier-1)

Occurrence
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Thioxoethenylidene&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Occurrence")]

CCS is found in space in large quantities.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-maier-1) This includes the [Taurus Molecular Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taurus_Molecular_Cloud "Taurus Molecular Cloud") in [TMC-1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TMC-1 "TMC-1"), [TMC-1c](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=TMC-1c&action=edit&redlink=1 "TMC-1c (page does not exist)") and [L1521B](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=L1521B&action=edit&redlink=1 "L1521B (page does not exist)"). These are likely in young starless molecular cloud cores.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-roy-2)

Production
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Thioxoethenylidene&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Production")]

By condensing propadienedithione SCCCS or thioxopropadienone OCCCS in solid argon and irradiating with [ultraviolet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultraviolet "Ultraviolet") radiation, CCS is formed.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-maier-1) Another way is via a glow discharge in a mixture of carbon disulfide and helium. Yet another way is through electron irradiation of sulfur containing heterocycles.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-maier-1)

CCS and the anion CCS− can be formed in solid neon matrices also.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-3)

Properties
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Thioxoethenylidene&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Properties")]

CCS can be a ligand. It can form an asymmetrical bridge between two molybdenum atoms in Mo2(μ,σ(C):η 2(C′S)-CCS)(CO)4(hydrotris(3,5-dimethylpyrazol-1-yl)borate)2 In this one carbon atom has a triple bond to a molybdenum and the other has a double bond to the other molybdenum atom, which also has a single bond to the sulfur atom.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-4)

The ultraviolet spectrum shows absorption bands between 2800 and 3370 Å and also in the near infrared between 7500 and 10000 Å.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-maier-1) CCS can react with CCCS to form C 5 S.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-maier-1)

The [infrared spectrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infrared_spectrum "Infrared spectrum") in solid argon shows a vibration band at 1666.6 cm−1 called v 1 and another called v 2 at 862.7 cm−1. The 2v 1 overtone is at 3311.1 cm−1. A combination vibration and bending band is at 2763.4 cm−1[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-maier-1)

The [microwave spectrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microwave_spectrum "Microwave spectrum") has emission lines 4 3 − 3 2 at 45.4[GHz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GHz "GHz") and 2 1 - 1 0 at 22.3 GHz, important for detection of molecules in molecular clouds.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-roy-2)

Theoretical predictions show that the C-C bond is 1.304 Å long and the C–S bond is 1.550 Å.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioxoethenylidene#cite_note-5)
