Thirty-seven radioisotopes have been characterized, with the most stable being 158 Tb with a half-life of 180 years, 157 Tb with a half-life of 71 years, and 160 Tb with a half-life of 72.3 days. All of the remaining radioactive isotopes have half-lives that are less than one week, and the majority of these have half-lives that are less than 24 seconds. This element also has 27 meta states , with the most stable being 156m2 Tb (t 1/2 = 24.4 hours), 154m2 Tb (t 1/2 = 22.7 hours) and 154m1 Tb (t 1/2 = 21.5 hours).

The primary decay mode before the most abundant stable isotope, 159 Tb, is electron capture to gadolinium isotopes, and the primary mode after is beta decay to dysprosium isotopes.
