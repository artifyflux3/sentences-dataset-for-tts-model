From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

**Vertical position** or **vertical location** is a [position](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Position_(mathematics) "Position (mathematics)") along a [vertical direction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_direction "Vertical direction") (the [plumb line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plumb_line "Plumb line") direction) above or below a given [vertical datum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_datum "Vertical datum") (a reference level surface, such as [mean sea level](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mean_sea_level "Mean sea level")). **Vertical distance** or **vertical separation** is the [distance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distance "Distance") between two vertical positions. Many **vertical coordinates** exist for expressing vertical position: depth, height, altitude, elevation, etc. Points lying on an [equigeopotential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equigeopotential "Equigeopotential") surface are said to be on the same **vertical level**, as in a [water level](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_level_(device) "Water level (device)"). A [function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Function_(mathematics) "Function (mathematics)") with [domain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domain_of_a_function "Domain of a function") along the vertical line is called a _vertical distribution_ or _vertical profile_.

The [International Organization for Standardization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization "International Organization for Standardization") (ISO), more specifically [ISO 19111](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spatial_reference_system "Spatial reference system"), offers the following two definitions:[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-ISO6709:2008-1)

*   _depth_: "distance of a point from a chosen reference surface measured downward along a line perpendicular to that surface."[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-ISO6709:2008-1)
*   _height_: "distance of a point from a chosen reference surface measured upward along a line perpendicular to that surface";[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-ISO6709:2008-1)

[ISO 6709](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO_6709 "ISO 6709") (2008 version) makes the following additional definition:

*   _[altitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Altitude "Altitude")_: "height where the chosen reference surface is mean sea level"[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-ISO6709:2008-1)

The [International Civil Aviation Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization "International Civil Aviation Organization") (ICAO) offers similar definitions:[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-annex4-2)

*   _[altitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Altitude "Altitude")_: "the vertical distance of a level, a point or an object considered as a point, measured from the mean sea level (MSL);"[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-annex4-2)
*   _height_: "the vertical distance of a level, a point or an object considered as a point, measured from a specific datum."[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-annex4-2)

ICAO further defines:

*   _[elevation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elevation "Elevation")_: "the vertical distance of a point or a level, on or affixed to the surface of the earth, measured from mean sea level."[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-annex4-2)

I.e., elevation would be the altitude of the ground or a building.

Vertical distance quantities, such as [orthometric height](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthometric_height "Orthometric height"), may be expressed in various units: [metres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metres "Metres"), [feet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feet "Feet"), etc.

Certain vertical coordinates are not based on [length](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Length "Length"), for example, [geopotential numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geopotential_number "Geopotential number") have units of m 2/s 2. Normalization by a constant nominal gravity value (units of m/s 2) yields units of metre, as in [geopotential height](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geopotential_height "Geopotential height") (based on [standard gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_gravity "Standard gravity")) or [dynamic height](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamic_height "Dynamic height") (based on [normal gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Normal_gravity "Normal gravity") at 45 degrees latitude). Despite the [physical dimension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_dimension "Physical dimension") and [unit of length](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unit_of_length "Unit of length"), the vertical coordinate does not represent distance in [physical space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_space "Physical space"), as would be measured with a ruler or [tape measure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tape_measure "Tape measure"). Sometimes a _**geopotential metre**_ (symbol gpm or m') or _dynamic metre_ is introduced for emphasis.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-Bjerknes1910-4) However, this practice is not acceptable with the [International System of Units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_System_of_Units "International System of Units") (SI).[[a]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-6)

Another non-SI unit is the _vertical metre_, introduced when there may be confusion between vertical, horizontal, or [slant distances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slant_distance "Slant distance"). It is used for distance climbed during sports such as [mountaineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mountaineering "Mountaineering"), [skiing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpine_skiing "Alpine skiing"), [hiking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hiking "Hiking"), [running](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Running "Running") or [cycling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cycling "Cycling")[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vertical_position#cite_note-7) In German-speaking countries the abbreviation 'Hm' for _Höhenmeter_ ("height metre") is used; if it is preceded by a '±' it refers to the [cumulative elevation gain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cumulative_elevation_gain "Cumulative elevation gain").
