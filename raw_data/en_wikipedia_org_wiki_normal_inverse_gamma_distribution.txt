>1} In probability theory and statistics , the normal-inverse-gamma distribution (or Gaussian-inverse-gamma distribution ) is a four-parameter family of multivariate continuous probability distributions . It is the conjugate prior of a normal distribution with unknown mean and variance .

Then ( x , σ σ 2 ) {\displaystyle (x,\sigma ^{2})} has a normal-inverse-gamma distribution, denoted as ( x , σ σ 2 ) ∼ ∼ N- Γ Γ − − 1 ( μ μ , λ λ , α α , β β ) .
