Find sources: "Phialide" – news · newspapers · books · scholar · JSTOR ( May 2019 ) phialide of Ophiocordyceps termiticola The phialide ( / ˈ f aɪ ə l aɪ d / FY -ə-lyde ; Greek : phialis , diminutive of phiale, a broad, flat vessel) is a flask-shaped projection from the vesicle (dilated part of the top of conidiophore ) of certain fungi .  It projects from the mycelium without increasing in length unless a subsequent increase in the formation of conidia occurs.

[ 1 ] Diagram showing the phialide with other structures It is the end cell of a phialosphore .
