**Formamide** is an [amide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amide "Amide") derived from [formic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formic_acid "Formic acid"). It is a colorless liquid which is miscible with water and has an [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia")-like odor. It is chemical feedstock for the manufacture of [sulfa drugs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulfa_drug "Sulfa drug") and other [pharmaceuticals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharmaceutical "Pharmaceutical"), [herbicides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbicide "Herbicide") and [pesticides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pesticides "Pesticides"), and in the manufacture of [hydrocyanic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrocyanic_acid "Hydrocyanic acid"). It has been used as a [softener](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fabric_softener "Fabric softener") for paper and fiber. It is a [solvent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solvent "Solvent") for many [ionic compounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionic_compound "Ionic compound"). It has also been used as a solvent for [resins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resins "Resins") and [plasticizers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasticizers "Plasticizers").[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-KirkOthmer-4) Some astrobiologists suggest that it may be an alternative to water as the main solvent in other forms of life.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-5)

**Formamides** are compounds of the type RR′NCHO. One important formamide is [dimethylformamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethylformamide "Dimethylformamide"), (CH 3)2 NCHO.

### Historical production

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Formamide&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Historical production")]

In the past, formamide was produced by treating [formic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formic_acid "Formic acid") with [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia"), which produces [ammonium formate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonium_formate "Ammonium formate"), which in turn yields formamide upon heating:[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-6)

HCOOH + NH 3 → HCOO−

NH+

4 HCOO−

NH+

4 → HCONH 2 + [H 2 O](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_(molecule) "Water (molecule)")
Formamide is also generated by [aminolysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aminolysis "Aminolysis") of [ethyl formate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethyl_formate "Ethyl formate"):[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-7)

HCOOCH 2 CH 3 + NH 3 → HCONH 2 + CH 3 CH 2 OH
The current industrial process for the manufacture of formamide involves the [carbonylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbonylation "Carbonylation") of ammonia:[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-KirkOthmer-4)

[CO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_monoxide "Carbon monoxide") + NH 3 → HCONH 2
An alternative two-stage process involves the ammonolysis of [methyl formate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methyl_formate "Methyl formate"), which is formed from carbon monoxide and [methanol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanol "Methanol"):

CO + CH 3 OH → HCOOCH 3 HCO 2 CH 3 + NH 3 → HCONH 2 + CH 3 OH
Formamide is used in the industrial production of [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide"). It is also used as a solvent for processing various polymers such as [polyacrylonitrile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyacrylonitrile "Polyacrylonitrile").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-bipp-8)

It is also known that formamide is an excellent swelling agent for [cellulosic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellulose "Cellulose") fibres,[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-9) and is used in some specific wood-related applications.

Formamide decomposes into carbon monoxide and ammonia when heated above 100°C.

HCONH 2 → CO + NH 3
The reaction is slow below 160°C, but accelerates thereafter. At very high temperatures, the reaction products shift to [hydrogen cyanide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_cyanide "Hydrogen cyanide") (HCN) and water instead:

HC(O)NH 2 → HCN + H 2 O
The same effect occurs in the presence of [solid acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solid_acid "Solid acid") catalysts.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-bipp-8)

### Niche or laboratory applications

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Formamide&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Niche or laboratory applications")]

Formamide is a constituent of cryoprotectant [vitrification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryopreservation#Vitrification "Cryopreservation") mixtures used for cryopreservation of tissues and [organs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organ_(anatomy) "Organ (anatomy)").

Formamide is also used as an RNA stabiliser in [gel electrophoresis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gel_electrophoresis "Gel electrophoresis") by deionizing RNA. In capillary electrophoresis, it is used for stabilizing (single) strands of denatured DNA.

Another use is to add it in [sol-gel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sol-gel "Sol-gel") solutions in order to avoid cracking during [sintering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sintering "Sintering").

Formamide, in its pure state, has been used as an alternative solvent for the electrostatic [self-assembly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-assembly "Self-assembly") of polymer nanofilms.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-10)

Formamide is used to prepare primary amines directly from ketones via their N-formyl derivatives, using the [Leuckart reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leuckart_reaction "Leuckart reaction").

[![Image 18](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Methanogenesis_cycle.png/330px-Methanogenesis_cycle.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Methanogenesis_cycle.png)

Cycle for [methanogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanogenesis "Methanogenesis"), showing two formamide-containing intermediates.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-RT-11)

Formamides are intermediates in the [methanogenesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methanogenesis "Methanogenesis") cycle.

### Prebiotic chemistry

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Formamide&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Prebiotic chemistry")]

Formamide has been proposed as an alternative [solvent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solvent "Solvent") to water, perhaps with the ability to support life with alternative biochemistries to that currently found on Earth. It forms by the hydrolysis of hydrogen cyanide. With a large dipole moment, its solvation properties are similar to those of water.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-12)

Formamide has been shown to convert to traces of [guanine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guanine "Guanine") upon heating in the presence of ultraviolet light.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-13)

Several prebiotic chemical reactions producing amino acid derivatives have been shown to take place in formamide.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-14) Similar scenarios implicate [thioformamide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thioformamide "Thioformamide").[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-15)

Contact with skin and eyes is not recommended. With an [LD50](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LD50 "LD50") of grams per kg, formamide is of low acute toxicity. It also has low mutagenicity.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-bipp-8)

Formamide is classified as toxic to reproductive health.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formamide#cite_note-16)
