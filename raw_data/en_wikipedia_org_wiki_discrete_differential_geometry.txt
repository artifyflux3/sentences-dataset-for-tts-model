From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Area of mathematics

**Discrete differential geometry** is the study of discrete counterparts of notions in [differential geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_geometry "Differential geometry"). Instead of smooth curves and surfaces, there are [polygons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polygon "Polygon"), [meshes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polygon_mesh "Polygon mesh"), and [simplicial complexes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplicial_complexes "Simplicial complexes"). It is used in the study of [computer graphics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_graphics "Computer graphics"), [geometry processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometry_processing "Geometry processing") and [topological combinatorics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topological_combinatorics "Topological combinatorics").
