**Daniel Bernoulli**[FRS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society "Fellow of the Royal Society") ( [_bur-NOO-lee_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key"); Swiss Standard German:[[ˈdaːni̯eːl bɛrˈnʊli]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German "Help:IPA/Standard German");[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-1) 8 February[[O.S.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates "Old Style and New Style dates") 29 January]1700 – 27 March 1782) was a [Swiss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swiss_people "Swiss people")[mathematician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematician "Mathematician") and [physicist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physicist "Physicist") and was one of the many prominent mathematicians in the [Bernoulli family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernoulli_family "Bernoulli family") from Basel. He is particularly remembered for his applications of mathematics to mechanics, especially [fluid mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluid_mechanics "Fluid mechanics"), and for his pioneering work in [probability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probability "Probability") and [statistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistics "Statistics").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-2) His name is commemorated in the [Bernoulli's principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernoulli%27s_principle "Bernoulli's principle"), a particular example of the [conservation of energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_of_energy "Conservation of energy"), which describes the mathematics of the mechanism underlying the operation of two important technologies of the 20th century: the [carburetor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carburetor "Carburetor") and the aeroplane [wing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wing "Wing").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-4)

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/HYDRODYNAMICA%2C_Danielis_Bernoulli.png/250px-HYDRODYNAMICA%2C_Danielis_Bernoulli.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:HYDRODYNAMICA,_Danielis_Bernoulli.png)

Frontpage of _[Hydrodynamica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrodynamica "Hydrodynamica")_ (1738)

Daniel Bernoulli was born in [Groningen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Groningen_(city) "Groningen (city)"), in the Netherlands, into a [family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernoulli_family "Bernoulli family") of distinguished mathematicians.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-Rothbard-5) The Bernoulli family came originally from Antwerp, at that time in the [Spanish Netherlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Netherlands "Spanish Netherlands"), but emigrated to escape the Spanish persecution of the [Protestants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protestant "Protestant"). After a brief period in Frankfurt the family moved to Basel, in Switzerland.

Daniel was the son of [Johann Bernoulli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Bernoulli "Johann Bernoulli") (one of the early developers of [calculus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calculus "Calculus")) and a nephew of [Jacob Bernoulli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacob_Bernoulli "Jacob Bernoulli") (an early researcher in [probability theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probability_theory "Probability theory") and the discoverer of the mathematical constant [e](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E_(mathematical_constant) "E (mathematical constant)")).[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-Rothbard-5) He had two brothers, [Niklaus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolaus_II_Bernoulli "Nicolaus II Bernoulli") and [Johann II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_II_Bernoulli "Johann II Bernoulli"). Daniel Bernoulli was described by [W. W. Rouse Ball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._W._Rouse_Ball "W. W. Rouse Ball") as "by far the ablest of the younger Bernoullis".[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-ball-6)

He is said to have had a bad relationship with his father. Both of them entered and tied for first place in a scientific contest at the [University of Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Paris "University of Paris"). Johann banned Daniel from his house, allegedly being unable to bear the "shame" of Daniel being considered his equal. Johann allegedly plagiarized key ideas from Daniel's book _[Hydrodynamica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrodynamica "Hydrodynamica")_ in his book _Hydraulica_ and backdated them to before _Hydrodynamica_.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_] Daniel's attempts at reconciliation with his father were unsuccessful.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-mactutor-7)

When he was in school, Johann encouraged Daniel to study business citing poor financial compensation for mathematicians. Daniel initially refused but later relented and studied both business and [medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medicine "Medicine") at his father's behest under the condition that his father would teach him mathematics privately.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-mactutor-7) Daniel studied medicine at [Basel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Basel "University of Basel"), [Heidelberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heidelberg_University "Heidelberg University"), and [Strasbourg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Strasbourg "University of Strasbourg"), and earned a PhD in anatomy and botany in 1721.

He was a contemporary and close friend of [Leonhard Euler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonhard_Euler "Leonhard Euler").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-andhist-8)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-9) He went to [St. Petersburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint_Petersburg "Saint Petersburg") in 1724 as professor of mathematics, but was very unhappy there. A temporary illness[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-mactutor-7) together with the censorship by the [Russian Orthodox Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Orthodox_Church "Russian Orthodox Church")[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-10) and disagreements over his salary gave him an excuse for leaving St. Petersburg in 1733.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-11) He returned to the [University of Basel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Basel "University of Basel"), where he successively held the chairs of [medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medicine "Medicine"), [metaphysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metaphysics "Metaphysics"), and [natural philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_philosophy "Natural philosophy") until his death.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-eb-12)

In May 1750 he was elected a [Fellow of the Royal Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society "Fellow of the Royal Society").[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-13)

Daniel Bernoulli

His earliest mathematical work was the _Exercitationes_ (_Mathematical Exercises_), published in 1724 with the help of [Goldbach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Goldbach "Christian Goldbach"). Two years later he pointed out for the first time the frequent desirability of resolving a compound motion into motions of translation and motion of rotation. In 1729, he published a [polynomial root-finding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynomial_root-finding "Polynomial root-finding") algorithm which became known as [Bernoulli's method](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernoulli%27s_method "Bernoulli's method").[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-chabert-14) His chief work is _[Hydrodynamica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrodynamica "Hydrodynamica")_, published in 1738. It resembles [Joseph Louis Lagrange](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Louis_Lagrange "Joseph Louis Lagrange")'s _Mécanique Analytique_ in being arranged so that all the results are consequences of a single principle, namely, the conservation of [vis viva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vis_viva "Vis viva"), an early version of the [conservation of energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_of_energy "Conservation of energy"). This was followed by a memoir on the theory of the tides, to which, conjointly with the memoirs by Euler and [Colin Maclaurin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colin_Maclaurin "Colin Maclaurin"), a prize was awarded by the [French Academy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Academy "French Academy"): these three memoirs contain all that was done on this subject between the publication of [Isaac Newton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Newton "Isaac Newton")'s _[Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophiae\_Naturalis\_Principia\_Mathematica "Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica")_ and the investigations of [Pierre-Simon Laplace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre-Simon_Laplace "Pierre-Simon Laplace"). Bernoulli also wrote a large number of papers on various mechanical questions, especially on problems connected with [vibrating strings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vibrating_string "Vibrating string"), and the solutions given by [Brook Taylor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brook_Taylor "Brook Taylor") and by [Jean le Rond d'Alembert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_le_Rond_d%27Alembert "Jean le Rond d'Alembert").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-ball-6)

Economics and statistics
------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Bernoulli&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Economics and statistics")]

In his 1738 book _Specimen theoriae novae de mensura sortis (Exposition of a New Theory on the Measurement of Risk)_,[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-15) Bernoulli offered a solution to the [St. Petersburg paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._Petersburg_paradox "St. Petersburg paradox") as the basis of the economic theory of [risk aversion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Risk_aversion "Risk aversion"), [risk premium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Risk_premium "Risk premium"), and [utility](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utility "Utility").[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-16) Bernoulli often noticed that when making decisions that involved some uncertainty, people did not always try to maximize their possible monetary gain, but rather tried to maximize "[utility](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utility "Utility")", an economic term encompassing their personal satisfaction and benefit. Bernoulli realized that for humans, there is a direct relationship between money gained and utility, but that it diminishes as the money gained increases. For example, to a person whose income is $10,000 per year, an additional $100 in income will provide more utility than it would to a person whose income is $50,000 per year.[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-FOOTNOTECooterUlen201644%E2%80%9345-17)

One of the earliest attempts to analyze a statistical problem involving [censored data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Censoring_(statistics) "Censoring (statistics)") was Bernoulli's 1766 analysis of [smallpox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smallpox "Smallpox")[morbidity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morbidity "Morbidity") and [mortality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mortality_rate "Mortality rate") data to demonstrate the efficacy of [inoculation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inoculation "Inoculation").[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-18)

In _Hydrodynamica_ (1738) he laid the basis for the [kinetic theory of gases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_theory_of_gases "Kinetic theory of gases"), and applied the idea to explain [Boyle's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boyle%27s_law "Boyle's law").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-ball-6)

He worked with Euler on [elasticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elasticity_(physics) "Elasticity (physics)") and the development of the [Euler–Bernoulli beam equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euler%E2%80%93Bernoulli_beam_equation "Euler–Bernoulli beam equation").[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-19)[Bernoulli's principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernoulli%27s_principle "Bernoulli's principle") is of critical use in [hydrodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrodynamics "Hydrodynamics").

According to [Léon Brillouin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Brillouin "Léon Brillouin"), the [principle of superposition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superposition_principle "Superposition principle") was first stated by Daniel Bernoulli in 1753: "The general motion of a vibrating system is given by a superposition of its proper vibrations."[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli#cite_note-20)
