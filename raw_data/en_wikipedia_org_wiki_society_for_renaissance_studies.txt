It pursues this though organising a wide range of activities, allocating funding for research, and supporting publications, including a book series and Renaissance Studies ( ISSN 0269-1213 ), a flagship journal for the field.

[ 1 ] [ 2 ] The society was formed in 1967, the founding meeting held at the Institute of Historical Research on 12 July of that year.

[ 3 ] This meeting was chaired by the noted historian Denys Hay and amongst those participating were the art historians Ernst Gombrich and Kenneth Clark and the historian Sydney Anglo , the society's first secretary.
