From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A [timeline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chronology "Chronology") of events in the [history](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_thermodynamics "History of thermodynamics") of [thermodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermodynamics "Thermodynamics").

*   1593 – [Galileo Galilei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galileo_Galilei "Galileo Galilei") invents one of the first [thermoscopes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermoscope "Thermoscope"), also known as [Galileo thermometer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galileo_thermometer "Galileo thermometer")[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-1)
*   1650– [Otto von Guericke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otto_von_Guericke "Otto von Guericke") builds the first [vacuum pump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vacuum_pump "Vacuum pump")
*   1660– [Robert Boyle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Boyle "Robert Boyle") experimentally discovers [Boyle's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boyle%27s_law "Boyle's law"), relating the pressure and volume of a gas (published 1662)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-2)
*   1665– [Robert Hooke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Hooke "Robert Hooke") published his book _[Micrographia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micrographia "Micrographia")_, which contained the statement: "Heat being nothing else but a very brisk and vehement agitation of the parts of a body."[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-3)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-4)
*   1667– [J. J. Becher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._J._Becher "J. J. Becher") puts forward a theory of [combustion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combustion "Combustion") involving _combustible earth_ in his book _Physica subterranea_[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-5) (see [Phlogiston theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phlogiston_theory "Phlogiston theory")).
*   1676–1689– [Gottfried Leibniz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gottfried_Leibniz "Gottfried Leibniz") develops the concept of _[vis viva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vis\_viva "Vis viva")_, a limited version of the [conservation of energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_of_energy "Conservation of energy")
*   1679– [Denis Papin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denis_Papin "Denis Papin") designed a [steam digester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steam_digester "Steam digester") which inspired the development of the piston-and-cylinder steam engine.
*   1694–1734– [Georg Ernst Stahl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georg_Ernst_Stahl "Georg Ernst Stahl") names Becher's combustible earth as [phlogiston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phlogiston "Phlogiston") and develops the theory
*   1698– [Thomas Savery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Savery "Thomas Savery") patents an early steam engine[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-jenkins-6)
*   1702– [Guillaume Amontons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guillaume_Amontons "Guillaume Amontons") introduces the concept of [absolute zero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absolute_zero "Absolute zero"), based on observations of [gases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas "Gas")
*   1738– [Daniel Bernoulli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli "Daniel Bernoulli") publishes _[Hydrodynamica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrodynamica "Hydrodynamica")_, initiating the [kinetic theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_theory_of_gases "Kinetic theory of gases")
*   1749– [Émilie du Châtelet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89milie_du_Ch%C3%A2telet "Émilie du Châtelet"), in her French translation and commentary on [Newton's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Newton "Isaac Newton")_[Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophiae\_Naturalis\_Principia\_Mathematica "Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica")_, derives the [conservation of energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_of_energy "Conservation of energy") from the first principles of Newtonian mechanics.
*   1761– [Joseph Black](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Black "Joseph Black") discovers that ice absorbs heat without changing its temperature when melting
*   1772– Black's student [Daniel Rutherford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Rutherford "Daniel Rutherford") discovers [nitrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen "Nitrogen"),[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-7)[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-8) which he calls _phlogisticated air_, and together they explain the results in terms of the [phlogiston theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phlogiston_theory "Phlogiston theory")
*   1776– [John Smeaton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Smeaton "John Smeaton") publishes a paper on [experiments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Experiment "Experiment") related to [power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_(physics) "Power (physics)"), [work](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanical_work "Mechanical work"), [momentum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Momentum "Momentum"), and [kinetic energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_energy "Kinetic energy"), supporting the conservation of energy
*   1777– [Carl Wilhelm Scheele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carl_Wilhelm_Scheele "Carl Wilhelm Scheele") distinguishes [heat transfer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_transfer "Heat transfer") by [thermal radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_radiation "Thermal radiation") from that by [convection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convection "Convection") and [conduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_conduction "Heat conduction")
*   1783– [Antoine Lavoisier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoine_Lavoisier "Antoine Lavoisier") discovers [oxygen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxygen "Oxygen") and develops an explanation for combustion; in his paper "Réflexions sur le phlogistique", he deprecates the phlogiston theory and proposes a [caloric theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caloric_theory "Caloric theory")
*   1784– [Jan Ingenhousz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jan_Ingenhousz "Jan Ingenhousz") describes [Brownian motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brownian_motion "Brownian motion") of charcoal particles on water
*   1791– [Pierre Prévost](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Pr%C3%A9vost_(physicist) "Pierre Prévost (physicist)") shows that all bodies radiate heat, no matter how hot or cold they are[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-:0-9)
*   1798– Count Rumford ([Benjamin Thompson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Thompson "Benjamin Thompson")) publishes his paper "[An Inquiry Concerning the Source of the Heat Which Is Excited by Friction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/An_Inquiry_Concerning_the_Source_of_the_Heat_Which_Is_Excited_by_Friction "An Inquiry Concerning the Source of the Heat Which Is Excited by Friction")" detailing measurements of the [frictional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friction "Friction") heat generated in [boring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boring_(manufacturing) "Boring (manufacturing)")[cannons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cannon "Cannon") and develops the idea that heat is a form of [kinetic energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_energy "Kinetic energy"); his measurements are inconsistent with caloric theory, but are also sufficiently imprecise as to leave room for doubt.

*   1802– [Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Louis_Gay-Lussac "Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac") publishes [Charles's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles%27s_law "Charles's law"), discovered (but unpublished) by [Jacques Charles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Charles "Jacques Charles") around 1787; this shows the dependency between temperature and volume. Gay-Lussac also formulates the law relating temperature with pressure (the pressure law, or [Gay-Lussac's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gay-Lussac%27s_law "Gay-Lussac's law"))
*   1804– Sir [John Leslie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Leslie_(physicist) "John Leslie (physicist)") observes that a matte black surface radiates heat more effectively than a polished surface, suggesting the importance of [black-body radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black-body_radiation "Black-body radiation")
*   1805– [William Hyde Wollaston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Hyde_Wollaston "William Hyde Wollaston") defends the conservation of energy in _On the Force of Percussion_
*   1808– [John Dalton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Dalton "John Dalton") defends caloric theory in _A New System of Chemistry_ and describes how it combines with matter, especially [gases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gas "Gas"); he proposes that the [heat capacity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_capacity "Heat capacity") of gases varies inversely with [atomic weight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic_weight "Atomic weight")
*   1810– Sir John Leslie freezes water to ice artificially
*   1813– [Peter Ewart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Ewart "Peter Ewart") supports the idea of the conservation of energy in his paper _On the measure of moving force_; the paper strongly influences Dalton and his pupil, [James Joule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Prescott_Joule "James Prescott Joule")
*   1819– [Pierre Louis Dulong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Louis_Dulong "Pierre Louis Dulong") and [Alexis Thérèse Petit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexis_Th%C3%A9r%C3%A8se_Petit "Alexis Thérèse Petit") give the [Dulong-Petit law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dulong-Petit_law "Dulong-Petit law") for the [specific heat capacity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Specific_heat_capacity "Specific heat capacity") of a [crystal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystal "Crystal")
*   1820– [John Herapath](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Herapath "John Herapath") develops some ideas in the kinetic theory of gases but mistakenly associates temperature with [molecular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecule "Molecule")[momentum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Momentum "Momentum") rather than [kinetic energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_energy "Kinetic energy"); his work receives little attention other than from Joule
*   1822– [Joseph Fourier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Fourier "Joseph Fourier") formally introduces the use of [dimensions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimension "Dimension") for physical quantities in his _Théorie Analytique de la Chaleur_
*   1822– [Marc Seguin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marc_Seguin "Marc Seguin") writes to [John Herschel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Herschel "John Herschel") supporting the conservation of energy and kinetic theory
*   1824– [Sadi Carnot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolas_L%C3%A9onard_Sadi_Carnot "Nicolas Léonard Sadi Carnot") analyzes the efficiency of [steam engines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steam_engine "Steam engine") using [caloric theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caloric_theory "Caloric theory"); he develops the notion of a [reversible process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reversible_process_(thermodynamics) "Reversible process (thermodynamics)") and, in postulating that no such thing exists in nature, lays the foundation for the [second law of thermodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_law_of_thermodynamics "Second law of thermodynamics"), and initiating the science of thermodynamics
*   1827– [Robert Brown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Brown_(Scottish_botanist_from_Montrose) "Robert Brown (Scottish botanist from Montrose)") discovers the [Brownian motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brownian_motion "Brownian motion") of [pollen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pollen "Pollen") and dye particles in water [[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-10)
*   1831– [Macedonio Melloni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macedonio_Melloni "Macedonio Melloni") demonstrates that black-body radiation can be [reflected](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflection_(physics) "Reflection (physics)"), [refracted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Refraction "Refraction"), and [polarised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polarization_(waves) "Polarization (waves)") in the same way as light
*   1834– [Émile Clapeyron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89mile_Clapeyron "Émile Clapeyron") popularises Carnot's work through a graphical and analytic formulation. He also combined [Boyle's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boyle%27s_law "Boyle's law"), [Charles's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles%27s_law "Charles's law"), and [Gay-Lussac's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gay-Lussac%27s_law "Gay-Lussac's law") to produce a [combined gas law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combined_gas_law "Combined gas law"). PV/T = k [[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-11)
*   1841– [Julius Robert von Mayer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julius_Robert_von_Mayer "Julius Robert von Mayer"), an [amateur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amateur "Amateur") scientist, writes a paper on the conservation of energy, but his lack of academic training leads to its rejection
*   1842– Mayer makes a connection between work, heat, and the human [metabolism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metabolism "Metabolism") based on his observations of blood made while a ship's surgeon; he calculates the [mechanical equivalent of heat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanical_equivalent_of_heat "Mechanical equivalent of heat")
*   1842– [William Robert Grove](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Robert_Grove "William Robert Grove") demonstrates the thermal dissociation of molecules into their constituent atoms, by showing that steam can be disassociated into oxygen and hydrogen, and the process [reversed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reversible_process_(thermodynamics) "Reversible process (thermodynamics)")
*   1843– [John James Waterston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_James_Waterston "John James Waterston") fully expounds the kinetic theory of gases,[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-12) but according to D Levermore "there is no evidence that any physical scientist read the book; perhaps it was overlooked because of its misleading title, Thoughts on the Mental Functions."[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-13)
*   1843– [James Joule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Joule "James Joule") experimentally finds the mechanical equivalent of heat [[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-14)
*   1845– [Henri Victor Regnault](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henri_Victor_Regnault "Henri Victor Regnault") added [Avogadro's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avogadro%27s_law "Avogadro's law") to the [combined gas law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combined_gas_law "Combined gas law") to produce the [ideal gas law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ideal_gas_law "Ideal gas law"). PV = nRT
*   1846– Grove publishes an account of the general theory of the conservation of energy in _On The Correlation of Physical Forces_[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-15)
*   1847– [Hermann von Helmholtz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermann_von_Helmholtz "Hermann von Helmholtz") publishes a definitive statement of the conservation of energy, the [first law of thermodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_law_of_thermodynamics "First law of thermodynamics")[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-16)

*   1848– [William Thomson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Thomson,_1st_Baron_Kelvin "William Thomson, 1st Baron Kelvin") extends the concept of absolute zero from gases to all substances
*   1849– [William John Macquorn Rankine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_John_Macquorn_Rankine "William John Macquorn Rankine") calculates the correct relationship between [saturated vapour pressure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturation_vapor_pressure "Saturation vapor pressure") and [temperature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature "Temperature") using his _hypothesis of molecular vortices_
*   1850– Rankine uses his _vortex_ theory to establish accurate relationships between the temperature, [pressure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pressure "Pressure"), and [density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Density "Density") of gases, and expressions for the [latent heat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latent_heat "Latent heat") of [evaporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evaporation "Evaporation") of a liquid; he accurately predicts the surprising fact that the apparent [specific heat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Specific_heat "Specific heat") of saturated [steam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steam "Steam") will be negative
*   1850– [Rudolf Clausius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudolf_Clausius "Rudolf Clausius") coined the term "entropy" (das Wärmegewicht, symbolized S) to denote heat lost or turned into waste. ("Wärmegewicht" translates literally as "heat-weight"; the corresponding English term stems from the Greek τρέπω, "I turn".)
*   1850– Clausius gives the first clear joint statement of the [first](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_law_of_thermodynamics "First law of thermodynamics") and [second](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_law_of_thermodynamics "Second law of thermodynamics") law of thermodynamics, abandoning the caloric theory, but preserving Carnot's principle
*   1851– Thomson gives an alternative statement of the second law
*   1852– Joule and Thomson demonstrate that a rapidly expanding gas cools, later named the [Joule–Thomson effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joule%E2%80%93Thomson_effect "Joule–Thomson effect") or Joule–Kelvin effect
*   1854– Helmholtz puts forward the idea of the [heat death of the universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_death_of_the_universe "Heat death of the universe")
*   1854– Clausius establishes the importance of _dQ/T_ ([Clausius's theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clausius%27s_theorem "Clausius's theorem")), but does not yet name the quantity
*   1854– Rankine introduces his _thermodynamic function_, later identified as [entropy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entropy "Entropy")
*   1856– [August Krönig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/August_Kr%C3%B6nig "August Krönig") publishes an account of the kinetic theory of gases, probably after reading Waterston's work
*   1857– Clausius gives a modern and compelling account of the kinetic theory of gases in his _On the nature of motion called heat_
*   1859– [James Clerk Maxwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Clerk_Maxwell "James Clerk Maxwell") discovers the [distribution law of molecular velocities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maxwell%E2%80%93Boltzmann_distribution "Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution")
*   1859– [Gustav Kirchhoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gustav_Kirchhoff "Gustav Kirchhoff") shows that energy emission from a [black body](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_body "Black body") is a function of only temperature and frequency
*   1862– "[Disgregation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disgregation "Disgregation")", a precursor of [entropy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entropy "Entropy"), was defined in 1862 by Clausius as the magnitude of the degree of separation of molecules of a body
*   1865– Clausius introduces the modern [macroscopic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macroscopic "Macroscopic") concept of entropy
*   1865– [Josef Loschmidt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josef_Loschmidt "Josef Loschmidt") applies Maxwell's theory to estimate the number-density of molecules in gases, given observed gas viscosities.
*   1867– Maxwell asks whether [Maxwell's demon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maxwell%27s_demon "Maxwell's demon") could reverse irreversible processes
*   1870– Clausius proves the scalar [virial theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virial_theorem "Virial theorem")
*   1872– [Ludwig Boltzmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludwig_Boltzmann "Ludwig Boltzmann") states the [Boltzmann equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boltzmann_equation "Boltzmann equation") for the temporal development of [distribution functions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distribution_function_(physics) "Distribution function (physics)") in [phase space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phase_space "Phase space"), and publishes his [H-theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H-theorem "H-theorem")
*   1873- [Johannes Diderik van der Waals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannes_Diderik_van_der_Waals "Johannes Diderik van der Waals") formulates his [equation of state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equation_of_state "Equation of state")
*   1874– Thomson formally states the [second law of thermodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_law_of_thermodynamics "Second law of thermodynamics")
*   1876– [Josiah Willard Gibbs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josiah_Willard_Gibbs "Josiah Willard Gibbs") publishes the first of two papers (the second appears in 1878) which discuss phase equilibria, [statistical ensembles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_ensemble "Statistical ensemble"), the [free energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermodynamic_free_energy "Thermodynamic free energy") as the driving force behind [chemical reactions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_reaction "Chemical reaction"), and [chemical thermodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_thermodynamics "Chemical thermodynamics") in general.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]
*   1876– Loschmidt criticises Boltzmann's H theorem as being incompatible with microscopic reversibility ([Loschmidt's paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loschmidt%27s_paradox "Loschmidt's paradox")).
*   1877– Boltzmann states the relationship between entropy and [probability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probability "Probability")
*   1879– [Jožef Stefan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Stefan "Joseph Stefan") observes that the total radiant flux from a blackbody is proportional to the fourth power of its temperature and states the [Stefan–Boltzmann law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stefan%E2%80%93Boltzmann_law "Stefan–Boltzmann law")
*   1884– Boltzmann derives the Stefan–Boltzmann blackbody radiant flux law from thermodynamic considerations
*   1888– [Henri-Louis Le Chatelier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henri-Louis_Le_Chatelier "Henri-Louis Le Chatelier") states his [principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Le_Ch%C3%A2telier%27s_principle "Le Châtelier's principle") that the response of a chemical system perturbed from equilibrium will be to counteract the perturbation
*   1889– [Walther Nernst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walther_Nernst "Walther Nernst") relates the voltage of electrochemical cells to their chemical thermodynamics via the [Nernst equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nernst_equation "Nernst equation")
*   1889– [Svante Arrhenius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Svante_Arrhenius "Svante Arrhenius") introduces the idea of [activation energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Activation_energy "Activation energy") for chemical reactions, giving the [Arrhenius equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arrhenius_equation "Arrhenius equation")
*   1893– [Wilhelm Wien](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilhelm_Wien "Wilhelm Wien") discovers the displacement law for a blackbody's maximum specific intensity

*   1900– [Max Planck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Planck "Max Planck") suggests that light may be emitted in discrete frequencies, giving his [law of black-body radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planck%27s_law_of_black-body_radiation "Planck's law of black-body radiation")[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-17)
*   1905– [Albert Einstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Einstein "Albert Einstein"), in the first of his [miracle year papers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annus_Mirabilis_papers "Annus Mirabilis papers"), argues that the reality of [quanta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum "Quantum") would explain the [photoelectric effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photoelectric_effect "Photoelectric effect")[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-18)
*   1905– Einstein mathematically analyzes [Brownian motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brownian_motion "Brownian motion") as a result of random molecular motion in his paper [On the movement of small particles suspended in a stationary liquid demanded by the molecular-kinetic theory of heat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%9Cber_die_von_der_molekularkinetischen_Theorie_der_W%C3%A4rme_geforderte_Bewegung_von_in_ruhenden_Fl%C3%BCssigkeiten_suspendierten_Teilchen "Über die von der molekularkinetischen Theorie der Wärme geforderte Bewegung von in ruhenden Flüssigkeiten suspendierten Teilchen")
*   1906– Nernst presents a formulation of the [third law of thermodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_law_of_thermodynamics "Third law of thermodynamics")
*   1907– Einstein uses quantum theory to estimate the [heat capacity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_capacity "Heat capacity") of an [Einstein solid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einstein_solid "Einstein solid")
*   1909– [Constantin Carathéodory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constantin_Carath%C3%A9odory "Constantin Carathéodory") develops an [axiomatic system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiomatic_system "Axiomatic system") of thermodynamics [[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-19)
*   1910– Einstein and [Marian Smoluchowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marian_Smoluchowski "Marian Smoluchowski") find the [Einstein–Smoluchowski formula](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Einstein%E2%80%93Smoluchowski_formula&action=edit&redlink=1 "Einstein–Smoluchowski formula (page does not exist)") for the attenuation coefficient due to density fluctuations in a gas
*   1911– [Paul Ehrenfest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Ehrenfest "Paul Ehrenfest") and [Tatjana Ehrenfest–Afanassjewa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tatyana_Afanasyeva "Tatyana Afanasyeva") publish their classical review on the statistical mechanics of Boltzmann, _Begriffliche Grundlagen der statistischen Auffassung in der Mechanik_
*   1912– [Peter Debye](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Debye "Peter Debye") gives an [improved heat capacity estimate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debye_model "Debye model") by allowing low-frequency [phonons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phonons "Phonons")[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-20)
*   1916– [Sydney Chapman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sydney_Chapman_(mathematician) "Sydney Chapman (mathematician)") and [David Enskog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Enskog "David Enskog") systematically develop the kinetic theory of gases
*   1916– Einstein considers the thermodynamics of [atomic spectral lines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic_spectral_line "Atomic spectral line") and predicts [stimulated emission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stimulated_emission "Stimulated emission")
*   1919– [James Jeans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Jeans "James Jeans") discovers that the dynamical constants of motion determine the distribution function for a system of particles
*   1920– [Meghnad Saha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meghnad_Saha "Meghnad Saha") states his [ionization equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saha_ionization_equation "Saha ionization equation")[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-21)
*   1923– Debye and [Erich Hückel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erich_H%C3%BCckel "Erich Hückel") publish a statistical treatment of the dissociation of [electrolytes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrolyte "Electrolyte")
*   1924– [Satyendra Nath Bose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satyendra_Nath_Bose "Satyendra Nath Bose") introduces [Bose–Einstein statistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bose%E2%80%93Einstein_statistics "Bose–Einstein statistics"), in a paper translated by Einstein
*   1926– [Enrico Fermi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enrico_Fermi "Enrico Fermi")[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-Fermi1926-22) and [Paul Dirac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Dirac "Paul Dirac")[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-Dirac1926-23) introduce [Fermi–Dirac statistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermi%E2%80%93Dirac_statistics "Fermi–Dirac statistics")
*   1927– [John von Neumann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_von_Neumann "John von Neumann") introduces the [density matrix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Density_matrix "Density matrix") representation,[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-24) establishing [quantum statistical mechanics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_statistical_mechanics "Quantum statistical mechanics")
*   1928– [John B. Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Bertrand_Johnson "John Bertrand Johnson") discovers [Johnson noise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johnson_noise "Johnson noise") in a resistor [[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-25)[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-26)
*   1928– [Harry Nyquist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Nyquist "Harry Nyquist") derives the [fluctuation-dissipation theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluctuation-dissipation_theorem "Fluctuation-dissipation theorem"), a relationship to explain [Johnson noise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johnson_noise "Johnson noise") in a resistor [[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-27)
*   1931– [Lars Onsager](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lars_Onsager "Lars Onsager") publishes his groundbreaking paper deriving the [Onsager reciprocal relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Onsager_reciprocal_relations "Onsager reciprocal relations")[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-onsager-28)
*   1935– [Ralph H. Fowler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ralph_H._Fowler "Ralph H. Fowler") invents the title '[the zeroth law of thermodynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zeroth_law_of_thermodynamics "Zeroth law of thermodynamics")' to summarise postulates made by earlier physicists that thermal equilibrium between systems is a [transitive relation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transitive_relation "Transitive relation")
*   1938– [Anatoly Vlasov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatoly_Vlasov "Anatoly Vlasov") proposes the [Vlasov equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vlasov_equation "Vlasov equation") for a correct dynamical description of ensembles of particles with collective long range interaction[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-29)[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-30)
*   1939– [Nikolay Krylov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolay_Mitrofanovich_Krylov "Nikolay Mitrofanovich Krylov") and [Nikolay Bogolyubov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolay_Bogolyubov "Nikolay Bogolyubov") give the first consistent microscopic derivation of the [Fokker–Planck equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fokker%E2%80%93Planck_equation "Fokker–Planck equation") in the single scheme of classical and quantum mechanics [[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-31)[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-32)
*   1942– [Joseph L. Doob](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_L._Doob "Joseph L. Doob") states his theorem on [Gauss–Markov processes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauss%E2%80%93Markov_process "Gauss–Markov process")
*   1944– [Lars Onsager](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lars_Onsager "Lars Onsager") gives an analytic solution to the 2-dimensional [Ising model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ising_model "Ising model"), including its [phase transition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phase_transition "Phase transition")[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-33)

*   1945–1946– [Nikolay Bogoliubov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolay_Bogoliubov "Nikolay Bogoliubov") develops a general method for a microscopic derivation of kinetic equations for classical statistical systems using [BBGKY hierarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBGKY_hierarchy "BBGKY hierarchy")[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-a-34)[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-b-35)
*   1947– [Nikolay Bogoliubov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolay_Bogoliubov "Nikolay Bogoliubov") and [Kirill Gurov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kirill_Gurov "Kirill Gurov") extend this method for a microscopic derivation of kinetic equations for quantum statistical systems
*   1948– [Claude Elwood Shannon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Claude_Elwood_Shannon "Claude Elwood Shannon") establishes [information theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_theory "Information theory")[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-36)
*   1957– [Aleksandr Solomonovich Kompaneets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleksandr_Solomonovich_Kompaneets "Aleksandr Solomonovich Kompaneets") derives his Compton scattering [Fokker–Planck equation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fokker%E2%80%93Planck_equation "Fokker–Planck equation")
*   1957– [Ryogo Kubo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryogo_Kubo "Ryogo Kubo") derives the first of the [Green-Kubo relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green-Kubo_relations "Green-Kubo relations") for linear transport coefficients [[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-37)
*   1957– [Edwin T. Jaynes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edwin_T._Jaynes "Edwin T. Jaynes") publishes two papers detailing the [MaxEnt interpretation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximum_entropy_thermodynamics "Maximum entropy thermodynamics") of thermodynamics from information theory [[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-38)[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_thermodynamics#cite_note-39)
*   1960–1965– [Dmitry Zubarev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dmitry_Zubarev "Dmitry Zubarev") develops the method of [non-equilibrium statistical operator](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Non-equilibrium_statistical_operator&action=edit&redlink=1 "Non-equilibrium statistical operator (page does not exist)"), which becomes a classical tool in the statistical theory of non-equilibrium processes
*   1972– [Jacob Bekenstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacob_Bekenstein "Jacob Bekenstein") suggests that [black holes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_holes "Black holes") have an entropy proportional to their surface area
*   1974– [Stephen Hawking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_Hawking "Stephen Hawking") predicts that black holes will [radiate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawking_radiation "Hawking radiation") particles with a black-body spectrum which can cause black hole evaporation
*   1977– [Ilya Prigogine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilya_Prigogine "Ilya Prigogine") wins the Nobel prize for his work on [dissipative structures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissipative_structures "Dissipative structures") in thermodynamic systems far from equilibrium. The importation and dissipation of energy could reverse the 2nd law of thermodynamics
