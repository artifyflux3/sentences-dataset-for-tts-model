From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Branch of solar physics and aeronomy

[![Image 6](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Sunspots_11000_years.svg/330px-Sunspots_11000_years.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Sunspots_11000_years.svg)

Reconstruction of solar activity over 11,400 years. Period of equally high activity over 8,000 years ago marked.

**Space climate** is the long-term variation in [solar activity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_phenomena "Solar phenomena") within the [heliosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliosphere "Heliosphere"), including the [solar wind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_wind "Solar wind"), the [Interplanetary magnetic field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interplanetary_magnetic_field "Interplanetary magnetic field") (IMF), and their effects in the near-Earth environment, including the [magnetosphere of Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetosphere_of_Earth "Magnetosphere of Earth") and the [ionosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionosphere "Ionosphere"), the upper and lower atmosphere, [climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate "Climate"), and other related systems. The scientific study of space climate is an interdisciplinary field of [space physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_physics "Space physics"), [solar physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_physics "Solar physics"), [heliophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliophysics "Heliophysics"), and [geophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geophysics "Geophysics"). It is thus conceptually related to terrestrial [climatology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climatology "Climatology"), and its effects on the [atmosphere of Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmosphere_of_Earth "Atmosphere of Earth") are considered in climate science.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-Climate_Intro-1)[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-Solar_Origins-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-Historical_Space_Climate-3)

Background
----------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Space_climate&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Background")]

Space climatology considers long-term (longer than the latitudinally variable 27-day [solar rotation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_rotation "Solar rotation") period, through the 11-year [solar cycle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_cycle "Solar cycle") and beyond, up to and exceeding millennia) variability of solar indices, [cosmic ray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmic_ray "Cosmic ray"), heliospheric parameters, and the induced geomagnetic, ionospheric, atmospheric, and climate effects.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-Climate_Intro-1) It studies mechanisms and physical processes responsible for their variability in the past with projections onto future.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-Solar_Origins-2) It is a broader and more general concept than [space weather](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_weather "Space weather"), to which it is related like the conventional climate and [weather](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weather "Weather").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-Climate_Intro-1)

In addition to real-time [solar observations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_observation "Solar observation"), the field of research also covers analysis of historical space climate data. This has included analysis and reconstruction that has allowed solar wind and heliospheric [magnetic field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetic_field "Magnetic field") strengths to be determined from back to 1611.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-Historical_Space_Climate-3)

[![Image 7](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/DSCOVR_spacecraft_model.png/330px-DSCOVR_spacecraft_model.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:DSCOVR_spacecraft_model.png)

Artist's rendering of Deep Space Climate Observatory (DSCOVR)

The importance of space climate research has been recognized, in particular, by NASA which launched a special space mission [Deep Space Climate Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_Space_Climate_Observatory "Deep Space Climate Observatory") (DSCOVR)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-4) dedicated to monitoring of space climate.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-5) New results, ideas and discoveries in the field of Space Climate are published in a focused peer-review research _Journal of Space Weather and Space Climate_ (JSWSC).[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-6) Since 2013, research awards and medals in space weather and space climate are annually awarded by the European Space Weather Week.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-7) Another recent space observatory platform is the [Solar Radiation and Climate Experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_Radiation_and_Climate_Experiment "Solar Radiation and Climate Experiment") (SORCE).

Space climate research has three main aims:[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-Climate_Intro-1)

1.   to better understand the long-term [solar variability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_variation "Solar variation"), including also the observed extremes and features of this variability in the [solar wind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_wind "Solar wind") and in the [heliospheric magnetic field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliospheric_magnetic_field "Heliospheric magnetic field")
2.   to better understand the physical relationships between the [Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun "Sun"), the heliosphere, and various related [proxies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proxy_(statistics) "Proxy (statistics)") (geomagnetic fields, cosmic rays, etc.)
3.   to better understand the long-term effect of solar variability on the near-[Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth") environment, including the different atmospheric layers, and ultimately on Earth's global climate

### History

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Space_climate&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: History")]

In the early 2000s, when the concept of space weather became common, a small initiative group, led by Kalevi Mursula and [Ilya G. Usoskin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilya_G._Usoskin "Ilya G. Usoskin") the [University of Oulu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Oulu "University of Oulu") in Finland had realized that physical drivers of solar variability and its terrestrial effects can be better understood with a more general and broader view. The concept of _Space Climate_ had been developed, and the corresponding research community formed, which presently includes a few hundred active members around the world. In particular, a series of International Space Climate Symposia (biennial since 2004) was organized,[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-8) with the first inaugural symposium being held in Oulu (Finland) in 2004, followed by those in Romania (2006), Finland (2009), India (2011), Finland (2013), Finland (2016), Canada (2019), Poland (2022) and Japan (2024) as well as topical space climate sessions are regularly held at the General Assemblies of the [Committee on Space Research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Committee_on_Space_Research "Committee on Space Research") and Earth Science.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_climate#cite_note-10)

Dissemination
-------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Space_climate&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Dissemination")]

Research results related to Space Climate are published in a bunch of peer-reviewed journals, such as [Astronomy & Astrophysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomy_%26_Astrophysics "Astronomy & Astrophysics"), [Journal of Geophysical Research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Journal_of_Geophysical_Research "Journal of Geophysical Research"), [Geophysical Research Letters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geophysical_Research_Letters "Geophysical Research Letters"), [Solar Physics (journal)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_Physics_(journal) "Solar Physics (journal)"), [Advances in Space Research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advances_in_Space_Research "Advances in Space Research").
