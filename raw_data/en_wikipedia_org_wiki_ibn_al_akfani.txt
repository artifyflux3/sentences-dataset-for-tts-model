[ 1 ] Life [ edit ] Ibn al-Akfani was born in Sinjar and lived in Cairo , the capital of Mamluk Egypt . He worked at Al-Mansuri Hospital during a time where the Black Death swept across Egypt with Cairo reporting around 20,000 deaths per day. He died in either 1348 or 1349 of the bubonic plague .

[ 2 ] Work [ edit ] He wrote at least 22 books.

[ 3 ] Most of his books were science related, including logic , gemology , mathematics , medicine and astronomy .

[ 2 ] Specific subjects include bloodletting , slavery and ophthalmology .

[ 4 ] His most famous work was a science encyclopedia called Iršād al-qāsid ilā asnā' al-maqāsid .

[ 2 ] The encyclopedia examines 60 subjects with bibliographies and a glossary of terms.

[ 3 ] His book, Kitāb nuhab al-dahā'ir fī ahwāl al-jawāhir , is about gemstones , with a focus about jacinth .
