_**Nicotiana tabacum**_, or **cultivated tobacco**, is an [annually grown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annual_plant "Annual plant")[herbaceous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbaceous "Herbaceous") plant of the [genus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genus "Genus")_[Nicotiana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana "Nicotiana")_. _N. tabacum_ is the most commonly grown species in the genus _Nicotiana,_ as the plant's leaves are commercially harvested to be processed into [tobacco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tobacco "Tobacco") for human use. The plant is tropical in origin, is commonly grown throughout the world, and is often found in [cultivation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cultivation_of_tobacco "Cultivation of tobacco"). It grows to heights between 1 and 2 metres (3.3 and 6.6 ft). Research is ongoing into its ancestry among wild _[Nicotiana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana "Nicotiana")_ species, but it is believed to be a hybrid of _[Nicotiana sylvestris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana\_sylvestris "Nicotiana sylvestris")_, _[N. tomentosiformis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana\_tomentosiformis "Nicotiana tomentosiformis")_, and possibly _[N. otophora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana\_otophora "Nicotiana otophora")_.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-1)

It is an annual plant that grows 1 to 3 metres (3 to 10 feet) high and is sticky haired on all parts. The stems are thick and not very branched. The [leaves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leaves "Leaves") can be over 50 centimetres (1 ft 8 in) long with the [blades](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leaf_blade "Leaf blade") ovate to elliptical, or [obovate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obovate "Obovate"), pointed towards the front and, at the base, run down the stem or are [sessile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sessility_(botany) "Sessility (botany)"), encompassing the stem.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

The scented [inflorescences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inflorescence "Inflorescence") are multi-branched [panicles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panicles "Panicles"). The flower stalks are 5 to 15 millimetres (3⁄16 to 9⁄16 in) long. The [calyx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calyx_(botany) "Calyx (botany)") is 12 to 18 millimetres (1⁄2 to 11⁄16 in) and is covered with uneven 4 to 8 mm (3⁄16 to 5⁄16 in) narrow pointed calyx lobes shorter than the calyx tube. The [crown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_(botany) "Crown (botany)") is plate-shaped, the coronet is white, pink or red, the [corolla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corolla_(botany) "Corolla (botany)") tube greenish-cream, pink or red. The corolla tube has a total length of 3.5 to 4.5 cm (1+3⁄8 to 1+3⁄4 in) and is 3 to 5 mm (1⁄8 to 3⁄16 in) wide in the lower part and widens to 7 to 12 mm (1⁄4 to 1⁄2 in) in the upper part. The coronet is lobed or pentagonal. The [stamens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stamen "Stamen") are designed unevenly and start below the center of the corolla tube. The [anthers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthers "Anthers") of the four longer stamens are close to the opening of the corolla tube or are slightly above it. The fifth stamen is significantly shorter than both longer pairs. The stamens have a length of 2.5 to 3.5 cm (1 to 1+3⁄8 in), significantly longer than the anthers, and are hairy at the base.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

The fruit is a 1.5 to 2 cm (9⁄16 to 13⁄16 in) long capsule that is narrowly elliptical to egg-shaped. It can stand out over the calyx or be enclosed by it. The seeds are spherical or broadly elliptical and are up to 0.5 mm (1⁄64 in) long with a wavy networked surface.

Almost every part of the plant except the seed contains nicotine, but the concentration is related to different factors such as species, type of land, culture and weather conditions. The concentration of nicotine increases with the age of the plant. Tobacco leaves contain 2 to 8% nicotine combined as [malate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malate "Malate") or [citrate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citrate "Citrate"). The distribution of the nicotine in the mature plant is widely variable: 64% of the total nicotine exists in the leaves, 18% in the stem, 13% in the root, and 5% in the flowers.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Natural tobacco [polysaccharides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polysaccharide "Polysaccharide"), including [cellulose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellulose "Cellulose"), have been shown to be the primary precursors of [acetaldehyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acetaldehyde "Acetaldehyde") in [tobacco smoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tobacco_smoke "Tobacco smoke").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-2) The main [polyphenols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyphenols "Polyphenols") contained in the [tobacco leaf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tobacco_leaf "Tobacco leaf") are [rutin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rutin "Rutin") and [chlorogenic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chlorogenic_acid "Chlorogenic acid"). [Amino acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acids "Amino acids") contained include [glutamic acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glutamic_acids "Glutamic acids"), [asparagine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asparagine "Asparagine"), [glutamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glutamine "Glutamine"), and [γ-Aminobutyric acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%CE%93-Aminobutyric_acid "Γ-Aminobutyric acid")[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-3)

[Pyridine alkaloids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyridine_alkaloids "Pyridine alkaloids") are present in tobacco as [free bases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_bases "Free bases") and [salts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salts "Salts"). [Nicotine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotine "Nicotine") accounts for 90–95% of the plant's [pyridines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyridines "Pyridines") with [nornicotine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nornicotine "Nornicotine") and [anatabine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatabine "Anatabine") accounting for roughly 2.5% each.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-4)[Pyridyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyridyl "Pyridyl")[functional groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_groups "Functional groups") present in minute amounts include [anabasine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anabasine "Anabasine"), [myosmine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myosmine "Myosmine"), [cotinine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cotinine "Cotinine") and [2, 3′-bipyridyl](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=2,_3%E2%80%B2-bipyridyl&action=edit&redlink=1 "2, 3′-bipyridyl (page does not exist)").[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-5) Indole alkaloids are also present in leaves and stems which includes harmala alkaloids as well. [Yohimbine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yohimbine "Yohimbine"), [harmine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harmine "Harmine"), [harmaline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harmaline "Harmaline") and [ajmalicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ajmalicine "Ajmalicine") occurs in descending order, yohimbine being highest.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-pmid32708364-6)

The [tobacco plant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tobacco_plant "Tobacco plant") readily absorbs [heavy metals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heavy_metals "Heavy metals") from the surrounding soil and accumulates them in its leaves. These are readily absorbed into the user's body following smoke inhalation.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-7)

Tobacco also contains the following [phytochemicals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phytochemicals "Phytochemicals"): [glucosides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glucoside "Glucoside") ([tabacinine](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tabacinine&action=edit&redlink=1 "Tabacinine (page does not exist)"), [tabacine](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tabacine&action=edit&redlink=1 "Tabacine (page does not exist)")), [2,3,6-trimethyl-1,4-naphthoquinone](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=2,3,6-trimethyl-1,4-naphthoquinone&action=edit&redlink=1 "2,3,6-trimethyl-1,4-naphthoquinone (page does not exist)"), [2-methylquinone](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=2-methylquinone&action=edit&redlink=1 "2-methylquinone (page does not exist)"), [2-Naphthylamine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2-Naphthylamine "2-Naphthylamine"), [propionic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propionic_acid "Propionic acid"), [anthalin](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Anthalin&action=edit&redlink=1 "Anthalin (page does not exist)"), [anethole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anethole "Anethole"), [acrolein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acrolein "Acrolein"), [cembrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cembrene "Cembrene"), [choline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choline "Choline"), [nicotelline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotelline "Nicotelline"), [nicotianine](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nicotianine&action=edit&redlink=1 "Nicotianine (page does not exist)"), and [pyrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrene "Pyrene").[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-8)

Tobacco aroma is due to presence of different volatile compounds; [megastigmatrienone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megastigmatrienone "Megastigmatrienone") being the key flavor compound in tobacco.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-pmid24528661-9)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-pmid26948587-10) Megastigmatrienone is used as flavour in tobacco products as well.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-Kr%C3%BCsemannVisser2018-11)

Megastigmatrienone, a [cyclohexenone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclohexenone "Cyclohexenone"),[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-ymdb.ca-01739-12) and a carotenoid-derived aromatic compound, produces [spice notes associated with wine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aroma_of_wine#Identified_aroma_compounds "Aroma of wine").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-pmid24528661-9)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-13)[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-j.foodchem.2016.02.034-14)[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-ncats.io/drug/3QY2F7PCN3-15)

Megastigmatrienone is a metabolite of [Brewer's yeast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saccharomyces_cerevisiae "Saccharomyces cerevisiae").[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-ymdb.ca-01739-12)

Megastigmatrienone, as _Tabanone_, is manufactured by [Symrise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symrise "Symrise") as a perfume ingredient.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-16)[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-17)[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-18)[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-patent/US8513180B2-19)

Habitat and ecology
-------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nicotiana_tabacum&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Habitat and ecology")]

_N. tabacum_ is a native of [tropical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical "Tropical") and [subtropical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subtropical "Subtropical")[America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americas "Americas"), also appearing as a [weed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weed "Weed").[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

_N. tabacum_ is sensitive to temperature, air, ground humidity and the type of land. Temperatures of 20–30°C (68–86°F) are best for adequate growth; an atmospheric humidity of 80 to 85% and soil without a high level of [nitrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrogen "Nitrogen") are also optimal.

The potato tuber moth (_[Phthorimaea operculella](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phthorimaea\_operculella "Phthorimaea operculella")_) is an oligophagous insect that prefers to feed on plants of the family Solanaceae such as tobacco plants. Female _P. operculella_ use the leaves to lay their eggs and the hatched larvae will eat away at the mesophyll of the leaf.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-20)

The plant is native to the [Caribbean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caribbean "Caribbean"), where the [Taíno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno "Taíno") people were the first recorded peoples to use it and cultivate it. In 1560, [Jean Nicot de Villemain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Nicot_de_Villemain "Jean Nicot de Villemain"), then French ambassador to [Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal "Portugal"), brought tobacco seeds and leaves as a "wonder drug" to the [French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France "France") court. In 1586 the [botanist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Botanist "Botanist") Jaques Dalechamps gave the plant the name of _Herba nicotiana_, which was also adopted by [Linné](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linn%C3%A9 "Linné"). It was considered a [decorative plant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decorative_plant "Decorative plant") at first, then a [panacea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panacea_(medicine) "Panacea (medicine)"), before it became a common snuff and tobacco plant.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Tobacco arrived in [Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "Africa") at the beginning of the 17th century. The leaf extract was a popular pest control method up to the beginning of the 20th century. In 1851, the [Belgian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgium "Belgium")[chemist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemist "Chemist")[Jean Stas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Stas "Jean Stas") documented the use of tobacco extract as a murder [poison](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poison "Poison"). The Belgian count [Hippolyte Visart de Bocarmé](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hippolyte_Visart_de_Bocarm%C3%A9 "Hippolyte Visart de Bocarmé") had poisoned his brother-in-law with tobacco leaf extract in order to acquire some urgently needed money. This was the first exact proof of [alkaloids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkaloids "Alkaloids") in [forensic medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forensic_medicine "Forensic medicine").[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-21)

It is now commercially [cultivated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horticulture "Horticulture") worldwide. All parts of the plant contain nicotine, which can be extracted and used as an insecticide. The dried leaves can also be used; they remain effective for 6 months after drying. The juice of the leaves can be rubbed on the body as an insect repellent. The leaves can be dried and chewed as an [intoxicant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intoxicant "Intoxicant"). The dried leaves are also used as snuff or are smoked. This is the main species that is used to make [cigarettes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cigarettes "Cigarettes"), [cigars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cigar "Cigar"), and other products. A [drying oil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drying_oil "Drying oil") is obtained from the seed.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Other varieties are cultivated as [ornamental plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ornamental_plants "Ornamental plants").

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Dried_tobacco_at_the_North_Carolina_State_Fair.jpg/250px-Dried_tobacco_at_the_North_Carolina_State_Fair.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Dried_tobacco_at_the_North_Carolina_State_Fair.jpg)

Dried tobacco at the North Carolina State Fair

After tobacco is harvested, it is cured (dried), and then aged to improve its flavor. There are four common methods of [curing tobacco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curing_tobacco "Curing tobacco"): air curing, fire curing, flue curing, and sun curing. The curing method used depends on the type of tobacco and its intended use. Air-cured tobacco is sheltered from wind and sun in a well-ventilated barn, where it air dries for six to eight weeks. Air-cured tobacco is low in sugar, which gives the tobacco smoke a light, sweet flavour, and high in nicotine. Cigar and burley tobaccos are air cured.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

In fire curing, smoke from a low-burning fire on the barn floor permeates the leaves. This gives the leaves a distinctive smoky aroma and flavor. Fire curing takes three to ten weeks and produces a tobacco low in sugar and high in nicotine. Pipe tobacco, chewing tobacco, and snuff are fire cured.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Flue-cured tobacco is kept in an enclosed barn heated by flues (pipes) of hot air, but the tobacco is not directly exposed to smoke. This method produces cigarette tobacco that is high in sugar and has medium to high levels of nicotine. It is the fastest method of curing, requiring about a week. Virginia tobacco that has been flue cured is also called bright tobacco, because flue curing turns its leaves gold, orange, or yellow.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Sun-cured tobacco dries uncovered in the sun. This method is used in Greece, Turkey, and other Mediterranean countries to produce oriental tobacco. Sun-cured tobacco is low in sugar and nicotine and is used in cigarettes.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

Once the tobacco is cured, workers tie it into small bundles of about 20 leaves, called hands, or use a machine to make large blocks, called bales. The hands or bales are aged for one to three years to improve flavor and reduce bitterness.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

### Ethnomedicinal uses

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nicotiana_tabacum&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Ethnomedicinal uses")]

The regions that have histories of use of the plant include:[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation\_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]

*   [Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil "Brazil"): The leaf juice is taken orally to induce [vomiting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vomiting "Vomiting") and narcosis.
*   [Colombia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colombia "Colombia"): Fresh leaf is used as poultice over boils and infected wounds; the leaves are crushed with oil from palms and used as hair treatment to prevent baldness.
*   [Cuba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuba "Cuba"): Extract of the leaf is taken orally to treat dysmenorrhea.
*   [East Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Africa "East Africa"): Dried leaves of _Nicotiana tabacum_ and _[Securinega](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Securinega "Securinega") viros_ a are mixed into a paste and used externally to destroy worms in sores.
*   [Ecuador](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecuador "Ecuador"): Leaf juice is used for indisposition, chills and snake bites and to treat pulmonary ailments.
*   [Fiji](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiji "Fiji"): Fresh root is taken orally for asthma and indigestion; fresh root is applied ophthalmically as drops for bloodshot eyes and other problems; seed is taken orally for rheumatism and to treat hoarseness.
*   [Guatemala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guatemala "Guatemala"): Leaves are applied externally by adults for myiasis, headache and wounds; hot water extract of the dried leaf is applied externally for ring worms, fungal diseases of the n, wounds, ulcers, bruises, sores, mouth lesions, stomatitis and mucosa; leaf is orally taken for kidney diseases.
*   [Haiti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haiti "Haiti"): [Decoction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decoction "Decoction") of dried leaf is taken orally for bronchitis and pneumonia.
*   [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong"): Fresh leaves are mashed and combined with vegetable oil to create a potion that is applied to injuries for it to heal faster. This practice is also apparent in other places in [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China").
*   [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran"): Infusion of the dried leaf is applied externally as an insect repellent; ointments made from crushed leaves are used for baldness, dermatitis and infectious ulceration and as a pediculicide.
*   [Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico "Mexico") (south-eastern): Among the [ancient Maya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_civilization "Maya civilization"), _Nicotiana_ was considered a sacred plant, closely associated with deities of earth and sky, and used for both [visionary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visionary "Visionary") and [therapeutic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Therapeutic "Therapeutic") ends. The contemporary [Tzeltal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tzeltal_people "Tzeltal people") and [Tzotzil Maya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tzotzil_people "Tzotzil people") of [Highland Chiapas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiapas_highlands "Chiapas highlands") (Mexico) are bearers of this [ethnobotanical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnobotanical "Ethnobotanical") inheritance, preserving a rich and varied tradition of _Nicotiana_ use and [folklore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Folklore "Folklore"). The entire tobacco plant is viewed as a primordial medicine and a powerful botanical "helper" or "protector". Depending on the condition to be treated, whole _Nicotiana_ leaves are used alone or in combination with other herbs in the preparation of various medicinal plasters and teas. In its most common form, fresh or _green_ leaves are ground with slaked lime to produce an intoxicating oral snuff that serves as both a protective and therapeutic agent.[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-22)
*   [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States"): Extract of _N. tabacum_ is taken orally to treat tiredness, ward off diseases, and quiet fear.
*   [Tanzania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania "Tanzania"): Leaves of _Nicotiana tabacum_ are placed in the vagina to stimulate labour.
*   [Zimbabwe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe "Zimbabwe"): Leaves or root of the plant are infused and taken by mouth for asthma and other respiratory problems. Leaves and roots are also rubbed against warts and wounds as ointment.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotiana_tabacum#cite_note-23)
