From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Upper boundary of the thermosphere

The **thermopause** is the [atmospheric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere "Earth's atmosphere") boundary of Earth's energy system, located at the top of the [thermosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermosphere "Thermosphere").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermopause#cite_note-Torres-1) The temperature of the thermopause could range from nearly absolute zero to 987.547°C (1,810°F).

Below this, the atmosphere is defined to be active[_[clarification needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Please\_clarify "Wikipedia:Please clarify")_] on the [insolation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insolation "Insolation") received, due to the increased presence of heavier gases such as monatomic oxygen. The [solar constant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_constant "Solar constant") is thus expressed at the thermopause. Beyond (above) this, the [exosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exosphere "Exosphere") describes the thinnest remainder of atmospheric particles with large mean free path, mostly hydrogen and helium. As a lower boundary for the exosphere this boundary is also called the **exobase**.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermopause#cite_note-Torres-1)

The exact altitude varies by the energy inputs of location, time of day, solar flux, season, etc. and can be between 500 and 1,000 kilometres (310 and 620 mi) high at a given place and time because of these. A [portion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Atlantic_Anomaly "South Atlantic Anomaly") of the [magnetosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetosphere "Magnetosphere") dips below this layer as well.

Although these are all named layers of the atmosphere, the pressure is so negligible that the chiefly-used definitions of [outer space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_space "Outer space") are actually below this altitude. Orbiting satellites do not experience significant atmospheric heating, but their orbits do decay over time, depending on orbit altitude. Space missions such as the [ISS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Space_Station "International Space Station"), [Space Shuttle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_Shuttle "Space Shuttle"), and [Soyuz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soyuz_programme "Soyuz programme") operate under this boundary.
