the Church Dominican Order Empiricism List of institutions named after Aquinas Saint Thomas Aquinas Church Catholic Church portal Philosophy portal v t e The Peripatetic axiom is: "Nothing is in the intellect that was not first in the senses"  ( Latin : Nihil est in intellectu quod non sit prius in sensu ). It is found in De veritate (q. 2 a. 3 arg. 19) by Thomas Aquinas .

[ 1 ] Aquinas adopted this principle from the Peripatetic school of Greek philosophy , established by Aristotle in his Lyceum in ancient Athens. Aquinas argued that the existence of God could be proved by reasoning from sense data.

[ 2 ] He used a variation on the Aristotelian notion of the " active intellect " (Latin: intellectus agens ), [ 3 ] which he interpreted as the ability to abstract universal meanings from particular empirical data.
