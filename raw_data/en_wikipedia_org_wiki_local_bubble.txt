The **Local Bubble**, or **Local Cavity**,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-Abt2015-3) is a relative [cavity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superbubble "Superbubble") in the [interstellar medium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") (ISM) of the [Orion Arm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orion_Arm "Orion Arm") in the [Milky Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way "Milky Way"). It contains the [nearest stars and brown dwarfs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_nearest_stars_and_brown_dwarfs "List of nearest stars and brown dwarfs") and, among others, the [Local Interstellar Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud "Local Interstellar Cloud") (which contains the [Solar System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_System "Solar System")), the neighboring [G-Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G-Cloud "G-Cloud"), the [Ursa Major moving group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursa_Major_moving_group "Ursa Major moving group") ([List of nearby stellar associations and moving groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_nearby_stellar_associations_and_moving_groups "List of nearby stellar associations and moving groups") stellar [moving group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_kinematics#moving_group "Stellar kinematics")), and the [Hyades](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyades_(star_cluster) "Hyades (star cluster)") (the nearest [open cluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_cluster "Open cluster")). It is estimated to be at least 1000[light years](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light_years "Light years") in size,[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-4)[_[clarification needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Please\_clarify "Wikipedia:Please clarify")_] and is defined by its neutral-[hydrogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen "Hydrogen") density of about 0.05[atoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atom "Atom")/cm 3, or approximately one tenth of the average for the ISM in the Milky Way (0.5 atoms/cm 3), and one sixth that of the Local Interstellar Cloud (0.3 atoms/cm 3).[_[dubious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy\_dispute#Disputed\_statement "Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute") – [discuss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Talk:Local\_Bubble#Dubious "Talk:Local Bubble")_][[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-nasa-5)

The exceptionally sparse gas of the Local Bubble is the result of [supernovae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supernova "Supernova") that exploded within the past ten to twenty million years. [Geminga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geminga "Geminga"), a [pulsar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pulsar "Pulsar") in the [constellation Gemini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemini_(constellation) "Gemini (constellation)"), was once thought to be the remnant of a single supernova that created the Local Bubble, but now multiple supernovae in subgroup B1 of the [Pleiades](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pleiades "Pleiades")[moving group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moving_group "Moving group") are thought to have been responsible,[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-T._W._Berghoefer,_D._Breitschwerdt_2002-6) becoming a remnant [supershell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supershell "Supershell").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-7) Other research suggests that the subgroups Lower Centaurus–Crux (LCC) and Upper Centaurus–Lupus (UCL), of the [Scorpius–Centaurus association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scorpius%E2%80%93Centaurus_association "Scorpius–Centaurus association") created both the Local Bubble and the Loop I Bubble, with LCC being responsible for the Local Bubble and UCL being responsible for the Loop I Bubble.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-8) It was found that 14 to 20 supernovae originated from LCC and UCL, which could have formed these bubbles.[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-9)

The [Solar System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_System "Solar System") has been traveling through the region currently occupied by the Local Bubble for the last five to ten million years.[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-solstation-10) Its current location lies in the [Local Interstellar Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud "Local Interstellar Cloud") (LIC), a minor region of denser material within the Bubble. The LIC formed where the Local Bubble and the [Loop I Bubble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loop_I_Bubble "Loop I Bubble") met. The gas within the LIC has a density of approximately 0.3 atoms per cubic centimeter.

The Local Bubble is not spherical, but appears to be narrower in the [galactic plane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galactic_plane "Galactic plane"), becoming somewhat egg-shaped or elliptical, and may widen above and below the galactic plane, becoming shaped like an hourglass. It abuts other bubbles of less dense interstellar medium (ISM), including, in particular, the Loop I Bubble. The Loop I Bubble was cleared, heated, and maintained by supernovae and [stellar winds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_wind "Stellar wind") in the [Scorpius–Centaurus association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scorpius%E2%80%93Centaurus_association "Scorpius–Centaurus association"), some 500 light years from the [Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun "Sun"). The Loop I Bubble contains the star [Antares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antares "Antares") (also known as α Sco, or Alpha Scorpii), as shown on the diagram above right. Several tunnels connect the cavities of the Local Bubble with the Loop I Bubble, called the "Lupus Tunnel".[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-11) Other bubbles adjacent to the Local Bubble are the [Loop II Bubble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loop_II_Bubble "Loop II Bubble") and the [Loop III Bubble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loop_III_Bubble "Loop III Bubble"). In 2019, researchers found interstellar iron in Antarctica which they relate to the [Local Interstellar Cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Interstellar_Cloud "Local Interstellar Cloud"), which might be related to the formation of the Local Bubble.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-Interstellar_Iron-12)

Local stars in the galactic plane (click for rotation)

Launched in February 2003 and active until April 2008, a small space observatory called [Cosmic Hot Interstellar Plasma Spectrometer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CHIPSat "CHIPSat") (CHIPSat) examined the hot gas within the Local Bubble.[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-13) The Local Bubble was also the region of interest for the [Extreme Ultraviolet Explorer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extreme_Ultraviolet_Explorer "Extreme Ultraviolet Explorer") mission (1992–2001), which examined hot EUV sources within the bubble. Sources beyond the edge of the bubble were identified but attenuated by the denser interstellar medium. In 2019, the first 3D map of the Local Bubble was reported using observations of diffuse interstellar bands.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-14) In 2020, the shape of the dusty envelope surrounding the Local Bubble was retrieved and modeled from 3D maps of the dust density obtained from stellar extinction data.[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-15)

Impact on star formation
------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Local_Bubble&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Impact on star formation")]

[![Image 3](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Localbubble_formation.gif/250px-Localbubble_formation.gif)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Localbubble_formation.gif)

As the bubble expands it sweeps [interstellar gas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_medium "Interstellar medium") and dust which collapse to form new stars on its surface but not inside. The [Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun "Sun") entered the bubble around five million years ago.[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-:0-16)[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-:1-17)

[![Image 4](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Localbubble.png/250px-Localbubble.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Localbubble.png)

Local Bubble and its [molecular clouds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_cloud "Molecular cloud")

In January 2022, a paper in the journal _Nature_ found that observations and modeling had determined that the action of the expanding surface of the bubble had collected gas and debris and was responsible for the formation of all young, nearby stars.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-18)

These new stars are typically in molecular clouds like the [Taurus molecular cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taurus_molecular_cloud "Taurus molecular cloud") and the open star cluster [Pleiades](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pleiades "Pleiades").

Connection to radioactive isotopes on Earth
-------------------------------------------

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Local_Bubble&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Connection to radioactive isotopes on Earth")]

Several [radioactive isotopes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radionuclide "Radionuclide") on [Earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth "Earth") have been connected to supernovae occurring relatively nearby to the solar system. The most common source is found in deep sea [ferromanganese crusts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manganese_nodule "Manganese nodule"), which are constantly growing, aggregating iron, manganese, and other elements. Samples are divided into layers which are dated, for example, with [Beryllium-10](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beryllium-10 "Beryllium-10"). Some of these layers have higher concentrations of radioactive isotopes.[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-:3-19) The isotope most commonly associated with supernovae on Earth is [Iron-60](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isotopes_of_iron "Isotopes of iron") from [deep sea sediments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_sediment "Marine sediment"),[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-20)[Antarctic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic "Antarctic") snow,[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-21) and [lunar soil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lunar_soil "Lunar soil").[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-22) Other isotopes are [Manganese-53](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isotopes_of_manganese "Isotopes of manganese")[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-23) and [Plutonium-244](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plutonium-244 "Plutonium-244")[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-:3-19) from deep sea materials. Supernova-originated [Aluminium-26](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium-26 "Aluminium-26"), which was expected from cosmic ray studies, was not confirmed.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-24) Iron-60 and Manganese-53 have a peak 1.7–3.2 million years ago, and Iron-60 has a second peak 6.5–8.7 million years ago. The older peak likely originated when the solar system moved through the [Orion–Eridanus Superbubble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orion%E2%80%93Eridanus_Superbubble "Orion–Eridanus Superbubble") and the younger peak was generated when the solar system entered the Local Bubble 4.5 million years ago.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-:2-25) One of the supernovae creating the younger peak might have created the pulsar [PSR B1706-16](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=PSR_B1706-16&action=edit&redlink=1 "PSR B1706-16 (page does not exist)") and turned [Zeta Ophiuchi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zeta_Ophiuchi "Zeta Ophiuchi") into a [runaway star](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_kinematics "Stellar kinematics"). Both originated from UCL and were released by a supernova 1.78 ± 0.21 million years ago.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-26) Another explanation for the older peak is that it was produced by one supernova in the [Tucana-Horologium association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tucana-Horologium_association "Tucana-Horologium association") 7-9 million years ago.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Bubble#cite_note-27)
