[ 1 ] It belongs to the class Thermoplasmata , which thrive in acidic and high-temperature environments.

Thermoplasma are facultative anaerobes and respire using sulfur and organic carbon.  They do not contain a cell wall but instead contain a unique membrane composed mainly of a tetraether lipoglycan containing atypical archaeal tetraether lipid attached to a glucose - and mannose -containing oligosaccharide.  This lipoglycan is presumably responsible for the acid and thermal stability of the Thermoplasma membrane.
