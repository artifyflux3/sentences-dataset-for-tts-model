Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist who is best known for developing the theory of relativity.
Einstein also made important contributions to quantum mechanics.
His mass–energy equivalence formula E = mc² has been called the world's most famous equation.
He received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for his services to theoretical physics, especially for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect.
Born in the German Empire, Einstein moved to Switzerland in 1895 and renounced his German citizenship the following year.
In 1905, he published four groundbreaking papers that revolutionized physics.
Einstein proposed that space and time are interwoven into a single continuum known as spacetime.
His work laid the foundation for modern cosmology and our understanding of black holes.
In 1915, Einstein formulated the general theory of relativity, which describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime.
Einstein's theories have been confirmed through numerous experiments and observations over the decades.
As a pacifist, Einstein opposed war and militarism throughout his life.
He became a US citizen in 1940 after fleeing Nazi Germany in 1933.
Einstein warned President Roosevelt about the potential for nuclear weapons, indirectly contributing to the Manhattan Project.
After World War II, Einstein advocated for nuclear disarmament and world government.
Einstein declined the offer to become Israel's second president in 1952.
His intellectual achievements made Einstein synonymous with genius in popular culture.
Einstein played violin skillfully and considered music an essential part of his life.
The element einsteinium was named in his honor in 1955.
Einstein's brain was preserved after his death for scientific study.
His theories continue to shape our understanding of the universe today.
Einstein published hundreds of scientific papers and over 150 non-scientific works during his lifetime.
He maintained a lifelong fascination with the fundamental nature of reality.
Einstein's thought experiments helped him visualize complex physical principles.
The photoelectric effect work demonstrated light's particle-like properties.
Brownian motion studies provided evidence for the existence of atoms.
Special relativity introduced radical new concepts of space and time.
General relativity extended these insights to include accelerated motion and gravity.
Einstein spent much of his later career searching for a unified field theory.
His correspondence reveals deep philosophical reflections on science and humanity.
Einstein advocated for civil rights and opposed racism in America.
The philosophical implications of quantum mechanics troubled Einstein deeply.
He famously questioned whether God plays dice with the universe.
