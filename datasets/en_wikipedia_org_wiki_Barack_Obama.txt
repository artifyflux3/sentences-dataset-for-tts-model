Barack Obama, born on August 4, 1961, in Honolulu, Hawaii, is an American politician who served as the 44th President of the United States from 2009 to 2017. Before his presidency, Obama represented Illinois in the U.S. Senate (2005-2008) and the Illinois State Senate (1997-2004). He was the first African American to be elected president of the United States.

Obama graduated from Columbia University in 1983 with a degree in political science and later earned his law degree from Harvard Law School in 1991, where he became the first African American president of the Harvard Law Review. After law school, Obama worked as a civil rights attorney and taught constitutional law at the University of Chicago Law School.

During his presidency, Obama signed several significant pieces of legislation, including the Affordable Care Act (often referred to as "Obamacare"), which expanded health insurance coverage to millions of Americans. He also signed the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act in response to the 2008 financial crisis and oversaw the operation that resulted in the death of Osama bin Laden in 2011.

On the foreign policy front, Obama worked to restore America's image abroad, normalized relations with Cuba, signed the Paris Agreement on climate change, and negotiated the Iran nuclear deal. His administration also saw the legalization of same-sex marriage nationwide following the Supreme Court's ruling in Obergefell v. Hodges in 2015.

Obama left office with high approval ratings and continues to be active in public life through his philanthropic work with the Obama Foundation. He and his wife, Michelle Obama, have two daughters, Malia and Sasha.
